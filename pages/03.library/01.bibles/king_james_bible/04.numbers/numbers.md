# Numbers

### THE FOURTH BOOK OF MOSES CALLED NUMBERS

## CHAPTER 1.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses in the wilderness of Sinai, in the tabernacle of the congregation, on the first _day_ of the second month, in the second year after they were come out of the land of Egypt, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Take ye the sum of all the congregation of the children of Israel, after their families, by the house of their fathers, with the number of _their_ names, every male by their polls;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>From twenty years old and upward, all that are able to go forth to war in Israel: thou and Aaron shall number them by their armies.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And with you there shall be a man of every tribe; every one head of the house of his fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And these _are_ the names of the men that shall stand with you: of _the tribe of_ Reuben; Elizur the son of Shedeur.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Of Simeon; Shelumiel the son of Zurishaddai.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Of Judah; Nahshon the son of Amminadab.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Of Issachar; Nethaneel the son of Zuar.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Of Zebulun; Eliab the son of Helon.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Of the children of Joseph: of Ephraim; Elishama the son of Ammihud: of Manasseh; Gamaliel the son of Pedahzur.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Of Benjamin; Abidan the son of Gideoni.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Of Dan; Ahiezer the son of Ammishaddai.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Of Asher; Pagiel the son of Ocran.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Of Gad; Eliasaph the son of Deuel.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Of Naphtali; Ahira the son of Enan.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>These _were_ the renowned of the congregation, princes of the tribes of their fathers, heads of thousands in Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses and Aaron took these men which are expressed by _their_ names:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they assembled all the congregation together on the first _day_ of the second month, and they declared their pedigrees after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, by their polls.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>As the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses, so he numbered them in the wilderness of Sinai.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the children of Reuben, Israel’s eldest son, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, by their polls, every male from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Reuben, _were_ forty and six thousand and five hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Simeon, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, those that were numbered of them, according to the number of the names, by their polls, every male from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Simeon, _were_ fifty and nine thousand and three hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Gad, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Gad, _were_ forty and five thousand six hundred and fifty.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Judah, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Judah, _were_ threescore and fourteen thousand and six hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Issachar, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Issachar, _were_ fifty and four thousand and four hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Zebulun, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Zebulun, _were_ fifty and seven thousand and four hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Joseph, _namely_, of the children of Ephraim, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Ephraim, _were_ forty thousand and five hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Manasseh, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Manasseh, _were_ thirty and two thousand and two hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Benjamin, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Benjamin, _were_ thirty and five thousand and four hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Dan, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Dan, _were_ threescore and two thousand and seven hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Asher, by their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Asher, _were_ forty and one thousand and five hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>¶ Of the children of Naphtali, throughout their generations, after their families, by the house of their fathers, according to the number of the names, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war;  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, _even_ of the tribe of Naphtali, _were_ fifty and three thousand and four hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>¶ These _are_ those that were numbered, which Moses and Aaron numbered, and the princes of Israel, _being_ twelve men: each one was for the house of his fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>So were all those that were numbered of the children of Israel, by the house of their fathers, from twenty years old and upward, all that were able to go forth to war in Israel;  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>Even all they that were numbered were six hundred thousand and three thousand and five hundred and fifty.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>¶ But the Levites after the tribe of their fathers were not numbered among them.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had spoken unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>Only thou shalt not number the tribe of Levi, neither take the sum of them among the children of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>But thou shalt appoint the Levites over the tabernacle of testimony, and over all the vessels thereof, and over all things that _belong_ to it: they shall bear the tabernacle, and all the vessels thereof; and they shall minister unto it, and shall encamp round about the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>And when the tabernacle setteth forward, the Levites shall take it down: and when the tabernacle is to be pitched, the Levites shall set it up: and the stranger that cometh nigh shall be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel shall pitch their tents, every man by his own camp, and every man by his own standard, throughout their hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>But the Levites shall pitch round about the tabernacle of testimony, that there be no wrath upon the congregation of the children of Israel: and the Levites shall keep the charge of the tabernacle of testimony.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel did according to all that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses, so did they.  

## CHAPTER 2.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and unto Aaron, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Every man of the children of Israel shall pitch by his own standard, with the ensign of their father’s house: far off about the tabernacle of the congregation shall they pitch.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And on the east side toward the rising of the sun shall they of the standard of the camp of Judah pitch throughout their armies: and Nahshon the son of Amminadab _shall be_ captain of the children of Judah.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ threescore and fourteen thousand and six hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And those that do pitch next unto him _shall be_ the tribe of Issachar: and Nethaneel the son of Zuar _shall be_ captain of the children of Issachar.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered thereof, _were_ fifty and four thousand and four hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_Then_ the tribe of Zebulun: and Eliab the son of Helon _shall be_ captain of the children of Zebulun.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered thereof, _were_ fifty and seven thousand and four hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>All that were numbered in the camp of Judah _were_ an hundred thousand and fourscore thousand and six thousand and four hundred, throughout their armies. These shall first set forth.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ On the south side _shall be_ the standard of the camp of Reuben according to their armies: and the captain of the children of Reuben _shall be_ Elizur the son of Shedeur.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered thereof, _were_ forty and six thousand and five hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And those which pitch by him _shall be_ the tribe of Simeon: and the captain of the children of Simeon _shall be_ Shelumiel the son of Zurishaddai.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ fifty and nine thousand and three hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Then the tribe of Gad: and the captain of the sons of Gad _shall be_ Eliasaph the son of Reuel.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ forty and five thousand and six hundred and fifty.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>All that were numbered in the camp of Reuben _were_ an hundred thousand and fifty and one thousand and four hundred and fifty, throughout their armies. And they shall set forth in the second rank.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Then the tabernacle of the congregation shall set forward with the camp of the Levites in the midst of the camp: as they encamp, so shall they set forward, every man in his place by their standards.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ On the west side _shall be_ the standard of the camp of Ephraim according to their armies: and the captain of the sons of Ephraim _shall be_ Elishama the son of Ammihud.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ forty thousand and five hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And by him _shall be_ the tribe of Manasseh: and the captain of the children of Manasseh _shall be_ Gamaliel the son of Pedahzur.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ thirty and two thousand and two hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Then the tribe of Benjamin: and the captain of the sons of Benjamin _shall be_ Abidan the son of Gideoni.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ thirty and five thousand and four hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>All that were numbered of the camp of Ephraim _were_ an hundred thousand and eight thousand and an hundred, throughout their armies. And they shall go forward in the third rank.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ The standard of the camp of Dan _shall be_ on the north side by their armies: and the captain of the children of Dan _shall be_ Ahiezer the son of Ammishaddai.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ threescore and two thousand and seven hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And those that encamp by him _shall be_ the tribe of Asher: and the captain of the children of Asher _shall be_ Pagiel the son of Ocran.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ forty and one thousand and five hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ Then the tribe of Naphtali: and the captain of the children of Naphtali _shall be_ Ahira the son of Enan.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And his host, and those that were numbered of them, _were_ fifty and three thousand and four hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>All they that were numbered in the camp of Dan _were_ an hundred thousand and fifty and seven thousand and six hundred. They shall go hindmost with their standards.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ These _are_ those which were numbered of the children of Israel by the house of their fathers: all those that were numbered of the camps throughout their hosts _were_ six hundred thousand and three thousand and five hundred and fifty.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>But the Levites were not numbered among the children of Israel; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel did according to all that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses: so they pitched by their standards, and so they set forward, every one after their families, according to the house of their fathers.  

## CHAPTER 3.

These also _are_ the generations of Aaron and Moses in the day _that_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake with Moses in mount Sinai.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And these _are_ the names of the sons of Aaron; Nadab the firstborn, and Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>These _are_ the names of the sons of Aaron, the priests which were anointed, whom he consecrated to minister in the priest’s office.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Nadab and Abihu died before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when they offered strange fire before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, in the wilderness of Sinai, and they had no children: and Eleazar and Ithamar ministered in the priest’s office in the sight of Aaron their father.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Bring the tribe of Levi near, and present them before Aaron the priest, that they may minister unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they shall keep his charge, and the charge of the whole congregation before the tabernacle of the congregation, to do the service of the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they shall keep all the instruments of the tabernacle of the congregation, and the charge of the children of Israel, to do the service of the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And thou shalt give the Levites unto Aaron and to his sons: they _are_ wholly given unto him out of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And thou shalt appoint Aaron and his sons, and they shall wait on their priest’s office: and the stranger that cometh nigh shall be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And I, behold, I have taken the Levites from among the children of Israel instead of all the firstborn that openeth the matrix among the children of Israel: therefore the Levites shall be mine;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Because all the firstborn _are_ mine; _for_ on the day that I smote all the firstborn in the land of Egypt I hallowed unto me all the firstborn in Israel, both man and beast: mine shall they be: I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses in the wilderness of Sinai, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Number the children of Levi after the house of their fathers, by their families: every male from a month old and upward shalt thou number them.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Moses numbered them according to the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as he was commanded.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And these were the sons of Levi by their names; Gershon, and Kohath, and Merari.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And these _are_ the names of the sons of Gershon by their families; Libni, and Shimei.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the sons of Kohath by their families; Amram, and Izehar, Hebron, and Uzziel.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the sons of Merari by their families; Mahli, and Mushi. These _are_ the families of the Levites according to the house of their fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Of Gershon _was_ the family of the Libnites, and the family of the Shimites: these _are_ the families of the Gershonites.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Those that were numbered of them, according to the number of all the males, from a month old and upward, _even_ those that were numbered of them _were_ seven thousand and five hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The families of the Gershonites shall pitch behind the tabernacle westward.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the chief of the house of the father of the Gershonites _shall be_ Eliasaph the son of Lael.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the charge of the sons of Gershon in the tabernacle of the congregation _shall be_ the tabernacle, and the tent, the covering thereof, and the hanging for the door of the tabernacle of the congregation,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And the hangings of the court, and the curtain for the door of the court, which _is_ by the tabernacle, and by the altar round about, and the cords of it for all the service thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And of Kohath _was_ the family of the Amramites, and the family of the Izeharites, and the family of the Hebronites, and the family of the Uzzielites: these _are_ the families of the Kohathites.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>In the number of all the males, from a month old and upward, _were_ eight thousand and six hundred, keeping the charge of the sanctuary.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>The families of the sons of Kohath shall pitch on the side of the tabernacle southward.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the chief of the house of the father of the families of the Kohathites _shall be_ Elizaphan the son of Uzziel.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And their charge _shall be_ the ark, and the table, and the candlestick, and the altars, and the vessels of the sanctuary wherewith they minister, and the hanging, and all the service thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And Eleazar the son of Aaron the priest _shall be_ chief over the chief of the Levites, _and have_ the oversight of them that keep the charge of the sanctuary.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ Of Merari _was_ the family of the Mahlites, and the family of the Mushites: these _are_ the families of Merari.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And those that were numbered of them, according to the number of all the males, from a month old and upward, _were_ six thousand and two hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the chief of the house of the father of the families of Merari _was_ Zuriel the son of Abihail: _these_ shall pitch on the side of the tabernacle northward.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And _under_ the custody and charge of the sons of Merari _shall be_ the boards of the tabernacle, and the bars thereof, and the pillars thereof, and the sockets thereof, and all the vessels thereof, and all that serveth thereto,  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And the pillars of the court round about, and their sockets, and their pins, and their cords.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>¶ But those that encamp before the tabernacle toward the east, _even_ before the tabernacle of the congregation eastward, _shall be_ Moses, and Aaron and his sons, keeping the charge of the sanctuary for the charge of the children of Israel; and the stranger that cometh nigh shall be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>All that were numbered of the Levites, which Moses and Aaron numbered at the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, throughout their families, all the males from a month old and upward, _were_ twenty and two thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Number all the firstborn of the males of the children of Israel from a month old and upward, and take the number of their names.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take the Levites for me (I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>) instead of all the firstborn among the children of Israel; and the cattle of the Levites instead of all the firstlings among the cattle of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And Moses numbered, as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded him, all the firstborn among the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And all the firstborn males by the number of names, from a month old and upward, of those that were numbered of them, were twenty and two thousand two hundred and threescore and thirteen.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>Take the Levites instead of all the firstborn among the children of Israel, and the cattle of the Levites instead of their cattle; and the Levites shall be mine: I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And for those that are to be redeemed of the two hundred and threescore and thirteen of the firstborn of the children of Israel, which are more than the Levites;  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>Thou shalt even take five shekels apiece by the poll, after the shekel of the sanctuary shalt thou take _them_: (the shekel _is_ twenty gerahs:)  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And thou shalt give the money, wherewith the odd number of them is to be redeemed, unto Aaron and to his sons.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>And Moses took the redemption money of them that were over and above them that were redeemed by the Levites:  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>Of the firstborn of the children of Israel took he the money; a thousand three hundred and threescore and five _shekels_, after the shekel of the sanctuary:  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>And Moses gave the money of them that were redeemed unto Aaron and to his sons, according to the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  

## CHAPTER 4.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and unto Aaron, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Take the sum of the sons of Kohath from among the sons of Levi, after their families, by the house of their fathers,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>From thirty years old and upward even until fifty years old, all that enter into the host, to do the work in the tabernacle of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>This _shall be_ the service of the sons of Kohath in the tabernacle of the congregation, _about_ the most holy things:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And when the camp setteth forward, Aaron shall come, and his sons, and they shall take down the covering vail, and cover the ark of testimony with it:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And shall put thereon the covering of badgers’ skins, and shall spread over _it_ a cloth wholly of blue, and shall put in the staves thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And upon the table of shewbread they shall spread a cloth of blue, and put thereon the dishes, and the spoons, and the bowls, and covers to cover withal: and the continual bread shall be thereon:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they shall spread upon them a cloth of scarlet, and cover the same with a covering of badgers’ skins, and shall put in the staves thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And they shall take a cloth of blue, and cover the candlestick of the light, and his lamps, and his tongs, and his snuffdishes, and all the oil vessels thereof, wherewith they minister unto it:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they shall put it and all the vessels thereof within a covering of badgers’ skins, and shall put _it_ upon a bar.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And upon the golden altar they shall spread a cloth of blue, and cover it with a covering of badgers’ skins, and shall put to the staves thereof:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And they shall take all the instruments of ministry, wherewith they minister in the sanctuary, and put _them_ in a cloth of blue, and cover them with a covering of badgers’ skins, and shall put _them_ on a bar:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And they shall take away the ashes from the altar, and spread a purple cloth thereon:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they shall put upon it all the vessels thereof, wherewith they minister about it, _even_ the censers, the fleshhooks, and the shovels, and the basons, all the vessels of the altar; and they shall spread upon it a covering of badgers’ skins, and put to the staves of it.

## CHAPTER 5.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Command the children of Israel, that they put out of the camp every leper, and every one that hath an issue, and whosoever is defiled by the dead:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Both male and female shall ye put out, without the camp shall ye put them; that they defile not their camps, in the midst whereof I dwell.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel did so, and put them out without the camp: as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, so did the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, When a man or woman shall commit any sin that men commit, to do a trespass against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and that person be guilty;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then they shall confess their sin which they have done: and he shall recompense his trespass with the principal thereof, and add unto it the fifth _part_ thereof, and give _it_ unto _him_ against whom he hath trespassed.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But if the man have no kinsman to recompense the trespass unto, let the trespass be recompensed unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _even_ to the priest; beside the ram of the atonement, whereby an atonement shall be made for him.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And every offering of all the holy things of the children of Israel, which they bring unto the priest, shall be his.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And every man’s hallowed things shall be his: whatsoever any man giveth the priest, it shall be his.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, If any man’s wife go aside, and commit a trespass against him,  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And a man lie with her carnally, and it be hid from the eyes of her husband, and be kept close, and she be defiled, and _there be_ no witness against her, neither she be taken _with the manner_;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the spirit of jealousy come upon him, and he be jealous of his wife, and she be defiled: or if the spirit of jealousy come upon him, and he be jealous of his wife, and she be not defiled:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Then shall the man bring his wife unto the priest, and he shall bring her offering for her, the tenth _part_ of an ephah of barley meal; he shall pour no oil upon it, nor put frankincense thereon; for it _is_ an offering of jealousy, an offering of memorial, bringing iniquity to remembrance.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the priest shall bring her near, and set her before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the priest shall take holy water in an earthen vessel; and of the dust that is in the floor of the tabernacle the priest shall take, and put _it_ into the water:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the priest shall set the woman before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and uncover the woman’s head, and put the offering of memorial in her hands, which _is_ the jealousy offering: and the priest shall have in his hand the bitter water that causeth the curse:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the priest shall charge her by an oath, and say unto the woman, If no man have lain with thee, and if thou hast not gone aside to uncleanness _with another_ instead of thy husband, be thou free from this bitter water that causeth the curse:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But if thou hast gone aside _to another_ instead of thy husband, and if thou be defiled, and some man have lain with thee beside thine husband:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Then the priest shall charge the woman with an oath of cursing, and the priest shall say unto the woman, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> make thee a curse and an oath among thy people, when the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> doth make thy thigh to rot, and thy belly to swell;

<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the priest shall write these curses in a book, and he shall blot _them_ out with the bitter water:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And he shall cause the woman to drink the bitter water that causeth the curse: and the water that causeth the curse shall enter into her, _and become_ bitter.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Then the priest shall take the jealousy offering out of the woman’s hand, and shall wave the offering before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and offer it upon the altar:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And the priest shall take an handful of the offering, _even_ the memorial thereof, and burn _it_ upon the altar, and afterward shall cause the woman to drink the water.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And when he hath made her to drink the water, then it shall come to pass, _that_, if she be defiled, and have done trespass against her husband, that the water that causeth the curse shall enter into her, _and become_ bitter, and her belly shall swell, and her thigh shall rot: and the woman shall be a curse among her people.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And if the woman be not defiled, but be clean; then she shall be free, and shall conceive seed.

<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Or when the spirit of jealousy cometh upon him, and he be jealous over his wife, and shall set the woman before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and the priest shall execute upon her all this law.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Then shall the man be guiltless from iniquity, and this woman shall bear her iniquity.  

## CHAPTER 6.

<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>At the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> they rested in the tents, and at the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> they journeyed: they kept the charge of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, at the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> by the hand of Moses.

<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And the mixt multitude that _was_ among them fell a lusting: and the children of Israel also wept again, and said, Who shall give us flesh to eat?

<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>_And_ the people went about, and gathered _it_, and ground _it_ in mills, or beat _it_ in a mortar, and baked _it_ in pans, and made cakes of it: and the taste of it was as the taste of fresh oil.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And when the dew fell upon the camp in the night, the manna fell upon it.

<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Wherefore hast thou afflicted thy servant? and wherefore have I not found favour in thy sight, that thou layest the burden of all this people upon me?

<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I am not able to bear all this people alone, because _it is_ too heavy for me.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And if thou deal thus with me, kill me, I pray thee, out of hand, if I have found favour in thy sight; and let me not see my wretchedness.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Gather unto me seventy men of the elders of Israel, whom thou knowest to be the elders of the people, and officers over them; and bring them unto the tabernacle of the congregation, that they may stand there with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And I will come down and talk with thee there: and I will take of the spirit which _is_ upon thee, and will put _it_ upon them; and they shall bear the burden of the people with thee, that thou bear _it_ not thyself alone.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And say thou unto the people, Sanctify yourselves against to morrow, and ye shall eat flesh: for ye have wept in the ears of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, Who shall give us flesh to eat? for _it was_ well with us in Egypt: therefore the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will give you flesh, and ye shall eat.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Ye shall not eat one day, nor two days, nor five days, neither ten days, nor twenty days;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>_But_ even a whole month, until it come out at your nostrils, and it be loathsome unto you: because that ye have despised the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> which _is_ among you, and have wept before him, saying, Why came we forth out of Egypt?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Moses said, The people, among whom I _am_, _are_ six hundred thousand footmen; and thou hast said, I will give them flesh, that they may eat a whole month.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Shall the flocks and the herds be slain for them, to suffice them? or shall all the fish of the sea be gathered together for them, to suffice them?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Is the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> hand waxed short? thou shalt see now whether my word shall come to pass unto thee or not.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses went out, and told the people the words of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and gathered the seventy men of the elders of the people, and set them round about the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came down in a cloud, and spake unto him, and took of the spirit that _was_ upon him, and gave _it_ unto the seventy elders: and it came to pass, _that_, when the spirit rested upon them, they prophesied, and did not cease.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But there remained two _of the_ men in the camp, the name of the one _was_ Eldad, and the name of the other Medad: and the spirit rested upon them; and they _were_ of them that were written, but went not out unto the tabernacle: and they prophesied in the camp.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And there ran a young man, and told Moses, and said, Eldad and Medad do prophesy in the camp.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And Joshua the son of Nun, the servant of Moses, _one_ of his young men, answered and said, My lord Moses, forbid them.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto him, Enviest thou for my sake? would God that all the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> people were prophets, _and_ that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> would put his spirit upon them!  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Moses gat him into the camp, he and the elders of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And there went forth a wind from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and brought quails from the sea, and let _them_ fall by the camp, as it were a day’s journey on this side, and as it were a day’s journey on the other side, round about the camp, and as it were two cubits _high_ upon the face of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And the people stood up all that day, and all _that_ night, and all the next day, and they gathered the quails: he that gathered least gathered ten homers: and they spread _them_ all abroad for themselves round about the camp.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And while the flesh _was_ yet between their teeth, ere it was chewed, the wrath of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was kindled against the people, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> smote the people with a very great plague.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And he called the name of that place Kibroth–hattaavah: because there they buried the people that lusted.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>_And_ the people journeyed from Kibroth–hattaavah unto Hazeroth; and abode at Hazeroth.  

## CHAPTER 12.

And Miriam and Aaron spake against Moses because of the Ethiopian woman whom he had married: for he had married an Ethiopian woman.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And they said, Hath the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> indeed spoken only by Moses? hath he not spoken also by us? And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> heard _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>(Now the man Moses _was_ very meek, above all the men which _were_ upon the face of the earth.)  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake suddenly unto Moses, and unto Aaron, and unto Miriam, Come out ye three unto the tabernacle of the congregation. And they three came out.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came down in the pillar of the cloud, and stood _in_ the door of the tabernacle, and called Aaron and Miriam: and they both came forth.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he said, Hear now my words: If there be a prophet among you, _I_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will make myself known unto him in a vision, _and_ will speak unto him in a dream.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>My servant Moses _is_ not so, who _is_ faithful in all mine house.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>With him will I speak mouth to mouth, even apparently, and not in dark speeches; and the similitude of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall he behold: wherefore then were ye not afraid to speak against my servant Moses?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the anger of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was kindled against them; and he departed.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the cloud departed from off the tabernacle; and, behold, Miriam _became_ leprous, _white_ as snow: and Aaron looked upon Miriam, and, behold, _she was_ leprous.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Aaron said unto Moses, Alas, my lord, I beseech thee, lay not the sin upon us, wherein we have done foolishly, and wherein we have sinned.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Let her not be as one dead, of whom the flesh is half consumed when he cometh out of his mother’s womb.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Moses cried unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, Heal her now, O God, I beseech thee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, If her father had but spit in her face, should she not be ashamed seven days? let her be shut out from the camp seven days, and after that let her be received in _again_.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Miriam was shut out from the camp seven days: and the people journeyed not till Miriam was brought in _again_.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And afterward the people removed from Hazeroth, and pitched in the wilderness of Paran.  

## CHAPTER 13.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Send thou men, that they may search the land of Canaan, which I give unto the children of Israel: of every tribe of their fathers shall ye send a man, every one a ruler among them.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Moses by the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> sent them from the wilderness of Paran: all those men _were_ heads of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And these _were_ their names: of the tribe of Reuben, Shammua the son of Zaccur.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Simeon, Shaphat the son of Hori.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Judah, Caleb the son of Jephunneh.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Issachar, Igal the son of Joseph.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Ephraim, Oshea the son of Nun.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Benjamin, Palti the son of Raphu.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Zebulun, Gaddiel the son of Sodi.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Joseph, _namely_, of the tribe of Manasseh, Gaddi the son of Susi.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Dan, Ammiel the son of Gemalli.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Asher, Sethur the son of Michael.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Naphtali, Nahbi the son of Vophsi.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Gad, Geuel the son of Machi.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>These _are_ the names of the men which Moses sent to spy out the land. And Moses called Oshea the son of Nun Jehoshua.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses sent them to spy out the land of Canaan, and said unto them, Get you up this _way_ southward, and go up into the mountain:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And see the land, what it _is_; and the people that dwelleth therein, whether they _be_ strong or weak, few or many;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And what the land _is_ that they dwell in, whether it _be_ good or bad; and what cities _they be_ that they dwell in, whether in tents, or in strong holds;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And what the land _is_, whether it _be_ fat or lean, whether there be wood therein, or not. And be ye of good courage, and bring of the fruit of the land. Now the time _was_ the time of the firstripe grapes.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ So they went up, and searched the land from the wilderness of Zin unto Rehob, as men come to Hamath.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And they ascended by the south, and came unto Hebron; where Ahiman, Sheshai, and Talmai, the children of Anak, _were_. (Now Hebron was built seven years before Zoan in Egypt.)  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they came unto the brook of Eshcol, and cut down from thence a branch with one cluster of grapes, and they bare it between two upon a staff; and _they brought_ of the pomegranates, and of the figs.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>The place was called the brook Eshcol, because of the cluster of grapes which the children of Israel cut down from thence.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And they returned from searching of the land after forty days.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And they went and came to Moses, and to Aaron, and to all the congregation of the children of Israel, unto the wilderness of Paran, to Kadesh; and brought back word unto them, and unto all the congregation, and shewed them the fruit of the land.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And they told him, and said, We came unto the land whither thou sentest us, and surely it floweth with milk and honey; and this _is_ the fruit of it.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Nevertheless the people _be_ strong that dwell in the land, and the cities _are_ walled, _and_ very great: and moreover we saw the children of Anak there.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>The Amalekites dwell in the land of the south: and the Hittites, and the Jebusites, and the Amorites, dwell in the mountains: and the Canaanites dwell by the sea, and by the coast of Jordan.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Caleb stilled the people before Moses, and said, Let us go up at once, and possess it; for we are well able to overcome it.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>But the men that went up with him said, We be not able to go up against the people; for they _are_ stronger than we.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And they brought up an evil report of the land which they had searched unto the children of Israel, saying, The land, through which we have gone to search it, _is_ a land that eateth up the inhabitants thereof; and all the people that we saw in it _are_ men of a great stature.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And there we saw the giants, the sons of Anak, _which come_ of the giants: and we were in our own sight as grasshoppers, and so we were in their sight.  

## CHAPTER 14.

And all the congregation lifted up their voice, and cried; and the people wept that night.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And all the children of Israel murmured against Moses and against Aaron: and the whole congregation said unto them, Would God that we had died in the land of Egypt! or would God we had died in this wilderness!  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And wherefore hath the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought us unto this land, to fall by the sword, that our wives and our children should be a prey? were it not better for us to return into Egypt?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And they said one to another, Let us make a captain, and let us return into Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Then Moses and Aaron fell on their faces before all the assembly of the congregation of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And Joshua the son of Nun, and Caleb the son of Jephunneh, _which were_ of them that searched the land, rent their clothes:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they spake unto all the company of the children of Israel, saying, The land, which we passed through to search it, _is_ an exceeding good land.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>If the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> delight in us, then he will bring us into this land, and give it us; a land which floweth with milk and honey.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Only rebel not ye against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, neither fear ye the people of the land; for they _are_ bread for us: their defence is departed from them, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ with us: fear them not.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But all the congregation bade stone them with stones. And the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> appeared in the tabernacle of the congregation before all the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, How long will this people provoke me? and how long will it be ere they believe me, for all the signs which I have shewed among them?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I will smite them with the pestilence, and disinherit them, and will make of thee a greater nation and mightier than they.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Then the Egyptians shall hear _it_, (for thou broughtest up this people in thy might from among them;)  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they will tell _it_ to the inhabitants of this land: _for_ they have heard that thou <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _art_ among this people, that thou <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> art seen face to face, and _that_ thy cloud standeth over them, and _that_ thou goest before them, by day time in a pillar of a cloud, and in a pillar of fire by night.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Now _if_ thou shalt kill _all_ this people as one man, then the nations which have heard the fame of thee will speak, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Because the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was not able to bring this people into the land which he sware unto them, therefore he hath slain them in the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And now, I beseech thee, let the power of my Lord be great, according as thou hast spoken, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ longsuffering, and of great mercy, forgiving iniquity and transgression, and by no means clearing _the guilty_, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth _generation_.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Pardon, I beseech thee, the iniquity of this people according unto the greatness of thy mercy, and as thou hast forgiven this people, from Egypt even until now.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said, I have pardoned according to thy word:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But _as_ truly _as_ I live, all the earth shall be filled with the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Because all those men which have seen my glory, and my miracles, which I did in Egypt and in the wilderness, and have tempted me now these ten times, and have not hearkened to my voice;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Surely they shall not see the land which I sware unto their fathers, neither shall any of them that provoked me see it:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>But my servant Caleb, because he had another spirit with him, and hath followed me fully, him will I bring into the land whereinto he went; and his seed shall possess it.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>(Now the Amalekites and the Canaanites dwelt in the valley.) To morrow turn you, and get you into the wilderness by the way of the Red sea.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and unto Aaron, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>How long _shall I bear with_ this evil congregation, which murmur against me? I have heard the murmurings of the children of Israel, which they murmur against me.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Say unto them, _As truly as_ I live, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as ye have spoken in mine ears, so will I do to you:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Your carcases shall fall in this wilderness; and all that were numbered of you, according to your whole number, from twenty years old and upward, which have murmured against me,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Doubtless ye shall not come into the land, _concerning_ which I sware to make you dwell therein, save Caleb the son of Jephunneh, and Joshua the son of Nun.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>But your little ones, which ye said should be a prey, them will I bring in, and they shall know the land which ye have despised.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>But _as for_ you, your carcases, they shall fall in this wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And your children shall wander in the wilderness forty years, and bear your whoredoms, until your carcases be wasted in the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, _even_ forty days, each day for a year, shall ye bear your iniquities, _even_ forty years, and ye shall know my breach of promise.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have said, I will surely do it unto all this evil congregation, that are gathered together against me: in this wilderness they shall be consumed, and there they shall die.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ And the men, which Moses sent to search the land, who returned, and made all the congregation to murmur against him, by bringing up a slander upon the land,  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Even those men that did bring up the evil report upon the land, died by the plague before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>But Joshua the son of Nun, and Caleb the son of Jephunneh, _which were_ of the men that went to search the land, lived _still_.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And Moses told these sayings unto all the children of Israel: and the people mourned greatly.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>¶ And they rose up early in the morning, and gat them up into the top of the mountain, saying, Lo, we _be here_, and will go up unto the place which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath promised: for we have sinned.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And Moses said, Wherefore now do ye transgress the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>? but it shall not prosper.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>Go not up, for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ not among you; that ye be not smitten before your enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>For the Amalekites and the Canaanites _are_ there before you, and ye shall fall by the sword: because ye are turned away from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, therefore the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will not be with you.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>But they presumed to go up unto the hill top: nevertheless the ark of the covenant of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and Moses, departed not out of the camp.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>Then the Amalekites came down, and the Canaanites which dwelt in that hill, and smote them, and discomfited them, _even_ unto Hormah.  

## CHAPTER 15.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, When ye be come into the land of your habitations, which I give unto you,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And will make an offering by fire unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, a burnt offering, or a sacrifice in performing a vow, or in a freewill offering, or in your solemn feasts, to make a sweet savour unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, of the herd, or of the flock:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then shall he that offereth his offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> bring a meat offering of a tenth deal of flour mingled with the fourth _part_ of an hin of oil.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the fourth _part_ of an hin of wine for a drink offering shalt thou prepare with the burnt offering or sacrifice, for one lamb.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Or for a ram, thou shalt prepare _for_ a meat offering two tenth deals of flour mingled with the third _part_ of an hin of oil.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And for a drink offering thou shalt offer the third _part_ of an hin of wine, _for_ a sweet savour unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And when thou preparest a bullock _for_ a burnt offering, or _for_ a sacrifice in performing a vow, or peace offerings unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then shall he bring with a bullock a meat offering of three tenth deals of flour mingled with half an hin of oil.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And thou shalt bring for a drink offering half an hin of wine, _for_ an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Thus shall it be done for one bullock, or for one ram, or for a lamb, or a kid.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>According to the number that ye shall prepare, so shall ye do to every one according to their number.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>All that are born of the country shall do these things after this manner, in offering an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And if a stranger sojourn with you, or whosoever _be_ among you in your generations, and will offer an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; as ye do, so he shall do.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>One ordinance _shall be both_ for you of the congregation, and also for the stranger that sojourneth _with you_, an ordinance for ever in your generations: as ye _are_, so shall the stranger be before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>One law and one manner shall be for you, and for the stranger that sojourneth with you.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, When ye come into the land whither I bring you,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Then it shall be, that, when ye eat of the bread of the land, ye shall offer up an heave offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Ye shall offer up a cake of the first of your dough _for_ an heave offering: as _ye do_ the heave offering of the threshingfloor, so shall ye heave it.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Of the first of your dough ye shall give unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> an heave offering in your generations.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And if ye have erred, and not observed all these commandments, which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken unto Moses,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>_Even_ all that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath commanded you by the hand of Moses, from the day that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded _Moses_, and henceforward among your generations;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Then it shall be, if _ought_ be committed by ignorance without the knowledge of the congregation, that all the congregation shall offer one young bullock for a burnt offering, for a sweet savour unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, with his meat offering, and his drink offering, according to the manner, and one kid of the goats for a sin offering.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the priest shall make an atonement for all the congregation of the children of Israel, and it shall be forgiven them; for it _is_ ignorance: and they shall bring their offering, a sacrifice made by fire unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and their sin offering before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, for their ignorance:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And it shall be forgiven all the congregation of the children of Israel, and the stranger that sojourneth among them; seeing all the people _were_ in ignorance.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And if any soul sin through ignorance, then he shall bring a she goat of the first year for a sin offering.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the priest shall make an atonement for the soul that sinneth ignorantly, when he sinneth by ignorance before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to make an atonement for him; and it shall be forgiven him.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Ye shall have one law for him that sinneth through ignorance, _both for_ him that is born among the children of Israel, and for the stranger that sojourneth among them.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ But the soul that doeth _ought_ presumptuously, _whether he be_ born in the land, or a stranger, the same reproacheth the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and that soul shall be cut off from among his people.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Because he hath despised the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and hath broken his commandment, that soul shall utterly be cut off; his iniquity _shall be_ upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ And while the children of Israel were in the wilderness, they found a man that gathered sticks upon the sabbath day.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And they that found him gathering sticks brought him unto Moses and Aaron, and unto all the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And they put him in ward, because it was not declared what should be done to him.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, The man shall be surely put to death: all the congregation shall stone him with stones without the camp.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And all the congregation brought him without the camp, and stoned him with stones, and he died; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, and bid them that they make them fringes in the borders of their garments throughout their generations, and that they put upon the fringe of the borders a ribband of blue:  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And it shall be unto you for a fringe, that ye may look upon it, and remember all the commandments of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and do them; and that ye seek not after your own heart and your own eyes, after which ye use to go a whoring:  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>That ye may remember, and do all my commandments, and be holy unto your God.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God, which brought you out of the land of Egypt, to be your God: I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God.  

## CHAPTER 16.

Now Korah, the son of Izhar, the son of Kohath, the son of Levi, and Dathan and Abiram, the sons of Eliab, and On, the son of Peleth, sons of Reuben, took _men_:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And they rose up before Moses, with certain of the children of Israel, two hundred and fifty princes of the assembly, famous in the congregation, men of renown:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And they gathered themselves together against Moses and against Aaron, and said unto them, _Ye take_ too much upon you, seeing all the congregation _are_ holy, every one of them, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ among them: wherefore then lift ye up yourselves above the congregation of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And when Moses heard _it_, he fell upon his face:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And he spake unto Korah and unto all his company, saying, Even to morrow the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will shew who _are_ his, and _who is_ holy; and will cause _him_ to come near unto him: even _him_ whom he hath chosen will he cause to come near unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>This do; Take you censers, Korah, and all his company;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And put fire therein, and put incense in them before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to morrow: and it shall be _that_ the man whom the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> doth choose, he _shall be_ holy: _ye take_ too much upon you, ye sons of Levi.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto Korah, Hear, I pray you, ye sons of Levi:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_Seemeth it but_ a small thing unto you, that the God of Israel hath separated you from the congregation of Israel, to bring you near to himself to do the service of the tabernacle of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and to stand before the congregation to minister unto them?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he hath brought thee near _to him_, and all thy brethren the sons of Levi with thee: and seek ye the priesthood also?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For which cause _both_ thou and all thy company _are_ gathered together against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and what _is_ Aaron, that ye murmur against him?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses sent to call Dathan and Abiram, the sons of Eliab: which said, We will not come up:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>_Is it_ a small thing that thou hast brought us up out of a land that floweth with milk and honey, to kill us in the wilderness, except thou make thyself altogether a prince over us?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Moreover thou hast not brought us into a land that floweth with milk and honey, or given us inheritance of fields and vineyards: wilt thou put out the eyes of these men? we will not come up.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Moses was very wroth, and said unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Respect not thou their offering: I have not taken one ass from them, neither have I hurt one of them.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto Korah, Be thou and all thy company before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou, and they, and Aaron, to morrow:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And take every man his censer, and put incense in them, and bring ye before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> every man his censer, two hundred and fifty censers; thou also, and Aaron, each _of you_ his censer.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they took every man his censer, and put fire in them, and laid incense thereon, and stood in the door of the tabernacle of the congregation with Moses and Aaron.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And Korah gathered all the congregation against them unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation: and the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> appeared unto all the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and unto Aaron, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Separate yourselves from among this congregation, that I may consume them in a moment.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And they fell upon their faces, and said, O God, the God of the spirits of all flesh, shall one man sin, and wilt thou be wroth with all the congregation?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Speak unto the congregation, saying, Get you up from about the tabernacle of Korah, Dathan, and Abiram.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And Moses rose up and went unto Dathan and Abiram; and the elders of Israel followed him.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And he spake unto the congregation, saying, Depart, I pray you, from the tents of these wicked men, and touch nothing of theirs, lest ye be consumed in all their sins.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>So they gat up from the tabernacle of Korah, Dathan, and Abiram, on every side: and Dathan and Abiram came out, and stood in the door of their tents, and their wives, and their sons, and their little children.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And Moses said, Hereby ye shall know that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath sent me to do all these works; for _I have_ not _done them_ of mine own mind.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>If these men die the common death of all men, or if they be visited after the visitation of all men; _then_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath not sent me.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>But if the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> make a new thing, and the earth open her mouth, and swallow them up, with all that _appertain_ unto them, and they go down quick into the pit; then ye shall understand that these men have provoked the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, as he had made an end of speaking all these words, that the ground clave asunder that _was_ under them:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And the earth opened her mouth, and swallowed them up, and their houses, and all the men that _appertained_ unto Korah, and all _their_ goods.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>They, and all that _appertained_ to them, went down alive into the pit, and the earth closed upon them: and they perished from among the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And all Israel that _were_ round about them fled at the cry of them: for they said, Lest the earth swallow us up _also_.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>¶ And there came out a fire from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and consumed the two hundred and fifty men that offered incense.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Speak unto Eleazar the son of Aaron the priest, that he take up the censers out of the burning, and scatter thou the fire yonder; for they are hallowed.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>The censers of these sinners against their own souls, let them make them broad plates _for_ a covering of the altar: for they offered them before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, therefore they are hallowed: and they shall be a sign unto the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And Eleazar the priest took the brasen censers, wherewith they that were burnt had offered; and they were made broad _plates for_ a covering of the altar:  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>_To be_ a memorial unto the children of Israel, that no stranger, which _is_ not of the seed of Aaron, come near to offer incense before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; that he be not as Korah, and as his company: as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said to him by the hand of Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>¶ But on the morrow all the congregation of the children of Israel murmured against Moses and against Aaron, saying, Ye have killed the people of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when the congregation was gathered against Moses and against Aaron, that they looked toward the tabernacle of the congregation: and, behold, the cloud covered it, and the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> appeared.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron came before the tabernacle of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>Get you up from among this congregation, that I may consume them as in a moment. And they fell upon their faces.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto Aaron, Take a censer, and put fire therein from off the altar, and put on incense, and go quickly unto the congregation, and make an atonement for them: for there is wrath gone out from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; the plague is begun.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And Aaron took as Moses commanded, and ran into the midst of the congregation; and, behold, the plague was begun among the people: and he put on incense, and made an atonement for the people.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And he stood between the dead and the living; and the plague was stayed.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>Now they that died in the plague were fourteen thousand and seven hundred, beside them that died about the matter of Korah.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>And Aaron returned unto Moses unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation: and the plague was stayed.  

## CHAPTER 17.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, and take of every one of them a rod according to the house of _their_ fathers, of all their princes according to the house of their fathers twelve rods: write thou every man’s name upon his rod.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And thou shalt write Aaron’s name upon the rod of Levi: for one rod _shall be_ for the head of the house of their fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And thou shalt lay them up in the tabernacle of the congregation before the testimony, where I will meet with you.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ the man’s rod, whom I shall choose, shall blossom: and I will make to cease from me the murmurings of the children of Israel, whereby they murmur against you.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses spake unto the children of Israel, and every one of their princes gave him a rod apiece, for each prince one, according to their fathers’ houses, _even_ twelve rods: and the rod of Aaron _was_ among their rods.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And Moses laid up the rods before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the tabernacle of witness.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, that on the morrow Moses went into the tabernacle of witness; and, behold, the rod of Aaron for the house of Levi was budded, and brought forth buds, and bloomed blossoms, and yielded almonds.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Moses brought out all the rods from before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto all the children of Israel: and they looked, and took every man his rod.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Bring Aaron’s rod again before the testimony, to be kept for a token against the rebels; and thou shalt quite take away their murmurings from me, that they die not.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Moses did _so_: as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded him, so did he.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel spake unto Moses, saying, Behold, we die, we perish, we all perish.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Whosoever cometh any thing near unto the tabernacle of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall die: shall we be consumed with dying?  

## CHAPTER 18.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Aaron, Thou and thy sons and thy father’s house with thee shall bear the iniquity of the sanctuary: and thou and thy sons with thee shall bear the iniquity of your priesthood.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And thy brethren also of the tribe of Levi, the tribe of thy father, bring thou with thee, that they may be joined unto thee, and minister unto thee: but thou and thy sons with thee _shall minister_ before the tabernacle of witness.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And they shall keep thy charge, and the charge of all the tabernacle: only they shall not come nigh the vessels of the sanctuary and the altar, that neither they, nor ye also, die.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And they shall be joined unto thee, and keep the charge of the tabernacle of the congregation, for all the service of the tabernacle: and a stranger shall not come nigh unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And ye shall keep the charge of the sanctuary, and the charge of the altar: that there be no wrath any more upon the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And I, behold, I have taken your brethren the Levites from among the children of Israel: to you _they are_ given _as_ a gift for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to do the service of the tabernacle of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore thou and thy sons with thee shall keep your priest’s office for every thing of the altar, and within the vail; and ye shall serve: I have given your priest’s office _unto you_ as a service of gift: and the stranger that cometh nigh shall be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Aaron, Behold, I also have given thee the charge of mine heave offerings of all the hallowed things of the children of Israel; unto thee have I given them by reason of the anointing, and to thy sons, by an ordinance for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>This shall be thine of the most holy things, _reserved_ from the fire: every oblation of theirs, every meat offering of theirs, and every sin offering of theirs, and every trespass offering of theirs, which they shall render unto me, _shall be_ most holy for thee and for thy sons.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>In the most holy _place_ shalt thou eat it; every male shall eat it: it shall be holy unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And this _is_ thine; the heave offering of their gift, with all the wave offerings of the children of Israel: I have given them unto thee, and to thy sons and to thy daughters with thee, by a statute for ever: every one that is clean in thy house shall eat of it.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>All the best of the oil, and all the best of the wine, and of the wheat, the firstfruits of them which they shall offer unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, them have I given thee.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>_And_ whatsoever is first ripe in the land, which they shall bring unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, shall be thine; every one that is clean in thine house shall eat _of_ it.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Every thing devoted in Israel shall be thine.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Every thing that openeth the matrix in all flesh, which they bring unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _whether it be_ of men or beasts, shall be thine: nevertheless the firstborn of man shalt thou surely redeem, and the firstling of unclean beasts shalt thou redeem.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And those that are to be redeemed from a month old shalt thou redeem, according to thine estimation, for the money of five shekels, after the shekel of the sanctuary, which _is_ twenty gerahs.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But the firstling of a cow, or the firstling of a sheep, or the firstling of a goat, thou shalt not redeem; they _are_ holy: thou shalt sprinkle their blood upon the altar, and shalt burn their fat _for_ an offering made by fire, for a sweet savour unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the flesh of them shall be thine, as the wave breast and as the right shoulder are thine.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>All the heave offerings of the holy things, which the children of Israel offer unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, have I given thee, and thy sons and thy daughters with thee, by a statute for ever: it _is_ a covenant of salt for ever before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto thee and to thy seed with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Aaron, Thou shalt have no inheritance in their land, neither shalt thou have any part among them: I _am_ thy part and thine inheritance among the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And, behold, I have given the children of Levi all the tenth in Israel for an inheritance, for their service which they serve, _even_ the service of the tabernacle of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Neither must the children of Israel henceforth come nigh the tabernacle of the congregation, lest they bear sin, and die.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But the Levites shall do the service of the tabernacle of the congregation, and they shall bear their iniquity: _it shall be_ a statute for ever throughout your generations, that among the children of Israel they have no inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>But the tithes of the children of Israel, which they offer _as_ an heave offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I have given to the Levites to inherit: therefore I have said unto them, Among the children of Israel they shall have no inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Thus speak unto the Levites, and say unto them, When ye take of the children of Israel the tithes which I have given you from them for your inheritance, then ye shall offer up an heave offering of it for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _even_ a tenth _part_ of the tithe.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And _this_ your heave offering shall be reckoned unto you, as though _it were_ the corn of the threshingfloor, and as the fulness of the winepress.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Thus ye also shall offer an heave offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of all your tithes, which ye receive of the children of Israel; and ye shall give thereof the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> heave offering to Aaron the priest.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Out of all your gifts ye shall offer every heave offering of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, of all the best thereof, _even_ the hallowed part thereof out of it.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Therefore thou shalt say unto them, When ye have heaved the best thereof from it, then it shall be counted unto the Levites as the increase of the threshingfloor, and as the increase of the winepress.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And ye shall eat it in every place, ye and your households: for it _is_ your reward for your service in the tabernacle of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And ye shall bear no sin by reason of it, when ye have heaved from it the best of it: neither shall ye pollute the holy things of the children of Israel, lest ye die.  

## CHAPTER 19.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and unto Aaron, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>This _is_ the ordinance of the law which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath commanded, saying, Speak unto the children of Israel, that they bring thee a red heifer without spot, wherein _is_ no blemish, _and_ upon which never came yoke:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And ye shall give her unto Eleazar the priest, that he may bring her forth without the camp, and _one_ shall slay her before his face:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Eleazar the priest shall take of her blood with his finger, and sprinkle of her blood directly before the tabernacle of the congregation seven times:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And _one_ shall burn the heifer in his sight; her skin, and her flesh, and her blood, with her dung, shall he burn:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the priest shall take cedar wood, and hyssop, and scarlet, and cast _it_ into the midst of the burning of the heifer.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then the priest shall wash his clothes, and he shall bathe his flesh in water, and afterward he shall come into the camp, and the priest shall be unclean until the even.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he that burneth her shall wash his clothes in water, and bathe his flesh in water, and shall be unclean until the even.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And a man _that is_ clean shall gather up the ashes of the heifer, and lay _them_ up without the camp in a clean place, and it shall be kept for the congregation of the children of Israel for a water of separation: it _is_ a purification for sin.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he that gathereth the ashes of the heifer shall wash his clothes, and be unclean until the even: and it shall be unto the children of Israel, and unto the stranger that sojourneth among them, for a statute for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ He that toucheth the dead body of any man shall be unclean seven days.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>He shall purify himself with it on the third day, and on the seventh day he shall be clean: but if he purify not himself the third day, then the seventh day he shall not be clean.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Whosoever toucheth the dead body of any man that is dead, and purifieth not himself, defileth the tabernacle of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and that soul shall be cut off from Israel: because the water of separation was not sprinkled upon him, he shall be unclean; his uncleanness _is_ yet upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>This _is_ the law, when a man dieth in a tent: all that come into the tent, and all that _is_ in the tent, shall be unclean seven days.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And every open vessel, which hath no covering bound upon it, _is_ unclean.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And whosoever toucheth one that is slain with a sword in the open fields, or a dead body, or a bone of a man, or a grave, shall be unclean seven days.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And for an unclean _person_ they shall take of the ashes of the burnt heifer of purification for sin, and running water shall be put thereto in a vessel:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And a clean person shall take hyssop, and dip _it_ in the water, and sprinkle _it_ upon the tent, and upon all the vessels, and upon the persons that were there, and upon him that touched a bone, or one slain, or one dead, or a grave:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the clean _person_ shall sprinkle upon the unclean on the third day, and on the seventh day: and on the seventh day he shall purify himself, and wash his clothes, and bathe himself in water, and shall be clean at even.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But the man that shall be unclean, and shall not purify himself, that soul shall be cut off from among the congregation, because he hath defiled the sanctuary of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: the water of separation hath not been sprinkled upon him; he _is_ unclean.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And it shall be a perpetual statute unto them, that he that sprinkleth the water of separation shall wash his clothes; and he that toucheth the water of separation shall be unclean until even.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And whatsoever the unclean _person_ toucheth shall be unclean; and the soul that toucheth _it_ shall be unclean until even.  

## CHAPTER 20.

Then came the children of Israel, _even_ the whole congregation, into the desert of Zin in the first month: and the people abode in Kadesh; and Miriam died there, and was buried there.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And there was no water for the congregation: and they gathered themselves together against Moses and against Aaron.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the people chode with Moses, and spake, saying, Would God that we had died when our brethren died before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>!  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And why have ye brought up the congregation of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> into this wilderness, that we and our cattle should die there?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And wherefore have ye made us to come up out of Egypt, to bring us in unto this evil place? it _is_ no place of seed, or of figs, or of vines, or of pomegranates; neither _is_ there any water to drink.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron went from the presence of the assembly unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, and they fell upon their faces: and the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> appeared unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Take the rod, and gather thou the assembly together, thou, and Aaron thy brother, and speak ye unto the rock before their eyes; and it shall give forth his water, and thou shalt bring forth to them water out of the rock: so thou shalt give the congregation and their beasts drink.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Moses took the rod from before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as he commanded him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron gathered the congregation together before the rock, and he said unto them, Hear now, ye rebels; must we fetch you water out of this rock?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Moses lifted up his hand, and with his rod he smote the rock twice: and the water came out abundantly, and the congregation drank, and their beasts _also_.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and Aaron, Because ye believed me not, to sanctify me in the eyes of the children of Israel, therefore ye shall not bring this congregation into the land which I have given them.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>This _is_ the water of Meribah; because the children of Israel strove with the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and he was sanctified in them.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses sent messengers from Kadesh unto the king of Edom, Thus saith thy brother Israel, Thou knowest all the travail that hath befallen us:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>How our fathers went down into Egypt, and we have dwelt in Egypt a long time; and the Egyptians vexed us, and our fathers:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And when we cried unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, he heard our voice, and sent an angel, and hath brought us forth out of Egypt: and, behold, we _are_ in Kadesh, a city in the uttermost of thy border:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Let us pass, I pray thee, through thy country: we will not pass through the fields, or through the vineyards, neither will we drink _of_ the water of the wells: we will go by the king’s _high_ way, we will not turn to the right hand nor to the left, until we have passed thy borders.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Edom said unto him, Thou shalt not pass by me, lest I come out against thee with the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel said unto him, We will go by the high way: and if I and my cattle drink of thy water, then I will pay for it: I will only, without _doing_ any thing _else_, go through on my feet.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And he said, Thou shalt not go through. And Edom came out against him with much people, and with a strong hand.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Thus Edom refused to give Israel passage through his border: wherefore Israel turned away from him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And the children of Israel, _even_ the whole congregation, journeyed from Kadesh, and came unto mount Hor.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and Aaron in mount Hor, by the coast of the land of Edom, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Aaron shall be gathered unto his people: for he shall not enter into the land which I have given unto the children of Israel, because ye rebelled against my word at the water of Meribah.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Take Aaron and Eleazar his son, and bring them up unto mount Hor:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And strip Aaron of his garments, and put them upon Eleazar his son: and Aaron shall be gathered _unto his people_, and shall die there.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And Moses did as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded: and they went up into mount Hor in the sight of all the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And Moses stripped Aaron of his garments, and put them upon Eleazar his son; and Aaron died there in the top of the mount: and Moses and Eleazar came down from the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And when all the congregation saw that Aaron was dead, they mourned for Aaron thirty days, _even_ all the house of Israel.  

## CHAPTER 21.

And _when_ king Arad the Canaanite, which dwelt in the south, heard tell that Israel came by the way of the spies; then he fought against Israel, and took _some_ of them prisoners.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Israel vowed a vow unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, If thou wilt indeed deliver this people into my hand, then I will utterly destroy their cities.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hearkened to the voice of Israel, and delivered up the Canaanites; and they utterly destroyed them and their cities: and he called the name of the place Hormah.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And they journeyed from mount Hor by the way of the Red sea, to compass the land of Edom: and the soul of the people was much discouraged because of the way.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the people spake against God, and against Moses, Wherefore have ye brought us up out of Egypt to die in the wilderness? for _there is_ no bread, neither _is there any_ water; and our soul loatheth this light bread.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> sent fiery serpents among the people, and they bit the people; and much people of Israel died.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore the people came to Moses, and said, We have sinned, for we have spoken against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and against thee; pray unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that he take away the serpents from us. And Moses prayed for the people.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Make thee a fiery serpent, and set it upon a pole: and it shall come to pass, that every one that is bitten, when he looketh upon it, shall live.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Moses made a serpent of brass, and put it upon a pole, and it came to pass, that if a serpent had bitten any man, when he beheld the serpent of brass, he lived.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the children of Israel set forward, and pitched in Oboth.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And they journeyed from Oboth, and pitched at Ije–abarim, in the wilderness which _is_ before Moab, toward the sunrising.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ From thence they removed, and pitched in the valley of Zared.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>From thence they removed, and pitched on the other side of Arnon, which _is_ in the wilderness that cometh out of the coasts of the Amorites: for Arnon _is_ the border of Moab, between Moab and the Amorites.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Wherefore it is said in the book of the wars of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, What he did in the Red sea, and in the brooks of Arnon,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And at the stream of the brooks that goeth down to the dwelling of Ar, and lieth upon the border of Moab.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And from thence _they went_ to Beer: that _is_ the well whereof the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, Gather the people together, and I will give them water.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Then Israel sang this song, Spring up, O well; sing ye unto it:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The princes digged the well, the nobles of the people digged it, by _the direction of_ the lawgiver, with their staves. And from the wilderness _they went_ to Mattanah:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And from Mattanah to Nahaliel: and from Nahaliel to Bamoth:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And from Bamoth _in_ the valley, that _is_ in the country of Moab, to the top of Pisgah, which looketh toward Jeshimon.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And Israel sent messengers unto Sihon king of the Amorites, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Let me pass through thy land: we will not turn into the fields, or into the vineyards; we will not drink _of_ the waters of the well: _but_ we will go along by the king’s _high_ way, until we be past thy borders.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And Sihon would not suffer Israel to pass through his border: but Sihon gathered all his people together, and went out against Israel into the wilderness: and he came to Jahaz, and fought against Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And Israel smote him with the edge of the sword, and possessed his land from Arnon unto Jabbok, even unto the children of Ammon: for the border of the children of Ammon _was_ strong.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And Israel took all these cities: and Israel dwelt in all the cities of the Amorites, in Heshbon, and in all the villages thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For Heshbon _was_ the city of Sihon the king of the Amorites, who had fought against the former king of Moab, and taken all his land out of his hand, even unto Arnon.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Wherefore they that speak in proverbs say, Come into Heshbon, let the city of Sihon be built and prepared:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For there is a fire gone out of Heshbon, a flame from the city of Sihon: it hath consumed Ar of Moab, _and_ the lords of the high places of Arnon.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Woe to thee, Moab! thou art undone, O people of Chemosh: he hath given his sons that escaped, and his daughters, into captivity unto Sihon king of the Amorites.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>We have shot at them; Heshbon is perished even unto Dibon, and we have laid them waste even unto Nophah, which _reacheth_ unto Medeba.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ Thus Israel dwelt in the land of the Amorites.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And Moses sent to spy out Jaazer, and they took the villages thereof, and drove out the Amorites that _were_ there.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ And they turned and went up by the way of Bashan: and Og the king of Bashan went out against them, he, and all his people, to the battle at Edrei.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Fear him not: for I have delivered him into thy hand, and all his people, and his land; and thou shalt do to him as thou didst unto Sihon king of the Amorites, which dwelt at Heshbon.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>So they smote him, and his sons, and all his people, until there was none left him alive: and they possessed his land.  

## CHAPTER 22.

And the children of Israel set forward, and pitched in the plains of Moab on this side Jordan _by_ Jericho.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ And Balak the son of Zippor saw all that Israel had done to the Amorites.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Moab was sore afraid of the people, because they _were_ many: and Moab was distressed because of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Moab said unto the elders of Midian, Now shall this company lick up all _that are_ round about us, as the ox licketh up the grass of the field. And Balak the son of Zippor _was_ king of the Moabites at that time.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>He sent messengers therefore unto Balaam the son of Beor to Pethor, which _is_ by the river of the land of the children of his people, to call him, saying, Behold, there is a people come out from Egypt: behold, they cover the face of the earth, and they abide over against me:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Come now therefore, I pray thee, curse me this people; for they _are_ too mighty for me: peradventure I shall prevail, _that_ we may smite them, and _that_ I may drive them out of the land: for I wot that he whom thou blessest _is_ blessed, and he whom thou cursest is cursed.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the elders of Moab and the elders of Midian departed with the rewards of divination in their hand; and they came unto Balaam, and spake unto him the words of Balak.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Lodge here this night, and I will bring you word again, as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall speak unto me: and the princes of Moab abode with Balaam.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And God came unto Balaam, and said, What men _are_ these with thee?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Balaam said unto God, Balak the son of Zippor, king of Moab, hath sent unto me, _saying_,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Behold, _there is_ a people come out of Egypt, which covereth the face of the earth: come now, curse me them; peradventure I shall be able to overcome them, and drive them out.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And God said unto Balaam, Thou shalt not go with them; thou shalt not curse the people: for they _are_ blessed.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Balaam rose up in the morning, and said unto the princes of Balak, Get you into your land: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> refuseth to give me leave to go with you.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the princes of Moab rose up, and they went unto Balak, and said, Balaam refuseth to come with us.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And Balak sent yet again princes, more, and more honourable than they.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And they came to Balaam, and said to him, Thus saith Balak the son of Zippor, Let nothing, I pray thee, hinder thee from coming unto me:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For I will promote thee unto very great honour, and I will do whatsoever thou sayest unto me: come therefore, I pray thee, curse me this people.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Balaam answered and said unto the servants of Balak, If Balak would give me his house full of silver and gold, I cannot go beyond the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> my God, to do less or more.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Now therefore, I pray you, tarry ye also here this night, that I may know what the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will say unto me more.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And God came unto Balaam at night, and said unto him, If the men come to call thee, rise up, _and_ go with them; but yet the word which I shall say unto thee, that shalt thou do.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Balaam rose up in the morning, and saddled his ass, and went with the princes of Moab.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And God’s anger was kindled because he went: and the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> stood in the way for an adversary against him. Now he was riding upon his ass, and his two servants _were_ with him.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the ass saw the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> standing in the way, and his sword drawn in his hand: and the ass turned aside out of the way, and went into the field: and Balaam smote the ass, to turn her into the way.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>But the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> stood in a path of the vineyards, a wall _being_ on this side, and a wall on that side.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And when the ass saw the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, she thrust herself unto the wall, and crushed Balaam’s foot against the wall: and he smote her again.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> went further, and stood in a narrow place, where _was_ no way to turn either to the right hand or to the left.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And when the ass saw the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, she fell down under Balaam: and Balaam’s anger was kindled, and he smote the ass with a staff.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> opened the mouth of the ass, and she said unto Balaam, What have I done unto thee, that thou hast smitten me these three times?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And Balaam said unto the ass, Because thou hast mocked me: I would there were a sword in mine hand, for now would I kill thee.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the ass said unto Balaam, _Am_ not I thine ass, upon which thou hast ridden ever since _I was_ thine unto this day? was I ever wont to do so unto thee? And he said, Nay.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Then the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> opened the eyes of Balaam, and he saw the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> standing in the way, and his sword drawn in his hand: and he bowed down his head, and fell flat on his face.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto him, Wherefore hast thou smitten thine ass these three times? behold, I went out to withstand thee, because _thy_ way is perverse before me:  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And the ass saw me, and turned from me these three times: unless she had turned from me, surely now also I had slain thee, and saved her alive.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And Balaam said unto the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I have sinned; for I knew not that thou stoodest in the way against me: now therefore, if it displease thee, I will get me back again.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Balaam, Go with the men: but only the word that I shall speak unto thee, that thou shalt speak. So Balaam went with the princes of Balak.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ And when Balak heard that Balaam was come, he went out to meet him unto a city of Moab, which _is_ in the border of Arnon, which _is_ in the utmost coast.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And Balak said unto Balaam, Did I not earnestly send unto thee to call thee? wherefore camest thou not unto me? am I not able indeed to promote thee to honour?  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And Balaam said unto Balak, Lo, I am come unto thee: have I now any power at all to say any thing? the word that God putteth in my mouth, that shall I speak.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And Balaam went with Balak, and they came unto Kirjath–huzoth.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And Balak offered oxen and sheep, and sent to Balaam, and to the princes that _were_ with him.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And it came to pass on the morrow, that Balak took Balaam, and brought him up into the high places of Baal, that thence he might see the utmost _part_ of the people.  

## CHAPTER 23.

And Balaam said unto Balak, Build me here seven altars, and prepare me here seven oxen and seven rams.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Balak did as Balaam had spoken; and Balak and Balaam offered on _every_ altar a bullock and a ram.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Balaam said unto Balak, Stand by thy burnt offering, and I will go: peradventure the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will come to meet me: and whatsoever he sheweth me I will tell thee. And he went to an high place.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And God met Balaam: and he said unto him, I have prepared seven altars, and I have offered upon _every_ altar a bullock and a ram.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> put a word in Balaam’s mouth, and said, Return unto Balak, and thus thou shalt speak.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he returned unto him, and, lo, he stood by his burnt sacrifice, he, and all the princes of Moab.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he took up his parable, and said, Balak the king of Moab hath brought me from Aram, out of the mountains of the east, _saying_, Come, curse me Jacob, and come, defy Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>How shall I curse, whom God hath not cursed? or how shall I defy, _whom_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath not defied?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For from the top of the rocks I see him, and from the hills I behold him: lo, the people shall dwell alone, and shall not be reckoned among the nations.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Who can count the dust of Jacob, and the number of the fourth _part_ of Israel? Let me die the death of the righteous, and let my last end be like his!  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Balak said unto Balaam, What hast thou done unto me? I took thee to curse mine enemies, and, behold, thou hast blessed _them_ altogether.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he answered and said, Must I not take heed to speak that which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath put in my mouth?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And Balak said unto him, Come, I pray thee, with me unto another place, from whence thou mayest see them: thou shalt see but the utmost part of them, and shalt not see them all: and curse me them from thence.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And he brought him into the field of Zophim, to the top of Pisgah, and built seven altars, and offered a bullock and a ram on _every_ altar.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he said unto Balak, Stand here by thy burnt offering, while I meet _the Lord_ yonder.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> met Balaam, and put a word in his mouth, and said, Go again unto Balak, and say thus.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And when he came to him, behold, he stood by his burnt offering, and the princes of Moab with him. And Balak said unto him, What hath the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spoken?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And he took up his parable, and said, Rise up, Balak, and hear; hearken unto me, thou son of Zippor:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>God _is_ not a man, that he should lie; neither the son of man, that he should repent: hath he said, and shall he not do _it_? or hath he spoken, and shall he not make it good?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Behold, I have received _commandment_ to bless: and he hath blessed; and I cannot reverse it.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>He hath not beheld iniquity in Jacob, neither hath he seen perverseness in Israel: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> his God _is_ with him, and the shout of a king _is_ among them.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>God brought them out of Egypt; he hath as it were the strength of an unicorn.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Surely _there is_ no enchantment against Jacob, neither _is there_ any divination against Israel: according to this time it shall be said of Jacob and of Israel, What hath God wrought!  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Behold, the people shall rise up as a great lion, and lift up himself as a young lion: he shall not lie down until he eat _of_ the prey, and drink the blood of the slain.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And Balak said unto Balaam, Neither curse them at all, nor bless them at all.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But Balaam answered and said unto Balak, Told not I thee, saying, All that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> speaketh, that I must do?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And Balak said unto Balaam, Come, I pray thee, I will bring thee unto another place; peradventure it will please God that thou mayest curse me them from thence.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And Balak brought Balaam unto the top of Peor, that looketh toward Jeshimon.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And Balaam said unto Balak, Build me here seven altars, and prepare me here seven bullocks and seven rams.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Balak did as Balaam had said, and offered a bullock and a ram on _every_ altar.  

## CHAPTER 24.

And when Balaam saw that it pleased the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to bless Israel, he went not, as at other times, to seek for enchantments, but he set his face toward the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Balaam lifted up his eyes, and he saw Israel abiding _in his tents_ according to their tribes; and the spirit of God came upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he took up his parable, and said, Balaam the son of Beor hath said, and the man whose eyes are open hath said:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>He hath said, which heard the words of God, which saw the vision of the Almighty, falling _into a trance_, but having his eyes open:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>How goodly are thy tents, O Jacob, _and_ thy tabernacles, O Israel!  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>As the valleys are they spread forth, as gardens by the river’s side, as the trees of lign aloes which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath planted, _and_ as cedar trees beside the waters.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>He shall pour the water out of his buckets, and his seed _shall be_ in many waters, and his king shall be higher than Agag, and his kingdom shall be exalted.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>God brought him forth out of Egypt; he hath as it were the strength of an unicorn: he shall eat up the nations his enemies, and shall break their bones, and pierce _them_ through with his arrows.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>He couched, he lay down as a lion, and as a great lion: who shall stir him up? Blessed _is_ he that blesseth thee, and cursed _is_ he that curseth thee.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And Balak’s anger was kindled against Balaam, and he smote his hands together: and Balak said unto Balaam, I called thee to curse mine enemies, and, behold, thou hast altogether blessed _them_ these three times.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Therefore now flee thou to thy place: I thought to promote thee unto great honour; but, lo, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath kept thee back from honour.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Balaam said unto Balak, Spake I not also to thy messengers which thou sentest unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>If Balak would give me his house full of silver and gold, I cannot go beyond the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to do _either_ good or bad of mine own mind; _but_ what the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> saith, that will I speak?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And now, behold, I go unto my people: come _therefore, and_ I will advertise thee what this people shall do to thy people in the latter days.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And he took up his parable, and said, Balaam the son of Beor hath said, and the man whose eyes are open hath said:

<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Why should the name of our father be done away from among his family, because he hath no son? Give unto us _therefore_ a possession among the brethren of our father.

<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And if his father have no brethren, then ye shall give his inheritance unto his kinsman that is next to him of his family, and he shall possess it: and it shall be unto the children of Israel a statute of judgment, as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Get thee up into this mount Abarim, and see the land which I have given unto the children of Israel.

## CHAPTER 30.

And Moses spake unto the heads of the tribes concerning the children of Israel, saying, This _is_ the thing which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath commanded.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>If a man vow a vow unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, or swear an oath to bind his soul with a bond; he shall not break his word, he shall do according to all that proceedeth out of his mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ If a woman also vow a vow unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and bind herself by a bond, _being_ in her father’s house in her youth;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And her father hear her vow, and her bond wherewith she hath bound her soul, and her father shall hold his peace at her: then all her vows shall stand, and every bond wherewith she hath bound her soul shall stand.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But if her father disallow her in the day that he heareth; not any of her vows, or of her bonds wherewith she hath bound her soul, shall stand: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall forgive her, because her father disallowed her.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And if she had at all an husband, when she vowed, or uttered ought out of her lips, wherewith she bound her soul;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And her husband heard _it_, and held his peace at her in the day that he heard _it_: then her vows shall stand, and her bonds wherewith she bound her soul shall stand.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But if her husband disallowed her on the day that he heard _it_; then he shall make her vow which she vowed, and that which she uttered with her lips, wherewith she bound her soul, of none effect: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall forgive her.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But every vow of a widow, and of her that is divorced, wherewith they have bound their souls, shall stand against her.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And if she vowed in her husband’s house, or bound her soul by a bond with an oath;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And her husband heard _it_, and held his peace at her, _and_ disallowed her not: then all her vows shall stand, and every bond wherewith she bound her soul shall stand.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But if her husband hath utterly made them void on the day he heard _them; then_ whatsoever proceeded out of her lips concerning her vows, or concerning the bond of her soul, shall not stand: her husband hath made them void; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall forgive her.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Every vow, and every binding oath to afflict the soul, her husband may establish it, or her husband may make it void.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But if her husband altogether hold his peace at her from day to day; then he establisheth all her vows, or all her bonds, which _are_ upon her: he confirmeth them, because he held his peace at her in the day that he heard _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But if he shall any ways make them void after that he hath heard _them_; then he shall bear her iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>These _are_ the statutes, which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses, between a man and his wife, between the father and his daughter, _being yet_ in her youth in her father’s house.  

## CHAPTER 31.

<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Moses sent them to the war, a thousand of _every_ tribe, them and Phinehas the son of Eleazar the priest, to the war, with the holy instruments, and the trumpets to blow in his hand.

<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And they brought the captives, and the prey, and the spoil, unto Moses, and Eleazar the priest, and unto the congregation of the children of Israel, unto the camp at the plains of Moab, which _are_ by Jordan _near_ Jericho.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses, and Eleazar the priest, and all the princes of the congregation, went forth to meet them without the camp.

<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto them, Have ye saved all the women alive?

<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And threescore and twelve thousand beeves,  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And threescore and one thousand asses,

## CHAPTER 32.

Now the children of Reuben and the children of Gad had a very great multitude of cattle: and when they saw the land of Jazer, and the land of Gilead, that, behold, the place _was_ a place for cattle;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The children of Gad and the children of Reuben came and spake unto Moses, and to Eleazar the priest, and unto the princes of the congregation, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Ataroth, and Dibon, and Jazer, and Nimrah, and Heshbon, and Elealeh, and Shebam, and Nebo, and Beon,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>_Even_ the country which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> smote before the congregation of Israel, _is_ a land for cattle, and thy servants have cattle:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Wherefore, said they, if we have found grace in thy sight, let this land be given unto thy servants for a possession, _and_ bring us not over Jordan.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto the children of Gad and to the children of Reuben, Shall your brethren go to war, and shall ye sit here?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And wherefore discourage ye the heart of the children of Israel from going over into the land which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath given them?

<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For when they went up unto the valley of Eshcol, and saw the land, they discouraged the heart of the children of Israel, that they should not go into the land which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had given them.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> anger was kindled the same time, and he sware, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Surely none of the men that came up out of Egypt, from twenty years old and upward, shall see the land which I sware unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob; because they have not wholly followed me:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Save Caleb the son of Jephunneh the Kenezite, and Joshua the son of Nun: for they have wholly followed the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> anger was kindled against Israel, and he made them wander in the wilderness forty years, until all the generation, that had done evil in the sight of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, was consumed.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And, behold, ye are risen up in your fathers’ stead, an increase of sinful men, to augment yet the fierce anger of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> toward Israel.

<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But we ourselves will go ready armed before the children of Israel, until we have brought them unto their place: and our little ones shall dwell in the fenced cities because of the inhabitants of the land.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>We will not return unto our houses, until the children of Israel have inherited every man his inheritance.

<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto them, If ye will do this thing, if ye will go armed before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to war,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And will go all of you armed over Jordan before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, until he hath driven out his enemies from before him,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the land be subdued before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: then afterward ye shall return, and be guiltless before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and before Israel; and this land shall be your possession before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But if ye will not do so, behold, ye have sinned against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and be sure your sin will find you out.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Build you cities for your little ones, and folds for your sheep; and do that which hath proceeded out of your mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the children of Gad and the children of Reuben spake unto Moses, saying, Thy servants will do as my lord commandeth.

<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And Moses gave unto them, _even_ to the children of Gad, and to the children of Reuben, and unto half the tribe of Manasseh the son of Joseph, the kingdom of Sihon king of the Amorites, and the kingdom of Og king of Bashan, the land, with the cities thereof in the coasts, _even_ the cities of the country round about.

<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And Atroth, Shophan, and Jaazer, and Jogbehah,

<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And the children of Reuben built Heshbon, and Elealeh, and Kirjathaim,

<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And Moses gave Gilead unto Machir the son of Manasseh; and he dwelt therein.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And Jair the son of Manasseh went and took the small towns thereof, and called them Havoth–jair.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And Nobah went and took Kenath, and the villages thereof, and called it Nobah, after his own name.  

## CHAPTER 33.

These _are_ the journeys of the children of Israel, which went forth out of the land of Egypt with their armies under the hand of Moses and Aaron.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Moses wrote their goings out according to their journeys by the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and these _are_ their journeys according to their goings out.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And they departed from Rameses in the first month, on the fifteenth day of the first month; on the morrow after the passover the children of Israel went out with an high hand in the sight of all the Egyptians.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For the Egyptians buried all _their_ firstborn, which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had smitten among them: upon their gods also the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> executed judgments.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel removed from Rameses, and pitched in Succoth.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they departed from Succoth, and pitched in Etham, which _is_ in the edge of the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they removed from Etham, and turned again unto Pi–hahiroth, which _is_ before Baal–zephon: and they pitched before Migdol.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they departed from before Pi–hahiroth, and passed through the midst of the sea into the wilderness, and went three days’ journey in the wilderness of Etham, and pitched in Marah.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And they removed from Marah, and came unto Elim: and in Elim _were_ twelve fountains of water, and threescore and ten palm trees; and they pitched there.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they removed from Elim, and encamped by the Red sea.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And they removed from the Red sea, and encamped in the wilderness of Sin.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And they took their journey out of the wilderness of Sin, and encamped in Dophkah.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And they departed from Dophkah, and encamped in Alush.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they removed from Alush, and encamped at Rephidim, where was no water for the people to drink.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And they departed from Rephidim, and pitched in the wilderness of Sinai.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And they removed from the desert of Sinai, and pitched at Kibroth–hattaavah.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And they departed from Kibroth–hattaavah, and encamped at Hazeroth.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they departed from Hazeroth, and pitched in Rithmah.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And they departed from Rithmah, and pitched at Rimmon–parez.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And they departed from Rimmon–parez, and pitched in Libnah.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And they removed from Libnah, and pitched at Rissah.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And they journeyed from Rissah, and pitched in Kehelathah.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they went from Kehelathah, and pitched in mount Shapher.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they removed from mount Shapher, and encamped in Haradah.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And they removed from Haradah, and pitched in Makheloth.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And they removed from Makheloth, and encamped at Tahath.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And they departed from Tahath, and pitched at Tarah.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And they removed from Tarah, and pitched in Mithcah.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And they went from Mithcah, and pitched in Hashmonah.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And they departed from Hashmonah, and encamped at Moseroth.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And they departed from Moseroth, and pitched in Bene–jaakan.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And they removed from Bene–jaakan, and encamped at Hor–hagidgad.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And they went from Hor–hagidgad, and pitched in Jotbathah.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And they removed from Jotbathah, and encamped at Ebronah.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And they departed from Ebronah, and encamped at Ezion–gaber.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And they removed from Ezion–gaber, and pitched in the wilderness of Zin, which _is_ Kadesh.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And they removed from Kadesh, and pitched in mount Hor, in the edge of the land of Edom.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And Aaron the priest went up into mount Hor at the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and died there, in the fortieth year after the children of Israel were come out of the land of Egypt, in the first _day_ of the fifth month.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And Aaron _was_ an hundred and twenty and three years old when he died in mount Hor.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And king Arad the Canaanite, which dwelt in the south in the land of Canaan, heard of the coming of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And they departed from mount Hor, and pitched in Zalmonah.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And they departed from Zalmonah, and pitched in Punon.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And they departed from Punon, and pitched in Oboth.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And they departed from Oboth, and pitched in Ije–abarim, in the border of Moab.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And they departed from Iim, and pitched in Dibon–gad.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And they removed from Dibon–gad, and encamped in Almon–diblathaim.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And they removed from Almon–diblathaim, and pitched in the mountains of Abarim, before Nebo.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And they departed from the mountains of Abarim, and pitched in the plains of Moab by Jordan _near_ Jericho.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>And they pitched by Jordan, from Beth–jesimoth _even_ unto Abel–shittim in the plains of Moab.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses in the plains of Moab by Jordan _near_ Jericho, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, When ye are passed over Jordan into the land of Canaan;  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>Then ye shall drive out all the inhabitants of the land from before you, and destroy all their pictures, and destroy all their molten images, and quite pluck down all their high places:  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>And ye shall dispossess _the inhabitants_ of the land, and dwell therein: for I have given you the land to possess it.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>And ye shall divide the land by lot for an inheritance among your families: _and_ to the more ye shall give the more inheritance, and to the fewer ye shall give the less inheritance: every man’s _inheritance_ shall be in the place where his lot falleth; according to the tribes of your fathers ye shall inherit.  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>But if ye will not drive out the inhabitants of the land from before you; then it shall come to pass, that those which ye let remain of them _shall be_ pricks in your eyes, and thorns in your sides, and shall vex you in the land wherein ye dwell.  
<span class="verse-number">56&ensp;</span>Moreover it shall come to pass, _that_ I shall do unto you, as I thought to do unto them.  

## CHAPTER 34.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Command the children of Israel, and say unto them, When ye come into the land of Canaan; (this _is_ the land that shall fall unto you for an inheritance, _even_ the land of Canaan with the coasts thereof:)  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Then your south quarter shall be from the wilderness of Zin along by the coast of Edom, and your south border shall be the outmost coast of the salt sea eastward:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And your border shall turn from the south to the ascent of Akrabbim, and pass on to Zin: and the going forth thereof shall be from the south to Kadesh–barnea, and shall go on to Hazar–addar, and pass on to Azmon:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the border shall fetch a compass from Azmon unto the river of Egypt, and the goings out of it shall be at the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And _as for_ the western border, ye shall even have the great sea for a border: this shall be your west border.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And this shall be your north border: from the great sea ye shall point out for you mount Hor:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>From mount Hor ye shall point out _your border_ unto the entrance of Hamath; and the goings forth of the border shall be to Zedad:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And the border shall go on to Ziphron, and the goings out of it shall be at Hazar–enan: this shall be your north border.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And ye shall point out your east border from Hazar–enan to Shepham:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the coast shall go down from Shepham to Riblah, on the east side of Ain; and the border shall descend, and shall reach unto the side of the sea of Chinnereth eastward:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the border shall go down to Jordan, and the goings out of it shall be at the salt sea: this shall be your land with the coasts thereof round about.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Moses commanded the children of Israel, saying, This _is_ the land which ye shall inherit by lot, which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded to give unto the nine tribes, and to the half tribe:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For the tribe of the children of Reuben according to the house of their fathers, and the tribe of the children of Gad according to the house of their fathers, have received _their inheritance_; and half the tribe of Manasseh have received their inheritance:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The two tribes and the half tribe have received their inheritance on this side Jordan _near_ Jericho eastward, toward the sunrising.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>These _are_ the names of the men which shall divide the land unto you: Eleazar the priest, and Joshua the son of Nun.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And ye shall take one prince of every tribe, to divide the land by inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the names of the men _are_ these: Of the tribe of Judah, Caleb the son of Jephunneh.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And of the tribe of the children of Simeon, Shemuel the son of Ammihud.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Of the tribe of Benjamin, Elidad the son of Chislon.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the prince of the tribe of the children of Dan, Bukki the son of Jogli.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The prince of the children of Joseph, for the tribe of the children of Manasseh, Hanniel the son of Ephod.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the prince of the tribe of the children of Ephraim, Kemuel the son of Shiphtan.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the prince of the tribe of the children of Zebulun, Elizaphan the son of Parnach.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And the prince of the tribe of the children of Issachar, Paltiel the son of Azzan.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And the prince of the tribe of the children of Asher, Ahihud the son of Shelomi.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the prince of the tribe of the children of Naphtali, Pedahel the son of Ammihud.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>These _are they_ whom the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded to divide the inheritance unto the children of Israel in the land of Canaan.  

## CHAPTER 35.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses in the plains of Moab by Jordan _near_ Jericho, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Command the children of Israel, that they give unto the Levites of the inheritance of their possession cities to dwell in; and ye shall give _also_ unto the Levites suburbs for the cities round about them.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the cities shall they have to dwell in; and the suburbs of them shall be for their cattle, and for their goods, and for all their beasts.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the suburbs of the cities, which ye shall give unto the Levites, _shall reach_ from the wall of the city and outward a thousand cubits round about.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And ye shall measure from without the city on the east side two thousand cubits, and on the south side two thousand cubits, and on the west side two thousand cubits, and on the north side two thousand cubits; and the city _shall be_ in the midst: this shall be to them the suburbs of the cities.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And among the cities which ye shall give unto the Levites _there shall be_ six cities for refuge, which ye shall appoint for the manslayer, that he may flee thither: and to them ye shall add forty and two cities.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_So_ all the cities which ye shall give to the Levites _shall be_ forty and eight cities: them _shall ye give_ with their suburbs.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the cities which ye shall give _shall be_ of the possession of the children of Israel: from _them that have_ many ye shall give many; but from _them that have_ few ye shall give few: every one shall give of his cities unto the Levites according to his inheritance which he inheriteth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, When ye be come over Jordan into the land of Canaan;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then ye shall appoint you cities to be cities of refuge for you; that the slayer may flee thither, which killeth any person at unawares.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And they shall be unto you cities for refuge from the avenger; that the manslayer die not, until he stand before the congregation in judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And of these cities which ye shall give six cities shall ye have for refuge.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Ye shall give three cities on this side Jordan, and three cities shall ye give in the land of Canaan, _which_ shall be cities of refuge.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>These six cities shall be a refuge, _both_ for the children of Israel, and for the stranger, and for the sojourner among them: that every one that killeth any person unawares may flee thither.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And if he smite him with an instrument of iron, so that he die, he _is_ a murderer: the murderer shall surely be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And if he smite him with throwing a stone, wherewith he may die, and he die, he _is_ a murderer: the murderer shall surely be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Or _if_ he smite him with an hand weapon of wood, wherewith he may die, and he die, he _is_ a murderer: the murderer shall surely be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The revenger of blood himself shall slay the murderer: when he meeteth him, he shall slay him.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But if he thrust him of hatred, or hurl at him by laying of wait, that he die;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Or in enmity smite him with his hand, that he die: he that smote _him_ shall surely be put to death; _for_ he _is_ a murderer: the revenger of blood shall slay the murderer, when he meeteth him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But if he thrust him suddenly without enmity, or have cast upon him any thing without laying of wait,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Or with any stone, wherewith a man may die, seeing _him_ not, and cast _it_ upon him, that he die, and _was_ not his enemy, neither sought his harm:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Then the congregation shall judge between the slayer and the revenger of blood according to these judgments:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the congregation shall deliver the slayer out of the hand of the revenger of blood, and the congregation shall restore him to the city of his refuge, whither he was fled: and he shall abide in it unto the death of the high priest, which was anointed with the holy oil.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But if the slayer shall at any time come without the border of the city of his refuge, whither he was fled;  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And the revenger of blood find him without the borders of the city of his refuge, and the revenger of blood kill the slayer; he shall not be guilty of blood:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Because he should have remained in the city of his refuge until the death of the high priest: but after the death of the high priest the slayer shall return into the land of his possession.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>So these _things_ shall be for a statute of judgment unto you throughout your generations in all your dwellings.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Whoso killeth any person, the murderer shall be put to death by the mouth of witnesses: but one witness shall not testify against any person _to cause him_ to die.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Moreover ye shall take no satisfaction for the life of a murderer, which _is_ guilty of death: but he shall be surely put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And ye shall take no satisfaction for him that is fled to the city of his refuge, that he should come again to dwell in the land, until the death of the priest.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>So ye shall not pollute the land wherein ye _are_: for blood it defileth the land: and the land cannot be cleansed of the blood that is shed therein, but by the blood of him that shed it.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Defile not therefore the land which ye shall inhabit, wherein I dwell: for I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> dwell among the children of Israel.  

## CHAPTER 36.

And the chief fathers of the families of the children of Gilead, the son of Machir, the son of Manasseh, of the families of the sons of Joseph, came near, and spake before Moses, and before the princes, the chief fathers of the children of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And they said, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded my lord to give the land for an inheritance by lot to the children of Israel: and my lord was commanded by the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to give the inheritance of Zelophehad our brother unto his daughters.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And if they be married to any of the sons of the _other_ tribes of the children of Israel, then shall their inheritance be taken from the inheritance of our fathers, and shall be put to the inheritance of the tribe whereunto they are received: so shall it be taken from the lot of our inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And when the jubile of the children of Israel shall be, then shall their inheritance be put unto the inheritance of the tribe whereunto they are received: so shall their inheritance be taken away from the inheritance of the tribe of our fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses commanded the children of Israel according to the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, The tribe of the sons of Joseph hath said well.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>This _is_ the thing which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> doth command concerning the daughters of Zelophehad, saying, Let them marry to whom they think best; only to the family of the tribe of their father shall they marry.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>So shall not the inheritance of the children of Israel remove from tribe to tribe: for every one of the children of Israel shall keep himself to the inheritance of the tribe of his fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And every daughter, that possesseth an inheritance in any tribe of the children of Israel, shall be wife unto one of the family of the tribe of her father, that the children of Israel may enjoy every man the inheritance of his fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Neither shall the inheritance remove from _one_ tribe to another tribe; but every one of the tribes of the children of Israel shall keep himself to his own inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Even as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses, so did the daughters of Zelophehad:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For Mahlah, Tirzah, and Hoglah, and Milcah, and Noah, the daughters of Zelophehad, were married unto their father’s brothers’ sons:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>_And_ they were married into the families of the sons of Manasseh the son of Joseph, and their inheritance remained in the tribe of the family of their father.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>These _are_ the commandments and the judgments, which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded by the hand of Moses unto the children of Israel in the plains of Moab by Jordan _near_ Jericho.
