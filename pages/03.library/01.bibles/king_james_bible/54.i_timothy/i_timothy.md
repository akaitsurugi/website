# I Timothy

### THE FIRST EPISTLE OF PAUL THE APOSTLE TO TIMOTHY

## CHAPTER 1.

Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ by the commandment of God our Saviour, and Lord Jesus Christ, _which is_ our hope;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Unto Timothy, _my_ own son in the faith: Grace, mercy, _and_ peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ As I besought thee to abide still at Ephesus, when I went into Macedonia, that thou mightest charge some that they teach no other doctrine,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Neither give heed to fables and endless genealogies, which minister questions, rather than godly edifying which is in faith: _so do_.   
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Now the end of the commandment is charity out of a pure heart, and _of_ a good conscience, and _of_ faith unfeigned:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>From which some having swerved have turned aside unto vain jangling;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Desiring to be teachers of the law; understanding neither what they say, nor whereof they affirm.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But we know that the law _is_ good, if a man use it lawfully;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Knowing this, that the law is not made for a righteous man, but for the lawless and disobedient, for the ungodly and for sinners, for unholy and profane, for murderers of fathers and murderers of mothers, for manslayers,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For whoremongers, for them that defile themselves with mankind, for menstealers, for liars, for perjured persons, and if there be any other thing that is contrary to sound doctrine;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>According to the glorious gospel of the blessed God, which was committed to my trust.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And I thank Christ Jesus our Lord, who hath enabled me, for that he counted me faithful, putting me into the ministry;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Who was before a blasphemer, and a persecutor, and injurious: but I obtained mercy, because I did _it_ ignorantly in unbelief.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the grace of our Lord was exceeding abundant with faith and love which is in Christ Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>This _is_ a faithful saying, and worthy of all acceptation, that Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners; of whom I am chief.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Howbeit for this cause I obtained mercy, that in me first Jesus Christ might shew forth all longsuffering, for a pattern to them which should hereafter believe on him to life everlasting.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Now unto the King eternal, immortal, invisible, the only wise God, _be_ honour and glory for ever and ever. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>This charge I commit unto thee, son Timothy, according to the prophecies which went before on thee, that thou by them mightest war a good warfare;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Holding faith, and a good conscience; which some having put away concerning faith have made shipwreck:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Of whom is Hymenaeus and Alexander; whom I have delivered unto Satan, that they may learn not to blaspheme.  

## CHAPTER 2.

I exhort therefore, that, first of all, supplications, prayers, intercessions, _and_ giving of thanks, be made for all men;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For kings, and _for_ all that are in authority; that we may lead a quiet and peaceable life in all godliness and honesty.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For this _is_ good and acceptable in the sight of God our Saviour;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who will have all men to be saved, and to come unto the knowledge of the truth.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For _there is_ one God, and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Who gave himself a ransom for all, to be testified in due time.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Whereunto I am ordained a preacher, and an apostle, (I speak the truth in Christ, _and_ lie not;) a teacher of the Gentiles in faith and verity.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>I will therefore that men pray every where, lifting up holy hands, without wrath and doubting.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>In like manner also, that women adorn themselves in modest apparel, with shamefacedness and sobriety; not with broided hair, or gold, or pearls, or costly array;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But (which becometh women professing godliness) with good works.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Let the woman learn in silence with all subjection.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But I suffer not a woman to teach, nor to usurp authority over the man, but to be in silence.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For Adam was first formed, then Eve.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And Adam was not deceived, but the woman being deceived was in the transgression.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Notwithstanding she shall be saved in childbearing, if they continue in faith and charity and holiness with sobriety.  

## CHAPTER 3.

This _is_ a true saying, If a man desire the office of a bishop, he desireth a good work.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>A bishop then must be blameless, the husband of one wife, vigilant, sober, of good behaviour, given to hospitality, apt to teach;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Not given to wine, no striker, not greedy of filthy lucre; but patient, not a brawler, not covetous;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>One that ruleth well his own house, having his children in subjection with all gravity;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>(For if a man know not how to rule his own house, how shall he take care of the church of God?)  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Not a novice, lest being lifted up with pride he fall into the condemnation of the devil.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Moreover he must have a good report of them which are without; lest he fall into reproach and the snare of the devil.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Likewise _must_ the deacons _be_ grave, not doubletongued, not given to much wine, not greedy of filthy lucre;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Holding the mystery of the faith in a pure conscience.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And let these also first be proved; then let them use the office of a deacon, being _found_ blameless.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Even so _must their_ wives _be_ grave, not slanderers, sober, faithful in all things.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Let the deacons be the husbands of one wife, ruling their children and their own houses well.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For they that have used the office of a deacon well purchase to themselves a good degree, and great boldness in the faith which is in Christ Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>These things write I unto thee, hoping to come unto thee shortly:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But if I tarry long, that thou mayest know how thou oughtest to behave thyself in the house of God, which is the church of the living God, the pillar and ground of the truth.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And without controversy great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh, justified in the Spirit, seen of angels, preached unto the Gentiles, believed on in the world, received up into glory.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Now the Spirit speaketh expressly, that in the latter times some shall depart from the faith, giving heed to seducing spirits, and doctrines of devils;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Speaking lies in hypocrisy; having their conscience seared with a hot iron;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Forbidding to marry, _and commanding_ to abstain from meats, which God hath created to be received with thanksgiving of them which believe and know the truth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For every creature of God _is_ good, and nothing to be refused, if it be received with thanksgiving:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For it is sanctified by the word of God and prayer.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>If thou put the brethren in remembrance of these things, thou shalt be a good minister of Jesus Christ, nourished up in the words of faith and of good doctrine, whereunto thou hast attained.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But refuse profane and old wives’ fables, and exercise thyself _rather_ unto godliness.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For bodily exercise profiteth little: but godliness is profitable unto all things, having promise of the life that now is, and of that which is to come.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>This _is_ a faithful saying and worthy of all acceptation.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For therefore we both labour and suffer reproach, because we trust in the living God, who is the Saviour of all men, specially of those that believe.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ These things command and teach.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Let no man despise thy youth; but be thou an example of the believers, in word, in conversation, in charity, in spirit, in faith, in purity.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Till I come, give attendance to reading, to exhortation, to doctrine.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Neglect not the gift that is in thee, which was given thee by prophecy, with the laying on of the hands of the presbytery.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Meditate upon these things; give thyself wholly to them; that thy profiting may appear to all.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Take heed unto thyself, and unto the doctrine; continue in them: for in doing this thou shalt both save thyself, and them that hear thee.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Rebuke not an elder, but intreat _him_ as a father; _and_ the younger men as brethren;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The elder women as mothers; the younger as sisters, with all purity.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Honour widows that are widows indeed.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But if any widow have children or nephews, let them learn first to shew piety at home, and to requite their parents: for that is good and acceptable before God.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Now she that is a widow indeed, and desolate, trusteth in God, and continueth in supplications and prayers night and day.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But she that liveth in pleasure is dead while she liveth.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And these things give in charge, that they may be blameless.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But if any provide not for his own, and specially for those of his own house, he hath denied the faith, and is worse than an infidel.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Let not a widow be taken into the number under threescore years old, having been the wife of one man,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Well reported of for good works; if she have brought up children, if she have lodged strangers, if she have washed the saints’ feet, if she have relieved the afflicted, if she have diligently followed every good work.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But the younger widows refuse: for when they have begun to wax wanton against Christ, they will marry;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Having damnation, because they have cast off their first faith.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And withal they learn _to be_ idle, wandering about from house to house; and not only idle, but tattlers also and busybodies, speaking things which they ought not.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I will therefore that the younger women marry, bear children, guide the house, give none occasion to the adversary to speak reproachfully.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For some are already turned aside after Satan.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>If any man or woman that believeth have widows, let them relieve them, and let not the church be charged; that it may relieve them that are widows indeed.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Let the elders that rule well be counted worthy of double honour, especially they who labour in the word and doctrine.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For the scripture saith, Thou shalt not muzzle the ox that treadeth out the corn. And, The labourer _is_ worthy of his reward.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Against an elder receive not an accusation, but before two or three witnesses.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Them that sin rebuke before all, that others also may fear.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>I charge _thee_ before God, and the Lord Jesus Christ, and the elect angels, that thou observe these things without preferring one before another, doing nothing by partiality.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Lay hands suddenly on no man, neither be partaker of other men’s sins: keep thyself pure.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Drink no longer water, but use a little wine for thy stomach’s sake and thine often infirmities.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Some men’s sins are open beforehand, going before to judgment; and some _men_ they follow after.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Likewise also the good works _of some_ are manifest beforehand; and they that are otherwise cannot be hid.  

## CHAPTER 6.

Let as many servants as are under the yoke count their own masters worthy of all honour, that the name of God and _his_ doctrine be not blasphemed.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And they that have believing masters, let them not despise _them_, because they are brethren; but rather do _them_ service, because they are faithful and beloved, partakers of the benefit. These things teach and exhort.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ If any man teach otherwise, and consent not to wholesome words, _even_ the words of our Lord Jesus Christ, and to the doctrine which is according to godliness;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>He is proud, knowing nothing, but doting about questions and strifes of words, whereof cometh envy, strife, railings, evil surmisings,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Perverse disputings of men of corrupt minds, and destitute of the truth, supposing that gain is godliness: from such withdraw thyself.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But godliness with contentment is great gain.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For we brought nothing into _this_ world, _and it is_ certain we can carry nothing out.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And having food and raiment let us be therewith content.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But they that will be rich fall into temptation and a snare, and _into_ many foolish and hurtful lusts, which drown men in destruction and perdition.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For the love of money is the root of all evil: which while some coveted after, they have erred from the faith, and pierced themselves through with many sorrows.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But thou, O man of God, flee these things; and follow after righteousness, godliness, faith, love, patience, meekness.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Fight the good fight of faith, lay hold on eternal life, whereunto thou art also called, and hast professed a good profession before many witnesses.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>I give thee charge in the sight of God, who quickeneth all things, and _before_ Christ Jesus, who before Pontius Pilate witnessed a good confession;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>That thou keep _this_ commandment without spot, unrebukeable, until the appearing of our Lord Jesus Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Which in his times he shall shew, _who is_ the blessed and only Potentate, the King of kings, and Lord of lords;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Who only hath immortality, dwelling in the light which no man can approach unto; whom no man hath seen, nor can see: to whom _be_ honour and power everlasting. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Charge them that are rich in this world, that they be not highminded, nor trust in uncertain riches, but in the living God, who giveth us richly all things to enjoy;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>That they do good, that they be rich in good works, ready to distribute, willing to communicate;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Laying up in store for themselves a good foundation against the time to come, that they may lay hold on eternal life.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>O Timothy, keep that which is committed to thy trust, avoiding profane _and_ vain babblings, and oppositions of science falsely so called:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Which some professing have erred concerning the faith. Grace _be_ with thee. Amen.  

<div class="colophon">The first to Timothy was written from Laodicea, which is the chiefest city of Phrygia Pacatiana.</div>
