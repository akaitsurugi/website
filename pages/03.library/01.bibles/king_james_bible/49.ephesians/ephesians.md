# Ephesians

### THE EPISTLE OF PAUL THE APOSTLE TO THE EPHESIANS

## CHAPTER 1.

Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ by the will of God, to the saints which are at Ephesus, and to the faithful in Christ Jesus:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Grace _be_ to you, and peace, from God our Father, and _from_ the Lord Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Blessed _be_ the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who hath blessed us with all spiritual blessings in heavenly _places_ in Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>According as he hath chosen us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and without blame before him in love:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Having predestinated us unto the adoption of children by Jesus Christ to himself, according to the good pleasure of his will,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>To the praise of the glory of his grace, wherein he hath made us accepted in the beloved.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>In whom we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins, according to the riches of his grace;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Wherein he hath abounded toward us in all wisdom and prudence;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Having made known unto us the mystery of his will, according to his good pleasure which he hath purposed in himself:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>That in the dispensation of the fulness of times he might gather together in one all things in Christ, both which are in heaven, and which are on earth; _even_ in him:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>In whom also we have obtained an inheritance, being predestinated according to the purpose of him who worketh all things after the counsel of his own will:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>That we should be to the praise of his glory, who first trusted in Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>In whom ye also _trusted_, after that ye heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation: in whom also after that ye believed, ye were sealed with that holy Spirit of promise,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Which is the earnest of our inheritance until the redemption of the purchased possession, unto the praise of his glory.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore I also, after I heard of your faith in the Lord Jesus, and love unto all the saints,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Cease not to give thanks for you, making mention of you in my prayers;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>That the God of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Father of glory, may give unto you the spirit of wisdom and revelation in the knowledge of him:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The eyes of your understanding being enlightened; that ye may know what is the hope of his calling, and what the riches of the glory of his inheritance in the saints,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And what _is_ the exceeding greatness of his power to us-ward who believe, according to the working of his mighty power,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Which he wrought in Christ, when he raised him from the dead, and set _him_ at his own right hand in the heavenly _places_,   
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Far above all principality, and power, and might, and dominion, and every name that is named, not only in this world, but also in that which is to come:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And hath put all _things_ under his feet, and gave him _to be_ the head over all _things_ to the church,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Which is his body, the fulness of him that filleth all in all.  

## CHAPTER 2.

And you _hath he quickened_, who were dead in trespasses and sins;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Wherein in time past ye walked according to the course of this world, according to the prince of the power of the air, the spirit that now worketh in the children of disobedience:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Among whom also we all had our conversation in times past in the lusts of our flesh, fulfilling the desires of the flesh and of the mind; and were by nature the children of wrath, even as others.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But God, who is rich in mercy, for his great love wherewith he loved us,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Even when we were dead in sins, hath quickened us together with Christ, (by grace ye are saved;)  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And hath raised _us_ up together, and made _us_ sit together in heavenly _places_ in Christ Jesus:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>That in the ages to come he might shew the exceeding riches of his grace in _his_ kindness toward us through Christ Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: _it is_ the gift of God:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Not of works, lest any man should boast.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus unto good works, which God hath before ordained that we should walk in them.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore remember, that ye _being_ in time past Gentiles in the flesh, who are called Uncircumcision by that which is called the Circumcision in the flesh made by hands;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>That at that time ye were without Christ, being aliens from the commonwealth of Israel, and strangers from the covenants of promise, having no hope, and without God in the world:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But now in Christ Jesus ye who sometimes were far off are made nigh by the blood of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For he is our peace, who hath made both one, and hath broken down the middle wall of partition _between us_;   
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Having abolished in his flesh the enmity, _even_ the law of commandments _contained_ in ordinances; for to make in himself of twain one new man, _so_ making peace;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And that he might reconcile both unto God in one body by the cross, having slain the enmity thereby:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And came and preached peace to you which were afar off, and to them that were nigh.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For through him we both have access by one Spirit unto the Father.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Now therefore ye are no more strangers and foreigners, but fellowcitizens with the saints, and of the household of God;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And are built upon the foundation of the apostles and prophets, Jesus Christ himself being the chief corner _stone_;   
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>In whom all the building fitly framed together groweth unto an holy temple in the Lord:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>In whom ye also are builded together for an habitation of God through the Spirit.  

## CHAPTER 3.

For this cause I Paul, the prisoner of Jesus Christ for you Gentiles,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>If ye have heard of the dispensation of the grace of God which is given me to you-ward:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>How that by revelation he made known unto me the mystery; (as I wrote afore in few words,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Whereby, when ye read, ye may understand my knowledge in the mystery of Christ)  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Which in other ages was not made known unto the sons of men, as it is now revealed unto his holy apostles and prophets by the Spirit;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>That the Gentiles should be fellowheirs, and of the same body, and partakers of his promise in Christ by the gospel:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Whereof I was made a minister, according to the gift of the grace of God given unto me by the effectual working of his power.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Unto me, who am less than the least of all saints, is this grace given, that I should preach among the Gentiles the unsearchable riches of Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And to make all _men_ see what _is_ the fellowship of the mystery, which from the beginning of the world hath been hid in God, who created all things by Jesus Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>To the intent that now unto the principalities and powers in heavenly _places_ might be known by the church the manifold wisdom of God,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>According to the eternal purpose which he purposed in Christ Jesus our Lord:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>In whom we have boldness and access with confidence by the faith of him.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Wherefore I desire that ye faint not at my tribulations for you, which is your glory.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For this cause I bow my knees unto the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Of whom the whole family in heaven and earth is named,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>That he would grant you, according to the riches of his glory, to be strengthened with might by his Spirit in the inner man;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>That Christ may dwell in your hearts by faith; that ye, being rooted and grounded in love,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>May be able to comprehend with all saints what _is_ the breadth, and length, and depth, and height;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And to know the love of Christ, which passeth knowledge, that ye might be filled with all the fulness of God.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Now unto him that is able to do exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that worketh in us,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Unto him _be_ glory in the church by Christ Jesus throughout all ages, world without end. Amen.  

## CHAPTER 4.

I therefore, the prisoner of the Lord, beseech you that ye walk worthy of the vocation wherewith ye are called,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>With all lowliness and meekness, with longsuffering, forbearing one another in love;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Endeavouring to keep the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>_There is_ one body, and one Spirit, even as ye are called in one hope of your calling;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>One Lord, one faith, one baptism,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>One God and Father of all, who _is_ above all, and through all, and in you all.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But unto every one of us is given grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Wherefore he saith, When he ascended up on high, he led captivity captive, and gave gifts unto men.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>(Now that he ascended, what is it but that he also descended first into the lower parts of the earth?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He that descended is the same also that ascended up far above all heavens, that he might fill all things.)  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And he gave some, apostles; and some, prophets; and some, evangelists; and some, pastors and teachers;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the perfecting of the saints, for the work of the ministry, for the edifying of the body of Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Till we all come in the unity of the faith, and of the knowledge of the Son of God, unto a perfect man, unto the measure of the stature of the fulness of Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>That we _henceforth_ be no more children, tossed to and fro, and carried about with every wind of doctrine, by the sleight of men, _and_ cunning craftiness, whereby they lie in wait to deceive;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But speaking the truth in love, may grow up into him in all things, which is the head, _even_ Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>From whom the whole body fitly joined together and compacted by that which every joint supplieth, according to the effectual working in the measure of every part, maketh increase of the body unto the edifying of itself in love.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ This I say therefore, and testify in the Lord, that ye henceforth walk not as other Gentiles walk, in the vanity of their mind,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Having the understanding darkened, being alienated from the life of God through the ignorance that is in them, because of the blindness of their heart:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Who being past feeling have given themselves over unto lasciviousness, to work all uncleanness with greediness.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But ye have not so learned Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>If so be that ye have heard him, and have been taught by him, as the truth is in Jesus:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>That ye put off concerning the former conversation the old man, which is corrupt according to the deceitful lusts;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And be renewed in the spirit of your mind;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And that ye put on the new man, which after God is created in righteousness and true holiness.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore putting away lying, speak every man truth with his neighbour: for we are members one of another.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Be ye angry, and sin not: let not the sun go down upon your wrath:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Neither give place to the devil.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Let him that stole steal no more: but rather let him labour, working with _his_ hands the thing which is good, that he may have to give to him that needeth.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Let no corrupt communication proceed out of your mouth, but that which is good to the use of edifying, that it may minister grace unto the hearers.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And grieve not the holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Let all bitterness, and wrath, and anger, and clamour, and evil speaking, be put away from you, with all malice:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And be ye kind one to another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, even as God for Christ’s sake hath forgiven you.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Be ye therefore followers of God, as dear children;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And walk in love, as Christ also hath loved us, and hath given himself for us an offering and a sacrifice to God for a sweetsmelling savour.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But fornication, and all uncleanness, or covetousness, let it not be once named among you, as becometh saints;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Neither filthiness, nor foolish talking, nor jesting, which are not convenient: but rather giving of thanks.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For this ye know, that no whoremonger, nor unclean person, nor covetous man, who is an idolater, hath any inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and of God.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Let no man deceive you with vain words: for because of these things cometh the wrath of God upon the children of disobedience.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Be not ye therefore partakers with them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For ye were sometimes darkness, but now _are ye_ light in the Lord: walk as children of light:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>(For the fruit of the Spirit _is_ in all goodness and righteousness and truth;)  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Proving what is acceptable unto the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather reprove _them_.   
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For it is a shame even to speak of those things which are done of them in secret.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But all things that are reproved are made manifest by the light: for whatsoever doth make manifest is light.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Wherefore he saith, Awake thou that sleepest, and arise from the dead, and Christ shall give thee light.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>See then that ye walk circumspectly, not as fools, but as wise,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Redeeming the time, because the days are evil.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Wherefore be ye not unwise, but understanding what the will of the Lord _is_.   
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And be not drunk with wine, wherein is excess; but be filled with the Spirit;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Speaking to yourselves in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing and making melody in your heart to the Lord;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Giving thanks always for all things unto God and the Father in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Submitting yourselves one to another in the fear of God.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands, as unto the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For the husband is the head of the wife, even as Christ is the head of the church: and he is the saviour of the body.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Therefore as the church is subject unto Christ, so _let_ the wives _be_ to their own husbands in every thing.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>That he might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the word,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>That he might present it to himself a glorious church, not having spot, or wrinkle, or any such thing; but that it should be holy and without blemish.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>So ought men to love their wives as their own bodies. He that loveth his wife loveth himself.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For no man ever yet hated his own flesh; but nourisheth and cherisheth it, even as the Lord the church:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>For we are members of his body, of his flesh, and of his bones.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>For this cause shall a man leave his father and mother, and shall be joined unto his wife, and they two shall be one flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Nevertheless let every one of you in particular so love his wife even as himself; and the wife _see_ that she reverence _her_ husband.  

## CHAPTER 6.

Children, obey your parents in the Lord: for this is right.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Honour thy father and mother; (which is the first commandment with promise;)  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>That it may be well with thee, and thou mayest live long on the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And, ye fathers, provoke not your children to wrath: but bring them up in the nurture and admonition of the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Servants, be obedient to them that are _your_ masters according to the flesh, with fear and trembling, in singleness of your heart, as unto Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Not with eyeservice, as menpleasers; but as the servants of Christ, doing the will of God from the heart;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>With good will doing service, as to the Lord, and not to men:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Knowing that whatsoever good thing any man doeth, the same shall he receive of the Lord, whether _he be_ bond or free.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And, ye masters, do the same things unto them, forbearing threatening: knowing that your Master also is in heaven; neither is there respect of persons with him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Finally, my brethren, be strong in the Lord, and in the power of his might.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Put on the whole armour of God, that ye may be able to stand against the wiles of the devil.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against spiritual wickedness in high _places_.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Wherefore take unto you the whole armour of God, that ye may be able to withstand in the evil day, and having done all, to stand.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Stand therefore, having your loins girt about with truth, and having on the breastplate of righteousness;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And your feet shod with the preparation of the gospel of peace;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Above all, taking the shield of faith, wherewith ye shall be able to quench all the fiery darts of the wicked.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And take the helmet of salvation, and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Praying always with all prayer and supplication in the Spirit, and watching thereunto with all perseverance and supplication for all saints;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And for me, that utterance may be given unto me, that I may open my mouth boldly, to make known the mystery of the gospel,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For which I am an ambassador in bonds: that therein I may speak boldly, as I ought to speak.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ But that ye also may know my affairs, _and_ how I do, Tychicus, a beloved brother and faithful minister in the Lord, shall make known to you all things:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Whom I have sent unto you for the same purpose, that ye might know our affairs, and _that_ he might comfort your hearts.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Peace _be_ to the brethren, and love with faith, from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Grace _be_ with all them that love our Lord Jesus Christ in sincerity. Amen.  

<div class="colophon">Written from Rome unto the Ephesians by Tychicus.</div>
