# Zephaniah

### ZEPHANIAH

## CHAPTER 1.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> which came unto Zephaniah the son of Cushi, the son of Gedaliah, the son of Amariah, the son of Hizkiah, in the days of Josiah the son of Amon, king of Judah.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>I will utterly consume all _things_ from off the land, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I will consume man and beast; I will consume the fowls of the heaven, and the fishes of the sea, and the stumblingblocks with the wicked; and I will cut off man from off the land, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I will also stretch out mine hand upon Judah, and upon all the inhabitants of Jerusalem; and I will cut off the remnant of Baal from this place, _and_ the name of the Chemarims with the priests;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And them that worship the host of heaven upon the housetops; and them that worship _and_ that swear by the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and that swear by Malcham;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And them that are turned back from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and _those_ that have not sought the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, nor enquired for him.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Hold thy peace at the presence of the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>: for the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ at hand: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath prepared a sacrifice, he hath bid his guests.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> sacrifice, that I will punish the princes, and the king’s children, and all such as are clothed with strange apparel.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>In the same day also will I punish all those that leap on the threshold, which fill their masters’ houses with violence and deceit.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _that there shall be_ the noise of a cry from the fish gate, and an howling from the second, and a great crashing from the hills.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Howl, ye inhabitants of Maktesh, for all the merchant people are cut down; all they that bear silver are cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass at that time, _that_ I will search Jerusalem with candles, and punish the men that are settled on their lees: that say in their heart, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will not do good, neither will he do evil.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Therefore their goods shall become a booty, and their houses a desolation: they shall also build houses, but not inhabit _them_; and they shall plant vineyards, but not drink the wine thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ The great day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ near, _it is_ near, and hasteth greatly, _even_ the voice of the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: the mighty man shall cry there bitterly.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>That day _is_ a day of wrath, a day of trouble and distress, a day of wasteness and desolation, a day of darkness and gloominess, a day of clouds and thick darkness,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>A day of the trumpet and alarm against the fenced cities, and against the high towers.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And I will bring distress upon men, that they shall walk like blind men, because they have sinned against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and their blood shall be poured out as dust, and their flesh as the dung.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Neither their silver nor their gold shall be able to deliver them in the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> wrath; but the whole land shall be devoured by the fire of his jealousy: for he shall make even a speedy riddance of all them that dwell in the land.  

## CHAPTER 2.

Gather yourselves together, yea, gather together, O nation not desired;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Before the decree bring forth, _before_ the day pass as the chaff, before the fierce anger of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> come upon you, before the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> anger come upon you.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Seek ye the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, all ye meek of the earth, which have wrought his judgment; seek righteousness, seek meekness: it may be ye shall be hid in the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> anger.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ For Gaza shall be forsaken, and Ashkelon a desolation: they shall drive out Ashdod at the noon day, and Ekron shall be rooted up.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Woe unto the inhabitants of the sea coast, the nation of the Cherethites! the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ against you; O Canaan, the land of the Philistines, I will even destroy thee, that there shall be no inhabitant.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the sea coast shall be dwellings _and_ cottages for shepherds, and folds for flocks.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the coast shall be for the remnant of the house of Judah; they shall feed thereupon: in the houses of Ashkelon shall they lie down in the evening: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God shall visit them, and turn away their captivity.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ I have heard the reproach of Moab, and the revilings of the children of Ammon, whereby they have reproached my people, and magnified _themselves_ against their border.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Therefore _as_ I live, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel, Surely Moab shall be as Sodom, and the children of Ammon as Gomorrah, _even_ the breeding of nettles, and saltpits, and a perpetual desolation: the residue of my people shall spoil them, and the remnant of my people shall possess them.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>This shall they have for their pride, because they have reproached and magnified _themselves_ against the people of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _will be_ terrible unto them: for he will famish all the gods of the earth; and _men_ shall worship him, every one from his place, _even_ all the isles of the heathen.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Ye Ethiopians also, ye _shall be_ slain by my sword.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And he will stretch out his hand against the north, and destroy Assyria; and will make Nineveh a desolation, _and_ dry like a wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And flocks shall lie down in the midst of her, all the beasts of the nations: both the cormorant and the bittern shall lodge in the upper lintels of it; _their_ voice shall sing in the windows; desolation _shall be_ in the thresholds: for he shall uncover the cedar work.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>This _is_ the rejoicing city that dwelt carelessly, that said in her heart, I _am_, and _there is_ none beside me: how is she become a desolation, a place for beasts to lie down in! every one that passeth by her shall hiss, _and_ wag his hand.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Woe to her that is filthy and polluted, to the oppressing city!  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>She obeyed not the voice; she received not correction; she trusted not in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; she drew not near to her God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Her princes within her _are_ roaring lions; her judges _are_ evening wolves; they gnaw not the bones till the morrow.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Her prophets _are_ light _and_ treacherous persons: her priests have polluted the sanctuary, they have done violence to the law.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The just <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ in the midst thereof; he will not do iniquity: every morning doth he bring his judgment to light, he faileth not; but the unjust knoweth no shame.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>I have cut off the nations: their towers are desolate; I made their streets waste, that none passeth by: their cities are destroyed, so that there is no man, that there is none inhabitant.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>I said, Surely thou wilt fear me, thou wilt receive instruction; so their dwelling should not be cut off, howsoever I punished them: but they rose early, _and_ corrupted all their doings.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore wait ye upon me, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, until the day that I rise up to the prey: for my determination _is_ to gather the nations, that I may assemble the kingdoms, to pour upon them mine indignation, _even_ all my fierce anger: for all the earth shall be devoured with the fire of my jealousy.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For then will I turn to the people a pure language, that they may all call upon the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to serve him with one consent.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>From beyond the rivers of Ethiopia my suppliants, _even_ the daughter of my dispersed, shall bring mine offering.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>In that day shalt thou not be ashamed for all thy doings, wherein thou hast transgressed against me: for then I will take away out of the midst of thee them that rejoice in thy pride, and thou shalt no more be haughty because of my holy mountain.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I will also leave in the midst of thee an afflicted and poor people, and they shall trust in the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The remnant of Israel shall not do iniquity, nor speak lies; neither shall a deceitful tongue be found in their mouth: for they shall feed and lie down, and none shall make _them_ afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Sing, O daughter of Zion; shout, O Israel; be glad and rejoice with all the heart, O daughter of Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath taken away thy judgments, he hath cast out thine enemy: the king of Israel, _even_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _is_ in the midst of thee: thou shalt not see evil any more.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>In that day it shall be said to Jerusalem, Fear thou not: _and to_ Zion, Let not thine hands be slack.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God in the midst of thee _is_ mighty; he will save, he will rejoice over thee with joy; he will rest in his love, he will joy over thee with singing.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>I will gather _them that are_ sorrowful for the solemn assembly, _who_ are of thee, _to whom_ the reproach of it _was_ a burden.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Behold, at that time I will undo all that afflict thee: and I will save her that halteth, and gather her that was driven out; and I will get them praise and fame in every land where they have been put to shame.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>At that time will I bring you _again_, even in the time that I gather you: for I will make you a name and a praise among all people of the earth, when I turn back your captivity before your eyes, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.
