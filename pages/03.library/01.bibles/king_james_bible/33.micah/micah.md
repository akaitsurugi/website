# Micah

### MICAH

## CHAPTER 1.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that came to Micah the Morasthite in the days of Jotham, Ahaz, _and_ Hezekiah, kings of Judah, which he saw concerning Samaria and Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Hear, all ye people; hearken, O earth, and all that therein is: and let the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> be witness against you, the Lord from his holy temple.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For, behold, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> cometh forth out of his place, and will come down, and tread upon the high places of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the mountains shall be molten under him, and the valleys shall be cleft, as wax before the fire, _and_ as the waters _that are_ poured down a steep place.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For the transgression of Jacob _is_ all this, and for the sins of the house of Israel. What _is_ the transgression of Jacob? _is it_ not Samaria? and what _are_ the high places of Judah? _are they_ not Jerusalem?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Therefore I will make Samaria as an heap of the field, _and_ as plantings of a vineyard: and I will pour down the stones thereof into the valley, and I will discover the foundations thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And all the graven images thereof shall be beaten to pieces, and all the hires thereof shall be burned with the fire, and all the idols thereof will I lay desolate: for she gathered _it_ of the hire of an harlot, and they shall return to the hire of an harlot.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore I will wail and howl, I will go stripped and naked: I will make a wailing like the dragons, and mourning as the owls.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For her wound _is_ incurable; for it is come unto Judah; he is come unto the gate of my people, _even_ to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Declare ye _it_ not at Gath, weep ye not at all: in the house of Aphrah roll thyself in the dust.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Pass ye away, thou inhabitant of Saphir, having thy shame naked: the inhabitant of Zaanan came not forth in the mourning of Beth–ezel; he shall receive of you his standing.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the inhabitant of Maroth waited carefully for good: but evil came down from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto the gate of Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>O thou inhabitant of Lachish, bind the chariot to the swift beast: she _is_ the beginning of the sin to the daughter of Zion: for the transgressions of Israel were found in thee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore shalt thou give presents to Moresheth–gath: the houses of Achzib _shall be_ a lie to the kings of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Yet will I bring an heir unto thee, O inhabitant of Mareshah: he shall come unto Adullam the glory of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Make thee bald, and poll thee for thy delicate children; enlarge thy baldness as the eagle; for they are gone into captivity from thee.  

## CHAPTER 2.

Woe to them that devise iniquity, and work evil upon their beds! when the morning is light, they practise it, because it is in the power of their hand.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And they covet fields, and take _them_ by violence; and houses, and take _them_ away: so they oppress a man and his house, even a man and his heritage.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; Behold, against this family do I devise an evil, from which ye shall not remove your necks; neither shall ye go haughtily: for this time _is_ evil.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ In that day shall _one_ take up a parable against you, and lament with a doleful lamentation, _and_ say, We be utterly spoiled: he hath changed the portion of my people: how hath he removed _it_ from me! turning away he hath divided our fields.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Therefore thou shalt have none that shall cast a cord by lot in the congregation of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Prophesy ye not, _say they to them that_ prophesy: they shall not prophesy to them, _that_ they shall not take shame.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ O _thou that art_ named the house of Jacob, is the spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> straitened? _are_ these his doings? do not my words do good to him that walketh uprightly?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Even of late my people is risen up as an enemy: ye pull off the robe with the garment from them that pass by securely as men averse from war.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The women of my people have ye cast out from their pleasant houses; from their children have ye taken away my glory for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Arise ye, and depart; for this _is_ not _your_ rest: because it is polluted, it shall destroy _you_, even with a sore destruction.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>If a man walking in the spirit and falsehood do lie, _saying_, I will prophesy unto thee of wine and of strong drink; he shall even be the prophet of this people.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ I will surely assemble, O Jacob, all of thee; I will surely gather the remnant of Israel; I will put them together as the sheep of Bozrah, as the flock in the midst of their fold: they shall make great noise by reason of _the multitude of_ men.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The breaker is come up before them: they have broken up, and have passed through the gate, and are gone out by it: and their king shall pass before them, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> on the head of them.  

## CHAPTER 3.

And I said, Hear, I pray you, O heads of Jacob, and ye princes of the house of Israel; _Is it_ not for you to know judgment?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Who hate the good, and love the evil; who pluck off their skin from off them, and their flesh from off their bones;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Who also eat the flesh of my people, and flay their skin from off them; and they break their bones, and chop them in pieces, as for the pot, and as flesh within the caldron.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then shall they cry unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, but he will not hear them: he will even hide his face from them at that time, as they have behaved themselves ill in their doings.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> concerning the prophets that make my people err, that bite with their teeth, and cry, Peace; and he that putteth not into their mouths, they even prepare war against him.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Therefore night _shall be_ unto you, that ye shall not have a vision; and it shall be dark unto you, that ye shall not divine; and the sun shall go down over the prophets, and the day shall be dark over them.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then shall the seers be ashamed, and the diviners confounded: yea, they shall all cover their lips; for _there is_ no answer of God.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ But truly I am full of power by the spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and of judgment, and of might, to declare unto Jacob his transgression, and to Israel his sin.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Hear this, I pray you, ye heads of the house of Jacob, and princes of the house of Israel, that abhor judgment, and pervert all equity.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>They build up Zion with blood, and Jerusalem with iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The heads thereof judge for reward, and the priests thereof teach for hire, and the prophets thereof divine for money: yet will they lean upon the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and say, _Is_ not the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> among us? none evil can come upon us.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Therefore shall Zion for your sake be plowed _as_ a field, and Jerusalem shall become heaps, and the mountain of the house as the high places of the forest.  

## CHAPTER 4.

But in the last days it shall come to pass, _that_ the mountain of the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be established in the top of the mountains, and it shall be exalted above the hills; and people shall flow unto it.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And many nations shall come, and say, Come, and let us go up to the mountain of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and to the house of the God of Jacob; and he will teach us of his ways, and we will walk in his paths: for the law shall go forth of Zion, and the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> from Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And he shall judge among many people, and rebuke strong nations afar off; and they shall beat their swords into plowshares, and their spears into pruninghooks: nation shall not lift up a sword against nation, neither shall they learn war any more.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But they shall sit every man under his vine and under his fig tree; and none shall make _them_ afraid: for the mouth of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts hath spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For all people will walk every one in the name of his god, and we will walk in the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God for ever and ever.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>In that day, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, will I assemble her that halteth, and I will gather her that is driven out, and her that I have afflicted;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And I will make her that halted a remnant, and her that was cast far off a strong nation: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall reign over them in mount Zion from henceforth, even for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And thou, O tower of the flock, the strong hold of the daughter of Zion, unto thee shall it come, even the first dominion; the kingdom shall come to the daughter of Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Now why dost thou cry out aloud? _is there_ no king in thee? is thy counsellor perished? for pangs have taken thee as a woman in travail.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Be in pain, and labour to bring forth, O daughter of Zion, like a woman in travail: for now shalt thou go forth out of the city, and thou shalt dwell in the field, and thou shalt go _even_ to Babylon; there shalt thou be delivered; there the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall redeem thee from the hand of thine enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Now also many nations are gathered against thee, that say, Let her be defiled, and let our eye look upon Zion.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But they know not the thoughts of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, neither understand they his counsel: for he shall gather them as the sheaves into the floor.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Arise and thresh, O daughter of Zion: for I will make thine horn iron, and I will make thy hoofs brass: and thou shalt beat in pieces many people: and I will consecrate their gain unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and their substance unto the Lord of the whole earth.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Now gather thyself in troops, O daughter of troops: he hath laid siege against us: they shall smite the judge of Israel with a rod upon the cheek.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>But thou, Beth–lehem Ephratah, _though_ thou be little among the thousands of Judah, _yet_ out of thee shall he come forth unto me _that is_ to be ruler in Israel; whose goings forth _have been_ from of old, from everlasting.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore will he give them up, until the time _that_ she which travaileth hath brought forth: then the remnant of his brethren shall return unto the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And he shall stand and feed in the strength of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, in the majesty of the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> his God; and they shall abide: for now shall he be great unto the ends of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And this _man_ shall be the peace, when the Assyrian shall come into our land: and when he shall tread in our palaces, then shall we raise against him seven shepherds, and eight principal men.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they shall waste the land of Assyria with the sword, and the land of Nimrod in the entrances thereof: thus shall he deliver _us_ from the Assyrian, when he cometh into our land, and when he treadeth within our borders.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And the remnant of Jacob shall be in the midst of many people as a dew from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as the showers upon the grass, that tarrieth not for man, nor waiteth for the sons of men.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And the remnant of Jacob shall be among the Gentiles in the midst of many people as a lion among the beasts of the forest, as a young lion among the flocks of sheep: who, if he go through, both treadeth down, and teareth in pieces, and none can deliver.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Thine hand shall be lifted up upon thine adversaries, and all thine enemies shall be cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that I will cut off thy horses out of the midst of thee, and I will destroy thy chariots:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And I will cut off the cities of thy land, and throw down all thy strong holds:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And I will cut off witchcrafts out of thine hand; and thou shalt have no _more_ soothsayers:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thy graven images also will I cut off, and thy standing images out of the midst of thee; and thou shalt no more worship the work of thine hands.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And I will pluck up thy groves out of the midst of thee: so will I destroy thy cities.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And I will execute vengeance in anger and fury upon the heathen, such as they have not heard.  

## CHAPTER 6.

Hear ye now what the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> saith; Arise, contend thou before the mountains, and let the hills hear thy voice.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Hear ye, O mountains, the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> controversy, and ye strong foundations of the earth: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath a controversy with his people, and he will plead with Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>O my people, what have I done unto thee? and wherein have I wearied thee? testify against me.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For I brought thee up out of the land of Egypt, and redeemed thee out of the house of servants; and I sent before thee Moses, Aaron, and Miriam.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>O my people, remember now what Balak king of Moab consulted, and what Balaam the son of Beor answered him from Shittim unto Gilgal; that ye may know the righteousness of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Wherewith shall I come before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _and_ bow myself before the high God? shall I come before him with burnt offerings, with calves of a year old?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Will the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> be pleased with thousands of rams, _or_ with ten thousands of rivers of oil? shall I give my firstborn _for_ my transgression, the fruit of my body _for_ the sin of my soul?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He hath shewed thee, O man, what _is_ good; and what doth the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> require of thee, but to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ The <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> voice crieth unto the city, and _the man of_ wisdom shall see thy name: hear ye the rod, and who hath appointed it.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Are there yet the treasures of wickedness in the house of the wicked, and the scant measure _that is_ abominable?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Shall I count _them_ pure with the wicked balances, and with the bag of deceitful weights?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the rich men thereof are full of violence, and the inhabitants thereof have spoken lies, and their tongue _is_ deceitful in their mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Therefore also will I make _thee_ sick in smiting thee, in making _thee_ desolate because of thy sins.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thou shalt eat, but not be satisfied; and thy casting down _shall be_ in the midst of thee; and thou shalt take hold, but shalt not deliver; and _that_ which thou deliverest will I give up to the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thou shalt sow, but thou shalt not reap; thou shalt tread the olives, but thou shalt not anoint thee with oil; and sweet wine, but shalt not drink wine.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ For the statutes of Omri are kept, and all the works of the house of Ahab, and ye walk in their counsels; that I should make thee a desolation, and the inhabitants thereof an hissing: therefore ye shall bear the reproach of my people.  

## CHAPTER 7.

Woe is me! for I am as when they have gathered the summer fruits, as the grapegleanings of the vintage: _there is_ no cluster to eat: my soul desired the firstripe fruit.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The good _man_ is perished out of the earth: and _there is_ none upright among men: they all lie in wait for blood; they hunt every man his brother with a net.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ That they may do evil with both hands earnestly, the prince asketh, and the judge _asketh_ for a reward; and the great _man_, he uttereth his mischievous desire: so they wrap it up.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The best of them _is_ as a brier: the most upright _is sharper_ than a thorn hedge: the day of thy watchmen _and_ thy visitation cometh; now shall be their perplexity.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Trust ye not in a friend, put ye not confidence in a guide: keep the doors of thy mouth from her that lieth in thy bosom.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For the son dishonoureth the father, the daughter riseth up against her mother, the daughter in law against her mother in law; a man’s enemies _are_ the men of his own house.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore I will look unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; I will wait for the God of my salvation: my God will hear me.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Rejoice not against me, O mine enemy: when I fall, I shall arise; when I sit in darkness, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _shall be_ a light unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>I will bear the indignation of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, because I have sinned against him, until he plead my cause, and execute judgment for me: he will bring me forth to the light, _and_ I shall behold his righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Then _she that is_ mine enemy shall see _it_, and shame shall cover her which said unto me, Where is the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God? mine eyes shall behold her: now shall she be trodden down as the mire of the streets.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_In_ the day that thy walls are to be built, _in_ that day shall the decree be far removed.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>_In_ that day _also_ he shall come even to thee from Assyria, and _from_ the fortified cities, and from the fortress even to the river, and from sea to sea, and _from_ mountain to mountain.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Notwithstanding the land shall be desolate because of them that dwell therein, for the fruit of their doings.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Feed thy people with thy rod, the flock of thine heritage, which dwell solitarily _in_ the wood, in the midst of Carmel: let them feed _in_ Bashan and Gilead, as in the days of old.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>According to the days of thy coming out of the land of Egypt will I shew unto him marvellous _things_.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ The nations shall see and be confounded at all their might: they shall lay _their_ hand upon _their_ mouth, their ears shall be deaf.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>They shall lick the dust like a serpent, they shall move out of their holes like worms of the earth: they shall be afraid of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God, and shall fear because of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Who _is_ a God like unto thee, that pardoneth iniquity, and passeth by the transgression of the remnant of his heritage? he retaineth not his anger for ever, because he delighteth _in_ mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>He will turn again, he will have compassion upon us; he will subdue our iniquities; and thou wilt cast all their sins into the depths of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Thou wilt perform the truth to Jacob, _and_ the mercy to Abraham, which thou hast sworn unto our fathers from the days of old.
