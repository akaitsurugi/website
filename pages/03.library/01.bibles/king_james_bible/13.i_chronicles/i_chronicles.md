# I Chronicles

### THE FIRST BOOK OF THE CHRONICLES

## CHAPTER 1.

Adam, Sheth, Enosh,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Kenan, Mahalaleel, Jered,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Henoch, Methuselah, Lamech,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Noah, Shem, Ham, and Japheth.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Japheth; Gomer, and Magog, and Madai, and Javan, and Tubal, and Meshech, and Tiras.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the sons of Gomer; Ashchenaz, and Riphath, and Togarmah.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the sons of Javan; Elishah, and Tarshish, Kittim, and Dodanim.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Ham; Cush, and Mizraim, Put, and Canaan.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the sons of Cush; Seba, and Havilah, and Sabta, and Raamah, and Sabtecha. And the sons of Raamah; Sheba, and Dedan.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Cush begat Nimrod: he began to be mighty upon the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Mizraim begat Ludim, and Anamim, and Lehabim, and Naphtuhim,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Pathrusim, and Casluhim, (of whom came the Philistines,) and Caphthorim.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Canaan begat Zidon his firstborn, and Heth,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The Jebusite also, and the Amorite, and the Girgashite,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the Hivite, and the Arkite, and the Sinite,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the Arvadite, and the Zemarite, and the Hamathite.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Shem; Elam, and Asshur, and Arphaxad, and Lud, and Aram, and Uz, and Hul, and Gether, and Meshech.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Arphaxad begat Shelah, and Shelah begat Eber.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And unto Eber were born two sons: the name of the one _was_ Peleg; because in his days the earth was divided: and his brother’s name _was_ Joktan.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Joktan begat Almodad, and Sheleph, and Hazarmaveth, and Jerah,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Hadoram also, and Uzal, and Diklah,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Ebal, and Abimael, and Sheba,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And Ophir, and Havilah, and Jobab. All these _were_ the sons of Joktan.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Shem, Arphaxad, Shelah,  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Eber, Peleg, Reu,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Serug, Nahor, Terah,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Abram; the same _is_ Abraham.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Abraham; Isaac, and Ishmael.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ These _are_ their generations: The firstborn of Ishmael, Nebaioth; then Kedar, and Adbeel, and Mibsam,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Mishma, and Dumah, Massa, Hadad, and Tema,  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah. These are the sons of Ishmael.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ Now the sons of Keturah, Abraham’s concubine: she bare Zimran, and Jokshan, and Medan, and Midian, and Ishbak, and Shuah. And the sons of Jokshan; Sheba, and Dedan.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And the sons of Midian; Ephah, and Epher, and Henoch, and Abida, and Eldaah. All these _are_ the sons of Keturah.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And Abraham begat Isaac. The sons of Isaac; Esau and Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Esau; Eliphaz, Reuel, and Jeush, and Jaalam, and Korah.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>The sons of Eliphaz; Teman, and Omar, Zephi, and Gatam, Kenaz, and Timna, and Amalek.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>The sons of Reuel; Nahath, Zerah, Shammah, and Mizzah.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And the sons of Seir; Lotan, and Shobal, and Zibeon, and Anah, and Dishon, and Ezer, and Dishan.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And the sons of Lotan; Hori, and Homam: and Timna _was_ Lotan’s sister.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>The sons of Shobal; Alian, and Manahath, and Ebal, Shephi, and Onam. And the sons of Zibeon; Aiah, and Anah.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>The sons of Anah; Dishon. And the sons of Dishon; Amram, and Eshban, and Ithran, and Cheran.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>The sons of Ezer; Bilhan, and Zavan, _and_ Jakan. The sons of Dishan; Uz, and Aran.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>¶ Now these _are_ the kings that reigned in the land of Edom before _any_ king reigned over the children of Israel; Bela the son of Beor: and the name of his city _was_ Dinhabah.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And when Bela was dead, Jobab the son of Zerah of Bozrah reigned in his stead.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And when Jobab was dead, Husham of the land of the Temanites reigned in his stead.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And when Husham was dead, Hadad the son of Bedad, which smote Midian in the field of Moab, reigned in his stead: and the name of his city _was_ Avith.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And when Hadad was dead, Samlah of Masrekah reigned in his stead.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And when Samlah was dead, Shaul of Rehoboth by the river reigned in his stead.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>And when Shaul was dead, Baal–hanan the son of Achbor reigned in his stead.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>And when Baal–hanan was dead, Hadad reigned in his stead: and the name of his city _was_ Pai; and his wife’s name _was_ Mehetabel, the daughter of Matred, the daughter of Mezahab.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>¶ Hadad died also. And the dukes of Edom were; duke Timnah, duke Aliah, duke Jetheth,  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>Duke Aholibamah, duke Elah, duke Pinon,  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>Duke Kenaz, duke Teman, duke Mibzar,  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>Duke Magdiel, duke Iram. These _are_ the dukes of Edom.  

## CHAPTER 2.

These _are_ the sons of Israel; Reuben, Simeon, Levi, and Judah, Issachar, and Zebulun,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Dan, Joseph, and Benjamin, Naphtali, Gad, and Asher.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Judah; Er, and Onan, and Shelah: _which_ three were born unto him of the daughter of Shua the Canaanitess. And Er, the firstborn of Judah, was evil in the sight of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and he slew him.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Tamar his daughter in law bare him Pharez and Zerah. All the sons of Judah _were_ five.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The sons of Pharez; Hezron, and Hamul.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the sons of Zerah; Zimri, and Ethan, and Heman, and Calcol, and Dara: five of them in all.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the sons of Carmi; Achar, the troubler of Israel, who transgressed in the thing accursed.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the sons of Ethan; Azariah.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The sons also of Hezron, that were born unto him; Jerahmeel, and Ram, and Chelubai.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Ram begat Amminadab; and Amminadab begat Nahshon, prince of the children of Judah;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Nahshon begat Salma, and Salma begat Boaz,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Boaz begat Obed, and Obed begat Jesse,  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And Jesse begat his firstborn Eliab, and Abinadab the second, and Shimma the third,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Nethaneel the fourth, Raddai the fifth,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Ozem the sixth, David the seventh:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Whose sisters _were_ Zeruiah, and Abigail. And the sons of Zeruiah; Abishai, and Joab, and Asahel, three.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And Abigail bare Amasa: and the father of Amasa _was_ Jether the Ishmeelite.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And Caleb the son of Hezron begat _children_ of Azubah _his_ wife, and of Jerioth: her sons _are_ these; Jesher, and Shobab, and Ardon.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And when Azubah was dead, Caleb took unto him Ephrath, which bare him Hur.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Hur begat Uri, and Uri begat Bezaleel.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And afterward Hezron went in to the daughter of Machir the father of Gilead, whom he married when he _was_ threescore years old; and she bare him Segub.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Segub begat Jair, who had three and twenty cities in the land of Gilead.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And he took Geshur, and Aram, with the towns of Jair, from them, with Kenath, and the towns thereof, _even_ threescore cities. All these _belonged to_ the sons of Machir the father of Gilead.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And after that Hezron was dead in Caleb–ephratah, then Abiah Hezron’s wife bare him Ashur the father of Tekoa.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And the sons of Jerahmeel the firstborn of Hezron were, Ram the firstborn, and Bunah, and Oren, and Ozem, _and_ Ahijah.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Jerahmeel had also another wife, whose name _was_ Atarah; she _was_ the mother of Onam.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And the sons of Ram the firstborn of Jerahmeel were, Maaz, and Jamin, and Eker.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the sons of Onam were, Shammai, and Jada. And the sons of Shammai; Nadab, and Abishur.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And the name of the wife of Abishur _was_ Abihail, and she bare him Ahban, and Molid.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the sons of Nadab; Seled, and Appaim: but Seled died without children.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the sons of Appaim; Ishi. And the sons of Ishi; Sheshan. And the children of Sheshan; Ahlai.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And the sons of Jada the brother of Shammai; Jether, and Jonathan: and Jether died without children.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And the sons of Jonathan; Peleth, and Zaza. These were the sons of Jerahmeel.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>¶ Now Sheshan had no sons, but daughters. And Sheshan had a servant, an Egyptian, whose name _was_ Jarha.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And Sheshan gave his daughter to Jarha his servant to wife; and she bare him Attai.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And Attai begat Nathan, and Nathan begat Zabad,  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And Zabad begat Ephlal, and Ephlal begat Obed,  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And Obed begat Jehu, and Jehu begat Azariah,  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And Azariah begat Helez, and Helez begat Eleasah,  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And Eleasah begat Sisamai, and Sisamai begat Shallum,  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And Shallum begat Jekamiah, and Jekamiah begat Elishama.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>¶ Now the sons of Caleb the brother of Jerahmeel _were_, Mesha his firstborn, which was the father of Ziph; and the sons of Mareshah the father of Hebron.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And the sons of Hebron; Korah, and Tappuah, and Rekem, and Shema.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And Shema begat Raham, the father of Jorkoam: and Rekem begat Shammai.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And the son of Shammai _was_ Maon: and Maon _was_ the father of Beth–zur.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And Ephah, Caleb’s concubine, bare Haran, and Moza, and Gazez: and Haran begat Gazez.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And the sons of Jahdai; Regem, and Jotham, and Geshan, and Pelet, and Ephah, and Shaaph.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>Maachah, Caleb’s concubine, bare Sheber, and Tirhanah.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>She bare also Shaaph the father of Madmannah, Sheva the father of Machbenah, and the father of Gibea: and the daughter of Caleb _was_ Achsah.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>¶ These were the sons of Caleb the son of Hur, the firstborn of Ephratah; Shobal the father of Kirjath–jearim,  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>Salma the father of Beth–lehem, Hareph the father of Beth–gader.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>And Shobal the father of Kirjath–jearim had sons; Haroeh, _and_ half of the Manahethites.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>And the families of Kirjath–jearim; the Ithrites, and the Puhites, and the Shumathites, and the Mishraites; of them came the Zareathites, and the Eshtaulites.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>The sons of Salma; Beth–lehem, and the Netophathites, Ataroth, the house of Joab, and half of the Manahethites, the Zorites.  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>And the families of the scribes which dwelt at Jabez; the Tirathites, the Shimeathites, _and_ Suchathites. These _are_ the Kenites that came of Hemath, the father of the house of Rechab.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Now these were the sons of David, which were born unto him in Hebron; the firstborn Amnon, of Ahinoam the Jezreelitess; the second Daniel, of Abigail the Carmelitess:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The third, Absalom the son of Maachah the daughter of Talmai king of Geshur: the fourth, Adonijah the son of Haggith:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The fifth, Shephatiah of Abital: the sixth, Ithream by Eglah his wife.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>_These_ six were born unto him in Hebron; and there he reigned seven years and six months: and in Jerusalem he reigned thirty and three years.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And these were born unto him in Jerusalem; Shimea, and Shobab, and Nathan, and Solomon, four, of Bath–shua the daughter of Ammiel:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Ibhar also, and Elishama, and Eliphelet,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And Nogah, and Nepheg, and Japhia,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Elishama, and Eliada, and Eliphelet, nine.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_These were_ all the sons of David, beside the sons of the concubines, and Tamar their sister.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And Solomon’s son _was_ Rehoboam, Abia his son, Asa his son, Jehoshaphat his son,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Joram his son, Ahaziah his son, Joash his son,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Amaziah his son, Azariah his son, Jotham his son,  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Ahaz his son, Hezekiah his son, Manasseh his son,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Amon his son, Josiah his son.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the sons of Josiah _were_, the firstborn Johanan, the second Jehoiakim, the third Zedekiah, the fourth Shallum.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the sons of Jehoiakim: Jeconiah his son, Zedekiah his son.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And the sons of Jeconiah; Assir, Salathiel his son,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Malchiram also, and Pedaiah, and Shenazar, Jecamiah, Hoshama, and Nedabiah.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the sons of Pedaiah _were_, Zerubbabel, and Shimei: and the sons of Zerubbabel; Meshullam, and Hananiah, and Shelomith their sister:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Hashubah, and Ohel, and Berechiah, and Hasadiah, Jushab–hesed, five.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the sons of Hananiah; Pelatiah, and Jesaiah: the sons of Rephaiah, the sons of Arnan, the sons of Obadiah, the sons of Shechaniah.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the sons of Shechaniah; Shemaiah: and the sons of Shemaiah; Hattush, and Igeal, and Bariah, and Neariah, and Shaphat, six.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the sons of Neariah; Elioenai, and Hezekiah, and Azrikam, three.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the sons of Elioenai _were_, Hodaiah, and Eliashib, and Pelaiah, and Akkub, and Johanan, and Dalaiah, and Anani, seven.  

## CHAPTER 4.

The sons of Judah; Pharez, Hezron, and Carmi, and Hur, and Shobal.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Reaiah the son of Shobal begat Jahath; and Jahath begat Ahumai, and Lahad. These _are_ the families of the Zorathites.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And these _were of_ the father of Etam; Jezreel, and Ishma, and Idbash: and the name of their sister _was_ Hazelelponi:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Penuel the father of Gedor, and Ezer the father of Hushah. These _are_ the sons of Hur, the firstborn of Ephratah, the father of Beth–lehem.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And Ashur the father of Tekoa had two wives, Helah and Naarah.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Naarah bare him Ahuzam, and Hepher, and Temeni, and Haahashtari. These _were_ the sons of Naarah.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the sons of Helah _were_, Zereth, and Jezoar, and Ethnan.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Coz begat Anub, and Zobebah, and the families of Aharhel the son of Harum.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And Jabez was more honourable than his brethren: and his mother called his name Jabez, saying, Because I bare him with sorrow.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Jabez called on the God of Israel, saying, Oh that thou wouldest bless me indeed, and enlarge my coast, and that thine hand might be with me, and that thou wouldest keep _me_ from evil, that it may not grieve me! And God granted him that which he requested.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And Chelub the brother of Shuah begat Mehir, which _was_ the father of Eshton.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Eshton begat Beth–rapha, and Paseah, and Tehinnah the father of Ir–nahash. These _are_ the men of Rechah.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the sons of Kenaz; Othniel, and Seraiah: and the sons of Othniel; Hathath.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And Meonothai begat Ophrah: and Seraiah begat Joab, the father of the valley of Charashim; for they were craftsmen.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the sons of Caleb the son of Jephunneh; Iru, Elah, and Naam: and the sons of Elah, even Kenaz.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the sons of Jehaleleel; Ziph, and Ziphah, Tiria, and Asareel.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the sons of Ezra _were_, Jether, and Mered, and Epher, and Jalon: and she bare Miriam, and Shammai, and Ishbah the father of Eshtemoa.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And his wife Jehudijah bare Jered the father of Gedor, and Heber the father of Socho, and Jekuthiel the father of Zanoah. And these _are_ the sons of Bithiah the daughter of Pharaoh, which Mered took.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the sons of _his_ wife Hodiah the sister of Naham, the father of Keilah the Garmite, and Eshtemoa the Maachathite.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the sons of Shimon _were_, Amnon, and Rinnah, Ben–hanan, and Tilon. And the sons of Ishi _were_, Zoheth, and Ben–zoheth.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Shelah the son of Judah _were_, Er the father of Lecah, and Laadah the father of Mareshah, and the families of the house of them that wrought fine linen, of the house of Ashbea,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Jokim, and the men of Chozeba, and Joash, and Saraph, who had the dominion in Moab, and Jashubi–lehem. And _these are_ ancient things.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>These _were_ the potters, and those that dwelt among plants and hedges: there they dwelt with the king for his work.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Simeon _were_, Nemuel, and Jamin, Jarib, Zerah, _and_ Shaul:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Shallum his son, Mibsam his son, Mishma his son.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And the sons of Mishma; Hamuel his son, Zacchur his son, Shimei his son.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And Shimei had sixteen sons and six daughters; but his brethren had not many children, neither did all their family multiply, like to the children of Judah.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And they dwelt at Beer–sheba, and Moladah, and Hazar–shual,  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And at Bilhah, and at Ezem, and at Tolad,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And at Bethuel, and at Hormah, and at Ziklag,  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And at Beth–marcaboth, and Hazar–susim, and at Beth–birei, and at Shaaraim. These _were_ their cities unto the reign of David.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And their villages _were_, Etam, and Ain, Rimmon, and Tochen, and Ashan, five cities:  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And all their villages that _were_ round about the same cities, unto Baal. These _were_ their habitations, and their genealogy.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And Meshobab, and Jamlech, and Joshah the son of Amaziah,  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And Joel, and Jehu the son of Josibiah, the son of Seraiah, the son of Asiel,  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And Elioenai, and Jaakobah, and Jeshohaiah, and Asaiah, and Adiel, and Jesimiel, and Benaiah,  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And Ziza the son of Shiphi, the son of Allon, the son of Jedaiah, the son of Shimri, the son of Shemaiah;  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>These mentioned by _their_ names _were_ princes in their families: and the house of their fathers increased greatly.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>¶ And they went to the entrance of Gedor, _even_ unto the east side of the valley, to seek pasture for their flocks.

<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And of the half tribe of Manasseh eighteen thousand, which were expressed by name, to come and make David king.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And of the children of Issachar, _which were men_ that had understanding of the times, to know what Israel ought to do; the heads of them _were_ two hundred; and all their brethren _were_ at their commandment.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Of Zebulun, such as went forth to battle, expert in war, with all instruments of war, fifty thousand, which could keep rank: _they were_ not of double heart.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And of Naphtali a thousand captains, and with them with shield and spear thirty and seven thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And of the Danites expert in war twenty and eight thousand and six hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And of Asher, such as went forth to battle, expert in war, forty thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And on the other side of Jordan, of the Reubenites, and the Gadites, and of the half tribe of Manasseh, with all manner of instruments of war for the battle, an hundred and twenty thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>All these men of war, that could keep rank, came with a perfect heart to Hebron, to make David king over all Israel: and all the rest also of Israel _were_ of one heart to make David king.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And there they were with David three days, eating and drinking: for their brethren had prepared for them.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>Moreover they that were nigh them, _even_ unto Issachar and Zebulun and Naphtali, brought bread on asses, and on camels, and on mules, and on oxen, _and_ meat, meal, cakes of figs, and bunches of raisins, and wine, and oil, and oxen, and sheep abundantly: for _there was_ joy in Israel.  

## CHAPTER 13.

And David consulted with the captains of thousands and hundreds, _and_ with every leader.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And David said unto all the congregation of Israel, If _it seem_ good unto you, and _that it be_ of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God, let us send abroad unto our brethren every where, _that are_ left in all the land of Israel, and with them _also_ to the priests and Levites _which are_ in their cities _and_ suburbs, that they may gather themselves unto us:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And let us bring again the ark of our God to us: for we enquired not at it in the days of Saul.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And all the congregation said that they would do so: for the thing was right in the eyes of all the people.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>So David gathered all Israel together, from Shihor of Egypt even unto the entering of Hemath, to bring the ark of God from Kirjath–jearim.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And David went up, and all Israel, to Baalah, _that is_, to Kirjath–jearim, which _belonged_ to Judah, to bring up thence the ark of God the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that dwelleth _between_ the cherubims, whose name is called _on it_.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they carried the ark of God in a new cart out of the house of Abinadab: and Uzza and Ahio drave the cart.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And David and all Israel played before God with all _their_ might, and with singing, and with harps, and with psalteries, and with timbrels, and with cymbals, and with trumpets.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And when they came unto the threshingfloor of Chidon, Uzza put forth his hand to hold the ark; for the oxen stumbled.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the anger of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was kindled against Uzza, and he smote him, because he put his hand to the ark: and there he died before God.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And David was displeased, because the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had made a breach upon Uzza: wherefore that place is called Perez–uzza to this day.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And David was afraid of God that day, saying, How shall I bring the ark of God _home_ to me?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>So David brought not the ark _home_ to himself to the city of David, but carried it aside into the house of Obed–edom the Gittite.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the ark of God remained with the family of Obed–edom in his house three months. And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> blessed the house of Obed–edom, and all that he had.  

## CHAPTER 14.

Now Hiram king of Tyre sent messengers to David, and timber of cedars, with masons and carpenters, to build him an house.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And David perceived that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had confirmed him king over Israel, for his kingdom was lifted up on high, because of his people Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And David took more wives at Jerusalem: and David begat more sons and daughters.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Now these _are_ the names of _his_ children which he had in Jerusalem; Shammua, and Shobab, Nathan, and Solomon,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And Ibhar, and Elishua, and Elpalet,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Nogah, and Nepheg, and Japhia,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And Elishama, and Beeliada, and Eliphalet.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And when the Philistines heard that David was anointed king over all Israel, all the Philistines went up to seek David. And David heard _of it_, and went out against them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the Philistines came and spread themselves in the valley of Rephaim.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And David enquired of God, saying, Shall I go up against the Philistines? and wilt thou deliver them into mine hand? And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto him, Go up; for I will deliver them into thine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>So they came up to Baal–perazim; and David smote them there. Then David said, God hath broken in upon mine enemies by mine hand like the breaking forth of waters: therefore they called the name of that place Baal–perazim.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And when they had left their gods there, David gave a commandment, and they were burned with fire.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the Philistines yet again spread themselves abroad in the valley.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore David enquired again of God; and God said unto him, Go not up after them; turn away from them, and come upon them over against the mulberry trees.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And it shall be, when thou shalt hear a sound of going in the tops of the mulberry trees, _that_ then thou shalt go out to battle: for God is gone forth before thee to smite the host of the Philistines.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>David therefore did as God commanded him: and they smote the host of the Philistines from Gibeon even to Gazer.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the fame of David went out into all lands; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought the fear of him upon all nations.  

## CHAPTER 15.

And _David_ made him houses in the city of David, and prepared a place for the ark of God, and pitched for it a tent.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then David said, None ought to carry the ark of God but the Levites: for them hath the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> chosen to carry the ark of God, and to minister unto him for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And David gathered all Israel together to Jerusalem, to bring up the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto his place, which he had prepared for it.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And David assembled the children of Aaron, and the Levites:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Kohath; Uriel the chief, and his brethren an hundred and twenty:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Merari; Asaiah the chief, and his brethren two hundred and twenty:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Gershom; Joel the chief, and his brethren an hundred and thirty:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Elizaphan; Shemaiah the chief, and his brethren two hundred:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Hebron; Eliel the chief, and his brethren fourscore:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Uzziel; Amminadab the chief, and his brethren an hundred and twelve.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And David called for Zadok and Abiathar the priests, and for the Levites, for Uriel, Asaiah, and Joel, Shemaiah, and Eliel, and Amminadab,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And said unto them, Ye _are_ the chief of the fathers of the Levites: sanctify yourselves, _both_ ye and your brethren, that ye may bring up the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel unto _the place that_ I have prepared for it.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For because ye _did it_ not at the first, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God made a breach upon us, for that we sought him not after the due order.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>So the priests and the Levites sanctified themselves to bring up the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the children of the Levites bare the ark of God upon their shoulders with the staves thereon, as Moses commanded according to the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And David spake to the chief of the Levites to appoint their brethren _to be_ the singers with instruments of musick, psalteries and harps and cymbals, sounding, by lifting up the voice with joy.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>So the Levites appointed Heman the son of Joel; and of his brethren, Asaph the son of Berechiah; and of the sons of Merari their brethren, Ethan the son of Kushaiah;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And with them their brethren of the second _degree_, Zechariah, Ben, and Jaaziel, and Shemiramoth, and Jehiel, and Unni, Eliab, and Benaiah, and Maaseiah, and Mattithiah, and Elipheleh, and Mikneiah, and Obed–edom, and Jeiel, the porters.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>So the singers, Heman, Asaph, and Ethan, _were appointed_ to sound with cymbals of brass;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Zechariah, and Aziel, and Shemiramoth, and Jehiel, and Unni, and Eliab, and Maaseiah, and Benaiah, with psalteries on Alamoth;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Mattithiah, and Elipheleh, and Mikneiah, and Obed–edom, and Jeiel, and Azaziah, with harps on the Sheminith to excel.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Chenaniah, chief of the Levites, _was_ for song: he instructed about the song, because he _was_ skilful.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And Berechiah and Elkanah _were_ doorkeepers for the ark.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And Shebaniah, and Jehoshaphat, and Nethaneel, and Amasai, and Zechariah, and Benaiah, and Eliezer, the priests, did blow with the trumpets before the ark of God: and Obed–edom and Jehiah _were_ doorkeepers for the ark.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ So David, and the elders of Israel, and the captains over thousands, went to bring up the ark of the covenant of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> out of the house of Obed–edom with joy.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when God helped the Levites that bare the ark of the covenant of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that they offered seven bullocks and seven rams.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And David _was_ clothed with a robe of fine linen, and all the Levites that bare the ark, and the singers, and Chenaniah the master of the song with the singers: David also _had_ upon him an ephod of linen.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Thus all Israel brought up the ark of the covenant of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> with shouting, and with sound of the cornet, and with trumpets, and with cymbals, making a noise with psalteries and harps.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, _as_ the ark of the covenant of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came to the city of David, that Michal the daughter of Saul looking out at a window saw king David dancing and playing: and she despised him in her heart.  

## CHAPTER 16.

So they brought the ark of God, and set it in the midst of the tent that David had pitched for it: and they offered burnt sacrifices and peace offerings before God.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And when David had made an end of offering the burnt offerings and the peace offerings, he blessed the people in the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he dealt to every one of Israel, both man and woman, to every one a loaf of bread, and a good piece of flesh, and a flagon _of wine_.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And he appointed _certain_ of the Levites to minister before the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and to record, and to thank and praise the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Asaph the chief, and next to him Zechariah, Jeiel, and Shemiramoth, and Jehiel, and Mattithiah, and Eliab, and Benaiah, and Obed–edom: and Jeiel with psalteries and with harps; but Asaph made a sound with cymbals;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Benaiah also and Jahaziel the priests with trumpets continually before the ark of the covenant of God.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Then on that day David delivered first _this psalm_ to thank the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> into the hand of Asaph and his brethren.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Give thanks unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, call upon his name, make known his deeds among the people.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Sing unto him, sing psalms unto him, talk ye of all his wondrous works.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Glory ye in his holy name: let the heart of them rejoice that seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> and his strength, seek his face continually.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Remember his marvellous works that he hath done, his wonders, and the judgments of his mouth;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>O ye seed of Israel his servant, ye children of Jacob, his chosen ones.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>He _is_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God; his judgments _are_ in all the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Be ye mindful always of his covenant; the word _which_ he commanded to a thousand generations;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>_Even of the covenant_ which he made with Abraham, and of his oath unto Isaac;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And hath confirmed the same to Jacob for a law, _and_ to Israel _for_ an everlasting covenant,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Saying, Unto thee will I give the land of Canaan, the lot of your inheritance;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>When ye were but few, even a few, and strangers in it.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And _when_ they went from nation to nation, and from _one_ kingdom to another people;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>He suffered no man to do them wrong: yea, he reproved kings for their sakes,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>_Saying_, Touch not mine anointed, and do my prophets no harm.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Sing unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, all the earth; shew forth from day to day his salvation.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Declare his glory among the heathen; his marvellous works among all nations.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For great _is_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and greatly to be praised: he also _is_ to be feared above all gods.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For all the gods of the people _are_ idols: but the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> made the heavens.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Glory and honour _are_ in his presence; strength and gladness _are_ in his place.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Give unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, ye kindreds of the people, give unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> glory and strength.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Give unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> the glory _due_ unto his name: bring an offering, and come before him: worship the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the beauty of holiness.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Fear before him, all the earth: the world also shall be stable, that it be not moved.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Let the heavens be glad, and let the earth rejoice: and let _men_ say among the nations, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> reigneth.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Let the sea roar, and the fulness thereof: let the fields rejoice, and all that _is_ therein.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Then shall the trees of the wood sing out at the presence of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, because he cometh to judge the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>O give thanks unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; for _he is_ good; for his mercy _endureth_ for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And say ye, Save us, O God of our salvation, and gather us together, and deliver us from the heathen, that we may give thanks to thy holy name, _and_ glory in thy praise.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Blessed _be_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel for ever and ever. And all the people said, Amen, and praised the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>¶ So he left there before the ark of the covenant of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> Asaph and his brethren, to minister before the ark continually, as every day’s work required:  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And Obed–edom with their brethren, threescore and eight; Obed–edom also the son of Jeduthun and Hosah _to be_ porters:  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And Zadok the priest, and his brethren the priests, before the tabernacle of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the high place that _was_ at Gibeon,  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>To offer burnt offerings unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> upon the altar of the burnt offering continually morning and evening, and _to do_ according to all that is written in the law of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, which he commanded Israel;  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And with them Heman and Jeduthun, and the rest that were chosen, who were expressed by name, to give thanks to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, because his mercy _endureth_ for ever;  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And with them Heman and Jeduthun with trumpets and cymbals for those that should make a sound, and with musical instruments of God. And the sons of Jeduthun _were_ porters.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And all the people departed every man to his house: and David returned to bless his house.  

## CHAPTER 17.

Now it came to pass, as David sat in his house, that David said to Nathan the prophet, Lo, I dwell in an house of cedars, but the ark of the covenant of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _remaineth_ under curtains.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then Nathan said unto David, Do all that _is_ in thine heart; for God _is_ with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass the same night, that the word of God came to Nathan, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Go and tell David my servant, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Thou shalt not build me an house to dwell in:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For I have not dwelt in an house since the day that I brought up Israel unto this day; but have gone from tent to tent, and from _one_ tabernacle _to another_.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Wheresoever I have walked with all Israel, spake I a word to any of the judges of Israel, whom I commanded to feed my people, saying, Why have ye not built me an house of cedars?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Now therefore thus shalt thou say unto my servant David, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, I took thee from the sheepcote, _even_ from following the sheep, that thou shouldest be ruler over my people Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And I have been with thee whithersoever thou hast walked, and have cut off all thine enemies from before thee, and have made thee a name like the name of the great men that _are_ in the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Also I will ordain a place for my people Israel, and will plant them, and they shall dwell in their place, and shall be moved no more; neither shall the children of wickedness waste them any more, as at the beginning,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And since the time that I commanded judges _to be_ over my people Israel. Moreover I will subdue all thine enemies. Furthermore I tell thee that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will build thee an house.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass, when thy days be expired that thou must go _to be_ with thy fathers, that I will raise up thy seed after thee, which shall be of thy sons; and I will establish his kingdom.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>He shall build me an house, and I will stablish his throne for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>I will be his father, and he shall be my son: and I will not take my mercy away from him, as I took _it_ from _him_ that was before thee:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But I will settle him in mine house and in my kingdom for ever: and his throne shall be established for evermore.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>According to all these words, and according to all this vision, so did Nathan speak unto David.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And David the king came and sat before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, Who _am_ I, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God, and what _is_ mine house, that thou hast brought me hitherto?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And _yet_ this was a small thing in thine eyes, O God; for thou hast _also_ spoken of thy servant’s house for a great while to come, and hast regarded me according to the estate of a man of high degree, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>What can David _speak_ more to thee for the honour of thy servant? for thou knowest thy servant.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, for thy servant’s sake, and according to thine own heart, hast thou done all this greatness, in making known all _these_ great things.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _there is_ none like thee, neither _is there any_ God beside thee, according to all that we have heard with our ears.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And what one nation in the earth _is_ like thy people Israel, whom God went to redeem _to be_ his own people, to make thee a name of greatness and terribleness, by driving out nations from before thy people, whom thou hast redeemed out of Egypt?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For thy people Israel didst thou make thine own people for ever; and thou, <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, becamest their God.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Therefore now, <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, let the thing that thou hast spoken concerning thy servant and concerning his house be established for ever, and do as thou hast said.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Let it even be established, that thy name may be magnified for ever, saying, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts _is_ the God of Israel, _even_ a God to Israel: and _let_ the house of David thy servant _be_ established before thee.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For thou, O my God, hast told thy servant that thou wilt build him an house: therefore thy servant hath found _in his heart_ to pray before thee.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And now, <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou art God, and hast promised this goodness unto thy servant:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Now therefore let it please thee to bless the house of thy servant, that it may be before thee for ever: for thou blessest, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and _it shall be_ blessed for ever.  

## CHAPTER 18.

Now after this it came to pass, that David smote the Philistines, and subdued them, and took Gath and her towns out of the hand of the Philistines.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he smote Moab; and the Moabites became David’s servants, _and_ brought gifts.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And David smote Hadarezer king of Zobah unto Hamath, as he went to stablish his dominion by the river Euphrates.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And David took from him a thousand chariots, and seven thousand horsemen, and twenty thousand footmen: David also houghed all the chariot _horses_, but reserved of them an hundred chariots.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And when the Syrians of Damascus came to help Hadarezer king of Zobah, David slew of the Syrians two and twenty thousand men.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Then David put _garrisons_ in Syria–damascus; and the Syrians became David’s servants, _and_ brought gifts. Thus the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> preserved David whithersoever he went.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And David took the shields of gold that were on the servants of Hadarezer, and brought them to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Likewise from Tibhath, and from Chun, cities of Hadarezer, brought David very much brass, wherewith Solomon made the brasen sea, and the pillars, and the vessels of brass.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Now when Tou king of Hamath heard how David had smitten all the host of Hadarezer king of Zobah;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He sent Hadoram his son to king David, to enquire of his welfare, and to congratulate him, because he had fought against Hadarezer, and smitten him; (for Hadarezer had war with Tou;) and _with him_ all manner of vessels of gold and silver and brass.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Them also king David dedicated unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, with the silver and the gold that he brought from all _these_ nations; from Edom, and from Moab, and from the children of Ammon, and from the Philistines, and from Amalek.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Moreover Abishai the son of Zeruiah slew of the Edomites in the valley of salt eighteen thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And he put garrisons in Edom; and all the Edomites became David’s servants. Thus the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> preserved David whithersoever he went.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ So David reigned over all Israel, and executed judgment and justice among all his people.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Joab the son of Zeruiah _was_ over the host; and Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud, recorder.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Zadok the son of Ahitub, and Abimelech the son of Abiathar, _were_ the priests; and Shavsha was scribe;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And Benaiah the son of Jehoiada _was_ over the Cherethites and the Pelethites; and the sons of David _were_ chief about the king.  

## CHAPTER 19.

Now it came to pass after this, that Nahash the king of the children of Ammon died, and his son reigned in his stead.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And David said, I will shew kindness unto Hanun the son of Nahash, because his father shewed kindness to me. And David sent messengers to comfort him concerning his father. So the servants of David came into the land of the children of Ammon to Hanun, to comfort him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But the princes of the children of Ammon said to Hanun, Thinkest thou that David doth honour thy father, that he hath sent comforters unto thee? are not his servants come unto thee for to search, and to overthrow, and to spy out the land?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Wherefore Hanun took David’s servants, and shaved them, and cut off their garments in the midst hard by their buttocks, and sent them away.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Then there went _certain_, and told David how the men were served. And he sent to meet them: for the men were greatly ashamed. And the king said, Tarry at Jericho until your beards be grown, and _then_ return.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And when the children of Ammon saw that they had made themselves odious to David, Hanun and the children of Ammon sent a thousand talents of silver to hire them chariots and horsemen out of Mesopotamia, and out of Syria–maachah, and out of Zobah.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>So they hired thirty and two thousand chariots, and the king of Maachah and his people; who came and pitched before Medeba. And the children of Ammon gathered themselves together from their cities, and came to battle.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And when David heard _of it_, he sent Joab, and all the host of the mighty men.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the children of Ammon came out, and put the battle in array before the gate of the city: and the kings that were come _were_ by themselves in the field.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Now when Joab saw that the battle was set against him before and behind, he chose out of all the choice of Israel, and put _them_ in array against the Syrians.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the rest of the people he delivered unto the hand of Abishai his brother, and they set _themselves_ in array against the children of Ammon.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he said, If the Syrians be too strong for me, then thou shalt help me: but if the children of Ammon be too strong for thee, then I will help thee.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Be of good courage, and let us behave ourselves valiantly for our people, and for the cities of our God: and let the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> do _that which is_ good in his sight.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>So Joab and the people that _were_ with him drew nigh before the Syrians unto the battle; and they fled before him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And when the children of Ammon saw that the Syrians were fled, they likewise fled before Abishai his brother, and entered into the city. Then Joab came to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And when the Syrians saw that they were put to the worse before Israel, they sent messengers, and drew forth the Syrians that _were_ beyond the river: and Shophach the captain of the host of Hadarezer _went_ before them.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And it was told David; and he gathered all Israel, and passed over Jordan, and came upon them, and set _the battle_ in array against them. So when David had put the battle in array against the Syrians, they fought with him.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But the Syrians fled before Israel; and David slew of the Syrians seven thousand _men which fought in_ chariots, and forty thousand footmen, and killed Shophach the captain of the host.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And when the servants of Hadarezer saw that they were put to the worse before Israel, they made peace with David, and became his servants: neither would the Syrians help the children of Ammon any more.  

## CHAPTER 20.

And it came to pass, that after the year was expired, at the time that kings go out _to battle_, Joab led forth the power of the army, and wasted the country of the children of Ammon, and came and besieged Rabbah. But David tarried at Jerusalem. And Joab smote Rabbah, and destroyed it.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And David took the crown of their king from off his head, and found it to weigh a talent of gold, and _there were_ precious stones in it; and it was set upon David’s head: and he brought also exceeding much spoil out of the city.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he brought out the people that _were_ in it, and cut _them_ with saws, and with harrows of iron, and with axes. Even so dealt David with all the cities of the children of Ammon. And David and all the people returned to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass after this, that there arose war at Gezer with the Philistines; at which time Sibbechai the Hushathite slew Sippai, _that was_ of the children of the giant: and they were subdued.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And there was war again with the Philistines; and Elhanan the son of Jair slew Lahmi the brother of Goliath the Gittite, whose spear staff _was_ like a weaver’s beam.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And yet again there was war at Gath, where was a man of _great_ stature, whose fingers and toes _were_ four and twenty, six _on each hand_, and six _on each foot_: and he also was the son of the giant.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But when he defied Israel, Jonathan the son of Shimea David’s brother slew him.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>These were born unto the giant in Gath; and they fell by the hand of David, and by the hand of his servants.  

## CHAPTER 21.

And Satan stood up against Israel, and provoked David to number Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And David said to Joab and to the rulers of the people, Go, number Israel from Beer–sheba even to Dan; and bring the number of them to me, that I may know _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Joab answered, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> make his people an hundred times so many more as they _be_: but, my lord the king, _are_ they not all my lord’s servants? why then doth my lord require this thing? why will he be a cause of trespass to Israel?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Nevertheless the king’s word prevailed against Joab. Wherefore Joab departed, and went throughout all Israel, and came to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And Joab gave the sum of the number of the people unto David. And all _they of_ Israel were a thousand thousand and an hundred thousand men that drew sword: and Judah _was_ four hundred threescore and ten thousand men that drew sword.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But Levi and Benjamin counted he not among them: for the king’s word was abominable to Joab.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And God was displeased with this thing; therefore he smote Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And David said unto God, I have sinned greatly, because I have done this thing: but now, I beseech thee, do away the iniquity of thy servant; for I have done very foolishly.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Gad, David’s seer, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Go and tell David, saying, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I offer thee three _things_: choose thee one of them, that I may do _it_ unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>So Gad came to David, and said unto him, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Choose thee  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Either three years’ famine; or three months to be destroyed before thy foes, while that the sword of thine enemies overtaketh _thee_; or else three days the sword of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, even the pestilence, in the land, and the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> destroying throughout all the coasts of Israel. Now therefore advise thyself what word I shall bring again to him that sent me.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And David said unto Gad, I am in a great strait: let me fall now into the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; for very great _are_ his mercies: but let me not fall into the hand of man.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ So the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> sent pestilence upon Israel: and there fell of Israel seventy thousand men.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And God sent an angel unto Jerusalem to destroy it: and as he was destroying, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> beheld, and he repented him of the evil, and said to the angel that destroyed, It is enough, stay now thine hand. And the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> stood by the threshingfloor of Ornan the Jebusite.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And David lifted up his eyes, and saw the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> stand between the earth and the heaven, having a drawn sword in his hand stretched out over Jerusalem. Then David and the elders _of Israel, who were_ clothed in sackcloth, fell upon their faces.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And David said unto God, _Is it_ not I _that_ commanded the people to be numbered? even I it is that have sinned and done evil indeed; but _as for_ these sheep, what have they done? let thine hand, I pray thee, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> my God, be on me, and on my father’s house; but not on thy people, that they should be plagued.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Then the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Gad to say to David, that David should go up, and set up an altar unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the threshingfloor of Ornan the Jebusite.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And David went up at the saying of Gad, which he spake in the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Ornan turned back, and saw the angel; and his four sons with him hid themselves. Now Ornan was threshing wheat.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And as David came to Ornan, Ornan looked and saw David, and went out of the threshingfloor, and bowed himself to David with _his_ face to the ground.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Then David said to Ornan, Grant me the place of _this_ threshingfloor, that I may build an altar therein unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: thou shalt grant it me for the full price: that the plague may be stayed from the people.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And Ornan said unto David, Take _it_ to thee, and let my lord the king do _that which is_ good in his eyes: lo, I give _thee_ the oxen _also_ for burnt offerings, and the threshing instruments for wood, and the wheat for the meat offering; I give it all.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And king David said to Ornan, Nay; but I will verily buy it for the full price: for I will not take _that_ which _is_ thine for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, nor offer burnt offerings without cost.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>So David gave to Ornan for the place six hundred shekels of gold by weight.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And David built there an altar unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and offered burnt offerings and peace offerings, and called upon the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and he answered him from heaven by fire upon the altar of burnt offering.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded the angel; and he put up his sword again into the sheath thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ At that time when David saw that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had answered him in the threshingfloor of Ornan the Jebusite, then he sacrificed there.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For the tabernacle of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, which Moses made in the wilderness, and the altar of the burnt offering, _were_ at that season in the high place at Gibeon.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>But David could not go before it to enquire of God: for he was afraid because of the sword of the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 22.

Then David said, This _is_ the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God, and this _is_ the altar of the burnt offering for Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And David commanded to gather together the strangers that _were_ in the land of Israel; and he set masons to hew wrought stones to build the house of God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And David prepared iron in abundance for the nails for the doors of the gates, and for the joinings; and brass in abundance without weight;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Also cedar trees in abundance: for the Zidonians and they of Tyre brought much cedar wood to David.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And David said, Solomon my son _is_ young and tender, and the house _that is_ to be builded for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _must be_ exceeding magnifical, of fame and of glory throughout all countries: I will _therefore_ now make preparation for it. So David prepared abundantly before his death.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Then he called for Solomon his son, and charged him to build an house for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And David said to Solomon, My son, as for me, it was in my mind to build an house unto the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> my God:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came to me, saying, Thou hast shed blood abundantly, and hast made great wars: thou shalt not build an house unto my name, because thou hast shed much blood upon the earth in my sight.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Behold, a son shall be born to thee, who shall be a man of rest; and I will give him rest from all his enemies round about: for his name shall be Solomon, and I will give peace and quietness unto Israel in his days.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He shall build an house for my name; and he shall be my son, and I _will be_ his father; and I will establish the throne of his kingdom over Israel for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Now, my son, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> be with thee; and prosper thou, and build the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, as he hath said of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Only the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> give thee wisdom and understanding, and give thee charge concerning Israel, that thou mayest keep the law of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou prosper, if thou takest heed to fulfil the statutes and judgments which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> charged Moses with concerning Israel: be strong, and of good courage; dread not, nor be dismayed.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Now, behold, in my trouble I have prepared for the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> an hundred thousand talents of gold, and a thousand thousand talents of silver; and of brass and iron without weight; for it is in abundance: timber also and stone have I prepared; and thou mayest add thereto.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Moreover _there are_ workmen with thee in abundance, hewers and workers of stone and timber, and all manner of cunning men for every manner of work.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Of the gold, the silver, and the brass, and the iron, _there is_ no number. Arise _therefore_, and be doing, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> be with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ David also commanded all the princes of Israel to help Solomon his son, _saying_,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>_Is_ not the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God with you? and hath he _not_ given you rest on every side? for he hath given the inhabitants of the land into mine hand; and the land is subdued before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and before his people.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Now set your heart and your soul to seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God; arise therefore, and build ye the sanctuary of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God, to bring the ark of the covenant of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and the holy vessels of God, into the house that is to be built to the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 23.

So when David was old and full of days, he made Solomon his son king over Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ And he gathered together all the princes of Israel, with the priests and the Levites.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Now the Levites were numbered from the age of thirty years and upward: and their number by their polls, man by man, was thirty and eight thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Of which, twenty and four thousand _were_ to set forward the work of the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and six thousand _were_ officers and judges:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Moreover four thousand _were_ porters; and four thousand praised the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> with the instruments which I made, _said David_, to praise _therewith_.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And David divided them into courses among the sons of Levi, _namely_, Gershon, Kohath, and Merari.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Of the Gershonites _were_, Laadan, and Shimei.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The sons of Laadan; the chief _was_ Jehiel, and Zetham, and Joel, three.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The sons of Shimei; Shelomith, and Haziel, and Haran, three. These _were_ the chief of the fathers of Laadan.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the sons of Shimei _were_, Jahath, Zina, and Jeush, and Beriah. These four _were_ the sons of Shimei.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Jahath was the chief, and Zizah the second: but Jeush and Beriah had not many sons; therefore they were in one reckoning, according to _their_ father’s house.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Kohath; Amram, Izhar, Hebron, and Uzziel, four.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The sons of Amram; Aaron and Moses: and Aaron was separated, that he should sanctify the most holy things, he and his sons for ever, to burn incense before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to minister unto him, and to bless in his name for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Now _concerning_ Moses the man of God, his sons were named of the tribe of Levi.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The sons of Moses _were_, Gershom, and Eliezer.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Gershom, Shebuel _was_ the chief.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the sons of Eliezer _were_, Rehabiah the chief. And Eliezer had none other sons; but the sons of Rehabiah were very many.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Izhar; Shelomith the chief.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Hebron; Jeriah the first, Amariah the second, Jahaziel the third, and Jekameam the fourth.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Of the sons of Uzziel; Michah the first, and Jesiah the second.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ The sons of Merari; Mahli, and Mushi. The sons of Mahli; Eleazar, and Kish.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Eleazar died, and had no sons, but daughters: and their brethren the sons of Kish took them.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The sons of Mushi; Mahli, and Eder, and Jeremoth, three.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ These _were_ the sons of Levi after the house of their fathers; _even_ the chief of the fathers, as they were counted by number of names by their polls, that did the work for the service of the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, from the age of twenty years and upward.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For David said, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel hath given rest unto his people, that they may dwell in Jerusalem for ever:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And also unto the Levites; they shall no _more_ carry the tabernacle, nor any vessels of it for the service thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For by the last words of David the Levites _were_ numbered from twenty years old and above:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Because their office _was_ to wait on the sons of Aaron for the service of the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, in the courts, and in the chambers, and in the purifying of all holy things, and the work of the service of the house of God;  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Both for the shewbread, and for the fine flour for meat offering, and for the unleavened cakes, and for _that which is baked in_ the pan, and for that which is fried, and for all manner of measure and size;  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And to stand every morning to thank and praise the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and likewise at even;  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And to offer all burnt sacrifices unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the sabbaths, in the new moons, and on the set feasts, by number, according to the order commanded unto them, continually before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And that they should keep the charge of the tabernacle of the congregation, and the charge of the holy _place_, and the charge of the sons of Aaron their brethren, in the service of the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 24.

Now _these are_ the divisions of the sons of Aaron. The sons of Aaron; Nadab, and Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>But Nadab and Abihu died before their father, and had no children: therefore Eleazar and Ithamar executed the priest’s office.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And David distributed them, both Zadok of the sons of Eleazar, and Ahimelech of the sons of Ithamar, according to their offices in their service.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And there were more chief men found of the sons of Eleazar than of the sons of Ithamar; and _thus_ were they divided. Among the sons of Eleazar _there were_ sixteen chief men of the house of _their_ fathers, and eight among the sons of Ithamar according to the house of their fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Thus were they divided by lot, one sort with another; for the governors of the sanctuary, and governors _of the house_ of God, were of the sons of Eleazar, and of the sons of Ithamar.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Shemaiah the son of Nethaneel the scribe, _one_ of the Levites, wrote them before the king, and the princes, and Zadok the priest, and Ahimelech the son of Abiathar, and _before_ the chief of the fathers of the priests and Levites: one principal household being taken for Eleazar, and _one_ taken for Ithamar.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Now the first lot came forth to Jehoiarib, the second to Jedaiah,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The third to Harim, the fourth to Seorim,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The fifth to Malchijah, the sixth to Mijamin,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The seventh to Hakkoz, the eighth to Abijah,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The ninth to Jeshua, the tenth to Shecaniah,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The eleventh to Eliashib, the twelfth to Jakim,  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The thirteenth to Huppah, the fourteenth to Jeshebeab,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The fifteenth to Bilgah, the sixteenth to Immer,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The seventeenth to Hezir, the eighteenth to Aphses,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>The nineteenth to Pethahiah, the twentieth to Jehezekel,  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>The one and twentieth to Jachin, the two and twentieth to Gamul,

<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they with whom _precious_ stones were found gave _them_ to the treasure of the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, by the hand of Jehiel the Gershonite.

<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore David blessed the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> before all the congregation: and David said, Blessed _be_ thou, <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel our father, for ever and ever.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Thine, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _is_ the greatness, and the power, and the glory, and the victory, and the majesty: for all _that is_ in the heaven and in the earth _is thine_; thine _is_ the kingdom, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and thou art exalted as head above all.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Both riches and honour _come_ of thee, and thou reignest over all; and in thine hand _is_ power and might; and in thine hand _it is_ to make great, and to give strength unto all.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Now therefore, our God, we thank thee, and praise thy glorious name.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But who _am_ I, and what _is_ my people, that we should be able to offer so willingly after this sort? for all things _come_ of thee, and of thine own have we given thee.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For we _are_ strangers before thee, and sojourners, as _were_ all our fathers: our days on the earth _are_ as a shadow, and _there is_ none abiding.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God, all this store that we have prepared to build thee an house for thine holy name _cometh_ of thine hand, and _is_ all thine own.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>I know also, my God, that thou triest the heart, and hast pleasure in uprightness. As for me, in the uprightness of mine heart I have willingly offered all these things: and now have I seen with joy thy people, which are present here, to offer willingly unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Abraham, Isaac, and of Israel, our fathers, keep this for ever in the imagination of the thoughts of the heart of thy people, and prepare their heart unto thee:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And give unto Solomon my son a perfect heart, to keep thy commandments, thy testimonies, and thy statutes, and to do all _these things_, and to build the palace, _for_ the which I have made provision.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And David said to all the congregation, Now bless the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God. And all the congregation blessed the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of their fathers, and bowed down their heads, and worshipped the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and the king.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And they sacrificed sacrifices unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and offered burnt offerings unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, on the morrow after that day, _even_ a thousand bullocks, a thousand rams, _and_ a thousand lambs, with their drink offerings, and sacrifices in abundance for all Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And did eat and drink before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> on that day with great gladness. And they made Solomon the son of David king the second time, and anointed _him_ unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _to be_ the chief governor, and Zadok _to be_ priest.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Then Solomon sat on the throne of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> as king instead of David his father, and prospered; and all Israel obeyed him.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And all the princes, and the mighty men, and all the sons likewise of king David, submitted themselves unto Solomon the king.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> magnified Solomon exceedingly in the sight of all Israel, and bestowed upon him _such_ royal majesty as had not been on any king before him in Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ Thus David the son of Jesse reigned over all Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And the time that he reigned over Israel _was_ forty years; seven years reigned he in Hebron, and thirty and three _years_ reigned he in Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And he died in a good old age, full of days, riches, and honour: and Solomon his son reigned in his stead.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Now the acts of David the king, first and last, behold, they _are_ written in the book of Samuel the seer, and in the book of Nathan the prophet, and in the book of Gad the seer,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>With all his reign and his might, and the times that went over him, and over Israel, and over all the kingdoms of the countries.
