# Philemon

### THE EPISTLE OF PAUL THE APOSTLE TO PHILEMON

Paul, a prisoner of Jesus Christ, and Timothy _our_ brother, unto Philemon our dearly beloved, and fellowlabourer,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And to _our_ beloved Apphia, and Archippus our fellowsoldier, and to the church in thy house:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ I thank my God, making mention of thee always in my prayers,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Hearing of thy love and faith, which thou hast toward the Lord Jesus, and toward all saints;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>That the communication of thy faith may become effectual by the acknowledging of every good thing which is in you in Christ Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For we have great joy and consolation in thy love, because the bowels of the saints are refreshed by thee, brother.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Wherefore, though I might be much bold in Christ to enjoin thee that which is convenient,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Yet for love’s sake I rather beseech _thee_, being such an one as Paul the aged, and now also a prisoner of Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>I beseech thee for my son Onesimus, whom I have begotten in my bonds:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Which in time past was to thee unprofitable, but now profitable to thee and to me:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Whom I have sent again: thou therefore receive him, that is, mine own bowels:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Whom I would have retained with me, that in thy stead he might have ministered unto me in the bonds of the gospel:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But without thy mind would I do nothing; that thy benefit should not be as it were of necessity, but willingly.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For perhaps he therefore departed for a season, that thou shouldest receive him for ever;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved, specially to me, but how much more unto thee, both in the flesh, and in the Lord?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>If thou count me therefore a partner, receive him as myself.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>If he hath wronged thee, or oweth _thee_ ought, put that on mine account;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>I Paul have written _it_ with mine own hand, I will repay _it_: albeit I do not say to thee how thou owest unto me even thine own self besides.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Yea, brother, let me have joy of thee in the Lord: refresh my bowels in the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Having confidence in thy obedience I wrote unto thee, knowing that thou wilt also do more than I say.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But withal prepare me also a lodging: for I trust that through your prayers I shall be given unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ There salute thee Epaphras, my fellowprisoner in Christ Jesus;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Marcus, Aristarchus, Demas, Lucas, my fellowlabourers.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ _be_ with your spirit. Amen.  

<div class="colophon">Written from Rome to Philemon, by Onesimus a servant.</div>
