# Exodus

### THE SECOND BOOK OF MOSES CALLED EXODUS

## CHAPTER 1.

Now these _are_ the names of the children of Israel, which came into Egypt; every man and his household came with Jacob.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Reuben, Simeon, Levi, and Judah,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Issachar, Zebulun, and Benjamin,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Dan, and Naphtali, Gad, and Asher.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And all the souls that came out of the loins of Jacob were seventy souls: for Joseph was in Egypt _already_.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Joseph died, and all his brethren, and all that generation.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And the children of Israel were fruitful, and increased abundantly, and multiplied, and waxed exceeding mighty; and the land was filled with them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Now there arose up a new king over Egypt, which knew not Joseph.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he said unto his people, Behold, the people of the children of Israel _are_ more and mightier than we:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Come on, let us deal wisely with them; lest they multiply, and it come to pass, that, when there falleth out any war, they join also unto our enemies, and fight against us, and _so_ get them up out of the land.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Therefore they did set over them taskmasters to afflict them with their burdens. And they built for Pharaoh treasure cities, Pithom and Raamses.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But the more they afflicted them, the more they multiplied and grew. And they were grieved because of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the Egyptians made the children of Israel to serve with rigour:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they made their lives bitter with hard bondage, in morter, and in brick, and in all manner of service in the field: all their service, wherein they made them serve, _was_ with rigour.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And the king of Egypt spake to the Hebrew midwives, of which the name of the one _was_ Shiphrah, and the name of the other Puah:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And he said, When ye do the office of a midwife to the Hebrew women, and see _them_ upon the stools; if it _be_ a son, then ye shall kill him: but if it _be_ a daughter, then she shall live.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But the midwives feared God, and did not as the king of Egypt commanded them, but saved the men children alive.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the king of Egypt called for the midwives, and said unto them, Why have ye done this thing, and have saved the men children alive?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the midwives said unto Pharaoh, Because the Hebrew women _are_ not as the Egyptian women; for they _are_ lively, and are delivered ere the midwives come in unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Therefore God dealt well with the midwives: and the people multiplied, and waxed very mighty.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, because the midwives feared God, that he made them houses.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh charged all his people, saying, Every son that is born ye shall cast into the river, and every daughter ye shall save alive.  

## CHAPTER 2.

And there went a man of the house of Levi, and took _to wife_ a daughter of Levi.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the woman conceived, and bare a son: and when she saw him that he _was a_ goodly _child_, she hid him three months.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And when she could not longer hide him, she took for him an ark of bulrushes, and daubed it with slime and with pitch, and put the child therein; and she laid _it_ in the flags by the river’s brink.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And his sister stood afar off, to wit what would be done to him.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And the daughter of Pharaoh came down to wash _herself_ at the river; and her maidens walked along by the river’s side; and when she saw the ark among the flags, she sent her maid to fetch it.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And when she had opened _it_, she saw the child: and, behold, the babe wept. And she had compassion on him, and said, This _is one_ of the Hebrews’ children.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then said his sister to Pharaoh’s daughter, Shall I go and call to thee a nurse of the Hebrew women, that she may nurse the child for thee?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh’s daughter said to her, Go. And the maid went and called the child’s mother.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh’s daughter said unto her, Take this child away, and nurse it for me, and I will give _thee_ thy wages. And the woman took the child, and nursed it.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the child grew, and she brought him unto Pharaoh’s daughter, and he became her son. And she called his name Moses: and she said, Because I drew him out of the water.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass in those days, when Moses was grown, that he went out unto his brethren, and looked on their burdens: and he spied an Egyptian smiting an Hebrew, one of his brethren.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he looked this way and that way, and when he saw that _there was_ no man, he slew the Egyptian, and hid him in the sand.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And when he went out the second day, behold, two men of the Hebrews strove together: and he said to him that did the wrong, Wherefore smitest thou thy fellow?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he said, Who made thee a prince and a judge over us? intendest thou to kill me, as thou killedst the Egyptian? And Moses feared, and said, Surely this thing is known.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Now when Pharaoh heard this thing, he sought to slay Moses. But Moses fled from the face of Pharaoh, and dwelt in the land of Midian: and he sat down by a well.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Now the priest of Midian had seven daughters: and they came and drew _water_, and filled the troughs to water their father’s flock.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the shepherds came and drove them away: but Moses stood up and helped them, and watered their flock.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And when they came to Reuel their father, he said, How _is it that_ ye are come so soon to day?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And they said, An Egyptian delivered us out of the hand of the shepherds, and also drew _water_ enough for us, and watered the flock.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And he said unto his daughters, And where _is_ he? why _is_ it _that_ ye have left the man? call him, that he may eat bread.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Moses was content to dwell with the man: and he gave Moses Zipporah his daughter.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And she bare _him_ a son, and he called his name Gershom: for he said, I have been a stranger in a strange land.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass in process of time, that the king of Egypt died: and the children of Israel sighed by reason of the bondage, and they cried, and their cry came up unto God by reason of the bondage.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And God heard their groaning, and God remembered his covenant with Abraham, with Isaac, and with Jacob.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And God looked upon the children of Israel, and God had respect unto _them_.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Now Moses kept the flock of Jethro his father in law, the priest of Midian: and he led the flock to the backside of the desert, and came to the mountain of God, _even_ to Horeb.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> appeared unto him in a flame of fire out of the midst of a bush: and he looked, and, behold, the bush burned with fire, and the bush _was_ not consumed.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Moses said, I will now turn aside, and see this great sight, why the bush is not burnt.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And when the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> saw that he turned aside to see, God called unto him out of the midst of the bush, and said, Moses, Moses. And he said, Here _am_ I.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And he said, Draw not nigh hither: put off thy shoes from off thy feet, for the place whereon thou standest _is_ holy ground.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Moreover he said, I _am_ the God of thy father, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob. And Moses hid his face; for he was afraid to look upon God.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said, I have surely seen the affliction of my people which _are_ in Egypt, and have heard their cry by reason of their taskmasters; for I know their sorrows;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And I am come down to deliver them out of the hand of the Egyptians, and to bring them up out of that land unto a good land and a large, unto a land flowing with milk and honey; unto the place of the Canaanites, and the Hittites, and the Amorites, and the Perizzites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Now therefore, behold, the cry of the children of Israel is come unto me: and I have also seen the oppression wherewith the Egyptians oppress them.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Come now therefore, and I will send thee unto Pharaoh, that thou mayest bring forth my people the children of Israel out of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto God, Who _am_ I, that I should go unto Pharaoh, and that I should bring forth the children of Israel out of Egypt?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he said, Certainly I will be with thee; and this _shall be_ a token unto thee, that I have sent thee: When thou hast brought forth the people out of Egypt, ye shall serve God upon this mountain.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto God, Behold, _when_ I come unto the children of Israel, and shall say unto them, The God of your fathers hath sent me unto you; and they shall say to me, What _is_ his name? what shall I say unto them?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And God said unto Moses, I AM THAT I AM: and he said, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, I AM hath sent me unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And God said moreover unto Moses, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of your fathers, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob, hath sent me unto you: this _is_ my name for ever, and this _is_ my memorial unto all generations.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Go, and gather the elders of Israel together, and say unto them, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of your fathers, the God of Abraham, of Isaac, and of Jacob, appeared unto me, saying, I have surely visited you, and _seen_ that which is done to you in Egypt:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And I have said, I will bring you up out of the affliction of Egypt unto the land of the Canaanites, and the Hittites, and the Amorites, and the Perizzites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites, unto a land flowing with milk and honey.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they shall hearken to thy voice: and thou shalt come, thou and the elders of Israel, unto the king of Egypt, and ye shall say unto him, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of the Hebrews hath met with us: and now let us go, we beseech thee, three days’ journey into the wilderness, that we may sacrifice to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And I am sure that the king of Egypt will not let you go, no, not by a mighty hand.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And I will stretch out my hand, and smite Egypt with all my wonders which I will do in the midst thereof: and after that he will let you go.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And I will give this people favour in the sight of the Egyptians: and it shall come to pass, that, when ye go, ye shall not go empty:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But every woman shall borrow of her neighbour, and of her that sojourneth in her house, jewels of silver, and jewels of gold, and raiment: and ye shall put _them_ upon your sons, and upon your daughters; and ye shall spoil the Egyptians.  

## CHAPTER 4.

And Moses answered and said, But, behold, they will not believe me, nor hearken unto my voice: for they will say, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath not appeared unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto him, What _is_ that in thine hand? And he said, A rod.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he said, Cast it on the ground. And he cast it on the ground, and it became a serpent; and Moses fled from before it.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Put forth thine hand, and take it by the tail. And he put forth his hand, and caught it, and it became a rod in his hand:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>That they may believe that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of their fathers, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob, hath appeared unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said furthermore unto him, Put now thine hand into thy bosom. And he put his hand into his bosom: and when he took it out, behold, his hand _was_ leprous as snow.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he said, Put thine hand into thy bosom again. And he put his hand into his bosom again; and plucked it out of his bosom, and, behold, it was turned again as his _other_ flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, if they will not believe thee, neither hearken to the voice of the first sign, that they will believe the voice of the latter sign.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, if they will not believe also these two signs, neither hearken unto thy voice, that thou shalt take of the water of the river, and pour _it_ upon the dry _land_: and the water which thou takest out of the river shall become blood upon the dry _land_.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, O my Lord, I _am_ not eloquent, neither heretofore, nor since thou hast spoken unto thy servant: but I _am_ slow of speech, and of a slow tongue.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto him, Who hath made man’s mouth? or who maketh the dumb, or deaf, or the seeing, or the blind? have not I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Now therefore go, and I will be with thy mouth, and teach thee what thou shalt say.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And he said, O my Lord, send, I pray thee, by the hand _of him whom_ thou wilt send.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the anger of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was kindled against Moses, and he said, _Is_ not Aaron the Levite thy brother? I know that he can speak well. And also, behold, he cometh forth to meet thee: and when he seeth thee, he will be glad in his heart.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And thou shalt speak unto him, and put words in his mouth: and I will be with thy mouth, and with his mouth, and will teach you what ye shall do.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And he shall be thy spokesman unto the people: and he shall be, _even_ he shall be to thee instead of a mouth, and thou shalt be to him instead of God.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take this rod in thine hand, wherewith thou shalt do signs.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses went and returned to Jethro his father in law, and said unto him, Let me go, I pray thee, and return unto my brethren which _are_ in Egypt, and see whether they be yet alive. And Jethro said to Moses, Go in peace.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses in Midian, Go, return into Egypt: for all the men are dead which sought thy life.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Moses took his wife and his sons, and set them upon an ass, and he returned to the land of Egypt: and Moses took the rod of God in his hand.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, When thou goest to return into Egypt, see that thou do all those wonders before Pharaoh, which I have put in thine hand: but I will harden his heart, that he shall not let the people go.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And thou shalt say unto Pharaoh, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Israel _is_ my son, _even_ my firstborn:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And I say unto thee, Let my son go, that he may serve me: and if thou refuse to let him go, behold, I will slay thy son, _even_ thy firstborn.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass by the way in the inn, that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> met him, and sought to kill him.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Then Zipporah took a sharp stone, and cut off the foreskin of her son, and cast _it_ at his feet, and said, Surely a bloody husband _art_ thou to me.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>So he let him go: then she said, A bloody husband _thou art_, because of the circumcision.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said to Aaron, Go into the wilderness to meet Moses. And he went, and met him in the mount of God, and kissed him.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And Moses told Aaron all the words of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> who had sent him, and all the signs which he had commanded him.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses and Aaron went and gathered together all the elders of the children of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Aaron spake all the words which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had spoken unto Moses, and did the signs in the sight of the people.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the people believed: and when they heard that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had visited the children of Israel, and that he had looked upon their affliction, then they bowed their heads and worshipped.  

## CHAPTER 5.

And afterward Moses and Aaron went in, and told Pharaoh, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel, Let my people go, that they may hold a feast unto me in the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh said, Who _is_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that I should obey his voice to let Israel go? I know not the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, neither will I let Israel go.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And they said, The God of the Hebrews hath met with us: let us go, we pray thee, three days’ journey into the desert, and sacrifice unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God; lest he fall upon us with pestilence, or with the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the king of Egypt said unto them, Wherefore do ye, Moses and Aaron, let the people from their works? get you unto your burdens.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh said, Behold, the people of the land now _are_ many, and ye make them rest from their burdens.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh commanded the same day the taskmasters of the people, and their officers, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Ye shall no more give the people straw to make brick, as heretofore: let them go and gather straw for themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the tale of the bricks, which they did make heretofore, ye shall lay upon them; ye shall not diminish _ought_ thereof: for they _be_ idle; therefore they cry, saying, Let us go _and_ sacrifice to our God.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Let there more work be laid upon the men, that they may labour therein; and let them not regard vain words.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the taskmasters of the people went out, and their officers, and they spake to the people, saying, Thus saith Pharaoh, I will not give you straw.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Go ye, get you straw where ye can find it: yet not ought of your work shall be diminished.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>So the people were scattered abroad throughout all the land of Egypt to gather stubble instead of straw.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the taskmasters hasted _them_, saying, Fulfil your works, _your_ daily tasks, as when there was straw.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the officers of the children of Israel, which Pharaoh’s taskmasters had set over them, were beaten, _and_ demanded, Wherefore have ye not fulfilled your task in making brick both yesterday and to day, as heretofore?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Then the officers of the children of Israel came and cried unto Pharaoh, saying, Wherefore dealest thou thus with thy servants?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>There is no straw given unto thy servants, and they say to us, Make brick: and, behold, thy servants _are_ beaten; but the fault _is_ in thine own people.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But he said, Ye _are_ idle, _ye are_ idle: therefore ye say, Let us go _and_ do sacrifice to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Go therefore now, _and_ work; for there shall no straw be given you, yet shall ye deliver the tale of bricks.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the officers of the children of Israel did see _that_ they _were_ in evil _case_, after it was said, Ye shall not minish _ought_ from your bricks of your daily task.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And they met Moses and Aaron, who stood in the way, as they came forth from Pharaoh:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And they said unto them, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> look upon you, and judge; because ye have made our savour to be abhorred in the eyes of Pharaoh, and in the eyes of his servants, to put a sword in their hand to slay us.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Moses returned unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, Lord, wherefore hast thou _so_ evil entreated this people? why _is_ it _that_ thou hast sent me?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For since I came to Pharaoh to speak in thy name, he hath done evil to this people; neither hast thou delivered thy people at all.  

## CHAPTER 6.

Then the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Now shalt thou see what I will do to Pharaoh: for with a strong hand shall he let them go, and with a strong hand shall he drive them out of his land.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And God spake unto Moses, and said unto him, I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And I appeared unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob, by _the name of_ God Almighty, but by my name <span class="small-caps">Jehovah</span> was I not known to them.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And I have also established my covenant with them, to give them the land of Canaan, the land of their pilgrimage, wherein they were strangers.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And I have also heard the groaning of the children of Israel, whom the Egyptians keep in bondage; and I have remembered my covenant.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Wherefore say unto the children of Israel, I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and I will bring you out from under the burdens of the Egyptians, and I will rid you out of their bondage, and I will redeem you with a stretched out arm, and with great judgments:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And I will take you to me for a people, and I will be to you a God: and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God, which bringeth you out from under the burdens of the Egyptians.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And I will bring you in unto the land, concerning the which I did swear to give it to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob; and I will give it you for an heritage: I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses spake so unto the children of Israel: but they hearkened not unto Moses for anguish of spirit, and for cruel bondage.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Go in, speak unto Pharaoh king of Egypt, that he let the children of Israel go out of his land.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Moses spake before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, Behold, the children of Israel have not hearkened unto me; how then shall Pharaoh hear me, who _am_ of uncircumcised lips?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and unto Aaron, and gave them a charge unto the children of Israel, and unto Pharaoh king of Egypt, to bring the children of Israel out of the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ These _be_ the heads of their fathers’ houses: The sons of Reuben the firstborn of Israel; Hanoch, and Pallu, Hezron, and Carmi: these _be_ the families of Reuben.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the sons of Simeon; Jemuel, and Jamin, and Ohad, and Jachin, and Zohar, and Shaul the son of a Canaanitish woman: these _are_ the families of Simeon.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And these _are_ the names of the sons of Levi according to their generations; Gershon, and Kohath, and Merari: and the years of the life of Levi _were_ an hundred thirty and seven years.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>The sons of Gershon; Libni, and Shimi, according to their families.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the sons of Kohath; Amram, and Izhar, and Hebron, and Uzziel: and the years of the life of Kohath _were_ an hundred thirty and three years.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the sons of Merari; Mahali and Mushi: these _are_ the families of Levi according to their generations.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Amram took him Jochebed his father’s sister to wife; and she bare him Aaron and Moses: and the years of the life of Amram _were_ an hundred and thirty and seven years.

<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the fish that _was_ in the river died; and the river stank, and the Egyptians could not drink of the water of the river; and there was blood throughout all the land of Egypt.

<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the river shall bring forth frogs abundantly, which shall go up and come into thine house, and into thy bedchamber, and upon thy bed, and into the house of thy servants, and upon thy people, and into thine ovens, and into thy kneadingtroughs:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the frogs shall come up both on thee, and upon thy people, and upon all thy servants.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, Say unto Aaron, Stretch forth thine hand with thy rod over the streams, over the rivers, and over the ponds, and cause frogs to come up upon the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Aaron stretched out his hand over the waters of Egypt; and the frogs came up, and covered the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the magicians did so with their enchantments, and brought up frogs upon the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Then Pharaoh called for Moses and Aaron, and said, Intreat the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that he may take away the frogs from me, and from my people; and I will let the people go, that they may do sacrifice unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto Pharaoh, Glory over me: when shall I intreat for thee, and for thy servants, and for thy people, to destroy the frogs from thee and thy houses, _that_ they may remain in the river only?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he said, To morrow. And he said, _Be it_ according to thy word: that thou mayest know that _there is_ none like unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the frogs shall depart from thee, and from thy houses, and from thy servants, and from thy people; they shall remain in the river only.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron went out from Pharaoh: and Moses cried unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> because of the frogs which he had brought against Pharaoh.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> did according to the word of Moses; and the frogs died out of the houses, out of the villages, and out of the fields.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they gathered them together upon heaps: and the land stank.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But when Pharaoh saw that there was respite, he hardened his heart, and hearkened not unto them; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had said.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Say unto Aaron, Stretch out thy rod, and smite the dust of the land, that it may become lice throughout all the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And they did so; for Aaron stretched out his hand with his rod, and smote the dust of the earth, and it became lice in man, and in beast; all the dust of the land became lice throughout all the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the magicians did so with their enchantments to bring forth lice, but they could not: so there were lice upon man, and upon beast.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Then the magicians said unto Pharaoh, This _is_ the finger of God: and Pharaoh’s heart was hardened, and he hearkened not unto them; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had said.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Rise up early in the morning, and stand before Pharaoh; lo, he cometh forth to the water; and say unto him, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Let my people go, that they may serve me.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Else, if thou wilt not let my people go, behold, I will send swarms _of flies_ upon thee, and upon thy servants, and upon thy people, and into thy houses: and the houses of the Egyptians shall be full of swarms _of flies_, and also the ground whereon they _are_.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And I will sever in that day the land of Goshen, in which my people dwell, that no swarms _of flies_ shall be there; to the end thou mayest know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the midst of the earth.

<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Moses went out from Pharaoh, and intreated the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> did according to the word of Moses; and he removed the swarms _of flies_ from Pharaoh, from his servants, and from his people; there remained not one.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh hardened his heart at this time also, neither would he let the people go.  

## CHAPTER 9.

Then the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Go in unto Pharaoh, and tell him, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of the Hebrews, Let my people go, that they may serve me.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For if thou refuse to let _them_ go, and wilt hold them still,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Behold, the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> is upon thy cattle which _is_ in the field, upon the horses, upon the asses, upon the camels, upon the oxen, and upon the sheep: _there shall be_ a very grievous murrain.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall sever between the cattle of Israel and the cattle of Egypt: and there shall nothing die of all _that is_ the children’s of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> appointed a set time, saying, To morrow the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall do this thing in the land.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> did that thing on the morrow, and all the cattle of Egypt died: but of the cattle of the children of Israel died not one.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh sent, and, behold, there was not one of the cattle of the Israelites dead. And the heart of Pharaoh was hardened, and he did not let the people go.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses and unto Aaron, Take to you handfuls of ashes of the furnace, and let Moses sprinkle it toward the heaven in the sight of Pharaoh.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And it shall become small dust in all the land of Egypt, and shall be a boil breaking forth _with_ blains upon man, and upon beast, throughout all the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they took ashes of the furnace, and stood before Pharaoh; and Moses sprinkled it up toward heaven; and it became a boil breaking forth _with_ blains upon man, and upon beast.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the magicians could not stand before Moses because of the boils; for the boil was upon the magicians, and upon all the Egyptians.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hardened the heart of Pharaoh, and he hearkened not unto them; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had spoken unto Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Rise up early in the morning, and stand before Pharaoh, and say unto him, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of the Hebrews, Let my people go, that they may serve me.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For I will at this time send all my plagues upon thine heart, and upon thy servants, and upon thy people; that thou mayest know that _there is_ none like me in all the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For now I will stretch out my hand, that I may smite thee and thy people with pestilence; and thou shalt be cut off from the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And in very deed for this _cause_ have I raised thee up, for to shew _in_ thee my power; and that my name may be declared throughout all the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>As yet exaltest thou thyself against my people, that thou wilt not let them go?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Behold, to morrow about this time I will cause it to rain a very grievous hail, such as hath not been in Egypt since the foundation thereof even until now.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Send therefore now, _and_ gather thy cattle, and all that thou hast in the field; _for upon_ every man and beast which shall be found in the field, and shall not be brought home, the hail shall come down upon them, and they shall die.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>He that feared the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> among the servants of Pharaoh made his servants and his cattle flee into the houses:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And he that regarded not the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> left his servants and his cattle in the field.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Stretch forth thine hand toward heaven, that there may be hail in all the land of Egypt, upon man, and upon beast, and upon every herb of the field, throughout the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And Moses stretched forth his rod toward heaven: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> sent thunder and hail, and the fire ran along upon the ground; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> rained hail upon the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>So there was hail, and fire mingled with the hail, very grievous, such as there was none like it in all the land of Egypt since it became a nation.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the hail smote throughout all the land of Egypt all that _was_ in the field, both man and beast; and the hail smote every herb of the field, and brake every tree of the field.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Only in the land of Goshen, where the children of Israel _were_, was there no hail.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And Pharaoh sent, and called for Moses and Aaron, and said unto them, I have sinned this time: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ righteous, and I and my people _are_ wicked.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Intreat the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> (for _it is_ enough) that there be no _more_ mighty thunderings and hail; and I will let you go, and ye shall stay no longer.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto him, As soon as I am gone out of the city, I will spread abroad my hands unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; _and_ the thunder shall cease, neither shall there be any more hail; that thou mayest know how that the earth _is_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>But as for thee and thy servants, I know that ye will not yet fear the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the flax and the barley was smitten: for the barley _was_ in the ear, and the flax _was_ bolled.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>But the wheat and the rie were not smitten: for they _were_ not grown up.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And Moses went out of the city from Pharaoh, and spread abroad his hands unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and the thunders and hail ceased, and the rain was not poured upon the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And when Pharaoh saw that the rain and the hail and the thunders were ceased, he sinned yet more, and hardened his heart, he and his servants.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the heart of Pharaoh was hardened, neither would he let the children of Israel go; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had spoken by Moses.  

## CHAPTER 10.

<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And that thou mayest tell in the ears of thy son, and of thy son’s son, what things I have wrought in Egypt, and my signs which I have done among them; that ye may know how that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron came in unto Pharaoh, and said unto him, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of the Hebrews, How long wilt thou refuse to humble thyself before me? let my people go, that they may serve me.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Else, if thou refuse to let my people go, behold, to morrow will I bring the locusts into thy coast:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they shall cover the face of the earth, that one cannot be able to see the earth: and they shall eat the residue of that which is escaped, which remaineth unto you from the hail, and shall eat every tree which groweth for you out of the field:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they shall fill thy houses, and the houses of all thy servants, and the houses of all the Egyptians; which neither thy fathers, nor thy fathers’ fathers have seen, since the day that they were upon the earth unto this day. And he turned himself, and went out from Pharaoh.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh’s servants said unto him, How long shall this man be a snare unto us? let the men go, that they may serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God: knowest thou not yet that Egypt is destroyed?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron were brought again unto Pharaoh: and he said unto them, Go, serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God: _but_ who _are_ they that shall go?

<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Let the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> be so with you, as I will let you go, and your little ones: look _to it_; for evil _is_ before you.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Not so: go now ye _that are_ men, and serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; for that ye did desire. And they were driven out from Pharaoh’s presence.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Stretch out thine hand over the land of Egypt for the locusts, that they may come up upon the land of Egypt, and eat every herb of the land, _even_ all that the hail hath left.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Moses stretched forth his rod over the land of Egypt, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought an east wind upon the land all that day, and all _that_ night; _and_ when it was morning, the east wind brought the locusts.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the locusts went up over all the land of Egypt, and rested in all the coasts of Egypt: very grievous _were they_; before them there were no such locusts as they, neither after them shall be such.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For they covered the face of the whole earth, so that the land was darkened; and they did eat every herb of the land, and all the fruit of the trees which the hail had left: and there remained not any green thing in the trees, or in the herbs of the field, through all the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Then Pharaoh called for Moses and Aaron in haste; and he said, I have sinned against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God, and against you.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Now therefore forgive, I pray thee, my sin only this once, and intreat the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God, that he may take away from me this death only.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And he went out from Pharaoh, and intreated the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> turned a mighty strong west wind, which took away the locusts, and cast them into the Red sea; there remained not one locust in all the coasts of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hardened Pharaoh’s heart, so that he would not let the children of Israel go.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Stretch out thine hand toward heaven, that there may be darkness over the land of Egypt, even darkness _which_ may be felt.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Moses stretched forth his hand toward heaven; and there was a thick darkness in all the land of Egypt three days:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>They saw not one another, neither rose any from his place for three days: but all the children of Israel had light in their dwellings.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And Pharaoh called unto Moses, and said, Go ye, serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; only let your flocks and your herds be stayed: let your little ones also go with you.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And Moses said, Thou must give us also sacrifices and burnt offerings, that we may sacrifice unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Our cattle also shall go with us; there shall not an hoof be left behind; for thereof must we take to serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God; and we know not with what we must serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, until we come thither.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ But the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hardened Pharaoh’s heart, and he would not let them go.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh said unto him, Get thee from me, take heed to thyself, see my face no more; for in _that_ day thou seest my face thou shalt die.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And Moses said, Thou hast spoken well, I will see thy face again no more.  

## CHAPTER 11.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Yet will I bring one plague _more_ upon Pharaoh, and upon Egypt; afterwards he will let you go hence: when he shall let _you_ go, he shall surely thrust you out hence altogether.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Speak now in the ears of the people, and let every man borrow of his neighbour, and every woman of her neighbour, jewels of silver, and jewels of gold.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> gave the people favour in the sight of the Egyptians. Moreover the man Moses _was_ very great in the land of Egypt, in the sight of Pharaoh’s servants, and in the sight of the people.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, About midnight will I go out into the midst of Egypt:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And all the firstborn in the land of Egypt shall die, from the firstborn of Pharaoh that sitteth upon his throne, even unto the firstborn of the maidservant that _is_ behind the mill; and all the firstborn of beasts.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And there shall be a great cry throughout all the land of Egypt, such as there was none like it, nor shall be like it any more.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But against any of the children of Israel shall not a dog move his tongue, against man or beast: that ye may know how that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> doth put a difference between the Egyptians and Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And all these thy servants shall come down unto me, and bow down themselves unto me, saying, Get thee out, and all the people that follow thee: and after that I will go out. And he went out from Pharaoh in a great anger.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Pharaoh shall not hearken unto you; that my wonders may be multiplied in the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron did all these wonders before Pharaoh: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hardened Pharaoh’s heart, so that he would not let the children of Israel go out of his land.  

## CHAPTER 12.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses and Aaron in the land of Egypt, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>This month _shall be_ unto you the beginning of months: it _shall be_ the first month of the year to you.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Speak ye unto all the congregation of Israel, saying, In the tenth _day_ of this month they shall take to them every man a lamb, according to the house of _their_ fathers, a lamb for an house:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And if the household be too little for the lamb, let him and his neighbour next unto his house take _it_ according to the number of the souls; every man according to his eating shall make your count for the lamb.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Your lamb shall be without blemish, a male of the first year: ye shall take _it_ out from the sheep, or from the goats:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And ye shall keep it up until the fourteenth day of the same month: and the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall kill it in the evening.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they shall take of the blood, and strike _it_ on the two side posts and on the upper door post of the houses, wherein they shall eat it.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they shall eat the flesh in that night, roast with fire, and unleavened bread; _and_ with bitter _herbs_ they shall eat it.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Eat not of it raw, nor sodden at all with water, but roast _with_ fire; his head with his legs, and with the purtenance thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And ye shall let nothing of it remain until the morning; and that which remaineth of it until the morning ye shall burn with fire.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And thus shall ye eat it; _with_ your loins girded, your shoes on your feet, and your staff in your hand; and ye shall eat it in haste: it _is_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> passover.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For I will pass through the land of Egypt this night, and will smite all the firstborn in the land of Egypt, both man and beast; and against all the gods of Egypt I will execute judgment: I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the blood shall be to you for a token upon the houses where ye _are_: and when I see the blood, I will pass over you, and the plague shall not be upon you to destroy _you_, when I smite the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And this day shall be unto you for a memorial; and ye shall keep it a feast to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> throughout your generations; ye shall keep it a feast by an ordinance for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Seven days shall ye eat unleavened bread; even the first day ye shall put away leaven out of your houses: for whosoever eateth leavened bread from the first day until the seventh day, that soul shall be cut off from Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And in the first day _there shall be_ an holy convocation, and in the seventh day there shall be an holy convocation to you; no manner of work shall be done in them, save _that_ which every man must eat, that only may be done of you.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And ye shall observe _the feast of_ unleavened bread; for in this selfsame day have I brought your armies out of the land of Egypt: therefore shall ye observe this day in your generations by an ordinance for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ In the first _month_, on the fourteenth day of the month at even, ye shall eat unleavened bread, until the one and twentieth day of the month at even.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Seven days shall there be no leaven found in your houses: for whosoever eateth that which is leavened, even that soul shall be cut off from the congregation of Israel, whether he be a stranger, or born in the land.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Ye shall eat nothing leavened; in all your habitations shall ye eat unleavened bread.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Then Moses called for all the elders of Israel, and said unto them, Draw out and take you a lamb according to your families, and kill the passover.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And ye shall take a bunch of hyssop, and dip _it_ in the blood that _is_ in the bason, and strike the lintel and the two side posts with the blood that _is_ in the bason; and none of you shall go out at the door of his house until the morning.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will pass through to smite the Egyptians; and when he seeth the blood upon the lintel, and on the two side posts, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will pass over the door, and will not suffer the destroyer to come in unto your houses to smite _you_.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And ye shall observe this thing for an ordinance to thee and to thy sons for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, when ye be come to the land which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will give you, according as he hath promised, that ye shall keep this service.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, when your children shall say unto you, What mean ye by this service?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>That ye shall say, It _is_ the sacrifice of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> passover, who passed over the houses of the children of Israel in Egypt, when he smote the Egyptians, and delivered our houses. And the people bowed the head and worshipped.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel went away, and did as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had commanded Moses and Aaron, so did they.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, that at midnight the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> smote all the firstborn in the land of Egypt, from the firstborn of Pharaoh that sat on his throne unto the firstborn of the captive that _was_ in the dungeon; and all the firstborn of cattle.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Pharaoh rose up in the night, he, and all his servants, and all the Egyptians; and there was a great cry in Egypt; for _there was_ not a house where _there was_ not one dead.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And he called for Moses and Aaron by night, and said, Rise up, _and_ get you forth from among my people, both ye and the children of Israel; and go, serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as ye have said.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Also take your flocks and your herds, as ye have said, and be gone; and bless me also.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And the Egyptians were urgent upon the people, that they might send them out of the land in haste; for they said, We _be_ all dead _men_.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And the people took their dough before it was leavened, their kneadingtroughs being bound up in their clothes upon their shoulders.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel did according to the word of Moses; and they borrowed of the Egyptians jewels of silver, and jewels of gold, and raiment:  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> gave the people favour in the sight of the Egyptians, so that they lent unto them _such things as they required_. And they spoiled the Egyptians.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>¶ And the children of Israel journeyed from Rameses to Succoth, about six hundred thousand on foot _that were_ men, beside children.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And a mixed multitude went up also with them; and flocks, and herds, _even_ very much cattle.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And they baked unleavened cakes of the dough which they brought forth out of Egypt, for it was not leavened; because they were thrust out of Egypt, and could not tarry, neither had they prepared for themselves any victual.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>¶ Now the sojourning of the children of Israel, who dwelt in Egypt, _was_ four hundred and thirty years.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And it came to pass at the end of the four hundred and thirty years, even the selfsame day it came to pass, that all the hosts of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> went out from the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>It _is_ a night to be much observed unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> for bringing them out from the land of Egypt: this _is_ that night of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to be observed of all the children of Israel in their generations.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses and Aaron, This _is_ the ordinance of the passover: There shall no stranger eat thereof:  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>But every man’s servant that is bought for money, when thou hast circumcised him, then shall he eat thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>A foreigner and an hired servant shall not eat thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>In one house shall it be eaten; thou shalt not carry forth ought of the flesh abroad out of the house; neither shall ye break a bone thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>All the congregation of Israel shall keep it.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And when a stranger shall sojourn with thee, and will keep the passover to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, let all his males be circumcised, and then let him come near and keep it; and he shall be as one that is born in the land: for no uncircumcised person shall eat thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>One law shall be to him that is homeborn, and unto the stranger that sojourneth among you.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>Thus did all the children of Israel; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses and Aaron, so did they.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>And it came to pass the selfsame day, _that_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> did bring the children of Israel out of the land of Egypt by their armies.  

## CHAPTER 13.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Sanctify unto me all the firstborn, whatsoever openeth the womb among the children of Israel, _both_ of man and of beast: it _is_ mine.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto the people, Remember this day, in which ye came out from Egypt, out of the house of bondage; for by strength of hand the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought you out from this _place_: there shall no leavened bread be eaten.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>This day came ye out in the month Abib.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall be when the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall bring thee into the land of the Canaanites, and the Hittites, and the Amorites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites, which he sware unto thy fathers to give thee, a land flowing with milk and honey, that thou shalt keep this service in this month.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Seven days thou shalt eat unleavened bread, and in the seventh day _shall be_ a feast to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Unleavened bread shall be eaten seven days; and there shall no leavened bread be seen with thee, neither shall there be leaven seen with thee in all thy quarters.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt shew thy son in that day, saying, _This is done_ because of that _which_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> did unto me when I came forth out of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And it shall be for a sign unto thee upon thine hand, and for a memorial between thine eyes, that the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> law may be in thy mouth: for with a strong hand hath the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought thee out of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thou shalt therefore keep this ordinance in his season from year to year.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall be when the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall bring thee into the land of the Canaanites, as he sware unto thee and to thy fathers, and shall give it thee,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>That thou shalt set apart unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> all that openeth the matrix, and every firstling that cometh of a beast which thou hast; the males _shall be_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And every firstling of an ass thou shalt redeem with a lamb; and if thou wilt not redeem it, then thou shalt break his neck: and all the firstborn of man among thy children shalt thou redeem.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall be when thy son asketh thee in time to come, saying, What _is_ this? that thou shalt say unto him, By strength of hand the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought us out from Egypt, from the house of bondage:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when Pharaoh would hardly let us go, that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> slew all the firstborn in the land of Egypt, both the firstborn of man, and the firstborn of beast: therefore I sacrifice to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> all that openeth the matrix, being males; but all the firstborn of my children I redeem.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And it shall be for a token upon thine hand, and for frontlets between thine eyes: for by strength of hand the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought us forth out of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, when Pharaoh had let the people go, that God led them not _through_ the way of the land of the Philistines, although that _was_ near; for God said, Lest peradventure the people repent when they see war, and they return to Egypt:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But God led the people about, _through_ the way of the wilderness of the Red sea: and the children of Israel went up harnessed out of the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And Moses took the bones of Joseph with him: for he had straitly sworn the children of Israel, saying, God will surely visit you; and ye shall carry up my bones away hence with you.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And they took their journey from Succoth, and encamped in Etham, in the edge of the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> went before them by day in a pillar of a cloud, to lead them the way; and by night in a pillar of fire, to give them light; to go by day and night:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>He took not away the pillar of the cloud by day, nor the pillar of fire by night, _from_ before the people.  

## CHAPTER 14.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, that they turn and encamp before Pi–hahiroth, between Migdol and the sea, over against Baal–zephon: before it shall ye encamp by the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For Pharaoh will say of the children of Israel, They _are_ entangled in the land, the wilderness hath shut them in.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And I will harden Pharaoh’s heart, that he shall follow after them; and I will be honoured upon Pharaoh, and upon all his host; that the Egyptians may know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>. And they did so.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And it was told the king of Egypt that the people fled: and the heart of Pharaoh and of his servants was turned against the people, and they said, Why have we done this, that we have let Israel go from serving us?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he made ready his chariot, and took his people with him:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he took six hundred chosen chariots, and all the chariots of Egypt, and captains over every one of them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hardened the heart of Pharaoh king of Egypt, and he pursued after the children of Israel: and the children of Israel went out with an high hand.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But the Egyptians pursued after them, all the horses _and_ chariots of Pharaoh, and his horsemen, and his army, and overtook them encamping by the sea, beside Pi–hahiroth, before Baal–zephon.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And when Pharaoh drew nigh, the children of Israel lifted up their eyes, and, behold, the Egyptians marched after them; and they were sore afraid: and the children of Israel cried out unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And they said unto Moses, Because _there were_ no graves in Egypt, hast thou taken us away to die in the wilderness? wherefore hast thou dealt thus with us, to carry us forth out of Egypt?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>_Is_ not this the word that we did tell thee in Egypt, saying, Let us alone, that we may serve the Egyptians? For _it had been_ better for us to serve the Egyptians, than that we should die in the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto the people, Fear ye not, stand still, and see the salvation of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, which he will shew to you to day: for the Egyptians whom ye have seen to day, ye shall see them again no more for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall fight for you, and ye shall hold your peace.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Wherefore criest thou unto me? speak unto the children of Israel, that they go forward:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But lift thou up thy rod, and stretch out thine hand over the sea, and divide it: and the children of Israel shall go on dry _ground_ through the midst of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And I, behold, I will harden the hearts of the Egyptians, and they shall follow them: and I will get me honour upon Pharaoh, and upon all his host, upon his chariots, and upon his horsemen.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the Egyptians shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I have gotten me honour upon Pharaoh, upon his chariots, and upon his horsemen.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And the angel of God, which went before the camp of Israel, removed and went behind them; and the pillar of the cloud went from before their face, and stood behind them:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And it came between the camp of the Egyptians and the camp of Israel; and it was a cloud and darkness _to them_, but it gave light by night _to these_: so that the one came not near the other all the night.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses stretched out his hand over the sea; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> caused the sea to go _back_ by a strong east wind all that night, and made the sea dry _land_, and the waters were divided.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel went into the midst of the sea upon the dry _ground_: and the waters _were_ a wall unto them on their right hand, and on their left.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And the Egyptians pursued, and went in after them to the midst of the sea, _even_ all Pharaoh’s horses, his chariots, and his horsemen.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that in the morning watch the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> looked unto the host of the Egyptians through the pillar of fire and of the cloud, and troubled the host of the Egyptians,  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And took off their chariot wheels, that they drave them heavily: so that the Egyptians said, Let us flee from the face of Israel; for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> fighteth for them against the Egyptians.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Stretch out thine hand over the sea, that the waters may come again upon the Egyptians, upon their chariots, and upon their horsemen.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And Moses stretched forth his hand over the sea, and the sea returned to his strength when the morning appeared; and the Egyptians fled against it; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> overthrew the Egyptians in the midst of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the waters returned, and covered the chariots, and the horsemen, _and_ all the host of Pharaoh that came into the sea after them; there remained not so much as one of them.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>But the children of Israel walked upon dry _land_ in the midst of the sea; and the waters _were_ a wall unto them on their right hand, and on their left.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Thus the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> saved Israel that day out of the hand of the Egyptians; and Israel saw the Egyptians dead upon the sea shore.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And Israel saw that great work which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> did upon the Egyptians: and the people feared the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and believed the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and his servant Moses.  

## CHAPTER 15.

Then sang Moses and the children of Israel this song unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and spake, saying, I will sing unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, for he hath triumphed gloriously: the horse and his rider hath he thrown into the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ my strength and song, and he is become my salvation: he _is_ my God, and I will prepare him an habitation; my father’s God, and I will exalt him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ a man of war: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ his name.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Pharaoh’s chariots and his host hath he cast into the sea: his chosen captains also are drowned in the Red sea.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The depths have covered them: they sank into the bottom as a stone.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Thy right hand, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, is become glorious in power: thy right hand, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, hath dashed in pieces the enemy.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And in the greatness of thine excellency thou hast overthrown them that rose up against thee: thou sentest forth thy wrath, _which_ consumed them as stubble.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And with the blast of thy nostrils the waters were gathered together, the floods stood upright as an heap, _and_ the depths were congealed in the heart of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The enemy said, I will pursue, I will overtake, I will divide the spoil; my lust shall be satisfied upon them; I will draw my sword, my hand shall destroy them.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thou didst blow with thy wind, the sea covered them: they sank as lead in the mighty waters.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Who _is_ like unto thee, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, among the gods? who _is_ like thee, glorious in holiness, fearful _in_ praises, doing wonders?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Thou stretchedst out thy right hand, the earth swallowed them.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thou in thy mercy hast led forth the people _which_ thou hast redeemed: thou hast guided _them_ in thy strength unto thy holy habitation.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The people shall hear, _and_ be afraid: sorrow shall take hold on the inhabitants of Palestina.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Then the dukes of Edom shall be amazed; the mighty men of Moab, trembling shall take hold upon them; all the inhabitants of Canaan shall melt away.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Fear and dread shall fall upon them; by the greatness of thine arm they shall be _as_ still as a stone; till thy people pass over, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, till the people pass over, _which_ thou hast purchased.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thou shalt bring them in, and plant them in the mountain of thine inheritance, _in_ the place, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _which_ thou hast made for thee to dwell in, _in_ the Sanctuary, O Lord, _which_ thy hands have established.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall reign for ever and ever.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For the horse of Pharaoh went in with his chariots and with his horsemen into the sea, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought again the waters of the sea upon them; but the children of Israel went on dry _land_ in the midst of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And Miriam the prophetess, the sister of Aaron, took a timbrel in her hand; and all the women went out after her with timbrels and with dances.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Miriam answered them, Sing ye to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, for he hath triumphed gloriously; the horse and his rider hath he thrown into the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ So Moses brought Israel from the Red sea, and they went out into the wilderness of Shur; and they went three days in the wilderness, and found no water.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And when they came to Marah, they could not drink of the waters of Marah, for they _were_ bitter: therefore the name of it was called Marah.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the people murmured against Moses, saying, What shall we drink?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And he cried unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shewed him a tree, _which_ when he had cast into the waters, the waters were made sweet: there he made for them a statute and an ordinance, and there he proved them,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And said, If thou wilt diligently hearken to the voice of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, and wilt do that which is right in his sight, and wilt give ear to his commandments, and keep all his statutes, I will put none of these diseases upon thee, which I have brought upon the Egyptians: for I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that healeth thee.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And they came to Elim, where _were_ twelve wells of water, and threescore and ten palm trees: and they encamped there by the waters.  

## CHAPTER 16.

And they took their journey from Elim, and all the congregation of the children of Israel came unto the wilderness of Sin, which _is_ between Elim and Sinai, on the fifteenth day of the second month after their departing out of the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the whole congregation of the children of Israel murmured against Moses and Aaron in the wilderness:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel said unto them, Would to God we had died by the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the land of Egypt, when we sat by the flesh pots, _and_ when we did eat bread to the full; for ye have brought us forth into this wilderness, to kill this whole assembly with hunger.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Then said the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto Moses, Behold, I will rain bread from heaven for you; and the people shall go out and gather a certain rate every day, that I may prove them, whether they will walk in my law, or no.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, that on the sixth day they shall prepare _that_ which they bring in; and it shall be twice as much as they gather daily.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron said unto all the children of Israel, At even, then ye shall know that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath brought you out from the land of Egypt:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And in the morning, then ye shall see the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; for that he heareth your murmurings against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and what _are_ we, that ye murmur against us?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Moses said, _This shall be_, when the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall give you in the evening flesh to eat, and in the morning bread to the full; for that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> heareth your murmurings which ye murmur against him: and what _are_ we? your murmurings _are_ not against us, but against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses spake unto Aaron, Say unto all the congregation of the children of Israel, Come near before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: for he hath heard your murmurings.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, as Aaron spake unto the whole congregation of the children of Israel, that they looked toward the wilderness, and, behold, the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> appeared in the cloud.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I have heard the murmurings of the children of Israel: speak unto them, saying, At even ye shall eat flesh, and in the morning ye shall be filled with bread; and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, that at even the quails came up, and covered the camp: and in the morning the dew lay round about the host.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And when the dew that lay was gone up, behold, upon the face of the wilderness _there lay_ a small round thing, _as_ small as the hoar frost on the ground.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And when the children of Israel saw _it_, they said one to another, It _is_ manna: for they wist not what it _was_. And Moses said unto them, This _is_ the bread which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath given you to eat.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ This _is_ the thing which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath commanded, Gather of it every man according to his eating, an omer for every man, _according to_ the number of your persons; take ye every man for _them_ which _are_ in his tents.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel did so, and gathered, some more, some less.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And when they did mete _it_ with an omer, he that gathered much had nothing over, and he that gathered little had no lack; they gathered every man according to his eating.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And Moses said, Let no man leave of it till the morning.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Notwithstanding they hearkened not unto Moses; but some of them left of it until the morning, and it bred worms, and stank: and Moses was wroth with them.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And they gathered it every morning, every man according to his eating: and when the sun waxed hot, it melted.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, _that_ on the sixth day they gathered twice as much bread, two omers for one _man_: and all the rulers of the congregation came and told Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, This _is that_ which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath said, To morrow _is_ the rest of the holy sabbath unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: bake _that_ which ye will bake _to day_, and seethe that ye will seethe; and that which remaineth over lay up for you to be kept until the morning.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they laid it up till the morning, as Moses bade: and it did not stink, neither was there any worm therein.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And Moses said, Eat that to day; for to day _is_ a sabbath unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: to day ye shall not find it in the field.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Six days ye shall gather it; but on the seventh day, _which is_ the sabbath, in it there shall be none.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, _that_ there went out _some_ of the people on the seventh day for to gather, and they found none.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, How long refuse ye to keep my commandments and my laws?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>See, for that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath given you the sabbath, therefore he giveth you on the sixth day the bread of two days; abide ye every man in his place, let no man go out of his place on the seventh day.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>So the people rested on the seventh day.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the house of Israel called the name thereof Manna: and it _was_ like coriander seed, white; and the taste of it _was_ like wafers _made_ with honey.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said, This _is_ the thing which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commandeth, Fill an omer of it to be kept for your generations; that they may see the bread wherewith I have fed you in the wilderness, when I brought you forth from the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto Aaron, Take a pot, and put an omer full of manna therein, and lay it up before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to be kept for your generations.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>As the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses, so Aaron laid it up before the Testimony, to be kept.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel did eat manna forty years, until they came to a land inhabited; they did eat manna, until they came unto the borders of the land of Canaan.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Now an omer _is_ the tenth _part_ of an ephah.  

## CHAPTER 17.

And all the congregation of the children of Israel journeyed from the wilderness of Sin, after their journeys, according to the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and pitched in Rephidim: and _there was_ no water for the people to drink.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Wherefore the people did chide with Moses, and said, Give us water that we may drink. And Moses said unto them, Why chide ye with me? wherefore do ye tempt the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the people thirsted there for water; and the people murmured against Moses, and said, Wherefore _is_ this _that_ thou hast brought us up out of Egypt, to kill us and our children and our cattle with thirst?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Moses cried unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, What shall I do unto this people? they be almost ready to stone me.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Go on before the people, and take with thee of the elders of Israel; and thy rod, wherewith thou smotest the river, take in thine hand, and go.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Behold, I will stand before thee there upon the rock in Horeb; and thou shalt smite the rock, and there shall come water out of it, that the people may drink. And Moses did so in the sight of the elders of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he called the name of the place Massah, and Meribah, because of the chiding of the children of Israel, and because they tempted the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, Is the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> among us, or not?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Then came Amalek, and fought with Israel in Rephidim.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto Joshua, Choose us out men, and go out, fight with Amalek: to morrow I will stand on the top of the hill with the rod of God in mine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>So Joshua did as Moses had said to him, and fought with Amalek: and Moses, Aaron, and Hur went up to the top of the hill.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when Moses held up his hand, that Israel prevailed: and when he let down his hand, Amalek prevailed.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But Moses’ hands _were_ heavy; and they took a stone, and put _it_ under him, and he sat thereon; and Aaron and Hur stayed up his hands, the one on the one side, and the other on the other side; and his hands were steady until the going down of the sun.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Joshua discomfited Amalek and his people with the edge of the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Write this _for_ a memorial in a book, and rehearse _it_ in the ears of Joshua: for I will utterly put out the remembrance of Amalek from under heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Moses built an altar, and called the name of it Jehovah–nissi:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For he said, Because the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath sworn _that_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _will have_ war with Amalek from generation to generation.  

## CHAPTER 18.

When Jethro, the priest of Midian, Moses’ father in law, heard of all that God had done for Moses, and for Israel his people, _and_ that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had brought Israel out of Egypt;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then Jethro, Moses’ father in law, took Zipporah, Moses’ wife, after he had sent her back,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And her two sons; of which the name of the one _was_ Gershom; for he said, I have been an alien in a strange land:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the name of the other _was_ Eliezer; for the God of my father, _said he, was_ mine help, and delivered me from the sword of Pharaoh:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And Jethro, Moses’ father in law, came with his sons and his wife unto Moses into the wilderness, where he encamped at the mount of God:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he said unto Moses, I thy father in law Jethro am come unto thee, and thy wife, and her two sons with her.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses went out to meet his father in law, and did obeisance, and kissed him; and they asked each other of _their_ welfare; and they came into the tent.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Moses told his father in law all that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had done unto Pharaoh and to the Egyptians for Israel’s sake, _and_ all the travail that had come upon them by the way, and _how_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> delivered them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Jethro rejoiced for all the goodness which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had done to Israel, whom he had delivered out of the hand of the Egyptians.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Jethro said, Blessed _be_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, who hath delivered you out of the hand of the Egyptians, and out of the hand of Pharaoh, who hath delivered the people from under the hand of the Egyptians.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Now I know that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ greater than all gods: for in the thing wherein they dealt proudly _he was_ above them.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Jethro, Moses’ father in law, took a burnt offering and sacrifices for God: and Aaron came, and all the elders of Israel, to eat bread with Moses’ father in law before God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass on the morrow, that Moses sat to judge the people: and the people stood by Moses from the morning unto the evening.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And when Moses’ father in law saw all that he did to the people, he said, What _is_ this thing that thou doest to the people? why sittest thou thyself alone, and all the people stand by thee from morning unto even?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto his father in law, Because the people come unto me to enquire of God:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>When they have a matter, they come unto me; and I judge between one and another, and I do make _them_ know the statutes of God, and his laws.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And Moses’ father in law said unto him, The thing that thou doest _is_ not good.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Thou wilt surely wear away, both thou, and this people that _is_ with thee: for this thing _is_ too heavy for thee; thou art not able to perform it thyself alone.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Hearken now unto my voice, I will give thee counsel, and God shall be with thee: Be thou for the people to God-ward, that thou mayest bring the causes unto God:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And thou shalt teach them ordinances and laws, and shalt shew them the way wherein they must walk, and the work that they must do.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Moreover thou shalt provide out of all the people able men, such as fear God, men of truth, hating covetousness; and place _such_ over them, _to be_ rulers of thousands, _and_ rulers of hundreds, rulers of fifties, and rulers of tens:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And let them judge the people at all seasons: and it shall be, _that_ every great matter they shall bring unto thee, but every small matter they shall judge: so shall it be easier for thyself, and they shall bear _the burden_ with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>If thou shalt do this thing, and God command thee _so_, then thou shalt be able to endure, and all this people shall also go to their place in peace.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>So Moses hearkened to the voice of his father in law, and did all that he had said.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And Moses chose able men out of all Israel, and made them heads over the people, rulers of thousands, rulers of hundreds, rulers of fifties, and rulers of tens.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And they judged the people at all seasons: the hard causes they brought unto Moses, but every small matter they judged themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses let his father in law depart; and he went his way into his own land.  

## CHAPTER 19.

In the third month, when the children of Israel were gone forth out of the land of Egypt, the same day came they _into_ the wilderness of Sinai.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For they were departed from Rephidim, and were come _to_ the desert of Sinai, and had pitched in the wilderness; and there Israel camped before the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Moses went up unto God, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> called unto him out of the mountain, saying, Thus shalt thou say to the house of Jacob, and tell the children of Israel;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Ye have seen what I did unto the Egyptians, and _how_ I bare you on eagles’ wings, and brought you unto myself.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Now therefore, if ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me above all people: for all the earth _is_ mine:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation. These _are_ the words which thou shalt speak unto the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses came and called for the elders of the people, and laid before their faces all these words which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded him.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And all the people answered together, and said, All that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken we will do. And Moses returned the words of the people unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Lo, I come unto thee in a thick cloud, that the people may hear when I speak with thee, and believe thee for ever. And Moses told the words of the people unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Go unto the people, and sanctify them to day and to morrow, and let them wash their clothes,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And be ready against the third day: for the third day the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will come down in the sight of all the people upon mount Sinai.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And thou shalt set bounds unto the people round about, saying, Take heed to yourselves, _that ye_ go _not_ up into the mount, or touch the border of it: whosoever toucheth the mount shall be surely put to death:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>There shall not an hand touch it, but he shall surely be stoned, or shot through; whether _it be_ beast or man, it shall not live: when the trumpet soundeth long, they shall come up to the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses went down from the mount unto the people, and sanctified the people; and they washed their clothes.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he said unto the people, Be ready against the third day: come not at _your_ wives.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass on the third day in the morning, that there were thunders and lightnings, and a thick cloud upon the mount, and the voice of the trumpet exceeding loud; so that all the people that _was_ in the camp trembled.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And Moses brought forth the people out of the camp to meet with God; and they stood at the nether part of the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And mount Sinai was altogether on a smoke, because the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> descended upon it in fire: and the smoke thereof ascended as the smoke of a furnace, and the whole mount quaked greatly.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And when the voice of the trumpet sounded long, and waxed louder and louder, Moses spake, and God answered him by a voice.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came down upon mount Sinai, on the top of the mount: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> called Moses _up_ to the top of the mount; and Moses went up.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Go down, charge the people, lest they break through unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to gaze, and many of them perish.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And let the priests also, which come near to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, sanctify themselves, lest the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> break forth upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, The people cannot come up to mount Sinai: for thou chargedst us, saying, Set bounds about the mount, and sanctify it.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto him, Away, get thee down, and thou shalt come up, thou, and Aaron with thee: but let not the priests and the people break through to come up unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, lest he break forth upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>So Moses went down unto the people, and spake unto them.  

## CHAPTER 20.

And God spake all these words, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, which have brought thee out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Thou shalt have no other gods before me.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness _of any thing_ that _is_ in heaven above, or that _is_ in the earth beneath, or that _is_ in the water under the earth:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not bow down thyself to them, nor serve them: for I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God _am_ a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth _generation_ of them that hate me;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And shewing mercy unto thousands of them that love me, and keep my commandments.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not take the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God in vain; for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will not hold him guiltless that taketh his name in vain.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Six days shalt thou labour, and do all thy work:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But the seventh day _is_ the sabbath of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God: _in it_ thou shalt not do any work, thou, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, thy manservant, nor thy maidservant, nor thy cattle, nor thy stranger that _is_ within thy gates:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For _in_ six days the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them _is_, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Honour thy father and thy mother: that thy days may be long upon the land which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God giveth thee.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not kill.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not commit adultery.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not steal.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not bear false witness against thy neighbour.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not covet thy neighbour’s house, thou shalt not covet thy neighbour’s wife, nor his manservant, nor his maidservant, nor his ox, nor his ass, nor any thing that _is_ thy neighbour’s.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And all the people saw the thunderings, and the lightnings, and the noise of the trumpet, and the mountain smoking: and when the people saw _it_, they removed, and stood afar off.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And they said unto Moses, Speak thou with us, and we will hear: but let not God speak with us, lest we die.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Moses said unto the people, Fear not: for God is come to prove you, and that his fear may be before your faces, that ye sin not.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the people stood afar off, and Moses drew near unto the thick darkness where God _was_.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Thus thou shalt say unto the children of Israel, Ye have seen that I have talked with you from heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Ye shall not make with me gods of silver, neither shall ye make unto you gods of gold.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ An altar of earth thou shalt make unto me, and shalt sacrifice thereon thy burnt offerings, and thy peace offerings, thy sheep, and thine oxen: in all places where I record my name I will come unto thee, and I will bless thee.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And if thou wilt make me an altar of stone, thou shalt not build it of hewn stone: for if thou lift up thy tool upon it, thou hast polluted it.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Neither shalt thou go up by steps unto mine altar, that thy nakedness be not discovered thereon.  

## CHAPTER 21.

Now these _are_ the judgments which thou shalt set before them.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>If thou buy an Hebrew servant, six years he shall serve: and in the seventh he shall go out free for nothing.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>If he came in by himself, he shall go out by himself: if he were married, then his wife shall go out with him.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>If his master have given him a wife, and she have born him sons or daughters; the wife and her children shall be her master’s, and he shall go out by himself.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And if the servant shall plainly say, I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Then his master shall bring him unto the judges; he shall also bring him to the door, or unto the door post; and his master shall bore his ear through with an aul; and he shall serve him for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And if a man sell his daughter to be a maidservant, she shall not go out as the menservants do.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>If she please not her master, who hath betrothed her to himself, then shall he let her be redeemed: to sell her unto a strange nation he shall have no power, seeing he hath dealt deceitfully with her.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And if he have betrothed her unto his son, he shall deal with her after the manner of daughters.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>If he take him another _wife_; her food, her raiment, and her duty of marriage, shall he not diminish.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And if he do not these three unto her, then shall she go out free without money.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ He that smiteth a man, so that he die, shall be surely put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And if a man lie not in wait, but God deliver _him_ into his hand; then I will appoint thee a place whither he shall flee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But if a man come presumptuously upon his neighbour, to slay him with guile; thou shalt take him from mine altar, that he may die.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And he that smiteth his father, or his mother, shall be surely put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And he that stealeth a man, and selleth him, or if he be found in his hand, he shall surely be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And he that curseth his father, or his mother, shall surely be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And if men strive together, and one smite another with a stone, or with _his_ fist, and he die not, but keepeth _his_ bed:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>If he rise again, and walk abroad upon his staff, then shall he that smote _him_ be quit: only he shall pay _for_ the loss of his time, and shall cause _him_ to be thoroughly healed.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And if a man smite his servant, or his maid, with a rod, and he die under his hand; he shall be surely punished.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Notwithstanding, if he continue a day or two, he shall not be punished: for he _is_ his money.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ If men strive, and hurt a woman with child, so that her fruit depart _from her_, and yet no mischief follow: he shall be surely punished, according as the woman’s husband will lay upon him; and he shall pay as the judges _determine_.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And if _any_ mischief follow, then thou shalt give life for life,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot,  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Burning for burning, wound for wound, stripe for stripe.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And if a man smite the eye of his servant, or the eye of his maid, that it perish; he shall let him go free for his eye’s sake.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And if he smite out his manservant’s tooth, or his maidservant’s tooth; he shall let him go free for his tooth’s sake.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ If an ox gore a man or a woman, that they die: then the ox shall be surely stoned, and his flesh shall not be eaten; but the owner of the ox _shall be_ quit.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>But if the ox were wont to push with his horn in time past, and it hath been testified to his owner, and he hath not kept him in, but that he hath killed a man or a woman; the ox shall be stoned, and his owner also shall be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>If there be laid on him a sum of money, then he shall give for the ransom of his life whatsoever is laid upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Whether he have gored a son, or have gored a daughter, according to this judgment shall it be done unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>If the ox shall push a manservant or a maidservant; he shall give unto their master thirty shekels of silver, and the ox shall be stoned.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ And if a man shall open a pit, or if a man shall dig a pit, and not cover it, and an ox or an ass fall therein;  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>The owner of the pit shall make _it_ good, _and_ give money unto the owner of them; and the dead _beast_ shall be his.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>¶ And if one man’s ox hurt another’s, that he die; then they shall sell the live ox, and divide the money of it; and the dead _ox_ also they shall divide.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Or if it be known that the ox hath used to push in time past, and his owner hath not kept him in; he shall surely pay ox for ox; and the dead shall be his own.  

## CHAPTER 22.

If a man shall steal an ox, or a sheep, and kill it, or sell it; he shall restore five oxen for an ox, and four sheep for a sheep.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ If a thief be found breaking up, and be smitten that he die, _there shall_ no blood _be shed_ for him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>If the sun be risen upon him, _there shall be_ blood _shed_ for him; _for_ he should make full restitution; if he have nothing, then he shall be sold for his theft.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>If the theft be certainly found in his hand alive, whether it be ox, or ass, or sheep; he shall restore double.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ If a man shall cause a field or vineyard to be eaten, and shall put in his beast, and shall feed in another man’s field; of the best of his own field, and of the best of his own vineyard, shall he make restitution.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ If fire break out, and catch in thorns, so that the stacks of corn, or the standing corn, or the field, be consumed _therewith_; he that kindled the fire shall surely make restitution.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ If a man shall deliver unto his neighbour money or stuff to keep, and it be stolen out of the man’s house; if the thief be found, let him pay double.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>If the thief be not found, then the master of the house shall be brought unto the judges, _to see_ whether he have put his hand unto his neighbour’s goods.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For all manner of trespass, _whether it be_ for ox, for ass, for sheep, for raiment, _or_ for any manner of lost thing, which _another_ challengeth to be his, the cause of both parties shall come before the judges; _and_ whom the judges shall condemn, he shall pay double unto his neighbour.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>If a man deliver unto his neighbour an ass, or an ox, or a sheep, or any beast, to keep; and it die, or be hurt, or driven away, no man seeing _it_:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_Then_ shall an oath of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> be between them both, that he hath not put his hand unto his neighbour’s goods; and the owner of it shall accept _thereof_, and he shall not make _it_ good.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And if it be stolen from him, he shall make restitution unto the owner thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>If it be torn in pieces, _then_ let him bring it _for_ witness, _and_ he shall not make good that which was torn.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And if a man borrow _ought_ of his neighbour, and it be hurt, or die, the owner thereof _being_ not with it, he shall surely make _it_ good.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>_But_ if the owner thereof _be_ with it, he shall not make _it_ good: if it _be_ an hired _thing_, it came for his hire.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And if a man entice a maid that is not betrothed, and lie with her, he shall surely endow her to be his wife.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>If her father utterly refuse to give her unto him, he shall pay money according to the dowry of virgins.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Thou shalt not suffer a witch to live.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Whosoever lieth with a beast shall surely be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ He that sacrificeth unto _any_ god, save unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> only, he shall be utterly destroyed.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Thou shalt neither vex a stranger, nor oppress him: for ye were strangers in the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Ye shall not afflict any widow, or fatherless child.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>If thou afflict them in any wise, and they cry at all unto me, I will surely hear their cry;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And my wrath shall wax hot, and I will kill you with the sword; and your wives shall be widows, and your children fatherless.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ If thou lend money to _any of_ my people _that is_ poor by thee, thou shalt not be to him as an usurer, neither shalt thou lay upon him usury.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>If thou at all take thy neighbour’s raiment to pledge, thou shalt deliver it unto him by that the sun goeth down:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For that _is_ his covering only, it _is_ his raiment for his skin: wherein shall he sleep? and it shall come to pass, when he crieth unto me, that I will hear; for I _am_ gracious.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ Thou shalt not revile the gods, nor curse the ruler of thy people.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ Thou shalt not delay _to offer_ the first of thy ripe fruits, and of thy liquors: the firstborn of thy sons shalt thou give unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Likewise shalt thou do with thine oxen, _and_ with thy sheep: seven days it shall be with his dam; on the eighth day thou shalt give it me.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And ye shall be holy men unto me: neither shall ye eat _any_ flesh _that is_ torn of beasts in the field; ye shall cast it to the dogs.  

## CHAPTER 23.

Thou shalt not raise a false report: put not thine hand with the wicked to be an unrighteous witness.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ Thou shalt not follow a multitude to _do_ evil; neither shalt thou speak in a cause to decline after many to wrest _judgment_:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Neither shalt thou countenance a poor man in his cause.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ If thou meet thine enemy’s ox or his ass going astray, thou shalt surely bring it back to him again.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>If thou see the ass of him that hateth thee lying under his burden, and wouldest forbear to help him, thou shalt surely help with him.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not wrest the judgment of thy poor in his cause.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Keep thee far from a false matter; and the innocent and righteous slay thou not: for I will not justify the wicked.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt take no gift: for the gift blindeth the wise, and perverteth the words of the righteous.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Also thou shalt not oppress a stranger: for ye know the heart of a stranger, seeing ye were strangers in the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And six years thou shalt sow thy land, and shalt gather in the fruits thereof:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But the seventh _year_ thou shalt let it rest and lie still; that the poor of thy people may eat: and what they leave the beasts of the field shall eat. In like manner thou shalt deal with thy vineyard, _and_ with thy oliveyard.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Six days thou shalt do thy work, and on the seventh day thou shalt rest: that thine ox and thine ass may rest, and the son of thy handmaid, and the stranger, may be refreshed.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And in all _things_ that I have said unto you be circumspect: and make no mention of the name of other gods, neither let it be heard out of thy mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Three times thou shalt keep a feast unto me in the year.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thou shalt keep the feast of unleavened bread: (thou shalt eat unleavened bread seven days, as I commanded thee, in the time appointed of the month Abib; for in it thou camest out from Egypt: and none shall appear before me empty:)  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the feast of harvest, the firstfruits of thy labours, which thou hast sown in the field: and the feast of ingathering, _which is_ in the end of the year, when thou hast gathered in thy labours out of the field.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Three times in the year all thy males shall appear before the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not offer the blood of my sacrifice with leavened bread; neither shall the fat of my sacrifice remain until the morning.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The first of the firstfruits of thy land thou shalt bring into the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God. Thou shalt not seethe a kid in his mother’s milk.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, I send an Angel before thee, to keep thee in the way, and to bring thee into the place which I have prepared.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Beware of him, and obey his voice, provoke him not; for he will not pardon your transgressions: for my name _is_ in him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But if thou shalt indeed obey his voice, and do all that I speak; then I will be an enemy unto thine enemies, and an adversary unto thine adversaries.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For mine Angel shall go before thee, and bring thee in unto the Amorites, and the Hittites, and the Perizzites, and the Canaanites, the Hivites, and the Jebusites: and I will cut them off.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not bow down to their gods, nor serve them, nor do after their works: but thou shalt utterly overthrow them, and quite break down their images.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And ye shall serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> your God, and he shall bless thy bread, and thy water; and I will take sickness away from the midst of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ There shall nothing cast their young, nor be barren, in thy land: the number of thy days I will fulfil.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>I will send my fear before thee, and will destroy all the people to whom thou shalt come, and I will make all thine enemies turn their backs unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And I will send hornets before thee, which shall drive out the Hivite, the Canaanite, and the Hittite, from before thee.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>I will not drive them out from before thee in one year; lest the land become desolate, and the beast of the field multiply against thee.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>By little and little I will drive them out from before thee, until thou be increased, and inherit the land.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And I will set thy bounds from the Red sea even unto the sea of the Philistines, and from the desert unto the river: for I will deliver the inhabitants of the land into your hand; and thou shalt drive them out before thee.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Thou shalt make no covenant with them, nor with their gods.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>They shall not dwell in thy land, lest they make thee sin against me: for if thou serve their gods, it will surely be a snare unto thee.  

## CHAPTER 24.

And he said unto Moses, Come up unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou, and Aaron, Nadab, and Abihu, and seventy of the elders of Israel; and worship ye afar off.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Moses alone shall come near the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: but they shall not come nigh; neither shall the people go up with him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses came and told the people all the words of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and all the judgments: and all the people answered with one voice, and said, All the words which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath said will we do.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Moses wrote all the words of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and rose up early in the morning, and builded an altar under the hill, and twelve pillars, according to the twelve tribes of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And he sent young men of the children of Israel, which offered burnt offerings, and sacrificed peace offerings of oxen unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Moses took half of the blood, and put _it_ in basons; and half of the blood he sprinkled on the altar.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he took the book of the covenant, and read in the audience of the people: and they said, All that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath said will we do, and be obedient.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Moses took the blood, and sprinkled _it_ on the people, and said, Behold the blood of the covenant, which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath made with you concerning all these words.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Then went up Moses, and Aaron, Nadab, and Abihu, and seventy of the elders of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they saw the God of Israel: and _there was_ under his feet as it were a paved work of a sapphire stone, and as it were the body of heaven in _his_ clearness.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And upon the nobles of the children of Israel he laid not his hand: also they saw God, and did eat and drink.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Come up to me into the mount, and be there: and I will give thee tables of stone, and a law, and commandments which I have written; that thou mayest teach them.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Moses rose up, and his minister Joshua: and Moses went up into the mount of God.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he said unto the elders, Tarry ye here for us, until we come again unto you: and, behold, Aaron and Hur _are_ with you: if any man have any matters to do, let him come unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Moses went up into the mount, and a cloud covered the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> abode upon mount Sinai, and the cloud covered it six days: and the seventh day he called unto Moses out of the midst of the cloud.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the sight of the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _was_ like devouring fire on the top of the mount in the eyes of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Moses went into the midst of the cloud, and gat him up into the mount: and Moses was in the mount forty days and forty nights.  

## CHAPTER 25.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Speak unto the children of Israel, that they bring me an offering: of every man that giveth it willingly with his heart ye shall take my offering.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And this _is_ the offering which ye shall take of them; gold, and silver, and brass,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine linen, and goats’ _hair_,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And rams’ skins dyed red, and badgers’ skins, and shittim wood,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Oil for the light, spices for anointing oil, and for sweet incense,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Onyx stones, and stones to be set in the ephod, and in the breastplate.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>According to all that I shew thee, _after_ the pattern of the tabernacle, and the pattern of all the instruments thereof, even so shall ye make _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And they shall make an ark _of_ shittim wood: two cubits and a half _shall be_ the length thereof, and a cubit and a half the breadth thereof, and a cubit and a half the height thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And thou shalt overlay it with pure gold, within and without shalt thou overlay it, and shalt make upon it a crown of gold round about.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And thou shalt cast four rings of gold for it, and put _them_ in the four corners thereof; and two rings _shall be_ in the one side of it, and two rings in the other side of it.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make staves _of_ shittim wood, and overlay them with gold.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put the staves into the rings by the sides of the ark, that the ark may be borne with them.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The staves shall be in the rings of the ark: they shall not be taken from it.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put into the ark the testimony which I shall give thee.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make a mercy seat _of_ pure gold: two cubits and a half _shall be_ the length thereof, and a cubit and a half the breadth thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make two cherubims _of_ gold, _of_ beaten work shalt thou make them, in the two ends of the mercy seat.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And make one cherub on the one end, and the other cherub on the other end: _even_ of the mercy seat shall ye make the cherubims on the two ends thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the cherubims shall stretch forth _their_ wings on high, covering the mercy seat with their wings, and their faces _shall look_ one to another; toward the mercy seat shall the faces of the cherubims be.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put the mercy seat above upon the ark; and in the ark thou shalt put the testimony that I shall give thee.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And there I will meet with thee, and I will commune with thee from above the mercy seat, from between the two cherubims which _are_ upon the ark of the testimony, of all _things_ which I will give thee in commandment unto the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Thou shalt also make a table _of_ shittim wood: two cubits _shall be_ the length thereof, and a cubit the breadth thereof, and a cubit and a half the height thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And thou shalt overlay it with pure gold, and make thereto a crown of gold round about.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make unto it a border of an hand breadth round about, and thou shalt make a golden crown to the border thereof round about.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make for it four rings of gold, and put the rings in the four corners that _are_ on the four feet thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Over against the border shall the rings be for places of the staves to bear the table.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make the staves _of_ shittim wood, and overlay them with gold, that the table may be borne with them.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make the dishes thereof, and spoons thereof, and covers thereof, and bowls thereof, to cover withal: _of_ pure gold shalt thou make them.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And thou shalt set upon the table shewbread before me alway.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make a candlestick _of_ pure gold: _of_ beaten work shall the candlestick be made: his shaft, and his branches, his bowls, his knops, and his flowers, shall be of the same.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And six branches shall come out of the sides of it; three branches of the candlestick out of the one side, and three branches of the candlestick out of the other side:  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Three bowls made like unto almonds, _with_ a knop and a flower in one branch; and three bowls made like almonds in the other branch, _with_ a knop and a flower: so in the six branches that come out of the candlestick.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And in the candlestick _shall be_ four bowls made like unto almonds, _with_ their knops and their flowers.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And _there shall be_ a knop under two branches of the same, and a knop under two branches of the same, and a knop under two branches of the same, according to the six branches that proceed out of the candlestick.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Their knops and their branches shall be of the same: all it _shall be_ one beaten work _of_ pure gold.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make the seven lamps thereof: and they shall light the lamps thereof, that they may give light over against it.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And the tongs thereof, and the snuffdishes thereof, _shall be of_ pure gold.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>_Of_ a talent of pure gold shall he make it, with all these vessels.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And look that thou make _them_ after their pattern, which was shewed thee in the mount.  

## CHAPTER 26.

Moreover thou shalt make the tabernacle _with_ ten curtains _of_ fine twined linen, and blue, and purple, and scarlet: _with_ cherubims of cunning work shalt thou make them.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The length of one curtain _shall be_ eight and twenty cubits, and the breadth of one curtain four cubits: and every one of the curtains shall have one measure.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The five curtains shall be coupled together one to another; and _other_ five curtains _shall be_ coupled one to another.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make loops of blue upon the edge of the one curtain from the selvedge in the coupling; and likewise shalt thou make in the uttermost edge of _another_ curtain, in the coupling of the second.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Fifty loops shalt thou make in the one curtain, and fifty loops shalt thou make in the edge of the curtain that _is_ in the coupling of the second; that the loops may take hold one of another.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make fifty taches of gold, and couple the curtains together with the taches: and it shall be one tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make curtains _of_ goats’ _hair_ to be a covering upon the tabernacle: eleven curtains shalt thou make.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The length of one curtain _shall be_ thirty cubits, and the breadth of one curtain four cubits: and the eleven curtains _shall be all_ of one measure.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And thou shalt couple five curtains by themselves, and six curtains by themselves, and shalt double the sixth curtain in the forefront of the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make fifty loops on the edge of the one curtain _that is_ outmost in the coupling, and fifty loops in the edge of the curtain which coupleth the second.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make fifty taches of brass, and put the taches into the loops, and couple the tent together, that it may be one.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the remnant that remaineth of the curtains of the tent, the half curtain that remaineth, shall hang over the backside of the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And a cubit on the one side, and a cubit on the other side of that which remaineth in the length of the curtains of the tent, it shall hang over the sides of the tabernacle on this side and on that side, to cover it.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make a covering for the tent _of_ rams’ skins dyed red, and a covering above _of_ badgers’ skins.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make boards for the tabernacle _of_ shittim wood standing up.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Ten cubits _shall be_ the length of a board, and a cubit and a half _shall be_ the breadth of one board.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Two tenons _shall there be_ in one board, set in order one against another: thus shalt thou make for all the boards of the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make the boards for the tabernacle, twenty boards on the south side southward.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make forty sockets of silver under the twenty boards; two sockets under one board for his two tenons, and two sockets under another board for his two tenons.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And for the second side of the tabernacle on the north side _there shall be_ twenty boards:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And their forty sockets _of_ silver; two sockets under one board, and two sockets under another board.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And for the sides of the tabernacle westward thou shalt make six boards.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And two boards shalt thou make for the corners of the tabernacle in the two sides.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they shall be coupled together beneath, and they shall be coupled together above the head of it unto one ring: thus shall it be for them both; they shall be for the two corners.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And they shall be eight boards, and their sockets _of_ silver, sixteen sockets; two sockets under one board, and two sockets under another board.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make bars _of_ shittim wood; five for the boards of the one side of the tabernacle,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And five bars for the boards of the other side of the tabernacle, and five bars for the boards of the side of the tabernacle, for the two sides westward.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the middle bar in the midst of the boards shall reach from end to end.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And thou shalt overlay the boards with gold, and make their rings _of_ gold _for_ places for the bars: and thou shalt overlay the bars with gold.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And thou shalt rear up the tabernacle according to the fashion thereof which was shewed thee in the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make a vail _of_ blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen of cunning work: with cherubims shall it be made:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And thou shalt hang it upon four pillars of shittim _wood_ overlaid with gold: their hooks _shall be of_ gold, upon the four sockets of silver.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt hang up the vail under the taches, that thou mayest bring in thither within the vail the ark of the testimony: and the vail shall divide unto you between the holy _place_ and the most holy.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put the mercy seat upon the ark of the testimony in the most holy _place_.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And thou shalt set the table without the vail, and the candlestick over against the table on the side of the tabernacle toward the south: and thou shalt put the table on the north side.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make an hanging for the door of the tent, _of_ blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen, wrought with needlework.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make for the hanging five pillars _of_ shittim _wood_, and overlay them with gold, _and_ their hooks _shall be of_ gold: and thou shalt cast five sockets of brass for them.  

## CHAPTER 27.

And thou shalt make an altar _of_ shittim wood, five cubits long, and five cubits broad; the altar shall be foursquare: and the height thereof _shall be_ three cubits.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make the horns of it upon the four corners thereof: his horns shall be of the same: and thou shalt overlay it with brass.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make his pans to receive his ashes, and his shovels, and his basons, and his fleshhooks, and his firepans: all the vessels thereof thou shalt make _of_ brass.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make for it a grate of network _of_ brass; and upon the net shalt thou make four brasen rings in the four corners thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put it under the compass of the altar beneath, that the net may be even to the midst of the altar.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make staves for the altar, staves _of_ shittim wood, and overlay them with brass.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the staves shall be put into the rings, and the staves shall be upon the two sides of the altar, to bear it.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Hollow with boards shalt thou make it: as it was shewed thee in the mount, so shall they make _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make the court of the tabernacle: for the south side southward _there shall be_ hangings for the court _of_ fine twined linen of an hundred cubits long for one side:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the twenty pillars thereof and their twenty sockets _shall be of_ brass; the hooks of the pillars and their fillets _shall be of_ silver.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And likewise for the north side in length _there shall be_ hangings of an hundred _cubits_ long, and his twenty pillars and their twenty sockets _of_ brass; the hooks of the pillars and their fillets _of_ silver.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And _for_ the breadth of the court on the west side _shall be_ hangings of fifty cubits: their pillars ten, and their sockets ten.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the breadth of the court on the east side eastward _shall be_ fifty cubits.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The hangings of one side _of the gate shall be_ fifteen cubits: their pillars three, and their sockets three.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And on the other side _shall be_ hangings fifteen _cubits_: their pillars three, and their sockets three.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And for the gate of the court _shall be_ an hanging of twenty cubits, _of_ blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen, wrought with needlework: _and_ their pillars _shall be_ four, and their sockets four.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>All the pillars round about the court _shall be_ filleted with silver; their hooks _shall be of_ silver, and their sockets _of_ brass.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ The length of the court _shall be_ an hundred cubits, and the breadth fifty every where, and the height five cubits _of_ fine twined linen, and their sockets _of_ brass.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>All the vessels of the tabernacle in all the service thereof, and all the pins thereof, and all the pins of the court, _shall be of_ brass.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt command the children of Israel, that they bring thee pure oil olive beaten for the light, to cause the lamp to burn always.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>In the tabernacle of the congregation without the vail, which _is_ before the testimony, Aaron and his sons shall order it from evening to morning before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: _it shall be_ a statute for ever unto their generations on the behalf of the children of Israel.  

## CHAPTER 28.

And take thou unto thee Aaron thy brother, and his sons with him, from among the children of Israel, that he may minister unto me in the priest’s office, _even_ Aaron, Nadab and Abihu, Eleazar and Ithamar, Aaron’s sons.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make holy garments for Aaron thy brother for glory and for beauty.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And thou shalt speak unto all _that are_ wise hearted, whom I have filled with the spirit of wisdom, that they may make Aaron’s garments to consecrate him, that he may minister unto me in the priest’s office.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And these _are_ the garments which they shall make; a breastplate, and an ephod, and a robe, and a broidered coat, a mitre, and a girdle: and they shall make holy garments for Aaron thy brother, and his sons, that he may minister unto me in the priest’s office.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they shall take gold, and blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine linen.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And they shall make the ephod _of_ gold, _of_ blue, and _of_ purple, _of_ scarlet, and fine twined linen, with cunning work.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>It shall have the two shoulderpieces thereof joined at the two edges thereof; and _so_ it shall be joined together.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the curious girdle of the ephod, which _is_ upon it, shall be of the same, according to the work thereof; _even of_ gold, _of_ blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take two onyx stones, and grave on them the names of the children of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Six of their names on one stone, and _the other_ six names of the rest on the other stone, according to their birth.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>With the work of an engraver in stone, _like_ the engravings of a signet, shalt thou engrave the two stones with the names of the children of Israel: thou shalt make them to be set in ouches of gold.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put the two stones upon the shoulders of the ephod _for_ stones of memorial unto the children of Israel: and Aaron shall bear their names before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> upon his two shoulders for a memorial.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make ouches _of_ gold;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And two chains _of_ pure gold at the ends; _of_ wreathen work shalt thou make them, and fasten the wreathen chains to the ouches.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make the breastplate of judgment with cunning work; after the work of the ephod thou shalt make it; _of_ gold, _of_ blue, and _of_ purple, and _of_ scarlet, and _of_ fine twined linen, shalt thou make it.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Foursquare it shall be _being_ doubled; a span _shall be_ the length thereof, and a span _shall be_ the breadth thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And thou shalt set in it settings of stones, _even_ four rows of stones: _the first_ row _shall be_ a sardius, a topaz, and a carbuncle: _this shall be_ the first row.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the second row _shall be_ an emerald, a sapphire, and a diamond.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the third row a ligure, an agate, and an amethyst.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the fourth row a beryl, and an onyx, and a jasper: they shall be set in gold in their inclosings.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the stones shall be with the names of the children of Israel, twelve, according to their names, _like_ the engravings of a signet; every one with his name shall they be according to the twelve tribes.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make upon the breastplate chains at the ends _of_ wreathen work _of_ pure gold.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make upon the breastplate two rings of gold, and shalt put the two rings on the two ends of the breastplate.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put the two wreathen _chains_ of gold in the two rings _which are_ on the ends of the breastplate.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And _the other_ two ends of the two wreathen _chains_ thou shalt fasten in the two ouches, and put _them_ on the shoulderpieces of the ephod before it.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make two rings of gold, and thou shalt put them upon the two ends of the breastplate in the border thereof, which _is_ in the side of the ephod inward.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And two _other_ rings of gold thou shalt make, and shalt put them on the two sides of the ephod underneath, toward the forepart thereof, over against the _other_ coupling thereof, above the curious girdle of the ephod.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And they shall bind the breastplate by the rings thereof unto the rings of the ephod with a lace of blue, that _it_ may be above the curious girdle of the ephod, and that the breastplate be not loosed from the ephod.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And Aaron shall bear the names of the children of Israel in the breastplate of judgment upon his heart, when he goeth in unto the holy _place_, for a memorial before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> continually.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt put in the breastplate of judgment the Urim and the Thummim; and they shall be upon Aaron’s heart, when he goeth in before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and Aaron shall bear the judgment of the children of Israel upon his heart before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> continually.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make the robe of the ephod all _of_ blue.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And there shall be an hole in the top of it, in the midst thereof: it shall have a binding of woven work round about the hole of it, as it were the hole of an habergeon, that it be not rent.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ And _beneath_ upon the hem of it thou shalt make pomegranates _of_ blue, and _of_ purple, and _of_ scarlet, round about the hem thereof; and bells of gold between them round about:  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>A golden bell and a pomegranate, a golden bell and a pomegranate, upon the hem of the robe round about.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And it shall be upon Aaron to minister: and his sound shall be heard when he goeth in unto the holy _place_ before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and when he cometh out, that he die not.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt make a plate _of_ pure gold, and grave upon it, _like_ the engravings of a signet, HOLINESS TO THE <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put it on a blue lace, that it may be upon the mitre; upon the forefront of the mitre it shall be.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And it shall be upon Aaron’s forehead, that Aaron may bear the iniquity of the holy things, which the children of Israel shall hallow in all their holy gifts; and it shall be always upon his forehead, that they may be accepted before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt embroider the coat of fine linen, and thou shalt make the mitre _of_ fine linen, and thou shalt make the girdle _of_ needlework.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>¶ And for Aaron’s sons thou shalt make coats, and thou shalt make for them girdles, and bonnets shalt thou make for them, for glory and for beauty.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put them upon Aaron thy brother, and his sons with him; and shalt anoint them, and consecrate them, and sanctify them, that they may minister unto me in the priest’s office.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make them linen breeches to cover their nakedness; from the loins even unto the thighs they shall reach:  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And they shall be upon Aaron, and upon his sons, when they come in unto the tabernacle of the congregation, or when they come near unto the altar to minister in the holy _place_; that they bear not iniquity, and die: _it shall be_ a statute for ever unto him and his seed after him.  

## CHAPTER 29.

And this _is_ the thing that thou shalt do unto them to hallow them, to minister unto me in the priest’s office: Take one young bullock, and two rams without blemish,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And unleavened bread, and cakes unleavened tempered with oil, and wafers unleavened anointed with oil: _of_ wheaten flour shalt thou make them.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put them into one basket, and bring them in the basket, with the bullock and the two rams.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Aaron and his sons thou shalt bring unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, and shalt wash them with water.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take the garments, and put upon Aaron the coat, and the robe of the ephod, and the ephod, and the breastplate, and gird him with the curious girdle of the ephod:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put the mitre upon his head, and put the holy crown upon the mitre.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou take the anointing oil, and pour _it_ upon his head, and anoint him.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And thou shalt bring his sons, and put coats upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And thou shalt gird them with girdles, Aaron and his sons, and put the bonnets on them: and the priest’s office shall be theirs for a perpetual statute: and thou shalt consecrate Aaron and his sons.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And thou shalt cause a bullock to be brought before the tabernacle of the congregation: and Aaron and his sons shall put their hands upon the head of the bullock.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And thou shalt kill the bullock before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _by_ the door of the tabernacle of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take of the blood of the bullock, and put _it_ upon the horns of the altar with thy finger, and pour all the blood beside the bottom of the altar.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take all the fat that covereth the inwards, and the caul _that is_ above the liver, and the two kidneys, and the fat that _is_ upon them, and burn _them_ upon the altar.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But the flesh of the bullock, and his skin, and his dung, shalt thou burn with fire without the camp: it _is_ a sin offering.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Thou shalt also take one ram; and Aaron and his sons shall put their hands upon the head of the ram.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And thou shalt slay the ram, and thou shalt take his blood, and sprinkle _it_ round about upon the altar.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And thou shalt cut the ram in pieces, and wash the inwards of him, and his legs, and put _them_ unto his pieces, and unto his head.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And thou shalt burn the whole ram upon the altar: it _is_ a burnt offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: it _is_ a sweet savour, an offering made by fire unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt take the other ram; and Aaron and his sons shall put their hands upon the head of the ram.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou kill the ram, and take of his blood, and put _it_ upon the tip of the right ear of Aaron, and upon the tip of the right ear of his sons, and upon the thumb of their right hand, and upon the great toe of their right foot, and sprinkle the blood upon the altar round about.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take of the blood that _is_ upon the altar, and of the anointing oil, and sprinkle _it_ upon Aaron, and upon his garments, and upon his sons, and upon the garments of his sons with him: and he shall be hallowed, and his garments, and his sons, and his sons’ garments with him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Also thou shalt take of the ram the fat and the rump, and the fat that covereth the inwards, and the caul _above_ the liver, and the two kidneys, and the fat that _is_ upon them, and the right shoulder; for it _is_ a ram of consecration:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And one loaf of bread, and one cake of oiled bread, and one wafer out of the basket of the unleavened bread that _is_ before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put all in the hands of Aaron, and in the hands of his sons; and shalt wave them _for_ a wave offering before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And thou shalt receive them of their hands, and burn _them_ upon the altar for a burnt offering, for a sweet savour before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: it _is_ an offering made by fire unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take the breast of the ram of Aaron’s consecration, and wave it _for_ a wave offering before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and it shall be thy part.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And thou shalt sanctify the breast of the wave offering, and the shoulder of the heave offering, which is waved, and which is heaved up, of the ram of the consecration, _even_ of _that_ which _is_ for Aaron, and of _that_ which is for his sons:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And it shall be Aaron’s and his sons’ by a statute for ever from the children of Israel: for it _is_ an heave offering: and it shall be an heave offering from the children of Israel of the sacrifice of their peace offerings, _even_ their heave offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ And the holy garments of Aaron shall be his sons’ after him, to be anointed therein, and to be consecrated in them.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>_And_ that son that is priest in his stead shall put them on seven days, when he cometh into the tabernacle of the congregation to minister in the holy _place_.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And thou shalt take the ram of the consecration, and seethe his flesh in the holy place.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And Aaron and his sons shall eat the flesh of the ram, and the bread that _is_ in the basket, _by_ the door of the tabernacle of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And they shall eat those things wherewith the atonement was made, to consecrate _and_ to sanctify them: but a stranger shall not eat _thereof_, because they _are_ holy.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And if ought of the flesh of the consecrations, or of the bread, remain unto the morning, then thou shalt burn the remainder with fire: it shall not be eaten, because it _is_ holy.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And thus shalt thou do unto Aaron, and to his sons, according to all _things_ which I have commanded thee: seven days shalt thou consecrate them.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And thou shalt offer every day a bullock _for_ a sin offering for atonement: and thou shalt cleanse the altar, when thou hast made an atonement for it, and thou shalt anoint it, to sanctify it.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Seven days thou shalt make an atonement for the altar, and sanctify it; and it shall be an altar most holy: whatsoever toucheth the altar shall be holy.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>¶ Now this _is that_ which thou shalt offer upon the altar; two lambs of the first year day by day continually.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>The one lamb thou shalt offer in the morning; and the other lamb thou shalt offer at even:  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And with the one lamb a tenth deal of flour mingled with the fourth part of an hin of beaten oil; and the fourth part of an hin of wine _for_ a drink offering.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And the other lamb thou shalt offer at even, and shalt do thereto according to the meat offering of the morning, and according to the drink offering thereof, for a sweet savour, an offering made by fire unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>_This shall be_ a continual burnt offering throughout your generations _at_ the door of the tabernacle of the congregation before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: where I will meet you, to speak there unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And there I will meet with the children of Israel, and _the tabernacle_ shall be sanctified by my glory.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And I will sanctify the tabernacle of the congregation, and the altar: I will sanctify also both Aaron and his sons, to minister to me in the priest’s office.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>¶ And I will dwell among the children of Israel, and will be their God.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God, that brought them forth out of the land of Egypt, that I may dwell among them: I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God.  

## CHAPTER 30.

And thou shalt make an altar to burn incense upon: _of_ shittim wood shalt thou make it.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>A cubit _shall be_ the length thereof, and a cubit the breadth thereof; foursquare shall it be: and two cubits _shall be_ the height thereof: the horns thereof _shall be_ of the same.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And thou shalt overlay it with pure gold, the top thereof, and the sides thereof round about, and the horns thereof; and thou shalt make unto it a crown of gold round about.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And two golden rings shalt thou make to it under the crown of it, by the two corners thereof, upon the two sides of it shalt thou make _it_; and they shall be for places for the staves to bear it withal.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make the staves _of_ shittim wood, and overlay them with gold.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put it before the vail that _is_ by the ark of the testimony, before the mercy seat that _is_ over the testimony, where I will meet with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And Aaron shall burn thereon sweet incense every morning: when he dresseth the lamps, he shall burn incense upon it.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And when Aaron lighteth the lamps at even, he shall burn incense upon it, a perpetual incense before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> throughout your generations.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Ye shall offer no strange incense thereon, nor burnt sacrifice, nor meat offering; neither shall ye pour drink offering thereon.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Aaron shall make an atonement upon the horns of it once in a year with the blood of the sin offering of atonements: once in the year shall he make atonement upon it throughout your generations: it _is_ most holy unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>When thou takest the sum of the children of Israel after their number, then shall they give every man a ransom for his soul unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when thou numberest them; that there be no plague among them, when _thou_ numberest them.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>This they shall give, every one that passeth among them that are numbered, half a shekel after the shekel of the sanctuary: (a shekel _is_ twenty gerahs:) an half shekel _shall be_ the offering of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Every one that passeth among them that are numbered, from twenty years old and above, shall give an offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The rich shall not give more, and the poor shall not give less than half a shekel, when _they_ give an offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to make an atonement for your souls.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take the atonement money of the children of Israel, and shalt appoint it for the service of the tabernacle of the congregation; that it may be a memorial unto the children of Israel before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to make an atonement for your souls.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Thou shalt also make a laver _of_ brass, and his foot _also of_ brass, to wash _withal_: and thou shalt put it between the tabernacle of the congregation and the altar, and thou shalt put water therein.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For Aaron and his sons shall wash their hands and their feet thereat:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>When they go into the tabernacle of the congregation, they shall wash with water, that they die not; or when they come near to the altar to minister, to burn offering made by fire unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>So they shall wash their hands and their feet, that they die not: and it shall be a statute for ever to them, _even_ to him and to his seed throughout their generations.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Moreover the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Take thou also unto thee principal spices, of pure myrrh five hundred _shekels_, and of sweet cinnamon half so much, _even_ two hundred and fifty _shekels_, and of sweet calamus two hundred and fifty _shekels_,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And of cassia five hundred _shekels_, after the shekel of the sanctuary, and of oil olive an hin:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make it an oil of holy ointment, an ointment compound after the art of the apothecary: it shall be an holy anointing oil.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And thou shalt anoint the tabernacle of the congregation therewith, and the ark of the testimony,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And the table and all his vessels, and the candlestick and his vessels, and the altar of incense,  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the altar of burnt offering with all his vessels, and the laver and his foot.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And thou shalt sanctify them, that they may be most holy: whatsoever toucheth them shall be holy.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And thou shalt anoint Aaron and his sons, and consecrate them, that _they_ may minister unto me in the priest’s office.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And thou shalt speak unto the children of Israel, saying, This shall be an holy anointing oil unto me throughout your generations.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Upon man’s flesh shall it not be poured, neither shall ye make _any other_ like it, after the composition of it: it _is_ holy, _and_ it shall be holy unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Whosoever compoundeth _any_ like it, or whosoever putteth _any_ of it upon a stranger, shall even be cut off from his people.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Take unto thee sweet spices, stacte, and onycha, and galbanum; _these_ sweet spices with pure frankincense: of each shall there be a like _weight_:  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And thou shalt make it a perfume, a confection after the art of the apothecary, tempered together, pure _and_ holy:  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And thou shalt beat _some_ of it very small, and put of it before the testimony in the tabernacle of the congregation, where I will meet with thee: it shall be unto you most holy.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And _as for_ the perfume which thou shalt make, ye shall not make to yourselves according to the composition thereof: it shall be unto thee holy for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>Whosoever shall make like unto that, to smell thereto, shall even be cut off from his people.  

## CHAPTER 31.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>See, I have called by name Bezaleel the son of Uri, the son of Hur, of the tribe of Judah:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And I have filled him with the spirit of God, in wisdom, and in understanding, and in knowledge, and in all manner of workmanship,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>To devise cunning works, to work in gold, and in silver, and in brass,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And in cutting of stones, to set _them_, and in carving of timber, to work in all manner of workmanship.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And I, behold, I have given with him Aholiab, the son of Ahisamach, of the tribe of Dan: and in the hearts of all that are wise hearted I have put wisdom, that they may make all that I have commanded thee;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The tabernacle of the congregation, and the ark of the testimony, and the mercy seat that _is_ thereupon, and all the furniture of the tabernacle,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the table and his furniture, and the pure candlestick with all his furniture, and the altar of incense,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the altar of burnt offering with all his furniture, and the laver and his foot,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the cloths of service, and the holy garments for Aaron the priest, and the garments of his sons, to minister in the priest’s office,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the anointing oil, and sweet incense for the holy _place_: according to all that I have commanded thee shall they do.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Speak thou also unto the children of Israel, saying, Verily my sabbaths ye shall keep: for it _is_ a sign between me and you throughout your generations; that _ye_ may know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that doth sanctify you.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Ye shall keep the sabbath therefore; for it _is_ holy unto you: every one that defileth it shall surely be put to death: for whosoever doeth _any_ work therein, that soul shall be cut off from among his people.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Six days may work be done; but in the seventh _is_ the sabbath of rest, holy to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: whosoever doeth _any_ work in the sabbath day, he shall surely be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Wherefore the children of Israel shall keep the sabbath, to observe the sabbath throughout their generations, _for_ a perpetual covenant.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>It _is_ a sign between me and the children of Israel for ever: for _in_ six days the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> made heaven and earth, and on the seventh day he rested, and was refreshed.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And he gave unto Moses, when he had made an end of communing with him upon mount Sinai, two tables of testimony, tables of stone, written with the finger of God.  

## CHAPTER 32.

And when the people saw that Moses delayed to come down out of the mount, the people gathered themselves together unto Aaron, and said unto him, Up, make us gods, which shall go before us; for _as for_ this Moses, the man that brought us up out of the land of Egypt, we wot not what is become of him.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Aaron said unto them, Break off the golden earrings, which _are_ in the ears of your wives, of your sons, and of your daughters, and bring _them_ unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And all the people brake off the golden earrings which _were_ in their ears, and brought _them_ unto Aaron.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And he received _them_ at their hand, and fashioned it with a graving tool, after he had made it a molten calf: and they said, These _be_ thy gods, O Israel, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And when Aaron saw _it_, he built an altar before it; and Aaron made proclamation, and said, To morrow _is_ a feast to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they rose up early on the morrow, and offered burnt offerings, and brought peace offerings; and the people sat down to eat and to drink, and rose up to play.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Go, get thee down; for thy people, which thou broughtest out of the land of Egypt, have corrupted _themselves_:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>They have turned aside quickly out of the way which I commanded them: they have made them a molten calf, and have worshipped it, and have sacrificed thereunto, and said, These _be_ thy gods, O Israel, which have brought thee up out of the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, I have seen this people, and, behold, it _is_ a stiffnecked people:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Now therefore let me alone, that my wrath may wax hot against them, and that I may consume them: and I will make of thee a great nation.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Moses besought the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> his God, and said, <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, why doth thy wrath wax hot against thy people, which thou hast brought forth out of the land of Egypt with great power, and with a mighty hand?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Wherefore should the Egyptians speak, and say, For mischief did he bring them out, to slay them in the mountains, and to consume them from the face of the earth? Turn from thy fierce wrath, and repent of this evil against thy people.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Remember Abraham, Isaac, and Israel, thy servants, to whom thou swarest by thine own self, and saidst unto them, I will multiply your seed as the stars of heaven, and all this land that I have spoken of will I give unto your seed, and they shall inherit _it_ for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> repented of the evil which he thought to do unto his people.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses turned, and went down from the mount, and the two tables of the testimony _were_ in his hand: the tables _were_ written on both their sides; on the one side and on the other _were_ they written.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the tables _were_ the work of God, and the writing _was_ the writing of God, graven upon the tables.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And when Joshua heard the noise of the people as they shouted, he said unto Moses, _There is_ a noise of war in the camp.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And he said, _It is_ not the voice of _them that_ shout for mastery, neither _is it_ the voice of _them that_ cry for being overcome: _but_ the noise of _them that_ sing do I hear.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, as soon as he came nigh unto the camp, that he saw the calf, and the dancing: and Moses’ anger waxed hot, and he cast the tables out of his hands, and brake them beneath the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And he took the calf which they had made, and burnt _it_ in the fire, and ground _it_ to powder, and strawed _it_ upon the water, and made the children of Israel drink _of it_.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto Aaron, What did this people unto thee, that thou hast brought so great a sin upon them?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Aaron said, Let not the anger of my lord wax hot: thou knowest the people, that they _are set_ on mischief.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For they said unto me, Make us gods, which shall go before us: for _as for_ this Moses, the man that brought us up out of the land of Egypt, we wot not what is become of him.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And I said unto them, Whosoever hath any gold, let them break _it_ off. So they gave _it_ me: then I cast it into the fire, and there came out this calf.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And when Moses saw that the people _were_ naked; (for Aaron had made them naked unto _their_ shame among their enemies:)  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Then Moses stood in the gate of the camp, and said, Who _is_ on the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> side? _let him come_ unto me. And all the sons of Levi gathered themselves together unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel, Put every man his sword by his side, _and_ go in and out from gate to gate throughout the camp, and slay every man his brother, and every man his companion, and every man his neighbour.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the children of Levi did according to the word of Moses: and there fell of the people that day about three thousand men.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For Moses had said, Consecrate yourselves to day to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, even every man upon his son, and upon his brother; that he may bestow upon you a blessing this day.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass on the morrow, that Moses said unto the people, Ye have sinned a great sin: and now I will go up unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; peradventure I shall make an atonement for your sin.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And Moses returned unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, Oh, this people have sinned a great sin, and have made them gods of gold.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Yet now, if thou wilt forgive their sin—; and if not, blot me, I pray thee, out of thy book which thou hast written.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Whosoever hath sinned against me, him will I blot out of my book.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Therefore now go, lead the people unto _the place_ of which I have spoken unto thee: behold, mine Angel shall go before thee: nevertheless in the day when I visit I will visit their sin upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> plagued the people, because they made the calf, which Aaron made.  

## CHAPTER 33.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Depart, _and_ go up hence, thou and the people which thou hast brought up out of the land of Egypt, unto the land which I sware unto Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, saying, Unto thy seed will I give it:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And I will send an angel before thee; and I will drive out the Canaanite, the Amorite, and the Hittite, and the Perizzite, the Hivite, and the Jebusite:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Unto a land flowing with milk and honey: for I will not go up in the midst of thee; for thou _art_ a stiffnecked people: lest I consume thee in the way.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And when the people heard these evil tidings, they mourned: and no man did put on him his ornaments.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had said unto Moses, Say unto the children of Israel, Ye _are_ a stiffnecked people: I will come up into the midst of thee in a moment, and consume thee: therefore now put off thy ornaments from thee, that I may know what to do unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the children of Israel stripped themselves of their ornaments by the mount Horeb.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses took the tabernacle, and pitched it without the camp, afar off from the camp, and called it the Tabernacle of the congregation. And it came to pass, _that_ every one which sought the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> went out unto the tabernacle of the congregation, which _was_ without the camp.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when Moses went out unto the tabernacle, _that_ all the people rose up, and stood every man _at_ his tent door, and looked after Moses, until he was gone into the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, as Moses entered into the tabernacle, the cloudy pillar descended, and stood _at_ the door of the tabernacle, and _the Lord_ talked with Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And all the people saw the cloudy pillar stand _at_ the tabernacle door: and all the people rose up and worshipped, every man _in_ his tent door.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses face to face, as a man speaketh unto his friend. And he turned again into the camp: but his servant Joshua, the son of Nun, a young man, departed not out of the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And Moses said unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, See, thou sayest unto me, Bring up this people: and thou hast not let me know whom thou wilt send with me. Yet thou hast said, I know thee by name, and thou hast also found grace in my sight.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Now therefore, I pray thee, if I have found grace in thy sight, shew me now thy way, that I may know thee, that I may find grace in thy sight: and consider that this nation _is_ thy people.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he said, My presence shall go _with thee_, and I will give thee rest.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, If thy presence go not _with me_, carry us not up hence.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For wherein shall it be known here that I and thy people have found grace in thy sight? _is it_ not in that thou goest with us? so shall we be separated, I and thy people, from all the people that _are_ upon the face of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, I will do this thing also that thou hast spoken: for thou hast found grace in my sight, and I know thee by name.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And he said, I beseech thee, shew me thy glory.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And he said, I will make all my goodness pass before thee, and I will proclaim the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> before thee; and will be gracious to whom I will be gracious, and will shew mercy on whom I will shew mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And he said, Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said, Behold, _there is_ a place by me, and thou shalt stand upon a rock:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, while my glory passeth by, that I will put thee in a clift of the rock, and will cover thee with my hand while I pass by:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And I will take away mine hand, and thou shalt see my back parts: but my face shall not be seen.  

## CHAPTER 34.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Moses, Hew thee two tables of stone like unto the first: and I will write upon _these_ tables the words that were in the first tables, which thou brakest.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And be ready in the morning, and come up in the morning unto mount Sinai, and present thyself there to me in the top of the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And no man shall come up with thee, neither let any man be seen throughout all the mount; neither let the flocks nor herds feed before that mount.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And he hewed two tables of stone like unto the first; and Moses rose up early in the morning, and went up unto mount Sinai, as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had commanded him, and took in his hand the two tables of stone.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> descended in the cloud, and stood with him there, and proclaimed the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> passed by before him, and proclaimed, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Keeping mercy for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, and that will by no means clear _the guilty_; visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children, and upon the children’s children, unto the third and to the fourth _generation_.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Moses made haste, and bowed his head toward the earth, and worshipped.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he said, If now I have found grace in thy sight, O Lord, let my Lord, I pray thee, go among us; for it _is_ a stiffnecked people; and pardon our iniquity and our sin, and take us for thine inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And he said, Behold, I make a covenant: before all thy people I will do marvels, such as have not been done in all the earth, nor in any nation: and all the people among which thou _art_ shall see the work of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: for it _is_ a terrible thing that I will do with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Observe thou that which I command thee this day: behold, I drive out before thee the Amorite, and the Canaanite, and the Hittite, and the Perizzite, and the Hivite, and the Jebusite.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Take heed to thyself, lest thou make a covenant with the inhabitants of the land whither thou goest, lest it be for a snare in the midst of thee:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But ye shall destroy their altars, break their images, and cut down their groves:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For thou shalt worship no other god: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, whose name _is_ Jealous, _is_ a jealous God:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Lest thou make a covenant with the inhabitants of the land, and they go a whoring after their gods, and do sacrifice unto their gods, and _one_ call thee, and thou eat of his sacrifice;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And thou take of their daughters unto thy sons, and their daughters go a whoring after their gods, and make thy sons go a whoring after their gods.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thou shalt make thee no molten gods.

<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And they did beat the gold into thin plates, and cut _it into_ wires, to work _it_ in the blue, and in the purple, and in the scarlet, and in the fine linen, _with_ cunning work.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>They made shoulderpieces for it, to couple _it_ together: by the two edges was it coupled together.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the curious girdle of his ephod, that _was_ upon it, _was_ of the same, according to the work thereof; _of_ gold, blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And they wrought onyx stones inclosed in ouches of gold, graven, as signets are graven, with the names of the children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he put them on the shoulders of the ephod, _that they should be_ stones for a memorial to the children of Israel; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And he made the breastplate _of_ cunning work, like the work of the ephod; _of_ gold, blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>It was foursquare; they made the breastplate double: a span _was_ the length thereof, and a span the breadth thereof, _being_ doubled.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they set in it four rows of stones: _the first_ row _was_ a sardius, a topaz, and a carbuncle: this _was_ the first row.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the second row, an emerald, a sapphire, and a diamond.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the third row, a ligure, an agate, and an amethyst.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the fourth row, a beryl, an onyx, and a jasper: _they were_ inclosed in ouches of gold in their inclosings.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the stones _were_ according to the names of the children of Israel, twelve, according to their names, _like_ the engravings of a signet, every one with his name, according to the twelve tribes.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And they made upon the breastplate chains at the ends, _of_ wreathen work _of_ pure gold.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And they made two ouches _of_ gold, and two gold rings; and put the two rings in the two ends of the breastplate.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And they put the two wreathen chains of gold in the two rings on the ends of the breastplate.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the two ends of the two wreathen chains they fastened in the two ouches, and put them on the shoulderpieces of the ephod, before it.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And they made two rings of gold, and put _them_ on the two ends of the breastplate, upon the border of it, which _was_ on the side of the ephod inward.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And they made two _other_ golden rings, and put them on the two sides of the ephod underneath, toward the forepart of it, over against the _other_ coupling thereof, above the curious girdle of the ephod.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And they did bind the breastplate by his rings unto the rings of the ephod with a lace of blue, that it might be above the curious girdle of the ephod, and that the breastplate might not be loosed from the ephod; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And he made the robe of the ephod _of_ woven work, all _of_ blue.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And _there was_ an hole in the midst of the robe, as the hole of an habergeon, _with_ a band round about the hole, that it should not rend.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they made upon the hems of the robe pomegranates _of_ blue, and purple, and scarlet, _and_ twined _linen_.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And they made bells _of_ pure gold, and put the bells between the pomegranates upon the hem of the robe, round about between the pomegranates;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>A bell and a pomegranate, a bell and a pomegranate, round about the hem of the robe to minister _in_; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And they made coats _of_ fine linen _of_ woven work for Aaron, and for his sons,  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And a mitre _of_ fine linen, and goodly bonnets _of_ fine linen, and linen breeches _of_ fine twined linen,  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And a girdle _of_ fine twined linen, and blue, and purple, and scarlet, _of_ needlework; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ And they made the plate of the holy crown _of_ pure gold, and wrote upon it a writing, _like to_ the engravings of a signet, HOLINESS TO THE <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And they tied unto it a lace of blue, to fasten _it_ on high upon the mitre; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ Thus was all the work of the tabernacle of the tent of the congregation finished: and the children of Israel did according to all that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses, so did they.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ And they brought the tabernacle unto Moses, the tent, and all his furniture, his taches, his boards, his bars, and his pillars, and his sockets,  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And the covering of rams’ skins dyed red, and the covering of badgers’ skins, and the vail of the covering,  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>The ark of the testimony, and the staves thereof, and the mercy seat,  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>The table, _and_ all the vessels thereof, and the shewbread,  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>The pure candlestick, _with_ the lamps thereof, _even with_ the lamps to be set in order, and all the vessels thereof, and the oil for light,  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And the golden altar, and the anointing oil, and the sweet incense, and the hanging for the tabernacle door,  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>The brasen altar, and his grate of brass, his staves, and all his vessels, the laver and his foot,  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>The hangings of the court, his pillars, and his sockets, and the hanging for the court gate, his cords, and his pins, and all the vessels of the service of the tabernacle, for the tent of the congregation,  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>The cloths of service to do service in the holy _place_, and the holy garments for Aaron the priest, and his sons’ garments, to minister in the priest’s office.

## CHAPTER 40.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto Moses, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>On the first day of the first month shalt thou set up the tabernacle of the tent of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put therein the ark of the testimony, and cover the ark with the vail.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And thou shalt bring in the table, and set in order the things that are to be set in order upon it; and thou shalt bring in the candlestick, and light the lamps thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And thou shalt set the altar of gold for the incense before the ark of the testimony, and put the hanging of the door to the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And thou shalt set the altar of the burnt offering before the door of the tabernacle of the tent of the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And thou shalt set the laver between the tent of the congregation and the altar, and shalt put water therein.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And thou shalt set up the court round about, and hang up the hanging at the court gate.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take the anointing oil, and anoint the tabernacle, and all that _is_ therein, and shalt hallow it, and all the vessels thereof: and it shall be holy.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And thou shalt anoint the altar of the burnt offering, and all his vessels, and sanctify the altar: and it shall be an altar most holy.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And thou shalt anoint the laver and his foot, and sanctify it.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And thou shalt bring Aaron and his sons unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, and wash them with water.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And thou shalt put upon Aaron the holy garments, and anoint him, and sanctify him; that he may minister unto me in the priest’s office.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And thou shalt bring his sons, and clothe them with coats:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And thou shalt anoint them, as thou didst anoint their father, that they may minister unto me in the priest’s office: for their anointing shall surely be an everlasting priesthood throughout their generations.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Thus did Moses: according to all that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded him, so did he.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass in the first month in the second year, on the first _day_ of the month, _that_ the tabernacle was reared up.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Moses reared up the tabernacle, and fastened his sockets, and set up the boards thereof, and put in the bars thereof, and reared up his pillars.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And he spread abroad the tent over the tabernacle, and put the covering of the tent above upon it; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And he took and put the testimony into the ark, and set the staves on the ark, and put the mercy seat above upon the ark:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And he brought the ark into the tabernacle, and set up the vail of the covering, and covered the ark of the testimony; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And he put the table in the tent of the congregation, upon the side of the tabernacle northward, without the vail.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And he set the bread in order upon it before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And he put the candlestick in the tent of the congregation, over against the table, on the side of the tabernacle southward.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And he lighted the lamps before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And he put the golden altar in the tent of the congregation before the vail:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And he burnt sweet incense thereon; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ And he set up the hanging _at_ the door of the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And he put the altar of burnt offering _by_ the door of the tabernacle of the tent of the congregation, and offered upon it the burnt offering and the meat offering; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ And he set the laver between the tent of the congregation and the altar, and put water there, to wash _withal_.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And Moses and Aaron and his sons washed their hands and their feet thereat:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>When they went into the tent of the congregation, and when they came near unto the altar, they washed; as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And he reared up the court round about the tabernacle and the altar, and set up the hanging of the court gate. So Moses finished the work.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>¶ Then a cloud covered the tent of the congregation, and the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> filled the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And Moses was not able to enter into the tent of the congregation, because the cloud abode thereon, and the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> filled the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And when the cloud was taken up from over the tabernacle, the children of Israel went onward in all their journeys:  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>But if the cloud were not taken up, then they journeyed not till the day that it was taken up.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>For the cloud of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _was_ upon the tabernacle by day, and fire was on it by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel, throughout all their journeys.
