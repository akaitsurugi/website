# Hosea

### HOSEA

## CHAPTER 1.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that came unto Hosea, the son of Beeri, in the days of Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz, _and_ Hezekiah, kings of Judah, and in the days of Jeroboam the son of Joash, king of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ The beginning of the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> by Hosea. And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said to Hosea, Go, take unto thee a wife of whoredoms and children of whoredoms: for the land hath committed great whoredom, _departing_ from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>So he went and took Gomer the daughter of Diblaim; which conceived, and bare him a son.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto him, Call his name Jezreel; for yet a little _while_, and I will avenge the blood of Jezreel upon the house of Jehu, and will cause to cease the kingdom of the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass at that day, that I will break the bow of Israel in the valley of Jezreel.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And she conceived again, and bare a daughter. And _God_ said unto him, Call her name Lo–ruhamah: for I will no more have mercy upon the house of Israel; but I will utterly take them away.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But I will have mercy upon the house of Judah, and will save them by the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God, and will not save them by bow, nor by sword, nor by battle, by horses, nor by horsemen.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Now when she had weaned Lo–ruhamah, she conceived, and bare a son.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then said _God_, Call his name Lo–ammi: for ye _are_ not my people, and I will not be your _God_.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Yet the number of the children of Israel shall be as the sand of the sea, which cannot be measured nor numbered; and it shall come to pass, _that_ in the place where it was said unto them, Ye _are_ not my people, _there_ it shall be said unto them, _Ye are_ the sons of the living God.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then shall the children of Judah and the children of Israel be gathered together, and appoint themselves one head, and they shall come up out of the land: for great _shall be_ the day of Jezreel.  

## CHAPTER 2.

Say ye unto your brethren, Ammi; and to your sisters, Ru–hamah.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Plead with your mother, plead: for she _is_ not my wife, neither _am_ I her husband: let her therefore put away her whoredoms out of her sight, and her adulteries from between her breasts;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Lest I strip her naked, and set her as in the day that she was born, and make her as a wilderness, and set her like a dry land, and slay her with thirst.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And I will not have mercy upon her children; for they _be_ the children of whoredoms.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For their mother hath played the harlot: she that conceived them hath done shamefully: for she said, I will go after my lovers, that give _me_ my bread and my water, my wool and my flax, mine oil and my drink.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore, behold, I will hedge up thy way with thorns, and make a wall, that she shall not find her paths.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And she shall follow after her lovers, but she shall not overtake them; and she shall seek them, but shall not find _them_: then shall she say, I will go and return to my first husband; for then _was it_ better with me than now.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For she did not know that I gave her corn, and wine, and oil, and multiplied her silver and gold, _which_ they prepared for Baal.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Therefore will I return, and take away my corn in the time thereof, and my wine in the season thereof, and will recover my wool and my flax _given_ to cover her nakedness.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And now will I discover her lewdness in the sight of her lovers, and none shall deliver her out of mine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I will also cause all her mirth to cease, her feast days, her new moons, and her sabbaths, and all her solemn feasts.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And I will destroy her vines and her fig trees, whereof she hath said, These _are_ my rewards that my lovers have given me: and I will make them a forest, and the beasts of the field shall eat them.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And I will visit upon her the days of Baalim, wherein she burned incense to them, and she decked herself with her earrings and her jewels, and she went after her lovers, and forgat me, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore, behold, I will allure her, and bring her into the wilderness, and speak comfortably unto her.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And I will give her her vineyards from thence, and the valley of Achor for a door of hope: and she shall sing there, as in the days of her youth, and as in the day when she came up out of the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And it shall be at that day, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _that_ thou shalt call me Ishi; and shalt call me no more Baali.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For I will take away the names of Baalim out of her mouth, and they shall no more be remembered by their name.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And in that day will I make a covenant for them with the beasts of the field, and with the fowls of heaven, and _with_ the creeping things of the ground: and I will break the bow and the sword and the battle out of the earth, and will make them to lie down safely.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And I will betroth thee unto me for ever; yea, I will betroth thee unto me in righteousness, and in judgment, and in lovingkindness, and in mercies.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>I will even betroth thee unto me in faithfulness: and thou shalt know the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, I will hear, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I will hear the heavens, and they shall hear the earth;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the earth shall hear the corn, and the wine, and the oil; and they shall hear Jezreel.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And I will sow her unto me in the earth; and I will have mercy upon her that had not obtained mercy; and I will say to _them which were_ not my people, Thou _art_ my people; and they shall say, _Thou art_ my God.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Then said the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto me, Go yet, love a woman beloved of _her_ friend, yet an adulteress, according to the love of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> toward the children of Israel, who look to other gods, and love flagons of wine.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>So I bought her to me for fifteen _pieces_ of silver, and _for_ an homer of barley, and an half homer of barley:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And I said unto her, Thou shalt abide for me many days; thou shalt not play the harlot, and thou shalt not be for _another_ man: so _will_ I also _be_ for thee.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For the children of Israel shall abide many days without a king, and without a prince, and without a sacrifice, and without an image, and without an ephod, and _without_ teraphim:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Afterward shall the children of Israel return, and seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God, and David their king; and shall fear the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> and his goodness in the latter days.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, ye children of Israel: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath a controversy with the inhabitants of the land, because _there is_ no truth, nor mercy, nor knowledge of God in the land.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>By swearing, and lying, and killing, and stealing, and committing adultery, they break out, and blood toucheth blood.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore shall the land mourn, and every one that dwelleth therein shall languish, with the beasts of the field, and with the fowls of heaven; yea, the fishes of the sea also shall be taken away.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Yet let no man strive, nor reprove another: for thy people _are_ as they that strive with the priest.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Therefore shalt thou fall in the day, and the prophet also shall fall with thee in the night, and I will destroy thy mother.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge: because thou hast rejected knowledge, I will also reject thee, that thou shalt be no priest to me: seeing thou hast forgotten the law of thy God, I will also forget thy children.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>As they were increased, so they sinned against me: _therefore_ will I change their glory into shame.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>They eat up the sin of my people, and they set their heart on their iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And there shall be, like people, like priest: and I will punish them for their ways, and reward them their doings.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For they shall eat, and not have enough: they shall commit whoredom, and shall not increase: because they have left off to take heed to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Whoredom and wine and new wine take away the heart.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ My people ask counsel at their stocks, and their staff declareth unto them: for the spirit of whoredoms hath caused _them_ to err, and they have gone a whoring from under their God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>They sacrifice upon the tops of the mountains, and burn incense upon the hills, under oaks and poplars and elms, because the shadow thereof _is_ good: therefore your daughters shall commit whoredom, and your spouses shall commit adultery.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I will not punish your daughters when they commit whoredom, nor your spouses when they commit adultery: for themselves are separated with whores, and they sacrifice with harlots: therefore the people _that_ doth not understand shall fall.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Though thou, Israel, play the harlot, _yet_ let not Judah offend; and come not ye unto Gilgal, neither go ye up to Beth–aven, nor swear, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> liveth.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For Israel slideth back as a backsliding heifer: now the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will feed them as a lamb in a large place.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Ephraim _is_ joined to idols: let him alone.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Their drink is sour: they have committed whoredom continually: her rulers _with_ shame do love, Give ye.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The wind hath bound her up in her wings, and they shall be ashamed because of their sacrifices.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Hear ye this, O priests; and hearken, ye house of Israel; and give ye ear, O house of the king; for judgment _is_ toward you, because ye have been a snare on Mizpah, and a net spread upon Tabor.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the revolters are profound to make slaughter, though I _have been_ a rebuker of them all.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I know Ephraim, and Israel is not hid from me: for now, O Ephraim, thou committest whoredom, _and_ Israel is defiled.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>They will not frame their doings to turn unto their God: for the spirit of whoredoms _is_ in the midst of them, and they have not known the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the pride of Israel doth testify to his face: therefore shall Israel and Ephraim fall in their iniquity; Judah also shall fall with them.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>They shall go with their flocks and with their herds to seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; but they shall not find _him_; he hath withdrawn himself from them.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>They have dealt treacherously against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: for they have begotten strange children: now shall a month devour them with their portions.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Blow ye the cornet in Gibeah, _and_ the trumpet in Ramah: cry aloud _at_ Beth–aven, after thee, O Benjamin.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Ephraim shall be desolate in the day of rebuke: among the tribes of Israel have I made known that which shall surely be.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The princes of Judah were like them that remove the bound: _therefore_ I will pour out my wrath upon them like water.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Ephraim _is_ oppressed _and_ broken in judgment, because he willingly walked after the commandment.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Therefore _will_ I _be_ unto Ephraim as a moth, and to the house of Judah as rottenness.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>When Ephraim saw his sickness, and Judah _saw_ his wound, then went Ephraim to the Assyrian, and sent to king Jareb: yet could he not heal you, nor cure you of your wound.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For I _will be_ unto Ephraim as a lion, and as a young lion to the house of Judah: I, _even_ I, will tear and go away; I will take away, and none shall rescue _him_.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ I will go _and_ return to my place, till they acknowledge their offence, and seek my face: in their affliction they will seek me early.  

## CHAPTER 6.

Come, and let us return unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: for he hath torn, and he will heal us; he hath smitten, and he will bind us up.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>After two days will he revive us: in the third day he will raise us up, and we shall live in his sight.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Then shall we know, _if_ we follow on to know the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: his going forth is prepared as the morning; and he shall come unto us as the rain, as the latter _and_ former rain unto the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ O Ephraim, what shall I do unto thee? O Judah, what shall I do unto thee? for your goodness _is_ as a morning cloud, and as the early dew it goeth away.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Therefore have I hewed _them_ by the prophets; I have slain them by the words of my mouth: and thy judgments _are as_ the light _that_ goeth forth.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For I desired mercy, and not sacrifice; and the knowledge of God more than burnt offerings.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But they like men have transgressed the covenant: there have they dealt treacherously against me.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Gilead _is_ a city of them that work iniquity, _and is_ polluted with blood.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And as troops of robbers wait for a man, _so_ the company of priests murder in the way by consent: for they commit lewdness.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>I have seen an horrible thing in the house of Israel: there _is_ the whoredom of Ephraim, Israel is defiled.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Also, O Judah, he hath set an harvest for thee, when I returned the captivity of my people.  

## CHAPTER 7.

When I would have healed Israel, then the iniquity of Ephraim was discovered, and the wickedness of Samaria: for they commit falsehood; and the thief cometh in, _and_ the troop of robbers spoileth without.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And they consider not in their hearts _that_ I remember all their wickedness: now their own doings have beset them about; they are before my face.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>They make the king glad with their wickedness, and the princes with their lies.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>They _are_ all adulterers, as an oven heated by the baker, _who_ ceaseth from raising after he hath kneaded the dough, until it be leavened.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>In the day of our king the princes have made _him_ sick with bottles of wine; he stretched out his hand with scorners.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For they have made ready their heart like an oven, whiles they lie in wait: their baker sleepeth all the night; in the morning it burneth as a flaming fire.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>They are all hot as an oven, and have devoured their judges; all their kings are fallen: _there is_ none among them that calleth unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Ephraim, he hath mixed himself among the people; Ephraim is a cake not turned.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Strangers have devoured his strength, and he knoweth _it_ not: yea, gray hairs are here and there upon him, yet he knoweth not.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the pride of Israel testifieth to his face: and they do not return to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God, nor seek him for all this.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Ephraim also is like a silly dove without heart: they call to Egypt, they go to Assyria.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>When they shall go, I will spread my net upon them; I will bring them down as the fowls of the heaven; I will chastise them, as their congregation hath heard.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Woe unto them! for they have fled from me: destruction unto them! because they have transgressed against me: though I have redeemed them, yet they have spoken lies against me.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they have not cried unto me with their heart, when they howled upon their beds: they assemble themselves for corn and wine, _and_ they rebel against me.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Though I have bound _and_ strengthened their arms, yet do they imagine mischief against me.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>They return, _but_ not to the most High: they are like a deceitful bow: their princes shall fall by the sword for the rage of their tongue: this _shall be_ their derision in the land of Egypt.  

## CHAPTER 8.

_Set_ the trumpet to thy mouth. _He shall come_ as an eagle against the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, because they have transgressed my covenant, and trespassed against my law.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Israel shall cry unto me, My God, we know thee.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Israel hath cast off _the thing that is_ good: the enemy shall pursue him.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>They have set up kings, but not by me: they have made princes, and I knew _it_ not: of their silver and their gold have they made them idols, that they may be cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Thy calf, O Samaria, hath cast _thee_ off; mine anger is kindled against them: how long _will it be_ ere they attain to innocency?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For from Israel _was_ it also: the workman made it; therefore it _is_ not God: but the calf of Samaria shall be broken in pieces.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For they have sown the wind, and they shall reap the whirlwind: it hath no stalk: the bud shall yield no meal: if so be it yield, the strangers shall swallow it up.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Israel is swallowed up: now shall they be among the Gentiles as a vessel wherein _is_ no pleasure.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For they are gone up to Assyria, a wild ass alone by himself: Ephraim hath hired lovers.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Yea, though they have hired among the nations, now will I gather them, and they shall sorrow a little for the burden of the king of princes.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Because Ephraim hath made many altars to sin, altars shall be unto him to sin.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I have written to him the great things of my law, _but_ they were counted as a strange thing.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>They sacrifice flesh _for_ the sacrifices of mine offerings, and eat _it; but_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> accepteth them not; now will he remember their iniquity, and visit their sins: they shall return to Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For Israel hath forgotten his Maker, and buildeth temples; and Judah hath multiplied fenced cities: but I will send a fire upon his cities, and it shall devour the palaces thereof.  

## CHAPTER 9.

Rejoice not, O Israel, for joy, as _other_ people: for thou hast gone a whoring from thy God, thou hast loved a reward upon every cornfloor.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The floor and the winepress shall not feed them, and the new wine shall fail in her.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>They shall not dwell in the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> land; but Ephraim shall return to Egypt, and they shall eat unclean _things_ in Assyria.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>They shall not offer wine _offerings_ to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, neither shall they be pleasing unto him: their sacrifices _shall be_ unto them as the bread of mourners; all that eat thereof shall be polluted: for their bread for their soul shall not come into the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>What will ye do in the solemn day, and in the day of the feast of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For, lo, they are gone because of destruction: Egypt shall gather them up, Memphis shall bury them: the pleasant _places_ for their silver, nettles shall possess them: thorns _shall be_ in their tabernacles.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ The days of visitation are come, the days of recompence are come; Israel shall know _it_: the prophet _is_ a fool, the spiritual man _is_ mad, for the multitude of thine iniquity, and the great hatred.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The watchman of Ephraim _was_ with my God: _but_ the prophet _is_ a snare of a fowler in all his ways, _and_ hatred in the house of his God.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>They have deeply corrupted _themselves_, as in the days of Gibeah: _therefore_ he will remember their iniquity, he will visit their sins.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>I found Israel like grapes in the wilderness; I saw your fathers as the firstripe in the fig tree at her first time: _but_ they went to Baal–peor, and separated themselves unto _that_ shame; and _their_ abominations were according as they loved.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ _As for_ Ephraim, their glory shall fly away like a bird, from the birth, and from the womb, and from the conception.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Though they bring up their children, yet will I bereave them, _that there shall_ not _be_ a man _left_: yea, woe also to them when I depart from them!  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Ephraim, as I saw Tyrus, _is_ planted in a pleasant place: but Ephraim shall bring forth his children to the murderer.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Give them, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: what wilt thou give? give them a miscarrying womb and dry breasts.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>All their wickedness _is_ in Gilgal: for there I hated them: for the wickedness of their doings I will drive them out of mine house, I will love them no more: all their princes _are_ revolters.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Ephraim is smitten, their root is dried up, they shall bear no fruit: yea, though they bring forth, yet will I slay _even_ the beloved _fruit_ of their womb.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>My God will cast them away, because they did not hearken unto him: and they shall be wanderers among the nations.  

## CHAPTER 10.

Israel _is_ an empty vine, he bringeth forth fruit unto himself: according to the multitude of his fruit he hath increased the altars; according to the goodness of his land they have made goodly images.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Their heart is divided; now shall they be found faulty: he shall break down their altars, he shall spoil their images.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For now they shall say, We have no king, because we feared not the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; what then should a king do to us?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>They have spoken words, swearing falsely in making a covenant: thus judgment springeth up as hemlock in the furrows of the field.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The inhabitants of Samaria shall fear because of the calves of Beth–aven: for the people thereof shall mourn over it, and the priests thereof _that_ rejoiced on it, for the glory thereof, because it is departed from it.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>It shall be also carried unto Assyria _for_ a present to king Jareb: Ephraim shall receive shame, and Israel shall be ashamed of his own counsel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_As for_ Samaria, her king is cut off as the foam upon the water.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The high places also of Aven, the sin of Israel, shall be destroyed: the thorn and the thistle shall come up on their altars; and they shall say to the mountains, Cover us; and to the hills, Fall on us.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ O Israel, thou hast sinned from the days of Gibeah: there they stood: the battle in Gibeah against the children of iniquity did not overtake them.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>_It is_ in my desire that I should chastise them; and the people shall be gathered against them, when they shall bind themselves in their two furrows.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Ephraim _is as_ an heifer _that is_ taught, _and_ loveth to tread out _the corn_; but I passed over upon her fair neck: I will make Ephraim to ride; Judah shall plow, _and_ Jacob shall break his clods.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Sow to yourselves in righteousness, reap in mercy; break up your fallow ground: for _it is_ time to seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, till he come and rain righteousness upon you.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Ye have plowed wickedness, ye have reaped iniquity; ye have eaten the fruit of lies: because thou didst trust in thy way, in the multitude of thy mighty men.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore shall a tumult arise among thy people, and all thy fortresses shall be spoiled, as Shalman spoiled Beth–arbel in the day of battle: the mother was dashed in pieces upon _her_ children.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So shall Beth–el do unto you because of your great wickedness: in a morning shall the king of Israel utterly be cut off.  

## CHAPTER 11.

When Israel _was_ a child, then I loved him, and called my son out of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>_As_ they called them, so they went from them: they sacrificed unto Baalim, and burned incense to graven images.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I taught Ephraim also to go, taking them by their arms; but they knew not that I healed them.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I drew them with cords of a man, with bands of love: and I was to them as they that take off the yoke on their jaws, and I laid meat unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ He shall not return into the land of Egypt, but the Assyrian shall be his king, because they refused to return.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the sword shall abide on his cities, and shall consume his branches, and devour _them_, because of their own counsels.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And my people are bent to backsliding from me: though they called them to the most High, none at all would exalt _him_.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ How shall I give thee up, Ephraim? _how_ shall I deliver thee, Israel? how shall I make thee as Admah? _how_ shall I set thee as Zeboim? mine heart is turned within me, my repentings are kindled together.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>I will not execute the fierceness of mine anger, I will not return to destroy Ephraim: for I _am_ God, and not man; the Holy One in the midst of thee: and I will not enter into the city.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>They shall walk after the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: he shall roar like a lion: when he shall roar, then the children shall tremble from the west.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>They shall tremble as a bird out of Egypt, and as a dove out of the land of Assyria: and I will place them in their houses, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Ephraim compasseth me about with lies, and the house of Israel with deceit: but Judah yet ruleth with God, and is faithful with the saints.  

## CHAPTER 12.

Ephraim feedeth on wind, and followeth after the east wind: he daily increaseth lies and desolation; and they do make a covenant with the Assyrians, and oil is carried into Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath also a controversy with Judah, and will punish Jacob according to his ways; according to his doings will he recompense him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ He took his brother by the heel in the womb, and by his strength he had power with God:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Yea, he had power over the angel, and prevailed: he wept, and made supplication unto him: he found him _in_ Beth–el, and there he spake with us;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Even the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of hosts; the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ his memorial.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Therefore turn thou to thy God: keep mercy and judgment, and wait on thy God continually.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ _He is_ a merchant, the balances of deceit _are_ in his hand: he loveth to oppress.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Ephraim said, Yet I am become rich, I have found me out substance: _in_ all my labours they shall find none iniquity in me that _were_ sin.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And I _that am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God from the land of Egypt will yet make thee to dwell in tabernacles, as in the days of the solemn feast.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>I have also spoken by the prophets, and I have multiplied visions, and used similitudes, by the ministry of the prophets.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_Is there_ iniquity _in_ Gilead? surely they are vanity: they sacrifice bullocks in Gilgal; yea, their altars _are_ as heaps in the furrows of the fields.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Jacob fled into the country of Syria, and Israel served for a wife, and for a wife he kept _sheep_.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And by a prophet the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> brought Israel out of Egypt, and by a prophet was he preserved.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Ephraim provoked _him_ to anger most bitterly: therefore shall he leave his blood upon him, and his reproach shall his Lord return unto him.  

## CHAPTER 13.

When Ephraim spake trembling, he exalted himself in Israel; but when he offended in Baal, he died.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And now they sin more and more, and have made them molten images of their silver, _and_ idols according to their own understanding, all of it the work of the craftsmen: they say of them, Let the men that sacrifice kiss the calves.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore they shall be as the morning cloud, and as the early dew that passeth away, as the chaff _that_ is driven with the whirlwind out of the floor, and as the smoke out of the chimney.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Yet I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God from the land of Egypt, and thou shalt know no god but me: for _there is_ no saviour beside me.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ I did know thee in the wilderness, in the land of great drought.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>According to their pasture, so were they filled; they were filled, and their heart was exalted; therefore have they forgotten me.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore I will be unto them as a lion: as a leopard by the way will I observe _them_:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>I will meet them as a bear _that is_ bereaved _of her whelps_, and will rend the caul of their heart, and there will I devour them like a lion: the wild beast shall tear them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ O Israel, thou hast destroyed thyself; but in me _is_ thine help.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>I will be thy king: where _is any other_ that may save thee in all thy cities? and thy judges of whom thou saidst, Give me a king and princes?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I gave thee a king in mine anger, and took _him_ away in my wrath.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The iniquity of Ephraim _is_ bound up; his sin _is_ hid.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The sorrows of a travailing woman shall come upon him: he _is_ an unwise son; for he should not stay long in _the place of_ the breaking forth of children.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I will ransom them from the power of the grave; I will redeem them from death: O death, I will be thy plagues; O grave, I will be thy destruction: repentance shall be hid from mine eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Though he be fruitful among _his_ brethren, an east wind shall come, the wind of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall come up from the wilderness, and his spring shall become dry, and his fountain shall be dried up: he shall spoil the treasure of all pleasant vessels.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Samaria shall become desolate; for she hath rebelled against her God: they shall fall by the sword: their infants shall be dashed in pieces, and their women with child shall be ripped up.  

## CHAPTER 14.

O Israel, return unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God; for thou hast fallen by thine iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Take with you words, and turn to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: say unto him, Take away all iniquity, and receive _us_ graciously: so will we render the calves of our lips.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Asshur shall not save us; we will not ride upon horses: neither will we say any more to the work of our hands, _Ye are_ our gods: for in thee the fatherless findeth mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ I will heal their backsliding, I will love them freely: for mine anger is turned away from him.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>I will be as the dew unto Israel: he shall grow as the lily, and cast forth his roots as Lebanon.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>His branches shall spread, and his beauty shall be as the olive tree, and his smell as Lebanon.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>They that dwell under his shadow shall return; they shall revive _as_ the corn, and grow as the vine: the scent thereof _shall be_ as the wine of Lebanon.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Ephraim _shall say_, What have I to do any more with idols? I have heard _him_, and observed him: I _am_ like a green fir tree. From me is thy fruit found.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Who _is_ wise, and he shall understand these _things_? prudent, and he shall know them? for the ways of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _are_ right, and the just shall walk in them: but the transgressors shall fall therein.
