# Job

### THE BOOK OF JOB

## CHAPTER 1.

There was a man in the land of Uz, whose name _was_ Job; and that man was perfect and upright, and one that feared God, and eschewed evil.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And there were born unto him seven sons and three daughters.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>His substance also was seven thousand sheep, and three thousand camels, and five hundred yoke of oxen, and five hundred she asses, and a very great household; so that this man was the greatest of all the men of the east.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And his sons went and feasted _in their_ houses, every one his day; and sent and called for their three sisters to eat and to drink with them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And it was so, when the days of _their_ feasting were gone about, that Job sent and sanctified them, and rose up early in the morning, and offered burnt offerings _according_ to the number of them all: for Job said, It may be that my sons have sinned, and cursed God in their hearts. Thus did Job continually.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Now there was a day when the sons of God came to present themselves before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and Satan came also among them.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Satan, Whence comest thou? Then Satan answered the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, From going to and fro in the earth, and from walking up and down in it.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Satan, Hast thou considered my servant Job, that _there is_ none like him in the earth, a perfect and an upright man, one that feareth God, and escheweth evil?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then Satan answered the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, Doth Job fear God for nought?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Hast not thou made an hedge about him, and about his house, and about all that he hath on every side? thou hast blessed the work of his hands, and his substance is increased in the land.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But put forth thine hand now, and touch all that he hath, and he will curse thee to thy face.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Satan, Behold, all that he hath _is_ in thy power; only upon himself put not forth thine hand. So Satan went forth from the presence of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And there was a day when his sons and his daughters _were_ eating and drinking wine in their eldest brother’s house:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And there came a messenger unto Job, and said, The oxen were plowing, and the asses feeding beside them:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the Sabeans fell _upon them_, and took them away; yea, they have slain the servants with the edge of the sword; and I only am escaped alone to tell thee.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>While he _was_ yet speaking, there came also another, and said, The fire of God is fallen from heaven, and hath burned up the sheep, and the servants, and consumed them; and I only am escaped alone to tell thee.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>While he _was_ yet speaking, there came also another, and said, The Chaldeans made out three bands, and fell upon the camels, and have carried them away, yea, and slain the servants with the edge of the sword; and I only am escaped alone to tell thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>While he _was_ yet speaking, there came also another, and said, Thy sons and thy daughters _were_ eating and drinking wine in their eldest brother’s house:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And, behold, there came a great wind from the wilderness, and smote the four corners of the house, and it fell upon the young men, and they are dead; and I only am escaped alone to tell thee.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Then Job arose, and rent his mantle, and shaved his head, and fell down upon the ground, and worshipped,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And said, Naked came I out of my mother’s womb, and naked shall I return thither: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> gave, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath taken away; blessed be the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>In all this Job sinned not, nor charged God foolishly.  

## CHAPTER 2.

Again there was a day when the sons of God came to present themselves before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and Satan came also among them to present himself before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Satan, From whence comest thou? And Satan answered the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, From going to and fro in the earth, and from walking up and down in it.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Satan, Hast thou considered my servant Job, that _there is_ none like him in the earth, a perfect and an upright man, one that feareth God, and escheweth evil? and still he holdeth fast his integrity, although thou movedst me against him, to destroy him without cause.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Satan answered the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, Skin for skin, yea, all that a man hath will he give for his life.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But put forth thine hand now, and touch his bone and his flesh, and he will curse thee to thy face.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto Satan, Behold, he _is_ in thine hand; but save his life.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ So went Satan forth from the presence of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and smote Job with sore boils from the sole of his foot unto his crown.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he took him a potsherd to scrape himself withal; and he sat down among the ashes.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Then said his wife unto him, Dost thou still retain thine integrity? curse God, and die.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But he said unto her, Thou speakest as one of the foolish women speaketh. What? shall we receive good at the hand of God, and shall we not receive evil? In all this did not Job sin with his lips.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Now when Job’s three friends heard of all this evil that was come upon him, they came every one from his own place; Eliphaz the Temanite, and Bildad the Shuhite, and Zophar the Naamathite: for they had made an appointment together to come to mourn with him and to comfort him.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And when they lifted up their eyes afar off, and knew him not, they lifted up their voice, and wept; and they rent every one his mantle, and sprinkled dust upon their heads toward heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>So they sat down with him upon the ground seven days and seven nights, and none spake a word unto him: for they saw that _his_ grief was very great.  

## CHAPTER 3.

After this opened Job his mouth, and cursed his day.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Job spake, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Let the day perish wherein I was born, and the night _in which_ it was said, There is a man child conceived.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Let that day be darkness; let not God regard it from above, neither let the light shine upon it.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Let darkness and the shadow of death stain it; let a cloud dwell upon it; let the blackness of the day terrify it.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>As _for_ that night, let darkness seize upon it; let it not be joined unto the days of the year, let it not come into the number of the months.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Lo, let that night be solitary, let no joyful voice come therein.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Let them curse it that curse the day, who are ready to raise up their mourning.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Let the stars of the twilight thereof be dark; let it look for light, but _have_ none; neither let it see the dawning of the day:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Because it shut not up the doors of my _mother’s_ womb, nor hid sorrow from mine eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Why died I not from the womb? _why_ did I _not_ give up the ghost when I came out of the belly?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Why did the knees prevent me? or why the breasts that I should suck?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For now should I have lain still and been quiet, I should have slept: then had I been at rest,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>With kings and counsellors of the earth, which built desolate places for themselves;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Or with princes that had gold, who filled their houses with silver:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Or as an hidden untimely birth I had not been; as infants _which_ never saw light.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>There the wicked cease _from_ troubling; and there the weary be at rest.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>_There_ the prisoners rest together; they hear not the voice of the oppressor.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The small and great are there; and the servant _is_ free from his master.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore is light given to him that is in misery, and life unto the bitter _in_ soul;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Which long for death, but it _cometh_ not; and dig for it more than for hid treasures;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Which rejoice exceedingly, _and_ are glad, when they can find the grave?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>_Why is light given_ to a man whose way is hid, and whom God hath hedged in?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For my sighing cometh before I eat, and my roarings are poured out like the waters.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For the thing which I greatly feared is come upon me, and that which I was afraid of is come unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>I was not in safety, neither had I rest, neither was I quiet; yet trouble came.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>_If_ we assay to commune with thee, wilt thou be grieved? but who can withhold himself from speaking?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Behold, thou hast instructed many, and thou hast strengthened the weak hands.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Thy words have upholden him that was falling, and thou hast strengthened the feeble knees.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But now it is come upon thee, and thou faintest; it toucheth thee, and thou art troubled.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>_Is_ not _this_ thy fear, thy confidence, thy hope, and the uprightness of thy ways?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Remember, I pray thee, who _ever_ perished, being innocent? or where were the righteous cut off?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Even as I have seen, they that plow iniquity, and sow wickedness, reap the same.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>By the blast of God they perish, and by the breath of his nostrils are they consumed.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The roaring of the lion, and the voice of the fierce lion, and the teeth of the young lions, are broken.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The old lion perisheth for lack of prey, and the stout lion’s whelps are scattered abroad.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Now a thing was secretly brought to me, and mine ear received a little thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>In thoughts from the visions of the night, when deep sleep falleth on men,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Fear came upon me, and trembling, which made all my bones to shake.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Then a spirit passed before my face; the hair of my flesh stood up:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>It stood still, but I could not discern the form thereof: an image _was_ before mine eyes, _there was_ silence, and I heard a voice, _saying_,  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Shall mortal man be more just than God? shall a man be more pure than his maker?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Behold, he put no trust in his servants; and his angels he charged with folly:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>How much less _in_ them that dwell in houses of clay, whose foundation _is_ in the dust, _which_ are crushed before the moth?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>They are destroyed from morning to evening: they perish for ever without any regarding _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Doth not their excellency _which is_ in them go away? they die, even without wisdom.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Call now, if there be any that will answer thee; and to which of the saints wilt thou turn?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For wrath killeth the foolish man, and envy slayeth the silly one.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I have seen the foolish taking root: but suddenly I cursed his habitation.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>His children are far from safety, and they are crushed in the gate, neither _is there_ any to deliver _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Whose harvest the hungry eateth up, and taketh it even out of the thorns, and the robber swalloweth up their substance.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Although affliction cometh not forth of the dust, neither doth trouble spring out of the ground;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Yet man is born unto trouble, as the sparks fly upward.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>I would seek unto God, and unto God would I commit my cause:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Which doeth great things and unsearchable; marvellous things without number:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Who giveth rain upon the earth, and sendeth waters upon the fields:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>To set up on high those that be low; that those which mourn may be exalted to safety.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>He disappointeth the devices of the crafty, so that their hands cannot perform _their_ enterprise.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>He taketh the wise in their own craftiness: and the counsel of the froward is carried headlong.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>They meet with darkness in the daytime, and grope in the noonday as in the night.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But he saveth the poor from the sword, from their mouth, and from the hand of the mighty.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>So the poor hath hope, and iniquity stoppeth her mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, happy _is_ the man whom God correcteth: therefore despise not thou the chastening of the Almighty:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For he maketh sore, and bindeth up: he woundeth, and his hands make whole.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>He shall deliver thee in six troubles: yea, in seven there shall no evil touch thee.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>In famine he shall redeem thee from death: and in war from the power of the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Thou shalt be hid from the scourge of the tongue: neither shalt thou be afraid of destruction when it cometh.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>At destruction and famine thou shalt laugh: neither shalt thou be afraid of the beasts of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For thou shalt be in league with the stones of the field: and the beasts of the field shall be at peace with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And thou shalt know that thy tabernacle _shall be_ in peace; and thou shalt visit thy habitation, and shalt not sin.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Thou shalt know also that thy seed _shall be_ great, and thine offspring as the grass of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Thou shalt come to _thy_ grave in a full age, like as a shock of corn cometh in in his season.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Lo this, we have searched it, so it _is_; hear it, and know thou _it_ for thy good.  

## CHAPTER 6.

But Job answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Oh that my grief were throughly weighed, and my calamity laid in the balances together!  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For now it would be heavier than the sand of the sea: therefore my words are swallowed up.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For the arrows of the Almighty _are_ within me, the poison whereof drinketh up my spirit: the terrors of God do set themselves in array against me.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Doth the wild ass bray when he hath grass? or loweth the ox over his fodder?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Can that which is unsavoury be eaten without salt? or is there _any_ taste in the white of an egg?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The things _that_ my soul refused to touch _are_ as my sorrowful meat.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Oh that I might have my request; and that God would grant _me_ the thing that I long for!  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Even that it would please God to destroy me; that he would let loose his hand, and cut me off!  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Then should I yet have comfort; yea, I would harden myself in sorrow: let him not spare; for I have not concealed the words of the Holy One.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>What _is_ my strength, that I should hope? and what _is_ mine end, that I should prolong my life?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>_Is_ my strength the strength of stones? or _is_ my flesh of brass?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>_Is_ not my help in me? and is wisdom driven quite from me?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ To him that is afflicted pity _should be shewed_ from his friend; but he forsaketh the fear of the Almighty.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>My brethren have dealt deceitfully as a brook, _and_ as the stream of brooks they pass away;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Which are blackish by reason of the ice, _and_ wherein the snow is hid:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>What time they wax warm, they vanish: when it is hot, they are consumed out of their place.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The paths of their way are turned aside; they go to nothing, and perish.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The troops of Tema looked, the companies of Sheba waited for them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>They were confounded because they had hoped; they came thither, and were ashamed.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For now ye are nothing; ye see _my_ casting down, and are afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Did I say, Bring unto me? or, Give a reward for me of your substance?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Or, Deliver me from the enemy’s hand? or, Redeem me from the hand of the mighty?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Teach me, and I will hold my tongue: and cause me to understand wherein I have erred.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>How forcible are right words! but what doth your arguing reprove?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Do ye imagine to reprove words, and the speeches of one that is desperate, _which are_ as wind?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Yea, ye overwhelm the fatherless, and ye dig _a pit_ for your friend.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Now therefore be content, look upon me; for _it is_ evident unto you if I lie.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Return, I pray you, let it not be iniquity; yea, return again, my righteousness _is_ in it.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Is there iniquity in my tongue? cannot my taste discern perverse things?  

## CHAPTER 7.

_Is there_ not an appointed time to man upon earth? _are not_ his days also like the days of an hireling?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>As a servant earnestly desireth the shadow, and as an hireling looketh for _the reward of_ his work:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>So am I made to possess months of vanity, and wearisome nights are appointed to me.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>When I lie down, I say, When shall I arise, and the night be gone? and I am full of tossings to and fro unto the dawning of the day.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>My flesh is clothed with worms and clods of dust; my skin is broken, and become loathsome.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>My days are swifter than a weaver’s shuttle, and are spent without hope.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ O remember that my life _is_ wind: mine eye shall no more see good.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The eye of him that hath seen me shall see me no _more_: thine eyes _are_ upon me, and I _am_ not.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_As_ the cloud is consumed and vanisheth away: so he that goeth down to the grave shall come up no _more_.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He shall return no more to his house, neither shall his place know him any more.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Therefore I will not refrain my mouth; I will speak in the anguish of my spirit; I will complain in the bitterness of my soul.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>_Am_ I a sea, or a whale, that thou settest a watch over me?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>When I say, My bed shall comfort me, my couch shall ease my complaint;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Then thou scarest me with dreams, and terrifiest me through visions:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So that my soul chooseth strangling, _and_ death rather than my life.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>I loathe _it_; I would not live alway: let me alone; for my days _are_ vanity.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ What _is_ man, that thou shouldest magnify him? and that thou shouldest set thine heart upon him?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And _that_ thou shouldest visit him every morning, _and_ try him every moment?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>How long wilt thou not depart from me, nor let me alone till I swallow down my spittle?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>I have sinned; what shall I do unto thee, O thou preserver of men? why hast thou set me as a mark against thee, so that I am a burden to myself?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And why dost thou not pardon my transgression, and take away mine iniquity? for now shall I sleep in the dust; and thou shalt seek me in the morning, but I _shall_ not _be_.  

## CHAPTER 8.

Then answered Bildad the Shuhite, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>How long wilt thou speak these _things_? and _how long shall_ the words of thy mouth _be like_ a strong wind?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Doth God pervert judgment? or doth the Almighty pervert justice?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>If thy children have sinned against him, and he have cast them away for their transgression;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>If thou wouldest seek unto God betimes, and make thy supplication to the Almighty;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>If thou _wert_ pure and upright; surely now he would awake for thee, and make the habitation of thy righteousness prosperous.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Though thy beginning was small, yet thy latter end should greatly increase.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ For enquire, I pray thee, of the former age, and prepare thyself to the search of their fathers:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>(For we _are but of_ yesterday, and know nothing, because our days upon earth _are_ a shadow:)  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Shall not they teach thee, _and_ tell thee, and utter words out of their heart?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Can the rush grow up without mire? can the flag grow without water?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Whilst it _is_ yet in his greenness, _and_ not cut down, it withereth before any _other_ herb.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>So _are_ the paths of all that forget God; and the hypocrite’s hope shall perish:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Whose hope shall be cut off, and whose trust _shall be_ a spider’s web.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>He shall lean upon his house, but it shall not stand: he shall hold it fast, but it shall not endure.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>He _is_ green before the sun, and his branch shooteth forth in his garden.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>His roots are wrapped about the heap, _and_ seeth the place of stones.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>If he destroy him from his place, then _it_ shall deny him, _saying_, I have not seen thee.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Behold, this _is_ the joy of his way, and out of the earth shall others grow.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, God will not cast away a perfect _man_, neither will he help the evil doers:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Till he fill thy mouth with laughing, and thy lips with rejoicing.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>They that hate thee shall be clothed with shame; and the dwelling place of the wicked shall come to nought.  

## CHAPTER 9.

Then Job answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>I know _it is_ so of a truth: but how should man be just with God?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>If he will contend with him, he cannot answer him one of a thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>_He is_ wise in heart, and mighty in strength: who hath hardened _himself_ against him, and hath prospered?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Which removeth the mountains, and they know not: which overturneth them in his anger.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Which shaketh the earth out of her place, and the pillars thereof tremble.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Which commandeth the sun, and it riseth not; and sealeth up the stars.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Which alone spreadeth out the heavens, and treadeth upon the waves of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Which maketh Arcturus, Orion, and Pleiades, and the chambers of the south.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Which doeth great things past finding out; yea, and wonders without number.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Lo, he goeth by me, and I see _him_ not: he passeth on also, but I perceive him not.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Behold, he taketh away, who can hinder him? who will say unto him, What doest thou?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>_If_ God will not withdraw his anger, the proud helpers do stoop under him.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ How much less shall I answer him, _and_ choose out my words _to reason_ with him?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Whom, though I were righteous, _yet_ would I not answer, _but_ I would make supplication to my judge.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>If I had called, and he had answered me; _yet_ would I not believe that he had hearkened unto my voice.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For he breaketh me with a tempest, and multiplieth my wounds without cause.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>He will not suffer me to take my breath, but filleth me with bitterness.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>If _I speak_ of strength, lo, _he is_ strong: and if of judgment, who shall set me a time _to plead_?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>If I justify myself, mine own mouth shall condemn me: _if I say_, I _am_ perfect, it shall also prove me perverse.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>_Though_ I _were_ perfect, _yet_ would I not know my soul: I would despise my life.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ This _is_ one _thing_, therefore I said _it_, He destroyeth the perfect and the wicked.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>If the scourge slay suddenly, he will laugh at the trial of the innocent.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>The earth is given into the hand of the wicked: he covereth the faces of the judges thereof; if not, where, _and_ who _is_ he?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ Now my days are swifter than a post: they flee away, they see no good.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>They are passed away as the swift ships: as the eagle _that_ hasteth to the prey.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>If I say, I will forget my complaint, I will leave off my heaviness, and comfort _myself_:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>I am afraid of all my sorrows, I know that thou wilt not hold me innocent.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>_If_ I be wicked, why then labour I in vain?  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>If I wash myself with snow water, and make my hands never so clean;  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Yet shalt thou plunge me in the ditch, and mine own clothes shall abhor me.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For _he is_ not a man, as I _am, that_ I should answer him, _and_ we should come together in judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Neither is there any daysman betwixt us, _that_ might lay his hand upon us both.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Let him take his rod away from me, and let not his fear terrify me:  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>_Then_ would I speak, and not fear him; but _it is_ not so with me.  

## CHAPTER 10.

My soul is weary of my life; I will leave my complaint upon myself; I will speak in the bitterness of my soul.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>I will say unto God, Do not condemn me; shew me wherefore thou contendest with me.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>_Is it_ good unto thee that thou shouldest oppress, that thou shouldest despise the work of thine hands, and shine upon the counsel of the wicked?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Hast thou eyes of flesh? or seest thou as man seeth?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>_Are_ thy days as the days of man? _are_ thy years as man’s days,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>That thou enquirest after mine iniquity, and searchest after my sin?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Thou knowest that I am not wicked; and _there is_ none that can deliver out of thine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Thine hands have made me and fashioned me together round about; yet thou dost destroy me.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Remember, I beseech thee, that thou hast made me as the clay; and wilt thou bring me into dust again?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Hast thou not poured me out as milk, and curdled me like cheese?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Thou hast clothed me with skin and flesh, and hast fenced me with bones and sinews.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Thou hast granted me life and favour, and thy visitation hath preserved my spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And these _things_ hast thou hid in thine heart: I know that this _is_ with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ If I sin, then thou markest me, and thou wilt not acquit me from mine iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>If I be wicked, woe unto me; and _if_ I be righteous, _yet_ will I not lift up my head. _I am_ full of confusion; therefore see thou mine affliction;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For it increaseth. Thou huntest me as a fierce lion: and again thou shewest thyself marvellous upon me.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thou renewest thy witnesses against me, and increasest thine indignation upon me; changes and war _are_ against me.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Wherefore then hast thou brought me forth out of the womb? Oh that I had given up the ghost, and no eye had seen me!  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>I should have been as though I had not been; I should have been carried from the womb to the grave.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>_Are_ not my days few? cease _then, and_ let me alone, that I may take comfort a little,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Before I go _whence_ I shall not return, _even_ to the land of darkness and the shadow of death;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>A land of darkness, as darkness _itself; and_ of the shadow of death, without any order, and _where_ the light _is_ as darkness.  

## CHAPTER 11.

Then answered Zophar the Naamathite, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Should not the multitude of words be answered? and should a man full of talk be justified?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Should thy lies make men hold their peace? and when thou mockest, shall no man make thee ashamed?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For thou hast said, My doctrine _is_ pure, and I am clean in thine eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But oh that God would speak, and open his lips against thee;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And that he would shew thee the secrets of wisdom, that _they are_ double to that which is! Know therefore that God exacteth of thee _less_ than thine iniquity _deserveth_.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Canst thou by searching find out God? canst thou find out the Almighty unto perfection?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>_It is_ as high as heaven; what canst thou do? deeper than hell; what canst thou know?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The measure thereof _is_ longer than the earth, and broader than the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>If he cut off, and shut up, or gather together, then who can hinder him?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For he knoweth vain men: he seeth wickedness also; will he not then consider _it_?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For vain man would be wise, though man be born _like_ a wild ass’s colt.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ If thou prepare thine heart, and stretch out thine hands toward him;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>If iniquity _be_ in thine hand, put it far away, and let not wickedness dwell in thy tabernacles.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For then shalt thou lift up thy face without spot; yea, thou shalt be stedfast, and shalt not fear:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Because thou shalt forget _thy_ misery, _and_ remember _it_ as waters _that_ pass away:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And _thine_ age shall be clearer than the noonday; thou shalt shine forth, thou shalt be as the morning.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And thou shalt be secure, because there is hope; yea, thou shalt dig _about thee, and_ thou shalt take thy rest in safety.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Also thou shalt lie down, and none shall make _thee_ afraid; yea, many shall make suit unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But the eyes of the wicked shall fail, and they shall not escape, and their hope _shall be as_ the giving up of the ghost.  

## CHAPTER 12.

And Job answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>No doubt but ye _are_ the people, and wisdom shall die with you.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But I have understanding as well as you; I _am_ not inferior to you: yea, who knoweth not such things as these?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I am _as_ one mocked of his neighbour, who calleth upon God, and he answereth him: the just upright _man is_ laughed to scorn.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>He that is ready to slip with _his_ feet _is as_ a lamp despised in the thought of him that is at ease.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ The tabernacles of robbers prosper, and they that provoke God are secure; into whose hand God bringeth _abundantly_.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But ask now the beasts, and they shall teach thee; and the fowls of the air, and they shall tell thee:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Or speak to the earth, and it shall teach thee: and the fishes of the sea shall declare unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Who knoweth not in all these that the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath wrought this?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>In whose hand _is_ the soul of every living thing, and the breath of all mankind.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Doth not the ear try words? and the mouth taste his meat?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ With the ancient _is_ wisdom; and in length of days understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>With him _is_ wisdom and strength, he hath counsel and understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Behold, he breaketh down, and it cannot be built again: he shutteth up a man, and there can be no opening.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Behold, he withholdeth the waters, and they dry up: also he sendeth them out, and they overturn the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>With him _is_ strength and wisdom: the deceived and the deceiver _are_ his.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>He leadeth counsellors away spoiled, and maketh the judges fools.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>He looseth the bond of kings, and girdeth their loins with a girdle.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>He leadeth princes away spoiled, and overthroweth the mighty.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>He removeth away the speech of the trusty, and taketh away the understanding of the aged.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>He poureth contempt upon princes, and weakeneth the strength of the mighty.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>He discovereth deep things out of darkness, and bringeth out to light the shadow of death.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>He increaseth the nations, and destroyeth them: he enlargeth the nations, and straiteneth them _again_.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>He taketh away the heart of the chief of the people of the earth, and causeth them to wander in a wilderness _where there is_ no way.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>They grope in the dark without light, and he maketh them to stagger like _a_ drunken _man_.  

## CHAPTER 13.

Lo, mine eye hath seen all _this_, mine ear hath heard and understood it.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>What ye know, _the same_ do I know also: I _am_ not inferior unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Surely I would speak to the Almighty, and I desire to reason with God.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But ye _are_ forgers of lies, ye _are_ all physicians of no value.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>O that ye would altogether hold your peace! and it should be your wisdom.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Hear now my reasoning, and hearken to the pleadings of my lips.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Will ye speak wickedly for God? and talk deceitfully for him?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Will ye accept his person? will ye contend for God?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Is it good that he should search you out? or as one man mocketh another, do ye _so_ mock him?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He will surely reprove you, if ye do secretly accept persons.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Shall not his excellency make you afraid? and his dread fall upon you?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Your remembrances _are_ like unto ashes, your bodies to bodies of clay.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Hold your peace, let me alone, that I may speak, and let come on me what _will_.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Wherefore do I take my flesh in my teeth, and put my life in mine hand?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him: but I will maintain mine own ways before him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>He also _shall be_ my salvation: for an hypocrite shall not come before him.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Hear diligently my speech, and my declaration with your ears.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Behold now, I have ordered _my_ cause; I know that I shall be justified.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Who _is_ he _that_ will plead with me? for now, if I hold my tongue, I shall give up the ghost.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Only do not two _things_ unto me: then will I not hide myself from thee.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Withdraw thine hand far from me: and let not thy dread make me afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Then call thou, and I will answer: or let me speak, and answer thou me.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ How many _are_ mine iniquities and sins? make me to know my transgression and my sin.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Wherefore hidest thou thy face, and holdest me for thine enemy?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Wilt thou break a leaf driven to and fro? and wilt thou pursue the dry stubble?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For thou writest bitter things against me, and makest me to possess the iniquities of my youth.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Thou puttest my feet also in the stocks, and lookest narrowly unto all my paths; thou settest a print upon the heels of my feet.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And he, as a rotten thing, consumeth, as a garment that is moth eaten.  

## CHAPTER 14.

Man _that is_ born of a woman _is_ of few days, and full of trouble.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>He cometh forth like a flower, and is cut down: he fleeth also as a shadow, and continueth not.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And dost thou open thine eyes upon such an one, and bringest me into judgment with thee?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who can bring a clean _thing_ out of an unclean? not one.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Seeing his days _are_ determined, the number of his months _are_ with thee, thou hast appointed his bounds that he cannot pass;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Turn from him, that he may rest, till he shall accomplish, as an hireling, his day.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ For there is hope of a tree, if it be cut down, that it will sprout again, and that the tender branch thereof will not cease.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Though the root thereof wax old in the earth, and the stock thereof die in the ground;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_Yet_ through the scent of water it will bud, and bring forth boughs like a plant.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But man dieth, and wasteth away: yea, man giveth up the ghost, and where _is_ he?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_As_ the waters fail from the sea, and the flood decayeth and drieth up:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>So man lieth down, and riseth not: till the heavens _be_ no more, they shall not awake, nor be raised out of their sleep.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>O that thou wouldest hide me in the grave, that thou wouldest keep me secret, until thy wrath be past, that thou wouldest appoint me a set time, and remember me!  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>If a man die, shall he live _again_? all the days of my appointed time will I wait, till my change come.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thou shalt call, and I will answer thee: thou wilt have a desire to the work of thine hands.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ For now thou numberest my steps: dost thou not watch over my sin?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>My transgression _is_ sealed up in a bag, and thou sewest up mine iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And surely the mountain falling cometh to nought, and the rock is removed out of his place.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The waters wear the stones: thou washest away the things which grow _out_ of the dust of the earth; and thou destroyest the hope of man.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Thou prevailest for ever against him, and he passeth: thou changest his countenance, and sendest him away.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>His sons come to honour, and he knoweth _it_ not; and they are brought low, but he perceiveth _it_ not of them.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But his flesh upon him shall have pain, and his soul within him shall mourn.  

## CHAPTER 15.

Then answered Eliphaz the Temanite, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Should a wise man utter vain knowledge, and fill his belly with the east wind?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Should he reason with unprofitable talk? or with speeches wherewith he can do no good?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Yea, thou castest off fear, and restrainest prayer before God.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For thy mouth uttereth thine iniquity, and thou choosest the tongue of the crafty.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Thine own mouth condemneth thee, and not I: yea, thine own lips testify against thee.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_Art_ thou the first man _that_ was born? or wast thou made before the hills?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Hast thou heard the secret of God? and dost thou restrain wisdom to thyself?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>What knowest thou, that we know not? _what_ understandest thou, which _is_ not in us?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>With us _are_ both the grayheaded and very aged men, much elder than thy father.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_Are_ the consolations of God small with thee? is there any secret thing with thee?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Why doth thine heart carry thee away? and what do thy eyes wink at,  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>That thou turnest thy spirit against God, and lettest _such_ words go out of thy mouth?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>What _is_ man, that he should be clean? and _he which is_ born of a woman, that he should be righteous?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Behold, he putteth no trust in his saints; yea, the heavens are not clean in his sight.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>How much more abominable and filthy _is_ man, which drinketh iniquity like water?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ I will shew thee, hear me; and that _which_ I have seen I will declare;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Which wise men have told from their fathers, and have not hid _it_:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Unto whom alone the earth was given, and no stranger passed among them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>The wicked man travaileth with pain all _his_ days, and the number of years is hidden to the oppressor.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>A dreadful sound _is_ in his ears: in prosperity the destroyer shall come upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>He believeth not that he shall return out of darkness, and he is waited for of the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>He wandereth abroad for bread, _saying_, Where _is it_? he knoweth that the day of darkness is ready at his hand.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Trouble and anguish shall make him afraid; they shall prevail against him, as a king ready to the battle.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For he stretcheth out his hand against God, and strengtheneth himself against the Almighty.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>He runneth upon him, _even_ on _his_ neck, upon the thick bosses of his bucklers:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Because he covereth his face with his fatness, and maketh collops of fat on _his_ flanks.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And he dwelleth in desolate cities, _and_ in houses which no man inhabiteth, which are ready to become heaps.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>He shall not be rich, neither shall his substance continue, neither shall he prolong the perfection thereof upon the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>He shall not depart out of darkness; the flame shall dry up his branches, and by the breath of his mouth shall he go away.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Let not him that is deceived trust in vanity: for vanity shall be his recompence.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>It shall be accomplished before his time, and his branch shall not be green.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>He shall shake off his unripe grape as the vine, and shall cast off his flower as the olive.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>For the congregation of hypocrites _shall be_ desolate, and fire shall consume the tabernacles of bribery.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>They conceive mischief, and bring forth vanity, and their belly prepareth deceit.  

## CHAPTER 16.

Then Job answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>I have heard many such things: miserable comforters _are_ ye all.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Shall vain words have an end? or what emboldeneth thee that thou answerest?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I also could speak as ye _do_: if your soul were in my soul’s stead, I could heap up words against you, and shake mine head at you.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>_But_ I would strengthen you with my mouth, and the moving of my lips should asswage _your grief_.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Though I speak, my grief is not asswaged: and _though_ I forbear, what am I eased?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But now he hath made me weary: thou hast made desolate all my company.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And thou hast filled me with wrinkles, _which_ is a witness _against me_: and my leanness rising up in me beareth witness to my face.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>He teareth _me_ in his wrath, who hateth me: he gnasheth upon me with his teeth; mine enemy sharpeneth his eyes upon me.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>They have gaped upon me with their mouth; they have smitten me upon the cheek reproachfully; they have gathered themselves together against me.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>God hath delivered me to the ungodly, and turned me over into the hands of the wicked.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I was at ease, but he hath broken me asunder: he hath also taken _me_ by my neck, and shaken me to pieces, and set me up for his mark.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>His archers compass me round about, he cleaveth my reins asunder, and doth not spare; he poureth out my gall upon the ground.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>He breaketh me with breach upon breach, he runneth upon me like a giant.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>I have sewed sackcloth upon my skin, and defiled my horn in the dust.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>My face is foul with weeping, and on my eyelids _is_ the shadow of death;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Not for _any_ injustice in mine hands: also my prayer _is_ pure.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>O earth, cover not thou my blood, and let my cry have no place.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Also now, behold, my witness _is_ in heaven, and my record _is_ on high.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>My friends scorn me: _but_ mine eye poureth out _tears_ unto God.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>O that one might plead for a man with God, as a man _pleadeth_ for his neighbour!  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>When a few years are come, then I shall go the way _whence_ I shall not return.  

## CHAPTER 17.

My breath is corrupt, my days are extinct, the graves _are ready_ for me.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>_Are there_ not mockers with me? and doth not mine eye continue in their provocation?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Lay down now, put me in a surety with thee; who _is_ he _that_ will strike hands with me?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For thou hast hid their heart from understanding: therefore shalt thou not exalt _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>He that speaketh flattery to _his_ friends, even the eyes of his children shall fail.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>He hath made me also a byword of the people; and aforetime I was as a tabret.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Mine eye also is dim by reason of sorrow, and all my members _are_ as a shadow.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Upright _men_ shall be astonied at this, and the innocent shall stir up himself against the hypocrite.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The righteous also shall hold on his way, and he that hath clean hands shall be stronger and stronger.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ But as for you all, do ye return, and come now: for I cannot find _one_ wise _man_ among you.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>My days are past, my purposes are broken off, _even_ the thoughts of my heart.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>They change the night into day: the light _is_ short because of darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>If I wait, the grave _is_ mine house: I have made my bed in the darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I have said to corruption, Thou _art_ my father: to the worm, _Thou art_ my mother, and my sister.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And where _is_ now my hope? as for my hope, who shall see it?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>They shall go down to the bars of the pit, when _our_ rest together _is_ in the dust.  

## CHAPTER 18.

Then answered Bildad the Shuhite, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>How long _will it be ere_ ye make an end of words? mark, and afterwards we will speak.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Wherefore are we counted as beasts, _and_ reputed vile in your sight?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>He teareth himself in his anger: shall the earth be forsaken for thee? and shall the rock be removed out of his place?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Yea, the light of the wicked shall be put out, and the spark of his fire shall not shine.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The light shall be dark in his tabernacle, and his candle shall be put out with him.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The steps of his strength shall be straitened, and his own counsel shall cast him down.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For he is cast into a net by his own feet, and he walketh upon a snare.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The gin shall take _him_ by the heel, _and_ the robber shall prevail against him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The snare _is_ laid for him in the ground, and a trap for him in the way.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Terrors shall make him afraid on every side, and shall drive him to his feet.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>His strength shall be hungerbitten, and destruction _shall be_ ready at his side.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>It shall devour the strength of his skin: _even_ the firstborn of death shall devour his strength.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>His confidence shall be rooted out of his tabernacle, and it shall bring him to the king of terrors.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>It shall dwell in his tabernacle, because _it is_ none of his: brimstone shall be scattered upon his habitation.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>His roots shall be dried up beneath, and above shall his branch be cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>His remembrance shall perish from the earth, and he shall have no name in the street.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>He shall be driven from light into darkness, and chased out of the world.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>He shall neither have son nor nephew among his people, nor any remaining in his dwellings.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>They that come after _him_ shall be astonied at his day, as they that went before were affrighted.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Surely such _are_ the dwellings of the wicked, and this _is_ the place _of him that_ knoweth not God.  

## CHAPTER 19.

Then Job answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>How long will ye vex my soul, and break me in pieces with words?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>These ten times have ye reproached me: ye are not ashamed _that_ ye make yourselves strange to me.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And be it indeed _that_ I have erred, mine error remaineth with myself.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>If indeed ye will magnify _yourselves_ against me, and plead against me my reproach:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Know now that God hath overthrown me, and hath compassed me with his net.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Behold, I cry out of wrong, but I am not heard: I cry aloud, but _there is_ no judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ He hath fenced up my way that I cannot pass, and he hath set darkness in my paths.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>He hath stripped me of my glory, and taken the crown _from_ my head.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He hath destroyed me on every side, and I am gone: and mine hope hath he removed like a tree.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He hath also kindled his wrath against me, and he counteth me unto him as _one of_ his enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>His troops come together, and raise up their way against me, and encamp round about my tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>He hath put my brethren far from me, and mine acquaintance are verily estranged from me.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>My kinsfolk have failed, and my familiar friends have forgotten me.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>They that dwell in mine house, and my maids, count me for a stranger: I am an alien in their sight.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>I called my servant, and he gave _me_ no answer; I intreated him with my mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>My breath is strange to my wife, though I intreated for the children’s _sake_ of mine own body.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Yea, young children despised me; I arose, and they spake against me.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>All my inward friends abhorred me: and they whom I loved are turned against me.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>My bone cleaveth to my skin and to my flesh, and I am escaped with the skin of my teeth.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Have pity upon me, have pity upon me, O ye my friends; for the hand of God hath touched me.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Why do ye persecute me as God, and are not satisfied with my flesh?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Oh that my words were now written! oh that they were printed in a book!  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>That they were graven with an iron pen and lead in the rock for ever!  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For I know _that_ my redeemer liveth, and _that_ he shall stand at the latter _day_ upon the earth:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And _though_ after my skin _worms_ destroy this _body_, yet in my flesh shall I see God:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Whom I shall see for myself, and mine eyes shall behold, and not another; _though_ my reins be consumed within me.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>But ye should say, Why persecute we him, seeing the root of the matter is found in me?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Be ye afraid of the sword: for wrath _bringeth_ the punishments of the sword, that ye may know _there is_ a judgment.  

## CHAPTER 20.

Then answered Zophar the Naamathite, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Therefore do my thoughts cause me to answer, and for _this_ I make haste.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I have heard the check of my reproach, and the spirit of my understanding causeth me to answer.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Knowest thou _not_ this of old, since man was placed upon earth,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>That the triumphing of the wicked _is_ short, and the joy of the hypocrite _but_ for a moment?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Though his excellency mount up to the heavens, and his head reach unto the clouds;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_Yet_ he shall perish for ever like his own dung: they which have seen him shall say, Where _is_ he?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He shall fly away as a dream, and shall not be found: yea, he shall be chased away as a vision of the night.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The eye also _which_ saw him shall _see him_ no more; neither shall his place any more behold him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ His children shall seek to please the poor, and his hands shall restore their goods.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>His bones are full _of the sin_ of his youth, which shall lie down with him in the dust.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Though wickedness be sweet in his mouth, _though_ he hide it under his tongue;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>_Though_ he spare it, and forsake it not; but keep it still within his mouth:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>_Yet_ his meat in his bowels is turned, _it is_ the gall of asps within him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>He hath swallowed down riches, and he shall vomit them up again: God shall cast them out of his belly.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>He shall suck the poison of asps: the viper’s tongue shall slay him.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>He shall not see the rivers, the floods, the brooks of honey and butter.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>That which he laboured for shall he restore, and shall not swallow _it_ down: according to _his_ substance _shall_ the restitution _be_, and he shall not rejoice _therein_.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Because he hath oppressed _and_ hath forsaken the poor; _because_ he hath violently taken away an house which he builded not;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Surely he shall not feel quietness in his belly, he shall not save of that which he desired.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>There shall none of his meat be left; therefore shall no man look for his goods.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>In the fulness of his sufficiency he shall be in straits: every hand of the wicked shall come upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ _When_ he is about to fill his belly, _God_ shall cast the fury of his wrath upon him, and shall rain _it_ upon him while he is eating.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>He shall flee from the iron weapon, _and_ the bow of steel shall strike him through.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>It is drawn, and cometh out of the body; yea, the glittering sword cometh out of his gall: terrors _are_ upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>All darkness _shall be_ hid in his secret places: a fire not blown shall consume him; it shall go ill with him that is left in his tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>The heaven shall reveal his iniquity; and the earth shall rise up against him.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>The increase of his house shall depart, _and his goods_ shall flow away in the day of his wrath.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>This _is_ the portion of a wicked man from God, and the heritage appointed unto him by God.  

## CHAPTER 21.

But Job answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Hear diligently my speech, and let this be your consolations.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Suffer me that I may speak; and after that I have spoken, mock on.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>As for me, _is_ my complaint to man? and if _it were so_, why should not my spirit be troubled?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Mark me, and be astonished, and lay _your_ hand upon _your_ mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Even when I remember I am afraid, and trembling taketh hold on my flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore do the wicked live, become old, yea, are mighty in power?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Their seed is established in their sight with them, and their offspring before their eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Their houses _are_ safe from fear, neither _is_ the rod of God upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Their bull gendereth, and faileth not; their cow calveth, and casteth not her calf.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>They send forth their little ones like a flock, and their children dance.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>They take the timbrel and harp, and rejoice at the sound of the organ.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>They spend their days in wealth, and in a moment go down to the grave.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore they say unto God, Depart from us; for we desire not the knowledge of thy ways.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>What _is_ the Almighty, that we should serve him? and what profit should we have, if we pray unto him?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Lo, their good _is_ not in their hand: the counsel of the wicked is far from me.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ How oft is the candle of the wicked put out! and _how oft_ cometh their destruction upon them! _God_ distributeth sorrows in his anger.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>They are as stubble before the wind, and as chaff that the storm carrieth away.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>God layeth up his iniquity for his children: he rewardeth him, and he shall know _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>His eyes shall see his destruction, and he shall drink of the wrath of the Almighty.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For what pleasure _hath_ he in his house after him, when the number of his months is cut off in the midst?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Shall _any_ teach God knowledge? seeing he judgeth those that are high.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>One dieth in his full strength, being wholly at ease and quiet.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>His breasts are full of milk, and his bones are moistened with marrow.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And another dieth in the bitterness of his soul, and never eateth with pleasure.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>They shall lie down alike in the dust, and the worms shall cover them.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, I know your thoughts, and the devices _which_ ye wrongfully imagine against me.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For ye say, Where _is_ the house of the prince? and where _are_ the dwelling places of the wicked?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Have ye not asked them that go by the way? and do ye not know their tokens,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>That the wicked is reserved to the day of destruction? they shall be brought forth to the day of wrath.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Who shall declare his way to his face? and who shall repay him _what_ he hath done?  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Yet shall he be brought to the grave, and shall remain in the tomb.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>The clods of the valley shall be sweet unto him, and every man shall draw after him, as _there are_ innumerable before him.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>How then comfort ye me in vain, seeing in your answers there remaineth falsehood?  

## CHAPTER 22.

Then Eliphaz the Temanite answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Can a man be profitable unto God, as he that is wise may be profitable unto himself?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>_Is it_ any pleasure to the Almighty, that thou art righteous? or _is it_ gain _to him_, that thou makest thy ways perfect?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Will he reprove thee for fear of thee? will he enter with thee into judgment?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ _Is_ not thy wickedness great? and thine iniquities infinite?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For thou hast taken a pledge from thy brother for nought, and stripped the naked of their clothing.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Thou hast not given water to the weary to drink, and thou hast withholden bread from the hungry.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But _as for_ the mighty man, he had the earth; and the honourable man dwelt in it.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Thou hast sent widows away empty, and the arms of the fatherless have been broken.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Therefore snares _are_ round about thee, and sudden fear troubleth thee;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Or darkness, _that_ thou canst not see; and abundance of waters cover thee.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>_Is_ not God in the height of heaven? and behold the height of the stars, how high they are!  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And thou sayest, How doth God know? can he judge through the dark cloud?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thick clouds _are_ a covering to him, that he seeth not; and he walketh in the circuit of heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Hast thou marked the old way which wicked men have trodden?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Which were cut down out of time, whose foundation was overflown with a flood:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Which said unto God, Depart from us: and what can the Almighty do for them?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Yet he filled their houses with good _things_: but the counsel of the wicked is far from me.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The righteous see _it_, and are glad: and the innocent laugh them to scorn.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Whereas our substance is not cut down, but the remnant of them the fire consumeth.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Acquaint now thyself with him, and be at peace: thereby good shall come unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Receive, I pray thee, the law from his mouth, and lay up his words in thine heart.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>If thou return to the Almighty, thou shalt be built up, thou shalt put away iniquity far from thy tabernacles.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou lay up gold as dust, and the _gold_ of Ophir as the stones of the brooks.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Yea, the Almighty shall be thy defence, and thou shalt have plenty of silver.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For then shalt thou have thy delight in the Almighty, and shalt lift up thy face unto God.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Thou shalt make thy prayer unto him, and he shall hear thee, and thou shalt pay thy vows.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Thou shalt also decree a thing, and it shall be established unto thee: and the light shall shine upon thy ways.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>When _men_ are cast down, then thou shalt say, _There is_ lifting up; and he shall save the humble person.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>He shall deliver the island of the innocent: and it is delivered by the pureness of thine hands.  

## CHAPTER 23.

Then Job answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Even to day _is_ my complaint bitter: my stroke is heavier than my groaning.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Oh that I knew where I might find him! _that_ I might come _even_ to his seat!  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I would order _my_ cause before him, and fill my mouth with arguments.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>I would know the words _which_ he would answer me, and understand what he would say unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Will he plead against me with _his_ great power? No; but he would put _strength_ in me.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>There the righteous might dispute with him; so should I be delivered for ever from my judge.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, I go forward, but he _is_ not _there_; and backward, but I cannot perceive him:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>On the left hand, where he doth work, but I cannot behold _him_: he hideth himself on the right hand, that I cannot see _him_:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But he knoweth the way that I take: _when_ he hath tried me, I shall come forth as gold.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>My foot hath held his steps, his way have I kept, and not declined.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Neither have I gone back from the commandment of his lips; I have esteemed the words of his mouth more than my necessary _food_.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ But he _is_ in one _mind_, and who can turn him? and _what_ his soul desireth, even _that_ he doeth.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For he performeth _the thing that is_ appointed for me: and many such _things are_ with him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Therefore am I troubled at his presence: when I consider, I am afraid of him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For God maketh my heart soft, and the Almighty troubleth me:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Because I was not cut off before the darkness, _neither_ hath he covered the darkness from my face.  

## CHAPTER 24.

Why, seeing times are not hidden from the Almighty, do they that know him not see his days?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>_Some_ remove the landmarks; they violently take away flocks, and feed _thereof_.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>They drive away the ass of the fatherless, they take the widow’s ox for a pledge.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>They turn the needy out of the way: the poor of the earth hide themselves together.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Behold, _as_ wild asses in the desert, go they forth to their work; rising betimes for a prey: the wilderness _yieldeth_ food for them _and_ for _their_ children.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>They reap _every one_ his corn in the field: and they gather the vintage of the wicked.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>They cause the naked to lodge without clothing, that _they have_ no covering in the cold.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>They are wet with the showers of the mountains, and embrace the rock for want of a shelter.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>They pluck the fatherless from the breast, and take a pledge of the poor.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>They cause _him_ to go naked without clothing, and they take away the sheaf _from_ the hungry;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_Which_ make oil within their walls, _and_ tread _their_ winepresses, and suffer thirst.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Men groan from out of the city, and the soul of the wounded crieth out: yet God layeth not folly _to them_.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ They are of those that rebel against the light; they know not the ways thereof, nor abide in the paths thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The murderer rising with the light killeth the poor and needy, and in the night is as a thief.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The eye also of the adulterer waiteth for the twilight, saying, No eye shall see me: and disguiseth _his_ face.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>In the dark they dig through houses, _which_ they had marked for themselves in the daytime: they know not the light.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For the morning _is_ to them even as the shadow of death: if _one_ know _them, they are in_ the terrors of the shadow of death.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ He _is_ swift as the waters; their portion is cursed in the earth: he beholdeth not the way of the vineyards.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Drought and heat consume the snow waters: _so doth_ the grave _those which_ have sinned.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>The womb shall forget him; the worm shall feed sweetly on him; he shall be no more remembered; and wickedness shall be broken as a tree.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>He evil entreateth the barren _that_ beareth not: and doeth not good to the widow.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>He draweth also the mighty with his power: he riseth up, and no _man_ is sure of life.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>_Though_ it be given him _to be_ in safety, whereon he resteth; yet his eyes _are_ upon their ways.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>They are exalted for a little while, but are gone and brought low; they are taken out of the way as all _other_, and cut off as the tops of the ears of corn.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And if _it be_ not _so_ now, who will make me a liar, and make my speech nothing worth?  

## CHAPTER 25.

Then answered Bildad the Shuhite, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Dominion and fear _are_ with him, he maketh peace in his high places.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Is there any number of his armies? and upon whom doth not his light arise?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>How then can man be justified with God? or how can he be clean _that is_ born of a woman?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Behold even to the moon, and it shineth not; yea, the stars are not pure in his sight.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>How much less man, _that is_ a worm? and the son of man, _which is_ a worm?  

## CHAPTER 26.

But Job answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>How hast thou helped _him that is_ without power? _how_ savest thou the arm _that hath_ no strength?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>How hast thou counselled _him that hath_ no wisdom? and _how_ hast thou plentifully declared the thing as it is?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>To whom hast thou uttered words? and whose spirit came from thee?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Dead _things_ are formed from under the waters, and the inhabitants thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Hell _is_ naked before him, and destruction hath no covering.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>He stretcheth out the north over the empty place, _and_ hangeth the earth upon nothing.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He bindeth up the waters in his thick clouds; and the cloud is not rent under them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>He holdeth back the face of his throne, _and_ spreadeth his cloud upon it.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He hath compassed the waters with bounds, until the day and night come to an end.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The pillars of heaven tremble and are astonished at his reproof.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>He divideth the sea with his power, and by his understanding he smiteth through the proud.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>By his spirit he hath garnished the heavens; his hand hath formed the crooked serpent.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Lo, these _are_ parts of his ways: but how little a portion is heard of him? but the thunder of his power who can understand?  

## CHAPTER 27.

Moreover Job continued his parable, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>_As_ God liveth, _who_ hath taken away my judgment; and the Almighty, _who_ hath vexed my soul;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>All the while my breath _is_ in me, and the spirit of God _is_ in my nostrils;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>My lips shall not speak wickedness, nor my tongue utter deceit.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>God forbid that I should justify you: till I die I will not remove mine integrity from me.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>My righteousness I hold fast, and will not let it go: my heart shall not reproach _me_ so long as I live.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Let mine enemy be as the wicked, and he that riseth up against me as the unrighteous.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For what _is_ the hope of the hypocrite, though he hath gained, when God taketh away his soul?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Will God hear his cry when trouble cometh upon him?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Will he delight himself in the Almighty? will he always call upon God?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ I will teach you by the hand of God: _that_ which _is_ with the Almighty will I not conceal.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Behold, all ye yourselves have seen _it_; why then are ye thus altogether vain?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>This _is_ the portion of a wicked man with God, and the heritage of oppressors, _which_ they shall receive of the Almighty.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>If his children be multiplied, _it is_ for the sword: and his offspring shall not be satisfied with bread.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Those that remain of him shall be buried in death: and his widows shall not weep.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Though he heap up silver as the dust, and prepare raiment as the clay;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>He may prepare _it_, but the just shall put _it_ on, and the innocent shall divide the silver.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>He buildeth his house as a moth, and as a booth _that_ the keeper maketh.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The rich man shall lie down, but he shall not be gathered: he openeth his eyes, and he _is_ not.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Terrors take hold on him as waters, a tempest stealeth him away in the night.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>The east wind carrieth him away, and he departeth: and as a storm hurleth him out of his place.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For _God_ shall cast upon him, and not spare: he would fain flee out of his hand.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>_Men_ shall clap their hands at him, and shall hiss him out of his place.  

## CHAPTER 28.

Surely there is a vein for the silver, and a place for gold _where_ they fine _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Iron is taken out of the earth, and brass _is_ molten _out of_ the stone.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>He setteth an end to darkness, and searcheth out all perfection: the stones of darkness, and the shadow of death.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The flood breaketh out from the inhabitant; _even the waters_ forgotten of the foot: they are dried up, they are gone away from men.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>_As for_ the earth, out of it cometh bread: and under it is turned up as it were fire.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The stones of it _are_ the place of sapphires: and it hath dust of gold.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_There is_ a path which no fowl knoweth, and which the vulture’s eye hath not seen:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The lion’s whelps have not trodden it, nor the fierce lion passed by it.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>He putteth forth his hand upon the rock; he overturneth the mountains by the roots.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He cutteth out rivers among the rocks; and his eye seeth every precious thing.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He bindeth the floods from overflowing; and _the thing that is_ hid bringeth he forth to light.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But where shall wisdom be found? and where _is_ the place of understanding?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Man knoweth not the price thereof; neither is it found in the land of the living.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ The depth saith, It _is_ not in me: and the sea saith, _It is_ not with me.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>It cannot be gotten for gold, neither shall silver be weighed _for_ the price thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>It cannot be valued with the gold of Ophir, with the precious onyx, or the sapphire.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>The gold and the crystal cannot equal it: and the exchange of it _shall not be for_ jewels of fine gold.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>No mention shall be made of coral, or of pearls: for the price of wisdom _is_ above rubies.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The topaz of Ethiopia shall not equal it, neither shall it be valued with pure gold.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Whence then cometh wisdom? and where _is_ the place of understanding?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Seeing it is hid from the eyes of all living, and kept close from the fowls of the air.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Destruction and death say, We have heard the fame thereof with our ears.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>God understandeth the way thereof, and he knoweth the place thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For he looketh to the ends of the earth, _and_ seeth under the whole heaven;  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>To make the weight for the winds; and he weigheth the waters by measure.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>When he made a decree for the rain, and a way for the lightning of the thunder:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Then did he see it, and declare it; he prepared it, yea, and searched it out.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And unto man he said, Behold, the fear of the Lord, that _is_ wisdom; and to depart from evil _is_ understanding.  

## CHAPTER 29.

Moreover Job continued his parable, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Oh that I were as _in_ months past, as _in_ the days _when_ God preserved me;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>When his candle shined upon my head, _and when_ by his light I walked _through_ darkness;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>As I was in the days of my youth, when the secret of God _was_ upon my tabernacle;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>When the Almighty _was_ yet with me, _when_ my children _were_ about me;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>When I washed my steps with butter, and the rock poured me out rivers of oil;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ When I went out to the gate through the city, _when_ I prepared my seat in the street!  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The young men saw me, and hid themselves: and the aged arose, _and_ stood up.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The princes refrained talking, and laid _their_ hand on their mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The nobles held their peace, and their tongue cleaved to the roof of their mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>When the ear heard _me_, then it blessed me; and when the eye saw _me_, it gave witness to me:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Because I delivered the poor that cried, and the fatherless, and _him that had_ none to help him.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The blessing of him that was ready to perish came upon me: and I caused the widow’s heart to sing for joy.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I put on righteousness, and it clothed me: my judgment _was_ as a robe and a diadem.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>I was eyes to the blind, and feet _was_ I to the lame.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>I _was_ a father to the poor: and the cause _which_ I knew not I searched out.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And I brake the jaws of the wicked, and plucked the spoil out of his teeth.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Then I said, I shall die in my nest, and I shall multiply _my_ days as the sand.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>My root _was_ spread out by the waters, and the dew lay all night upon my branch.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>My glory _was_ fresh in me, and my bow was renewed in my hand.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Unto me _men_ gave ear, and waited, and kept silence at my counsel.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>After my words they spake not again; and my speech dropped upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they waited for me as for the rain; and they opened their mouth wide _as_ for the latter rain.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>_If_ I laughed on them, they believed _it_ not; and the light of my countenance they cast not down.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>I chose out their way, and sat chief, and dwelt as a king in the army, as one _that_ comforteth the mourners.  

## CHAPTER 30.

But now _they that are_ younger than I have me in derision, whose fathers I would have disdained to have set with the dogs of my flock.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Yea, whereto _might_ the strength of their hands _profit_ me, in whom old age was perished?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For want and famine _they were_ solitary; fleeing into the wilderness in former time desolate and waste.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who cut up mallows by the bushes, and juniper roots _for_ their meat.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>They were driven forth from among _men_, (they cried after them as _after_ a thief;)  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>To dwell in the clifts of the valleys, _in_ caves of the earth, and _in_ the rocks.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Among the bushes they brayed; under the nettles they were gathered together.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>_They were_ children of fools, yea, children of base men: they were viler than the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And now am I their song, yea, I am their byword.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>They abhor me, they flee far from me, and spare not to spit in my face.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Because he hath loosed my cord, and afflicted me, they have also let loose the bridle before me.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Upon _my_ right _hand_ rise the youth; they push away my feet, and they raise up against me the ways of their destruction.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>They mar my path, they set forward my calamity, they have no helper.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>They came _upon me_ as a wide breaking in _of waters_: in the desolation they rolled themselves _upon me_.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Terrors are turned upon me: they pursue my soul as the wind: and my welfare passeth away as a cloud.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And now my soul is poured out upon me; the days of affliction have taken hold upon me.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>My bones are pierced in me in the night season: and my sinews take no rest.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>By the great force _of my disease_ is my garment changed: it bindeth me about as the collar of my coat.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>He hath cast me into the mire, and I am become like dust and ashes.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>I cry unto thee, and thou dost not hear me: I stand up, and thou regardest me _not_.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Thou art become cruel to me: with thy strong hand thou opposest thyself against me.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Thou liftest me up to the wind; thou causest me to ride _upon it_, and dissolvest my substance.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For I know _that_ thou wilt bring me _to_ death, and _to_ the house appointed for all living.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Howbeit he will not stretch out _his_ hand to the grave, though they cry in his destruction.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Did not I weep for him that was in trouble? was _not_ my soul grieved for the poor?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>When I looked for good, then evil came _unto me_: and when I waited for light, there came darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>My bowels boiled, and rested not: the days of affliction prevented me.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>I went mourning without the sun: I stood up, _and_ I cried in the congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>I am a brother to dragons, and a companion to owls.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>My skin is black upon me, and my bones are burned with heat.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>My harp also is _turned_ to mourning, and my organ into the voice of them that weep.  

## CHAPTER 31.

I made a covenant with mine eyes; why then should I think upon a maid?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For what portion of God _is there_ from above? and _what_ inheritance of the Almighty from on high?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>_Is_ not destruction to the wicked? and a strange _punishment_ to the workers of iniquity?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Doth not he see my ways, and count all my steps?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>If I have walked with vanity, or if my foot hath hasted to deceit;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Let me be weighed in an even balance, that God may know mine integrity.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>If my step hath turned out of the way, and mine heart walked after mine eyes, and if any blot hath cleaved to mine hands;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>_Then_ let me sow, and let another eat; yea, let my offspring be rooted out.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ If mine heart have been deceived by a woman, or _if_ I have laid wait at my neighbour’s door;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>_Then_ let my wife grind unto another, and let others bow down upon her.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For this _is_ an heinous crime; yea, it _is_ an iniquity _to be punished by_ the judges.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For it _is_ a fire _that_ consumeth to destruction, and would root out all mine increase.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>If I did despise the cause of my manservant or of my maidservant, when they contended with me;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>What then shall I do when God riseth up? and when he visiteth, what shall I answer him?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Did not he that made me in the womb make him? and did not one fashion us in the womb?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ If I have withheld the poor from _their_ desire, or have caused the eyes of the widow to fail;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Or have eaten my morsel myself alone, and the fatherless hath not eaten thereof;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>(For from my youth he was brought up with me, as _with_ a father, and I have guided her from my mother’s womb;)  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>If I have seen any perish for want of clothing, or any poor without covering;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>If his loins have not blessed me, and _if_ he were _not_ warmed with the fleece of my sheep;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>If I have lifted up my hand against the fatherless, when I saw my help in the gate:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>_Then_ let mine arm fall from my shoulder blade, and mine arm be broken from the bone.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For destruction _from_ God _was_ a terror to me, and by reason of his highness I could not endure.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ If I have made gold my hope, or have said to the fine gold, _Thou art_ my confidence;  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>If I rejoiced because my wealth _was_ great, and because mine hand had gotten much;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>If I beheld the sun when it shined, or the moon walking _in_ brightness;  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And my heart hath been secretly enticed, or my mouth hath kissed my hand:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>This also _were_ an iniquity _to be punished by_ the judge: for I should have denied the God _that is_ above.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>If I rejoiced at the destruction of him that hated me, or lifted up myself when evil found him:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Neither have I suffered my mouth to sin by wishing a curse to his soul.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>If the men of my tabernacle said not, Oh that we had of his flesh! we cannot be satisfied.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>The stranger did not lodge in the street: _but_ I opened my doors to the traveller.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ If I covered my transgressions as Adam, by hiding mine iniquity in my bosom:  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Did I fear a great multitude, or did the contempt of families terrify me, that I kept silence, _and_ went not out of the door?  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Oh that one would hear me! behold, my desire _is, that_ the Almighty would answer me, and _that_ mine adversary had written a book.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Surely I would take it upon my shoulder, _and_ bind it _as_ a crown to me.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>I would declare unto him the number of my steps; as a prince would I go near unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>If my land cry against me, or that the furrows likewise thereof complain;  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>If I have eaten the fruits thereof without money, or have caused the owners thereof to lose their life:  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>Let thistles grow instead of wheat, and cockle instead of barley. The words of Job are ended.  

## CHAPTER 32.

So these three men ceased to answer Job, because he _was_ righteous in his own eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then was kindled the wrath of Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite, of the kindred of Ram: against Job was his wrath kindled, because he justified himself rather than God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Also against his three friends was his wrath kindled, because they had found no answer, and _yet_ had condemned Job.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Now Elihu had waited till Job had spoken, because they _were_ elder than he.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>When Elihu saw that _there was_ no answer in the mouth of _these_ three men, then his wrath was kindled.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And Elihu the son of Barachel the Buzite answered and said, I _am_ young, and ye _are_ very old; wherefore I was afraid, and durst not shew you mine opinion.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>I said, Days should speak, and multitude of years should teach wisdom.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But _there is_ a spirit in man: and the inspiration of the Almighty giveth them understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Great men are not _always_ wise: neither do the aged understand judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Therefore I said, Hearken to me; I also will shew mine opinion.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Behold, I waited for your words; I gave ear to your reasons, whilst ye searched out what to say.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Yea, I attended unto you, and, behold, _there was_ none of you that convinced Job, _or_ that answered his words:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Lest ye should say, We have found out wisdom: God thrusteth him down, not man.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Now he hath not directed _his_ words against me: neither will I answer him with your speeches.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ They were amazed, they answered no more: they left off speaking.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>When I had waited, (for they spake not, but stood still, _and_ answered no more;)  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>_I said_, I will answer also my part, I also will shew mine opinion.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For I am full of matter, the spirit within me constraineth me.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Behold, my belly _is_ as wine _which_ hath no vent; it is ready to burst like new bottles.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>I will speak, that I may be refreshed: I will open my lips and answer.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Let me not, I pray you, accept any man’s person, neither let me give flattering titles unto man.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For I know not to give flattering titles; _in so doing_ my maker would soon take me away.  

## CHAPTER 33.

Wherefore, Job, I pray thee, hear my speeches, and hearken to all my words.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Behold, now I have opened my mouth, my tongue hath spoken in my mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>My words _shall be of_ the uprightness of my heart: and my lips shall utter knowledge clearly.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The Spirit of God hath made me, and the breath of the Almighty hath given me life.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>If thou canst answer me, set _thy words_ in order before me, stand up.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Behold, I _am_ according to thy wish in God’s stead: I also am formed out of the clay.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Behold, my terror shall not make thee afraid, neither shall my hand be heavy upon thee.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Surely thou hast spoken in mine hearing, and I have heard the voice of _thy_ words, _saying_,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>I am clean without transgression, I _am_ innocent; neither _is there_ iniquity in me.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Behold, he findeth occasions against me, he counteth me for his enemy,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He putteth my feet in the stocks, he marketh all my paths.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Behold, _in_ this thou art not just: I will answer thee, that God is greater than man.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Why dost thou strive against him? for he giveth not account of any of his matters.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ For God speaketh once, yea twice, _yet man_ perceiveth it not.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>In a dream, in a vision of the night, when deep sleep falleth upon men, in slumberings upon the bed;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Then he openeth the ears of men, and sealeth their instruction,  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>That he may withdraw man _from his_ purpose, and hide pride from man.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>He keepeth back his soul from the pit, and his life from perishing by the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ He is chastened also with pain upon his bed, and the multitude of his bones with strong _pain_:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>So that his life abhorreth bread, and his soul dainty meat.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>His flesh is consumed away, that it cannot be seen; and his bones _that_ were not seen stick out.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Yea, his soul draweth near unto the grave, and his life to the destroyers.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>If there be a messenger with him, an interpreter, one among a thousand, to shew unto man his uprightness:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Then he is gracious unto him, and saith, Deliver him from going down to the pit: I have found a ransom.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>His flesh shall be fresher than a child’s: he shall return to the days of his youth:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>He shall pray unto God, and he will be favourable unto him: and he shall see his face with joy: for he will render unto man his righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>He looketh upon men, and _if any_ say, I have sinned, and perverted _that which was_ right, and it profited me not;  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>He will deliver his soul from going into the pit, and his life shall see the light.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ Lo, all these _things_ worketh God oftentimes with man,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>To bring back his soul from the pit, to be enlightened with the light of the living.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Mark well, O Job, hearken unto me: hold thy peace, and I will speak.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>If thou hast any thing to say, answer me: speak, for I desire to justify thee.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>If not, hearken unto me: hold thy peace, and I shall teach thee wisdom.  

## CHAPTER 34.

Furthermore Elihu answered and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Hear my words, O ye wise _men_; and give ear unto me, ye that have knowledge.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For the ear trieth words, as the mouth tasteth meat.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Let us choose to us judgment: let us know among ourselves what _is_ good.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For Job hath said, I am righteous: and God hath taken away my judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Should I lie against my right? my wound _is_ incurable without transgression.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>What man _is_ like Job, _who_ drinketh up scorning like water?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Which goeth in company with the workers of iniquity, and walketh with wicked men.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For he hath said, It profiteth a man nothing that he should delight himself with God.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore hearken unto me, ye men of understanding: far be it from God, _that he should do_ wickedness; and _from_ the Almighty, _that he should commit_ iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For the work of a man shall he render unto him, and cause every man to find according to _his_ ways.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Yea, surely God will not do wickedly, neither will the Almighty pervert judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Who hath given him a charge over the earth? or who hath disposed the whole world?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>If he set his heart upon man, _if_ he gather unto himself his spirit and his breath;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>All flesh shall perish together, and man shall turn again unto dust.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ If now _thou hast_ understanding, hear this: hearken to the voice of my words.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Shall even he that hateth right govern? and wilt thou condemn him that is most just?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>_Is it fit_ to say to a king, _Thou art_ wicked? _and_ to princes, _Ye are_ ungodly?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>_How much less to him_ that accepteth not the persons of princes, nor regardeth the rich more than the poor? for they all _are_ the work of his hands.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>In a moment shall they die, and the people shall be troubled at midnight, and pass away: and the mighty shall be taken away without hand.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For his eyes _are_ upon the ways of man, and he seeth all his goings.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>_There is_ no darkness, nor shadow of death, where the workers of iniquity may hide themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For he will not lay upon man more _than right_; that he should enter into judgment with God.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>He shall break in pieces mighty men without number, and set others in their stead.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Therefore he knoweth their works, and he overturneth _them_ in the night, so that they are destroyed.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>He striketh them as wicked men in the open sight of others;  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Because they turned back from him, and would not consider any of his ways:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>So that they cause the cry of the poor to come unto him, and he heareth the cry of the afflicted.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>When he giveth quietness, who then can make trouble? and when he hideth _his_ face, who then can behold him? whether _it be done_ against a nation, or against a man only:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>That the hypocrite reign not, lest the people be ensnared.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ Surely it is meet to be said unto God, I have borne _chastisement_, I will not offend _any more_:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>_That which_ I see not teach thou me: if I have done iniquity, I will do no more.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>_Should it be_ according to thy mind? he will recompense it, whether thou refuse, or whether thou choose; and not I: therefore speak what thou knowest.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Let men of understanding tell me, and let a wise man hearken unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Job hath spoken without knowledge, and his words _were_ without wisdom.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>My desire _is that_ Job may be tried unto the end because of _his_ answers for wicked men.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>For he addeth rebellion unto his sin, he clappeth _his hands_ among us, and multiplieth his words against God.  

## CHAPTER 35.

Elihu spake moreover, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Thinkest thou this to be right, _that_ thou saidst, My righteousness _is_ more than God’s?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For thou saidst, What advantage will it be unto thee? _and_, What profit shall I have, _if I be cleansed_ from my sin?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I will answer thee, and thy companions with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Look unto the heavens, and see; and behold the clouds _which_ are higher than thou.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>If thou sinnest, what doest thou against him? or _if_ thy transgressions be multiplied, what doest thou unto him?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>If thou be righteous, what givest thou him? or what receiveth he of thine hand?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Thy wickedness _may hurt_ a man as thou _art_; and thy righteousness _may profit_ the son of man.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ By reason of the multitude of oppressions they make _the oppressed_ to cry: they cry out by reason of the arm of the mighty.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But none saith, Where _is_ God my maker, who giveth songs in the night;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Who teacheth us more than the beasts of the earth, and maketh us wiser than the fowls of heaven?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>There they cry, but none giveth answer, because of the pride of evil men.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Surely God will not hear vanity, neither will the Almighty regard it.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Although thou sayest thou shalt not see him, _yet_ judgment _is_ before him; therefore trust thou in him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But now, because _it is_ not _so_, he hath visited in his anger; yet he knoweth _it_ not in great extremity:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Therefore doth Job open his mouth in vain; he multiplieth words without knowledge.  

## CHAPTER 36.

Elihu also proceeded, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Suffer me a little, and I will shew thee that _I have_ yet to speak on God’s behalf.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I will fetch my knowledge from afar, and will ascribe righteousness to my Maker.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For truly my words _shall_ not _be_ false: he that is perfect in knowledge _is_ with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, God _is_ mighty, and despiseth not _any: he is_ mighty in strength _and_ wisdom.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>He preserveth not the life of the wicked: but giveth right to the poor.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>He withdraweth not his eyes from the righteous: but with kings _are they_ on the throne; yea, he doth establish them for ever, and they are exalted.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And if _they be_ bound in fetters, _and_ be holden in cords of affliction;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then he sheweth them their work, and their transgressions that they have exceeded.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He openeth also their ear to discipline, and commandeth that they return from iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>If they obey and serve _him_, they shall spend their days in prosperity, and their years in pleasures.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But if they obey not, they shall perish by the sword, and they shall die without knowledge.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But the hypocrites in heart heap up wrath: they cry not when he bindeth them.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>They die in youth, and their life _is_ among the unclean.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ He delivereth the poor in his affliction, and openeth their ears in oppression.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Even so would he have removed thee out of the strait _into_ a broad place, where _there is_ no straitness; and that which should be set on thy table _should be_ full of fatness.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But thou hast fulfilled the judgment of the wicked: judgment and justice take hold _on thee_.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Because _there is_ wrath, _beware_ lest he take thee away with _his_ stroke: then a great ransom cannot deliver thee.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Will he esteem thy riches? _no_, not gold, nor all the forces of strength.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Desire not the night, when people are cut off in their place.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Take heed, regard not iniquity: for this hast thou chosen rather than affliction.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Behold, God exalteth by his power: who teacheth like him?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Who hath enjoined him his way? or who can say, Thou hast wrought iniquity?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Remember that thou magnify his work, which men behold.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Every man may see it; man may behold _it_ afar off.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Behold, God _is_ great, and we know _him_ not, neither can the number of his years be searched out.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For he maketh small the drops of water: they pour down rain according to the vapour thereof:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Which the clouds do drop _and_ distil upon man abundantly.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Also can _any_ understand the spreadings of the clouds, _or_ the noise of his tabernacle?  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Behold, he spreadeth his light upon it, and covereth the bottom of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>For by them judgeth he the people; he giveth meat in abundance.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>With clouds he covereth the light; and commandeth it _not to shine_ by _the cloud_ that cometh betwixt.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>The noise thereof sheweth concerning it, the cattle also concerning the vapour.  

## CHAPTER 37.

At this also my heart trembleth, and is moved out of his place.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Hear attentively the noise of his voice, and the sound _that_ goeth out of his mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>He directeth it under the whole heaven, and his lightning unto the ends of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>After it a voice roareth: he thundereth with the voice of his excellency; and he will not stay them when his voice is heard.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>God thundereth marvellously with his voice; great things doeth he, which we cannot comprehend.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ For he saith to the snow, Be thou _on_ the earth; likewise to the small rain, and to the great rain of his strength.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>He sealeth up the hand of every man; that all men may know his work.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Then the beasts go into dens, and remain in their places.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Out of the south cometh the whirlwind: and cold out of the north.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>By the breath of God frost is given: and the breadth of the waters is straitened.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Also by watering he wearieth the thick cloud: he scattereth his bright cloud:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And it is turned round about by his counsels: that they may do whatsoever he commandeth them upon the face of the world in the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>He causeth it to come, whether for correction, or for his land, or for mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Hearken unto this, O Job: stand still, and consider the wondrous works of God.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Dost thou know when God disposed them, and caused the light of his cloud to shine?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Dost thou know the balancings of the clouds, the wondrous works of him which is perfect in knowledge?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>How thy garments _are_ warm, when he quieteth the earth by the south _wind_?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Hast thou with him spread out the sky, _which is_ strong, _and_ as a molten looking glass?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Teach us what we shall say unto him; _for_ we cannot order _our speech_ by reason of darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Shall it be told him that I speak? if a man speak, surely he shall be swallowed up.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And now _men_ see not the bright light which _is_ in the clouds: but the wind passeth, and cleanseth them.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Fair weather cometh out of the north: with God _is_ terrible majesty.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>_Touching_ the Almighty, we cannot find him out: _he is_ excellent in power, and in judgment, and in plenty of justice: he will not afflict.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Men do therefore fear him: he respecteth not any _that are_ wise of heart.  

## CHAPTER 38.

Then the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> answered Job out of the whirlwind, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Who _is_ this that darkeneth counsel by words without knowledge?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Gird up now thy loins like a man; for I will demand of thee, and answer thou me.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Where wast thou when I laid the foundations of the earth? declare, if thou hast understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Who hath laid the measures thereof, if thou knowest? or who hath stretched the line upon it?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Whereupon are the foundations thereof fastened? or who laid the corner stone thereof;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>When the morning stars sang together, and all the sons of God shouted for joy?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Or _who_ shut up the sea with doors, when it brake forth, _as if_ it had issued out of the womb?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>When I made the cloud the garment thereof, and thick darkness a swaddlingband for it,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And brake up for it my decreed _place_, and set bars and doors,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And said, Hitherto shalt thou come, but no further: and here shall thy proud waves be stayed?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Hast thou commanded the morning since thy days; _and_ caused the dayspring to know his place;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>That it might take hold of the ends of the earth, that the wicked might be shaken out of it?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>It is turned as clay _to_ the seal; and they stand as a garment.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And from the wicked their light is withholden, and the high arm shall be broken.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Hast thou entered into the springs of the sea? or hast thou walked in the search of the depth?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Have the gates of death been opened unto thee? or hast thou seen the doors of the shadow of death?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Hast thou perceived the breadth of the earth? declare if thou knowest it all.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Where _is_ the way _where_ light dwelleth? and _as for_ darkness, where _is_ the place thereof,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>That thou shouldest take it to the bound thereof, and that thou shouldest know the paths _to_ the house thereof?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Knowest thou _it_, because thou wast then born? or _because_ the number of thy days _is_ great?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Hast thou entered into the treasures of the snow? or hast thou seen the treasures of the hail,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Which I have reserved against the time of trouble, against the day of battle and war?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>By what way is the light parted, _which_ scattereth the east wind upon the earth?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ Who hath divided a watercourse for the overflowing of waters, or a way for the lightning of thunder;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>To cause it to rain on the earth, _where_ no man _is; on_ the wilderness, wherein _there is_ no man;  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>To satisfy the desolate and waste _ground_; and to cause the bud of the tender herb to spring forth?  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Hath the rain a father? or who hath begotten the drops of dew?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Out of whose womb came the ice? and the hoary frost of heaven, who hath gendered it?  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>The waters are hid as _with_ a stone, and the face of the deep is frozen.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Canst thou bind the sweet influences of Pleiades, or loose the bands of Orion?  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Canst thou bring forth Mazzaroth in his season? or canst thou guide Arcturus with his sons?  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Knowest thou the ordinances of heaven? canst thou set the dominion thereof in the earth?  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Canst thou lift up thy voice to the clouds, that abundance of waters may cover thee?  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Canst thou send lightnings, that they may go, and say unto thee, Here we _are_?  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Who hath put wisdom in the inward parts? or who hath given understanding to the heart?  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Who can number the clouds in wisdom? or who can stay the bottles of heaven,  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>When the dust groweth into hardness, and the clods cleave fast together?  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>Wilt thou hunt the prey for the lion? or fill the appetite of the young lions,  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>When they couch in _their_ dens, _and_ abide in the covert to lie in wait?  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>Who provideth for the raven his food? when his young ones cry unto God, they wander for lack of meat.  

## CHAPTER 39.

Knowest thou the time when the wild goats of the rock bring forth? _or_ canst thou mark when the hinds do calve?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Canst thou number the months _that_ they fulfil? or knowest thou the time when they bring forth?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>They bow themselves, they bring forth their young ones, they cast out their sorrows.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Their young ones are in good liking, they grow up with corn; they go forth, and return not unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Who hath sent out the wild ass free? or who hath loosed the bands of the wild ass?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Whose house I have made the wilderness, and the barren land his dwellings.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>He scorneth the multitude of the city, neither regardeth he the crying of the driver.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The range of the mountains _is_ his pasture, and he searcheth after every green thing.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Will the unicorn be willing to serve thee, or abide by thy crib?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Canst thou bind the unicorn with his band in the furrow? or will he harrow the valleys after thee?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Wilt thou trust him, because his strength _is_ great? or wilt thou leave thy labour to him?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Wilt thou believe him, that he will bring home thy seed, and gather _it into_ thy barn?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ _Gavest thou_ the goodly wings unto the peacocks? or wings and feathers unto the ostrich?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Which leaveth her eggs in the earth, and warmeth them in dust,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And forgetteth that the foot may crush them, or that the wild beast may break them.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>She is hardened against her young ones, as though _they were_ not hers: her labour is in vain without fear;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Because God hath deprived her of wisdom, neither hath he imparted to her understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>What time she lifteth up herself on high, she scorneth the horse and his rider.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Hast thou given the horse strength? hast thou clothed his neck with thunder?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Canst thou make him afraid as a grasshopper? the glory of his nostrils _is_ terrible.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>He paweth in the valley, and rejoiceth in _his_ strength: he goeth on to meet the armed men.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>He mocketh at fear, and is not affrighted; neither turneth he back from the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The quiver rattleth against him, the glittering spear and the shield.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>He swalloweth the ground with fierceness and rage: neither believeth he that _it is_ the sound of the trumpet.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>He saith among the trumpets, Ha, ha; and he smelleth the battle afar off, the thunder of the captains, and the shouting.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ Doth the hawk fly by thy wisdom, _and_ stretch her wings toward the south?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Doth the eagle mount up at thy command, and make her nest on high?  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>She dwelleth and abideth on the rock, upon the crag of the rock, and the strong place.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>From thence she seeketh the prey, _and_ her eyes behold afar off.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Her young ones also suck up blood: and where the slain _are_, there _is_ she.  

## CHAPTER 40.

Moreover the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> answered Job, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Shall he that contendeth with the Almighty instruct _him_? he that reproveth God, let him answer it.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Then Job answered the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Behold, I am vile; what shall I answer thee? I will lay mine hand upon my mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Once have I spoken; but I will not answer: yea, twice; but I will proceed no further.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Then answered the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto Job out of the whirlwind, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Gird up thy loins now like a man: I will demand of thee, and declare thou unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Wilt thou also disannul my judgment? wilt thou condemn me, that thou mayest be righteous?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Hast thou an arm like God? or canst thou thunder with a voice like him?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Deck thyself now _with_ majesty and excellency; and array thyself with glory and beauty.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Cast abroad the rage of thy wrath: and behold every one _that is_ proud, and abase him.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Look on every one _that is_ proud, _and_ bring him low; and tread down the wicked in their place.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Hide them in the dust together; _and_ bind their faces in secret.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Then will I also confess unto thee that thine own right hand can save thee.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Behold now behemoth, which I made with thee; he eateth grass as an ox.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Lo now, his strength _is_ in his loins, and his force _is_ in the navel of his belly.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>He moveth his tail like a cedar: the sinews of his stones are wrapped together.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>His bones _are as_ strong pieces of brass; his bones _are_ like bars of iron.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>He _is_ the chief of the ways of God: he that made him can make his sword to approach _unto him_.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Surely the mountains bring him forth food, where all the beasts of the field play.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>He lieth under the shady trees, in the covert of the reed, and fens.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>The shady trees cover him _with_ their shadow; the willows of the brook compass him about.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Behold, he drinketh up a river, _and_ hasteth not: he trusteth that he can draw up Jordan into his mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>He taketh it with his eyes: _his_ nose pierceth through snares.  

## CHAPTER 41.

Canst thou draw out leviathan with an hook? or his tongue with a cord _which_ thou lettest down?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Canst thou put an hook into his nose? or bore his jaw through with a thorn?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Will he make many supplications unto thee? will he speak soft _words_ unto thee?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Will he make a covenant with thee? wilt thou take him for a servant for ever?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Wilt thou play with him as _with_ a bird? or wilt thou bind him for thy maidens?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Shall the companions make a banquet of him? shall they part him among the merchants?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Canst thou fill his skin with barbed irons? or his head with fish spears?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Lay thine hand upon him, remember the battle, do no more.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Behold, the hope of him is in vain: shall not _one_ be cast down even at the sight of him?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>None _is so_ fierce that dare stir him up: who then is able to stand before me?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Who hath prevented me, that I should repay _him? whatsoever is_ under the whole heaven is mine.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I will not conceal his parts, nor his power, nor his comely proportion.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Who can discover the face of his garment? _or_ who can come _to him_ with his double bridle?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Who can open the doors of his face? his teeth _are_ terrible round about.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>_His_ scales _are his_ pride, shut up together _as with_ a close seal.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>One is so near to another, that no air can come between them.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>They are joined one to another, they stick together, that they cannot be sundered.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>By his neesings a light doth shine, and his eyes _are_ like the eyelids of the morning.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Out of his mouth go burning lamps, _and_ sparks of fire leap out.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Out of his nostrils goeth smoke, as _out_ of a seething pot or caldron.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>His breath kindleth coals, and a flame goeth out of his mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>In his neck remaineth strength, and sorrow is turned into joy before him.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The flakes of his flesh are joined together: they are firm in themselves; they cannot be moved.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>His heart is as firm as a stone; yea, as hard as a piece of the nether _millstone_.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>When he raiseth up himself, the mighty are afraid: by reason of breakings they purify themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>The sword of him that layeth at him cannot hold: the spear, the dart, nor the habergeon.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>He esteemeth iron as straw, _and_ brass as rotten wood.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>The arrow cannot make him flee: slingstones are turned with him into stubble.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Darts are counted as stubble: he laugheth at the shaking of a spear.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Sharp stones _are_ under him: he spreadeth sharp pointed things upon the mire.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>He maketh the deep to boil like a pot: he maketh the sea like a pot of ointment.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>He maketh a path to shine after him; _one_ would think the deep _to be_ hoary.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Upon earth there is not his like, who is made without fear.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>He beholdeth all high _things_: he _is_ a king over all the children of pride.  

## CHAPTER 42.

Then Job answered the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>I know that thou canst do every _thing_, and _that_ no thought can be withholden from thee.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Who _is_ he that hideth counsel without knowledge? therefore have I uttered that I understood not; things too wonderful for me, which I knew not.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Hear, I beseech thee, and I will speak: I will demand of thee, and declare thou unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>I have heard of thee by the hearing of the ear: but now mine eye seeth thee.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Wherefore I abhor _myself_, and repent in dust and ashes.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And it was _so_, that after the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had spoken these words unto Job, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said to Eliphaz the Temanite, My wrath is kindled against thee, and against thy two friends: for ye have not spoken of me _the thing that is_ right, as my servant Job _hath_.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Therefore take unto you now seven bullocks and seven rams, and go to my servant Job, and offer up for yourselves a burnt offering; and my servant Job shall pray for you: for him will I accept: lest I deal with you _after your_ folly, in that ye have not spoken of me _the thing which is_ right, like my servant Job.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>So Eliphaz the Temanite and Bildad the Shuhite _and_ Zophar the Naamathite went, and did according as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded them: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> also accepted Job.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> turned the captivity of Job, when he prayed for his friends: also the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> gave Job twice as much as he had before.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then came there unto him all his brethren, and all his sisters, and all they that had been of his acquaintance before, and did eat bread with him in his house: and they bemoaned him, and comforted him over all the evil that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had brought upon him: every man also gave him a piece of money, and every one an earring of gold.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>So the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> blessed the latter end of Job more than his beginning: for he had fourteen thousand sheep, and six thousand camels, and a thousand yoke of oxen, and a thousand she asses.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>He had also seven sons and three daughters.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he called the name of the first, Jemima; and the name of the second, Kezia; and the name of the third, Keren–happuch.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And in all the land were no women found _so_ fair as the daughters of Job: and their father gave them inheritance among their brethren.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>After this lived Job an hundred and forty years, and saw his sons, and his sons’ sons, _even_ four generations.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>So Job died, _being_ old and full of days.
