# Obadiah

### OBADIAH

The vision of Obadiah. Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> concerning Edom; We have heard a rumour from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and an ambassador is sent among the heathen, Arise ye, and let us rise up against her in battle.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Behold, I have made thee small among the heathen: thou art greatly despised.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ The pride of thine heart hath deceived thee, thou that dwellest in the clefts of the rock, whose habitation _is_ high; that saith in his heart, Who shall bring me down to the ground?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Though thou exalt _thyself_ as the eagle, and though thou set thy nest among the stars, thence will I bring thee down, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>If thieves came to thee, if robbers by night, (how art thou cut off!) would they not have stolen till they had enough? if the grapegatherers came to thee, would they not leave _some_ grapes?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>How are _the things_ of Esau searched out! _how_ are his hidden things sought up!  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>All the men of thy confederacy have brought thee _even_ to the border: the men that were at peace with thee have deceived thee, _and_ prevailed against thee; _they that eat_ thy bread have laid a wound under thee: _there is_ none understanding in him.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Shall I not in that day, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, even destroy the wise _men_ out of Edom, and understanding out of the mount of Esau?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And thy mighty _men_, O Teman, shall be dismayed, to the end that every one of the mount of Esau may be cut off by slaughter.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ For _thy_ violence against thy brother Jacob shame shall cover thee, and thou shalt be cut off for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>In the day that thou stoodest on the other side, in the day that the strangers carried away captive his forces, and foreigners entered into his gates, and cast lots upon Jerusalem, even thou _wast_ as one of them.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But thou shouldest not have looked on the day of thy brother in the day that he became a stranger; neither shouldest thou have rejoiced over the children of Judah in the day of their destruction; neither shouldest thou have spoken proudly in the day of distress.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thou shouldest not have entered into the gate of my people in the day of their calamity; yea, thou shouldest not have looked on their affliction in the day of their calamity, nor have laid _hands_ on their substance in the day of their calamity;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Neither shouldest thou have stood in the crossway, to cut off those of his that did escape; neither shouldest thou have delivered up those of his that did remain in the day of distress.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ near upon all the heathen: as thou hast done, it shall be done unto thee: thy reward shall return upon thine own head.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For as ye have drunk upon my holy mountain, _so_ shall all the heathen drink continually, yea, they shall drink, and they shall swallow down, and they shall be as though they had not been.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ But upon mount Zion shall be deliverance, and there shall be holiness; and the house of Jacob shall possess their possessions.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the house of Jacob shall be a fire, and the house of Joseph a flame, and the house of Esau for stubble, and they shall kindle in them, and devour them; and there shall not be _any_ remaining of the house of Esau; for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And _they of_ the south shall possess the mount of Esau; and _they of_ the plain the Philistines: and they shall possess the fields of Ephraim, and the fields of Samaria: and Benjamin _shall possess_ Gilead.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the captivity of this host of the children of Israel _shall possess_ that of the Canaanites, _even_ unto Zarephath; and the captivity of Jerusalem, which _is_ in Sepharad, shall possess the cities of the south.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And saviours shall come up on mount Zion to judge the mount of Esau; and the kingdom shall be the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span>.
