# Hebrews

### THE EPISTLE OF PAUL THE APOSTLE TO THE HEBREWS

## CHAPTER 1.

God, who at sundry times and in divers manners spake in time past unto the fathers by the prophets,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Hath in these last days spoken unto us by _his_ Son, whom he hath appointed heir of all things, by whom also he made the worlds;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Who being the brightness of _his_ glory, and the express image of his person, and upholding all things by the word of his power, when he had by himself purged our sins, sat down on the right hand of the Majesty on high;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Being made so much better than the angels, as he hath by inheritance obtained a more excellent name than they.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For unto which of the angels said he at any time, Thou art my Son, this day have I begotten thee? And again, I will be to him a Father, and he shall be to me a Son?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And again, when he bringeth in the firstbegotten into the world, he saith, And let all the angels of God worship him.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And of the angels he saith, Who maketh his angels spirits, and his ministers a flame of fire.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But unto the Son _he saith_, Thy throne, O God, _is_ for ever and ever: a sceptre of righteousness _is_ the sceptre of thy kingdom.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Thou hast loved righteousness, and hated iniquity; therefore God, _even_ thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And, Thou, Lord, in the beginning hast laid the foundation of the earth; and the heavens are the works of thine hands:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>They shall perish; but thou remainest; and they all shall wax old as doth a garment;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And as a vesture shalt thou fold them up, and they shall be changed: but thou art the same, and thy years shall not fail.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But to which of the angels said he at any time, Sit on my right hand, until I make thine enemies thy footstool?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Are they not all ministering spirits, sent forth to minister for them who shall be heirs of salvation?  

## CHAPTER 2.

Therefore we ought to give the more earnest heed to the things which we have heard, lest at any time we should let _them_ slip.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For if the word spoken by angels was stedfast, and every transgression and disobedience received a just recompence of reward;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>How shall we escape, if we neglect so great salvation; which at the first began to be spoken by the Lord, and was confirmed unto us by them that heard _him_;   
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>God also bearing _them_ witness, both with signs and wonders, and with divers miracles, and gifts of the Holy Ghost, according to his own will?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ For unto the angels hath he not put in subjection the world to come, whereof we speak.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But one in a certain place testified, saying, What is man, that thou art mindful of him? or the son of man, that thou visitest him?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Thou madest him a little lower than the angels; thou crownedst him with glory and honour, and didst set him over the works of thy hands:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Thou hast put all things in subjection under his feet. For in that he put all in subjection under him, he left nothing _that is_ not put under him. But now we see not yet all things put under him.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But we see Jesus, who was made a little lower than the angels for the suffering of death, crowned with glory and honour; that he by the grace of God should taste death for every man.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For it became him, for whom _are_ all things, and by whom _are_ all things, in bringing many sons unto glory, to make the captain of their salvation perfect through sufferings.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For both he that sanctifieth and they who are sanctified _are_ all of one: for which cause he is not ashamed to call them brethren,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Saying, I will declare thy name unto my brethren, in the midst of the church will I sing praise unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And again, I will put my trust in him. And again, Behold I and the children which God hath given me.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Forasmuch then as the children are partakers of flesh and blood, he also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And deliver them who through fear of death were all their lifetime subject to bondage.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For verily he took not on _him the nature of_ angels; but he took on _him_ the seed of Abraham.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Wherefore in all things it behoved him to be made like unto _his_ brethren, that he might be a merciful and faithful high priest in things _pertaining_ to God, to make reconciliation for the sins of the people.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For in that he himself hath suffered being tempted, he is able to succour them that are tempted.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Wherefore, holy brethren, partakers of the heavenly calling, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our profession, Christ Jesus;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Who was faithful to him that appointed him, as also Moses _was faithful_ in all his house.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For this _man_ was counted worthy of more glory than Moses, inasmuch as he who hath builded the house hath more honour than the house.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For every house is builded by some _man_; but he that built all things _is_ God.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And Moses verily _was_ faithful in all his house, as a servant, for a testimony of those things which were to be spoken after;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But Christ as a son over his own house; whose house are we, if we hold fast the confidence and the rejoicing of the hope firm unto the end.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Wherefore (as the Holy Ghost saith, To day if ye will hear his voice,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Harden not your hearts, as in the provocation, in the day of temptation in the wilderness:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>When your fathers tempted me, proved me, and saw my works forty years.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Wherefore I was grieved with that generation, and said, They do alway err in _their_ heart; and they have not known my ways.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>So I sware in my wrath, They shall not enter into my rest.)  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Take heed, brethren, lest there be in any of you an evil heart of unbelief, in departing from the living God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But exhort one another daily, while it is called To day; lest any of you be hardened through the deceitfulness of sin.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For we are made partakers of Christ, if we hold the beginning of our confidence stedfast unto the end;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>While it is said, To day if ye will hear his voice, harden not your hearts, as in the provocation.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For some, when they had heard, did provoke: howbeit not all that came out of Egypt by Moses.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But with whom was he grieved forty years? _was it_ not with them that had sinned, whose carcases fell in the wilderness?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And to whom sware he that they should not enter into his rest, but to them that believed not?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>So we see that they could not enter in because of unbelief.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Let us therefore fear, lest, a promise being left _us_ of entering into his rest, any of you should seem to come short of it.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For unto us was the gospel preached, as well as unto them: but the word preached did not profit them, not being mixed with faith in them that heard _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For we which have believed do enter into rest, as he said, As I have sworn in my wrath, if they shall enter into my rest: although the works were finished from the foundation of the world.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For he spake in a certain place of the seventh _day_ on this wise, And God did rest the seventh day from all his works.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And in this _place_ again, If they shall enter into my rest.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Seeing therefore it remaineth that some must enter therein, and they to whom it was first preached entered not in because of unbelief:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Again, he limiteth a certain day, saying in David, To day, after so long a time; as it is said, To day if ye will hear his voice, harden not your hearts.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For if Jesus had given them rest, then would he not afterward have spoken of another day.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>There remaineth therefore a rest to the people of God.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God _did_ from his.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Let us labour therefore to enter into that rest, lest any man fall after the same example of unbelief.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the word of God _is_ quick, and powerful, and sharper than any twoedged sword, piercing even to the dividing asunder of soul and spirit, and of the joints and marrow, and _is_ a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Neither is there any creature that is not manifest in his sight: but all things _are_ naked and opened unto the eyes of him with whom we have to do.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Seeing then that we have a great high priest, that is passed into the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold fast _our_ profession.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For we have not an high priest which cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points tempted like as _we are, yet_ without sin.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need.  

## CHAPTER 5.

For every high priest taken from among men is ordained for men in things _pertaining_ to God, that he may offer both gifts and sacrifices for sins:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Who can have compassion on the ignorant, and on them that are out of the way; for that he himself also is compassed with infirmity.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And by reason hereof he ought, as for the people, so also for himself, to offer for sins.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And no man taketh this honour unto himself, but he that is called of God, as _was_ Aaron.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>So also Christ glorified not himself to be made an high priest; but he that said unto him, Thou art my Son, to day have I begotten thee.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>As he saith also in another _place_, Thou _art_ a priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Who in the days of his flesh, when he had offered up prayers and supplications with strong crying and tears unto him that was able to save him from death, and was heard in that he feared;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Though he were a Son, yet learned he obedience by the things which he suffered;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And being made perfect, he became the author of eternal salvation unto all them that obey him;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Called of God an high priest after the order of Melchisedec.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Of whom we have many things to say, and hard to be uttered, seeing ye are dull of hearing.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For when for the time ye ought to be teachers, ye have need that one teach you again which _be_ the first principles of the oracles of God; and are become such as have need of milk, and not of strong meat.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For every one that useth milk _is_ unskilful in the word of righteousness: for he is a babe.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But strong meat belongeth to them that are of full age, _even_ those who by reason of use have their senses exercised to discern both good and evil.  

## CHAPTER 6.

Therefore leaving the principles of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto perfection; not laying again the foundation of repentance from dead works, and of faith toward God,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Of the doctrine of baptisms, and of laying on of hands, and of resurrection of the dead, and of eternal judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And this will we do, if God permit.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For _it is_ impossible for those who were once enlightened, and have tasted of the heavenly gift, and were made partakers of the Holy Ghost,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And have tasted the good word of God, and the powers of the world to come,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>If they shall fall away, to renew them again unto repentance; seeing they crucify to themselves the Son of God afresh, and put _him_ to an open shame.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For the earth which drinketh in the rain that cometh oft upon it, and bringeth forth herbs meet for them by whom it is dressed, receiveth blessing from God:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But that which beareth thorns and briers _is_ rejected, and _is_ nigh unto cursing; whose end _is_ to be burned.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But, beloved, we are persuaded better things of you, and things that accompany salvation, though we thus speak.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For God _is_ not unrighteous to forget your work and labour of love, which ye have shewed toward his name, in that ye have ministered to the saints, and do minister.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And we desire that every one of you do shew the same diligence to the full assurance of hope unto the end:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>That ye be not slothful, but followers of them who through faith and patience inherit the promises.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For when God made promise to Abraham, because he could swear by no greater, he sware by himself,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Saying, Surely blessing I will bless thee, and multiplying I will multiply thee.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And so, after he had patiently endured, he obtained the promise.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For men verily swear by the greater: and an oath for confirmation _is_ to them an end of all strife.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Wherein God, willing more abundantly to shew unto the heirs of promise the immutability of his counsel, confirmed _it_ by an oath:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>That by two immutable things, in which _it was_ impossible for God to lie, we might have a strong consolation, who have fled for refuge to lay hold upon the hope set before us:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Which _hope_ we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and stedfast, and which entereth into that within the veil;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Whither the forerunner is for us entered, _even_ Jesus, made an high priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec.  

## CHAPTER 7.

For this Melchisedec, king of Salem, priest of the most high God, who met Abraham returning from the slaughter of the kings, and blessed him;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>To whom also Abraham gave a tenth part of all; first being by interpretation King of righteousness, and after that also King of Salem, which is, King of peace;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Without father, without mother, without descent, having neither beginning of days, nor end of life; but made like unto the Son of God; abideth a priest continually.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Now consider how great this man _was_, unto whom even the patriarch Abraham gave the tenth of the spoils.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And verily they that are of the sons of Levi, who receive the office of the priesthood, have a commandment to take tithes of the people according to the law, that is, of their brethren, though they come out of the loins of Abraham:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But he whose descent is not counted from them received tithes of Abraham, and blessed him that had the promises.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And without all contradiction the less is blessed of the better.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And here men that die receive tithes; but there he _receiveth them_, of whom it is witnessed that he liveth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And as I may so say, Levi also, who receiveth tithes, payed tithes in Abraham.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For he was yet in the loins of his father, when Melchisedec met him.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>If therefore perfection were by the Levitical priesthood, (for under it the people received the law,) what further need _was there_ that another priest should rise after the order of Melchisedec, and not be called after the order of Aaron?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the priesthood being changed, there is made of necessity a change also of the law.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For he of whom these things are spoken pertaineth to another tribe, of which no man gave attendance at the altar.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For _it is_ evident that our Lord sprang out of Juda; of which tribe Moses spake nothing concerning priesthood.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And it is yet far more evident: for that after the similitude of Melchisedec there ariseth another priest,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Who is made, not after the law of a carnal commandment, but after the power of an endless life.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For he testifieth, Thou _art_ a priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For there is verily a disannulling of the commandment going before for the weakness and unprofitableness thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For the law made nothing perfect, but the bringing in of a better hope _did_; by the which we draw nigh unto God.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And inasmuch as not without an oath _he was made priest_:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>(For those priests were made without an oath; but this with an oath by him that said unto him, The Lord sware and will not repent, Thou _art_ a priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec:)  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>By so much was Jesus made a surety of a better testament.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they truly were many priests, because they were not suffered to continue by reason of death:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>But this _man_, because he continueth ever, hath an unchangeable priesthood.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For such an high priest became us, _who is_ holy, harmless, undefiled, separate from sinners, and made higher than the heavens;  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Who needeth not daily, as those high priests, to offer up sacrifice, first for his own sins, and then for the people’s: for this he did once, when he offered up himself.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For the law maketh men high priests which have infirmity; but the word of the oath, which was since the law, _maketh_ the Son, who is consecrated for evermore.  

## CHAPTER 8.

Now of the things which we have spoken _this is_ the sum: We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>A minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For every high priest is ordained to offer gifts and sacrifices: wherefore _it is_ of necessity that this man have somewhat also to offer.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For if he were on earth, he should not be a priest, seeing that there are priests that offer gifts according to the law:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Who serve unto the example and shadow of heavenly things, as Moses was admonished of God when he was about to make the tabernacle: for, See, saith he, _that_ thou make all things according to the pattern shewed to thee in the mount.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But now hath he obtained a more excellent ministry, by how much also he is the mediator of a better covenant, which was established upon better promises.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For if that first _covenant_ had been faultless, then should no place have been sought for the second.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For finding fault with them, he saith, Behold, the days come, saith the Lord, when I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel and with the house of Judah:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Not according to the covenant that I made with their fathers in the day when I took them by the hand to lead them out of the land of Egypt; because they continued not in my covenant, and I regarded them not, saith the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For this _is_ the covenant that I will make with the house of Israel after those days, saith the Lord; I will put my laws into their mind, and write them in their hearts: and I will be to them a God, and they shall be to me a people:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And they shall not teach every man his neighbour, and every man his brother, saying, Know the Lord: for all shall know me, from the least to the greatest.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For I will be merciful to their unrighteousness, and their sins and their iniquities will I remember no more.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>In that he saith, A new _covenant_, he hath made the first old. Now that which decayeth and waxeth old _is_ ready to vanish away.  

## CHAPTER 9.

Then verily the first _covenant_ had also ordinances of divine service, and a worldly sanctuary.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For there was a tabernacle made; the first, wherein _was_ the candlestick, and the table, and the shewbread; which is called the sanctuary.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And after the second veil, the tabernacle which is called the Holiest of all;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Which had the golden censer, and the ark of the covenant overlaid round about with gold, wherein _was_ the golden pot that had manna, and Aaron’s rod that budded, and the tables of the covenant;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And over it the cherubims of glory shadowing the mercyseat; of which we cannot now speak particularly.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Now when these things were thus ordained, the priests went always into the first tabernacle, accomplishing the service _of God_.   
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But into the second _went_ the high priest alone once every year, not without blood, which he offered for himself, and _for_ the errors of the people:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The Holy Ghost this signifying, that the way into the holiest of all was not yet made manifest, while as the first tabernacle was yet standing:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Which _was_ a figure for the time then present, in which were offered both gifts and sacrifices, that could not make him that did the service perfect, as pertaining to the conscience;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>_Which stood_ only in meats and drinks, and divers washings, and carnal ordinances, imposed _on them_ until the time of reformation.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But Christ being come an high priest of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect tabernacle, not made with hands, that is to say, not of this building;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Neither by the blood of goats and calves, but by his own blood he entered in once into the holy place, having obtained eternal redemption _for us_.   
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For if the blood of bulls and of goats, and the ashes of an heifer sprinkling the unclean, sanctifieth to the purifying of the flesh:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>How much more shall the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without spot to God, purge your conscience from dead works to serve the living God?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And for this cause he is the mediator of the new testament, that by means of death, for the redemption of the transgressions _that were_ under the first testament, they which are called might receive the promise of eternal inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For where a testament _is_, there must also of necessity be the death of the testator.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For a testament _is_ of force after men are dead: otherwise it is of no strength at all while the testator liveth.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Whereupon neither the first _testament_ was dedicated without blood.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For when Moses had spoken every precept to all the people according to the law, he took the blood of calves and of goats, with water, and scarlet wool, and hyssop, and sprinkled both the book, and all the people,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Saying, This _is_ the blood of the testament which God hath enjoined unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Moreover he sprinkled with blood both the tabernacle, and all the vessels of the ministry.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and without shedding of blood is no remission.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>_It was_ therefore necessary that the patterns of things in the heavens should be purified with these; but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, _which are_ the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Nor yet that he should offer himself often, as the high priest entereth into the holy place every year with blood of others;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For then must he often have suffered since the foundation of the world: but now once in the end of the world hath he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And as it is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>So Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many; and unto them that look for him shall he appear the second time without sin unto salvation.  

## CHAPTER 10.

For the law having a shadow of good things to come, _and_ not the very image of the things, can never with those sacrifices which they offered year by year continually make the comers thereunto perfect.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For then would they not have ceased to be offered? because that the worshippers once purged should have had no more conscience of sins.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But in those _sacrifices there is_ a remembrance again _made_ of sins every year.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For _it is_ not possible that the blood of bulls and of goats should take away sins.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Wherefore when he cometh into the world, he saith, Sacrifice and offering thou wouldest not, but a body hast thou prepared me:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>In burnt offerings and _sacrifices_ for sin thou hast had no pleasure.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then said I, Lo, I come (in the volume of the book it is written of me,) to do thy will, O God.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Above when he said, Sacrifice and offering and burnt offerings and _offering_ for sin thou wouldest not, neither hadst pleasure _therein_; which are offered by the law;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then said he, Lo, I come to do thy will, O God. He taketh away the first, that he may establish the second.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>By the which will we are sanctified through the offering of the body of Jesus Christ once _for all_.   
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And every priest standeth daily ministering and offering oftentimes the same sacrifices, which can never take away sins:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But this man, after he had offered one sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down on the right hand of God;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>From henceforth expecting till his enemies be made his footstool.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>_Whereof_ the Holy Ghost also is a witness to us: for after that he had said before,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>This _is_ the covenant that I will make with them after those days, saith the Lord, I will put my laws into their hearts, and in their minds will I write them;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And their sins and iniquities will I remember no more.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Now where remission of these _is, there is_ no more offering for sin.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And _having_ an high priest over the house of God;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Let us draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith, having our hearts sprinkled from an evil conscience, and our bodies washed with pure water.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Let us hold fast the profession of _our_ faith without wavering; (for he _is_ faithful that promised;)  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And let us consider one another to provoke unto love and to good works:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together, as the manner of some _is_; but exhorting _one another_: and so much the more, as ye see the day approaching.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For if we sin wilfully after that we have received the knowledge of the truth, there remaineth no more sacrifice for sins,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>But a certain fearful looking for of judgment and fiery indignation, which shall devour the adversaries.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>He that despised Moses’ law died without mercy under two or three witnesses:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Of how much sorer punishment, suppose ye, shall he be thought worthy, who hath trodden under foot the Son of God, and hath counted the blood of the covenant, wherewith he was sanctified, an unholy thing, and hath done despite unto the Spirit of grace?  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>For we know him that hath said, Vengeance _belongeth_ unto me, I will recompense, saith the Lord. And again, The Lord shall judge his people.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>_It is_ a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>But call to remembrance the former days, in which, after ye were illuminated, ye endured a great fight of afflictions;  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Partly, whilst ye were made a gazingstock both by reproaches and afflictions; and partly, whilst ye became companions of them that were so used.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>For ye had compassion of me in my bonds, and took joyfully the spoiling of your goods, knowing in yourselves that ye have in heaven a better and an enduring substance.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Cast not away therefore your confidence, which hath great recompence of reward.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>For ye have need of patience, that, after ye have done the will of God, ye might receive the promise.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>For yet a little while, and he that shall come will come, and will not tarry.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>Now the just shall live by faith: but if _any man_ draw back, my soul shall have no pleasure in him.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>But we are not of them who draw back unto perdition; but of them that believe to the saving of the soul.  

## CHAPTER 11.

Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For by it the elders obtained a good report.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Through faith we understand that the worlds were framed by the word of God, so that things which are seen were not made of things which do appear.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>By faith Abel offered unto God a more excellent sacrifice than Cain, by which he obtained witness that he was righteous, God testifying of his gifts: and by it he being dead yet speaketh.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>By faith Enoch was translated that he should not see death; and was not found, because God had translated him: for before his translation he had this testimony, that he pleased God.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But without faith _it is_ impossible to please _him_: for he that cometh to God must believe that he is, and _that_ he is a rewarder of them that diligently seek him.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>By faith Noah, being warned of God of things not seen as yet, moved with fear, prepared an ark to the saving of his house; by the which he condemned the world, and became heir of the righteousness which is by faith.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>By faith Abraham, when he was called to go out into a place which he should after receive for an inheritance, obeyed; and he went out, not knowing whither he went.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>By faith he sojourned in the land of promise, as _in_ a strange country, dwelling in tabernacles with Isaac and Jacob, the heirs with him of the same promise:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For he looked for a city which hath foundations, whose builder and maker _is_ God.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Through faith also Sara herself received strength to conceive seed, and was delivered of a child when she was past age, because she judged him faithful who had promised.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Therefore sprang there even of one, and him as good as dead, _so many_ as the stars of the sky in multitude, and as the sand which is by the sea shore innumerable.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>These all died in faith, not having received the promises, but having seen them afar off, and were persuaded of _them_, and embraced _them_, and confessed that they were strangers and pilgrims on the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For they that say such things declare plainly that they seek a country.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And truly, if they had been mindful of that _country_ from whence they came out, they might have had opportunity to have returned.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But now they desire a better _country_, that is, an heavenly: wherefore God is not ashamed to be called their God: for he hath prepared for them a city.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>By faith Abraham, when he was tried, offered up Isaac: and he that had received the promises offered up his only begotten _son_,   
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Of whom it was said, That in Isaac shall thy seed be called:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Accounting that God _was_ able to raise _him_ up, even from the dead; from whence also he received him in a figure.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>By faith Isaac blessed Jacob and Esau concerning things to come.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>By faith Jacob, when he was a dying, blessed both the sons of Joseph; and worshipped, _leaning_ upon the top of his staff.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>By faith Joseph, when he died, made mention of the departing of the children of Israel; and gave commandment concerning his bones.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>By faith Moses, when he was born, was hid three months of his parents, because they saw _he was_ a proper child; and they were not afraid of the king’s commandment.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>By faith Moses, when he was come to years, refused to be called the son of Pharaoh’s daughter;  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Choosing rather to suffer affliction with the people of God, than to enjoy the pleasures of sin for a season;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Esteeming the reproach of Christ greater riches than the treasures in Egypt: for he had respect unto the recompence of the reward.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>By faith he forsook Egypt, not fearing the wrath of the king: for he endured, as seeing him who is invisible.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Through faith he kept the passover, and the sprinkling of blood, lest he that destroyed the firstborn should touch them.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>By faith they passed through the Red sea as by dry _land_: which the Egyptians assaying to do were drowned.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>By faith the walls of Jericho fell down, after they were compassed about seven days.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>By faith the harlot Rahab perished not with them that believed not, when she had received the spies with peace.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And what shall I more say? for the time would fail me to tell of Gedeon, and _of_ Barak, and _of_ Samson, and _of_ Jephthae; _of_ David also, and Samuel, and _of_ the prophets:  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Who through faith subdued kingdoms, wrought righteousness, obtained promises, stopped the mouths of lions,  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Quenched the violence of fire, escaped the edge of the sword, out of weakness were made strong, waxed valiant in fight, turned to flight the armies of the aliens.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Women received their dead raised to life again: and others were tortured, not accepting deliverance; that they might obtain a better resurrection:  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And others had trial of _cruel_ mockings and scourgings, yea, moreover of bonds and imprisonment:  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>They were stoned, they were sawn asunder, were tempted, were slain with the sword: they wandered about in sheepskins and goatskins; being destitute, afflicted, tormented;  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>(Of whom the world was not worthy:) they wandered in deserts, and _in_ mountains, and _in_ dens and caves of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And these all, having obtained a good report through faith, received not the promise:  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>God having provided some better thing for us, that they without us should not be made perfect.  

## CHAPTER 12.

Wherefore seeing we also are compassed about with so great a cloud of witnesses, let us lay aside every weight, and the sin which doth so easily beset _us_, and let us run with patience the race that is set before us,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Looking unto Jesus the author and finisher of _our_ faith; who for the joy that was set before him endured the cross, despising the shame, and is set down at the right hand of the throne of God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For consider him that endured such contradiction of sinners against himself, lest ye be wearied and faint in your minds.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Ye have not yet resisted unto blood, striving against sin.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And ye have forgotten the exhortation which speaketh unto you as unto children, My son, despise not thou the chastening of the Lord, nor faint when thou art rebuked of him:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For whom the Lord loveth he chasteneth, and scourgeth every son whom he receiveth.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>If ye endure chastening, God dealeth with you as with sons; for what son is he whom the father chasteneth not?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But if ye be without chastisement, whereof all are partakers, then are ye bastards, and not sons.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Furthermore we have had fathers of our flesh which corrected _us_, and we gave _them_ reverence: shall we not much rather be in subjection unto the Father of spirits, and live?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For they verily for a few days chastened _us_ after their own pleasure; but he for _our_ profit, that _we_ might be partakers of his holiness.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Now no chastening for the present seemeth to be joyous, but grievous: nevertheless afterward it yieldeth the peaceable fruit of righteousness unto them which are exercised thereby.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Wherefore lift up the hands which hang down, and the feeble knees;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And make straight paths for your feet, lest that which is lame be turned out of the way; but let it rather be healed.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Follow peace with all _men_, and holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Looking diligently lest any man fail of the grace of God; lest any root of bitterness springing up trouble _you_, and thereby many be defiled;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Lest there _be_ any fornicator, or profane person, as Esau, who for one morsel of meat sold his birthright.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For ye know how that afterward, when he would have inherited the blessing, he was rejected: for he found no place of repentance, though he sought it carefully with tears.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For ye are not come unto the mount that might be touched, and that burned with fire, nor unto blackness, and darkness, and tempest,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the sound of a trumpet, and the voice of words; which _voice_ they that heard intreated that the word should not be spoken to them any more:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>(For they could not endure that which was commanded, And if so much as a beast touch the mountain, it shall be stoned, or thrust through with a dart:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And so terrible was the sight, _that_ Moses said, I exceedingly fear and quake:)  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But ye are come unto mount Sion, and unto the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem, and to an innumerable company of angels,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>To the general assembly and church of the firstborn, which are written in heaven, and to God the Judge of all, and to the spirits of just men made perfect,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And to Jesus the mediator of the new covenant, and to the blood of sprinkling, that speaketh better things than _that of_ Abel.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>See that ye refuse not him that speaketh. For if they escaped not who refused him that spake on earth, much more _shall not_ we _escape_, if we turn away from him that _speaketh_ from heaven:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Whose voice then shook the earth: but now he hath promised, saying, Yet once more I shake not the earth only, but also heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And this _word_, Yet once more, signifieth the removing of those things that are shaken, as of things that are made, that those things which cannot be shaken may remain.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Wherefore we receiving a kingdom which cannot be moved, let us have grace, whereby we may serve God acceptably with reverence and godly fear:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For our God _is_ a consuming fire.  

## CHAPTER 13.

Let brotherly love continue.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Be not forgetful to entertain strangers: for thereby some have entertained angels unawares.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Remember them that are in bonds, as bound with them; _and_ them which suffer adversity, as being yourselves also in the body.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Marriage _is_ honourable in all, and the bed undefiled: but whoremongers and adulterers God will judge.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>_Let your_ conversation _be_ without covetousness; _and be_ content with such things as ye have: for he hath said, I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>So that we may boldly say, The Lord _is_ my helper, and I will not fear what man shall do unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Remember them which have the rule over you, who have spoken unto you the word of God: whose faith follow, considering the end of _their_ conversation.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Be not carried about with divers and strange doctrines. For _it is_ a good thing that the heart be established with grace; not with meats, which have not profited them that have been occupied therein.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>We have an altar, whereof they have no right to eat which serve the tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For the bodies of those beasts, whose blood is brought into the sanctuary by the high priest for sin, are burned without the camp.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Wherefore Jesus also, that he might sanctify the people with his own blood, suffered without the gate.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Let us go forth therefore unto him without the camp, bearing his reproach.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For here have we no continuing city, but we seek one to come.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>By him therefore let us offer the sacrifice of praise to God continually, that is, the fruit of _our_ lips giving thanks to his name.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But to do good and to communicate forget not: for with such sacrifices God is well pleased.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Obey them that have the rule over you, and submit yourselves: for they watch for your souls, as they that must give account, that they may do it with joy, and not with grief: for that _is_ unprofitable for you.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Pray for us: for we trust we have a good conscience, in all things willing to live honestly.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But I beseech _you_ the rather to do this, that I may be restored to you the sooner.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Now the God of peace, that brought again from the dead our Lord Jesus, that great shepherd of the sheep, through the blood of the everlasting covenant,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Make you perfect in every good work to do his will, working in you that which is wellpleasing in his sight, through Jesus Christ; to whom _be_ glory for ever and ever. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And I beseech you, brethren, suffer the word of exhortation: for I have written a letter unto you in few words.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Know ye that _our_ brother Timothy is set at liberty; with whom, if he come shortly, I will see you.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Salute all them that have the rule over you, and all the saints. They of Italy salute you.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Grace _be_ with you all. Amen.
