# Jonah

### JONAH

## CHAPTER 1.

Now the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto Jonah the son of Amittai, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Arise, go to Nineveh, that great city, and cry against it; for their wickedness is come up before me.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But Jonah rose up to flee unto Tarshish from the presence of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and went down to Joppa; and he found a ship going to Tarshish: so he paid the fare thereof, and went down into it, to go with them unto Tarshish from the presence of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ But the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> sent out a great wind into the sea, and there was a mighty tempest in the sea, so that the ship was like to be broken.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Then the mariners were afraid, and cried every man unto his god, and cast forth the wares that _were_ in the ship into the sea, to lighten _it_ of them. But Jonah was gone down into the sides of the ship; and he lay, and was fast asleep.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>So the shipmaster came to him, and said unto him, What meanest thou, O sleeper? arise, call upon thy God, if so be that God will think upon us, that we perish not.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they said every one to his fellow, Come, and let us cast lots, that we may know for whose cause this evil _is_ upon us. So they cast lots, and the lot fell upon Jonah.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Then said they unto him, Tell us, we pray thee, for whose cause this evil _is_ upon us; What _is_ thine occupation? and whence comest thou? what _is_ thy country? and of what people _art_ thou?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, I _am_ an Hebrew; and I fear the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the God of heaven, which hath made the sea and the dry _land_.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Then were the men exceedingly afraid, and said unto him, Why hast thou done this? For the men knew that he fled from the presence of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, because he had told them.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Then said they unto him, What shall we do unto thee, that the sea may be calm unto us? for the sea wrought, and was tempestuous.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Take me up, and cast me forth into the sea; so shall the sea be calm unto you: for I know that for my sake this great tempest _is_ upon you.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Nevertheless the men rowed hard to bring _it_ to the land; but they could not: for the sea wrought, and was tempestuous against them.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Wherefore they cried unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, We beseech thee, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, we beseech thee, let us not perish for this man’s life, and lay not upon us innocent blood: for thou, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, hast done as it pleased thee.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So they took up Jonah, and cast him forth into the sea: and the sea ceased from her raging.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Then the men feared the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> exceedingly, and offered a sacrifice unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and made vows.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Now the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had prepared a great fish to swallow up Jonah. And Jonah was in the belly of the fish three days and three nights.  

## CHAPTER 2.

Then Jonah prayed unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> his God out of the fish’s belly,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And said, I cried by reason of mine affliction unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and he heard me; out of the belly of hell cried I, _and_ thou heardest my voice.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For thou hadst cast me into the deep, in the midst of the seas; and the floods compassed me about: all thy billows and thy waves passed over me.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then I said, I am cast out of thy sight; yet I will look again toward thy holy temple.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The waters compassed me about, _even_ to the soul: the depth closed me round about, the weeds were wrapped about my head.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>I went down to the bottoms of the mountains; the earth with her bars _was_ about me for ever: yet hast thou brought up my life from corruption, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> my God.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>When my soul fainted within me I remembered the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and my prayer came in unto thee, into thine holy temple.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>They that observe lying vanities forsake their own mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But I will sacrifice unto thee with the voice of thanksgiving; I will pay _that_ that I have vowed. Salvation _is_ of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake unto the fish, and it vomited out Jonah upon the dry _land_.  

## CHAPTER 3.

And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto Jonah the second time, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Arise, go unto Nineveh, that great city, and preach unto it the preaching that I bid thee.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>So Jonah arose, and went unto Nineveh, according to the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>. Now Nineveh was an exceeding great city of three days’ journey.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Jonah began to enter into the city a day’s journey, and he cried, and said, Yet forty days, and Nineveh shall be overthrown.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ So the people of Nineveh believed God, and proclaimed a fast, and put on sackcloth, from the greatest of them even to the least of them.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For word came unto the king of Nineveh, and he arose from his throne, and he laid his robe from him, and covered _him_ with sackcloth, and sat in ashes.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he caused _it_ to be proclaimed and published through Nineveh by the decree of the king and his nobles, saying, Let neither man nor beast, herd nor flock, taste any thing: let them not feed, nor drink water:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But let man and beast be covered with sackcloth, and cry mightily unto God: yea, let them turn every one from his evil way, and from the violence that _is_ in their hands.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Who can tell _if_ God will turn and repent, and turn away from his fierce anger, that we perish not?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And God saw their works, that they turned from their evil way; and God repented of the evil, that he had said that he would do unto them; and he did _it_ not.  

## CHAPTER 4.

But it displeased Jonah exceedingly, and he was very angry.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he prayed unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and said, I pray thee, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _was_ not this my saying, when I was yet in my country? Therefore I fled before unto Tarshish: for I knew that thou _art_ a gracious God, and merciful, slow to anger, and of great kindness, and repentest thee of the evil.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore now, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, take, I beseech thee, my life from me; for _it is_ better for me to die than to live.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Then said the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Doest thou well to be angry?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ So Jonah went out of the city, and sat on the east side of the city, and there made him a booth, and sat under it in the shadow, till he might see what would become of the city.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God prepared a gourd, and made _it_ to come up over Jonah, that it might be a shadow over his head, to deliver him from his grief. So Jonah was exceeding glad of the gourd.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But God prepared a worm when the morning rose the next day, and it smote the gourd that it withered.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when the sun did arise, that God prepared a vehement east wind; and the sun beat upon the head of Jonah, that he fainted, and wished in himself to die, and said, _It is_ better for me to die than to live.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And God said to Jonah, Doest thou well to be angry for the gourd? And he said, I do well to be angry, _even_ unto death.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Then said the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Thou hast had pity on the gourd, for the which thou hast not laboured, neither madest it grow; which came up in a night, and perished in a night:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than sixscore thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and _also_ much cattle?
