# Habakkuk

### HABAKKUK

## CHAPTER 1.

The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! _even_ cry out unto thee _of_ violence, and thou wilt not save!  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Why dost thou shew me iniquity, and cause _me_ to behold grievance? for spoiling and violence _are_ before me: and there are _that_ raise up strife and contention.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Therefore the law is slacked, and judgment doth never go forth: for the wicked doth compass about the righteous; therefore wrong judgment proceedeth.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Behold ye among the heathen, and regard, and wonder marvellously: for _I_ will work a work in your days, _which_ ye will not believe, though it be told _you_.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For, lo, I raise up the Chaldeans, _that_ bitter and hasty nation, which shall march through the breadth of the land, to possess the dwellingplaces _that are_ not theirs.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>They _are_ terrible and dreadful: their judgment and their dignity shall proceed of themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Their horses also are swifter than the leopards, and are more fierce than the evening wolves: and their horsemen shall spread themselves, and their horsemen shall come from far; they shall fly as the eagle _that_ hasteth to eat.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>They shall come all for violence: their faces shall sup up _as_ the east wind, and they shall gather the captivity as the sand.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they shall scoff at the kings, and the princes shall be a scorn unto them: they shall deride every strong hold; for they shall heap dust, and take it.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then shall _his_ mind change, and he shall pass over, and offend, _imputing_ this his power unto his god.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ _Art_ thou not from everlasting, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> my God, mine Holy One? we shall not die. O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou hast ordained them for judgment; and, O mighty God, thou hast established them for correction.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>_Thou art_ of purer eyes than to behold evil, and canst not look on iniquity: wherefore lookest thou upon them that deal treacherously, _and_ holdest thy tongue when the wicked devoureth _the man that is_ more righteous than he?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And makest men as the fishes of the sea, as the creeping things, _that have_ no ruler over them?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>They take up all of them with the angle, they catch them in their net, and gather them in their drag: therefore they rejoice and are glad.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Therefore they sacrifice unto their net, and burn incense unto their drag; because by them their portion _is_ fat, and their meat plenteous.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Shall they therefore empty their net, and not spare continually to slay the nations?  

## CHAPTER 2.

I will stand upon my watch, and set me upon the tower, and will watch to see what he will say unto me, and what I shall answer when I am reproved.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> answered me, and said, Write the vision, and make _it_ plain upon tables, that he may run that readeth it.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For the vision _is_ yet for an appointed time, but at the end it shall speak, and not lie: though it tarry, wait for it; because it will surely come, it will not tarry.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Behold, his soul _which_ is lifted up is not upright in him: but the just shall live by his faith.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Yea also, because he transgresseth by wine, _he is_ a proud man, neither keepeth at home, who enlargeth his desire as hell, and _is_ as death, and cannot be satisfied, but gathereth unto him all nations, and heapeth unto him all people:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Shall not all these take up a parable against him, and a taunting proverb against him, and say, Woe to him that increaseth _that which is_ not his! how long? and to him that ladeth himself with thick clay!  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Shall they not rise up suddenly that shall bite thee, and awake that shall vex thee, and thou shalt be for booties unto them?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Because thou hast spoiled many nations, all the remnant of the people shall spoil thee; because of men’s blood, and _for_ the violence of the land, of the city, and of all that dwell therein.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Woe to him that coveteth an evil covetousness to his house, that he may set his nest on high, that he may be delivered from the power of evil!  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thou hast consulted shame to thy house by cutting off many people, and hast sinned _against_ thy soul.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For the stone shall cry out of the wall, and the beam out of the timber shall answer it.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Woe to him that buildeth a town with blood, and stablisheth a city by iniquity!  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Behold, _is it_ not of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts that the people shall labour in the very fire, and the people shall weary themselves for very vanity?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as the waters cover the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Woe unto him that giveth his neighbour drink, that puttest thy bottle to _him_, and makest _him_ drunken also, that thou mayest look on their nakedness!  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Thou art filled with shame for glory: drink thou also, and let thy foreskin be uncovered: the cup of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> right hand shall be turned unto thee, and shameful spewing _shall be_ on thy glory.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For the violence of Lebanon shall cover thee, and the spoil of beasts, _which_ made them afraid, because of men’s blood, and for the violence of the land, of the city, and of all that dwell therein.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ What profiteth the graven image that the maker thereof hath graven it; the molten image, and a teacher of lies, that the maker of his work trusteth therein, to make dumb idols?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Woe unto him that saith to the wood, Awake; to the dumb stone, Arise, it shall teach! Behold, it _is_ laid over with gold and silver, and _there is_ no breath at all in the midst of it.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ in his holy temple: let all the earth keep silence before him.  

## CHAPTER 3.

A prayer of Habakkuk the prophet upon Shigionoth.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I have heard thy speech, _and_ was afraid: O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, revive thy work in the midst of the years, in the midst of the years make known; in wrath remember mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ God came from Teman, and the Holy One from mount Paran. Selah. His glory covered the heavens, and the earth was full of his praise.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And _his_ brightness was as the light; he had horns _coming_ out of his hand: and there _was_ the hiding of his power.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Before him went the pestilence, and burning coals went forth at his feet.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>He stood, and measured the earth: he beheld, and drove asunder the nations; and the everlasting mountains were scattered, the perpetual hills did bow: his ways _are_ everlasting.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>I saw the tents of Cushan in affliction: _and_ the curtains of the land of Midian did tremble.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Was the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> displeased against the rivers? _was_ thine anger against the rivers? _was_ thy wrath against the sea, that thou didst ride upon thine horses _and_ thy chariots of salvation?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Thy bow was made quite naked, _according_ to the oaths of the tribes, _even thy_ word. Selah. Thou didst cleave the earth with rivers.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The mountains saw thee, _and_ they trembled: the overflowing of the water passed by: the deep uttered his voice, _and_ lifted up his hands on high.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The sun _and_ moon stood still in their habitation: at the light of thine arrows they went, _and_ at the shining of thy glittering spear.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Thou didst march through the land in indignation, thou didst thresh the heathen in anger.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thou wentest forth for the salvation of thy people, _even_ for salvation with thine anointed; thou woundedst the head out of the house of the wicked, by discovering the foundation unto the neck. Selah.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thou didst strike through with his staves the head of his villages: they came out as a whirlwind to scatter me: their rejoicing _was_ as to devour the poor secretly.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thou didst walk through the sea with thine horses, _through_ the heap of great waters.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ When I heard, my belly trembled; my lips quivered at the voice: rottenness entered into my bones, and I trembled in myself, that I might rest in the day of trouble: when he cometh up unto the people, he will invade them with his troops.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Although the fig tree shall not blossom, neither _shall_ fruit _be_ in the vines; the labour of the olive shall fail, and the fields shall yield no meat; the flock shall be cut off from the fold, and _there shall be_ no herd in the stalls:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Yet I will rejoice in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I will joy in the God of my salvation.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God _is_ my strength, and he will make my feet like hinds’ _feet_, and he will make me to walk upon mine high places. To the chief singer on my stringed instruments.
