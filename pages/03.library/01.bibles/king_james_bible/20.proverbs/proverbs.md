# Proverbs

### THE PROVERBS

## CHAPTER 1.

The proverbs of Solomon the son of David, king of Israel;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>To know wisdom and instruction; to perceive the words of understanding;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>To receive the instruction of wisdom, justice, and judgment, and equity;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>To give subtilty to the simple, to the young man knowledge and discretion.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>A wise _man_ will hear, and will increase learning; and a man of understanding shall attain unto wise counsels:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>To understand a proverb, and the interpretation; the words of the wise, and their dark sayings.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ The fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ the beginning of knowledge: _but_ fools despise wisdom and instruction.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>My son, hear the instruction of thy father, and forsake not the law of thy mother:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For they _shall be_ an ornament of grace unto thy head, and chains about thy neck.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ My son, if sinners entice thee, consent thou not.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>If they say, Come with us, let us lay wait for blood, let us lurk privily for the innocent without cause:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Let us swallow them up alive as the grave; and whole, as those that go down into the pit:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>We shall find all precious substance, we shall fill our houses with spoil:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Cast in thy lot among us; let us all have one purse:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>My son, walk not thou in the way with them; refrain thy foot from their path:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For their feet run to evil, and make haste to shed blood.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Surely in vain the net is spread in the sight of any bird.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they lay wait for their _own_ blood; they lurk privily for their _own_ lives.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>So _are_ the ways of every one that is greedy of gain; _which_ taketh away the life of the owners thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Wisdom crieth without; she uttereth her voice in the streets:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>She crieth in the chief place of concourse, in the openings of the gates: in the city she uttereth her words, _saying_,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>How long, ye simple ones, will ye love simplicity? and the scorners delight in their scorning, and fools hate knowledge?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Turn you at my reproof: behold, I will pour out my spirit unto you, I will make known my words unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Because I have called, and ye refused; I have stretched out my hand, and no man regarded;  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But ye have set at nought all my counsel, and would none of my reproof:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>I also will laugh at your calamity; I will mock when your fear cometh;  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>When your fear cometh as desolation, and your destruction cometh as a whirlwind; when distress and anguish cometh upon you.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Then shall they call upon me, but I will not answer; they shall seek me early, but they shall not find me:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For that they hated knowledge, and did not choose the fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>They would none of my counsel: they despised all my reproof.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Therefore shall they eat of the fruit of their own way, and be filled with their own devices.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For the turning away of the simple shall slay them, and the prosperity of fools shall destroy them.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>But whoso hearkeneth unto me shall dwell safely, and shall be quiet from fear of evil.  

## CHAPTER 2.

My son, if thou wilt receive my words, and hide my commandments with thee;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>So that thou incline thine ear unto wisdom, _and_ apply thine heart to understanding;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Yea, if thou criest after knowledge, _and_ liftest up thy voice for understanding;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>If thou seekest her as silver, and searchest for her as _for_ hid treasures;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou understand the fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and find the knowledge of God.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> giveth wisdom: out of his mouth _cometh_ knowledge and understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>He layeth up sound wisdom for the righteous: _he is_ a buckler to them that walk uprightly.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He keepeth the paths of judgment, and preserveth the way of his saints.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou understand righteousness, and judgment, and equity; _yea_, every good path.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ When wisdom entereth into thine heart, and knowledge is pleasant unto thy soul;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Discretion shall preserve thee, understanding shall keep thee:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>To deliver thee from the way of the evil _man_, from the man that speaketh froward things;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Who leave the paths of uprightness, to walk in the ways of darkness;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Who rejoice to do evil, _and_ delight in the frowardness of the wicked;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Whose ways _are_ crooked, and _they_ froward in their paths:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>To deliver thee from the strange woman, _even_ from the stranger _which_ flattereth with her words;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Which forsaketh the guide of her youth, and forgetteth the covenant of her God.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For her house inclineth unto death, and her paths unto the dead.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>None that go unto her return again, neither take they hold of the paths of life.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>That thou mayest walk in the way of good _men_, and keep the paths of the righteous.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For the upright shall dwell in the land, and the perfect shall remain in it.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But the wicked shall be cut off from the earth, and the transgressors shall be rooted out of it.  

## CHAPTER 3.

My son, forget not my law; but let thine heart keep my commandments:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For length of days, and long life, and peace, shall they add to thee.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Let not mercy and truth forsake thee: bind them about thy neck; write them upon the table of thine heart:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>So shalt thou find favour and good understanding in the sight of God and man.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Trust in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>In all thy ways acknowledge him, and he shall direct thy paths.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Be not wise in thine own eyes: fear the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and depart from evil.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>It shall be health to thy navel, and marrow to thy bones.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Honour the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> with thy substance, and with the firstfruits of all thine increase:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>So shall thy barns be filled with plenty, and thy presses shall burst out with new wine.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ My son, despise not the chastening of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; neither be weary of his correction:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For whom the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> loveth he correcteth; even as a father the son _in whom_ he delighteth.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Happy _is_ the man _that_ findeth wisdom, and the man _that_ getteth understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For the merchandise of it _is_ better than the merchandise of silver, and the gain thereof than fine gold.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>She _is_ more precious than rubies: and all the things thou canst desire are not to be compared unto her.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Length of days _is_ in her right hand; _and_ in her left hand riches and honour.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Her ways _are_ ways of pleasantness, and all her paths _are_ peace.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>She _is_ a tree of life to them that lay hold upon her: and happy _is every one_ that retaineth her.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> by wisdom hath founded the earth; by understanding hath he established the heavens.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>By his knowledge the depths are broken up, and the clouds drop down the dew.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ My son, let not them depart from thine eyes: keep sound wisdom and discretion:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>So shall they be life unto thy soul, and grace to thy neck.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou walk in thy way safely, and thy foot shall not stumble.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>When thou liest down, thou shalt not be afraid: yea, thou shalt lie down, and thy sleep shall be sweet.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Be not afraid of sudden fear, neither of the desolation of the wicked, when it cometh.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be thy confidence, and shall keep thy foot from being taken.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ Withhold not good from them to whom it is due, when it is in the power of thine hand to do _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Say not unto thy neighbour, Go, and come again, and to morrow I will give; when thou hast it by thee.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Devise not evil against thy neighbour, seeing he dwelleth securely by thee.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ Strive not with a man without cause, if he have done thee no harm.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ Envy thou not the oppressor, and choose none of his ways.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For the froward _is_ abomination to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: but his secret _is_ with the righteous.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ The curse of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ in the house of the wicked: but he blesseth the habitation of the just.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Surely he scorneth the scorners: but he giveth grace unto the lowly.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>The wise shall inherit glory: but shame shall be the promotion of fools.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Hear, ye children, the instruction of a father, and attend to know understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For I give you good doctrine, forsake ye not my law.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For I was my father’s son, tender and only _beloved_ in the sight of my mother.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>He taught me also, and said unto me, Let thine heart retain my words: keep my commandments, and live.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Get wisdom, get understanding: forget _it_ not; neither decline from the words of my mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Forsake her not, and she shall preserve thee: love her, and she shall keep thee.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Wisdom _is_ the principal thing; _therefore_ get wisdom: and with all thy getting get understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Exalt her, and she shall promote thee: she shall bring thee to honour, when thou dost embrace her.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>She shall give to thine head an ornament of grace: a crown of glory shall she deliver to thee.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Hear, O my son, and receive my sayings; and the years of thy life shall be many.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I have taught thee in the way of wisdom; I have led thee in right paths.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>When thou goest, thy steps shall not be straitened; and when thou runnest, thou shalt not stumble.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Take fast hold of instruction; let _her_ not go: keep her; for she _is_ thy life.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Enter not into the path of the wicked, and go not in the way of evil _men_.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Avoid it, pass not by it, turn from it, and pass away.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For they sleep not, except they have done mischief; and their sleep is taken away, unless they cause _some_ to fall.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For they eat the bread of wickedness, and drink the wine of violence.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But the path of the just _is_ as the shining light, that shineth more and more unto the perfect day.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The way of the wicked _is_ as darkness: they know not at what they stumble.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ My son, attend to my words; incline thine ear unto my sayings.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Let them not depart from thine eyes; keep them in the midst of thine heart.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For they _are_ life unto those that find them, and health to all their flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Keep thy heart with all diligence; for out of it _are_ the issues of life.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Put away from thee a froward mouth, and perverse lips put far from thee.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Let thine eyes look right on, and let thine eyelids look straight before thee.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Ponder the path of thy feet, and let all thy ways be established.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Turn not to the right hand nor to the left: remove thy foot from evil.  

## CHAPTER 5.

My son, attend unto my wisdom, _and_ bow thine ear to my understanding:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That thou mayest regard discretion, and _that_ thy lips may keep knowledge.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ For the lips of a strange woman drop _as_ an honeycomb, and her mouth _is_ smoother than oil:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But her end is bitter as wormwood, sharp as a twoedged sword.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Her feet go down to death; her steps take hold on hell.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Lest thou shouldest ponder the path of life, her ways are moveable, _that_ thou canst not know _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Hear me now therefore, O ye children, and depart not from the words of my mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Remove thy way far from her, and come not nigh the door of her house:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Lest thou give thine honour unto others, and thy years unto the cruel:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Lest strangers be filled with thy wealth; and thy labours _be_ in the house of a stranger;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And thou mourn at the last, when thy flesh and thy body are consumed,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And say, How have I hated instruction, and my heart despised reproof;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And have not obeyed the voice of my teachers, nor inclined mine ear to them that instructed me!  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I was almost in all evil in the midst of the congregation and assembly.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Drink waters out of thine own cistern, and running waters out of thine own well.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Let thy fountains be dispersed abroad, _and_ rivers of waters in the streets.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Let them be only thine own, and not strangers’ with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Let thy fountain be blessed: and rejoice with the wife of thy youth.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>_Let her be as_ the loving hind and pleasant roe; let her breasts satisfy thee at all times; and be thou ravished always with her love.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And why wilt thou, my son, be ravished with a strange woman, and embrace the bosom of a stranger?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For the ways of man _are_ before the eyes of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and he pondereth all his goings.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ His own iniquities shall take the wicked himself, and he shall be holden with the cords of his sins.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>He shall die without instruction; and in the greatness of his folly he shall go astray.  

## CHAPTER 6.

My son, if thou be surety for thy friend, _if_ thou hast stricken thy hand with a stranger,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Thou art snared with the words of thy mouth, thou art taken with the words of thy mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Do this now, my son, and deliver thyself, when thou art come into the hand of thy friend; go, humble thyself, and make sure thy friend.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Give not sleep to thine eyes, nor slumber to thine eyelids.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Deliver thyself as a roe from the hand _of the hunter_, and as a bird from the hand of the fowler.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Go to the ant, thou sluggard; consider her ways, and be wise:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Which having no guide, overseer, or ruler,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Provideth her meat in the summer, _and_ gathereth her food in the harvest.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>How long wilt thou sleep, O sluggard? when wilt thou arise out of thy sleep?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>_Yet_ a little sleep, a little slumber, a little folding of the hands to sleep:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>So shall thy poverty come as one that travelleth, and thy want as an armed man.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ A naughty person, a wicked man, walketh with a froward mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>He winketh with his eyes, he speaketh with his feet, he teacheth with his fingers;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Frowardness _is_ in his heart, he deviseth mischief continually; he soweth discord.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Therefore shall his calamity come suddenly; suddenly shall he be broken without remedy.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ These six _things_ doth the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hate: yea, seven _are_ an abomination unto him:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>A proud look, a lying tongue, and hands that shed innocent blood,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>An heart that deviseth wicked imaginations, feet that be swift in running to mischief,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>A false witness _that_ speaketh lies, and he that soweth discord among brethren.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ My son, keep thy father’s commandment, and forsake not the law of thy mother:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Bind them continually upon thine heart, _and_ tie them about thy neck.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>When thou goest, it shall lead thee; when thou sleepest, it shall keep thee; and _when_ thou awakest, it shall talk with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For the commandment _is_ a lamp; and the law _is_ light; and reproofs of instruction _are_ the way of life:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>To keep thee from the evil woman, from the flattery of the tongue of a strange woman.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Lust not after her beauty in thine heart; neither let her take thee with her eyelids.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For by means of a whorish woman _a man is brought_ to a piece of bread: and the adulteress will hunt for the precious life.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Can a man take fire in his bosom, and his clothes not be burned?  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Can one go upon hot coals, and his feet not be burned?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>So he that goeth in to his neighbour’s wife; whosoever toucheth her shall not be innocent.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>_Men_ do not despise a thief, if he steal to satisfy his soul when he is hungry;  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>But _if_ he be found, he shall restore sevenfold; he shall give all the substance of his house.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>_But_ whoso committeth adultery with a woman lacketh understanding: he _that_ doeth it destroyeth his own soul.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>A wound and dishonour shall he get; and his reproach shall not be wiped away.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>For jealousy _is_ the rage of a man: therefore he will not spare in the day of vengeance.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>He will not regard any ransom; neither will he rest content, though thou givest many gifts.  

## CHAPTER 7.

My son, keep my words, and lay up my commandments with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Keep my commandments, and live; and my law as the apple of thine eye.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Bind them upon thy fingers, write them upon the table of thine heart.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Say unto wisdom, Thou _art_ my sister; and call understanding _thy_ kinswoman:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>That they may keep thee from the strange woman, from the stranger _which_ flattereth with her words.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ For at the window of my house I looked through my casement,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And beheld among the simple ones, I discerned among the youths, a young man void of understanding,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Passing through the street near her corner; and he went the way to her house,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>In the twilight, in the evening, in the black and dark night:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And, behold, there met him a woman _with_ the attire of an harlot, and subtil of heart.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>(She _is_ loud and stubborn; her feet abide not in her house:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Now _is she_ without, now in the streets, and lieth in wait at every corner.)  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>So she caught him, and kissed him, _and_ with an impudent face said unto him,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>_I have_ peace offerings with me; this day have I payed my vows.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Therefore came I forth to meet thee, diligently to seek thy face, and I have found thee.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>I have decked my bed with coverings of tapestry, with carved _works_, with fine linen of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>I have perfumed my bed with myrrh, aloes, and cinnamon.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Come, let us take our fill of love until the morning: let us solace ourselves with loves.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For the goodman _is_ not at home, he is gone a long journey:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>He hath taken a bag of money with him, _and_ will come home at the day appointed.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>With her much fair speech she caused him to yield, with the flattering of her lips she forced him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>He goeth after her straightway, as an ox goeth to the slaughter, or as a fool to the correction of the stocks;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Till a dart strike through his liver; as a bird hasteth to the snare, and knoweth not that it _is_ for his life.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Hearken unto me now therefore, O ye children, and attend to the words of my mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Let not thine heart decline to her ways, go not astray in her paths.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For she hath cast down many wounded: yea, many strong _men_ have been slain by her.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Her house _is_ the way to hell, going down to the chambers of death.  

## CHAPTER 8.

Doth not wisdom cry? and understanding put forth her voice?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>She standeth in the top of high places, by the way in the places of the paths.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>She crieth at the gates, at the entry of the city, at the coming in at the doors.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Unto you, O men, I call; and my voice _is_ to the sons of man.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>O ye simple, understand wisdom: and, ye fools, be ye of an understanding heart.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Hear; for I will speak of excellent things; and the opening of my lips _shall be_ right things.

<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Whoso stoppeth his ears at the cry of the poor, he also shall cry himself, but shall not be heard.

<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Whoso keepeth his mouth and his tongue keepeth his soul from troubles.

<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>He coveteth greedily all the day long: but the righteous giveth and spareth not.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>The sacrifice of the wicked _is_ abomination: how much more, _when_ he bringeth it with a wicked mind?

<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ _There is_ no wisdom nor understanding nor counsel against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.

## CHAPTER 22.

<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The rich and poor meet together: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ the maker of them all.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>A prudent _man_ foreseeth the evil, and hideth himself: but the simple pass on, and are punished.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>By humility _and_ the fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _are_ riches, and honour, and life.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Thorns _and_ snares _are_ in the way of the froward: he that doth keep his soul shall be far from them.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Train up a child in the way he should go: and when he is old, he will not depart from it.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The rich ruleth over the poor, and the borrower _is_ servant to the lender.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He that soweth iniquity shall reap vanity: and the rod of his anger shall fail.

<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Cast out the scorner, and contention shall go out; yea, strife and reproach shall cease.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He that loveth pureness of heart, _for_ the grace of his lips the king _shall be_ his friend.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The eyes of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> preserve knowledge, and he overthroweth the words of the transgressor.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The slothful _man_ saith, _There is_ a lion without, I shall be slain in the streets.

<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Foolishness _is_ bound in the heart of a child; _but_ the rod of correction shall drive it far from him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>He that oppresseth the poor to increase his _riches, and_ he that giveth to the rich, _shall_ surely _come_ to want.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Bow down thine ear, and hear the words of the wise, and apply thine heart unto my knowledge.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For _it is_ a pleasant thing if thou keep them within thee; they shall withal be fitted in thy lips.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>That thy trust may be in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I have made known to thee this day, even to thee.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Have not I written to thee excellent things in counsels and knowledge,

<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Rob not the poor, because he _is_ poor: neither oppress the afflicted in the gate:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will plead their cause, and spoil the soul of those that spoiled them.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Make no friendship with an angry man; and with a furious man thou shalt not go:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Lest thou learn his ways, and get a snare to thy soul.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ Be not thou _one_ of them that strike hands, _or_ of them that are sureties for debts.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>If thou hast nothing to pay, why should he take away thy bed from under thee?  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Remove not the ancient landmark, which thy fathers have set.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Seest thou a man diligent in his business? he shall stand before kings; he shall not stand before mean _men_.  

## CHAPTER 23.

When thou sittest to eat with a ruler, consider diligently what _is_ before thee:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And put a knife to thy throat, if thou _be_ a man given to appetite.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Be not desirous of his dainties: for they _are_ deceitful meat.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Labour not to be rich: cease from thine own wisdom.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Wilt thou set thine eyes upon that which is not? for _riches_ certainly make themselves wings; they fly away as an eagle toward heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Eat thou not the bread of _him that hath_ an evil eye, neither desire thou his dainty meats:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For as he thinketh in his heart, so _is_ he: Eat and drink, saith he to thee; but his heart _is_ not with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The morsel _which_ thou hast eaten shalt thou vomit up, and lose thy sweet words.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Speak not in the ears of a fool: for he will despise the wisdom of thy words.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Remove not the old landmark; and enter not into the fields of the fatherless:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For their redeemer _is_ mighty; he shall plead their cause with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Apply thine heart unto instruction, and thine ears to the words of knowledge.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Withhold not correction from the child: for _if_ thou beatest him with the rod, he shall not die.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thou shalt beat him with the rod, and shalt deliver his soul from hell.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>My son, if thine heart be wise, my heart shall rejoice, even mine.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Yea, my reins shall rejoice, when thy lips speak right things.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Let not thine heart envy sinners: but _be thou_ in the fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> all the day long.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For surely there is an end; and thine expectation shall not be cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Hear thou, my son, and be wise, and guide thine heart in the way.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Be not among winebibbers; among riotous eaters of flesh:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For the drunkard and the glutton shall come to poverty: and drowsiness shall clothe _a man_ with rags.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Hearken unto thy father that begat thee, and despise not thy mother when she is old.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Buy the truth, and sell _it_ not; _also_ wisdom, and instruction, and understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>The father of the righteous shall greatly rejoice: and he that begetteth a wise _child_ shall have joy of him.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Thy father and thy mother shall be glad, and she that bare thee shall rejoice.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>My son, give me thine heart, and let thine eyes observe my ways.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For a whore _is_ a deep ditch; and a strange woman _is_ a narrow pit.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>She also lieth in wait as _for_ a prey, and increaseth the transgressors among men.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ Who hath woe? who hath sorrow? who hath contentions? who hath babbling? who hath wounds without cause? who hath redness of eyes?  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>They that tarry long at the wine; they that go to seek mixed wine.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Look not thou upon the wine when it is red, when it giveth his colour in the cup, _when_ it moveth itself aright.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>At the last it biteth like a serpent, and stingeth like an adder.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Thine eyes shall behold strange women, and thine heart shall utter perverse things.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Yea, thou shalt be as he that lieth down in the midst of the sea, or as he that lieth upon the top of a mast.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>They have stricken me, _shalt thou say, and_ I was not sick; they have beaten me, _and_ I felt _it_ not: when shall I awake? I will seek it yet again.  

## CHAPTER 24.

Be not thou envious against evil men, neither desire to be with them.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For their heart studieth destruction, and their lips talk of mischief.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Through wisdom is an house builded; and by understanding it is established:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And by knowledge shall the chambers be filled with all precious and pleasant riches.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>A wise man _is_ strong; yea, a man of knowledge increaseth strength.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For by wise counsel thou shalt make thy war: and in multitude of counsellors _there is_ safety.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Wisdom _is_ too high for a fool: he openeth not his mouth in the gate.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He that deviseth to do evil shall be called a mischievous person.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The thought of foolishness _is_ sin: and the scorner _is_ an abomination to men.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>_If_ thou faint in the day of adversity, thy strength _is_ small.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ If thou forbear to deliver _them that are_ drawn unto death, and _those that are_ ready to be slain;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>If thou sayest, Behold, we knew it not; doth not he that pondereth the heart consider _it_? and he that keepeth thy soul, doth _not_ he know _it_? and shall _not_ he render to _every_ man according to his works?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ My son, eat thou honey, because _it is_ good; and the honeycomb, _which is_ sweet to thy taste:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>So _shall_ the knowledge of wisdom _be_ unto thy soul: when thou hast found _it_, then there shall be a reward, and thy expectation shall not be cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Lay not wait, O wicked _man_, against the dwelling of the righteous; spoil not his resting place:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For a just _man_ falleth seven times, and riseth up again: but the wicked shall fall into mischief.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Rejoice not when thine enemy falleth, and let not thine heart be glad when he stumbleth:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Lest the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> see _it_, and it displease him, and he turn away his wrath from him.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Fret not thyself because of evil _men_, neither be thou envious at the wicked;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For there shall be no reward to the evil _man_; the candle of the wicked shall be put out.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ My son, fear thou the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> and the king: _and_ meddle not with them that are given to change:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For their calamity shall rise suddenly; and who knoweth the ruin of them both?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ These _things_ also _belong_ to the wise. _It is_ not good to have respect of persons in judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>He that saith unto the wicked, Thou _art_ righteous; him shall the people curse, nations shall abhor him:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But to them that rebuke _him_ shall be delight, and a good blessing shall come upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>_Every man_ shall kiss _his_ lips that giveth a right answer.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Prepare thy work without, and make it fit for thyself in the field; and afterwards build thine house.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ Be not a witness against thy neighbour without cause; and deceive _not_ with thy lips.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Say not, I will do so to him as he hath done to me: I will render to the man according to his work.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ I went by the field of the slothful, and by the vineyard of the man void of understanding;  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And, lo, it was all grown over with thorns, _and_ nettles had covered the face thereof, and the stone wall thereof was broken down.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Then I saw, _and_ considered _it_ well: I looked upon _it, and_ received instruction.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>_Yet_ a little sleep, a little slumber, a little folding of the hands to sleep:  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>So shall thy poverty come _as_ one that travelleth; and thy want as an armed man.  

## CHAPTER 25.

These _are_ also proverbs of Solomon, which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied out.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ _It is_ the glory of God to conceal a thing: but the honour of kings _is_ to search out a matter.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The heaven for height, and the earth for depth, and the heart of kings _is_ unsearchable.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Take away the dross from the silver, and there shall come forth a vessel for the finer.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Take away the wicked _from_ before the king, and his throne shall be established in righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Put not forth thyself in the presence of the king, and stand not in the place of great _men_:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For better _it is_ that it be said unto thee, Come up hither; than that thou shouldest be put lower in the presence of the prince whom thine eyes have seen.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Go not forth hastily to strive, lest _thou know not_ what to do in the end thereof, when thy neighbour hath put thee to shame.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Debate thy cause with thy neighbour _himself_; and discover not a secret to another:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Lest he that heareth _it_ put thee to shame, and thine infamy turn not away.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ A word fitly spoken _is like_ apples of gold in pictures of silver.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>_As_ an earring of gold, and an ornament of fine gold, _so is_ a wise reprover upon an obedient ear.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>As the cold of snow in the time of harvest, _so is_ a faithful messenger to them that send him: for he refresheth the soul of his masters.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Whoso boasteth himself of a false gift _is like_ clouds and wind without rain.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>By long forbearing is a prince persuaded, and a soft tongue breaketh the bone.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Hast thou found honey? eat so much as is sufficient for thee, lest thou be filled therewith, and vomit it.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Withdraw thy foot from thy neighbour’s house; lest he be weary of thee, and _so_ hate thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>A man that beareth false witness against his neighbour _is_ a maul, and a sword, and a sharp arrow.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Confidence in an unfaithful man in time of trouble _is like_ a broken tooth, and a foot out of joint.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>_As_ he that taketh away a garment in cold weather, _and as_ vinegar upon nitre, so _is_ he that singeth songs to an heavy heart.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ If thine enemy be hungry, give him bread to eat; and if he be thirsty, give him water to drink:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For thou shalt heap coals of fire upon his head, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall reward thee.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The north wind driveth away rain: so _doth_ an angry countenance a backbiting tongue.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>_It is_ better to dwell in the corner of the housetop, than with a brawling woman and in a wide house.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>_As_ cold waters to a thirsty soul, so _is_ good news from a far country.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>A righteous man falling down before the wicked _is as_ a troubled fountain, and a corrupt spring.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>_It is_ not good to eat much honey: so _for men_ to search their own glory _is not_ glory.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>He that _hath_ no rule over his own spirit _is like_ a city _that is_ broken down, _and_ without walls.  

## CHAPTER 26.

As snow in summer, and as rain in harvest, so honour is not seemly for a fool.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>As the bird by wandering, as the swallow by flying, so the curse causeless shall not come.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>A whip for the horse, a bridle for the ass, and a rod for the fool’s back.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Answer not a fool according to his folly, lest thou also be like unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Answer a fool according to his folly, lest he be wise in his own conceit.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ He that sendeth a message by the hand of a fool cutteth off the feet, _and_ drinketh damage.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The legs of the lame are not equal: so _is_ a parable in the mouth of fools.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>As he that bindeth a stone in a sling, so _is_ he that giveth honour to a fool.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_As_ a thorn goeth up into the hand of a drunkard, so _is_ a parable in the mouth of fools.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The great _God_ that formed all _things_ both rewardeth the fool, and rewardeth transgressors.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>As a dog returneth to his vomit, _so_ a fool returneth to his folly.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Seest thou a man wise in his own conceit? _there is_ more hope of a fool than of him.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The slothful _man_ saith, _There is_ a lion in the way; a lion _is_ in the streets.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>_As_ the door turneth upon his hinges, so _doth_ the slothful upon his bed.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The slothful hideth his hand in _his_ bosom; it grieveth him to bring it again to his mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>The sluggard _is_ wiser in his own conceit than seven men that can render a reason.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>He that passeth by, _and_ meddleth with strife _belonging_ not to him, _is like_ one that taketh a dog by the ears.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ As a mad _man_ who casteth firebrands, arrows, and death,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>So _is_ the man _that_ deceiveth his neighbour, and saith, Am not I in sport?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Where no wood is, _there_ the fire goeth out: so where _there is_ no talebearer, the strife ceaseth.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>_As_ coals _are_ to burning coals, and wood to fire; so _is_ a contentious man to kindle strife.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>The words of a talebearer _are_ as wounds, and they go down into the innermost parts of the belly.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Burning lips and a wicked heart _are like_ a potsherd covered with silver dross.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ He that hateth dissembleth with his lips, and layeth up deceit within him;  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>When he speaketh fair, believe him not: for _there are_ seven abominations in his heart.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>_Whose_ hatred is covered by deceit, his wickedness shall be shewed before the _whole_ congregation.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Whoso diggeth a pit shall fall therein: and he that rolleth a stone, it will return upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>A lying tongue hateth _those that are_ afflicted by it; and a flattering mouth worketh ruin.  

## CHAPTER 27.

Boast not thyself of to morrow; for thou knowest not what a day may bring forth.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Let another man praise thee, and not thine own mouth; a stranger, and not thine own lips.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ A stone _is_ heavy, and the sand weighty; but a fool’s wrath _is_ heavier than them both.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Wrath _is_ cruel, and anger _is_ outrageous; but who _is_ able to stand before envy?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Open rebuke _is_ better than secret love.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Faithful _are_ the wounds of a friend; but the kisses of an enemy _are_ deceitful.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The full soul loatheth an honeycomb; but to the hungry soul every bitter thing is sweet.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>As a bird that wandereth from her nest, so _is_ a man that wandereth from his place.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Ointment and perfume rejoice the heart: so _doth_ the sweetness of a man’s friend by hearty counsel.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thine own friend, and thy father’s friend, forsake not; neither go into thy brother’s house in the day of thy calamity: _for_ better _is_ a neighbour _that is_ near than a brother far off.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>My son, be wise, and make my heart glad, that I may answer him that reproacheth me.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>A prudent _man_ foreseeth the evil, _and_ hideth himself; _but_ the simple pass on, _and_ are punished.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Take his garment that is surety for a stranger, and take a pledge of him for a strange woman.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>He that blesseth his friend with a loud voice, rising early in the morning, it shall be counted a curse to him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ A continual dropping in a very rainy day and a contentious woman are alike.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Whosoever hideth her hideth the wind, and the ointment of his right hand, _which_ bewrayeth _itself_.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Iron sharpeneth iron; so a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Whoso keepeth the fig tree shall eat the fruit thereof: so he that waiteth on his master shall be honoured.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>As in water face _answereth_ to face, so the heart of man to man.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Hell and destruction are never full; so the eyes of man are never satisfied.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>_As_ the fining pot for silver, and the furnace for gold; so _is_ a man to his praise.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Though thou shouldest bray a fool in a mortar among wheat with a pestle, _yet_ will not his foolishness depart from him.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Be thou diligent to know the state of thy flocks, _and_ look well to thy herds.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For riches _are_ not for ever: and doth the crown _endure_ to every generation?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>The hay appeareth, and the tender grass sheweth itself, and herbs of the mountains are gathered.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>The lambs _are_ for thy clothing, and the goats _are_ the price of the field.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And _thou shalt have_ goats’ milk enough for thy food, for the food of thy household, and _for_ the maintenance for thy maidens.  

## CHAPTER 28.

The wicked flee when no man pursueth: but the righteous are bold as a lion.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For the transgression of a land many _are_ the princes thereof: but by a man of understanding _and_ knowledge the state _thereof_ shall be prolonged.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>A poor man that oppresseth the poor _is like_ a sweeping rain which leaveth no food.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>They that forsake the law praise the wicked: but such as keep the law contend with them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Evil men understand not judgment: but they that seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> understand all _things_.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Better _is_ the poor that walketh in his uprightness, than _he that is_ perverse _in his_ ways, though he _be_ rich.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Whoso keepeth the law _is_ a wise son: but he that is a companion of riotous _men_ shameth his father.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He that by usury and unjust gain increaseth his substance, he shall gather it for him that will pity the poor.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>He that turneth away his ear from hearing the law, even his prayer _shall be_ abomination.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Whoso causeth the righteous to go astray in an evil way, he shall fall himself into his own pit: but the upright shall have good _things_ in possession.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The rich man _is_ wise in his own conceit; but the poor that hath understanding searcheth him out.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>When righteous _men_ do rejoice, _there is_ great glory: but when the wicked rise, a man is hidden.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>He that covereth his sins shall not prosper: but whoso confesseth and forsaketh _them_ shall have mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Happy _is_ the man that feareth alway: but he that hardeneth his heart shall fall into mischief.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>_As_ a roaring lion, and a ranging bear; _so is_ a wicked ruler over the poor people.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>The prince that wanteth understanding _is_ also a great oppressor: _but_ he that hateth covetousness shall prolong _his_ days.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>A man that doeth violence to the blood of _any_ person shall flee to the pit; let no man stay him.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Whoso walketh uprightly shall be saved: but _he that is_ perverse _in his_ ways shall fall at once.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>He that tilleth his land shall have plenty of bread: but he that followeth after vain _persons_ shall have poverty enough.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>A faithful man shall abound with blessings: but he that maketh haste to be rich shall not be innocent.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>To have respect of persons _is_ not good: for for a piece of bread _that_ man will transgress.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>He that hasteth to be rich _hath_ an evil eye, and considereth not that poverty shall come upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>He that rebuketh a man afterwards shall find more favour than he that flattereth with the tongue.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Whoso robbeth his father or his mother, and saith, _It is_ no transgression; the same _is_ the companion of a destroyer.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>He that is of a proud heart stirreth up strife: but he that putteth his trust in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be made fat.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>He that trusteth in his own heart is a fool: but whoso walketh wisely, he shall be delivered.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>He that giveth unto the poor shall not lack: but he that hideth his eyes shall have many a curse.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>When the wicked rise, men hide themselves: but when they perish, the righteous increase.  

## CHAPTER 29.

He, that being often reproved hardeneth _his_ neck, shall suddenly be destroyed, and that without remedy.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>When the righteous are in authority, the people rejoice: but when the wicked beareth rule, the people mourn.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Whoso loveth wisdom rejoiceth his father: but he that keepeth company with harlots spendeth _his_ substance.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The king by judgment establisheth the land: but he that receiveth gifts overthroweth it.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>A man that flattereth his neighbour spreadeth a net for his feet.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>In the transgression of an evil man _there is_ a snare: but the righteous doth sing and rejoice.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The righteous considereth the cause of the poor: _but_ the wicked regardeth not to know _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Scornful men bring a city into a snare: but wise _men_ turn away wrath.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_If_ a wise man contendeth with a foolish man, whether he rage or laugh, _there is_ no rest.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The bloodthirsty hate the upright: but the just seek his soul.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>A fool uttereth all his mind: but a wise _man_ keepeth it in till afterwards.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>If a ruler hearken to lies, all his servants _are_ wicked.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The poor and the deceitful man meet together: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> lighteneth both their eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The king that faithfully judgeth the poor, his throne shall be established for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The rod and reproof give wisdom: but a child left _to himself_ bringeth his mother to shame.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>When the wicked are multiplied, transgression increaseth: but the righteous shall see their fall.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Correct thy son, and he shall give thee rest; yea, he shall give delight unto thy soul.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Where _there is_ no vision, the people perish: but he that keepeth the law, happy _is_ he.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>A servant will not be corrected by words: for though he understand he will not answer.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Seest thou a man _that is_ hasty in his words? _there is_ more hope of a fool than of him.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>He that delicately bringeth up his servant from a child shall have him become _his_ son at the length.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>An angry man stirreth up strife, and a furious man aboundeth in transgression.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>A man’s pride shall bring him low: but honour shall uphold the humble in spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Whoso is partner with a thief hateth his own soul: he heareth cursing, and bewrayeth _it_ not.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>The fear of man bringeth a snare: but whoso putteth his trust in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be safe.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Many seek the ruler’s favour; but _every_ man’s judgment _cometh_ from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>An unjust man _is_ an abomination to the just: and _he that is_ upright in the way _is_ abomination to the wicked.  

## CHAPTER 30.

The words of Agur the son of Jakeh, _even_ the prophecy: the man spake unto Ithiel, even unto Ithiel and Ucal,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Surely I _am_ more brutish than _any_ man, and have not the understanding of a man.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I neither learned wisdom, nor have the knowledge of the holy.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who hath ascended up into heaven, or descended? who hath gathered the wind in his fists? who hath bound the waters in a garment? who hath established all the ends of the earth? what _is_ his name, and what _is_ his son’s name, if thou canst tell?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Every word of God _is_ pure: he _is_ a shield unto them that put their trust in him.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Add thou not unto his words, lest he reprove thee, and thou be found a liar.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Two _things_ have I required of thee; deny me _them_ not before I die:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Remove far from me vanity and lies: give me neither poverty nor riches; feed me with food convenient for me:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Lest I be full, and deny _thee_, and say, Who _is_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>? or lest I be poor, and steal, and take the name of my God _in vain_.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Accuse not a servant unto his master, lest he curse thee, and thou be found guilty.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_There is_ a generation _that_ curseth their father, and doth not bless their mother.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>_There is_ a generation _that are_ pure in their own eyes, and _yet_ is not washed from their filthiness.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>_There is_ a generation, O how lofty are their eyes! and their eyelids are lifted up.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>_There is_ a generation, whose teeth _are as_ swords, and their jaw teeth _as_ knives, to devour the poor from off the earth, and the needy from _among_ men.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ The horseleach hath two daughters, _crying_, Give, give. There are three _things that_ are never satisfied, _yea_, four _things_ say not, _It is_ enough:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>The grave; and the barren womb; the earth _that_ is not filled with water; and the fire _that_ saith not, _It is_ enough.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>The eye _that_ mocketh at _his_ father, and despiseth to obey _his_ mother, the ravens of the valley shall pick it out, and the young eagles shall eat it.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ There be three _things which_ are too wonderful for me, yea, four which I know not:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The way of an eagle in the air; the way of a serpent upon a rock; the way of a ship in the midst of the sea; and the way of a man with a maid.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Such _is_ the way of an adulterous woman; she eateth, and wipeth her mouth, and saith, I have done no wickedness.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For three _things_ the earth is disquieted, and for four _which_ it cannot bear:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For a servant when he reigneth; and a fool when he is filled with meat;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For an odious _woman_ when she is married; and an handmaid that is heir to her mistress.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ There be four _things which are_ little upon the earth, but they _are_ exceeding wise:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>The ants _are_ a people not strong, yet they prepare their meat in the summer;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>The conies _are but_ a feeble folk, yet make they their houses in the rocks;  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>The locusts have no king, yet go they forth all of them by bands;  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>The spider taketh hold with her hands, and is in kings’ palaces.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ There be three _things_ which go well, yea, four are comely in going:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>A lion _which is_ strongest among beasts, and turneth not away for any;  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>A greyhound; an he goat also; and a king, against whom _there is_ no rising up.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>If thou hast done foolishly in lifting up thyself, or if thou hast thought evil, _lay_ thine hand upon thy mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Surely the churning of milk bringeth forth butter, and the wringing of the nose bringeth forth blood: so the forcing of wrath bringeth forth strife.  

## CHAPTER 31.

The words of king Lemuel, the prophecy that his mother taught him.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>What, my son? and what, the son of my womb? and what, the son of my vows?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Give not thy strength unto women, nor thy ways to that which destroyeth kings.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>_It is_ not for kings, O Lemuel, _it is_ not for kings to drink wine; nor for princes strong drink:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Lest they drink, and forget the law, and pervert the judgment of any of the afflicted.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Give strong drink unto him that is ready to perish, and wine unto those that be of heavy hearts.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Let him drink, and forget his poverty, and remember his misery no more.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Open thy mouth for the dumb in the cause of all such as are appointed to destruction.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Open thy mouth, judge righteously, and plead the cause of the poor and needy.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Who can find a virtuous woman? for her price _is_ far above rubies.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The heart of her husband doth safely trust in her, so that he shall have no need of spoil.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>She will do him good and not evil all the days of her life.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>She seeketh wool, and flax, and worketh willingly with her hands.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>She is like the merchants’ ships; she bringeth her food from afar.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>She riseth also while it is yet night, and giveth meat to her household, and a portion to her maidens.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>She considereth a field, and buyeth it: with the fruit of her hands she planteth a vineyard.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>She girdeth her loins with strength, and strengtheneth her arms.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>She perceiveth that her merchandise _is_ good: her candle goeth not out by night.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>She layeth her hands to the spindle, and her hands hold the distaff.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>She stretcheth out her hand to the poor; yea, she reacheth forth her hands to the needy.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>She is not afraid of the snow for her household: for all her household _are_ clothed with scarlet.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>She maketh herself coverings of tapestry; her clothing _is_ silk and purple.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Her husband is known in the gates, when he sitteth among the elders of the land.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>She maketh fine linen, and selleth _it_; and delivereth girdles unto the merchant.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Strength and honour _are_ her clothing; and she shall rejoice in time to come.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>She openeth her mouth with wisdom; and in her tongue _is_ the law of kindness.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>She looketh well to the ways of her household, and eateth not the bread of idleness.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Her children arise up, and call her blessed; her husband _also_, and he praiseth her.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Many daughters have done virtuously, but thou excellest them all.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Favour _is_ deceitful, and beauty _is_ vain: _but_ a woman _that_ feareth the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, she shall be praised.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Give her of the fruit of her hands; and let her own works praise her in the gates.
