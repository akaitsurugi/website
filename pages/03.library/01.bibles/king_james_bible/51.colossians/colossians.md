# Colossians

### THE EPISTLE OF PAUL THE APOSTLE TO THE COLOSSIANS

## CHAPTER 1.

Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ by the will of God, and Timotheus _our_ brother,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>To the saints and faithful brethren in Christ which are at Colosse: Grace _be_ unto you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>We give thanks to God and the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, praying always for you,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Since we heard of your faith in Christ Jesus, and of the love _which ye have_ to all the saints,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For the hope which is laid up for you in heaven, whereof ye heard before in the word of the truth of the gospel;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Which is come unto you, as _it is_ in all the world; and bringeth forth fruit, as _it doth_ also in you, since the day ye heard _of it_, and knew the grace of God in truth:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>As ye also learned of Epaphras our dear fellowservant, who is for you a faithful minister of Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Who also declared unto us your love in the Spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For this cause we also, since the day we heard _it_, do not cease to pray for you, and to desire that ye might be filled with the knowledge of his will in all wisdom and spiritual understanding;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>That ye might walk worthy of the Lord unto all pleasing, being fruitful in every good work, and increasing in the knowledge of God;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Strengthened with all might, according to his glorious power, unto all patience and longsuffering with joyfulness;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Giving thanks unto the Father, which hath made us meet to be partakers of the inheritance of the saints in light:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Who hath delivered us from the power of darkness, and hath translated _us_ into the kingdom of his dear Son:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>In whom we have redemption through his blood, _even_ the forgiveness of sins:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Who is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of every creature:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For by him were all things created, that are in heaven, and that are in earth, visible and invisible, whether _they be_ thrones, or dominions, or principalities, or powers: all things were created by him, and for him:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he is before all things, and by him all things consist.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And he is the head of the body, the church: who is the beginning, the firstborn from the dead; that in all _things_ he might have the preeminence.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For it pleased _the Father_ that in him should all fulness dwell;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And, having made peace through the blood of his cross, by him to reconcile all things unto himself; by him, _I say_, whether _they be_ things in earth, or things in heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And you, that were sometime alienated and enemies in _your_ mind by wicked works, yet now hath he reconciled  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>In the body of his flesh through death, to present you holy and unblameable and unreproveable in his sight:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>If ye continue in the faith grounded and settled, and _be_ not moved away from the hope of the gospel, which ye have heard, _and_ which was preached to every creature which is under heaven; whereof I Paul am made a minister;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Who now rejoice in my sufferings for you, and fill up that which is behind of the afflictions of Christ in my flesh for his body’s sake, which is the church:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Whereof I am made a minister, according to the dispensation of God which is given to me for you, to fulfil the word of God;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>_Even_ the mystery which hath been hid from ages and from generations, but now is made manifest to his saints:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>To whom God would make known what _is_ the riches of the glory of this mystery among the Gentiles; which is Christ in you, the hope of glory:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Whom we preach, warning every man, and teaching every man in all wisdom; that we may present every man perfect in Christ Jesus:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Whereunto I also labour, striving according to his working, which worketh in me mightily.  

## CHAPTER 2.

For I would that ye knew what great conflict I have for you, and _for_ them at Laodicea, and _for_ as many as have not seen my face in the flesh;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That their hearts might be comforted, being knit together in love, and unto all riches of the full assurance of understanding, to the acknowledgement of the mystery of God, and of the Father, and of Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>In whom are hid all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And this I say, lest any man should beguile you with enticing words.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For though I be absent in the flesh, yet am I with you in the spirit, joying and beholding your order, and the stedfastness of your faith in Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ As ye have therefore received Christ Jesus the Lord, _so_ walk ye in him:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Rooted and built up in him, and stablished in the faith, as ye have been taught, abounding therein with thanksgiving.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Beware lest any man spoil you through philosophy and vain deceit, after the tradition of men, after the rudiments of the world, and not after Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For in him dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And ye are complete in him, which is the head of all principality and power:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>In whom also ye are circumcised with the circumcision made without hands, in putting off the body of the sins of the flesh by the circumcision of Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Buried with him in baptism, wherein also ye are risen with _him_ through the faith of the operation of God, who hath raised him from the dead.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And you, being dead in your sins and the uncircumcision of your flesh, hath he quickened together with him, having forgiven you all trespasses;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Blotting out the handwriting of ordinances that was against us, which was contrary to us, and took it out of the way, nailing it to his cross;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>_And_ having spoiled principalities and powers, he made a shew of them openly, triumphing over them in it.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink, or in respect of an holyday, or of the new moon, or of the sabbath _days_:   
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body _is_ of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Let no man beguile you of your reward in a voluntary humility and worshipping of angels, intruding into those things which he hath not seen, vainly puffed up by his fleshly mind,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And not holding the Head, from which all the body by joints and bands having nourishment ministered, and knit together, increaseth with the increase of God.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore if ye be dead with Christ from the rudiments of the world, why, as though living in the world, are ye subject to ordinances,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>(Touch not; taste not; handle not;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Which all are to perish with the using;) after the commandments and doctrines of men?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Which things have indeed a shew of wisdom in will worship, and humility, and neglecting of the body; not in any honour to the satisfying of the flesh.  

## CHAPTER 3.

If ye then be risen with Christ, seek those things which are above, where Christ sitteth on the right hand of God.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Set your affection on things above, not on things on the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For ye are dead, and your life is hid with Christ in God.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>When Christ, _who is_ our life, shall appear, then shall ye also appear with him in glory.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Mortify therefore your members which are upon the earth; fornication, uncleanness, inordinate affection, evil concupiscence, and covetousness, which is idolatry:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For which things’ sake the wrath of God cometh on the children of disobedience:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>In the which ye also walked some time, when ye lived in them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But now ye also put off all these; anger, wrath, malice, blasphemy, filthy communication out of your mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Lie not one to another, seeing that ye have put off the old man with his deeds;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And have put on the new _man_, which is renewed in knowledge after the image of him that created him:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Where there is neither Greek nor Jew, circumcision nor uncircumcision, Barbarian, Scythian, bond _nor_ free: but Christ _is_ all, and in all.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Put on therefore, as the elect of God, holy and beloved, bowels of mercies, kindness, humbleness of mind, meekness, longsuffering;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Forbearing one another, and forgiving one another, if any man have a quarrel against any: even as Christ forgave you, so also _do_ ye.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And above all these things _put on_ charity, which is the bond of perfectness.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And let the peace of God rule in your hearts, to the which also ye are called in one body; and be ye thankful.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly in all wisdom; teaching and admonishing one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing with grace in your hearts to the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And whatsoever ye do in word or deed, _do_ all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God and the Father by him.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands, as it is fit in the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Husbands, love _your_ wives, and be not bitter against them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Children, obey _your_ parents in all things: for this is well pleasing unto the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Fathers, provoke not your children _to anger_, lest they be discouraged.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Servants, obey in all things _your_ masters according to the flesh; not with eyeservice, as menpleasers; but in singleness of heart, fearing God:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And whatsoever ye do, do _it_ heartily, as to the Lord, and not unto men;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Knowing that of the Lord ye shall receive the reward of the inheritance: for ye serve the Lord Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But he that doeth wrong shall receive for the wrong which he hath done: and there is no respect of persons.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Masters, give unto _your_ servants that which is just and equal; knowing that ye also have a Master in heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ Continue in prayer, and watch in the same with thanksgiving;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Withal praying also for us, that God would open unto us a door of utterance, to speak the mystery of Christ, for which I am also in bonds:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>That I may make it manifest, as I ought to speak.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Walk in wisdom toward them that are without, redeeming the time.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Let your speech _be_ alway with grace, seasoned with salt, that ye may know how ye ought to answer every man.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ All my state shall Tychicus declare unto you, _who is_ a beloved brother, and a faithful minister and fellowservant in the Lord:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Whom I have sent unto you for the same purpose, that he might know your estate, and comfort your hearts;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>With Onesimus, a faithful and beloved brother, who is _one_ of you. They shall make known unto you all things which _are done_ here.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Aristarchus my fellowprisoner saluteth you, and Marcus, sister’s son to Barnabas, (touching whom ye received commandments: if he come unto you, receive him;)  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Jesus, which is called Justus, who are of the circumcision. These only _are my_ fellowworkers unto the kingdom of God, which have been a comfort unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Epaphras, who is _one_ of you, a servant of Christ, saluteth you, always labouring fervently for you in prayers, that ye may stand perfect and complete in all the will of God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For I bear him record, that he hath a great zeal for you, and them _that are_ in Laodicea, and them in Hierapolis.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Luke, the beloved physician, and Demas, greet you.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Salute the brethren which are in Laodicea, and Nymphas, and the church which is in his house.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And when this epistle is read among you, cause that it be read also in the church of the Laodiceans; and that ye likewise read the _epistle_ from Laodicea.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And say to Archippus, Take heed to the ministry which thou hast received in the Lord, that thou fulfil it.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The salutation by the hand of me Paul. Remember my bonds. Grace _be_ with you. Amen.  

<div class="colophon">Written from Rome to the Colossians by Tychicus and Onesimus.</div>
