# Titus

### THE EPISTLE OF PAUL THE APOSTLE TO TITUS

## CHAPTER 1.

Paul, a servant of God, and an apostle of Jesus Christ, according to the faith of God’s elect, and the acknowledging of the truth which is after godliness;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But hath in due times manifested his word through preaching, which is committed unto me according to the commandment of God our Saviour;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>To Titus, _mine_ own son after the common faith: Grace, mercy, _and_ peace, from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Saviour.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ For this cause left I thee in Crete, that thou shouldest set in order the things that are wanting, and ordain elders in every city, as I had appointed thee:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>If any be blameless, the husband of one wife, having faithful children not accused of riot or unruly.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For a bishop must be blameless, as the steward of God; not selfwilled, not soon angry, not given to wine, no striker, not given to filthy lucre;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But a lover of hospitality, a lover of good men, sober, just, holy, temperate;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Holding fast the faithful word as he hath been taught, that he may be able by sound doctrine both to exhort and to convince the gainsayers.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For there are many unruly and vain talkers and deceivers, specially they of the circumcision:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Whose mouths must be stopped, who subvert whole houses, teaching things which they ought not, for filthy lucre’s sake.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>One of themselves, _even_ a prophet of their own, said, The Cretians _are_ alway liars, evil beasts, slow bellies.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>This witness is true. Wherefore rebuke them sharply, that they may be sound in the faith;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Not giving heed to Jewish fables, and commandments of men, that turn from the truth.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Unto the pure all things _are_ pure: but unto them that are defiled and unbelieving _is_ nothing pure; but even their mind and conscience is defiled.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>They profess that they know God; but in works they deny _him_, being abominable, and disobedient, and unto every good work reprobate.  

## CHAPTER 2.

But speak thou the things which become sound doctrine:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That the aged men be sober, grave, temperate, sound in faith, in charity, in patience.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The aged women likewise, that _they be_ in behaviour as becometh holiness, not false accusers, not given to much wine, teachers of good things;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>That they may teach the young women to be sober, to love their husbands, to love their children,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>_To be_ discreet, chaste, keepers at home, good, obedient to their own husbands, that the word of God be not blasphemed.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Young men likewise exhort to be sober minded.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>In all things shewing thyself a pattern of good works: in doctrine _shewing_ uncorruptness, gravity, sincerity,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Sound speech, that cannot be condemned; that he that is of the contrary part may be ashamed, having no evil thing to say of you.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_Exhort_ servants to be obedient unto their own masters, _and_ to please _them_ well in all _things_; not answering again;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Not purloining, but shewing all good fidelity; that they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour in all things.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people, zealous of good works.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>These things speak, and exhort, and rebuke with all authority. Let no man despise thee.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Put them in mind to be subject to principalities and powers, to obey magistrates, to be ready to every good work,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>To speak evil of no man, to be no brawlers, _but_ gentle, shewing all meekness unto all men.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For we ourselves also were sometimes foolish, disobedient, deceived, serving divers lusts and pleasures, living in malice and envy, hateful, _and_ hating one another.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But after that the kindness and love of God our Saviour toward man appeared,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us, by the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Holy Ghost;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Which he shed on us abundantly through Jesus Christ our Saviour;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>That being justified by his grace, we should be made heirs according to the hope of eternal life.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>_This is_ a faithful saying, and these things I will that thou affirm constantly, that they which have believed in God might be careful to maintain good works. These things are good and profitable unto men.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But avoid foolish questions, and genealogies, and contentions, and strivings about the law; for they are unprofitable and vain.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>A man that is an heretick after the first and second admonition reject;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Knowing that he that is such is subverted, and sinneth, being condemned of himself.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ When I shall send Artemas unto thee, or Tychicus, be diligent to come unto me to Nicopolis: for I have determined there to winter.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Bring Zenas the lawyer and Apollos on their journey diligently, that nothing be wanting unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And let ours also learn to maintain good works for necessary uses, that they be not unfruitful.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>All that are with me salute thee. Greet them that love us in the faith. Grace _be_ with you all. Amen.  

<div class="colophon">It was written to Titus, ordained the first bishop of the church of the Cretians, from Nicopolis of Macedonia.</div>
