# Isaiah

### THE BOOK OF THE PROPHET ISAIAH

## CHAPTER 1.

The vision of Isaiah the son of Amoz, which he saw concerning Judah and Jerusalem in the days of Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz, _and_ Hezekiah, kings of Judah.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ Hear, O heavens, and give ear, O earth: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken, I have nourished and brought up children, and they have rebelled against me.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The ox knoweth his owner, and the ass his master’s crib: _but_ Israel doth not know, my people doth not consider.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Ah sinful nation, a people laden with iniquity, a seed of evildoers, children that are corrupters: they have forsaken the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, they have provoked the Holy One of Israel unto anger, they are gone away backward.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Why should ye be stricken any more? ye will revolt more and more: the whole head is sick, and the whole heart faint.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>From the sole of the foot even unto the head _there is_ no soundness in it; _but_ wounds, and bruises, and putrifying sores: they have not been closed, neither bound up, neither mollified with ointment.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Your country _is_ desolate, your cities _are_ burned with fire: your land, strangers devour it in your presence, and _it is_ desolate, as overthrown by strangers.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the daughter of Zion is left as a cottage in a vineyard, as a lodge in a garden of cucumbers, as a besieged city.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Except the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts had left unto us a very small remnant, we should have been as Sodom, _and_ we should have been like unto Gomorrah.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, ye rulers of Sodom; give ear unto the law of our God, ye people of Gomorrah.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>To what purpose _is_ the multitude of your sacrifices unto me? saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: I am full of the burnt offerings of rams, and the fat of fed beasts; and I delight not in the blood of bullocks, or of lambs, or of he goats.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>When ye come to appear before me, who hath required this at your hand, to tread my courts?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Bring no more vain oblations; incense is an abomination unto me; the new moons and sabbaths, the calling of assemblies, I cannot away with; _it is_ iniquity, even the solemn meeting.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Your new moons and your appointed feasts my soul hateth: they are a trouble unto me; I am weary to bear _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And when ye spread forth your hands, I will hide mine eyes from you: yea, when ye make many prayers, I will not hear: your hands are full of blood.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Wash you, make you clean; put away the evil of your doings from before mine eyes; cease to do evil;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Learn to do well; seek judgment, relieve the oppressed, judge the fatherless, plead for the widow.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Come now, and let us reason together, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they be red like crimson, they shall be as wool.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>If ye be willing and obedient, ye shall eat the good of the land:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But if ye refuse and rebel, ye shall be devoured with the sword: for the mouth of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ How is the faithful city become an harlot! it was full of judgment; righteousness lodged in it; but now murderers.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Thy silver is become dross, thy wine mixed with water:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Thy princes _are_ rebellious, and companions of thieves: every one loveth gifts, and followeth after rewards: they judge not the fatherless, neither doth the cause of the widow come unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Therefore saith the Lord, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, the mighty One of Israel, Ah, I will ease me of mine adversaries, and avenge me of mine enemies:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And I will turn my hand upon thee, and purely purge away thy dross, and take away all thy tin:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And I will restore thy judges as at the first, and thy counsellors as at the beginning: afterward thou shalt be called, The city of righteousness, the faithful city.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Zion shall be redeemed with judgment, and her converts with righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ And the destruction of the transgressors and of the sinners _shall be_ together, and they that forsake the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be consumed.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For they shall be ashamed of the oaks which ye have desired, and ye shall be confounded for the gardens that ye have chosen.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>For ye shall be as an oak whose leaf fadeth, and as a garden that hath no water.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the strong shall be as tow, and the maker of it as a spark, and they shall both burn together, and none shall quench _them_.  

## CHAPTER 2.

The word that Isaiah the son of Amoz saw concerning Judah and Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in the last days, _that_ the mountain of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> house shall be established in the top of the mountains, and shall be exalted above the hills; and all nations shall flow unto it.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And many people shall go and say, Come ye, and let us go up to the mountain of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to the house of the God of Jacob; and he will teach us of his ways, and we will walk in his paths: for out of Zion shall go forth the law, and the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> from Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And he shall judge among the nations, and shall rebuke many people: and they shall beat their swords into plowshares, and their spears into pruninghooks: nation shall not lift up sword against nation, neither shall they learn war any more.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>O house of Jacob, come ye, and let us walk in the light of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore thou hast forsaken thy people the house of Jacob, because they be replenished from the east, and _are_ soothsayers like the Philistines, and they please themselves in the children of strangers.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Their land also is full of silver and gold, neither _is there any_ end of their treasures; their land is also full of horses, neither _is there any_ end of their chariots:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Their land also is full of idols; they worship the work of their own hands, that which their own fingers have made:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the mean man boweth down, and the great man humbleth himself: therefore forgive them not.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Enter into the rock, and hide thee in the dust, for fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and for the glory of his majesty.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The lofty looks of man shall be humbled, and the haughtiness of men shall be bowed down, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> alone shall be exalted in that day.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts _shall be_ upon every _one that is_ proud and lofty, and upon every _one that is_ lifted up; and he shall be brought low:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And upon all the cedars of Lebanon, _that are_ high and lifted up, and upon all the oaks of Bashan,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And upon all the high mountains, and upon all the hills _that are_ lifted up,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And upon every high tower, and upon every fenced wall,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And upon all the ships of Tarshish, and upon all pleasant pictures.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the loftiness of man shall be bowed down, and the haughtiness of men shall be made low: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> alone shall be exalted in that day.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the idols he shall utterly abolish.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And they shall go into the holes of the rocks, and into the caves of the earth, for fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and for the glory of his majesty, when he ariseth to shake terribly the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>In that day a man shall cast his idols of silver, and his idols of gold, which they made _each one_ for himself to worship, to the moles and to the bats;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>To go into the clefts of the rocks, and into the tops of the ragged rocks, for fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and for the glory of his majesty, when he ariseth to shake terribly the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Cease ye from man, whose breath _is_ in his nostrils: for wherein is he to be accounted of?  

## CHAPTER 3.

For, behold, the Lord, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, doth take away from Jerusalem and from Judah the stay and the staff, the whole stay of bread, and the whole stay of water,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The mighty man, and the man of war, the judge, and the prophet, and the prudent, and the ancient,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The captain of fifty, and the honourable man, and the counsellor, and the cunning artificer, and the eloquent orator.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And I will give children _to be_ their princes, and babes shall rule over them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the people shall be oppressed, every one by another, and every one by his neighbour: the child shall behave himself proudly against the ancient, and the base against the honourable.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>When a man shall take hold of his brother of the house of his father, _saying_, Thou hast clothing, be thou our ruler, and _let_ this ruin _be_ under thy hand:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>In that day shall he swear, saying, I will not be an healer; for in my house _is_ neither bread nor clothing: make me not a ruler of the people.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For Jerusalem is ruined, and Judah is fallen: because their tongue and their doings _are_ against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to provoke the eyes of his glory.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ The shew of their countenance doth witness against them; and they declare their sin as Sodom, they hide _it_ not. Woe unto their soul! for they have rewarded evil unto themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Say ye to the righteous, that _it shall be_ well _with him_: for they shall eat the fruit of their doings.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Woe unto the wicked! _it shall be_ ill _with him_: for the reward of his hands shall be given him.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ _As for_ my people, children _are_ their oppressors, and women rule over them. O my people, they which lead thee cause _thee_ to err, and destroy the way of thy paths.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> standeth up to plead, and standeth to judge the people.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will enter into judgment with the ancients of his people, and the princes thereof: for ye have eaten up the vineyard; the spoil of the poor _is_ in your houses.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>What mean ye _that_ ye beat my people to pieces, and grind the faces of the poor? saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Moreover the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> saith, Because the daughters of Zion are haughty, and walk with stretched forth necks and wanton eyes, walking and mincing _as_ they go, and making a tinkling with their feet:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Therefore the Lord will smite with a scab the crown of the head of the daughters of Zion, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will discover their secret parts.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>In that day the Lord will take away the bravery of _their_ tinkling ornaments _about their feet_, and _their_ cauls, and _their_ round tires like the moon,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The chains, and the bracelets, and the mufflers,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>The bonnets, and the ornaments of the legs, and the headbands, and the tablets, and the earrings,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>The rings, and nose jewels,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>The changeable suits of apparel, and the mantles, and the wimples, and the crisping pins,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The glasses, and the fine linen, and the hoods, and the vails.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ instead of sweet smell there shall be stink; and instead of a girdle a rent; and instead of well set hair baldness; and instead of a stomacher a girding of sackcloth; _and_ burning instead of beauty.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Thy men shall fall by the sword, and thy mighty in the war.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And her gates shall lament and mourn; and she _being_ desolate shall sit upon the ground.  

## CHAPTER 4.

And in that day seven women shall take hold of one man, saying, We will eat our own bread, and wear our own apparel: only let us be called by thy name, to take away our reproach.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ In that day shall the branch of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> be beautiful and glorious, and the fruit of the earth _shall be_ excellent and comely for them that are escaped of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that he that is_ left in Zion, and _he that_ remaineth in Jerusalem, shall be called holy, _even_ every one that is written among the living in Jerusalem:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>When the Lord shall have washed away the filth of the daughters of Zion, and shall have purged the blood of Jerusalem from the midst thereof by the spirit of judgment, and by the spirit of burning.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will create upon every dwelling place of mount Zion, and upon her assemblies, a cloud and smoke by day, and the shining of a flaming fire by night: for upon all the glory _shall be_ a defence.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And there shall be a tabernacle for a shadow in the daytime from the heat, and for a place of refuge, and for a covert from storm and from rain.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Now will I sing to my wellbeloved a song of my beloved touching his vineyard. My wellbeloved hath a vineyard in a very fruitful hill:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he fenced it, and gathered out the stones thereof, and planted it with the choicest vine, and built a tower in the midst of it, and also made a winepress therein: and he looked that it should bring forth grapes, and it brought forth wild grapes.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And now, O inhabitants of Jerusalem, and men of Judah, judge, I pray you, betwixt me and my vineyard.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>What could have been done more to my vineyard, that I have not done in it? wherefore, when I looked that it should bring forth grapes, brought it forth wild grapes?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And now go to; I will tell you what I will do to my vineyard: I will take away the hedge thereof, and it shall be eaten up; _and_ break down the wall thereof, and it shall be trodden down:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And I will lay it waste: it shall not be pruned, nor digged; but there shall come up briers and thorns: I will also command the clouds that they rain no rain upon it.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For the vineyard of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts _is_ the house of Israel, and the men of Judah his pleasant plant: and he looked for judgment, but behold oppression; for righteousness, but behold a cry.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Woe unto them that join house to house, _that_ lay field to field, till _there be_ no place, that they may be placed alone in the midst of the earth!  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>In mine ears _said_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, Of a truth many houses shall be desolate, _even_ great and fair, without inhabitant.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Yea, ten acres of vineyard shall yield one bath, and the seed of an homer shall yield an ephah.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Woe unto them that rise up early in the morning, _that_ they may follow strong drink; that continue until night, _till_ wine inflame them!  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the harp, and the viol, the tabret, and pipe, and wine, are in their feasts: but they regard not the work of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, neither consider the operation of his hands.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore my people are gone into captivity, because _they have_ no knowledge: and their honourable men _are_ famished, and their multitude dried up with thirst.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore hell hath enlarged herself, and opened her mouth without measure: and their glory, and their multitude, and their pomp, and he that rejoiceth, shall descend into it.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the mean man shall be brought down, and the mighty man shall be humbled, and the eyes of the lofty shall be humbled:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts shall be exalted in judgment, and God that is holy shall be sanctified in righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Then shall the lambs feed after their manner, and the waste places of the fat ones shall strangers eat.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Woe unto them that draw iniquity with cords of vanity, and sin as it were with a cart rope:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>That say, Let him make speed, _and_ hasten his work, that we may see _it_: and let the counsel of the Holy One of Israel draw nigh and come, that we may know _it_!  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Woe unto them that call evil good, and good evil; that put darkness for light, and light for darkness; that put bitter for sweet, and sweet for bitter!  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Woe unto _them that are_ wise in their own eyes, and prudent in their own sight!  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Woe unto _them that are_ mighty to drink wine, and men of strength to mingle strong drink:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Which justify the wicked for reward, and take away the righteousness of the righteous from him!  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Therefore as the fire devoureth the stubble, and the flame consumeth the chaff, _so_ their root shall be as rottenness, and their blossom shall go up as dust: because they have cast away the law of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, and despised the word of the Holy One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Therefore is the anger of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> kindled against his people, and he hath stretched forth his hand against them, and hath smitten them: and the hills did tremble, and their carcases _were_ torn in the midst of the streets. For all this his anger is not turned away, but his hand _is_ stretched out still.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And he will lift up an ensign to the nations from far, and will hiss unto them from the end of the earth: and, behold, they shall come with speed swiftly:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>None shall be weary nor stumble among them; none shall slumber nor sleep; neither shall the girdle of their loins be loosed, nor the latchet of their shoes be broken:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Whose arrows _are_ sharp, and all their bows bent, their horses’ hoofs shall be counted like flint, and their wheels like a whirlwind:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Their roaring _shall be_ like a lion, they shall roar like young lions: yea, they shall roar, and lay hold of the prey, and shall carry _it_ away safe, and none shall deliver _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And in that day they shall roar against them like the roaring of the sea: and if _one_ look unto the land, behold darkness _and_ sorrow, and the light is darkened in the heavens thereof.  

## CHAPTER 6.

In the year that king Uzziah died I saw also the Lord sitting upon a throne, high and lifted up, and his train filled the temple.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Above it stood the seraphims: each one had six wings; with twain he covered his face, and with twain he covered his feet, and with twain he did fly.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And one cried unto another, and said, Holy, holy, holy, _is_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts: the whole earth _is_ full of his glory.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the posts of the door moved at the voice of him that cried, and the house was filled with smoke.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Then said I, Woe _is_ me! for I am undone; because I _am_ a man of unclean lips, and I dwell in the midst of a people of unclean lips: for mine eyes have seen the King, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Then flew one of the seraphims unto me, having a live coal in his hand, _which_ he had taken with the tongs from off the altar:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he laid _it_ upon my mouth, and said, Lo, this hath touched thy lips; and thine iniquity is taken away, and thy sin purged.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Also I heard the voice of the Lord, saying, Whom shall I send, and who will go for us? Then said I, Here _am_ I; send me.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And he said, Go, and tell this people, Hear ye indeed, but understand not; and see ye indeed, but perceive not.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Make the heart of this people fat, and make their ears heavy, and shut their eyes; lest they see with their eyes, and hear with their ears, and understand with their heart, and convert, and be healed.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then said I, Lord, how long? And he answered, Until the cities be wasted without inhabitant, and the houses without man, and the land be utterly desolate,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have removed men far away, and _there be_ a great forsaking in the midst of the land.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ But yet in it _shall be_ a tenth, and _it_ shall return, and shall be eaten: as a teil tree, and as an oak, whose substance _is_ in them, when they cast _their leaves: so_ the holy seed _shall be_ the substance thereof.  

## CHAPTER 7.

And it came to pass in the days of Ahaz the son of Jotham, the son of Uzziah, king of Judah, _that_ Rezin the king of Syria, and Pekah the son of Remaliah, king of Israel, went up toward Jerusalem to war against it, but could not prevail against it.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And it was told the house of David, saying, Syria is confederate with Ephraim. And his heart was moved, and the heart of his people, as the trees of the wood are moved with the wind.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Then said the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto Isaiah, Go forth now to meet Ahaz, thou, and Shear–jashub thy son, at the end of the conduit of the upper pool in the highway of the fuller’s field;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And say unto him, Take heed, and be quiet; fear not, neither be fainthearted for the two tails of these smoking firebrands, for the fierce anger of Rezin with Syria, and of the son of Remaliah.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Because Syria, Ephraim, and the son of Remaliah, have taken evil counsel against thee, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Let us go up against Judah, and vex it, and let us make a breach therein for us, and set a king in the midst of it, _even_ the son of Tabeal:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, It shall not stand, neither shall it come to pass.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For the head of Syria _is_ Damascus, and the head of Damascus _is_ Rezin; and within threescore and five years shall Ephraim be broken, that it be not a people.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the head of Ephraim _is_ Samaria, and the head of Samaria _is_ Remaliah’s son. If ye will not believe, surely ye shall not be established.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Moreover the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake again unto Ahaz, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Ask thee a sign of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God; ask it either in the depth, or in the height above.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But Ahaz said, I will not ask, neither will I tempt the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And he said, Hear ye now, O house of David; _Is it_ a small thing for you to weary men, but will ye weary my God also?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore the Lord himself shall give you a sign; Behold, a virgin shall conceive, and bear a son, and shall call his name Immanuel.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Butter and honey shall he eat, that he may know to refuse the evil, and choose the good.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For before the child shall know to refuse the evil, and choose the good, the land that thou abhorrest shall be forsaken of both her kings.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall bring upon thee, and upon thy people, and upon thy father’s house, days that have not come, from the day that Ephraim departed from Judah; _even_ the king of Assyria.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall hiss for the fly that _is_ in the uttermost part of the rivers of Egypt, and for the bee that _is_ in the land of Assyria.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And they shall come, and shall rest all of them in the desolate valleys, and in the holes of the rocks, and upon all thorns, and upon all bushes.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>In the same day shall the Lord shave with a razor that is hired, _namely_, by them beyond the river, by the king of Assyria, the head, and the hair of the feet: and it shall also consume the beard.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ a man shall nourish a young cow, and two sheep;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, for the abundance of milk _that_ they shall give he shall eat butter: for butter and honey shall every one eat that is left in the land.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ every place shall be, where there were a thousand vines at a thousand silverlings, it shall _even_ be for briers and thorns.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>With arrows and with bows shall _men_ come thither; because all the land shall become briers and thorns.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And _on_ all hills that shall be digged with the mattock, there shall not come thither the fear of briers and thorns: but it shall be for the sending forth of oxen, and for the treading of lesser cattle.  

## CHAPTER 8.

Moreover the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto me, Take thee a great roll, and write in it with a man’s pen concerning Maher–shalal–hash–baz.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And I took unto me faithful witnesses to record, Uriah the priest, and Zechariah the son of Jeberechiah.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And I went unto the prophetess; and she conceived, and bare a son. Then said the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to me, Call his name Maher–shalal–hash–baz.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For before the child shall have knowledge to cry, My father, and my mother, the riches of Damascus and the spoil of Samaria shall be taken away before the king of Assyria.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake also unto me again, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Forasmuch as this people refuseth the waters of Shiloah that go softly, and rejoice in Rezin and Remaliah’s son;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Now therefore, behold, the Lord bringeth up upon them the waters of the river, strong and many, _even_ the king of Assyria, and all his glory: and he shall come up over all his channels, and go over all his banks:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he shall pass through Judah; he shall overflow and go over, he shall reach _even_ to the neck; and the stretching out of his wings shall fill the breadth of thy land, O Immanuel.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Associate yourselves, O ye people, and ye shall be broken in pieces; and give ear, all ye of far countries: gird yourselves, and ye shall be broken in pieces; gird yourselves, and ye shall be broken in pieces.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Take counsel together, and it shall come to nought; speak the word, and it shall not stand: for God _is_ with us.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake thus to me with a strong hand, and instructed me that I should not walk in the way of this people, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Say ye not, A confederacy, to all _them to_ whom this people shall say, A confederacy; neither fear ye their fear, nor be afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Sanctify the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts himself; and _let_ him _be_ your fear, and _let_ him _be_ your dread.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he shall be for a sanctuary; but for a stone of stumbling and for a rock of offence to both the houses of Israel, for a gin and for a snare to the inhabitants of Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And many among them shall stumble, and fall, and be broken, and be snared, and be taken.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Bind up the testimony, seal the law among my disciples.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And I will wait upon the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that hideth his face from the house of Jacob, and I will look for him.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Behold, I and the children whom the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath given me _are_ for signs and for wonders in Israel from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, which dwelleth in mount Zion.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And when they shall say unto you, Seek unto them that have familiar spirits, and unto wizards that peep, and that mutter: should not a people seek unto their God? for the living to the dead?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>To the law and to the testimony: if they speak not according to this word, _it is_ because _there is_ no light in them.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And they shall pass through it, hardly bestead and hungry: and it shall come to pass, that when they shall be hungry, they shall fret themselves, and curse their king and their God, and look upward.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And they shall look unto the earth; and behold trouble and darkness, dimness of anguish; and _they shall be_ driven to darkness.  

## CHAPTER 9.

Nevertheless the dimness _shall_ not _be_ such as _was_ in her vexation, when at the first he lightly afflicted the land of Zebulun and the land of Naphtali, and afterward did more grievously afflict _her by_ the way of the sea, beyond Jordan, in Galilee of the nations.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The people that walked in darkness have seen a great light: they that dwell in the land of the shadow of death, upon them hath the light shined.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Thou hast multiplied the nation, _and_ not increased the joy: they joy before thee according to the joy in harvest, _and_ as _men_ rejoice when they divide the spoil.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For thou hast broken the yoke of his burden, and the staff of his shoulder, the rod of his oppressor, as in the day of Midian.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For every battle of the warrior _is_ with confused noise, and garments rolled in blood; but _this_ shall be with burning _and_ fuel of fire.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For unto us a child is born, unto us a son is given: and the government shall be upon his shoulder: and his name shall be called Wonderful, Counsellor, The mighty God, The everlasting Father, The Prince of Peace.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Of the increase of _his_ government and peace _there shall be_ no end, upon the throne of David, and upon his kingdom, to order it, and to establish it with judgment and with justice from henceforth even for ever. The zeal of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts will perform this.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ The Lord sent a word into Jacob, and it hath lighted upon Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And all the people shall know, _even_ Ephraim and the inhabitant of Samaria, that say in the pride and stoutness of heart,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The bricks are fallen down, but we will build with hewn stones: the sycomores are cut down, but we will change _them into_ cedars.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Therefore the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall set up the adversaries of Rezin against him, and join his enemies together;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The Syrians before, and the Philistines behind; and they shall devour Israel with open mouth. For all this his anger is not turned away, but his hand _is_ stretched out still.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ For the people turneth not unto him that smiteth them, neither do they seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will cut off from Israel head and tail, branch and rush, in one day.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The ancient and honourable, he _is_ the head; and the prophet that teacheth lies, he _is_ the tail.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For the leaders of this people cause _them_ to err; and _they that are_ led of them _are_ destroyed.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Therefore the Lord shall have no joy in their young men, neither shall have mercy on their fatherless and widows: for every one _is_ an hypocrite and an evildoer, and every mouth speaketh folly. For all this his anger is not turned away, but his hand _is_ stretched out still.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ For wickedness burneth as the fire: it shall devour the briers and thorns, and shall kindle in the thickets of the forest, and they shall mount up _like_ the lifting up of smoke.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Through the wrath of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts is the land darkened, and the people shall be as the fuel of the fire: no man shall spare his brother.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And he shall snatch on the right hand, and be hungry; and he shall eat on the left hand, and they shall not be satisfied: they shall eat every man the flesh of his own arm:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Manasseh, Ephraim; and Ephraim, Manasseh: _and_ they together _shall be_ against Judah. For all this his anger is not turned away, but his hand _is_ stretched out still.  

## CHAPTER 10.

Woe unto them that decree unrighteous decrees, and that write grievousness _which_ they have prescribed;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>To turn aside the needy from judgment, and to take away the right from the poor of my people, that widows may be their prey, and _that_ they may rob the fatherless!  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And what will ye do in the day of visitation, and in the desolation _which_ shall come from far? to whom will ye flee for help? and where will ye leave your glory?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Without me they shall bow down under the prisoners, and they shall fall under the slain. For all this his anger is not turned away, but his hand _is_ stretched out still.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ O Assyrian, the rod of mine anger, and the staff in their hand is mine indignation.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>I will send him against an hypocritical nation, and against the people of my wrath will I give him a charge, to take the spoil, and to take the prey, and to tread them down like the mire of the streets.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Howbeit he meaneth not so, neither doth his heart think so; but _it is_ in his heart to destroy and cut off nations not a few.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For he saith, _Are_ not my princes altogether kings?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_Is_ not Calno as Carchemish? _is_ not Hamath as Arpad? _is_ not Samaria as Damascus?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>As my hand hath found the kingdoms of the idols, and whose graven images did excel them of Jerusalem and of Samaria;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Shall I not, as I have done unto Samaria and her idols, so do to Jerusalem and her idols?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Wherefore it shall come to pass, _that_ when the Lord hath performed his whole work upon mount Zion and on Jerusalem, I will punish the fruit of the stout heart of the king of Assyria, and the glory of his high looks.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For he saith, By the strength of my hand I have done _it_, and by my wisdom; for I am prudent: and I have removed the bounds of the people, and have robbed their treasures, and I have put down the inhabitants like a valiant _man_:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And my hand hath found as a nest the riches of the people: and as one gathereth eggs _that are_ left, have I gathered all the earth; and there was none that moved the wing, or opened the mouth, or peeped.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Shall the axe boast itself against him that heweth therewith? _or_ shall the saw magnify itself against him that shaketh it? as if the rod should shake _itself_ against them that lift it up, _or_ as if the staff should lift up _itself, as if it were_ no wood.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Therefore shall the Lord, the Lord of hosts, send among his fat ones leanness; and under his glory he shall kindle a burning like the burning of a fire.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the light of Israel shall be for a fire, and his Holy One for a flame: and it shall burn and devour his thorns and his briers in one day;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And shall consume the glory of his forest, and of his fruitful field, both soul and body: and they shall be as when a standardbearer fainteth.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the rest of the trees of his forest shall be few, that a child may write them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ the remnant of Israel, and such as are escaped of the house of Jacob, shall no more again stay upon him that smote them; but shall stay upon the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the Holy One of Israel, in truth.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>The remnant shall return, _even_ the remnant of Jacob, unto the mighty God.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For though thy people Israel be as the sand of the sea, _yet_ a remnant of them shall return: the consumption decreed shall overflow with righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of hosts shall make a consumption, even determined, in the midst of all the land.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of hosts, O my people that dwellest in Zion, be not afraid of the Assyrian: he shall smite thee with a rod, and shall lift up his staff against thee, after the manner of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For yet a very little while, and the indignation shall cease, and mine anger in their destruction.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts shall stir up a scourge for him according to the slaughter of Midian at the rock of Oreb: and _as_ his rod _was_ upon the sea, so shall he lift it up after the manner of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ his burden shall be taken away from off thy shoulder, and his yoke from off thy neck, and the yoke shall be destroyed because of the anointing.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>He is come to Aiath, he is passed to Migron; at Michmash he hath laid up his carriages:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>They are gone over the passage: they have taken up their lodging at Geba; Ramah is afraid; Gibeah of Saul is fled.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Lift up thy voice, O daughter of Gallim: cause it to be heard unto Laish, O poor Anathoth.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Madmenah is removed; the inhabitants of Gebim gather themselves to flee.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>As yet shall he remain at Nob that day: he shall shake his hand _against_ the mount of the daughter of Zion, the hill of Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Behold, the Lord, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, shall lop the bough with terror: and the high ones of stature _shall be_ hewn down, and the haughty shall be humbled.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And he shall cut down the thickets of the forest with iron, and Lebanon shall fall by a mighty one.  

## CHAPTER 11.

And there shall come forth a rod out of the stem of Jesse, and a Branch shall grow out of his roots:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall rest upon him, the spirit of wisdom and understanding, the spirit of counsel and might, the spirit of knowledge and of the fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And shall make him of quick understanding in the fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and he shall not judge after the sight of his eyes, neither reprove after the hearing of his ears:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But with righteousness shall he judge the poor, and reprove with equity for the meek of the earth: and he shall smite the earth with the rod of his mouth, and with the breath of his lips shall he slay the wicked.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And righteousness shall be the girdle of his loins, and faithfulness the girdle of his reins.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The wolf also shall dwell with the lamb, and the leopard shall lie down with the kid; and the calf and the young lion and the fatling together; and a little child shall lead them.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the cow and the bear shall feed; their young ones shall lie down together: and the lion shall eat straw like the ox.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the sucking child shall play on the hole of the asp, and the weaned child shall put his hand on the cockatrice’ den.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>They shall not hurt nor destroy in all my holy mountain: for the earth shall be full of the knowledge of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as the waters cover the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And in that day there shall be a root of Jesse, which shall stand for an ensign of the people; to it shall the Gentiles seek: and his rest shall be glorious.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ the Lord shall set his hand again the second time to recover the remnant of his people, which shall be left, from Assyria, and from Egypt, and from Pathros, and from Cush, and from Elam, and from Shinar, and from Hamath, and from the islands of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he shall set up an ensign for the nations, and shall assemble the outcasts of Israel, and gather together the dispersed of Judah from the four corners of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The envy also of Ephraim shall depart, and the adversaries of Judah shall be cut off: Ephraim shall not envy Judah, and Judah shall not vex Ephraim.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But they shall fly upon the shoulders of the Philistines toward the west; they shall spoil them of the east together: they shall lay their hand upon Edom and Moab; and the children of Ammon shall obey them.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall utterly destroy the tongue of the Egyptian sea; and with his mighty wind shall he shake his hand over the river, and shall smite it in the seven streams, and make _men_ go over dryshod.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And there shall be an highway for the remnant of his people, which shall be left, from Assyria; like as it was to Israel in the day that he came up out of the land of Egypt.  

## CHAPTER 12.

And in that day thou shalt say, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I will praise thee: though thou wast angry with me, thine anger is turned away, and thou comfortedst me.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Behold, God _is_ my salvation; I will trust, and not be afraid: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> <span class="small-caps">Jehovah</span> _is_ my strength and _my_ song; he also is become my salvation.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore with joy shall ye draw water out of the wells of salvation.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And in that day shall ye say, Praise the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, call upon his name, declare his doings among the people, make mention that his name is exalted.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Sing unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; for he hath done excellent things: this _is_ known in all the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Cry out and shout, thou inhabitant of Zion: for great _is_ the Holy One of Israel in the midst of thee.  

## CHAPTER 13.

The burden of Babylon, which Isaiah the son of Amoz did see.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Lift ye up a banner upon the high mountain, exalt the voice unto them, shake the hand, that they may go into the gates of the nobles.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I have commanded my sanctified ones, I have also called my mighty ones for mine anger, _even_ them that rejoice in my highness.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The noise of a multitude in the mountains, like as of a great people; a tumultuous noise of the kingdoms of nations gathered together: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts mustereth the host of the battle.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>They come from a far country, from the end of heaven, _even_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and the weapons of his indignation, to destroy the whole land.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Howl ye; for the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ at hand; it shall come as a destruction from the Almighty.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore shall all hands be faint, and every man’s heart shall melt:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they shall be afraid: pangs and sorrows shall take hold of them; they shall be in pain as a woman that travaileth: they shall be amazed one at another; their faces _shall be as_ flames.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Behold, the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> cometh, cruel both with wrath and fierce anger, to lay the land desolate: and he shall destroy the sinners thereof out of it.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For the stars of heaven and the constellations thereof shall not give their light: the sun shall be darkened in his going forth, and the moon shall not cause her light to shine.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And I will punish the world for _their_ evil, and the wicked for their iniquity; and I will cause the arrogancy of the proud to cease, and will lay low the haughtiness of the terrible.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I will make a man more precious than fine gold; even a man than the golden wedge of Ophir.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Therefore I will shake the heavens, and the earth shall remove out of her place, in the wrath of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, and in the day of his fierce anger.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And it shall be as the chased roe, and as a sheep that no man taketh up: they shall every man turn to his own people, and flee every one into his own land.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Every one that is found shall be thrust through; and every one that is joined _unto them_ shall fall by the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Their children also shall be dashed to pieces before their eyes; their houses shall be spoiled, and their wives ravished.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Behold, I will stir up the Medes against them, which shall not regard silver; and _as for_ gold, they shall not delight in it.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>_Their_ bows also shall dash the young men to pieces; and they shall have no pity on the fruit of the womb; their eye shall not spare children.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And Babylon, the glory of kingdoms, the beauty of the Chaldees’ excellency, shall be as when God overthrew Sodom and Gomorrah.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>It shall never be inhabited, neither shall it be dwelt in from generation to generation: neither shall the Arabian pitch tent there; neither shall the shepherds make their fold there.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But wild beasts of the desert shall lie there; and their houses shall be full of doleful creatures; and owls shall dwell there, and satyrs shall dance there.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the wild beasts of the islands shall cry in their desolate houses, and dragons in _their_ pleasant palaces: and her time _is_ near to come, and her days shall not be prolonged.  

## CHAPTER 14.

For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will have mercy on Jacob, and will yet choose Israel, and set them in their own land: and the strangers shall be joined with them, and they shall cleave to the house of Jacob.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the people shall take them, and bring them to their place: and the house of Israel shall possess them in the land of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> for servants and handmaids: and they shall take them captives, whose captives they were; and they shall rule over their oppressors.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in the day that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall give thee rest from thy sorrow, and from thy fear, and from the hard bondage wherein thou wast made to serve,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ That thou shalt take up this proverb against the king of Babylon, and say, How hath the oppressor ceased! the golden city ceased!  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath broken the staff of the wicked, _and_ the sceptre of the rulers.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>He who smote the people in wrath with a continual stroke, he that ruled the nations in anger, is persecuted, _and_ none hindereth.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The whole earth is at rest, _and_ is quiet: they break forth into singing.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Yea, the fir trees rejoice at thee, _and_ the cedars of Lebanon, _saying_, Since thou art laid down, no feller is come up against us.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Hell from beneath is moved for thee to meet _thee_ at thy coming: it stirreth up the dead for thee, _even_ all the chief ones of the earth; it hath raised up from their thrones all the kings of the nations.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>All they shall speak and say unto thee, Art thou also become weak as we? art thou become like unto us?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Thy pomp is brought down to the grave, _and_ the noise of thy viols: the worm is spread under thee, and the worms cover thee.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>How art thou fallen from heaven, O Lucifer, son of the morning! _how_ art thou cut down to the ground, which didst weaken the nations!  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For thou hast said in thine heart, I will ascend into heaven, I will exalt my throne above the stars of God: I will sit also upon the mount of the congregation, in the sides of the north:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I will ascend above the heights of the clouds; I will be like the most High.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Yet thou shalt be brought down to hell, to the sides of the pit.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>They that see thee shall narrowly look upon thee, _and_ consider thee, _saying, Is_ this the man that made the earth to tremble, that did shake kingdoms;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>_That_ made the world as a wilderness, and destroyed the cities thereof; _that_ opened not the house of his prisoners?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>All the kings of the nations, _even_ all of them, lie in glory, every one in his own house.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But thou art cast out of thy grave like an abominable branch, _and as_ the raiment of those that are slain, thrust through with a sword, that go down to the stones of the pit; as a carcase trodden under feet.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not be joined with them in burial, because thou hast destroyed thy land, _and_ slain thy people: the seed of evildoers shall never be renowned.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Prepare slaughter for his children for the iniquity of their fathers; that they do not rise, nor possess the land, nor fill the face of the world with cities.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For I will rise up against them, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, and cut off from Babylon the name, and remnant, and son, and nephew, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>I will also make it a possession for the bittern, and pools of water: and I will sweep it with the besom of destruction, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts hath sworn, saying, Surely as I have thought, so shall it come to pass; and as I have purposed, _so_ shall it stand:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>That I will break the Assyrian in my land, and upon my mountains tread him under foot: then shall his yoke depart from off them, and his burden depart from off their shoulders.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>This _is_ the purpose that is purposed upon the whole earth: and this _is_ the hand that is stretched out upon all the nations.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts hath purposed, and who shall disannul _it_? and his hand _is_ stretched out, and who shall turn it back?  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>In the year that king Ahaz died was this burden.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ Rejoice not thou, whole Palestina, because the rod of him that smote thee is broken: for out of the serpent’s root shall come forth a cockatrice, and his fruit _shall be_ a fiery flying serpent.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the firstborn of the poor shall feed, and the needy shall lie down in safety: and I will kill thy root with famine, and he shall slay thy remnant.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Howl, O gate; cry, O city; thou, whole Palestina, _art_ dissolved: for there shall come from the north a smoke, and none _shall be_ alone in his appointed times.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>What shall _one_ then answer the messengers of the nation? That the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath founded Zion, and the poor of his people shall trust in it.  

## CHAPTER 15.

The burden of Moab. Because in the night Ar of Moab is laid waste, _and_ brought to silence; because in the night Kir of Moab is laid waste, _and_ brought to silence;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>He is gone up to Bajith, and to Dibon, the high places, to weep: Moab shall howl over Nebo, and over Medeba: on all their heads _shall be_ baldness, _and_ every beard cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>In their streets they shall gird themselves with sackcloth: on the tops of their houses, and in their streets, every one shall howl, weeping abundantly.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Heshbon shall cry, and Elealeh: their voice shall be heard _even_ unto Jahaz: therefore the armed soldiers of Moab shall cry out; his life shall be grievous unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>My heart shall cry out for Moab; his fugitives _shall flee_ unto Zoar, an heifer of three years old: for by the mounting up of Luhith with weeping shall they go it up; for in the way of Horonaim they shall raise up a cry of destruction.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ For the waters of Nimrim shall be desolate: for the hay is withered away, the grass faileth, there is no green thing.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore the abundance they have gotten, and that which they have laid up, shall they carry away to the brook of the willows.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For the cry is gone round about the borders of Moab; the howling thereof unto Eglaim, and the howling thereof unto Beer–elim.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For the waters of Dimon shall be full of blood: for I will bring more upon Dimon, lions upon him that escapeth of Moab, and upon the remnant of the land.  

## CHAPTER 16.

Send ye the lamb to the ruler of the land from Sela to the wilderness, unto the mount of the daughter of Zion.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For it shall be, _that_, as a wandering bird cast out of the nest, _so_ the daughters of Moab shall be at the fords of Arnon.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Take counsel, execute judgment; make thy shadow as the night in the midst of the noonday; hide the outcasts; bewray not him that wandereth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Let mine outcasts dwell with thee, Moab; be thou a covert to them from the face of the spoiler: for the extortioner is at an end, the spoiler ceaseth, the oppressors are consumed out of the land.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And in mercy shall the throne be established: and he shall sit upon it in truth in the tabernacle of David, judging, and seeking judgment, and hasting righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ We have heard of the pride of Moab; _he is_ very proud: _even_ of his haughtiness, and his pride, and his wrath: _but_ his lies _shall_ not _be_ so.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore shall Moab howl for Moab, every one shall howl: for the foundations of Kir–hareseth shall ye mourn; surely _they are_ stricken.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For the fields of Heshbon languish, _and_ the vine of Sibmah: the lords of the heathen have broken down the principal plants thereof, they are come _even_ unto Jazer, they wandered _through_ the wilderness: her branches are stretched out, they are gone over the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore I will bewail with the weeping of Jazer the vine of Sibmah: I will water thee with my tears, O Heshbon, and Elealeh: for the shouting for thy summer fruits and for thy harvest is fallen.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And gladness is taken away, and joy out of the plentiful field; and in the vineyards there shall be no singing, neither shall there be shouting: the treaders shall tread out no wine in _their_ presses; I have made _their vintage_ shouting to cease.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Wherefore my bowels shall sound like an harp for Moab, and mine inward parts for Kir–haresh.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass, when it is seen that Moab is weary on the high place, that he shall come to his sanctuary to pray; but he shall not prevail.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>This _is_ the word that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken concerning Moab since that time.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But now the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken, saying, Within three years, as the years of an hireling, and the glory of Moab shall be contemned, with all that great multitude; and the remnant _shall be_ very small _and_ feeble.  

## CHAPTER 17.

The burden of Damascus. Behold, Damascus is taken away from _being_ a city, and it shall be a ruinous heap.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The cities of Aroer _are_ forsaken: they shall be for flocks, which shall lie down, and none shall make _them_ afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The fortress also shall cease from Ephraim, and the kingdom from Damascus, and the remnant of Syria: they shall be as the glory of the children of Israel, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And in that day it shall come to pass, _that_ the glory of Jacob shall be made thin, and the fatness of his flesh shall wax lean.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And it shall be as when the harvestman gathereth the corn, and reapeth the ears with his arm; and it shall be as he that gathereth ears in the valley of Rephaim.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Yet gleaning grapes shall be left in it, as the shaking of an olive tree, two _or_ three berries in the top of the uppermost bough, four _or_ five in the outmost fruitful branches thereof, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>At that day shall a man look to his Maker, and his eyes shall have respect to the Holy One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he shall not look to the altars, the work of his hands, neither shall respect _that_ which his fingers have made, either the groves, or the images.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ In that day shall his strong cities be as a forsaken bough, and an uppermost branch, which they left because of the children of Israel: and there shall be desolation.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Because thou hast forgotten the God of thy salvation, and hast not been mindful of the rock of thy strength, therefore shalt thou plant pleasant plants, and shalt set it with strange slips:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>In the day shalt thou make thy plant to grow, and in the morning shalt thou make thy seed to flourish: _but_ the harvest _shall be_ a heap in the day of grief and of desperate sorrow.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Woe to the multitude of many people, _which_ make a noise like the noise of the seas; and to the rushing of nations, _that_ make a rushing like the rushing of mighty waters!  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The nations shall rush like the rushing of many waters: but _God_ shall rebuke them, and they shall flee far off, and shall be chased as the chaff of the mountains before the wind, and like a rolling thing before the whirlwind.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And behold at eveningtide trouble; _and_ before the morning he _is_ not. This _is_ the portion of them that spoil us, and the lot of them that rob us.  

## CHAPTER 18.

Woe to the land shadowing with wings, which _is_ beyond the rivers of Ethiopia:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That sendeth ambassadors by the sea, even in vessels of bulrushes upon the waters, _saying_, Go, ye swift messengers, to a nation scattered and peeled, to a people terrible from their beginning hitherto; a nation meted out and trodden down, whose land the rivers have spoiled!  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>All ye inhabitants of the world, and dwellers on the earth, see ye, when he lifteth up an ensign on the mountains; and when he bloweth a trumpet, hear ye.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For so the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto me, I will take my rest, and I will consider in my dwelling place like a clear heat upon herbs, _and_ like a cloud of dew in the heat of harvest.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For afore the harvest, when the bud is perfect, and the sour grape is ripening in the flower, he shall both cut off the sprigs with pruning hooks, and take away _and_ cut down the branches.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>They shall be left together unto the fowls of the mountains, and to the beasts of the earth: and the fowls shall summer upon them, and all the beasts of the earth shall winter upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ In that time shall the present be brought unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts of a people scattered and peeled, and from a people terrible from their beginning hitherto; a nation meted out and trodden under foot, whose land the rivers have spoiled, to the place of the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, the mount Zion.  

## CHAPTER 19.

The burden of Egypt. Behold, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> rideth upon a swift cloud, and shall come into Egypt: and the idols of Egypt shall be moved at his presence, and the heart of Egypt shall melt in the midst of it.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And I will set the Egyptians against the Egyptians: and they shall fight every one against his brother, and every one against his neighbour; city against city, _and_ kingdom against kingdom.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the spirit of Egypt shall fail in the midst thereof; and I will destroy the counsel thereof: and they shall seek to the idols, and to the charmers, and to them that have familiar spirits, and to the wizards.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the Egyptians will I give over into the hand of a cruel lord; and a fierce king shall rule over them, saith the Lord, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the waters shall fail from the sea, and the river shall be wasted and dried up.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they shall turn the rivers far away; _and_ the brooks of defence shall be emptied and dried up: the reeds and flags shall wither.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The paper reeds by the brooks, by the mouth of the brooks, and every thing sown by the brooks, shall wither, be driven away, and be no _more_.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The fishers also shall mourn, and all they that cast angle into the brooks shall lament, and they that spread nets upon the waters shall languish.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Moreover they that work in fine flax, and they that weave networks, shall be confounded.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they shall be broken in the purposes thereof, all that make sluices _and_ ponds for fish.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Surely the princes of Zoan _are_ fools, the counsel of the wise counsellors of Pharaoh is become brutish: how say ye unto Pharaoh, I _am_ the son of the wise, the son of ancient kings?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Where _are_ they? where _are_ thy wise _men_? and let them tell thee now, and let them know what the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts hath purposed upon Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The princes of Zoan are become fools, the princes of Noph are deceived; they have also seduced Egypt, _even they that are_ the stay of the tribes thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath mingled a perverse spirit in the midst thereof: and they have caused Egypt to err in every work thereof, as a drunken _man_ staggereth in his vomit.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Neither shall there be _any_ work for Egypt, which the head or tail, branch or rush, may do.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>In that day shall Egypt be like unto women: and it shall be afraid and fear because of the shaking of the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, which he shaketh over it.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the land of Judah shall be a terror unto Egypt, every one that maketh mention thereof shall be afraid in himself, because of the counsel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, which he hath determined against it.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ In that day shall five cities in the land of Egypt speak the language of Canaan, and swear to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts; one shall be called, The city of destruction.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>In that day shall there be an altar to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the midst of the land of Egypt, and a pillar at the border thereof to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And it shall be for a sign and for a witness unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts in the land of Egypt: for they shall cry unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> because of the oppressors, and he shall send them a saviour, and a great one, and he shall deliver them.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be known to Egypt, and the Egyptians shall know the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in that day, and shall do sacrifice and oblation; yea, they shall vow a vow unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and perform _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall smite Egypt: he shall smite and heal _it_: and they shall return _even_ to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and he shall be intreated of them, and shall heal them.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ In that day shall there be a highway out of Egypt to Assyria, and the Assyrian shall come into Egypt, and the Egyptian into Assyria, and the Egyptians shall serve with the Assyrians.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>In that day shall Israel be the third with Egypt and with Assyria, _even_ a blessing in the midst of the land:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Whom the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts shall bless, saying, Blessed _be_ Egypt my people, and Assyria the work of my hands, and Israel mine inheritance.  

## CHAPTER 20.

In the year that Tartan came unto Ashdod, (when Sargon the king of Assyria sent him,) and fought against Ashdod, and took it;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>At the same time spake the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> by Isaiah the son of Amoz, saying, Go and loose the sackcloth from off thy loins, and put off thy shoe from thy foot. And he did so, walking naked and barefoot.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said, Like as my servant Isaiah hath walked naked and barefoot three years _for_ a sign and wonder upon Egypt and upon Ethiopia;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>So shall the king of Assyria lead away the Egyptians prisoners, and the Ethiopians captives, young and old, naked and barefoot, even with _their_ buttocks uncovered, to the shame of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they shall be afraid and ashamed of Ethiopia their expectation, and of Egypt their glory.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the inhabitant of this isle shall say in that day, Behold, such _is_ our expectation, whither we flee for help to be delivered from the king of Assyria: and how shall we escape?  

## CHAPTER 21.

The burden of the desert of the sea. As whirlwinds in the south pass through; _so_ it cometh from the desert, from a terrible land.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>A grievous vision is declared unto me; the treacherous dealer dealeth treacherously, and the spoiler spoileth. Go up, O Elam: besiege, O Media; all the sighing thereof have I made to cease.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore are my loins filled with pain: pangs have taken hold upon me, as the pangs of a woman that travaileth: I was bowed down at the hearing _of it_; I was dismayed at the seeing _of it_.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>My heart panted, fearfulness affrighted me: the night of my pleasure hath he turned into fear unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Prepare the table, watch in the watchtower, eat, drink: arise, ye princes, _and_ anoint the shield.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For thus hath the Lord said unto me, Go, set a watchman, let him declare what he seeth.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he saw a chariot _with_ a couple of horsemen, a chariot of asses, _and_ a chariot of camels; and he hearkened diligently with much heed:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he cried, A lion: My lord, I stand continually upon the watchtower in the daytime, and I am set in my ward whole nights:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And, behold, here cometh a chariot of men, _with_ a couple of horsemen. And he answered and said, Babylon is fallen, is fallen; and all the graven images of her gods he hath broken unto the ground.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>O my threshing, and the corn of my floor: that which I have heard of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, the God of Israel, have I declared unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ The burden of Dumah. He calleth to me out of Seir, Watchman, what of the night? Watchman, what of the night?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The watchman said, The morning cometh, and also the night: if ye will enquire, enquire ye: return, come.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ The burden upon Arabia. In the forest in Arabia shall ye lodge, O ye travelling companies of Dedanim.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The inhabitants of the land of Tema brought water to him that was thirsty, they prevented with their bread him that fled.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For they fled from the swords, from the drawn sword, and from the bent bow, and from the grievousness of war.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For thus hath the Lord said unto me, Within a year, according to the years of an hireling, and all the glory of Kedar shall fail:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the residue of the number of archers, the mighty men of the children of Kedar, shall be diminished: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel hath spoken _it_.  

## CHAPTER 22.

The burden of the valley of vision. What aileth thee now, that thou art wholly gone up to the housetops?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Thou that art full of stirs, a tumultuous city, a joyous city: thy slain _men are_ not slain with the sword, nor dead in battle.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>All thy rulers are fled together, they are bound by the archers: all that are found in thee are bound together, _which_ have fled from far.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Therefore said I, Look away from me; I will weep bitterly, labour not to comfort me, because of the spoiling of the daughter of my people.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For _it is_ a day of trouble, and of treading down, and of perplexity by the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of hosts in the valley of vision, breaking down the walls, and of crying to the mountains.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And Elam bare the quiver with chariots of men _and_ horsemen, and Kir uncovered the shield.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ thy choicest valleys shall be full of chariots, and the horsemen shall set themselves in array at the gate.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And he discovered the covering of Judah, and thou didst look in that day to the armour of the house of the forest.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Ye have seen also the breaches of the city of David, that they are many: and ye gathered together the waters of the lower pool.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And ye have numbered the houses of Jerusalem, and the houses have ye broken down to fortify the wall.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Ye made also a ditch between the two walls for the water of the old pool: but ye have not looked unto the maker thereof, neither had respect unto him that fashioned it long ago.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And in that day did the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of hosts call to weeping, and to mourning, and to baldness, and to girding with sackcloth:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And behold joy and gladness, slaying oxen, and killing sheep, eating flesh, and drinking wine: let us eat and drink; for to morrow we shall die.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And it was revealed in mine ears by the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, Surely this iniquity shall not be purged from you till ye die, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of hosts, Go, get thee unto this treasurer, _even_ unto Shebna, which _is_ over the house, _and say_,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>What hast thou here? and whom hast thou here, that thou hast hewed thee out a sepulchre here, _as_ he that heweth him out a sepulchre on high, _and_ that graveth an habitation for himself in a rock?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Behold, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will carry thee away with a mighty captivity, and will surely cover thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>He will surely violently turn and toss thee _like_ a ball into a large country: there shalt thou die, and there the chariots of thy glory _shall be_ the shame of thy lord’s house.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And I will drive thee from thy station, and from thy state shall he pull thee down.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass in that day, that I will call my servant Eliakim the son of Hilkiah:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And I will clothe him with thy robe, and strengthen him with thy girdle, and I will commit thy government into his hand: and he shall be a father to the inhabitants of Jerusalem, and to the house of Judah.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the key of the house of David will I lay upon his shoulder; so he shall open, and none shall shut; and he shall shut, and none shall open.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And I will fasten him _as_ a nail in a sure place; and he shall be for a glorious throne to his father’s house.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they shall hang upon him all the glory of his father’s house, the offspring and the issue, all vessels of small quantity, from the vessels of cups, even to all the vessels of flagons.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>In that day, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, shall the nail that is fastened in the sure place be removed, and be cut down, and fall; and the burden that _was_ upon it shall be cut off: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken _it_.  

## CHAPTER 23.

The burden of Tyre. Howl, ye ships of Tarshish; for it is laid waste, so that there is no house, no entering in: from the land of Chittim it is revealed to them.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Be still, ye inhabitants of the isle; thou whom the merchants of Zidon, that pass over the sea, have replenished.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And by great waters the seed of Sihor, the harvest of the river, _is_ her revenue; and she is a mart of nations.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Be thou ashamed, O Zidon: for the sea hath spoken, _even_ the strength of the sea, saying, I travail not, nor bring forth children, neither do I nourish up young men, _nor_ bring up virgins.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>As at the report concerning Egypt, _so_ shall they be sorely pained at the report of Tyre.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Pass ye over to Tarshish; howl, ye inhabitants of the isle.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_Is_ this your joyous _city_, whose antiquity _is_ of ancient days? her own feet shall carry her afar off to sojourn.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Who hath taken this counsel against Tyre, the crowning _city_, whose merchants _are_ princes, whose traffickers _are_ the honourable of the earth?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts hath purposed it, to stain the pride of all glory, _and_ to bring into contempt all the honourable of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Pass through thy land as a river, O daughter of Tarshish: _there is_ no more strength.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He stretched out his hand over the sea, he shook the kingdoms: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath given a commandment against the merchant _city_, to destroy the strong holds thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he said, Thou shalt no more rejoice, O thou oppressed virgin, daughter of Zidon: arise, pass over to Chittim; there also shalt thou have no rest.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Behold the land of the Chaldeans; this people was not, _till_ the Assyrian founded it for them that dwell in the wilderness: they set up the towers thereof, they raised up the palaces thereof; _and_ he brought it to ruin.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Howl, ye ships of Tarshish: for your strength is laid waste.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass in that day, that Tyre shall be forgotten seventy years, according to the days of one king: after the end of seventy years shall Tyre sing as an harlot.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Take an harp, go about the city, thou harlot that hast been forgotten; make sweet melody, sing many songs, that thou mayest be remembered.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass after the end of seventy years, that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will visit Tyre, and she shall turn to her hire, and shall commit fornication with all the kingdoms of the world upon the face of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And her merchandise and her hire shall be holiness to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: it shall not be treasured nor laid up; for her merchandise shall be for them that dwell before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to eat sufficiently, and for durable clothing.  

## CHAPTER 24.

Behold, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> maketh the earth empty, and maketh it waste, and turneth it upside down, and scattereth abroad the inhabitants thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And it shall be, as with the people, so with the priest; as with the servant, so with his master; as with the maid, so with her mistress; as with the buyer, so with the seller; as with the lender, so with the borrower; as with the taker of usury, so with the giver of usury to him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The land shall be utterly emptied, and utterly spoiled: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken this word.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The earth mourneth _and_ fadeth away, the world languisheth _and_ fadeth away, the haughty people of the earth do languish.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The earth also is defiled under the inhabitants thereof; because they have transgressed the laws, changed the ordinance, broken the everlasting covenant.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Therefore hath the curse devoured the earth, and they that dwell therein are desolate: therefore the inhabitants of the earth are burned, and few men left.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The new wine mourneth, the vine languisheth, all the merryhearted do sigh.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The mirth of tabrets ceaseth, the noise of them that rejoice endeth, the joy of the harp ceaseth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>They shall not drink wine with a song; strong drink shall be bitter to them that drink it.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The city of confusion is broken down: every house is shut up, that no man may come in.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_There is_ a crying for wine in the streets; all joy is darkened, the mirth of the land is gone.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>In the city is left desolation, and the gate is smitten with destruction.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ When thus it shall be in the midst of the land among the people, _there shall be_ as the shaking of an olive tree, _and_ as the gleaning grapes when the vintage is done.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>They shall lift up their voice, they shall sing for the majesty of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, they shall cry aloud from the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Wherefore glorify ye the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the fires, _even_ the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel in the isles of the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ From the uttermost part of the earth have we heard songs, _even_ glory to the righteous. But I said, My leanness, my leanness, woe unto me! the treacherous dealers have dealt treacherously; yea, the treacherous dealers have dealt very treacherously.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Fear, and the pit, and the snare, _are_ upon thee, O inhabitant of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ he who fleeth from the noise of the fear shall fall into the pit; and he that cometh up out of the midst of the pit shall be taken in the snare: for the windows from on high are open, and the foundations of the earth do shake.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The earth is utterly broken down, the earth is clean dissolved, the earth is moved exceedingly.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>The earth shall reel to and fro like a drunkard, and shall be removed like a cottage; and the transgression thereof shall be heavy upon it; and it shall fall, and not rise again.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall punish the host of the high ones _that are_ on high, and the kings of the earth upon the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And they shall be gathered together, _as_ prisoners are gathered in the pit, and shall be shut up in the prison, and after many days shall they be visited.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Then the moon shall be confounded, and the sun ashamed, when the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts shall reign in mount Zion, and in Jerusalem, and before his ancients gloriously.  

## CHAPTER 25.

O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou _art_ my God; I will exalt thee, I will praise thy name; for thou hast done wonderful _things; thy_ counsels of old _are_ faithfulness _and_ truth.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For thou hast made of a city an heap; _of_ a defenced city a ruin: a palace of strangers to be no city; it shall never be built.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore shall the strong people glorify thee, the city of the terrible nations shall fear thee.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For thou hast been a strength to the poor, a strength to the needy in his distress, a refuge from the storm, a shadow from the heat, when the blast of the terrible ones _is_ as a storm _against_ the wall.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Thou shalt bring down the noise of strangers, as the heat in a dry place; _even_ the heat with the shadow of a cloud: the branch of the terrible ones shall be brought low.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And in this mountain shall the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts make unto all people a feast of fat things, a feast of wines on the lees, of fat things full of marrow, of wines on the lees well refined.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he will destroy in this mountain the face of the covering cast over all people, and the vail that is spread over all nations.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He will swallow up death in victory; and the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> will wipe away tears from off all faces; and the rebuke of his people shall he take away from off all the earth: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall be said in that day, Lo, this _is_ our God; we have waited for him, and he will save us: this _is_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; we have waited for him, we will be glad and rejoice in his salvation.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For in this mountain shall the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> rest, and Moab shall be trodden down under him, even as straw is trodden down for the dunghill.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And he shall spread forth his hands in the midst of them, as he that swimmeth spreadeth forth _his hands_ to swim: and he shall bring down their pride together with the spoils of their hands.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the fortress of the high fort of thy walls shall he bring down, lay low, _and_ bring to the ground, _even_ to the dust.  

## CHAPTER 26.

In that day shall this song be sung in the land of Judah; We have a strong city; salvation will _God_ appoint _for_ walls and bulwarks.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Open ye the gates, that the righteous nation which keepeth the truth may enter in.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Thou wilt keep _him_ in perfect peace, _whose_ mind _is_ stayed _on thee_: because he trusteth in thee.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Trust ye in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> for ever: for in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> <span class="small-caps">Jehovah</span> _is_ everlasting strength:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ For he bringeth down them that dwell on high; the lofty city, he layeth it low; he layeth it low, _even_ to the ground; he bringeth it _even_ to the dust.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The foot shall tread it down, _even_ the feet of the poor, _and_ the steps of the needy.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The way of the just _is_ uprightness: thou, most upright, dost weigh the path of the just.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Yea, in the way of thy judgments, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, have we waited for thee; the desire of _our_ soul _is_ to thy name, and to the remembrance of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>With my soul have I desired thee in the night; yea, with my spirit within me will I seek thee early: for when thy judgments _are_ in the earth, the inhabitants of the world will learn righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Let favour be shewed to the wicked, _yet_ will he not learn righteousness: in the land of uprightness will he deal unjustly, and will not behold the majesty of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span><span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _when_ thy hand is lifted up, they will not see: _but_ they shall see, and be ashamed for _their_ envy at the people; yea, the fire of thine enemies shall devour them.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou wilt ordain peace for us: for thou also hast wrought all our works in us.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God, _other_ lords beside thee have had dominion over us: _but_ by thee only will we make mention of thy name.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>_They are_ dead, they shall not live; _they are_ deceased, they shall not rise: therefore hast thou visited and destroyed them, and made all their memory to perish.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thou hast increased the nation, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou hast increased the nation: thou art glorified: thou hadst removed _it_ far _unto_ all the ends of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span><span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, in trouble have they visited thee, they poured out a prayer _when_ thy chastening _was_ upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Like as a woman with child, _that_ draweth near the time of her delivery, is in pain, _and_ crieth out in her pangs; so have we been in thy sight, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>We have been with child, we have been in pain, we have as it were brought forth wind; we have not wrought any deliverance in the earth; neither have the inhabitants of the world fallen.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Thy dead _men_ shall live, _together with_ my dead body shall they arise. Awake and sing, ye that dwell in dust: for thy dew _is as_ the dew of herbs, and the earth shall cast out the dead.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Come, my people, enter thou into thy chambers, and shut thy doors about thee: hide thyself as it were for a little moment, until the indignation be overpast.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For, behold, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> cometh out of his place to punish the inhabitants of the earth for their iniquity: the earth also shall disclose her blood, and shall no more cover her slain.  

## CHAPTER 27.

In that day the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> with his sore and great and strong sword shall punish leviathan the piercing serpent, even leviathan that crooked serpent; and he shall slay the dragon that _is_ in the sea.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>In that day sing ye unto her, A vineyard of red wine.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> do keep it; I will water it every moment: lest _any_ hurt it, I will keep it night and day.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Fury _is_ not in me: who would set the briers _and_ thorns against me in battle? I would go through them, I would burn them together.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Or let him take hold of my strength, _that_ he may make peace with me; _and_ he shall make peace with me.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>He shall cause them that come of Jacob to take root: Israel shall blossom and bud, and fill the face of the world with fruit.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Hath he smitten him, as he smote those that smote him? _or_ is he slain according to the slaughter of them that are slain by him?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>In measure, when it shooteth forth, thou wilt debate with it: he stayeth his rough wind in the day of the east wind.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>By this therefore shall the iniquity of Jacob be purged; and this _is_ all the fruit to take away his sin; when he maketh all the stones of the altar as chalkstones that are beaten in sunder, the groves and images shall not stand up.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Yet the defenced city _shall be_ desolate, _and_ the habitation forsaken, and left like a wilderness: there shall the calf feed, and there shall he lie down, and consume the branches thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>When the boughs thereof are withered, they shall be broken off: the women come, _and_ set them on fire: for it _is_ a people of no understanding: therefore he that made them will not have mercy on them, and he that formed them will shew them no favour.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall beat off from the channel of the river unto the stream of Egypt, and ye shall be gathered one by one, O ye children of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ the great trumpet shall be blown, and they shall come which were ready to perish in the land of Assyria, and the outcasts in the land of Egypt, and shall worship the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the holy mount at Jerusalem.  

## CHAPTER 28.

Woe to the crown of pride, to the drunkards of Ephraim, whose glorious beauty _is_ a fading flower, which _are_ on the head of the fat valleys of them that are overcome with wine!  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Behold, the Lord hath a mighty and strong one, _which_ as a tempest of hail _and_ a destroying storm, as a flood of mighty waters overflowing, shall cast down to the earth with the hand.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The crown of pride, the drunkards of Ephraim, shall be trodden under feet:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the glorious beauty, which _is_ on the head of the fat valley, shall be a fading flower, _and_ as the hasty fruit before the summer; which _when_ he that looketh upon it seeth, while it is yet in his hand he eateth it up.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ In that day shall the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts be for a crown of glory, and for a diadem of beauty, unto the residue of his people,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And for a spirit of judgment to him that sitteth in judgment, and for strength to them that turn the battle to the gate.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ But they also have erred through wine, and through strong drink are out of the way; the priest and the prophet have erred through strong drink, they are swallowed up of wine, they are out of the way through strong drink; they err in vision, they stumble _in_ judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For all tables are full of vomit _and_ filthiness, _so that there is_ no place _clean_.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Whom shall he teach knowledge? and whom shall he make to understand doctrine? _them that are_ weaned from the milk, _and_ drawn from the breasts.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For precept _must be_ upon precept, precept upon precept; line upon line, line upon line; here a little, _and_ there a little:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For with stammering lips and another tongue will he speak to this people.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>To whom he said, This _is_ the rest _wherewith_ ye may cause the weary to rest; and this _is_ the refreshing: yet they would not hear.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was unto them precept upon precept, precept upon precept; line upon line, line upon line; here a little, _and_ there a little; that they might go, and fall backward, and be broken, and snared, and taken.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, ye scornful men, that rule this people which _is_ in Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Because ye have said, We have made a covenant with death, and with hell are we at agreement; when the overflowing scourge shall pass through, it shall not come unto us: for we have made lies our refuge, and under falsehood have we hid ourselves:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, Behold, I lay in Zion for a foundation a stone, a tried stone, a precious corner _stone_, a sure foundation: he that believeth shall not make haste.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Judgment also will I lay to the line, and righteousness to the plummet: and the hail shall sweep away the refuge of lies, and the waters shall overflow the hiding place.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And your covenant with death shall be disannulled, and your agreement with hell shall not stand; when the overflowing scourge shall pass through, then ye shall be trodden down by it.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>From the time that it goeth forth it shall take you: for morning by morning shall it pass over, by day and by night: and it shall be a vexation only _to_ understand the report.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For the bed is shorter than that _a man_ can stretch himself _on it_: and the covering narrower than that he can wrap himself _in it_.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall rise up as _in_ mount Perazim, he shall be wroth as _in_ the valley of Gibeon, that he may do his work, his strange work; and bring to pass his act, his strange act.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Now therefore be ye not mockers, lest your bands be made strong: for I have heard from the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of hosts a consumption, even determined upon the whole earth.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Give ye ear, and hear my voice; hearken, and hear my speech.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Doth the plowman plow all day to sow? doth he open and break the clods of his ground?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>When he hath made plain the face thereof, doth he not cast abroad the fitches, and scatter the cummin, and cast in the principal wheat and the appointed barley and the rie in their place?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For his God doth instruct him to discretion, _and_ doth teach him.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For the fitches are not threshed with a threshing instrument, neither is a cart wheel turned about upon the cummin; but the fitches are beaten out with a staff, and the cummin with a rod.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Bread _corn_ is bruised; because he will not ever be threshing it, nor break _it with_ the wheel of his cart, nor bruise it _with_ his horsemen.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>This also cometh forth from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, _which_ is wonderful in counsel, _and_ excellent in working.  

## CHAPTER 29.

Woe to Ariel, to Ariel, the city _where_ David dwelt! add ye year to year; let them kill sacrifices.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Yet I will distress Ariel, and there shall be heaviness and sorrow: and it shall be unto me as Ariel.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And I will camp against thee round about, and will lay siege against thee with a mount, and I will raise forts against thee.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And thou shalt be brought down, _and_ shalt speak out of the ground, and thy speech shall be low out of the dust, and thy voice shall be, as of one that hath a familiar spirit, out of the ground, and thy speech shall whisper out of the dust.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Moreover the multitude of thy strangers shall be like small dust, and the multitude of the terrible ones _shall be_ as chaff that passeth away: yea, it shall be at an instant suddenly.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Thou shalt be visited of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts with thunder, and with earthquake, and great noise, with storm and tempest, and the flame of devouring fire.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And the multitude of all the nations that fight against Ariel, even all that fight against her and her munition, and that distress her, shall be as a dream of a night vision.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>It shall even be as when an hungry _man_ dreameth, and, behold, he eateth; but he awaketh, and his soul is empty: or as when a thirsty man dreameth, and, behold, he drinketh; but he awaketh, and, behold, _he is_ faint, and his soul hath appetite: so shall the multitude of all the nations be, that fight against mount Zion.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Stay yourselves, and wonder; cry ye out, and cry: they are drunken, but not with wine; they stagger, but not with strong drink.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath poured out upon you the spirit of deep sleep, and hath closed your eyes: the prophets and your rulers, the seers hath he covered.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the vision of all is become unto you as the words of a book that is sealed, which _men_ deliver to one that is learned, saying, Read this, I pray thee: and he saith, I cannot; for it _is_ sealed:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the book is delivered to him that is not learned, saying, Read this, I pray thee: and he saith, I am not learned.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore the Lord said, Forasmuch as this people draw near _me_ with their mouth, and with their lips do honour me, but have removed their heart far from me, and their fear toward me is taught by the precept of men:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore, behold, I will proceed to do a marvellous work among this people, _even_ a marvellous work and a wonder: for the wisdom of their wise _men_ shall perish, and the understanding of their prudent _men_ shall be hid.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Woe unto them that seek deep to hide their counsel from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and their works are in the dark, and they say, Who seeth us? and who knoweth us?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Surely your turning of things upside down shall be esteemed as the potter’s clay: for shall the work say of him that made it, He made me not? or shall the thing framed say of him that framed it, He had no understanding?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ _Is_ it not yet a very little while, and Lebanon shall be turned into a fruitful field, and the fruitful field shall be esteemed as a forest?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And in that day shall the deaf hear the words of the book, and the eyes of the blind shall see out of obscurity, and out of darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The meek also shall increase _their_ joy in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and the poor among men shall rejoice in the Holy One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For the terrible one is brought to nought, and the scorner is consumed, and all that watch for iniquity are cut off:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>That make a man an offender for a word, and lay a snare for him that reproveth in the gate, and turn aside the just for a thing of nought.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, who redeemed Abraham, concerning the house of Jacob, Jacob shall not now be ashamed, neither shall his face now wax pale.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But when he seeth his children, the work of mine hands, in the midst of him, they shall sanctify my name, and sanctify the Holy One of Jacob, and shall fear the God of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>They also that erred in spirit shall come to understanding, and they that murmured shall learn doctrine.  

## CHAPTER 30.

Woe to the rebellious children, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that take counsel, but not of me; and that cover with a covering, but not of my spirit, that they may add sin to sin:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That walk to go down into Egypt, and have not asked at my mouth; to strengthen themselves in the strength of Pharaoh, and to trust in the shadow of Egypt!  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore shall the strength of Pharaoh be your shame, and the trust in the shadow of Egypt _your_ confusion.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For his princes were at Zoan, and his ambassadors came to Hanes.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>They were all ashamed of a people _that_ could not profit them, nor be an help nor profit, but a shame, and also a reproach.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The burden of the beasts of the south: into the land of trouble and anguish, from whence _come_ the young and old lion, the viper and fiery flying serpent, they will carry their riches upon the shoulders of young asses, and their treasures upon the bunches of camels, to a people _that_ shall not profit _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For the Egyptians shall help in vain, and to no purpose: therefore have I cried concerning this, Their strength _is_ to sit still.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Now go, write it before them in a table, and note it in a book, that it may be for the time to come for ever and ever:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>That this _is_ a rebellious people, lying children, children _that_ will not hear the law of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Which say to the seers, See not; and to the prophets, Prophesy not unto us right things, speak unto us smooth things, prophesy deceits:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Get you out of the way, turn aside out of the path, cause the Holy One of Israel to cease from before us.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Wherefore thus saith the Holy One of Israel, Because ye despise this word, and trust in oppression and perverseness, and stay thereon:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Therefore this iniquity shall be to you as a breach ready to fall, swelling out in a high wall, whose breaking cometh suddenly at an instant.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he shall break it as the breaking of the potters’ vessel that is broken in pieces; he shall not spare: so that there shall not be found in the bursting of it a sherd to take fire from the hearth, or to take water _withal_ out of the pit.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, the Holy One of Israel; In returning and rest shall ye be saved; in quietness and in confidence shall be your strength: and ye would not.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But ye said, No; for we will flee upon horses; therefore shall ye flee: and, We will ride upon the swift; therefore shall they that pursue you be swift.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>One thousand _shall flee_ at the rebuke of one; at the rebuke of five shall ye flee: till ye be left as a beacon upon the top of a mountain, and as an ensign on an hill.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And therefore will the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> wait, that he may be gracious unto you, and therefore will he be exalted, that he may have mercy upon you: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ a God of judgment: blessed _are_ all they that wait for him.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For the people shall dwell in Zion at Jerusalem: thou shalt weep no more: he will be very gracious unto thee at the voice of thy cry; when he shall hear it, he will answer thee.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And _though_ the Lord give you the bread of adversity, and the water of affliction, yet shall not thy teachers be removed into a corner any more, but thine eyes shall see thy teachers:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And thine ears shall hear a word behind thee, saying, This _is_ the way, walk ye in it, when ye turn to the right hand, and when ye turn to the left.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Ye shall defile also the covering of thy graven images of silver, and the ornament of thy molten images of gold: thou shalt cast them away as a menstruous cloth; thou shalt say unto it, Get thee hence.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Then shall he give the rain of thy seed, that thou shalt sow the ground withal; and bread of the increase of the earth, and it shall be fat and plenteous: in that day shall thy cattle feed in large pastures.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>The oxen likewise and the young asses that ear the ground shall eat clean provender, which hath been winnowed with the shovel and with the fan.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And there shall be upon every high mountain, and upon every high hill, rivers _and_ streams of waters in the day of the great slaughter, when the towers fall.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Moreover the light of the moon shall be as the light of the sun, and the light of the sun shall be sevenfold, as the light of seven days, in the day that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> bindeth up the breach of his people, and healeth the stroke of their wound.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> cometh from far, burning _with_ his anger, and the burden _thereof is_ heavy: his lips are full of indignation, and his tongue as a devouring fire:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And his breath, as an overflowing stream, shall reach to the midst of the neck, to sift the nations with the sieve of vanity: and _there shall be_ a bridle in the jaws of the people, causing _them_ to err.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Ye shall have a song, as in the night _when_ a holy solemnity is kept; and gladness of heart, as when one goeth with a pipe to come into the mountain of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to the mighty One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall cause his glorious voice to be heard, and shall shew the lighting down of his arm, with the indignation of _his_ anger, and _with_ the flame of a devouring fire, _with_ scattering, and tempest, and hailstones.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>For through the voice of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall the Assyrian be beaten down, _which_ smote with a rod.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And _in_ every place where the grounded staff shall pass, which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall lay upon him, _it_ shall be with tabrets and harps: and in battles of shaking will he fight with it.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>For Tophet _is_ ordained of old; yea, for the king it is prepared; he hath made _it_ deep _and_ large: the pile thereof _is_ fire and much wood; the breath of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, like a stream of brimstone, doth kindle it.  

## CHAPTER 31.

Woe to them that go down to Egypt for help; and stay on horses, and trust in chariots, because _they are_ many; and in horsemen, because they are very strong; but they look not unto the Holy One of Israel, neither seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>!  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Yet he also _is_ wise, and will bring evil, and will not call back his words: but will arise against the house of the evildoers, and against the help of them that work iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Now the Egyptians _are_ men, and not God; and their horses flesh, and not spirit. When the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall stretch out his hand, both he that helpeth shall fall, and he that is holpen shall fall down, and they all shall fail together.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For thus hath the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spoken unto me, Like as the lion and the young lion roaring on his prey, when a multitude of shepherds is called forth against him, _he_ will not be afraid of their voice, nor abase himself for the noise of them: so shall the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts come down to fight for mount Zion, and for the hill thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>As birds flying, so will the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts defend Jerusalem; defending also he will deliver _it; and_ passing over he will preserve it.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Turn ye unto _him from_ whom the children of Israel have deeply revolted.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For in that day every man shall cast away his idols of silver, and his idols of gold, which your own hands have made unto you _for_ a sin.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Then shall the Assyrian fall with the sword, not of a mighty man; and the sword, not of a mean man, shall devour him: but he shall flee from the sword, and his young men shall be discomfited.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he shall pass over to his strong hold for fear, and his princes shall be afraid of the ensign, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, whose fire _is_ in Zion, and his furnace in Jerusalem.  

## CHAPTER 32.

Behold, a king shall reign in righteousness, and princes shall rule in judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And a man shall be as an hiding place from the wind, and a covert from the tempest; as rivers of water in a dry place, as the shadow of a great rock in a weary land.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the eyes of them that see shall not be dim, and the ears of them that hear shall hearken.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The heart also of the rash shall understand knowledge, and the tongue of the stammerers shall be ready to speak plainly.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The vile person shall be no more called liberal, nor the churl said _to be_ bountiful.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For the vile person will speak villany, and his heart will work iniquity, to practise hypocrisy, and to utter error against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to make empty the soul of the hungry, and he will cause the drink of the thirsty to fail.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The instruments also of the churl _are_ evil: he deviseth wicked devices to destroy the poor with lying words, even when the needy speaketh right.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But the liberal deviseth liberal things; and by liberal things shall he stand.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Rise up, ye women that are at ease; hear my voice, ye careless daughters; give ear unto my speech.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Many days and years shall ye be troubled, ye careless women: for the vintage shall fail, the gathering shall not come.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Tremble, ye women that are at ease; be troubled, ye careless ones: strip you, and make you bare, and gird _sackcloth_ upon _your_ loins.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>They shall lament for the teats, for the pleasant fields, for the fruitful vine.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Upon the land of my people shall come up thorns _and_ briers; yea, upon all the houses of joy _in_ the joyous city:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Because the palaces shall be forsaken; the multitude of the city shall be left; the forts and towers shall be for dens for ever, a joy of wild asses, a pasture of flocks;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Until the spirit be poured upon us from on high, and the wilderness be a fruitful field, and the fruitful field be counted for a forest.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Then judgment shall dwell in the wilderness, and righteousness remain in the fruitful field.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the work of righteousness shall be peace; and the effect of righteousness quietness and assurance for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And my people shall dwell in a peaceable habitation, and in sure dwellings, and in quiet resting places;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>When it shall hail, coming down on the forest; and the city shall be low in a low place.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Blessed _are_ ye that sow beside all waters, that send forth _thither_ the feet of the ox and the ass.  

## CHAPTER 33.

Woe to thee that spoilest, and thou _wast_ not spoiled; and dealest treacherously, and they dealt not treacherously with thee! when thou shalt cease to spoil, thou shalt be spoiled; _and_ when thou shalt make an end to deal treacherously, they shall deal treacherously with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, be gracious unto us; we have waited for thee: be thou their arm every morning, our salvation also in the time of trouble.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>At the noise of the tumult the people fled; at the lifting up of thyself the nations were scattered.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And your spoil shall be gathered _like_ the gathering of the caterpiller: as the running to and fro of locusts shall he run upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> is exalted; for he dwelleth on high: he hath filled Zion with judgment and righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And wisdom and knowledge shall be the stability of thy times, _and_ strength of salvation: the fear of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ his treasure.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Behold, their valiant ones shall cry without: the ambassadors of peace shall weep bitterly.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The highways lie waste, the wayfaring man ceaseth: he hath broken the covenant, he hath despised the cities, he regardeth no man.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The earth mourneth _and_ languisheth: Lebanon is ashamed _and_ hewn down: Sharon is like a wilderness; and Bashan and Carmel shake off _their fruits_.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Now will I rise, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; now will I be exalted; now will I lift up myself.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Ye shall conceive chaff, ye shall bring forth stubble: your breath, _as_ fire, shall devour you.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the people shall be _as_ the burnings of lime: _as_ thorns cut up shall they be burned in the fire.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Hear, ye _that are_ far off, what I have done; and, ye _that are_ near, acknowledge my might.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The sinners in Zion are afraid; fearfulness hath surprised the hypocrites. Who among us shall dwell with the devouring fire? who among us shall dwell with everlasting burnings?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>He that walketh righteously, and speaketh uprightly; he that despiseth the gain of oppressions, that shaketh his hands from holding of bribes, that stoppeth his ears from hearing of blood, and shutteth his eyes from seeing evil;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>He shall dwell on high: his place of defence _shall be_ the munitions of rocks: bread shall be given him; his waters _shall be_ sure.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thine eyes shall see the king in his beauty: they shall behold the land that is very far off.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Thine heart shall meditate terror. Where _is_ the scribe? where _is_ the receiver? where _is_ he that counted the towers?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Thou shalt not see a fierce people, a people of a deeper speech than thou canst perceive; of a stammering tongue, _that thou canst_ not understand.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Look upon Zion, the city of our solemnities: thine eyes shall see Jerusalem a quiet habitation, a tabernacle _that_ shall not be taken down; not one of the stakes thereof shall ever be removed, neither shall any of the cords thereof be broken.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But there the glorious <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _will be_ unto us a place of broad rivers _and_ streams; wherein shall go no galley with oars, neither shall gallant ship pass thereby.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ our judge, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ our lawgiver, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ our king; he will save us.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Thy tacklings are loosed; they could not well strengthen their mast, they could not spread the sail: then is the prey of a great spoil divided; the lame take the prey.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the inhabitant shall not say, I am sick: the people that dwell therein _shall be_ forgiven _their_ iniquity.  

## CHAPTER 34.

Come near, ye nations, to hear; and hearken, ye people: let the earth hear, and all that is therein; the world, and all things that come forth of it.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For the indignation of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ upon all nations, and _his_ fury upon all their armies: he hath utterly destroyed them, he hath delivered them to the slaughter.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Their slain also shall be cast out, and their stink shall come up out of their carcases, and the mountains shall be melted with their blood.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And all the host of heaven shall be dissolved, and the heavens shall be rolled together as a scroll: and all their host shall fall down, as the leaf falleth off from the vine, and as a falling _fig_ from the fig tree.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For my sword shall be bathed in heaven: behold, it shall come down upon Idumea, and upon the people of my curse, to judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The sword of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> is filled with blood, it is made fat with fatness, _and_ with the blood of lambs and goats, with the fat of the kidneys of rams: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath a sacrifice in Bozrah, and a great slaughter in the land of Idumea.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the unicorns shall come down with them, and the bullocks with the bulls; and their land shall be soaked with blood, and their dust made fat with fatness.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For _it is_ the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> vengeance, _and_ the year of recompences for the controversy of Zion.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And the streams thereof shall be turned into pitch, and the dust thereof into brimstone, and the land thereof shall become burning pitch.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>It shall not be quenched night nor day; the smoke thereof shall go up for ever: from generation to generation it shall lie waste; none shall pass through it for ever and ever.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ But the cormorant and the bittern shall possess it; the owl also and the raven shall dwell in it: and he shall stretch out upon it the line of confusion, and the stones of emptiness.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>They shall call the nobles thereof to the kingdom, but none _shall be_ there, and all her princes shall be nothing.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And thorns shall come up in her palaces, nettles and brambles in the fortresses thereof: and it shall be an habitation of dragons, _and_ a court for owls.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The wild beasts of the desert shall also meet with the wild beasts of the island, and the satyr shall cry to his fellow; the screech owl also shall rest there, and find for herself a place of rest.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>There shall the great owl make her nest, and lay, and hatch, and gather under her shadow: there shall the vultures also be gathered, every one with her mate.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Seek ye out of the book of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and read: no one of these shall fail, none shall want her mate: for my mouth it hath commanded, and his spirit it hath gathered them.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he hath cast the lot for them, and his hand hath divided it unto them by line: they shall possess it for ever, from generation to generation shall they dwell therein.  

## CHAPTER 35.

The wilderness and the solitary place shall be glad for them; and the desert shall rejoice, and blossom as the rose.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>It shall blossom abundantly, and rejoice even with joy and singing: the glory of Lebanon shall be given unto it, the excellency of Carmel and Sharon, they shall see the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _and_ the excellency of our God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Strengthen ye the weak hands, and confirm the feeble knees.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Say to them _that are_ of a fearful heart, Be strong, fear not: behold, your God will come _with_ vengeance, _even_ God _with_ a recompence; he will come and save you.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Then the eyes of the blind shall be opened, and the ears of the deaf shall be unstopped.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Then shall the lame _man_ leap as an hart, and the tongue of the dumb sing: for in the wilderness shall waters break out, and streams in the desert.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the parched ground shall become a pool, and the thirsty land springs of water: in the habitation of dragons, where each lay, _shall be_ grass with reeds and rushes.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And an highway shall be there, and a way, and it shall be called The way of holiness; the unclean shall not pass over it; but it _shall be_ for those: the wayfaring men, though fools, shall not err _therein_.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>No lion shall be there, nor _any_ ravenous beast shall go up thereon, it shall not be found there; but the redeemed shall walk _there_:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the ransomed of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall return, and come to Zion with songs and everlasting joy upon their heads: they shall obtain joy and gladness, and sorrow and sighing shall flee away.  

## CHAPTER 36.

Now it came to pass in the fourteenth year of king Hezekiah, _that_ Sennacherib king of Assyria came up against all the defenced cities of Judah, and took them.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the king of Assyria sent Rabshakeh from Lachish to Jerusalem unto king Hezekiah with a great army. And he stood by the conduit of the upper pool in the highway of the fuller’s field.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Then came forth unto him Eliakim, Hilkiah’s son, which was over the house, and Shebna the scribe, and Joah, Asaph’s son, the recorder.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And Rabshakeh said unto them, Say ye now to Hezekiah, Thus saith the great king, the king of Assyria, What confidence _is_ this wherein thou trustest?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>I say, _sayest thou_, (but _they are but_ vain words) _I have_ counsel and strength for war: now on whom dost thou trust, that thou rebellest against me?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Lo, thou trustest in the staff of this broken reed, on Egypt; whereon if a man lean, it will go into his hand, and pierce it: so _is_ Pharaoh king of Egypt to all that trust in him.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But if thou say to me, We trust in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God: _is it_ not he, whose high places and whose altars Hezekiah hath taken away, and said to Judah and to Jerusalem, Ye shall worship before this altar?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Now therefore give pledges, I pray thee, to my master the king of Assyria, and I will give thee two thousand horses, if thou be able on thy part to set riders upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>How then wilt thou turn away the face of one captain of the least of my master’s servants, and put thy trust on Egypt for chariots and for horsemen?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And am I now come up without the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> against this land to destroy it? the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto me, Go up against this land, and destroy it.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Then said Eliakim and Shebna and Joah unto Rabshakeh, Speak, I pray thee, unto thy servants in the Syrian language; for we understand _it_: and speak not to us in the Jews’ language, in the ears of the people that _are_ on the wall.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ But Rabshakeh said, Hath my master sent me to thy master and to thee to speak these words? _hath he_ not _sent me_ to the men that sit upon the wall, that they may eat their own dung, and drink their own piss with you?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Then Rabshakeh stood, and cried with a loud voice in the Jews’ language, and said, Hear ye the words of the great king, the king of Assyria.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thus saith the king, Let not Hezekiah deceive you: for he shall not be able to deliver you.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Neither let Hezekiah make you trust in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will surely deliver us: this city shall not be delivered into the hand of the king of Assyria.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Hearken not to Hezekiah: for thus saith the king of Assyria, Make _an agreement_ with me _by_ a present, and come out to me: and eat ye every one of his vine, and every one of his fig tree, and drink ye every one the waters of his own cistern;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Until I come and take you away to a land like your own land, a land of corn and wine, a land of bread and vineyards.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>_Beware_ lest Hezekiah persuade you, saying, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will deliver us. Hath any of the gods of the nations delivered his land out of the hand of the king of Assyria?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Where _are_ the gods of Hamath and Arphad? where _are_ the gods of Sepharvaim? and have they delivered Samaria out of my hand?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Who _are they_ among all the gods of these lands, that have delivered their land out of my hand, that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> should deliver Jerusalem out of my hand?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But they held their peace, and answered him not a word: for the king’s commandment was, saying, Answer him not.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Then came Eliakim, the son of Hilkiah, that _was_ over the household, and Shebna the scribe, and Joah, the son of Asaph, the recorder, to Hezekiah with _their_ clothes rent, and told him the words of Rabshakeh.  

## CHAPTER 37.

And it came to pass, when king Hezekiah heard _it_, that he rent his clothes, and covered himself with sackcloth, and went into the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he sent Eliakim, who _was_ over the household, and Shebna the scribe, and the elders of the priests covered with sackcloth, unto Isaiah the prophet the son of Amoz.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And they said unto him, Thus saith Hezekiah, This day _is_ a day of trouble, and of rebuke, and of blasphemy: for the children are come to the birth, and _there is_ not strength to bring forth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>It may be the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God will hear the words of Rabshakeh, whom the king of Assyria his master hath sent to reproach the living God, and will reprove the words which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God hath heard: wherefore lift up _thy_ prayer for the remnant that is left.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>So the servants of king Hezekiah came to Isaiah.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And Isaiah said unto them, Thus shall ye say unto your master, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Be not afraid of the words that thou hast heard, wherewith the servants of the king of Assyria have blasphemed me.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Behold, I will send a blast upon him, and he shall hear a rumour, and return to his own land; and I will cause him to fall by the sword in his own land.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ So Rabshakeh returned, and found the king of Assyria warring against Libnah: for he had heard that he was departed from Lachish.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he heard say concerning Tirhakah king of Ethiopia, He is come forth to make war with thee. And when he heard _it_, he sent messengers to Hezekiah, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thus shall ye speak to Hezekiah king of Judah, saying, Let not thy God, in whom thou trustest, deceive thee, saying, Jerusalem shall not be given into the hand of the king of Assyria.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Behold, thou hast heard what the kings of Assyria have done to all lands by destroying them utterly; and shalt thou be delivered?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Have the gods of the nations delivered them which my fathers have destroyed, _as_ Gozan, and Haran, and Rezeph, and the children of Eden which _were_ in Telassar?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Where _is_ the king of Hamath, and the king of Arphad, and the king of the city of Sepharvaim, Hena, and Ivah?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And Hezekiah received the letter from the hand of the messengers, and read it: and Hezekiah went up unto the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and spread it before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Hezekiah prayed unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, God of Israel, that dwellest _between_ the cherubims, thou _art_ the God, _even_ thou alone, of all the kingdoms of the earth: thou hast made heaven and earth.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Incline thine ear, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and hear; open thine eyes, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and see: and hear all the words of Sennacherib, which hath sent to reproach the living God.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Of a truth, <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the kings of Assyria have laid waste all the nations, and their countries,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And have cast their gods into the fire: for they _were_ no gods, but the work of men’s hands, wood and stone: therefore they have destroyed them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Now therefore, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> our God, save us from his hand, that all the kingdoms of the earth may know that thou _art_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _even_ thou only.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Then Isaiah the son of Amoz sent unto Hezekiah, saying, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel, Whereas thou hast prayed to me against Sennacherib king of Assyria:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>This _is_ the word which the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken concerning him; The virgin, the daughter of Zion, hath despised thee, _and_ laughed thee to scorn; the daughter of Jerusalem hath shaken her head at thee.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Whom hast thou reproached and blasphemed? and against whom hast thou exalted _thy_ voice, and lifted up thine eyes on high? _even_ against the Holy One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>By thy servants hast thou reproached the Lord, and hast said, By the multitude of my chariots am I come up to the height of the mountains, to the sides of Lebanon; and I will cut down the tall cedars thereof, _and_ the choice fir trees thereof: and I will enter into the height of his border, _and_ the forest of his Carmel.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>I have digged, and drunk water; and with the sole of my feet have I dried up all the rivers of the besieged places.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Hast thou not heard long ago, _how_ I have done it; _and_ of ancient times, that I have formed it? now have I brought it to pass, that thou shouldest be to lay waste defenced cities _into_ ruinous heaps.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Therefore their inhabitants _were_ of small power, they were dismayed and confounded: they were _as_ the grass of the field, and _as_ the green herb, _as_ the grass on the housetops, and _as corn_ blasted before it be grown up.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>But I know thy abode, and thy going out, and thy coming in, and thy rage against me.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Because thy rage against me, and thy tumult, is come up into mine ears, therefore will I put my hook in thy nose, and my bridle in thy lips, and I will turn thee back by the way by which thou camest.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And this _shall be_ a sign unto thee, Ye shall eat _this_ year such as groweth of itself; and the second year that which springeth of the same: and in the third year sow ye, and reap, and plant vineyards, and eat the fruit thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the remnant that is escaped of the house of Judah shall again take root downward, and bear fruit upward:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For out of Jerusalem shall go forth a remnant, and they that escape out of mount Zion: the zeal of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts shall do this.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> concerning the king of Assyria, He shall not come into this city, nor shoot an arrow there, nor come before it with shields, nor cast a bank against it.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>By the way that he came, by the same shall he return, and shall not come into this city, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>For I will defend this city to save it for mine own sake, and for my servant David’s sake.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Then the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> went forth, and smote in the camp of the Assyrians a hundred and fourscore and five thousand: and when they arose early in the morning, behold, they _were_ all dead corpses.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>¶ So Sennacherib king of Assyria departed, and went and returned, and dwelt at Nineveh.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, as he was worshipping in the house of Nisroch his god, that Adrammelech and Sharezer his sons smote him with the sword; and they escaped into the land of Armenia: and Esar–haddon his son reigned in his stead.  

## CHAPTER 38.

In those days was Hezekiah sick unto death. And Isaiah the prophet the son of Amoz came unto him, and said unto him, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Set thine house in order: for thou shalt die, and not live.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then Hezekiah turned his face toward the wall, and prayed unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And said, Remember now, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I beseech thee, how I have walked before thee in truth and with a perfect heart, and have done _that which is_ good in thy sight. And Hezekiah wept sore.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Then came the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to Isaiah, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Go, and say to Hezekiah, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the God of David thy father, I have heard thy prayer, I have seen thy tears: behold, I will add unto thy days fifteen years.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And I will deliver thee and this city out of the hand of the king of Assyria: and I will defend this city.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And this _shall be_ a sign unto thee from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will do this thing that he hath spoken;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Behold, I will bring again the shadow of the degrees, which is gone down in the sun dial of Ahaz, ten degrees backward. So the sun returned ten degrees, by which degrees it was gone down.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ The writing of Hezekiah king of Judah, when he had been sick, and was recovered of his sickness:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>I said in the cutting off of my days, I shall go to the gates of the grave: I am deprived of the residue of my years.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I said, I shall not see the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _even_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, in the land of the living: I shall behold man no more with the inhabitants of the world.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Mine age is departed, and is removed from me as a shepherd’s tent: I have cut off like a weaver my life: he will cut me off with pining sickness: from day _even_ to night wilt thou make an end of me.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>I reckoned till morning, _that_, as a lion, so will he break all my bones: from day _even_ to night wilt thou make an end of me.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Like a crane _or_ a swallow, so did I chatter: I did mourn as a dove: mine eyes fail _with looking_ upward: O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I am oppressed; undertake for me.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>What shall I say? he hath both spoken unto me, and himself hath done _it_: I shall go softly all my years in the bitterness of my soul.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>O Lord, by these _things men_ live, and in all these _things is_ the life of my spirit: so wilt thou recover me, and make me to live.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Behold, for peace I had great bitterness: but thou hast in love to my soul _delivered it_ from the pit of corruption: for thou hast cast all my sins behind thy back.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For the grave cannot praise thee, death can _not_ celebrate thee: they that go down into the pit cannot hope for thy truth.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The living, the living, he shall praise thee, as I _do_ this day: the father to the children shall make known thy truth.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _was ready_ to save me: therefore we will sing my songs to the stringed instruments all the days of our life in the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For Isaiah had said, Let them take a lump of figs, and lay _it_ for a plaister upon the boil, and he shall recover.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Hezekiah also had said, What _is_ the sign that I shall go up to the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>?  

## CHAPTER 39.

At that time Merodach–baladan, the son of Baladan, king of Babylon, sent letters and a present to Hezekiah: for he had heard that he had been sick, and was recovered.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Hezekiah was glad of them, and shewed them the house of his precious things, the silver, and the gold, and the spices, and the precious ointment, and all the house of his armour, and all that was found in his treasures: there was nothing in his house, nor in all his dominion, that Hezekiah shewed them not.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Then came Isaiah the prophet unto king Hezekiah, and said unto him, What said these men? and from whence came they unto thee? And Hezekiah said, They are come from a far country unto me, _even_ from Babylon.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then said he, What have they seen in thine house? And Hezekiah answered, All that _is_ in mine house have they seen: there is nothing among my treasures that I have not shewed them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Then said Isaiah to Hezekiah, Hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Behold, the days come, that all that _is_ in thine house, and _that_ which thy fathers have laid up in store until this day, shall be carried to Babylon: nothing shall be left, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And of thy sons that shall issue from thee, which thou shalt beget, shall they take away; and they shall be eunuchs in the palace of the king of Babylon.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Then said Hezekiah to Isaiah, Good _is_ the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> which thou hast spoken. He said moreover, For there shall be peace and truth in my days.  

## CHAPTER 40.

Comfort ye, comfort ye my people, saith your God.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Speak ye comfortably to Jerusalem, and cry unto her, that her warfare is accomplished, that her iniquity is pardoned: for she hath received of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> hand double for all her sins.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ The voice of him that crieth in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, make straight in the desert a highway for our God.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Every valley shall be exalted, and every mountain and hill shall be made low: and the crooked shall be made straight, and the rough places plain:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be revealed, and all flesh shall see _it_ together: for the mouth of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The voice said, Cry. And he said, What shall I cry? All flesh _is_ grass, and all the goodliness thereof _is_ as the flower of the field:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The grass withereth, the flower fadeth: because the spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> bloweth upon it: surely the people _is_ grass.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The grass withereth, the flower fadeth: but the word of our God shall stand for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ O Zion, that bringest good tidings, get thee up into the high mountain; O Jerusalem, that bringest good tidings, lift up thy voice with strength; lift _it_ up, be not afraid; say unto the cities of Judah, Behold your God!  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Behold, the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> will come with strong _hand_, and his arm shall rule for him: behold, his reward _is_ with him, and his work before him.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He shall feed his flock like a shepherd: he shall gather the lambs with his arm, and carry _them_ in his bosom, _and_ shall gently lead those that are with young.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Who hath measured the waters in the hollow of his hand, and meted out heaven with the span, and comprehended the dust of the earth in a measure, and weighed the mountains in scales, and the hills in a balance?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Who hath directed the Spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, or _being_ his counsellor hath taught him?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>With whom took he counsel, and _who_ instructed him, and taught him in the path of judgment, and taught him knowledge, and shewed to him the way of understanding?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Behold, the nations _are_ as a drop of a bucket, and are counted as the small dust of the balance: behold, he taketh up the isles as a very little thing.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Lebanon _is_ not sufficient to burn, nor the beasts thereof sufficient for a burnt offering.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>All nations before him _are_ as nothing; and they are counted to him less than nothing, and vanity.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ To whom then will ye liken God? or what likeness will ye compare unto him?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The workman melteth a graven image, and the goldsmith spreadeth it over with gold, and casteth silver chains.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>He that _is_ so impoverished that he hath no oblation chooseth a tree _that_ will not rot; he seeketh unto him a cunning workman to prepare a graven image, _that_ shall not be moved.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Have ye not known? have ye not heard? hath it not been told you from the beginning? have ye not understood from the foundations of the earth?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>_It is_ he that sitteth upon the circle of the earth, and the inhabitants thereof _are_ as grasshoppers; that stretcheth out the heavens as a curtain, and spreadeth them out as a tent to dwell in:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>That bringeth the princes to nothing; he maketh the judges of the earth as vanity.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Yea, they shall not be planted; yea, they shall not be sown: yea, their stock shall not take root in the earth: and he shall also blow upon them, and they shall wither, and the whirlwind shall take them away as stubble.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>To whom then will ye liken me, or shall I be equal? saith the Holy One.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Lift up your eyes on high, and behold who hath created these _things_, that bringeth out their host by number: he calleth them all by names by the greatness of his might, for that _he is_ strong in power; not one faileth.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ Why sayest thou, O Jacob, and speakest, O Israel, My way is hid from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and my judgment is passed over from my God?  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ Hast thou not known? hast thou not heard, _that_ the everlasting God, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the Creator of the ends of the earth, fainteth not, neither is weary? _there is_ no searching of his understanding.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>He giveth power to the faint; and to _them that have_ no might he increaseth strength.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Even the youths shall faint and be weary, and the young men shall utterly fall:  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>But they that wait upon the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall renew _their_ strength; they shall mount up with wings as eagles; they shall run, and not be weary; _and_ they shall walk, and not faint.  

## CHAPTER 41.

Keep silence before me, O islands; and let the people renew _their_ strength: let them come near; then let them speak: let us come near together to judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Who raised up the righteous _man_ from the east, called him to his foot, gave the nations before him, and made _him_ rule over kings? he gave _them_ as the dust to his sword, _and_ as driven stubble to his bow.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>He pursued them, _and_ passed safely; _even_ by the way _that_ he had not gone with his feet.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who hath wrought and done _it_, calling the generations from the beginning? I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the first, and with the last; I _am_ he.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>The isles saw _it_, and feared; the ends of the earth were afraid, drew near, and came.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>They helped every one his neighbour; and _every one_ said to his brother, Be of good courage.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>So the carpenter encouraged the goldsmith, _and_ he that smootheth _with_ the hammer him that smote the anvil, saying, It _is_ ready for the sodering: and he fastened it with nails, _that_ it should not be moved.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But thou, Israel, _art_ my servant, Jacob whom I have chosen, the seed of Abraham my friend.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_Thou_ whom I have taken from the ends of the earth, and called thee from the chief men thereof, and said unto thee, Thou _art_ my servant; I have chosen thee, and not cast thee away.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Fear thou not; for I _am_ with thee: be not dismayed; for I _am_ thy God: I will strengthen thee; yea, I will help thee; yea, I will uphold thee with the right hand of my righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Behold, all they that were incensed against thee shall be ashamed and confounded: they shall be as nothing; and they that strive with thee shall perish.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Thou shalt seek them, and shalt not find them, _even_ them that contended with thee: they that war against thee shall be as nothing, and as a thing of nought.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God will hold thy right hand, saying unto thee, Fear not; I will help thee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Fear not, thou worm Jacob, _and_ ye men of Israel; I will help thee, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and thy redeemer, the Holy One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Behold, I will make thee a new sharp threshing instrument having teeth: thou shalt thresh the mountains, and beat _them_ small, and shalt make the hills as chaff.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Thou shalt fan them, and the wind shall carry them away, and the whirlwind shall scatter them: and thou shalt rejoice in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _and_ shalt glory in the Holy One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>_When_ the poor and needy seek water, and _there is_ none, _and_ their tongue faileth for thirst, I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will hear them, I the God of Israel will not forsake them.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>I will open rivers in high places, and fountains in the midst of the valleys: I will make the wilderness a pool of water, and the dry land springs of water.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>I will plant in the wilderness the cedar, the shittah tree, and the myrtle, and the oil tree; I will set in the desert the fir tree, _and_ the pine, and the box tree together:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>That they may see, and know, and consider, and understand together, that the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath done this, and the Holy One of Israel hath created it.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Produce your cause, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; bring forth your strong _reasons_, saith the King of Jacob.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Let them bring _them_ forth, and shew us what shall happen: let them shew the former things, what they _be_, that we may consider them, and know the latter end of them; or declare us things for to come.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Shew the things that are to come hereafter, that we may know that ye _are_ gods: yea, do good, or do evil, that we may be dismayed, and behold _it_ together.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Behold, ye _are_ of nothing, and your work of nought: an abomination _is he that_ chooseth you.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>I have raised up _one_ from the north, and he shall come: from the rising of the sun shall he call upon my name: and he shall come upon princes as _upon_ morter, and as the potter treadeth clay.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Who hath declared from the beginning, that we may know? and beforetime, that we may say, _He is_ righteous? yea, _there is_ none that sheweth, yea, _there is_ none that declareth, yea, _there is_ none that heareth your words.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>The first _shall say_ to Zion, Behold, behold them: and I will give to Jerusalem one that bringeth good tidings.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For I beheld, and _there was_ no man; even among them, and _there was_ no counsellor, that, when I asked of them, could answer a word.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Behold, they _are_ all vanity; their works _are_ nothing: their molten images _are_ wind and confusion.  

## CHAPTER 42.

Behold my servant, whom I uphold; mine elect, _in whom_ my soul delighteth; I have put my spirit upon him: he shall bring forth judgment to the Gentiles.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>He shall not cry, nor lift up, nor cause his voice to be heard in the street.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>A bruised reed shall he not break, and the smoking flax shall he not quench: he shall bring forth judgment unto truth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>He shall not fail nor be discouraged, till he have set judgment in the earth: and the isles shall wait for his law.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith God the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, he that created the heavens, and stretched them out; he that spread forth the earth, and that which cometh out of it; he that giveth breath unto the people upon it, and spirit to them that walk therein:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have called thee in righteousness, and will hold thine hand, and will keep thee, and give thee for a covenant of the people, for a light of the Gentiles;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>To open the blind eyes, to bring out the prisoners from the prison, _and_ them that sit in darkness out of the prison house.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: that _is_ my name: and my glory will I not give to another, neither my praise to graven images.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Behold, the former things are come to pass, and new things do I declare: before they spring forth I tell you of them.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Sing unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> a new song, _and_ his praise from the end of the earth, ye that go down to the sea, and all that is therein; the isles, and the inhabitants thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Let the wilderness and the cities thereof lift up _their voice_, the villages _that_ Kedar doth inhabit: let the inhabitants of the rock sing, let them shout from the top of the mountains.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Let them give glory unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and declare his praise in the islands.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall go forth as a mighty man, he shall stir up jealousy like a man of war: he shall cry, yea, roar; he shall prevail against his enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I have long time holden my peace; I have been still, _and_ refrained myself: _now_ will I cry like a travailing woman; I will destroy and devour at once.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>I will make waste mountains and hills, and dry up all their herbs; and I will make the rivers islands, and I will dry up the pools.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And I will bring the blind by a way _that_ they knew not; I will lead them in paths _that_ they have not known: I will make darkness light before them, and crooked things straight. These things will I do unto them, and not forsake them.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ They shall be turned back, they shall be greatly ashamed, that trust in graven images, that say to the molten images, Ye _are_ our gods.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Hear, ye deaf; and look, ye blind, that ye may see.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Who _is_ blind, but my servant? or deaf, as my messenger _that_ I sent? who _is_ blind as _he that is_ perfect, and blind as the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> servant?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Seeing many things, but thou observest not; opening the ears, but he heareth not.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> is well pleased for his righteousness’ sake; he will magnify the law, and make _it_ honourable.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But this _is_ a people robbed and spoiled; _they are_ all of them snared in holes, and they are hid in prison houses: they are for a prey, and none delivereth; for a spoil, and none saith, Restore.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Who among you will give ear to this? _who_ will hearken and hear for the time to come?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Who gave Jacob for a spoil, and Israel to the robbers? did not the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, he against whom we have sinned? for they would not walk in his ways, neither were they obedient unto his law.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Therefore he hath poured upon him the fury of his anger, and the strength of battle: and it hath set him on fire round about, yet he knew not; and it burned him, yet he laid _it_ not to heart.  

## CHAPTER 43.

But now thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that created thee, O Jacob, and he that formed thee, O Israel, Fear not: for I have redeemed thee, I have called _thee_ by thy name; thou _art_ mine.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>When thou passest through the waters, I _will be_ with thee; and through the rivers, they shall not overflow thee: when thou walkest through the fire, thou shalt not be burned; neither shall the flame kindle upon thee.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, the Holy One of Israel, thy Saviour: I gave Egypt _for_ thy ransom, Ethiopia and Seba for thee.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Since thou wast precious in my sight, thou hast been honourable, and I have loved thee: therefore will I give men for thee, and people for thy life.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Fear not: for I _am_ with thee: I will bring thy seed from the east, and gather thee from the west;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>I will say to the north, Give up; and to the south, Keep not back: bring my sons from far, and my daughters from the ends of the earth;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_Even_ every one that is called by my name: for I have created him for my glory, I have formed him; yea, I have made him.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Bring forth the blind people that have eyes, and the deaf that have ears.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Let all the nations be gathered together, and let the people be assembled: who among them can declare this, and shew us former things? let them bring forth their witnesses, that they may be justified: or let them hear, and say, _It is_ truth.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Ye _are_ my witnesses, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and my servant whom I have chosen: that ye may know and believe me, and understand that I _am_ he: before me there was no God formed, neither shall there be after me.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I, _even_ I, _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and beside me _there is_ no saviour.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I have declared, and have saved, and I have shewed, when _there was_ no strange _god_ among you: therefore ye _are_ my witnesses, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that I _am_ God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Yea, before the day _was_ I _am_ he; and _there is_ none that can deliver out of my hand: I will work, and who shall let it?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, your redeemer, the Holy One of Israel; For your sake I have sent to Babylon, and have brought down all their nobles, and the Chaldeans, whose cry _is_ in the ships.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, your Holy One, the creator of Israel, your King.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, which maketh a way in the sea, and a path in the mighty waters;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Which bringeth forth the chariot and horse, the army and the power; they shall lie down together, they shall not rise: they are extinct, they are quenched as tow.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Remember ye not the former things, neither consider the things of old.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Behold, I will do a new thing; now it shall spring forth; shall ye not know it? I will even make a way in the wilderness, _and_ rivers in the desert.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>The beast of the field shall honour me, the dragons and the owls: because I give waters in the wilderness, _and_ rivers in the desert, to give drink to my people, my chosen.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>This people have I formed for myself; they shall shew forth my praise.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ But thou hast not called upon me, O Jacob; but thou hast been weary of me, O Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Thou hast not brought me the small cattle of thy burnt offerings; neither hast thou honoured me with thy sacrifices. I have not caused thee to serve with an offering, nor wearied thee with incense.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Thou hast bought me no sweet cane with money, neither hast thou filled me with the fat of thy sacrifices: but thou hast made me to serve with thy sins, thou hast wearied me with thine iniquities.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>I, _even_ I, _am_ he that blotteth out thy transgressions for mine own sake, and will not remember thy sins.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Put me in remembrance: let us plead together: declare thou, that thou mayest be justified.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Thy first father hath sinned, and thy teachers have transgressed against me.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Therefore I have profaned the princes of the sanctuary, and have given Jacob to the curse, and Israel to reproaches.  

## CHAPTER 44.

Yet now hear, O Jacob my servant; and Israel, whom I have chosen:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that made thee, and formed thee from the womb, _which_ will help thee; Fear not, O Jacob, my servant; and thou, Jesurun, whom I have chosen.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For I will pour water upon him that is thirsty, and floods upon the dry ground: I will pour my spirit upon thy seed, and my blessing upon thine offspring:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And they shall spring up _as_ among the grass, as willows by the water courses.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>One shall say, I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span>; and another shall call _himself_ by the name of Jacob; and another shall subscribe _with_ his hand unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and surname _himself_ by the name of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> the King of Israel, and his redeemer the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts; I _am_ the first, and I _am_ the last; and beside me _there is_ no God.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And who, as I, shall call, and shall declare it, and set it in order for me, since I appointed the ancient people? and the things that are coming, and shall come, let them shew unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Fear ye not, neither be afraid: have not I told thee from that time, and have declared _it_? ye _are_ even my witnesses. Is there a God beside me? yea, _there is_ no God; I know not _any_.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ They that make a graven image _are_ all of them vanity; and their delectable things shall not profit; and they _are_ their own witnesses; they see not, nor know; that they may be ashamed.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Who hath formed a god, or molten a graven image _that_ is profitable for nothing?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Behold, all his fellows shall be ashamed: and the workmen, they _are_ of men: let them all be gathered together, let them stand up; _yet_ they shall fear, _and_ they shall be ashamed together.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The smith with the tongs both worketh in the coals, and fashioneth it with hammers, and worketh it with the strength of his arms: yea, he is hungry, and his strength faileth: he drinketh no water, and is faint.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The carpenter stretcheth out _his_ rule; he marketh it out with a line; he fitteth it with planes, and he marketh it out with the compass, and maketh it after the figure of a man, according to the beauty of a man; that it may remain in the house.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>He heweth him down cedars, and taketh the cypress and the oak, which he strengtheneth for himself among the trees of the forest: he planteth an ash, and the rain doth nourish _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Then shall it be for a man to burn: for he will take thereof, and warm himself; yea, he kindleth _it_, and baketh bread; yea, he maketh a god, and worshippeth _it_; he maketh it a graven image, and falleth down thereto.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>He burneth part thereof in the fire; with part thereof he eateth flesh; he roasteth roast, and is satisfied: yea, he warmeth _himself_, and saith, Aha, I am warm, I have seen the fire:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the residue thereof he maketh a god, _even_ his graven image: he falleth down unto it, and worshippeth _it_, and prayeth unto it, and saith, Deliver me; for thou _art_ my god.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>They have not known nor understood: for he hath shut their eyes, that they cannot see; _and_ their hearts, that they cannot understand.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And none considereth in his heart, neither _is there_ knowledge nor understanding to say, I have burned part of it in the fire; yea, also I have baked bread upon the coals thereof; I have roasted flesh, and eaten _it_: and shall I make the residue thereof an abomination? shall I fall down to the stock of a tree?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>He feedeth on ashes: a deceived heart hath turned him aside, that he cannot deliver his soul, nor say, _Is there_ not a lie in my right hand?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Remember these, O Jacob and Israel; for thou _art_ my servant: I have formed thee; thou _art_ my servant: O Israel, thou shalt not be forgotten of me.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>I have blotted out, as a thick cloud, thy transgressions, and, as a cloud, thy sins: return unto me; for I have redeemed thee.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Sing, O ye heavens; for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath done _it_: shout, ye lower parts of the earth: break forth into singing, ye mountains, O forest, and every tree therein: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath redeemed Jacob, and glorified himself in Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thy redeemer, and he that formed thee from the womb, I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that maketh all _things_; that stretcheth forth the heavens alone; that spreadeth abroad the earth by myself;  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>That frustrateth the tokens of the liars, and maketh diviners mad; that turneth wise _men_ backward, and maketh their knowledge foolish;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>That confirmeth the word of his servant, and performeth the counsel of his messengers; that saith to Jerusalem, Thou shalt be inhabited; and to the cities of Judah, Ye shall be built, and I will raise up the decayed places thereof:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>That saith to the deep, Be dry, and I will dry up thy rivers:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>That saith of Cyrus, _He is_ my shepherd, and shall perform all my pleasure: even saying to Jerusalem, Thou shalt be built; and to the temple, Thy foundation shall be laid.  

## CHAPTER 45.

Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to his anointed, to Cyrus, whose right hand I have holden, to subdue nations before him; and I will loose the loins of kings, to open before him the two leaved gates; and the gates shall not be shut;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>I will go before thee, and make the crooked places straight: I will break in pieces the gates of brass, and cut in sunder the bars of iron:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And I will give thee the treasures of darkness, and hidden riches of secret places, that thou mayest know that I, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, which call _thee_ by thy name, _am_ the God of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For Jacob my servant’s sake, and Israel mine elect, I have even called thee by thy name: I have surnamed thee, though thou hast not known me.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and _there is_ none else, _there is_ no God beside me: I girded thee, though thou hast not known me:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>That they may know from the rising of the sun, and from the west, that _there is_ none beside me. I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and _there is_ none else.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>I form the light, and create darkness: I make peace, and create evil: I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> do all these _things_.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Drop down, ye heavens, from above, and let the skies pour down righteousness: let the earth open, and let them bring forth salvation, and let righteousness spring up together; I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have created it.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Woe unto him that striveth with his Maker! _Let_ the potsherd _strive_ with the potsherds of the earth. Shall the clay say to him that fashioneth it, What makest thou? or thy work, He hath no hands?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Woe unto him that saith unto _his_ father, What begettest thou? or to the woman, What hast thou brought forth?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the Holy One of Israel, and his Maker, Ask me of things to come concerning my sons, and concerning the work of my hands command ye me.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I have made the earth, and created man upon it: I, _even_ my hands, have stretched out the heavens, and all their host have I commanded.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>I have raised him up in righteousness, and I will direct all his ways: he shall build my city, and he shall let go my captives, not for price nor reward, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, The labour of Egypt, and merchandise of Ethiopia and of the Sabeans, men of stature, shall come over unto thee, and they shall be thine: they shall come after thee; in chains they shall come over, and they shall fall down unto thee, they shall make supplication unto thee, _saying_, Surely God _is_ in thee; and _there is_ none else, _there is_ no God.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Verily thou _art_ a God that hidest thyself, O God of Israel, the Saviour.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>They shall be ashamed, and also confounded, all of them: they shall go to confusion together _that are_ makers of idols.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>_But_ Israel shall be saved in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> with an everlasting salvation: ye shall not be ashamed nor confounded world without end.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that created the heavens; God himself that formed the earth and made it; he hath established it, he created it not in vain, he formed it to be inhabited: I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and _there is_ none else.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>I have not spoken in secret, in a dark place of the earth: I said not unto the seed of Jacob, Seek ye me in vain: I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> speak righteousness, I declare things that are right.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Assemble yourselves and come; draw near together, ye _that are_ escaped of the nations: they have no knowledge that set up the wood of their graven image, and pray unto a god _that_ cannot save.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Tell ye, and bring _them_ near; yea, let them take counsel together: who hath declared this from ancient time? _who_ hath told it from that time? _have_ not I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>? and _there is_ no God else beside me; a just God and a Saviour; _there is_ none beside me.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Look unto me, and be ye saved, all the ends of the earth: for I _am_ God, and _there is_ none else.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>I have sworn by myself, the word is gone out of my mouth _in_ righteousness, and shall not return, That unto me every knee shall bow, every tongue shall swear.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Surely, shall _one_ say, in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have I righteousness and strength: _even_ to him shall _men_ come; and all that are incensed against him shall be ashamed.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>In the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall all the seed of Israel be justified, and shall glory.  

## CHAPTER 46.

Bel boweth down, Nebo stoopeth, their idols were upon the beasts, and upon the cattle: your carriages _were_ heavy loaden; _they are_ a burden to the weary _beast_.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>They stoop, they bow down together; they could not deliver the burden, but themselves are gone into captivity.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Hearken unto me, O house of Jacob, and all the remnant of the house of Israel, which are borne _by me_ from the belly, which are carried from the womb:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And _even_ to _your_ old age I _am_ he; and _even_ to hoar hairs will I carry _you_: I have made, and I will bear; even I will carry, and will deliver _you_.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ To whom will ye liken me, and make _me_ equal, and compare me, that we may be like?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>They lavish gold out of the bag, and weigh silver in the balance, _and_ hire a goldsmith; and he maketh it a god: they fall down, yea, they worship.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>They bear him upon the shoulder, they carry him, and set him in his place, and he standeth; from his place shall he not remove: yea, _one_ shall cry unto him, yet can he not answer, nor save him out of his trouble.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Remember this, and shew yourselves men: bring _it_ again to mind, O ye transgressors.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Remember the former things of old: for I _am_ God, and _there is_ none else; _I am_ God, and _there is_ none like me,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Declaring the end from the beginning, and from ancient times _the things_ that are not _yet_ done, saying, My counsel shall stand, and I will do all my pleasure:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Calling a ravenous bird from the east, the man that executeth my counsel from a far country: yea, I have spoken _it_, I will also bring it to pass; I have purposed _it_, I will also do it.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Hearken unto me, ye stouthearted, that _are_ far from righteousness:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>I bring near my righteousness; it shall not be far off, and my salvation shall not tarry: and I will place salvation in Zion for Israel my glory.  

## CHAPTER 47.

Come down, and sit in the dust, O virgin daughter of Babylon, sit on the ground: _there is_ no throne, O daughter of the Chaldeans: for thou shalt no more be called tender and delicate.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Take the millstones, and grind meal: uncover thy locks, make bare the leg, uncover the thigh, pass over the rivers.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Thy nakedness shall be uncovered, yea, thy shame shall be seen: I will take vengeance, and I will not meet _thee as_ a man.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>_As for_ our redeemer, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts _is_ his name, the Holy One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Sit thou silent, and get thee into darkness, O daughter of the Chaldeans: for thou shalt no more be called, The lady of kingdoms.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ I was wroth with my people, I have polluted mine inheritance, and given them into thine hand: thou didst shew them no mercy; upon the ancient hast thou very heavily laid thy yoke.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And thou saidst, I shall be a lady for ever: _so_ that thou didst not lay these _things_ to thy heart, neither didst remember the latter end of it.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Therefore hear now this, _thou that art_ given to pleasures, that dwellest carelessly, that sayest in thine heart, I _am_, and none else beside me; I shall not sit _as_ a widow, neither shall I know the loss of children:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But these two _things_ shall come to thee in a moment in one day, the loss of children, and widowhood: they shall come upon thee in their perfection for the multitude of thy sorceries, _and_ for the great abundance of thine enchantments.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ For thou hast trusted in thy wickedness: thou hast said, None seeth me. Thy wisdom and thy knowledge, it hath perverted thee; and thou hast said in thine heart, I _am_, and none else beside me.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore shall evil come upon thee; thou shalt not know from whence it riseth: and mischief shall fall upon thee; thou shalt not be able to put it off: and desolation shall come upon thee suddenly, _which_ thou shalt not know.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Stand now with thine enchantments, and with the multitude of thy sorceries, wherein thou hast laboured from thy youth; if so be thou shalt be able to profit, if so be thou mayest prevail.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thou art wearied in the multitude of thy counsels. Let now the astrologers, the stargazers, the monthly prognosticators, stand up, and save thee from _these things_ that shall come upon thee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Behold, they shall be as stubble; the fire shall burn them; they shall not deliver themselves from the power of the flame: _there shall_ not _be_ a coal to warm at, _nor_ fire to sit before it.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thus shall they be unto thee with whom thou hast laboured, _even_ thy merchants, from thy youth: they shall wander every one to his quarter; none shall save thee.  

## CHAPTER 48.

Hear ye this, O house of Jacob, which are called by the name of Israel, and are come forth out of the waters of Judah, which swear by the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and make mention of the God of Israel, _but_ not in truth, nor in righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For they call themselves of the holy city, and stay themselves upon the God of Israel; The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts _is_ his name.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I have declared the former things from the beginning; and they went forth out of my mouth, and I shewed them; I did _them_ suddenly, and they came to pass.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Because I knew that thou _art_ obstinate, and thy neck _is_ an iron sinew, and thy brow brass;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>I have even from the beginning declared _it_ to thee; before it came to pass I shewed _it_ thee: lest thou shouldest say, Mine idol hath done them, and my graven image, and my molten image, hath commanded them.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Thou hast heard, see all this; and will not ye declare _it_? I have shewed thee new things from this time, even hidden things, and thou didst not know them.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>They are created now, and not from the beginning; even before the day when thou heardest them not; lest thou shouldest say, Behold, I knew them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Yea, thou heardest not; yea, thou knewest not; yea, from that time _that_ thine ear was not opened: for I knew that thou wouldest deal very treacherously, and wast called a transgressor from the womb.

<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then I said, I have laboured in vain, I have spent my strength for nought, and in vain: _yet_ surely my judgment _is_ with the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and my work with my God.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And now, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that formed me from the womb _to be_ his servant, to bring Jacob again to him, Though Israel be not gathered, yet shall I be glorious in the eyes of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and my God shall be my strength.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he said, It is a light thing that thou shouldest be my servant to raise up the tribes of Jacob, and to restore the preserved of Israel: I will also give thee for a light to the Gentiles, that thou mayest be my salvation unto the end of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the Redeemer of Israel, _and_ his Holy One, to him whom man despiseth, to him whom the nation abhorreth, to a servant of rulers, Kings shall see and arise, princes also shall worship, because of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that is faithful, _and_ the Holy One of Israel, and he shall choose thee.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, In an acceptable time have I heard thee, and in a day of salvation have I helped thee: and I will preserve thee, and give thee for a covenant of the people, to establish the earth, to cause to inherit the desolate heritages;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>That thou mayest say to the prisoners, Go forth; to them that _are_ in darkness, Shew yourselves. They shall feed in the ways, and their pastures _shall be_ in all high places.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>They shall not hunger nor thirst; neither shall the heat nor sun smite them: for he that hath mercy on them shall lead them, even by the springs of water shall he guide them.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And I will make all my mountains a way, and my highways shall be exalted.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Behold, these shall come from far: and, lo, these from the north and from the west; and these from the land of Sinim.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Sing, O heavens; and be joyful, O earth; and break forth into singing, O mountains: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath comforted his people, and will have mercy upon his afflicted.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But Zion said, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath forsaken me, and my Lord hath forgotten me.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Can a woman forget her sucking child, that she should not have compassion on the son of her womb? yea, they may forget, yet will I not forget thee.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Behold, I have graven thee upon the palms of _my_ hands; thy walls _are_ continually before me.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thy children shall make haste; thy destroyers and they that made thee waste shall go forth of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Lift up thine eyes round about, and behold: all these gather themselves together, _and_ come to thee. _As_ I live, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou shalt surely clothe thee with them all, as with an ornament, and bind them _on thee_, as a bride _doeth_.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For thy waste and thy desolate places, and the land of thy destruction, shall even now be too narrow by reason of the inhabitants, and they that swallowed thee up shall be far away.

<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou say in thine heart, Who hath begotten me these, seeing I have lost my children, and am desolate, a captive, and removing to and fro? and who hath brought up these? Behold, I was left alone; these, where _had_ they _been_?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, Behold, I will lift up mine hand to the Gentiles, and set up my standard to the people: and they shall bring thy sons in _their_ arms, and thy daughters shall be carried upon _their_ shoulders.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And kings shall be thy nursing fathers, and their queens thy nursing mothers: they shall bow down to thee with _their_ face toward the earth, and lick up the dust of thy feet; and thou shalt know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: for they shall not be ashamed that wait for me.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Shall the prey be taken from the mighty, or the lawful captive delivered?

<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And I will feed them that oppress thee with their own flesh; and they shall be drunken with their own blood, as with sweet wine: and all flesh shall know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _am_ thy Saviour and thy Redeemer, the mighty One of Jacob.  

## CHAPTER 50.

Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Where _is_ the bill of your mother’s divorcement, whom I have put away? or which of my creditors _is it_ to whom I have sold you? Behold, for your iniquities have ye sold yourselves, and for your transgressions is your mother put away.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Wherefore, when I came, _was there_ no man? when I called, _was there_ none to answer? Is my hand shortened at all, that it cannot redeem? or have I no power to deliver? behold, at my rebuke I dry up the sea, I make the rivers a wilderness: their fish stinketh, because _there is_ no water, and dieth for thirst.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I clothe the heavens with blackness, and I make sackcloth their covering.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ The Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> hath given me the tongue of the learned, that I should know how to speak a word in season to _him that is_ weary: he wakeneth morning by morning, he wakeneth mine ear to hear as the learned.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ The Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> hath opened mine ear, and I was not rebellious, neither turned away back.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>I gave my back to the smiters, and my cheeks to them that plucked off the hair: I hid not my face from shame and spitting.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ For the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> will help me; therefore shall I not be confounded: therefore have I set my face like a flint, and I know that I shall not be ashamed.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>_He is_ near that justifieth me; who will contend with me? let us stand together: who _is_ mine adversary? let him come near to me.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Behold, the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> will help me; who _is_ he _that_ shall condemn me? lo, they all shall wax old as a garment; the moth shall eat them up.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Who _is_ among you that feareth the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that obeyeth the voice of his servant, that walketh _in_ darkness, and hath no light? let him trust in the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and stay upon his God.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Behold, all ye that kindle a fire, that compass _yourselves_ about with sparks: walk in the light of your fire, and in the sparks _that_ ye have kindled. This shall ye have of mine hand; ye shall lie down in sorrow.  

## CHAPTER 51.

Hearken to me, ye that follow after righteousness, ye that seek the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: look unto the rock _whence_ ye are hewn, and to the hole of the pit _whence_ ye are digged.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Look unto Abraham your father, and unto Sarah _that_ bare you: for I called him alone, and blessed him, and increased him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall comfort Zion: he will comfort all her waste places; and he will make her wilderness like Eden, and her desert like the garden of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; joy and gladness shall be found therein, thanksgiving, and the voice of melody.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Hearken unto me, my people; and give ear unto me, O my nation: for a law shall proceed from me, and I will make my judgment to rest for a light of the people.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>My righteousness _is_ near; my salvation is gone forth, and mine arms shall judge the people; the isles shall wait upon me, and on mine arm shall they trust.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Lift up your eyes to the heavens, and look upon the earth beneath: for the heavens shall vanish away like smoke, and the earth shall wax old like a garment, and they that dwell therein shall die in like manner: but my salvation shall be for ever, and my righteousness shall not be abolished.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Hearken unto me, ye that know righteousness, the people in whose heart _is_ my law; fear ye not the reproach of men, neither be ye afraid of their revilings.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For the moth shall eat them up like a garment, and the worm shall eat them like wool: but my righteousness shall be for ever, and my salvation from generation to generation.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Awake, awake, put on strength, O arm of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; awake, as in the ancient days, in the generations of old. _Art_ thou not it that hath cut Rahab, _and_ wounded the dragon?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>_Art_ thou not it which hath dried the sea, the waters of the great deep; that hath made the depths of the sea a way for the ransomed to pass over?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Therefore the redeemed of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall return, and come with singing unto Zion; and everlasting joy _shall be_ upon their head: they shall obtain gladness and joy; _and_ sorrow and mourning shall flee away.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I, _even_ I, _am_ he that comforteth you: who _art_ thou, that thou shouldest be afraid of a man _that_ shall die, and of the son of man _which_ shall be made _as_ grass;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And forgettest the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy maker, that hath stretched forth the heavens, and laid the foundations of the earth; and hast feared continually every day because of the fury of the oppressor, as if he were ready to destroy? and where _is_ the fury of the oppressor?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The captive exile hasteneth that he may be loosed, and that he should not die in the pit, nor that his bread should fail.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, that divided the sea, whose waves roared: The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts _is_ his name.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And I have put my words in thy mouth, and I have covered thee in the shadow of mine hand, that I may plant the heavens, and lay the foundations of the earth, and say unto Zion, Thou _art_ my people.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Awake, awake, stand up, O Jerusalem, which hast drunk at the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> the cup of his fury; thou hast drunken the dregs of the cup of trembling, _and_ wrung _them_ out.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>_There is_ none to guide her among all the sons _whom_ she hath brought forth; neither _is there any_ that taketh her by the hand of all the sons _that_ she hath brought up.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>These two _things_ are come unto thee; who shall be sorry for thee? desolation, and destruction, and the famine, and the sword: by whom shall I comfort thee?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Thy sons have fainted, they lie at the head of all the streets, as a wild bull in a net: they are full of the fury of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the rebuke of thy God.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore hear now this, thou afflicted, and drunken, but not with wine:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Thus saith thy Lord the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and thy God _that_ pleadeth the cause of his people, Behold, I have taken out of thine hand the cup of trembling, _even_ the dregs of the cup of my fury; thou shalt no more drink it again:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But I will put it into the hand of them that afflict thee; which have said to thy soul, Bow down, that we may go over: and thou hast laid thy body as the ground, and as the street, to them that went over.  

## CHAPTER 52.

Awake, awake; put on thy strength, O Zion; put on thy beautiful garments, O Jerusalem, the holy city: for henceforth there shall no more come into thee the uncircumcised and the unclean.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Shake thyself from the dust; arise, _and_ sit down, O Jerusalem: loose thyself from the bands of thy neck, O captive daughter of Zion.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Ye have sold yourselves for nought; and ye shall be redeemed without money.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, My people went down aforetime into Egypt to sojourn there; and the Assyrian oppressed them without cause.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Now therefore, what have I here, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that my people is taken away for nought? they that rule over them make them to howl, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and my name continually every day _is_ blasphemed.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Therefore my people shall know my name: therefore _they shall know_ in that day that I _am_ he that doth speak: behold, _it is_ I.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings, that publisheth peace; that bringeth good tidings of good, that publisheth salvation; that saith unto Zion, Thy God reigneth!  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Thy watchmen shall lift up the voice; with the voice together shall they sing: for they shall see eye to eye, when the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall bring again Zion.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Break forth into joy, sing together, ye waste places of Jerusalem: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath comforted his people, he hath redeemed Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath made bare his holy arm in the eyes of all the nations; and all the ends of the earth shall see the salvation of our God.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Depart ye, depart ye, go ye out from thence, touch no unclean _thing_; go ye out of the midst of her; be ye clean, that bear the vessels of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For ye shall not go out with haste, nor go by flight: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will go before you; and the God of Israel _will be_ your rereward.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, my servant shall deal prudently, he shall be exalted and extolled, and be very high.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>As many were astonied at thee; his visage was so marred more than any man, and his form more than the sons of men:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So shall he sprinkle many nations; the kings shall shut their mouths at him: for _that_ which had not been told them shall they see; and _that_ which they had not heard shall they consider.  

## CHAPTER 53.

Who hath believed our report? and to whom is the arm of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> revealed?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For he shall grow up before him as a tender plant, and as a root out of a dry ground: he hath no form nor comeliness; and when we shall see him, _there is_ no beauty that we should desire him.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>He is despised and rejected of men; a man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief: and we hid as it were _our_ faces from him; he was despised, and we esteemed him not.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But he _was_ wounded for our transgressions, _he was_ bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace _was_ upon him; and with his stripes we are healed.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath laid on him the iniquity of us all.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth: he is brought as a lamb to the slaughter, and as a sheep before her shearers is dumb, so he openeth not his mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He was taken from prison and from judgment: and who shall declare his generation? for he was cut off out of the land of the living: for the transgression of my people was he stricken.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he made his grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death; because he had done no violence, neither _was any_ deceit in his mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Yet it pleased the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to bruise him; he hath put _him_ to grief: when thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin, he shall see _his_ seed, he shall prolong _his_ days, and the pleasure of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall prosper in his hand.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He shall see of the travail of his soul, _and_ shall be satisfied: by his knowledge shall my righteous servant justify many; for he shall bear their iniquities.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Therefore will I divide him _a portion_ with the great, and he shall divide the spoil with the strong; because he hath poured out his soul unto death: and he was numbered with the transgressors; and he bare the sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors.  

## CHAPTER 54.

Sing, O barren, thou _that_ didst not bear; break forth into singing, and cry aloud, thou _that_ didst not travail with child: for more _are_ the children of the desolate than the children of the married wife, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Enlarge the place of thy tent, and let them stretch forth the curtains of thine habitations: spare not, lengthen thy cords, and strengthen thy stakes;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For thou shalt break forth on the right hand and on the left; and thy seed shall inherit the Gentiles, and make the desolate cities to be inhabited.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Fear not; for thou shalt not be ashamed: neither be thou confounded; for thou shalt not be put to shame: for thou shalt forget the shame of thy youth, and shalt not remember the reproach of thy widowhood any more.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For thy Maker _is_ thine husband; the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts _is_ his name; and thy Redeemer the Holy One of Israel; The God of the whole earth shall he be called.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath called thee as a woman forsaken and grieved in spirit, and a wife of youth, when thou wast refused, saith thy God.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For a small moment have I forsaken thee; but with great mercies will I gather thee.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>In a little wrath I hid my face from thee for a moment; but with everlasting kindness will I have mercy on thee, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy Redeemer.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For this _is as_ the waters of Noah unto me: for _as_ I have sworn that the waters of Noah should no more go over the earth; so have I sworn that I would not be wroth with thee, nor rebuke thee.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For the mountains shall depart, and the hills be removed; but my kindness shall not depart from thee, neither shall the covenant of my peace be removed, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that hath mercy on thee.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ O thou afflicted, tossed with tempest, _and_ not comforted, behold, I will lay thy stones with fair colours, and lay thy foundations with sapphires.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And I will make thy windows of agates, and thy gates of carbuncles, and all thy borders of pleasant stones.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And all thy children _shall be_ taught of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and great _shall be_ the peace of thy children.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>In righteousness shalt thou be established: thou shalt be far from oppression; for thou shalt not fear: and from terror; for it shall not come near thee.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Behold, they shall surely gather together, _but_ not by me: whosoever shall gather together against thee shall fall for thy sake.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Behold, I have created the smith that bloweth the coals in the fire, and that bringeth forth an instrument for his work; and I have created the waster to destroy.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ No weapon that is formed against thee shall prosper; and every tongue _that_ shall rise against thee in judgment thou shalt condemn. This _is_ the heritage of the servants of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and their righteousness _is_ of me, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 55.

Ho, every one that thirsteth, come ye to the waters, and he that hath no money; come ye, buy, and eat; yea, come, buy wine and milk without money and without price.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Wherefore do ye spend money for _that which is_ not bread? and your labour for _that which_ satisfieth not? hearken diligently unto me, and eat ye _that which is_ good, and let your soul delight itself in fatness.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Incline your ear, and come unto me: hear, and your soul shall live; and I will make an everlasting covenant with you, _even_ the sure mercies of David.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Behold, I have given him _for_ a witness to the people, a leader and commander to the people.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Behold, thou shalt call a nation _that_ thou knowest not, and nations _that_ knew not thee shall run unto thee because of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, and for the Holy One of Israel; for he hath glorified thee.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Seek ye the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> while he may be found, call ye upon him while he is near:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Let the wicked forsake his way, and the unrighteous man his thoughts: and let him return unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and he will have mercy upon him; and to our God, for he will abundantly pardon.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ For my thoughts _are_ not your thoughts, neither _are_ your ways my ways, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For _as_ the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways, and my thoughts than your thoughts.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For as the rain cometh down, and the snow from heaven, and returneth not thither, but watereth the earth, and maketh it bring forth and bud, that it may give seed to the sower, and bread to the eater:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>So shall my word be that goeth forth out of my mouth: it shall not return unto me void, but it shall accomplish that which I please, and it shall prosper _in the thing_ whereto I sent it.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For ye shall go out with joy, and be led forth with peace: the mountains and the hills shall break forth before you into singing, and all the trees of the field shall clap _their_ hands.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Instead of the thorn shall come up the fir tree, and instead of the brier shall come up the myrtle tree: and it shall be to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> for a name, for an everlasting sign _that_ shall not be cut off.  

## CHAPTER 56.

Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Keep ye judgment, and do justice: for my salvation _is_ near to come, and my righteousness to be revealed.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Blessed _is_ the man _that_ doeth this, and the son of man _that_ layeth hold on it; that keepeth the sabbath from polluting it, and keepeth his hand from doing any evil.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Neither let the son of the stranger, that hath joined himself to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, speak, saying, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath utterly separated me from his people: neither let the eunuch say, Behold, I _am_ a dry tree.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto the eunuchs that keep my sabbaths, and choose _the things_ that please me, and take hold of my covenant;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Even unto them will I give in mine house and within my walls a place and a name better than of sons and of daughters: I will give them an everlasting name, that shall not be cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Also the sons of the stranger, that join themselves to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to serve him, and to love the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to be his servants, every one that keepeth the sabbath from polluting it, and taketh hold of my covenant;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Even them will I bring to my holy mountain, and make them joyful in my house of prayer: their burnt offerings and their sacrifices _shall be_ accepted upon mine altar; for mine house shall be called an house of prayer for all people.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> which gathereth the outcasts of Israel saith, Yet will I gather _others_ to him, beside those that are gathered unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ All ye beasts of the field, come to devour, _yea_, all ye beasts in the forest.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>His watchmen _are_ blind: they are all ignorant, they _are_ all dumb dogs, they cannot bark; sleeping, lying down, loving to slumber.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Yea, _they are_ greedy dogs _which_ can never have enough, and they _are_ shepherds _that_ cannot understand: they all look to their own way, every one for his gain, from his quarter.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Come ye, _say they_, I will fetch wine, and we will fill ourselves with strong drink; and to morrow shall be as this day, _and_ much more abundant.  

## CHAPTER 57.

The righteous perisheth, and no man layeth _it_ to heart: and merciful men _are_ taken away, none considering that the righteous is taken away from the evil _to come_.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>He shall enter into peace: they shall rest in their beds, _each one_ walking _in_ his uprightness.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ But draw near hither, ye sons of the sorceress, the seed of the adulterer and the whore.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Against whom do ye sport yourselves? against whom make ye a wide mouth, _and_ draw out the tongue? _are_ ye not children of transgression, a seed of falsehood,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Enflaming yourselves with idols under every green tree, slaying the children in the valleys under the clifts of the rocks?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Among the smooth _stones_ of the stream _is_ thy portion; they, they _are_ thy lot: even to them hast thou poured a drink offering, thou hast offered a meat offering. Should I receive comfort in these?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Upon a lofty and high mountain hast thou set thy bed: even thither wentest thou up to offer sacrifice.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Behind the doors also and the posts hast thou set up thy remembrance: for thou hast discovered _thyself to another_ than me, and art gone up; thou hast enlarged thy bed, and made thee _a covenant_ with them; thou lovedst their bed where thou sawest _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And thou wentest to the king with ointment, and didst increase thy perfumes, and didst send thy messengers far off, and didst debase _thyself even_ unto hell.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thou art wearied in the greatness of thy way; _yet_ saidst thou not, There is no hope: thou hast found the life of thine hand; therefore thou wast not grieved.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And of whom hast thou been afraid or feared, that thou hast lied, and hast not remembered me, nor laid _it_ to thy heart? have not I held my peace even of old, and thou fearest me not?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I will declare thy righteousness, and thy works; for they shall not profit thee.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ When thou criest, let thy companies deliver thee; but the wind shall carry them all away; vanity shall take _them_: but he that putteth his trust in me shall possess the land, and shall inherit my holy mountain;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And shall say, Cast ye up, cast ye up, prepare the way, take up the stumblingblock out of the way of my people.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For thus saith the high and lofty One that inhabiteth eternity, whose name _is_ Holy; I dwell in the high and holy _place_, with him also _that is_ of a contrite and humble spirit, to revive the spirit of the humble, and to revive the heart of the contrite ones.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For I will not contend for ever, neither will I be always wroth: for the spirit should fail before me, and the souls _which_ I have made.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ For the iniquity of his covetousness was I wroth, and smote him: I hid me, and was wroth, and he went on frowardly in the way of his heart.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>I have seen his ways, and will heal him: I will lead him also, and restore comforts unto him and to his mourners.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>I create the fruit of the lips; Peace, peace to _him that is_ far off, and to _him that is_ near, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and I will heal him.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But the wicked _are_ like the troubled sea, when it cannot rest, whose waters cast up mire and dirt.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>_There is_ no peace, saith my God, to the wicked.  

## CHAPTER 58.

Cry aloud, spare not, lift up thy voice like a trumpet, and shew my people their transgression, and the house of Jacob their sins.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Yet they seek me daily, and delight to know my ways, as a nation that did righteousness, and forsook not the ordinance of their God: they ask of me the ordinances of justice; they take delight in approaching to God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore have we fasted, _say they_, and thou seest not? _wherefore_ have we afflicted our soul, and thou takest no knowledge? Behold, in the day of your fast ye find pleasure, and exact all your labours.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Behold, ye fast for strife and debate, and to smite with the fist of wickedness: ye shall not fast as _ye do this_ day, to make your voice to be heard on high.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Is it such a fast that I have chosen? a day for a man to afflict his soul? _is it_ to bow down his head as a bulrush, and to spread sackcloth and ashes _under him_? wilt thou call this a fast, and an acceptable day to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>_Is_ not this the fast that I have chosen? to loose the bands of wickedness, to undo the heavy burdens, and to let the oppressed go free, and that ye break every yoke?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_Is it_ not to deal thy bread to the hungry, and that thou bring the poor that are cast out to thy house? when thou seest the naked, that thou cover him; and that thou hide not thyself from thine own flesh?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Then shall thy light break forth as the morning, and thine health shall spring forth speedily: and thy righteousness shall go before thee; the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be thy rereward.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou call, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall answer; thou shalt cry, and he shall say, Here I _am_. If thou take away from the midst of thee the yoke, the putting forth of the finger, and speaking vanity;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And _if_ thou draw out thy soul to the hungry, and satisfy the afflicted soul; then shall thy light rise in obscurity, and thy darkness _be_ as the noonday:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall guide thee continually, and satisfy thy soul in drought, and make fat thy bones: and thou shalt be like a watered garden, and like a spring of water, whose waters fail not.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And _they that shall be_ of thee shall build the old waste places: thou shalt raise up the foundations of many generations; and thou shalt be called, The repairer of the breach, The restorer of paths to dwell in.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ If thou turn away thy foot from the sabbath, _from_ doing thy pleasure on my holy day; and call the sabbath a delight, the holy of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, honourable; and shalt honour him, not doing thine own ways, nor finding thine own pleasure, nor speaking _thine own_ words:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou delight thyself in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and I will cause thee to ride upon the high places of the earth, and feed thee with the heritage of Jacob thy father: for the mouth of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken _it_.  

## CHAPTER 59.

Behold, the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> hand is not shortened, that it cannot save; neither his ear heavy, that it cannot hear:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>But your iniquities have separated between you and your God, and your sins have hid _his_ face from you, that he will not hear.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For your hands are defiled with blood, and your fingers with iniquity; your lips have spoken lies, your tongue hath muttered perverseness.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>None calleth for justice, nor _any_ pleadeth for truth: they trust in vanity, and speak lies; they conceive mischief, and bring forth iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>They hatch cockatrice’ eggs, and weave the spider’s web: he that eateth of their eggs dieth, and that which is crushed breaketh out into a viper.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Their webs shall not become garments, neither shall they cover themselves with their works: their works _are_ works of iniquity, and the act of violence _is_ in their hands.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Their feet run to evil, and they make haste to shed innocent blood: their thoughts _are_ thoughts of iniquity; wasting and destruction _are_ in their paths.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The way of peace they know not; and _there is_ no judgment in their goings: they have made them crooked paths: whosoever goeth therein shall not know peace.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore is judgment far from us, neither doth justice overtake us: we wait for light, but behold obscurity; for brightness, _but_ we walk in darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>We grope for the wall like the blind, and we grope as if _we had_ no eyes: we stumble at noonday as in the night; _we are_ in desolate places as dead _men_.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>We roar all like bears, and mourn sore like doves: we look for judgment, but _there is_ none; for salvation, _but_ it is far off from us.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For our transgressions are multiplied before thee, and our sins testify against us: for our transgressions _are_ with us; and _as for_ our iniquities, we know them;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>In transgressing and lying against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and departing away from our God, speaking oppression and revolt, conceiving and uttering from the heart words of falsehood.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And judgment is turned away backward, and justice standeth afar off: for truth is fallen in the street, and equity cannot enter.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Yea, truth faileth; and he _that_ departeth from evil maketh himself a prey: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> saw _it_, and it displeased him that _there was_ no judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And he saw that _there was_ no man, and wondered that _there was_ no intercessor: therefore his arm brought salvation unto him; and his righteousness, it sustained him.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For he put on righteousness as a breastplate, and an helmet of salvation upon his head; and he put on the garments of vengeance _for_ clothing, and was clad with zeal as a cloke.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>According to _their_ deeds, accordingly he will repay, fury to his adversaries, recompence to his enemies; to the islands he will repay recompence.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>So shall they fear the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> from the west, and his glory from the rising of the sun. When the enemy shall come in like a flood, the Spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall lift up a standard against him.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And the Redeemer shall come to Zion, and unto them that turn from transgression in Jacob, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>As for me, this _is_ my covenant with them, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; My spirit that _is_ upon thee, and my words which I have put in thy mouth, shall not depart out of thy mouth, nor out of the mouth of thy seed, nor out of the mouth of thy seed’s seed, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, from henceforth and for ever.  

## CHAPTER 60.

Arise, shine; for thy light is come, and the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> is risen upon thee.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For, behold, the darkness shall cover the earth, and gross darkness the people: but the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall arise upon thee, and his glory shall be seen upon thee.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the Gentiles shall come to thy light, and kings to the brightness of thy rising.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Lift up thine eyes round about, and see: all they gather themselves together, they come to thee: thy sons shall come from far, and thy daughters shall be nursed at _thy_ side.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Then thou shalt see, and flow together, and thine heart shall fear, and be enlarged; because the abundance of the sea shall be converted unto thee, the forces of the Gentiles shall come unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The multitude of camels shall cover thee, the dromedaries of Midian and Ephah; all they from Sheba shall come: they shall bring gold and incense; and they shall shew forth the praises of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>All the flocks of Kedar shall be gathered together unto thee, the rams of Nebaioth shall minister unto thee: they shall come up with acceptance on mine altar, and I will glorify the house of my glory.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Who _are_ these _that_ fly as a cloud, and as the doves to their windows?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Surely the isles shall wait for me, and the ships of Tarshish first, to bring thy sons from far, their silver and their gold with them, unto the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, and to the Holy One of Israel, because he hath glorified thee.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the sons of strangers shall build up thy walls, and their kings shall minister unto thee: for in my wrath I smote thee, but in my favour have I had mercy on thee.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Therefore thy gates shall be open continually; they shall not be shut day nor night; that _men_ may bring unto thee the forces of the Gentiles, and _that_ their kings _may be_ brought.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the nation and kingdom that will not serve thee shall perish; yea, _those_ nations shall be utterly wasted.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The glory of Lebanon shall come unto thee, the fir tree, the pine tree, and the box together, to beautify the place of my sanctuary; and I will make the place of my feet glorious.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The sons also of them that afflicted thee shall come bending unto thee; and all they that despised thee shall bow themselves down at the soles of thy feet; and they shall call thee, The city of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, The Zion of the Holy One of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Whereas thou hast been forsaken and hated, so that no man went through _thee_, I will make thee an eternal excellency, a joy of many generations.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Thou shalt also suck the milk of the Gentiles, and shalt suck the breast of kings: and thou shalt know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _am_ thy Saviour and thy Redeemer, the mighty One of Jacob.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For brass I will bring gold, and for iron I will bring silver, and for wood brass, and for stones iron: I will also make thy officers peace, and thine exactors righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Violence shall no more be heard in thy land, wasting nor destruction within thy borders; but thou shalt call thy walls Salvation, and thy gates Praise.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The sun shall be no more thy light by day; neither for brightness shall the moon give light unto thee: but the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be unto thee an everlasting light, and thy God thy glory.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Thy sun shall no more go down; neither shall thy moon withdraw itself: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be thine everlasting light, and the days of thy mourning shall be ended.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Thy people also _shall be_ all righteous: they shall inherit the land for ever, the branch of my planting, the work of my hands, that I may be glorified.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>A little one shall become a thousand, and a small one a strong nation: I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will hasten it in his time.  

## CHAPTER 61.

The Spirit of the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> _is_ upon me; because the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath anointed me to preach good tidings unto the meek; he hath sent me to bind up the brokenhearted, to proclaim liberty to the captives, and the opening of the prison to _them that are_ bound;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>To proclaim the acceptable year of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and the day of vengeance of our God; to comfort all that mourn;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>To appoint unto them that mourn in Zion, to give unto them beauty for ashes, the oil of joy for mourning, the garment of praise for the spirit of heaviness; that they might be called trees of righteousness, the planting of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that he might be glorified.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And they shall build the old wastes, they shall raise up the former desolations, and they shall repair the waste cities, the desolations of many generations.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And strangers shall stand and feed your flocks, and the sons of the alien _shall be_ your plowmen and your vinedressers.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But ye shall be named the Priests of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: _men_ shall call you the Ministers of our God: ye shall eat the riches of the Gentiles, and in their glory shall ye boast yourselves.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ For your shame _ye shall have_ double; and _for_ confusion they shall rejoice in their portion: therefore in their land they shall possess the double: everlasting joy shall be unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> love judgment, I hate robbery for burnt offering; and I will direct their work in truth, and I will make an everlasting covenant with them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And their seed shall be known among the Gentiles, and their offspring among the people: all that see them shall acknowledge them, that they _are_ the seed _which_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath blessed.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ I will greatly rejoice in the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, my soul shall be joyful in my God; for he hath clothed me with the garments of salvation, he hath covered me with the robe of righteousness, as a bridegroom decketh _himself_ with ornaments, and as a bride adorneth _herself_ with her jewels.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For as the earth bringeth forth her bud, and as the garden causeth the things that are sown in it to spring forth; so the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> will cause righteousness and praise to spring forth before all the nations.  

## CHAPTER 62.

For Zion’s sake will I not hold my peace, and for Jerusalem’s sake I will not rest, until the righteousness thereof go forth as brightness, and the salvation thereof as a lamp _that_ burneth.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the Gentiles shall see thy righteousness, and all kings thy glory: and thou shalt be called by a new name, which the mouth of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall name.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Thou shalt also be a crown of glory in the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and a royal diadem in the hand of thy God.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Thou shalt no more be termed Forsaken; neither shall thy land any more be termed Desolate: but thou shalt be called Hephzi–bah, and thy land Beulah: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> delighteth in thee, and thy land shall be married.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ For _as_ a young man marrieth a virgin, _so_ shall thy sons marry thee: and _as_ the bridegroom rejoiceth over the bride, _so_ shall thy God rejoice over thee.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ I have set watchmen upon thy walls, O Jerusalem, _which_ shall never hold their peace day nor night: ye that make mention of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, keep not silence,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And give him no rest, till he establish, and till he make Jerusalem a praise in the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath sworn by his right hand, and by the arm of his strength, Surely I will no more give thy corn _to be_ meat for thine enemies; and the sons of the stranger shall not drink thy wine, for the which thou hast laboured:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But they that have gathered it shall eat it, and praise the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and they that have brought it together shall drink it in the courts of my holiness.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Go through, go through the gates; prepare ye the way of the people; cast up, cast up the highway; gather out the stones; lift up a standard for the people.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Behold, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath proclaimed unto the end of the world, Say ye to the daughter of Zion, Behold, thy salvation cometh; behold, his reward _is_ with him, and his work before him.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And they shall call them, The holy people, The redeemed of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and thou shalt be called, Sought out, A city not forsaken.  

## CHAPTER 63.

Who _is_ this that cometh from Edom, with dyed garments from Bozrah? this _that is_ glorious in his apparel, travelling in the greatness of his strength? I that speak in righteousness, mighty to save.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Wherefore _art thou_ red in thine apparel, and thy garments like him that treadeth in the winefat?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I have trodden the winepress alone; and of the people _there was_ none with me: for I will tread them in mine anger, and trample them in my fury; and their blood shall be sprinkled upon my garments, and I will stain all my raiment.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For the day of vengeance _is_ in mine heart, and the year of my redeemed is come.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And I looked, and _there was_ none to help; and I wondered that _there was_ none to uphold: therefore mine own arm brought salvation unto me; and my fury, it upheld me.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And I will tread down the people in mine anger, and make them drunk in my fury, and I will bring down their strength to the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ I will mention the lovingkindnesses of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _and_ the praises of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, according to all that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath bestowed on us, and the great goodness toward the house of Israel, which he hath bestowed on them according to his mercies, and according to the multitude of his lovingkindnesses.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For he said, Surely they _are_ my people, children _that_ will not lie: so he was their Saviour.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>In all their affliction he was afflicted, and the angel of his presence saved them: in his love and in his pity he redeemed them; and he bare them, and carried them all the days of old.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ But they rebelled, and vexed his holy Spirit: therefore he was turned to be their enemy, _and_ he fought against them.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then he remembered the days of old, Moses, _and_ his people, _saying_, Where _is_ he that brought them up out of the sea with the shepherd of his flock? where _is_ he that put his holy Spirit within him?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>That led _them_ by the right hand of Moses with his glorious arm, dividing the water before them, to make himself an everlasting name?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>That led them through the deep, as an horse in the wilderness, _that_ they should not stumble?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>As a beast goeth down into the valley, the Spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> caused him to rest: so didst thou lead thy people, to make thyself a glorious name.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Look down from heaven, and behold from the habitation of thy holiness and of thy glory: where _is_ thy zeal and thy strength, the sounding of thy bowels and of thy mercies toward me? are they restrained?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Doubtless thou _art_ our father, though Abraham be ignorant of us, and Israel acknowledge us not: thou, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _art_ our father, our redeemer; thy name _is_ from everlasting.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, why hast thou made us to err from thy ways, _and_ hardened our heart from thy fear? Return for thy servants’ sake, the tribes of thine inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The people of thy holiness have possessed _it_ but a little while: our adversaries have trodden down thy sanctuary.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>We are _thine_: thou never barest rule over them; they were not called by thy name.  

## CHAPTER 64.

Oh that thou wouldest rend the heavens, that thou wouldest come down, that the mountains might flow down at thy presence,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>As _when_ the melting fire burneth, the fire causeth the waters to boil, to make thy name known to thine adversaries, _that_ the nations may tremble at thy presence!  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>When thou didst terrible things _which_ we looked not for, thou camest down, the mountains flowed down at thy presence.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For since the beginning of the world _men_ have not heard, nor perceived by the ear, neither hath the eye seen, O God, beside thee, _what_ he hath prepared for him that waiteth for him.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Thou meetest him that rejoiceth and worketh righteousness, _those that_ remember thee in thy ways: behold, thou art wroth; for we have sinned: in those is continuance, and we shall be saved.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ But we are all as an unclean _thing_, and all our righteousnesses _are_ as filthy rags; and we all do fade as a leaf; and our iniquities, like the wind, have taken us away.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And _there is_ none that calleth upon thy name, that stirreth up himself to take hold of thee: for thou hast hid thy face from us, and hast consumed us, because of our iniquities.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But now, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thou _art_ our father; we _are_ the clay, and thou our potter; and we all _are_ the work of thy hand.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Be not wroth very sore, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, neither remember iniquity for ever: behold, see, we beseech thee, we _are_ all thy people.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thy holy cities are a wilderness, Zion is a wilderness, Jerusalem a desolation.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Our holy and our beautiful house, where our fathers praised thee, is burned up with fire: and all our pleasant things are laid waste.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Wilt thou refrain thyself for these _things_, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>? wilt thou hold thy peace, and afflict us very sore?  

## CHAPTER 65.

I am sought of _them that_ asked not _for me_; I am found of _them that_ sought me not: I said, Behold me, behold me, unto a nation _that_ was not called by my name.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>I have spread out my hands all the day unto a rebellious people, which walketh in a way _that was_ not good, after their own thoughts;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>A people that provoketh me to anger continually to my face; that sacrificeth in gardens, and burneth incense upon altars of brick;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Which remain among the graves, and lodge in the monuments, which eat swine’s flesh, and broth of abominable _things is in_ their vessels;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Which say, Stand by thyself, come not near to me; for I am holier than thou. These _are_ a smoke in my nose, a fire that burneth all the day.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Behold, _it is_ written before me: I will not keep silence, but will recompense, even recompense into their bosom,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Your iniquities, and the iniquities of your fathers together, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, which have burned incense upon the mountains, and blasphemed me upon the hills: therefore will I measure their former work into their bosom.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, As the new wine is found in the cluster, and _one_ saith, Destroy it not; for a blessing _is_ in it: so will I do for my servants’ sakes, that I may not destroy them all.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And I will bring forth a seed out of Jacob, and out of Judah an inheritor of my mountains: and mine elect shall inherit it, and my servants shall dwell there.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Sharon shall be a fold of flocks, and the valley of Achor a place for the herds to lie down in, for my people that have sought me.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ But ye _are_ they that forsake the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that forget my holy mountain, that prepare a table for that troop, and that furnish the drink offering unto that number.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Therefore will I number you to the sword, and ye shall all bow down to the slaughter: because when I called, ye did not answer; when I spake, ye did not hear; but did evil before mine eyes, and did choose _that_ wherein I delighted not.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, Behold, my servants shall eat, but ye shall be hungry: behold, my servants shall drink, but ye shall be thirsty: behold, my servants shall rejoice, but ye shall be ashamed:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Behold, my servants shall sing for joy of heart, but ye shall cry for sorrow of heart, and shall howl for vexation of spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And ye shall leave your name for a curse unto my chosen: for the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> shall slay thee, and call his servants by another name:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>That he who blesseth himself in the earth shall bless himself in the God of truth; and he that sweareth in the earth shall swear by the God of truth; because the former troubles are forgotten, and because they are hid from mine eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ For, behold, I create new heavens and a new earth: and the former shall not be remembered, nor come into mind.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But be ye glad and rejoice for ever _in that_ which I create: for, behold, I create Jerusalem a rejoicing, and her people a joy.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And I will rejoice in Jerusalem, and joy in my people: and the voice of weeping shall be no more heard in her, nor the voice of crying.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>There shall be no more thence an infant of days, nor an old man that hath not filled his days: for the child shall die an hundred years old; but the sinner _being_ an hundred years old shall be accursed.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And they shall build houses, and inhabit _them_; and they shall plant vineyards, and eat the fruit of them.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>They shall not build, and another inhabit; they shall not plant, and another eat: for as the days of a tree _are_ the days of my people, and mine elect shall long enjoy the work of their hands.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>They shall not labour in vain, nor bring forth for trouble; for they _are_ the seed of the blessed of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and their offspring with them.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, that before they call, I will answer; and while they are yet speaking, I will hear.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>The wolf and the lamb shall feed together, and the lion shall eat straw like the bullock: and dust _shall be_ the serpent’s meat. They shall not hurt nor destroy in all my holy mountain, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 66.

Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, The heaven _is_ my throne, and the earth _is_ my footstool: where _is_ the house that ye build unto me? and where _is_ the place of my rest?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For all those _things_ hath mine hand made, and all those _things_ have been, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: but to this _man_ will I look, _even_ to _him that is_ poor and of a contrite spirit, and trembleth at my word.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>He that killeth an ox _is as if_ he slew a man; he that sacrificeth a lamb, _as if_ he cut off a dog’s neck; he that offereth an oblation, _as if he offered_ swine’s blood; he that burneth incense, _as if_ he blessed an idol. Yea, they have chosen their own ways, and their soul delighteth in their abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I also will choose their delusions, and will bring their fears upon them; because when I called, none did answer; when I spake, they did not hear: but they did evil before mine eyes, and chose _that_ in which I delighted not.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, ye that tremble at his word; Your brethren that hated you, that cast you out for my name’s sake, said, Let the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> be glorified: but he shall appear to your joy, and they shall be ashamed.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>A voice of noise from the city, a voice from the temple, a voice of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that rendereth recompence to his enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Before she travailed, she brought forth; before her pain came, she was delivered of a man child.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Who hath heard such a thing? who hath seen such things? Shall the earth be made to bring forth in one day? _or_ shall a nation be born at once? for as soon as Zion travailed, she brought forth her children.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Shall I bring to the birth, and not cause to bring forth? saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: shall I cause to bring forth, and shut _the womb_? saith thy God.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Rejoice ye with Jerusalem, and be glad with her, all ye that love her: rejoice for joy with her, all ye that mourn for her:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>That ye may suck, and be satisfied with the breasts of her consolations; that ye may milk out, and be delighted with the abundance of her glory.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Behold, I will extend peace to her like a river, and the glory of the Gentiles like a flowing stream: then shall ye suck, ye shall be borne upon _her_ sides, and be dandled upon _her_ knees.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>As one whom his mother comforteth, so will I comfort you; and ye shall be comforted in Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And when ye see _this_, your heart shall rejoice, and your bones shall flourish like an herb: and the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be known toward his servants, and _his_ indignation toward his enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ For, behold, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will come with fire, and with his chariots like a whirlwind, to render his anger with fury, and his rebuke with flames of fire.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For by fire and by his sword will the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> plead with all flesh: and the slain of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be many.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>They that sanctify themselves, and purify themselves in the gardens behind one _tree_ in the midst, eating swine’s flesh, and the abomination, and the mouse, shall be consumed together, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For I _know_ their works and their thoughts: it shall come, that I will gather all nations and tongues; and they shall come, and see my glory.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And I will set a sign among them, and I will send those that escape of them unto the nations, _to_ Tarshish, Pul, and Lud, that draw the bow, _to_ Tubal, and Javan, _to_ the isles afar off, that have not heard my fame, neither have seen my glory; and they shall declare my glory among the Gentiles.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And they shall bring all your brethren _for_ an offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> out of all nations upon horses, and in chariots, and in litters, and upon mules, and upon swift beasts, to my holy mountain Jerusalem, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, as the children of Israel bring an offering in a clean vessel into the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And I will also take of them for priests _and_ for Levites, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For as the new heavens and the new earth, which I will make, shall remain before me, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, so shall your seed and your name remain.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ from one new moon to another, and from one sabbath to another, shall all flesh come to worship before me, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they shall go forth, and look upon the carcases of the men that have transgressed against me: for their worm shall not die, neither shall their fire be quenched; and they shall be an abhorring unto all flesh.
