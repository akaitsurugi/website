# Romans

### THE EPISTLE OF PAUL THE APOSTLE TO THE ROMANS

## CHAPTER 1.

Paul, a servant of Jesus Christ, called _to be_ an apostle, separated unto the gospel of God,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>(Which he had promised afore by his prophets in the holy scriptures,)  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Concerning his Son Jesus Christ our Lord, which was made of the seed of David according to the flesh;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And declared _to be_ the Son of God with power, according to the spirit of holiness, by the resurrection from the dead:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>By whom we have received grace and apostleship, for obedience to the faith among all nations, for his name:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Among whom are ye also the called of Jesus Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>To all that be in Rome, beloved of God, called _to be_ saints: Grace to you and peace from God our Father, and the Lord Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ First, I thank my God through Jesus Christ for you all, that your faith is spoken of throughout the whole world.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For God is my witness, whom I serve with my spirit in the gospel of his Son, that without ceasing I make mention of you always in my prayers;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Making request, if by any means now at length I might have a prosperous journey by the will of God to come unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For I long to see you, that I may impart unto you some spiritual gift, to the end ye may be established;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>That is, that I may be comforted together with you by the mutual faith both of you and me.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Now I would not have you ignorant, brethren, that oftentimes I purposed to come unto you, (but was let hitherto,) that I might have some fruit among you also, even as among other Gentiles.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I am debtor both to the Greeks, and to the Barbarians; both to the wise, and to the unwise.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So, as much as in me is, I am ready to preach the gospel to you that are at Rome also.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ: for it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth; to the Jew first, and also to the Greek.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For therein is the righteousness of God revealed from faith to faith: as it is written, The just shall live by faith.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ For the wrath of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness and unrighteousness of men, who hold the truth in unrighteousness;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Because that which may be known of God is manifest in them; for God hath shewed _it_ unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For the invisible things of him from the creation of the world are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made, _even_ his eternal power and Godhead; so that they are without excuse:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Because that, when they knew God, they glorified _him_ not as God, neither were thankful; but became vain in their imaginations, and their foolish heart was darkened.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Professing themselves to be wise, they became fools,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And changed the glory of the uncorruptible God into an image made like to corruptible man, and to birds, and fourfooted beasts, and creeping things.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore God also gave them up to uncleanness through the lusts of their own hearts, to dishonour their own bodies between themselves:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Who changed the truth of God into a lie, and worshipped and served the creature more than the Creator, who is blessed for ever. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For this cause God gave them up unto vile affections: for even their women did change the natural use into that which is against nature:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And likewise also the men, leaving the natural use of the woman, burned in their lust one toward another; men with men working that which is unseemly, and receiving in themselves that recompence of their error which was meet.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And even as they did not like to retain God in _their_ knowledge, God gave them over to a reprobate mind, to do those things which are not convenient;  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Being filled with all unrighteousness, fornication, wickedness, covetousness, maliciousness; full of envy, murder, debate, deceit, malignity; whisperers,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Backbiters, haters of God, despiteful, proud, boasters, inventors of evil things, disobedient to parents,  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Without understanding, covenantbreakers, without natural affection, implacable, unmerciful:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Who knowing the judgment of God, that they which commit such things are worthy of death, not only do the same, but have pleasure in them that do them.  

## CHAPTER 2.

Therefore thou art inexcusable, O man, whosoever thou art that judgest: for wherein thou judgest another, thou condemnest thyself; for thou that judgest doest the same things.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>But we are sure that the judgment of God is according to truth against them which commit such things.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And thinkest thou this, O man, that judgest them which do such things, and doest the same, that thou shalt escape the judgment of God?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Or despisest thou the riches of his goodness and forbearance and longsuffering; not knowing that the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But after thy hardness and impenitent heart treasurest up unto thyself wrath against the day of wrath and revelation of the righteous judgment of God;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Who will render to every man according to his deeds:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>To them who by patient continuance in well doing seek for glory and honour and immortality, eternal life:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But unto them that are contentious, and do not obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness, indignation and wrath,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Tribulation and anguish, upon every soul of man that doeth evil, of the Jew first, and also of the Gentile;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But glory, honour, and peace, to every man that worketh good, to the Jew first, and also to the Gentile:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For there is no respect of persons with God.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For as many as have sinned without law shall also perish without law: and as many as have sinned in the law shall be judged by the law;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>(For not the hearers of the law _are_ just before God, but the doers of the law shall be justified.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For when the Gentiles, which have not the law, do by nature the things contained in the law, these, having not the law, are a law unto themselves:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Which shew the work of the law written in their hearts, their conscience also bearing witness, and _their_ thoughts the mean while accusing or else excusing one another;)  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>In the day when God shall judge the secrets of men by Jesus Christ according to my gospel.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, thou art called a Jew, and restest in the law, and makest thy boast of God,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And knowest _his_ will, and approvest the things that are more excellent, being instructed out of the law;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And art confident that thou thyself art a guide of the blind, a light of them which are in darkness,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>An instructor of the foolish, a teacher of babes, which hast the form of knowledge and of the truth in the law.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Thou therefore which teachest another, teachest thou not thyself? thou that preachest a man should not steal, dost thou steal?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Thou that sayest a man should not commit adultery, dost thou commit adultery? thou that abhorrest idols, dost thou commit sacrilege?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Thou that makest thy boast of the law, through breaking the law dishonourest thou God?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For the name of God is blasphemed among the Gentiles through you, as it is written.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For circumcision verily profiteth, if thou keep the law: but if thou be a breaker of the law, thy circumcision is made uncircumcision.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Therefore if the uncircumcision keep the righteousness of the law, shall not his uncircumcision be counted for circumcision?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And shall not uncircumcision which is by nature, if it fulfil the law, judge thee, who by the letter and circumcision dost transgress the law?  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For he is not a Jew, which is one outwardly; neither _is that_ circumcision, which is outward in the flesh:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>But he _is_ a Jew, which is one inwardly; and circumcision _is that_ of the heart, in the spirit, _and_ not in the letter; whose praise _is_ not of men, but of God.  

## CHAPTER 3.

What advantage then hath the Jew? or what profit _is there_ of circumcision?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Much every way: chiefly, because that unto them were committed the oracles of God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For what if some did not believe? shall their unbelief make the faith of God without effect?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>God forbid: yea, let God be true, but every man a liar; as it is written, That thou mightest be justified in thy sayings, and mightest overcome when thou art judged.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But if our unrighteousness commend the righteousness of God, what shall we say? _Is_ God unrighteous who taketh vengeance? (I speak as a man)  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>God forbid: for then how shall God judge the world?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For if the truth of God hath more abounded through my lie unto his glory; why yet am I also judged as a sinner?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And not _rather_, (as we be slanderously reported, and as some affirm that we say,) Let us do evil, that good may come? whose damnation is just.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ What then? are we better _than they_? No, in no wise: for we have before proved both Jews and Gentiles, that they are all under sin;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>As it is written, There is none righteous, no, not one:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>There is none that understandeth, there is none that seeketh after God.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>They are all gone out of the way, they are together become unprofitable; there is none that doeth good, no, not one.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Their throat _is_ an open sepulchre; with their tongues they have used deceit; the poison of asps _is_ under their lips:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Whose mouth _is_ full of cursing and bitterness:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Their feet _are_ swift to shed blood:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Destruction and misery _are_ in their ways:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the way of peace have they not known:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>There is no fear of God before their eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Therefore by the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified in his sight: for by the law _is_ the knowledge of sin.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ But now the righteousness of God without the law is manifested, being witnessed by the law and the prophets;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Even the righteousness of God _which is_ by faith of Jesus Christ unto all and upon all them that believe: for there is no difference:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Whom God hath set forth _to be_ a propitiation through faith in his blood, to declare his righteousness for the remission of sins that are past, through the forbearance of God;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>To declare, _I say_, at this time his righteousness: that he might be just, and the justifier of him which believeth in Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Where _is_ boasting then? It is excluded. By what law? of works? Nay: but by the law of faith.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Therefore we conclude that a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>_Is he_ the God of the Jews only? _is he_ not also of the Gentiles? Yes, of the Gentiles also:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Seeing _it is_ one God, which shall justify the circumcision by faith, and uncircumcision through faith.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we establish the law.  

## CHAPTER 4.

What shall we say then that Abraham our father, as pertaining to the flesh, hath found?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For if Abraham were justified by works, he hath _whereof_ to glory; but not before God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For what saith the scripture? Abraham believed God, and it was counted unto him for righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Now to him that worketh is the reward not reckoned of grace, but of debt.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But to him that worketh not, but believeth on him that justifieth the ungodly, his faith is counted for righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Even as David also describeth the blessedness of the man, unto whom God imputeth righteousness without works,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>_Saying_, Blessed _are_ they whose iniquities are forgiven, and whose sins are covered.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Blessed _is_ the man to whom the Lord will not impute sin.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>_Cometh_ this blessedness then upon the circumcision _only_, or upon the uncircumcision also? for we say that faith was reckoned to Abraham for righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>How was it then reckoned? when he was in circumcision, or in uncircumcision? Not in circumcision, but in uncircumcision.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And he received the sign of circumcision, a seal of the righteousness of the faith which _he had yet_ being uncircumcised: that he might be the father of all them that believe, though they be not circumcised; that righteousness might be imputed unto them also:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the father of circumcision to them who are not of the circumcision only, but who also walk in the steps of that faith of our father Abraham, which _he had_ being _yet_ uncircumcised.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For the promise, that he should be the heir of the world, _was_ not to Abraham, or to his seed, through the law, but through the righteousness of faith.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For if they which are of the law _be_ heirs, faith is made void, and the promise made of none effect:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Because the law worketh wrath: for where no law is, _there is_ no transgression.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Therefore _it is_ of faith, that _it might be_ by grace; to the end the promise might be sure to all the seed; not to that only which is of the law, but to that also which is of the faith of Abraham; who is the father of us all,  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>(As it is written, I have made thee a father of many nations,) before him whom he believed, _even_ God, who quickeneth the dead, and calleth those things which be not as though they were.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Who against hope believed in hope, that he might become the father of many nations; according to that which was spoken, So shall thy seed be.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And being not weak in faith, he considered not his own body now dead, when he was about an hundred years old, neither yet the deadness of Sara’s womb:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>He staggered not at the promise of God through unbelief; but was strong in faith, giving glory to God;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And being fully persuaded that, what he had promised, he was able also to perform.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And therefore it was imputed to him for righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Now it was not written for his sake alone, that it was imputed to him;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>But for us also, to whom it shall be imputed, if we believe on him that raised up Jesus our Lord from the dead;  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Who was delivered for our offences, and was raised again for our justification.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>By whom also we have access by faith into this grace wherein we stand, and rejoice in hope of the glory of God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And not only _so_, but we glory in tribulations also: knowing that tribulation worketh patience;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And patience, experience; and experience, hope:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For when we were yet without strength, in due time Christ died for the ungodly.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For scarcely for a righteous man will one die: yet peradventure for a good man some would even dare to die.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Much more then, being now justified by his blood, we shall be saved from wrath through him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For if, when we were enemies, we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son, much more, being reconciled, we shall be saved by his life.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And not only _so_, but we also joy in God through our Lord Jesus Christ, by whom we have now received the atonement.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>(For until the law sin was in the world: but sin is not imputed when there is no law.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Nevertheless death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over them that had not sinned after the similitude of Adam’s transgression, who is the figure of him that was to come.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But not as the offence, so also _is_ the free gift. For if through the offence of one many be dead, much more the grace of God, and the gift by grace, _which is_ by one man, Jesus Christ, hath abounded unto many.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And not as _it was_ by one that sinned, _so is_ the gift: for the judgment _was_ by one to condemnation, but the free gift _is_ of many offences unto justification.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For if by one man’s offence death reigned by one; much more they which receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness shall reign in life by one, Jesus Christ.)  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Therefore as by the offence of one _judgment came_ upon all men to condemnation; even so by the righteousness of one _the free gift came_ upon all men unto justification of life.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For as by one man’s disobedience many were made sinners, so by the obedience of one shall many be made righteous.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Moreover the law entered, that the offence might abound. But where sin abounded, grace did much more abound:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>That as sin hath reigned unto death, even so might grace reign through righteousness unto eternal life by Jesus Christ our Lord.  

## CHAPTER 6.

What shall we say then? Shall we continue in sin, that grace may abound?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>God forbid. How shall we, that are dead to sin, live any longer therein?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Know ye not, that so many of us as were baptized into Jesus Christ were baptized into his death?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Therefore we are buried with him by baptism into death: that like as Christ was raised up from the dead by the glory of the Father, even so we also should walk in newness of life.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For if we have been planted together in the likeness of his death, we shall be also _in the likeness_ of _his_ resurrection:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Knowing this, that our old man is crucified with _him_, that the body of sin might be destroyed, that henceforth we should not serve sin.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For he that is dead is freed from sin.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Now if we be dead with Christ, we believe that we shall also live with him:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Knowing that Christ being raised from the dead dieth no more; death hath no more dominion over him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For in that he died, he died unto sin once: but in that he liveth, he liveth unto God.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Likewise reckon ye also yourselves to be dead indeed unto sin, but alive unto God through Jesus Christ our Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Let not sin therefore reign in your mortal body, that ye should obey it in the lusts thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Neither yield ye your members _as_ instruments of unrighteousness unto sin: but yield yourselves unto God, as those that are alive from the dead, and your members _as_ instruments of righteousness unto God.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For sin shall not have dominion over you: for ye are not under the law, but under grace.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>What then? shall we sin, because we are not under the law, but under grace? God forbid.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Know ye not, that to whom ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are to whom ye obey; whether of sin unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But God be thanked, that ye were the servants of sin, but ye have obeyed from the heart that form of doctrine which was delivered you.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Being then made free from sin, ye became the servants of righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>I speak after the manner of men because of the infirmity of your flesh: for as ye have yielded your members servants to uncleanness and to iniquity unto iniquity; even so now yield your members servants to righteousness unto holiness.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For when ye were the servants of sin, ye were free from righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>What fruit had ye then in those things whereof ye are now ashamed? for the end of those things _is_ death.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But now being made free from sin, and become servants to God, ye have your fruit unto holiness, and the end everlasting life.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For the wages of sin _is_ death; but the gift of God _is_ eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.  

## CHAPTER 7.

Know ye not, brethren, (for I speak to them that know the law,) how that the law hath dominion over a man as long as he liveth?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For the woman which hath an husband is bound by the law to _her_ husband so long as he liveth; but if the husband be dead, she is loosed from the law of _her_ husband.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>So then if, while _her_ husband liveth, she be married to another man, she shall be called an adulteress: but if her husband be dead, she is free from that law; so that she is no adulteress, though she be married to another man.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Wherefore, my brethren, ye also are become dead to the law by the body of Christ; that ye should be married to another, _even_ to him who is raised from the dead, that we should bring forth fruit unto God.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For when we were in the flesh, the motions of sins, which were by the law, did work in our members to bring forth fruit unto death.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But now we are delivered from the law, that being dead wherein we were held; that we should serve in newness of spirit, and not _in_ the oldness of the letter.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ What shall we say then? _Is_ the law sin? God forbid. Nay, I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But sin, taking occasion by the commandment, wrought in me all manner of concupiscence. For without the law sin _was_ dead.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For I was alive without the law once: but when the commandment came, sin revived, and I died.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the commandment, which _was ordained_ to life, I found _to be_ unto death.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For sin, taking occasion by the commandment, deceived me, and by it slew _me_.   
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Wherefore the law _is_ holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Was then that which is good made death unto me? God forbid. But sin, that it might appear sin, working death in me by that which is good; that sin by the commandment might become exceeding sinful.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For we know that the law is spiritual: but I am carnal, sold under sin.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For that which I do I allow not: for what I would, that do I not; but what I hate, that do I.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>If then I do that which I would not, I consent unto the law that _it is_ good.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Now then it is no more I that do it, but sin that dwelleth in me.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For I know that in me (that is, in my flesh,) dwelleth no good thing: for to will is present with me; but _how_ to perform that which is good I find not.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For the good that I would I do not: but the evil which I would not, that I do.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Now if I do that I would not, it is no more I that do it, but sin that dwelleth in me.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>I find then a law, that, when I would do good, evil is present with me.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For I delight in the law of God after the inward man:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But I see another law in my members, warring against the law of my mind, and bringing me into captivity to the law of sin which is in my members.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>O wretched man that I am! who shall deliver me from the body of this death?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>I thank God through Jesus Christ our Lord. So then with the mind I myself serve the law of God; but with the flesh the law of sin.  

## CHAPTER 8.

_There is_ therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For the law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus hath made me free from the law of sin and death.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For what the law could not do, in that it was weak through the flesh, God sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh, and for sin, condemned sin in the flesh:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>That the righteousness of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For they that are after the flesh do mind the things of the flesh; but they that are after the Spirit the things of the Spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For to be carnally minded _is_ death; but to be spiritually minded _is_ life and peace.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Because the carnal mind _is_ enmity against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>So then they that are in the flesh cannot please God.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But ye are not in the flesh, but in the Spirit, if so be that the Spirit of God dwell in you. Now if any man have not the Spirit of Christ, he is none of his.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And if Christ _be_ in you, the body _is_ dead because of sin; but the Spirit _is_ life because of righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But if the Spirit of him that raised up Jesus from the dead dwell in you, he that raised up Christ from the dead shall also quicken your mortal bodies by his Spirit that dwelleth in you.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore, brethren, we are debtors, not to the flesh, to live after the flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For if ye live after the flesh, ye shall die: but if ye through the Spirit do mortify the deeds of the body, ye shall live.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For as many as are led by the Spirit of God, they are the sons of God.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For ye have not received the spirit of bondage again to fear; but ye have received the Spirit of adoption, whereby we cry, Abba, Father.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And if children, then heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ; if so be that we suffer with _him_, that we may be also glorified together.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ For I reckon that the sufferings of this present time _are_ not worthy _to be compared_ with the glory which shall be revealed in us.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For the earnest expectation of the creature waiteth for the manifestation of the sons of God.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For the creature was made subject to vanity, not willingly, but by reason of him who hath subjected _the same_ in hope,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Because the creature itself also shall be delivered from the bondage of corruption into the glorious liberty of the children of God.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For we know that the whole creation groaneth and travaileth in pain together until now.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And not only _they_, but ourselves also, which have the firstfruits of the Spirit, even we ourselves groan within ourselves, waiting for the adoption, _to wit_, the redemption of our body.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For we are saved by hope: but hope that is seen is not hope: for what a man seeth, why doth he yet hope for?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But if we hope for that we see not, _then_ do we with patience wait for _it_.   
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And he that searcheth the hearts knoweth what _is_ the mind of the Spirit, because he maketh intercession for the saints according to _the will of_ God.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to _his_ purpose.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For whom he did foreknow, he also did predestinate _to be_ conformed to the image of his Son, that he might be the firstborn among many brethren.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Moreover whom he did predestinate, them he also called: and whom he called, them he also justified: and whom he justified, them he also glorified.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>What shall we then say to these things? If God _be_ for us, who _can be_ against us?  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>He that spared not his own Son, but delivered him up for us all, how shall he not with him also freely give us all things?  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Who shall lay any thing to the charge of God’s elect? _It is_ God that justifieth.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Who _is_ he that condemneth? _It is_ Christ that died, yea rather, that is risen again, who is even at the right hand of God, who also maketh intercession for us.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? _shall_ tribulation, or distress, or persecution, or famine, or nakedness, or peril, or sword?  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>As it is written, For thy sake we are killed all the day long; we are accounted as sheep for the slaughter.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Nay, in all these things we are more than conquerors through him that loved us.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>For I am persuaded, that neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor principalities, nor powers, nor things present, nor things to come,  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>Nor height, nor depth, nor any other creature, shall be able to separate us from the love of God, which is in Christ Jesus our Lord.  

## CHAPTER 9.

I say the truth in Christ, I lie not, my conscience also bearing me witness in the Holy Ghost,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That I have great heaviness and continual sorrow in my heart.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For I could wish that myself were accursed from Christ for my brethren, my kinsmen according to the flesh:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who are Israelites; to whom _pertaineth_ the adoption, and the glory, and the covenants, and the giving of the law, and the service _of God_, and the promises;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Whose _are_ the fathers, and of whom as concerning the flesh Christ _came_, who is over all, God blessed for ever. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Not as though the word of God hath taken none effect. For they _are_ not all Israel, which are of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Neither, because they are the seed of Abraham, _are they_ all children: but, In Isaac shall thy seed be called.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>That is, They which are the children of the flesh, these _are_ not the children of God: but the children of the promise are counted for the seed.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For this _is_ the word of promise, At this time will I come, and Sara shall have a son.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And not only _this_; but when Rebecca also had conceived by one, _even_ by our father Isaac;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>(For _the children_ being not yet born, neither having done any good or evil, that the purpose of God according to election might stand, not of works, but of him that calleth;)  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>It was said unto her, The elder shall serve the younger.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>As it is written, Jacob have I loved, but Esau have I hated.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ What shall we say then? _Is there_ unrighteousness with God? God forbid.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For he saith to Moses, I will have mercy on whom I will have mercy, and I will have compassion on whom I will have compassion.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>So then _it is_ not of him that willeth, nor of him that runneth, but of God that sheweth mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For the scripture saith unto Pharaoh, Even for this same purpose have I raised thee up, that I might shew my power in thee, and that my name might be declared throughout all the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Therefore hath he mercy on whom he will _have mercy_, and whom he will he hardeneth.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Thou wilt say then unto me, Why doth he yet find fault? For who hath resisted his will?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Nay but, O man, who art thou that repliest against God? Shall the thing formed say to him that formed _it_, Why hast thou made me thus?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Hath not the potter power over the clay, of the same lump to make one vessel unto honour, and another unto dishonour?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>_What_ if God, willing to shew _his_ wrath, and to make his power known, endured with much longsuffering the vessels of wrath fitted to destruction:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And that he might make known the riches of his glory on the vessels of mercy, which he had afore prepared unto glory,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Even us, whom he hath called, not of the Jews only, but also of the Gentiles?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>As he saith also in Osee, I will call them my people, which were not my people; and her beloved, which was not beloved.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ in the place where it was said unto them, Ye _are_ not my people; there shall they be called the children of the living God.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Esaias also crieth concerning Israel, Though the number of the children of Israel be as the sand of the sea, a remnant shall be saved:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For he will finish the work, and cut _it_ short in righteousness: because a short work will the Lord make upon the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And as Esaias said before, Except the Lord of Sabaoth had left us a seed, we had been as Sodoma, and been made like unto Gomorrha.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>What shall we say then? That the Gentiles, which followed not after righteousness, have attained to righteousness, even the righteousness which is of faith.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>But Israel, which followed after the law of righteousness, hath not attained to the law of righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Wherefore? Because _they sought it_ not by faith, but as it were by the works of the law. For they stumbled at that stumblingstone;  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>As it is written, Behold, I lay in Sion a stumblingstone and rock of offence: and whosoever believeth on him shall not be ashamed.  

## CHAPTER 10.

Brethren, my heart’s desire and prayer to God for Israel is, that they might be saved.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For I bear them record that they have a zeal of God, but not according to knowledge.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For they being ignorant of God’s righteousness, and going about to establish their own righteousness, have not submitted themselves unto the righteousness of God.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For Christ _is_ the end of the law for righteousness to every one that believeth.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For Moses describeth the righteousness which is of the law, That the man which doeth those things shall live by them.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But the righteousness which is of faith speaketh on this wise, Say not in thine heart, Who shall ascend into heaven? (that is, to bring Christ down _from above_:)  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Or, Who shall descend into the deep? (that is, to bring up Christ again from the dead.)  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But what saith it? The word is nigh thee, _even_ in thy mouth, and in thy heart: that is, the word of faith, which we preach;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For with the heart man believeth unto righteousness; and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For the scripture saith, Whosoever believeth on him shall not be ashamed.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For there is no difference between the Jew and the Greek: for the same Lord over all is rich unto all that call upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>How then shall they call on him in whom they have not believed? and how shall they believe in him of whom they have not heard? and how shall they hear without a preacher?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And how shall they preach, except they be sent? as it is written, How beautiful are the feet of them that preach the gospel of peace, and bring glad tidings of good things!  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But they have not all obeyed the gospel. For Esaias saith, Lord, who hath believed our report?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>So then faith _cometh_ by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But I say, Have they not heard? Yes verily, their sound went into all the earth, and their words unto the ends of the world.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But I say, Did not Israel know? First Moses saith, I will provoke you to jealousy by _them that are_ no people, _and_ by a foolish nation I will anger you.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But Esaias is very bold, and saith, I was found of them that sought me not; I was made manifest unto them that asked not after me.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But to Israel he saith, All day long I have stretched forth my hands unto a disobedient and gainsaying people.  

## CHAPTER 11.

I say then, Hath God cast away his people? God forbid. For I also am an Israelite, of the seed of Abraham, _of_ the tribe of Benjamin.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>God hath not cast away his people which he foreknew. Wot ye not what the scripture saith of Elias? how he maketh intercession to God against Israel, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Lord, they have killed thy prophets, and digged down thine altars; and I am left alone, and they seek my life.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But what saith the answer of God unto him? I have reserved to myself seven thousand men, who have not bowed the knee to _the image of_ Baal.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Even so then at this present time also there is a remnant according to the election of grace.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And if by grace, then _is it_ no more of works: otherwise grace is no more grace. But if _it be_ of works, then is it no more grace: otherwise work is no more work.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ What then? Israel hath not obtained that which he seeketh for; but the election hath obtained it, and the rest were blinded  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>(According as it is written, God hath given them the spirit of slumber, eyes that they should not see, and ears that they should not hear;) unto this day.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And David saith, Let their table be made a snare, and a trap, and a stumblingblock, and a recompence unto them:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Let their eyes be darkened, that they may not see, and bow down their back alway.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I say then, Have they stumbled that they should fall? God forbid: but _rather_ through their fall salvation _is come_ unto the Gentiles, for to provoke them to jealousy.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Now if the fall of them _be_ the riches of the world, and the diminishing of them the riches of the Gentiles; how much more their fulness?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ For I speak to you Gentiles, inasmuch as I am the apostle of the Gentiles, I magnify mine office:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>If by any means I may provoke to emulation _them which are_ my flesh, and might save some of them.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For if the casting away of them _be_ the reconciling of the world, what _shall_ the receiving _of them be_, but life from the dead?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For if the firstfruit _be_ holy, the lump _is_ also _holy_: and if the root _be_ holy, so _are_ the branches.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And if some of the branches be broken off, and thou, being a wild olive tree, wert graffed in among them, and with them partakest of the root and fatness of the olive tree;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Boast not against the branches. But if thou boast, thou bearest not the root, but the root thee.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Thou wilt say then, The branches were broken off, that I might be graffed in.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Well; because of unbelief they were broken off, and thou standest by faith. Be not highminded, but fear:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For if God spared not the natural branches, _take heed_ lest he also spare not thee.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Behold therefore the goodness and severity of God: on them which fell, severity; but toward thee, goodness, if thou continue in _his_ goodness: otherwise thou also shalt be cut off.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they also, if they abide not still in unbelief, shall be graffed in: for God is able to graff them in again.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For if thou wert cut out of the olive tree which is wild by nature, and wert graffed contrary to nature into a good olive tree: how much more shall these, which be the natural _branches_, be graffed into their own olive tree?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ For I would not, brethren, that ye should be ignorant of this mystery, lest ye should be wise in your own conceits; that blindness in part is happened to Israel, until the fulness of the Gentiles be come in.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And so all Israel shall be saved: as it is written, There shall come out of Sion the Deliverer, and shall turn away ungodliness from Jacob:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For this _is_ my covenant unto them, when I shall take away their sins.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>As concerning the gospel, _they are_ enemies for your sakes: but as touching the election, _they are_ beloved for the fathers’ sakes.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For the gifts and calling of God _are_ without repentance.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>For as ye in times past have not believed God, yet have now obtained mercy through their unbelief:  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Even so have these also now not believed, that through your mercy they also may obtain mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For God hath concluded them all in unbelief, that he might have mercy upon all.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! how unsearchable _are_ his judgments, and his ways past finding out!  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>For who hath known the mind of the Lord? or who hath been his counsellor?  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Or who hath first given to him, and it shall be recompensed unto him again?  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>For of him, and through him, and to him, _are_ all things: to whom _be_ glory for ever. Amen.  

## CHAPTER 12.

I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that ye present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, _which is_ your reasonable service.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind, that ye may prove what _is_ that good, and acceptable, and perfect, will of God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ For I say, through the grace given unto me, to every man that is among you, not to think _of himself_ more highly than he ought to think; but to think soberly, according as God hath dealt to every man the measure of faith.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For as we have many members in one body, and all members have not the same office:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>So we, _being_ many, are one body in Christ, and every one members one of another.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Having then gifts differing according to the grace that is given to us, whether prophecy, _let us prophesy_ according to the proportion of faith;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Or ministry, _let us wait_ on _our_ ministering: or he that teacheth, on teaching;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Or he that exhorteth, on exhortation: he that giveth, _let him do it_ with simplicity; he that ruleth, with diligence; he that sheweth mercy, with cheerfulness.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ _Let_ love be without dissimulation. Abhor that which is evil; cleave to that which is good.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>_Be_ kindly affectioned one to another with brotherly love; in honour preferring one another;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Not slothful in business; fervent in spirit; serving the Lord;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Rejoicing in hope; patient in tribulation; continuing instant in prayer;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Distributing to the necessity of saints; given to hospitality.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Bless them which persecute you: bless, and curse not.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Rejoice with them that do rejoice, and weep with them that weep.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>_Be_ of the same mind one toward another. Mind not high things, but condescend to men of low estate. Be not wise in your own conceits.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Recompense to no man evil for evil. Provide things honest in the sight of all men.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>If it be possible, as much as lieth in you, live peaceably with all men.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Dearly beloved, avenge not yourselves, but _rather_ give place unto wrath: for it is written, Vengeance _is_ mine; I will repay, saith the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Therefore if thine enemy hunger, feed him; if he thirst, give him drink: for in so doing thou shalt heap coals of fire on his head.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Be not overcome of evil, but overcome evil with good.  

## CHAPTER 13.

Let every soul be subject unto the higher powers. For there is no power but of God: the powers that be are ordained of God.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Whosoever therefore resisteth the power, resisteth the ordinance of God: and they that resist shall receive to themselves damnation.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For rulers are not a terror to good works, but to the evil. Wilt thou then not be afraid of the power? do that which is good, and thou shalt have praise of the same:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For he is the minister of God to thee for good. But if thou do that which is evil, be afraid; for he beareth not the sword in vain: for he is the minister of God, a revenger to _execute_ wrath upon him that doeth evil.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Wherefore _ye_ must needs be subject, not only for wrath, but also for conscience sake.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For for this cause pay ye tribute also: for they are God’s ministers, attending continually upon this very thing.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Render therefore to all their dues: tribute to whom tribute _is due_; custom to whom custom; fear to whom fear; honour to whom honour.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Owe no man any thing, but to love one another: for he that loveth another hath fulfilled the law.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For this, Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not kill, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not covet; and if _there be_ any other commandment, it is briefly comprehended in this saying, namely, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Love worketh no ill to his neighbour: therefore love _is_ the fulfilling of the law.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And that, knowing the time, that now _it is_ high time to awake out of sleep: for now _is_ our salvation nearer than when we believed.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The night is far spent, the day is at hand: let us therefore cast off the works of darkness, and let us put on the armour of light.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Let us walk honestly, as in the day; not in rioting and drunkenness, not in chambering and wantonness, not in strife and envying.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ, and make not provision for the flesh, to _fulfil_ the lusts _thereof_.  

## CHAPTER 14.

Him that is weak in the faith receive ye, _but_ not to doubtful disputations.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For one believeth that he may eat all things: another, who is weak, eateth herbs.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Let not him that eateth despise him that eateth not; and let not him which eateth not judge him that eateth: for God hath received him.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who art thou that judgest another man’s servant? to his own master he standeth or falleth. Yea, he shall be holden up: for God is able to make him stand.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>One man esteemeth one day above another: another esteemeth every day _alike_. Let every man be fully persuaded in his own mind.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>He that regardeth the day, regardeth _it_ unto the Lord; and he that regardeth not the day, to the Lord he doth not regard _it_. He that eateth, eateth to the Lord, for he giveth God thanks; and he that eateth not, to the Lord he eateth not, and giveth God thanks.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For none of us liveth to himself, and no man dieth to himself.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For whether we live, we live unto the Lord; and whether we die, we die unto the Lord: whether we live therefore, or die, we are the Lord’s.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For to this end Christ both died, and rose, and revived, that he might be Lord both of the dead and living.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But why dost thou judge thy brother? or why dost thou set at nought thy brother? for we shall all stand before the judgment seat of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For it is written, _As_ I live, saith the Lord, every knee shall bow to me, and every tongue shall confess to God.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>So then every one of us shall give account of himself to God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Let us not therefore judge one another any more: but judge this rather, that no man put a stumblingblock or an occasion to fall in _his_ brother’s way.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I know, and am persuaded by the Lord Jesus, that _there is_ nothing unclean of itself: but to him that esteemeth any thing to be unclean, to him _it is_ unclean.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But if thy brother be grieved with _thy_ meat, now walkest thou not charitably. Destroy not him with thy meat, for whom Christ died.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Let not then your good be evil spoken of:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For the kingdom of God is not meat and drink; but righteousness, and peace, and joy in the Holy Ghost.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For he that in these things serveth Christ _is_ acceptable to God, and approved of men.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Let us therefore follow after the things which make for peace, and things wherewith one may edify another.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For meat destroy not the work of God. All things indeed _are_ pure; but _it is_ evil for that man who eateth with offence.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>_It is_ good neither to eat flesh, nor to drink wine, nor _any thing_ whereby thy brother stumbleth, or is offended, or is made weak.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Hast thou faith? have _it_ to thyself before God. Happy _is_ he that condemneth not himself in that thing which he alloweth.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And he that doubteth is damned if he eat, because _he eateth_ not of faith: for whatsoever _is_ not of faith is sin.  

## CHAPTER 15.

We then that are strong ought to bear the infirmities of the weak, and not to please ourselves.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Let every one of us please _his_ neighbour for _his_ good to edification.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For even Christ pleased not himself; but, as it is written, The reproaches of them that reproached thee fell on me.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For whatsoever things were written aforetime were written for our learning, that we through patience and comfort of the scriptures might have hope.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Now the God of patience and consolation grant you to be likeminded one toward another according to Christ Jesus:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>That ye may with one mind _and_ one mouth glorify God, even the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore receive ye one another, as Christ also received us to the glory of God.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Now I say that Jesus Christ was a minister of the circumcision for the truth of God, to confirm the promises _made_ unto the fathers:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And that the Gentiles might glorify God for _his_ mercy; as it is written, For this cause I will confess to thee among the Gentiles, and sing unto thy name.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And again he saith, Rejoice, ye Gentiles, with his people.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And again, Praise the Lord, all ye Gentiles; and laud him, all ye people.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And again, Esaias saith, There shall be a root of Jesse, and he that shall rise to reign over the Gentiles; in him shall the Gentiles trust.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Now the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace in believing, that ye may abound in hope, through the power of the Holy Ghost.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And I myself also am persuaded of you, my brethren, that ye also are full of goodness, filled with all knowledge, able also to admonish one another.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Nevertheless, brethren, I have written the more boldly unto you in some sort, as putting you in mind, because of the grace that is given to me of God,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>That I should be the minister of Jesus Christ to the Gentiles, ministering the gospel of God, that the offering up of the Gentiles might be acceptable, being sanctified by the Holy Ghost.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>I have therefore whereof I may glory through Jesus Christ in those things which pertain to God.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For I will not dare to speak of any of those things which Christ hath not wrought by me, to make the Gentiles obedient, by word and deed,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Through mighty signs and wonders, by the power of the Spirit of God; so that from Jerusalem, and round about unto Illyricum, I have fully preached the gospel of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Yea, so have I strived to preach the gospel, not where Christ was named, lest I should build upon another man’s foundation:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But as it is written, To whom he was not spoken of, they shall see: and they that have not heard shall understand.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For which cause also I have been much hindered from coming to you.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But now having no more place in these parts, and having a great desire these many years to come unto you;  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Whensoever I take my journey into Spain, I will come to you: for I trust to see you in my journey, and to be brought on my way thitherward by you, if first I be somewhat filled with your _company_.   
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But now I go unto Jerusalem to minister unto the saints.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For it hath pleased them of Macedonia and Achaia to make a certain contribution for the poor saints which are at Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>It hath pleased them verily; and their debtors they are. For if the Gentiles have been made partakers of their spiritual things, their duty is also to minister unto them in carnal things.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>When therefore I have performed this, and have sealed to them this fruit, I will come by you into Spain.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And I am sure that, when I come unto you, I shall come in the fulness of the blessing of the gospel of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ Now I beseech you, brethren, for the Lord Jesus Christ’s sake, and for the love of the Spirit, that ye strive together with me in _your_ prayers to God for me;  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>That I may be delivered from them that do not believe in Judaea; and that my service which _I have_ for Jerusalem may be accepted of the saints;  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>That I may come unto you with joy by the will of God, and may with you be refreshed.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Now the God of peace _be_ with you all. Amen.  

## CHAPTER 16.

I commend unto you Phebe our sister, which is a servant of the church which is at Cenchrea:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That ye receive her in the Lord, as becometh saints, and that ye assist her in whatsoever business she hath need of you: for she hath been a succourer of many, and of myself also.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Greet Priscilla and Aquila my helpers in Christ Jesus:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who have for my life laid down their own necks: unto whom not only I give thanks, but also all the churches of the Gentiles.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Likewise _greet_ the church that is in their house. Salute my wellbeloved Epaenetus, who is the firstfruits of Achaia unto Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Greet Mary, who bestowed much labour on us.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Salute Andronicus and Junia, my kinsmen, and my fellowprisoners, who are of note among the apostles, who also were in Christ before me.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Greet Amplias my beloved in the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Salute Urbane, our helper in Christ, and Stachys my beloved.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Salute Apelles approved in Christ. Salute them which are of Aristobulus’ _household_.   
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Salute Herodion my kinsman. Greet them that be of the _household_ of Narcissus, which are in the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Salute Tryphena and Tryphosa, who labour in the Lord. Salute the beloved Persis, which laboured much in the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Salute Rufus chosen in the Lord, and his mother and mine.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Salute Asyncritus, Phlegon, Hermas, Patrobas, Hermes, and the brethren which are with them.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Salute Philologus, and Julia, Nereus, and his sister, and Olympas, and all the saints which are with them.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Salute one another with an holy kiss. The churches of Christ salute you.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Now I beseech you, brethren, mark them which cause divisions and offences contrary to the doctrine which ye have learned; and avoid them.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For they that are such serve not our Lord Jesus Christ, but their own belly; and by good words and fair speeches deceive the hearts of the simple.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For your obedience is come abroad unto all _men_. I am glad therefore on your behalf: but yet I would have you wise unto that which is good, and simple concerning evil.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the God of peace shall bruise Satan under your feet shortly. The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ _be_ with you. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Timotheus my workfellow, and Lucius, and Jason, and Sosipater, my kinsmen, salute you.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>I Tertius, who wrote _this_ epistle, salute you in the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Gaius mine host, and of the whole church, saluteth you. Erastus the chamberlain of the city saluteth you, and Quartus a brother.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ _be_ with you all. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ Now to him that is of power to stablish you according to my gospel, and the preaching of Jesus Christ, according to the revelation of the mystery, which was kept secret since the world began,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But now is made manifest, and by the scriptures of the prophets, according to the commandment of the everlasting God, made known to all nations for the obedience of faith:  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>To God only wise, _be_ glory through Jesus Christ for ever. Amen.
