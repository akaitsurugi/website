# Ezekiel

### THE BOOK OF THE PROPHET EZEKIEL

## CHAPTER 1.

Now it came to pass in the thirtieth year, in the fourth _month_, in the fifth _day_ of the month, as I _was_ among the captives by the river of Chebar, _that_ the heavens were opened, and I saw visions of God.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>In the fifth _day_ of the month, which _was_ the fifth year of king Jehoiachin’s captivity,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came expressly unto Ezekiel the priest, the son of Buzi, in the land of the Chaldeans by the river Chebar; and the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was there upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And I looked, and, behold, a whirlwind came out of the north, a great cloud, and a fire infolding itself, and a brightness _was_ about it, and out of the midst thereof as the colour of amber, out of the midst of the fire.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Also out of the midst thereof _came_ the likeness of four living creatures. And this _was_ their appearance; they had the likeness of a man.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And every one had four faces, and every one had four wings.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And their feet _were_ straight feet; and the sole of their feet _was_ like the sole of a calf’s foot: and they sparkled like the colour of burnished brass.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And _they had_ the hands of a man under their wings on their four sides; and they four had their faces and their wings.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Their wings _were_ joined one to another; they turned not when they went; they went every one straight forward.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>As for the likeness of their faces, they four had the face of a man, and the face of a lion, on the right side: and they four had the face of an ox on the left side; they four also had the face of an eagle.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Thus _were_ their faces: and their wings _were_ stretched upward; two _wings_ of every one _were_ joined one to another, and two covered their bodies.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And they went every one straight forward: whither the spirit was to go, they went; _and_ they turned not when they went.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>As for the likeness of the living creatures, their appearance _was_ like burning coals of fire, _and_ like the appearance of lamps: it went up and down among the living creatures; and the fire was bright, and out of the fire went forth lightning.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the living creatures ran and returned as the appearance of a flash of lightning.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Now as I beheld the living creatures, behold one wheel upon the earth by the living creatures, with his four faces.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>The appearance of the wheels and their work _was_ like unto the colour of a beryl: and they four had one likeness: and their appearance and their work _was_ as it were a wheel in the middle of a wheel.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>When they went, they went upon their four sides: _and_ they turned not when they went.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>As for their rings, they were so high that they were dreadful; and their rings _were_ full of eyes round about them four.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And when the living creatures went, the wheels went by them: and when the living creatures were lifted up from the earth, the wheels were lifted up.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Whithersoever the spirit was to go, they went, thither _was their_ spirit to go; and the wheels were lifted up over against them: for the spirit of the living creature _was_ in the wheels.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>When those went, _these_ went; and when those stood, _these_ stood; and when those were lifted up from the earth, the wheels were lifted up over against them: for the spirit of the living creature _was_ in the wheels.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the likeness of the firmament upon the heads of the living creature _was_ as the colour of the terrible crystal, stretched forth over their heads above.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And under the firmament _were_ their wings straight, the one toward the other: every one had two, which covered on this side, and every one had two, which covered on that side, their bodies.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And when they went, I heard the noise of their wings, like the noise of great waters, as the voice of the Almighty, the voice of speech, as the noise of an host: when they stood, they let down their wings.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And there was a voice from the firmament that _was_ over their heads, when they stood, _and_ had let down their wings.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And above the firmament that _was_ over their heads _was_ the likeness of a throne, as the appearance of a sapphire stone: and upon the likeness of the throne _was_ the likeness as the appearance of a man above upon it.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And I saw as the colour of amber, as the appearance of fire round about within it, from the appearance of his loins even upward, and from the appearance of his loins even downward, I saw as it were the appearance of fire, and it had brightness round about.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>As the appearance of the bow that is in the cloud in the day of rain, so _was_ the appearance of the brightness round about. This _was_ the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>. And when I saw _it_, I fell upon my face, and I heard a voice of one that spake.  

## CHAPTER 2.

And he said unto me, Son of man, stand upon thy feet, and I will speak unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the spirit entered into me when he spake unto me, and set me upon my feet, that I heard him that spake unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he said unto me, Son of man, I send thee to the children of Israel, to a rebellious nation that hath rebelled against me: they and their fathers have transgressed against me, _even_ unto this very day.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For _they are_ impudent children and stiffhearted. I do send thee unto them; and thou shalt say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they, whether they will hear, or whether they will forbear, (for they _are_ a rebellious house,) yet shall know that there hath been a prophet among them.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And thou, son of man, be not afraid of them, neither be afraid of their words, though briers and thorns _be_ with thee, and thou dost dwell among scorpions: be not afraid of their words, nor be dismayed at their looks, though they _be_ a rebellious house.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And thou shalt speak my words unto them, whether they will hear, or whether they will forbear: for they _are_ most rebellious.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But thou, son of man, hear what I say unto thee; Be not thou rebellious like that rebellious house: open thy mouth, and eat that I give thee.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And when I looked, behold, an hand _was_ sent unto me; and, lo, a roll of a book _was_ therein;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he spread it before me; and it _was_ written within and without: and _there was_ written therein lamentations, and mourning, and woe.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Moreover he said unto me, Son of man, eat that thou findest; eat this roll, and go speak unto the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>So I opened my mouth, and he caused me to eat that roll.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he said unto me, Son of man, cause thy belly to eat, and fill thy bowels with this roll that I give thee. Then did I eat _it_; and it was in my mouth as honey for sweetness.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And he said unto me, Son of man, go, get thee unto the house of Israel, and speak with my words unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For thou _art_ not sent to a people of a strange speech and of an hard language, _but_ to the house of Israel;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Not to many people of a strange speech and of an hard language, whose words thou canst not understand. Surely, had I sent thee to them, they would have hearkened unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But the house of Israel will not hearken unto thee; for they will not hearken unto me: for all the house of Israel _are_ impudent and hardhearted.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Behold, I have made thy face strong against their faces, and thy forehead strong against their foreheads.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>As an adamant harder than flint have I made thy forehead: fear them not, neither be dismayed at their looks, though they _be_ a rebellious house.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Moreover he said unto me, Son of man, all my words that I shall speak unto thee receive in thine heart, and hear with thine ears.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And go, get thee to them of the captivity, unto the children of thy people, and speak unto them, and tell them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; whether they will hear, or whether they will forbear.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Then the spirit took me up, and I heard behind me a voice of a great rushing, _saying_, Blessed _be_ the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> from his place.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>_I heard_ also the noise of the wings of the living creatures that touched one another, and the noise of the wheels over against them, and a noise of a great rushing.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>So the spirit lifted me up, and took me away, and I went in bitterness, in the heat of my spirit; but the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was strong upon me.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Then I came to them of the captivity at Tel–abib, that dwelt by the river of Chebar, and I sat where they sat, and remained there astonished among them seven days.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass at the end of seven days, that the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Son of man, I have made thee a watchman unto the house of Israel: therefore hear the word at my mouth, and give them warning from me.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>When I say unto the wicked, Thou shalt surely die; and thou givest him not warning, nor speakest to warn the wicked from his wicked way, to save his life; the same wicked _man_ shall die in his iniquity; but his blood will I require at thine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Yet if thou warn the wicked, and he turn not from his wickedness, nor from his wicked way, he shall die in his iniquity; but thou hast delivered thy soul.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Again, When a righteous _man_ doth turn from his righteousness, and commit iniquity, and I lay a stumblingblock before him, he shall die: because thou hast not given him warning, he shall die in his sin, and his righteousness which he hath done shall not be remembered; but his blood will I require at thine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Nevertheless if thou warn the righteous _man_, that the righteous sin not, and he doth not sin, he shall surely live, because he is warned; also thou hast delivered thy soul.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was there upon me; and he said unto me, Arise, go forth into the plain, and I will there talk with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Then I arose, and went forth into the plain: and, behold, the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> stood there, as the glory which I saw by the river of Chebar: and I fell on my face.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Then the spirit entered into me, and set me upon my feet, and spake with me, and said unto me, Go, shut thyself within thine house.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But thou, O son of man, behold, they shall put bands upon thee, and shall bind thee with them, and thou shalt not go out among them:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And I will make thy tongue cleave to the roof of thy mouth, that thou shalt be dumb, and shalt not be to them a reprover: for they _are_ a rebellious house.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>But when I speak with thee, I will open thy mouth, and thou shalt say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; He that heareth, let him hear; and he that forbeareth, let him forbear: for they _are_ a rebellious house.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Thou also, son of man, take thee a tile, and lay it before thee, and pourtray upon it the city, _even_ Jerusalem:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And lay siege against it, and build a fort against it, and cast a mount against it; set the camp also against it, and set _battering_ rams against it round about.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Moreover take thou unto thee an iron pan, and set it _for_ a wall of iron between thee and the city: and set thy face against it, and it shall be besieged, and thou shalt lay siege against it. This _shall be_ a sign to the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Lie thou also upon thy left side, and lay the iniquity of the house of Israel upon it: _according_ to the number of the days that thou shalt lie upon it thou shalt bear their iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For I have laid upon thee the years of their iniquity, according to the number of the days, three hundred and ninety days: so shalt thou bear the iniquity of the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And when thou hast accomplished them, lie again on thy right side, and thou shalt bear the iniquity of the house of Judah forty days: I have appointed thee each day for a year.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore thou shalt set thy face toward the siege of Jerusalem, and thine arm _shall be_ uncovered, and thou shalt prophesy against it.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And, behold, I will lay bands upon thee, and thou shalt not turn thee from one side to another, till thou hast ended the days of thy siege.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Take thou also unto thee wheat, and barley, and beans, and lentiles, and millet, and fitches, and put them in one vessel, and make thee bread thereof, _according_ to the number of the days that thou shalt lie upon thy side, three hundred and ninety days shalt thou eat thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And thy meat which thou shalt eat _shall be_ by weight, twenty shekels a day: from time to time shalt thou eat it.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Thou shalt drink also water by measure, the sixth part of an hin: from time to time shalt thou drink.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And thou shalt eat it _as_ barley cakes, and thou shalt bake it with dung that cometh out of man, in their sight.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said, Even thus shall the children of Israel eat their defiled bread among the Gentiles, whither I will drive them.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Then said I, Ah Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>! behold, my soul hath not been polluted: for from my youth up even till now have I not eaten of that which dieth of itself, or is torn in pieces; neither came there abominable flesh into my mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Then he said unto me, Lo, I have given thee cow’s dung for man’s dung, and thou shalt prepare thy bread therewith.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Moreover he said unto me, Son of man, behold, I will break the staff of bread in Jerusalem: and they shall eat bread by weight, and with care; and they shall drink water by measure, and with astonishment:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>That they may want bread and water, and be astonied one with another, and consume away for their iniquity.  

## CHAPTER 5.

And thou, son of man, take thee a sharp knife, take thee a barber’s razor, and cause _it_ to pass upon thine head and upon thy beard: then take thee balances to weigh, and divide the _hair_.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Thou shalt burn with fire a third part in the midst of the city, when the days of the siege are fulfilled: and thou shalt take a third part, _and_ smite about it with a knife: and a third part thou shalt scatter in the wind; and I will draw out a sword after them.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Thou shalt also take thereof a few in number, and bind them in thy skirts.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then take of them again, and cast them into the midst of the fire, and burn them in the fire; _for_ thereof shall a fire come forth into all the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; This _is_ Jerusalem: I have set it in the midst of the nations and countries _that are_ round about her.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And she hath changed my judgments into wickedness more than the nations, and my statutes more than the countries that _are_ round about her: for they have refused my judgments and my statutes, they have not walked in them.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because ye multiplied more than the nations that _are_ round about you, _and_ have not walked in my statutes, neither have kept my judgments, neither have done according to the judgments of the nations that _are_ round about you;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I, even I, _am_ against thee, and will execute judgments in the midst of thee in the sight of the nations.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And I will do in thee that which I have not done, and whereunto I will not do any more the like, because of all thine abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Therefore the fathers shall eat the sons in the midst of thee, and the sons shall eat their fathers; and I will execute judgments in thee, and the whole remnant of thee will I scatter into all the winds.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Wherefore, _as_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Surely, because thou hast defiled my sanctuary with all thy detestable things, and with all thine abominations, therefore will I also diminish _thee_; neither shall mine eye spare, neither will I have any pity.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ A third part of thee shall die with the pestilence, and with famine shall they be consumed in the midst of thee: and a third part shall fall by the sword round about thee; and I will scatter a third part into all the winds, and I will draw out a sword after them.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thus shall mine anger be accomplished, and I will cause my fury to rest upon them, and I will be comforted: and they shall know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_ in my zeal, when I have accomplished my fury in them.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Moreover I will make thee waste, and a reproach among the nations that _are_ round about thee, in the sight of all that pass by.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So it shall be a reproach and a taunt, an instruction and an astonishment unto the nations that _are_ round about thee, when I shall execute judgments in thee in anger and in fury and in furious rebukes. I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>When I shall send upon them the evil arrows of famine, which shall be for _their_ destruction, _and_ which I will send to destroy you: and I will increase the famine upon you, and will break your staff of bread:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>So will I send upon you famine and evil beasts, and they shall bereave thee; and pestilence and blood shall pass through thee; and I will bring the sword upon thee. I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_.  

## CHAPTER 6.

And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, set thy face toward the mountains of Israel, and prophesy against them,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And say, Ye mountains of Israel, hear the word of the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> to the mountains, and to the hills, to the rivers, and to the valleys; Behold, I, _even_ I, will bring a sword upon you, and I will destroy your high places.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And your altars shall be desolate, and your images shall be broken: and I will cast down your slain _men_ before your idols.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And I will lay the dead carcases of the children of Israel before their idols; and I will scatter your bones round about your altars.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>In all your dwellingplaces the cities shall be laid waste, and the high places shall be desolate; that your altars may be laid waste and made desolate, and your idols may be broken and cease, and your images may be cut down, and your works may be abolished.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the slain shall fall in the midst of you, and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Yet will I leave a remnant, that ye may have _some_ that shall escape the sword among the nations, when ye shall be scattered through the countries.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And they that escape of you shall remember me among the nations whither they shall be carried captives, because I am broken with their whorish heart, which hath departed from me, and with their eyes, which go a whoring after their idols: and they shall lothe themselves for the evils which they have committed in all their abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _and that_ I have not said in vain that I would do this evil unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Smite with thine hand, and stamp with thy foot, and say, Alas for all the evil abominations of the house of Israel! for they shall fall by the sword, by the famine, and by the pestilence.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>He that is far off shall die of the pestilence; and he that is near shall fall by the sword; and he that remaineth and is besieged shall die by the famine: thus will I accomplish my fury upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Then shall ye know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when their slain _men_ shall be among their idols round about their altars, upon every high hill, in all the tops of the mountains, and under every green tree, and under every thick oak, the place where they did offer sweet savour to all their idols.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>So will I stretch out my hand upon them, and make the land desolate, yea, more desolate than the wilderness toward Diblath, in all their habitations: and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 7.

Moreover the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Also, thou son of man, thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> unto the land of Israel; An end, the end is come upon the four corners of the land.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Now _is_ the end _come_ upon thee, and I will send mine anger upon thee, and will judge thee according to thy ways, and will recompense upon thee all thine abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And mine eye shall not spare thee, neither will I have pity: but I will recompense thy ways upon thee, and thine abominations shall be in the midst of thee: and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; An evil, an only evil, behold, is come.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>An end is come, the end is come: it watcheth for thee; behold, it is come.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>The morning is come unto thee, O thou that dwellest in the land: the time is come, the day of trouble _is_ near, and not the sounding again of the mountains.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Now will I shortly pour out my fury upon thee, and accomplish mine anger upon thee: and I will judge thee according to thy ways, and will recompense thee for all thine abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And mine eye shall not spare, neither will I have pity: I will recompense thee according to thy ways and thine abominations _that_ are in the midst of thee; and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that smiteth.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Behold the day, behold, it is come: the morning is gone forth; the rod hath blossomed, pride hath budded.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Violence is risen up into a rod of wickedness: none of them _shall remain_, nor of their multitude, nor of any of theirs: neither _shall there be_ wailing for them.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The time is come, the day draweth near: let not the buyer rejoice, nor the seller mourn: for wrath _is_ upon all the multitude thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For the seller shall not return to that which is sold, although they were yet alive: for the vision _is_ touching the whole multitude thereof, _which_ shall not return; neither shall any strengthen himself in the iniquity of his life.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>They have blown the trumpet, even to make all ready; but none goeth to the battle: for my wrath _is_ upon all the multitude thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The sword _is_ without, and the pestilence and the famine within: he that _is_ in the field shall die with the sword; and he that _is_ in the city, famine and pestilence shall devour him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ But they that escape of them shall escape, and shall be on the mountains like doves of the valleys, all of them mourning, every one for his iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>All hands shall be feeble, and all knees shall be weak _as_ water.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>They shall also gird _themselves_ with sackcloth, and horror shall cover them; and shame _shall be_ upon all faces, and baldness upon all their heads.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>They shall cast their silver in the streets, and their gold shall be removed: their silver and their gold shall not be able to deliver them in the day of the wrath of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: they shall not satisfy their souls, neither fill their bowels: because it is the stumblingblock of their iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ As for the beauty of his ornament, he set it in majesty: but they made the images of their abominations _and_ of their detestable things therein: therefore have I set it far from them.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And I will give it into the hands of the strangers for a prey, and to the wicked of the earth for a spoil; and they shall pollute it.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>My face will I turn also from them, and they shall pollute my secret _place_: for the robbers shall enter into it, and defile it.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Make a chain: for the land is full of bloody crimes, and the city is full of violence.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Wherefore I will bring the worst of the heathen, and they shall possess their houses: I will also make the pomp of the strong to cease; and their holy places shall be defiled.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Destruction cometh; and they shall seek peace, and _there shall be_ none.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Mischief shall come upon mischief, and rumour shall be upon rumour; then shall they seek a vision of the prophet; but the law shall perish from the priest, and counsel from the ancients.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>The king shall mourn, and the prince shall be clothed with desolation, and the hands of the people of the land shall be troubled: I will do unto them after their way, and according to their deserts will I judge them; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 8.

And it came to pass in the sixth year, in the sixth _month_, in the fifth _day_ of the month, _as_ I sat in mine house, and the elders of Judah sat before me, that the hand of the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> fell there upon me.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then I beheld, and lo a likeness as the appearance of fire: from the appearance of his loins even downward, fire; and from his loins even upward, as the appearance of brightness, as the colour of amber.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he put forth the form of an hand, and took me by a lock of mine head; and the spirit lifted me up between the earth and the heaven, and brought me in the visions of God to Jerusalem, to the door of the inner gate that looketh toward the north; where _was_ the seat of the image of jealousy, which provoketh to jealousy.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And, behold, the glory of the God of Israel _was_ there, according to the vision that I saw in the plain.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Then said he unto me, Son of man, lift up thine eyes now the way toward the north. So I lifted up mine eyes the way toward the north, and behold northward at the gate of the altar this image of jealousy in the entry.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>He said furthermore unto me, Son of man, seest thou what they do? _even_ the great abominations that the house of Israel committeth here, that I should go far off from my sanctuary? but turn thee yet again, _and_ thou shalt see greater abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And he brought me to the door of the court; and when I looked, behold a hole in the wall.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Then said he unto me, Son of man, dig now in the wall: and when I had digged in the wall, behold a door.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he said unto me, Go in, and behold the wicked abominations that they do here.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>So I went in and saw; and behold every form of creeping things, and abominable beasts, and all the idols of the house of Israel, pourtrayed upon the wall round about.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And there stood before them seventy men of the ancients of the house of Israel, and in the midst of them stood Jaazaniah the son of Shaphan, with every man his censer in his hand; and a thick cloud of incense went up.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Then said he unto me, Son of man, hast thou seen what the ancients of the house of Israel do in the dark, every man in the chambers of his imagery? for they say, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> seeth us not; the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath forsaken the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ He said also unto me, Turn thee yet again, _and_ thou shalt see greater abominations that they do.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Then he brought me to the door of the gate of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> house which _was_ toward the north; and, behold, there sat women weeping for Tammuz.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Then said he unto me, Hast thou seen _this_, O son of man? turn thee yet again, _and_ thou shalt see greater abominations than these.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And he brought me into the inner court of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> house, and, behold, at the door of the temple of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, between the porch and the altar, _were_ about five and twenty men, with their backs toward the temple of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and their faces toward the east; and they worshipped the sun toward the east.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Then he said unto me, Hast thou seen _this_, O son of man? Is it a light thing to the house of Judah that they commit the abominations which they commit here? for they have filled the land with violence, and have returned to provoke me to anger: and, lo, they put the branch to their nose.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Therefore will I also deal in fury: mine eye shall not spare, neither will I have pity: and though they cry in mine ears with a loud voice, _yet_ will I not hear them.  

## CHAPTER 9.

He cried also in mine ears with a loud voice, saying, Cause them that have charge over the city to draw near, even every man _with_ his destroying weapon in his hand.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And, behold, six men came from the way of the higher gate, which lieth toward the north, and every man a slaughter weapon in his hand; and one man among them _was_ clothed with linen, with a writer’s inkhorn by his side: and they went in, and stood beside the brasen altar.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the glory of the God of Israel was gone up from the cherub, whereupon he was, to the threshold of the house. And he called to the man clothed with linen, which _had_ the writer’s inkhorn by his side;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto him, Go through the midst of the city, through the midst of Jerusalem, and set a mark upon the foreheads of the men that sigh and that cry for all the abominations that be done in the midst thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And to the others he said in mine hearing, Go ye after him through the city, and smite: let not your eye spare, neither have ye pity:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Slay utterly old _and_ young, both maids, and little children, and women: but come not near any man upon whom _is_ the mark; and begin at my sanctuary. Then they began at the ancient men which _were_ before the house.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Defile the house, and fill the courts with the slain: go ye forth. And they went forth, and slew in the city.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, while they were slaying them, and I was left, that I fell upon my face, and cried, and said, Ah Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>! wilt thou destroy all the residue of Israel in thy pouring out of thy fury upon Jerusalem?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then said he unto me, The iniquity of the house of Israel and Judah _is_ exceeding great, and the land is full of blood, and the city full of perverseness: for they say, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath forsaken the earth, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> seeth not.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And as for me also, mine eye shall not spare, neither will I have pity, _but_ I will recompense their way upon their head.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And, behold, the man clothed with linen, which _had_ the inkhorn by his side, reported the matter, saying, I have done as thou hast commanded me.  

## CHAPTER 10.

Then I looked, and, behold, in the firmament that was above the head of the cherubims there appeared over them as it were a sapphire stone, as the appearance of the likeness of a throne.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he spake unto the man clothed with linen, and said, Go in between the wheels, _even_ under the cherub, and fill thine hand with coals of fire from between the cherubims, and scatter _them_ over the city. And he went in in my sight.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Now the cherubims stood on the right side of the house, when the man went in; and the cloud filled the inner court.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> went up from the cherub, _and stood_ over the threshold of the house; and the house was filled with the cloud, and the court was full of the brightness of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> glory.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the sound of the cherubims’ wings was heard _even_ to the outer court, as the voice of the Almighty God when he speaketh.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, _that_ when he had commanded the man clothed with linen, saying, Take fire from between the wheels, from between the cherubims; then he went in, and stood beside the wheels.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And _one_ cherub stretched forth his hand from between the cherubims unto the fire that _was_ between the cherubims, and took _thereof_, and put _it_ into the hands of _him that was_ clothed with linen: who took _it_, and went out.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And there appeared in the cherubims the form of a man’s hand under their wings.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And when I looked, behold the four wheels by the cherubims, one wheel by one cherub, and another wheel by another cherub: and the appearance of the wheels _was_ as the colour of a beryl stone.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And _as for_ their appearances, they four had one likeness, as if a wheel had been in the midst of a wheel.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>When they went, they went upon their four sides; they turned not as they went, but to the place whither the head looked they followed it; they turned not as they went.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And their whole body, and their backs, and their hands, and their wings, and the wheels, _were_ full of eyes round about, _even_ the wheels that they four had.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>As for the wheels, it was cried unto them in my hearing, O wheel.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And every one had four faces: the first face _was_ the face of a cherub, and the second face _was_ the face of a man, and the third the face of a lion, and the fourth the face of an eagle.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the cherubims were lifted up. This _is_ the living creature that I saw by the river of Chebar.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And when the cherubims went, the wheels went by them: and when the cherubims lifted up their wings to mount up from the earth, the same wheels also turned not from beside them.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>When they stood, _these_ stood; and when they were lifted up, _these_ lifted up themselves _also_: for the spirit of the living creature _was_ in them.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Then the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> departed from off the threshold of the house, and stood over the cherubims.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the cherubims lifted up their wings, and mounted up from the earth in my sight: when they went out, the wheels also _were_ beside them, and _every one_ stood at the door of the east gate of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> house; and the glory of the God of Israel _was_ over them above.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>This _is_ the living creature that I saw under the God of Israel by the river of Chebar; and I knew that they _were_ the cherubims.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Every one had four faces apiece, and every one four wings; and the likeness of the hands of a man _was_ under their wings.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the likeness of their faces _was_ the same faces which I saw by the river of Chebar, their appearances and themselves: they went every one straight forward.  

## CHAPTER 11.

Moreover the spirit lifted me up, and brought me unto the east gate of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> house, which looketh eastward: and behold at the door of the gate five and twenty men; among whom I saw Jaazaniah the son of Azur, and Pelatiah the son of Benaiah, princes of the people.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then said he unto me, Son of man, these _are_ the men that devise mischief, and give wicked counsel in this city:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Which say, _It is_ not near; let us build houses: this _city is_ the caldron, and we _be_ the flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore prophesy against them, prophesy, O son of man.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the Spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> fell upon me, and said unto me, Speak; Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; Thus have ye said, O house of Israel: for I know the things that come into your mind, _every one of_ them.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Ye have multiplied your slain in this city, and ye have filled the streets thereof with the slain.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Your slain whom ye have laid in the midst of it, they _are_ the flesh, and this _city is_ the caldron: but I will bring you forth out of the midst of it.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Ye have feared the sword; and I will bring a sword upon you, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And I will bring you out of the midst thereof, and deliver you into the hands of strangers, and will execute judgments among you.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Ye shall fall by the sword; I will judge you in the border of Israel; and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>This _city_ shall not be your caldron, neither shall ye be the flesh in the midst thereof; _but_ I will judge you in the border of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: for ye have not walked in my statutes, neither executed my judgments, but have done after the manners of the heathen that _are_ round about you.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, when I prophesied, that Pelatiah the son of Benaiah died. Then fell I down upon my face, and cried with a loud voice, and said, Ah Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>! wilt thou make a full end of the remnant of Israel?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Again the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Son of man, thy brethren, _even_ thy brethren, the men of thy kindred, and all the house of Israel wholly, _are_ they unto whom the inhabitants of Jerusalem have said, Get you far from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: unto us is this land given in possession.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Therefore say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Although I have cast them far off among the heathen, and although I have scattered them among the countries, yet will I be to them as a little sanctuary in the countries where they shall come.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Therefore say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will even gather you from the people, and assemble you out of the countries where ye have been scattered, and I will give you the land of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they shall come thither, and they shall take away all the detestable things thereof and all the abominations thereof from thence.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And I will give them one heart, and I will put a new spirit within you; and I will take the stony heart out of their flesh, and will give them an heart of flesh:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>That they may walk in my statutes, and keep mine ordinances, and do them: and they shall be my people, and I will be their God.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But _as for them_ whose heart walketh after the heart of their detestable things and their abominations, I will recompense their way upon their own heads, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Then did the cherubims lift up their wings, and the wheels beside them; and the glory of the God of Israel _was_ over them above.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the glory of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> went up from the midst of the city, and stood upon the mountain which _is_ on the east side of the city.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Afterwards the spirit took me up, and brought me in a vision by the Spirit of God into Chaldea, to them of the captivity. So the vision that I had seen went up from me.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Then I spake unto them of the captivity all the things that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had shewed me.  

## CHAPTER 12.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> also came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, thou dwellest in the midst of a rebellious house, which have eyes to see, and see not; they have ears to hear, and hear not: for they _are_ a rebellious house.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore, thou son of man, prepare thee stuff for removing, and remove by day in their sight; and thou shalt remove from thy place to another place in their sight: it may be they will consider, though they _be_ a rebellious house.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then shalt thou bring forth thy stuff by day in their sight, as stuff for removing: and thou shalt go forth at even in their sight, as they that go forth into captivity.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Dig thou through the wall in their sight, and carry out thereby.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>In their sight shalt thou bear _it_ upon _thy_ shoulders, _and_ carry _it_ forth in the twilight: thou shalt cover thy face, that thou see not the ground: for I have set thee _for_ a sign unto the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And I did so as I was commanded: I brought forth my stuff by day, as stuff for captivity, and in the even I digged through the wall with mine hand; I brought _it_ forth in the twilight, _and_ I bare _it_ upon _my_ shoulder in their sight.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And in the morning came the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Son of man, hath not the house of Israel, the rebellious house, said unto thee, What doest thou?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Say thou unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; This burden _concerneth_ the prince in Jerusalem, and all the house of Israel that _are_ among them.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Say, I _am_ your sign: like as I have done, so shall it be done unto them: they shall remove _and_ go into captivity.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the prince that _is_ among them shall bear upon _his_ shoulder in the twilight, and shall go forth: they shall dig through the wall to carry out thereby: he shall cover his face, that he see not the ground with _his_ eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>My net also will I spread upon him, and he shall be taken in my snare: and I will bring him to Babylon _to_ the land of the Chaldeans; yet shall he not see it, though he shall die there.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And I will scatter toward every wind all that _are_ about him to help him, and all his bands; and I will draw out the sword after them.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I shall scatter them among the nations, and disperse them in the countries.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But I will leave a few men of them from the sword, from the famine, and from the pestilence; that they may declare all their abominations among the heathen whither they come; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Moreover the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came to me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Son of man, eat thy bread with quaking, and drink thy water with trembling and with carefulness;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And say unto the people of the land, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> of the inhabitants of Jerusalem, _and_ of the land of Israel; They shall eat their bread with carefulness, and drink their water with astonishment, that her land may be desolate from all that is therein, because of the violence of all them that dwell therein.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the cities that are inhabited shall be laid waste, and the land shall be desolate; and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Son of man, what _is_ that proverb _that_ ye have in the land of Israel, saying, The days are prolonged, and every vision faileth?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Tell them therefore, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will make this proverb to cease, and they shall no more use it as a proverb in Israel; but say unto them, The days are at hand, and the effect of every vision.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For there shall be no more any vain vision nor flattering divination within the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: I will speak, and the word that I shall speak shall come to pass; it shall be no more prolonged: for in your days, O rebellious house, will I say the word, and will perform it, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ Again the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came to me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Son of man, behold, _they of_ the house of Israel say, The vision that he seeth _is_ for many days _to come_, and he prophesieth of the times _that are_ far off.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Therefore say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; There shall none of my words be prolonged any more, but the word which I have spoken shall be done, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 13.

And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, prophesy against the prophets of Israel that prophesy, and say thou unto them that prophesy out of their own hearts, Hear ye the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Woe unto the foolish prophets, that follow their own spirit, and have seen nothing!  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>O Israel, thy prophets are like the foxes in the deserts.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Ye have not gone up into the gaps, neither made up the hedge for the house of Israel to stand in the battle in the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>They have seen vanity and lying divination, saying, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> saith: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath not sent them: and they have made _others_ to hope that they would confirm the word.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Have ye not seen a vain vision, and have ye not spoken a lying divination, whereas ye say, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> saith _it_; albeit I have not spoken?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because ye have spoken vanity, and seen lies, therefore, behold, I _am_ against you, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And mine hand shall be upon the prophets that see vanity, and that divine lies: they shall not be in the assembly of my people, neither shall they be written in the writing of the house of Israel, neither shall they enter into the land of Israel; and ye shall know that I _am_ the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Because, even because they have seduced my people, saying, Peace; and _there was_ no peace; and one built up a wall, and, lo, others daubed it with untempered _morter_:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Say unto them which daub _it_ with untempered _morter_, that it shall fall: there shall be an overflowing shower; and ye, O great hailstones, shall fall; and a stormy wind shall rend _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Lo, when the wall is fallen, shall it not be said unto you, Where _is_ the daubing wherewith ye have daubed _it_?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will even rend _it_ with a stormy wind in my fury; and there shall be an overflowing shower in mine anger, and great hailstones in _my_ fury to consume _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>So will I break down the wall that ye have daubed with untempered _morter_, and bring it down to the ground, so that the foundation thereof shall be discovered, and it shall fall, and ye shall be consumed in the midst thereof: and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thus will I accomplish my wrath upon the wall, and upon them that have daubed it with untempered _morter_, and will say unto you, The wall _is_ no _more_, neither they that daubed it;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>_To wit_, the prophets of Israel which prophesy concerning Jerusalem, and which see visions of peace for her, and _there is_ no peace, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Likewise, thou son of man, set thy face against the daughters of thy people, which prophesy out of their own heart; and prophesy thou against them,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Woe to the _women_ that sew pillows to all armholes, and make kerchiefs upon the head of every stature to hunt souls! Will ye hunt the souls of my people, and will ye save the souls alive _that come_ unto you?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And will ye pollute me among my people for handfuls of barley and for pieces of bread, to slay the souls that should not die, and to save the souls alive that should not live, by your lying to my people that hear _your_ lies?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Wherefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I _am_ against your pillows, wherewith ye there hunt the souls to make _them_ fly, and I will tear them from your arms, and will let the souls go, _even_ the souls that ye hunt to make _them_ fly.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Your kerchiefs also will I tear, and deliver my people out of your hand, and they shall be no more in your hand to be hunted; and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Because with lies ye have made the heart of the righteous sad, whom I have not made sad; and strengthened the hands of the wicked, that he should not return from his wicked way, by promising him life:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Therefore ye shall see no more vanity, nor divine divinations: for I will deliver my people out of your hand: and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 14.

Then came certain of the elders of Israel unto me, and sat before me.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Son of man, these men have set up their idols in their heart, and put the stumblingblock of their iniquity before their face: should I be enquired of at all by them?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Therefore speak unto them, and say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Every man of the house of Israel that setteth up his idols in his heart, and putteth the stumblingblock of his iniquity before his face, and cometh to the prophet; I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will answer him that cometh according to the multitude of his idols;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>That I may take the house of Israel in their own heart, because they are all estranged from me through their idols.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore say unto the house of Israel, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Repent, and turn _yourselves_ from your idols; and turn away your faces from all your abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For every one of the house of Israel, or of the stranger that sojourneth in Israel, which separateth himself from me, and setteth up his idols in his heart, and putteth the stumblingblock of his iniquity before his face, and cometh to a prophet to enquire of him concerning me; I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will answer him by myself:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And I will set my face against that man, and will make him a sign and a proverb, and I will cut him off from the midst of my people; and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And if the prophet be deceived when he hath spoken a thing, I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have deceived that prophet, and I will stretch out my hand upon him, and will destroy him from the midst of my people Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they shall bear the punishment of their iniquity: the punishment of the prophet shall be even as the punishment of him that seeketh _unto him_;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>That the house of Israel may go no more astray from me, neither be polluted any more with all their transgressions; but that they may be my people, and I may be their God, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came again to me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Son of man, when the land sinneth against me by trespassing grievously, then will I stretch out mine hand upon it, and will break the staff of the bread thereof, and will send famine upon it, and will cut off man and beast from it:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Though these three men, Noah, Daniel, and Job, were in it, they should deliver _but_ their own souls by their righteousness, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ If I cause noisome beasts to pass through the land, and they spoil it, so that it be desolate, that no man may pass through because of the beasts:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>_Though_ these three men _were_ in it, _as_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, they shall deliver neither sons nor daughters; they only shall be delivered, but the land shall be desolate.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Or _if_ I bring a sword upon that land, and say, Sword, go through the land; so that I cut off man and beast from it:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Though these three men _were_ in it, _as_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, they shall deliver neither sons nor daughters, but they only shall be delivered themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Or _if_ I send a pestilence into that land, and pour out my fury upon it in blood, to cut off from it man and beast:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Though Noah, Daniel, and Job, _were_ in it, _as_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, they shall deliver neither son nor daughter; they shall _but_ deliver their own souls by their righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; How much more when I send my four sore judgments upon Jerusalem, the sword, and the famine, and the noisome beast, and the pestilence, to cut off from it man and beast?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Yet, behold, therein shall be left a remnant that shall be brought forth, _both_ sons and daughters: behold, they shall come forth unto you, and ye shall see their way and their doings: and ye shall be comforted concerning the evil that I have brought upon Jerusalem, _even_ concerning all that I have brought upon it.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they shall comfort you, when ye see their ways and their doings: and ye shall know that I have not done without cause all that I have done in it, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 15.

And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, What is the vine tree more than any tree, _or than_ a branch which is among the trees of the forest?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Shall wood be taken thereof to do any work? or will _men_ take a pin of it to hang any vessel thereon?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Behold, it is cast into the fire for fuel; the fire devoureth both the ends of it, and the midst of it is burned. Is it meet for _any_ work?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Behold, when it was whole, it was meet for no work: how much less shall it be meet yet for _any_ work, when the fire hath devoured it, and it is burned?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; As the vine tree among the trees of the forest, which I have given to the fire for fuel, so will I give the inhabitants of Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And I will set my face against them; they shall go out from _one_ fire, and _another_ fire shall devour them; and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I set my face against them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And I will make the land desolate, because they have committed a trespass, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 16.

Again the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, cause Jerusalem to know her abominations,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> unto Jerusalem; Thy birth and thy nativity _is_ of the land of Canaan; thy father _was_ an Amorite, and thy mother an Hittite.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And _as for_ thy nativity, in the day thou wast born thy navel was not cut, neither wast thou washed in water to supple _thee_; thou wast not salted at all, nor swaddled at all.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>None eye pitied thee, to do any of these unto thee, to have compassion upon thee; but thou wast cast out in the open field, to the lothing of thy person, in the day that thou wast born.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And when I passed by thee, and saw thee polluted in thine own blood, I said unto thee _when thou wast_ in thy blood, Live; yea, I said unto thee _when thou wast_ in thy blood, Live.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>I have caused thee to multiply as the bud of the field, and thou hast increased and waxen great, and thou art come to excellent ornaments: _thy_ breasts are fashioned, and thine hair is grown, whereas thou _wast_ naked and bare.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Now when I passed by thee, and looked upon thee, behold, thy time _was_ the time of love; and I spread my skirt over thee, and covered thy nakedness: yea, I sware unto thee, and entered into a covenant with thee, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, and thou becamest mine.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then washed I thee with water; yea, I throughly washed away thy blood from thee, and I anointed thee with oil.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>I clothed thee also with broidered work, and shod thee with badgers’ skin, and I girded thee about with fine linen, and I covered thee with silk.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I decked thee also with ornaments, and I put bracelets upon thy hands, and a chain on thy neck.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And I put a jewel on thy forehead, and earrings in thine ears, and a beautiful crown upon thine head.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thus wast thou decked with gold and silver; and thy raiment _was of_ fine linen, and silk, and broidered work; thou didst eat fine flour, and honey, and oil: and thou wast exceeding beautiful, and thou didst prosper into a kingdom.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And thy renown went forth among the heathen for thy beauty: for it _was_ perfect through my comeliness, which I had put upon thee, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ But thou didst trust in thine own beauty, and playedst the harlot because of thy renown, and pouredst out thy fornications on every one that passed by; his it was.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And of thy garments thou didst take, and deckedst thy high places with divers colours, and playedst the harlot thereupon: _the like things_ shall not come, neither shall it be _so_.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thou hast also taken thy fair jewels of my gold and of my silver, which I had given thee, and madest to thyself images of men, and didst commit whoredom with them,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And tookest thy broidered garments, and coveredst them: and thou hast set mine oil and mine incense before them.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>My meat also which I gave thee, fine flour, and oil, and honey, _wherewith_ I fed thee, thou hast even set it before them for a sweet savour: and _thus_ it was, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Moreover thou hast taken thy sons and thy daughters, whom thou hast borne unto me, and these hast thou sacrificed unto them to be devoured. _Is this_ of thy whoredoms a small matter,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>That thou hast slain my children, and delivered them to cause them to pass through _the fire_ for them?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And in all thine abominations and thy whoredoms thou hast not remembered the days of thy youth, when thou wast naked and bare, _and_ wast polluted in thy blood.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And it came to pass after all thy wickedness, (woe, woe unto thee! saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>;)  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>_That_ thou hast also built unto thee an eminent place, and hast made thee an high place in every street.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Thou hast built thy high place at every head of the way, and hast made thy beauty to be abhorred, and hast opened thy feet to every one that passed by, and multiplied thy whoredoms.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Thou hast also committed fornication with the Egyptians thy neighbours, great of flesh; and hast increased thy whoredoms, to provoke me to anger.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Behold, therefore I have stretched out my hand over thee, and have diminished thine ordinary _food_, and delivered thee unto the will of them that hate thee, the daughters of the Philistines, which are ashamed of thy lewd way.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Thou hast played the whore also with the Assyrians, because thou wast unsatiable; yea, thou hast played the harlot with them, and yet couldest not be satisfied.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Thou hast moreover multiplied thy fornication in the land of Canaan unto Chaldea; and yet thou wast not satisfied herewith.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>How weak is thine heart, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, seeing thou doest all these _things_, the work of an imperious whorish woman;  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>In that thou buildest thine eminent place in the head of every way, and makest thine high place in every street; and hast not been as an harlot, in that thou scornest hire;  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>_But as_ a wife that committeth adultery, _which_ taketh strangers instead of her husband!  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>They give gifts to all whores: but thou givest thy gifts to all thy lovers, and hirest them, that they may come unto thee on every side for thy whoredom.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And the contrary is in thee from _other_ women in thy whoredoms, whereas none followeth thee to commit whoredoms: and in that thou givest a reward, and no reward is given unto thee, therefore thou art contrary.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore, O harlot, hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because thy filthiness was poured out, and thy nakedness discovered through thy whoredoms with thy lovers, and with all the idols of thy abominations, and by the blood of thy children, which thou didst give unto them;  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Behold, therefore I will gather all thy lovers, with whom thou hast taken pleasure, and all _them_ that thou hast loved, with all _them_ that thou hast hated; I will even gather them round about against thee, and will discover thy nakedness unto them, that they may see all thy nakedness.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And I will judge thee, as women that break wedlock and shed blood are judged; and I will give thee blood in fury and jealousy.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And I will also give thee into their hand, and they shall throw down thine eminent place, and shall break down thy high places: they shall strip thee also of thy clothes, and shall take thy fair jewels, and leave thee naked and bare.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>They shall also bring up a company against thee, and they shall stone thee with stones, and thrust thee through with their swords.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And they shall burn thine houses with fire, and execute judgments upon thee in the sight of many women: and I will cause thee to cease from playing the harlot, and thou also shalt give no hire any more.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>So will I make my fury toward thee to rest, and my jealousy shall depart from thee, and I will be quiet, and will be no more angry.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>Because thou hast not remembered the days of thy youth, but hast fretted me in all these _things_; behold, therefore I also will recompense thy way upon _thine_ head, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>: and thou shalt not commit this lewdness above all thine abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, every one that useth proverbs shall use _this_ proverb against thee, saying, As _is_ the mother, _so is_ her daughter.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>Thou _art_ thy mother’s daughter, that lotheth her husband and her children; and thou _art_ the sister of thy sisters, which lothed their husbands and their children: your mother _was_ an Hittite, and your father an Amorite.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And thine elder sister _is_ Samaria, she and her daughters that dwell at thy left hand: and thy younger sister, that dwelleth at thy right hand, _is_ Sodom and her daughters.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>Yet hast thou not walked after their ways, nor done after their abominations: but, as _if that were_ a very little _thing_, thou wast corrupted more than they in all thy ways.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>_As_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, Sodom thy sister hath not done, she nor her daughters, as thou hast done, thou and thy daughters.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>Behold, this was the iniquity of thy sister Sodom, pride, fulness of bread, and abundance of idleness was in her and in her daughters, neither did she strengthen the hand of the poor and needy.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>And they were haughty, and committed abomination before me: therefore I took them away as I saw _good_.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>Neither hath Samaria committed half of thy sins; but thou hast multiplied thine abominations more than they, and hast justified thy sisters in all thine abominations which thou hast done.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>Thou also, which hast judged thy sisters, bear thine own shame for thy sins that thou hast committed more abominable than they: they are more righteous than thou: yea, be thou confounded also, and bear thy shame, in that thou hast justified thy sisters.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>When I shall bring again their captivity, the captivity of Sodom and her daughters, and the captivity of Samaria and her daughters, then _will I bring again_ the captivity of thy captives in the midst of them:  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>That thou mayest bear thine own shame, and mayest be confounded in all that thou hast done, in that thou art a comfort unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>When thy sisters, Sodom and her daughters, shall return to their former estate, and Samaria and her daughters shall return to their former estate, then thou and thy daughters shall return to your former estate.  
<span class="verse-number">56&ensp;</span>For thy sister Sodom was not mentioned by thy mouth in the day of thy pride,  
<span class="verse-number">57&ensp;</span>Before thy wickedness was discovered, as at the time of _thy_ reproach of the daughters of Syria, and all _that are_ round about her, the daughters of the Philistines, which despise thee round about.  
<span class="verse-number">58&ensp;</span>Thou hast borne thy lewdness and thine abominations, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">59&ensp;</span>For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will even deal with thee as thou hast done, which hast despised the oath in breaking the covenant.  
<span class="verse-number">60&ensp;</span>¶ Nevertheless I will remember my covenant with thee in the days of thy youth, and I will establish unto thee an everlasting covenant.  
<span class="verse-number">61&ensp;</span>Then thou shalt remember thy ways, and be ashamed, when thou shalt receive thy sisters, thine elder and thy younger: and I will give them unto thee for daughters, but not by thy covenant.  
<span class="verse-number">62&ensp;</span>And I will establish my covenant with thee; and thou shalt know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">63&ensp;</span>That thou mayest remember, and be confounded, and never open thy mouth any more because of thy shame, when I am pacified toward thee for all that thou hast done, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 17.

And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, put forth a riddle, and speak a parable unto the house of Israel;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; A great eagle with great wings, longwinged, full of feathers, which had divers colours, came unto Lebanon, and took the highest branch of the cedar:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>He cropped off the top of his young twigs, and carried it into a land of traffick; he set it in a city of merchants.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>He took also of the seed of the land, and planted it in a fruitful field; he placed _it_ by great waters, _and_ set it _as_ a willow tree.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And it grew, and became a spreading vine of low stature, whose branches turned toward him, and the roots thereof were under him: so it became a vine, and brought forth branches, and shot forth sprigs.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>There was also another great eagle with great wings and many feathers: and, behold, this vine did bend her roots toward him, and shot forth her branches toward him, that he might water it by the furrows of her plantation.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>It was planted in a good soil by great waters, that it might bring forth branches, and that it might bear fruit, that it might be a goodly vine.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Say thou, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Shall it prosper? shall he not pull up the roots thereof, and cut off the fruit thereof, that it wither? it shall wither in all the leaves of her spring, even without great power or many people to pluck it up by the roots thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Yea, behold, _being_ planted, shall it prosper? shall it not utterly wither, when the east wind toucheth it? it shall wither in the furrows where it grew.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Moreover the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Say now to the rebellious house, Know ye not what these _things mean_? tell _them_, Behold, the king of Babylon is come to Jerusalem, and hath taken the king thereof, and the princes thereof, and led them with him to Babylon;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And hath taken of the king’s seed, and made a covenant with him, and hath taken an oath of him: he hath also taken the mighty of the land:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>That the kingdom might be base, that it might not lift itself up, _but_ that by keeping of his covenant it might stand.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But he rebelled against him in sending his ambassadors into Egypt, that they might give him horses and much people. Shall he prosper? shall he escape that doeth such _things_? or shall he break the covenant, and be delivered?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>_As_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, surely in the place _where_ the king _dwelleth_ that made him king, whose oath he despised, and whose covenant he brake, _even_ with him in the midst of Babylon he shall die.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Neither shall Pharaoh with _his_ mighty army and great company make for him in the war, by casting up mounts, and building forts, to cut off many persons:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Seeing he despised the oath by breaking the covenant, when, lo, he had given his hand, and hath done all these _things_, he shall not escape.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; _As_ I live, surely mine oath that he hath despised, and my covenant that he hath broken, even it will I recompense upon his own head.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And I will spread my net upon him, and he shall be taken in my snare, and I will bring him to Babylon, and will plead with him there for his trespass that he hath trespassed against me.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And all his fugitives with all his bands shall fall by the sword, and they that remain shall be scattered toward all winds: and ye shall know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will also take of the highest branch of the high cedar, and will set _it_; I will crop off from the top of his young twigs a tender one, and will plant _it_ upon an high mountain and eminent:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>In the mountain of the height of Israel will I plant it: and it shall bring forth boughs, and bear fruit, and be a goodly cedar: and under it shall dwell all fowl of every wing; in the shadow of the branches thereof shall they dwell.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And all the trees of the field shall know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have brought down the high tree, have exalted the low tree, have dried up the green tree, and have made the dry tree to flourish: I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken and have done _it_.  

## CHAPTER 18.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me again, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>What mean ye, that ye use this proverb concerning the land of Israel, saying, The fathers have eaten sour grapes, and the children’s teeth are set on edge?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>_As_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, ye shall not have _occasion_ any more to use this proverb in Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Behold, all souls are mine; as the soul of the father, so also the soul of the son is mine: the soul that sinneth, it shall die.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ But if a man be just, and do that which is lawful and right,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>_And_ hath not eaten upon the mountains, neither hath lifted up his eyes to the idols of the house of Israel, neither hath defiled his neighbour’s wife, neither hath come near to a menstruous woman,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And hath not oppressed any, _but_ hath restored to the debtor his pledge, hath spoiled none by violence, hath given his bread to the hungry, and hath covered the naked with a garment;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He _that_ hath not given forth upon usury, neither hath taken any increase, _that_ hath withdrawn his hand from iniquity, hath executed true judgment between man and man,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Hath walked in my statutes, and hath kept my judgments, to deal truly; he _is_ just, he shall surely live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ If he beget a son _that is_ a robber, a shedder of blood, and _that_ doeth the like to _any_ one of these _things_,

<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But I wrought for my name’s sake, that it should not be polluted before the heathen, among whom they _were_, in whose sight I made myself known unto them, in bringing them forth out of the land of Egypt.

<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But the house of Israel rebelled against me in the wilderness: they walked not in my statutes, and they despised my judgments, which _if_ a man do, he shall even live in them; and my sabbaths they greatly polluted: then I said, I would pour out my fury upon them in the wilderness, to consume them.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But I wrought for my name’s sake, that it should not be polluted before the heathen, in whose sight I brought them out.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Yet also I lifted up my hand unto them in the wilderness, that I would not bring them into the land which I had given _them_, flowing with milk and honey, which _is_ the glory of all lands;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Because they despised my judgments, and walked not in my statutes, but polluted my sabbaths: for their heart went after their idols.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Nevertheless mine eye spared them from destroying them, neither did I make an end of them in the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But I said unto their children in the wilderness, Walk ye not in the statutes of your fathers, neither observe their judgments, nor defile yourselves with their idols:

<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Notwithstanding the children rebelled against me: they walked not in my statutes, neither kept my judgments to do them, which _if_ a man do, he shall even live in them; they polluted my sabbaths: then I said, I would pour out my fury upon them, to accomplish my anger against them in the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Nevertheless I withdrew mine hand, and wrought for my name’s sake, that it should not be polluted in the sight of the heathen, in whose sight I brought them forth.

<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Then I said unto them, What _is_ the high place whereunto ye go? And the name thereof is called Bamah unto this day.

<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>For when ye offer your gifts, when ye make your sons to pass through the fire, ye pollute yourselves with all your idols, even unto this day: and shall I be enquired of by you, O house of Israel? _As_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, I will not be enquired of by you.

<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And I will bring you into the wilderness of the people, and there will I plead with you face to face.

<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And I will cause you to pass under the rod, and I will bring you into the bond of the covenant:

<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I shall bring you into the land of Israel, into the country _for_ the which I lifted up mine hand to give it to your fathers.

<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I have wrought with you for my name’s sake, not according to your wicked ways, nor according to your corrupt doings, O ye house of Israel, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.

<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And all flesh shall see that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have kindled it: it shall not be quenched.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>Then said I, Ah Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>! they say of me, Doth he not speak parables?

## CHAPTER 22.

Moreover the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,

<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, therefore I have smitten mine hand at thy dishonest gain which thou hast made, and at thy blood which hath been in the midst of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Can thine heart endure, or can thine hands be strong, in the days that I shall deal with thee? I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_, and will do _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And I will scatter thee among the heathen, and disperse thee in the countries, and will consume thy filthiness out of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And thou shalt take thine inheritance in thyself in the sight of the heathen, and thou shalt know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Son of man, the house of Israel is to me become dross: all they _are_ brass, and tin, and iron, and lead, in the midst of the furnace; they are _even_ the dross of silver.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because ye are all become dross, behold, therefore I will gather you into the midst of Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>_As_ they gather silver, and brass, and iron, and lead, and tin, into the midst of the furnace, to blow the fire upon it, to melt _it_; so will I gather _you_ in mine anger and in my fury, and I will leave _you there_, and melt you.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Yea, I will gather you, and blow upon you in the fire of my wrath, and ye shall be melted in the midst thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>As silver is melted in the midst of the furnace, so shall ye be melted in the midst thereof; and ye shall know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have poured out my fury upon you.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Son of man, say unto her, Thou _art_ the land that is not cleansed, nor rained upon in the day of indignation.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>_There is_ a conspiracy of her prophets in the midst thereof, like a roaring lion ravening the prey; they have devoured souls; they have taken the treasure and precious things; they have made her many widows in the midst thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Her priests have violated my law, and have profaned mine holy things: they have put no difference between the holy and profane, neither have they shewed _difference_ between the unclean and the clean, and have hid their eyes from my sabbaths, and I am profaned among them.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Her princes in the midst thereof _are_ like wolves ravening the prey, to shed blood, _and_ to destroy souls, to get dishonest gain.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And her prophets have daubed them with untempered _morter_, seeing vanity, and divining lies unto them, saying, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, when the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath not spoken.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>The people of the land have used oppression, and exercised robbery, and have vexed the poor and needy: yea, they have oppressed the stranger wrongfully.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And I sought for a man among them, that should make up the hedge, and stand in the gap before me for the land, that I should not destroy it: but I found none.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Therefore have I poured out mine indignation upon them; I have consumed them with the fire of my wrath: their own way have I recompensed upon their heads, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 23.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came again unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, there were two women, the daughters of one mother:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And they committed whoredoms in Egypt; they committed whoredoms in their youth: there were their breasts pressed, and there they bruised the teats of their virginity.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the names of them _were_ Aholah the elder, and Aholibah her sister: and they were mine, and they bare sons and daughters. Thus _were_ their names; Samaria _is_ Aholah, and Jerusalem Aholibah.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And Aholah played the harlot when she was mine; and she doted on her lovers, on the Assyrians _her_ neighbours,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>_Which were_ clothed with blue, captains and rulers, all of them desirable young men, horsemen riding upon horses.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Thus she committed her whoredoms with them, with all them _that were_ the chosen men of Assyria, and with all on whom she doted: with all their idols she defiled herself.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Neither left she her whoredoms _brought_ from Egypt: for in her youth they lay with her, and they bruised the breasts of her virginity, and poured their whoredom upon her.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Wherefore I have delivered her into the hand of her lovers, into the hand of the Assyrians, upon whom she doted.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>These discovered her nakedness: they took her sons and her daughters, and slew her with the sword: and she became famous among women; for they had executed judgment upon her.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And when her sister Aholibah saw _this_, she was more corrupt in her inordinate love than she, and in her whoredoms more than her sister in _her_ whoredoms.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>She doted upon the Assyrians _her_ neighbours, captains and rulers clothed most gorgeously, horsemen riding upon horses, all of them desirable young men.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Then I saw that she was defiled, _that_ they _took_ both one way,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And _that_ she increased her whoredoms: for when she saw men pourtrayed upon the wall, the images of the Chaldeans pourtrayed with vermilion,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Girded with girdles upon their loins, exceeding in dyed attire upon their heads, all of them princes to look to, after the manner of the Babylonians of Chaldea, the land of their nativity:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And as soon as she saw them with her eyes, she doted upon them, and sent messengers unto them into Chaldea.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the Babylonians came to her into the bed of love, and they defiled her with their whoredom, and she was polluted with them, and her mind was alienated from them.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>So she discovered her whoredoms, and discovered her nakedness: then my mind was alienated from her, like as my mind was alienated from her sister.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Yet she multiplied her whoredoms, in calling to remembrance the days of her youth, wherein she had played the harlot in the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For she doted upon their paramours, whose flesh _is as_ the flesh of asses, and whose issue _is like_ the issue of horses.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Thus thou calledst to remembrance the lewdness of thy youth, in bruising thy teats by the Egyptians for the paps of thy youth.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore, O Aholibah, thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will raise up thy lovers against thee, from whom thy mind is alienated, and I will bring them against thee on every side;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The Babylonians, and all the Chaldeans, Pekod, and Shoa, and Koa, _and_ all the Assyrians with them: all of them desirable young men, captains and rulers, great lords and renowned, all of them riding upon horses.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they shall come against thee with chariots, wagons, and wheels, and with an assembly of people, _which_ shall set against thee buckler and shield and helmet round about: and I will set judgment before them, and they shall judge thee according to their judgments.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And I will set my jealousy against thee, and they shall deal furiously with thee: they shall take away thy nose and thine ears; and thy remnant shall fall by the sword: they shall take thy sons and thy daughters; and thy residue shall be devoured by the fire.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>They shall also strip thee out of thy clothes, and take away thy fair jewels.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Thus will I make thy lewdness to cease from thee, and thy whoredom _brought_ from the land of Egypt: so that thou shalt not lift up thine eyes unto them, nor remember Egypt any more.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will deliver thee into the hand _of them_ whom thou hatest, into the hand _of them_ from whom thy mind is alienated:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And they shall deal with thee hatefully, and shall take away all thy labour, and shall leave thee naked and bare: and the nakedness of thy whoredoms shall be discovered, both thy lewdness and thy whoredoms.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>I will do these _things_ unto thee, because thou hast gone a whoring after the heathen, _and_ because thou art polluted with their idols.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Thou hast walked in the way of thy sister; therefore will I give her cup into thine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Thou shalt drink of thy sister’s cup deep and large: thou shalt be laughed to scorn and had in derision; it containeth much.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Thou shalt be filled with drunkenness and sorrow, with the cup of astonishment and desolation, with the cup of thy sister Samaria.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Thou shalt even drink it and suck _it_ out, and thou shalt break the sherds thereof, and pluck off thine own breasts: for I have spoken _it_, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because thou hast forgotten me, and cast me behind thy back, therefore bear thou also thy lewdness and thy whoredoms.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said moreover unto me; Son of man, wilt thou judge Aholah and Aholibah? yea, declare unto them their abominations;  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>That they have committed adultery, and blood _is_ in their hands, and with their idols have they committed adultery, and have also caused their sons, whom they bare unto me, to pass for them through _the fire_, to devour _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>Moreover this they have done unto me: they have defiled my sanctuary in the same day, and have profaned my sabbaths.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>For when they had slain their children to their idols, then they came the same day into my sanctuary to profane it; and, lo, thus have they done in the midst of mine house.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And furthermore, that ye have sent for men to come from far, unto whom a messenger _was_ sent; and, lo, they came: for whom thou didst wash thyself, paintedst thy eyes, and deckedst thyself with ornaments,  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And satest upon a stately bed, and a table prepared before it, whereupon thou hast set mine incense and mine oil.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And a voice of a multitude being at ease _was_ with her: and with the men of the common sort _were_ brought Sabeans from the wilderness, which put bracelets upon their hands, and beautiful crowns upon their heads.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>Then said I unto _her that was_ old in adulteries, Will they now commit whoredoms with her, and she _with them_?  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>Yet they went in unto her, as they go in unto a woman that playeth the harlot: so went they in unto Aholah and unto Aholibah, the lewd women.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>¶ And the righteous men, they shall judge them after the manner of adulteresses, and after the manner of women that shed blood; because they _are_ adulteresses, and blood _is_ in their hands.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will bring up a company upon them, and will give them to be removed and spoiled.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And the company shall stone them with stones, and dispatch them with their swords; they shall slay their sons and their daughters, and burn up their houses with fire.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>Thus will I cause lewdness to cease out of the land, that all women may be taught not to do after your lewdness.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>And they shall recompense your lewdness upon you, and ye shall bear the sins of your idols: and ye shall know that I _am_ the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 24.

Again in the ninth year, in the tenth month, in the tenth _day_ of the month, the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, write thee the name of the day, _even_ of this same day: the king of Babylon set himself against Jerusalem this same day.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And utter a parable unto the rebellious house, and say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Set on a pot, set _it_ on, and also pour water into it:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Gather the pieces thereof into it, _even_ every good piece, the thigh, and the shoulder; fill _it_ with the choice bones.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Take the choice of the flock, and burn also the bones under it, _and_ make it boil well, and let them seethe the bones of it therein.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Wherefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Woe to the bloody city, to the pot whose scum _is_ therein, and whose scum is not gone out of it! bring it out piece by piece; let no lot fall upon it.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For her blood is in the midst of her; she set it upon the top of a rock; she poured it not upon the ground, to cover it with dust;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>That it might cause fury to come up to take vengeance; I have set her blood upon the top of a rock, that it should not be covered.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Woe to the bloody city! I will even make the pile for fire great.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Heap on wood, kindle the fire, consume the flesh, and spice it well, and let the bones be burned.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then set it empty upon the coals thereof, that the brass of it may be hot, and may burn, and _that_ the filthiness of it may be molten in it, _that_ the scum of it may be consumed.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>She hath wearied _herself_ with lies, and her great scum went not forth out of her: her scum _shall be_ in the fire.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>In thy filthiness _is_ lewdness: because I have purged thee, and thou wast not purged, thou shalt not be purged from thy filthiness any more, till I have caused my fury to rest upon thee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_: it shall come to pass, and I will do _it_; I will not go back, neither will I spare, neither will I repent; according to thy ways, and according to thy doings, shall they judge thee, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Also the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Son of man, behold, I take away from thee the desire of thine eyes with a stroke: yet neither shalt thou mourn nor weep, neither shall thy tears run down.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Forbear to cry, make no mourning for the dead, bind the tire of thine head upon thee, and put on thy shoes upon thy feet, and cover not _thy_ lips, and eat not the bread of men.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>So I spake unto the people in the morning: and at even my wife died; and I did in the morning as I was commanded.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And the people said unto me, Wilt thou not tell us what these _things are_ to us, that thou doest _so_?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Then I answered them, The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Speak unto the house of Israel, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will profane my sanctuary, the excellency of your strength, the desire of your eyes, and that which your soul pitieth; and your sons and your daughters whom ye have left shall fall by the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And ye shall do as I have done: ye shall not cover _your_ lips, nor eat the bread of men.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And your tires _shall be_ upon your heads, and your shoes upon your feet: ye shall not mourn nor weep; but ye shall pine away for your iniquities, and mourn one toward another.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Thus Ezekiel is unto you a sign: according to all that he hath done shall ye do: and when this cometh, ye shall know that I _am_ the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Also, thou son of man, _shall it_ not _be_ in the day when I take from them their strength, the joy of their glory, the desire of their eyes, and that whereupon they set their minds, their sons and their daughters,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>_That_ he that escapeth in that day shall come unto thee, to cause _thee_ to hear _it_ with _thine_ ears?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>In that day shall thy mouth be opened to him which is escaped, and thou shalt speak, and be no more dumb: and thou shalt be a sign unto them; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 25.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came again unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, set thy face against the Ammonites, and prophesy against them;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And say unto the Ammonites, Hear the word of the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because thou saidst, Aha, against my sanctuary, when it was profaned; and against the land of Israel, when it was desolate; and against the house of Judah, when they went into captivity;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Behold, therefore I will deliver thee to the men of the east for a possession, and they shall set their palaces in thee, and make their dwellings in thee: they shall eat thy fruit, and they shall drink thy milk.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And I will make Rabbah a stable for camels, and the Ammonites a couchingplace for flocks: and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because thou hast clapped _thine_ hands, and stamped with the feet, and rejoiced in heart with all thy despite against the land of Israel;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Behold, therefore I will stretch out mine hand upon thee, and will deliver thee for a spoil to the heathen; and I will cut thee off from the people, and I will cause thee to perish out of the countries: I will destroy thee; and thou shalt know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because that Moab and Seir do say, Behold, the house of Judah _is_ like unto all the heathen;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Therefore, behold, I will open the side of Moab from the cities, from his cities _which are_ on his frontiers, the glory of the country, Beth–jeshimoth, Baal–meon, and Kiriathaim,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Unto the men of the east with the Ammonites, and will give them in possession, that the Ammonites may not be remembered among the nations.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And I will execute judgments upon Moab; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because that Edom hath dealt against the house of Judah by taking vengeance, and hath greatly offended, and revenged himself upon them;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will also stretch out mine hand upon Edom, and will cut off man and beast from it; and I will make it desolate from Teman; and they of Dedan shall fall by the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And I will lay my vengeance upon Edom by the hand of my people Israel: and they shall do in Edom according to mine anger and according to my fury; and they shall know my vengeance, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because the Philistines have dealt by revenge, and have taken vengeance with a despiteful heart, to destroy _it_ for the old hatred;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will stretch out mine hand upon the Philistines, and I will cut off the Cherethims, and destroy the remnant of the sea coast.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And I will execute great vengeance upon them with furious rebukes; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I shall lay my vengeance upon them.  

## CHAPTER 26.

And it came to pass in the eleventh year, in the first _day_ of the month, _that_ the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, because that Tyrus hath said against Jerusalem, Aha, she is broken _that was_ the gates of the people: she is turned unto me: I shall be replenished, _now_ she is laid waste:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I _am_ against thee, O Tyrus, and will cause many nations to come up against thee, as the sea causeth his waves to come up.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And they shall destroy the walls of Tyrus, and break down her towers: I will also scrape her dust from her, and make her like the top of a rock.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>It shall be _a place for_ the spreading of nets in the midst of the sea: for I have spoken _it_, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>: and it shall become a spoil to the nations.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And her daughters which _are_ in the field shall be slain by the sword; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will bring upon Tyrus Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon, a king of kings, from the north, with horses, and with chariots, and with horsemen, and companies, and much people.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He shall slay with the sword thy daughters in the field: and he shall make a fort against thee, and cast a mount against thee, and lift up the buckler against thee.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he shall set engines of war against thy walls, and with his axes he shall break down thy towers.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>By reason of the abundance of his horses their dust shall cover thee: thy walls shall shake at the noise of the horsemen, and of the wheels, and of the chariots, when he shall enter into thy gates, as men enter into a city wherein is made a breach.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>With the hoofs of his horses shall he tread down all thy streets: he shall slay thy people by the sword, and thy strong garrisons shall go down to the ground.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And they shall make a spoil of thy riches, and make a prey of thy merchandise: and they shall break down thy walls, and destroy thy pleasant houses: and they shall lay thy stones and thy timber and thy dust in the midst of the water.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And I will cause the noise of thy songs to cease; and the sound of thy harps shall be no more heard.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And I will make thee like the top of a rock: thou shalt be _a place_ to spread nets upon; thou shalt be built no more: for I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> to Tyrus; Shall not the isles shake at the sound of thy fall, when the wounded cry, when the slaughter is made in the midst of thee?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Then all the princes of the sea shall come down from their thrones, and lay away their robes, and put off their broidered garments: they shall clothe themselves with trembling; they shall sit upon the ground, and shall tremble at _every_ moment, and be astonished at thee.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And they shall take up a lamentation for thee, and say to thee, How art thou destroyed, _that wast_ inhabited of seafaring men, the renowned city, which wast strong in the sea, she and her inhabitants, which cause their terror _to be_ on all that haunt it!  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Now shall the isles tremble in the day of thy fall; yea, the isles that _are_ in the sea shall be troubled at thy departure.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; When I shall make thee a desolate city, like the cities that are not inhabited; when I shall bring up the deep upon thee, and great waters shall cover thee;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>When I shall bring thee down with them that descend into the pit, with the people of old time, and shall set thee in the low parts of the earth, in places desolate of old, with them that go down to the pit, that thou be not inhabited; and I shall set glory in the land of the living;  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>I will make thee a terror, and thou _shalt be_ no _more_: though thou be sought for, yet shalt thou never be found again, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 27.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came again unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Now, thou son of man, take up a lamentation for Tyrus;

<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Thy riches, and thy fairs, thy merchandise, thy mariners, and thy pilots, thy calkers, and the occupiers of thy merchandise, and all thy men of war, that _are_ in thee, and in all thy company which _is_ in the midst of thee, shall fall into the midst of the seas in the day of thy ruin.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>The suburbs shall shake at the sound of the cry of thy pilots.

<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, say unto the prince of Tyrus, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because thine heart _is_ lifted up, and thou hast said, I _am_ a God, I sit _in_ the seat of God, in the midst of the seas; yet thou _art_ a man, and not God, though thou set thine heart as the heart of God:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Behold, thou _art_ wiser than Daniel; there is no secret that they can hide from thee:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>With thy wisdom and with thine understanding thou hast gotten thee riches, and hast gotten gold and silver into thy treasures:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>By thy great wisdom _and_ by thy traffick hast thou increased thy riches, and thine heart is lifted up because of thy riches:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because thou hast set thine heart as the heart of God;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Behold, therefore I will bring strangers upon thee, the terrible of the nations: and they shall draw their swords against the beauty of thy wisdom, and they shall defile thy brightness.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>They shall bring thee down to the pit, and thou shalt die the deaths of _them that are_ slain in the midst of the seas.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Wilt thou yet say before him that slayeth thee, I _am_ God? but thou _shalt be_ a man, and no God, in the hand of him that slayeth thee.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thou shalt die the deaths of the uncircumcised by the hand of strangers: for I have spoken _it_, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Moreover the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Son of man, take up a lamentation upon the king of Tyrus, and say unto him, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Thou sealest up the sum, full of wisdom, and perfect in beauty.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thou hast been in Eden the garden of God; every precious stone _was_ thy covering, the sardius, topaz, and the diamond, the beryl, the onyx, and the jasper, the sapphire, the emerald, and the carbuncle, and gold: the workmanship of thy tabrets and of thy pipes was prepared in thee in the day that thou wast created.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thou _art_ the anointed cherub that covereth; and I have set thee _so_: thou wast upon the holy mountain of God; thou hast walked up and down in the midst of the stones of fire.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thou _wast_ perfect in thy ways from the day that thou wast created, till iniquity was found in thee.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>By the multitude of thy merchandise they have filled the midst of thee with violence, and thou hast sinned: therefore I will cast thee as profane out of the mountain of God: and I will destroy thee, O covering cherub, from the midst of the stones of fire.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thine heart was lifted up because of thy beauty, thou hast corrupted thy wisdom by reason of thy brightness: I will cast thee to the ground, I will lay thee before kings, that they may behold thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Thou hast defiled thy sanctuaries by the multitude of thine iniquities, by the iniquity of thy traffick; therefore will I bring forth a fire from the midst of thee, it shall devour thee, and I will bring thee to ashes upon the earth in the sight of all them that behold thee.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>All they that know thee among the people shall be astonished at thee: thou shalt be a terror, and never _shalt_ thou _be_ any more.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Again the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Son of man, set thy face against Zidon, and prophesy against it,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I _am_ against thee, O Zidon; and I will be glorified in the midst of thee: and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I shall have executed judgments in her, and shall be sanctified in her.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For I will send into her pestilence, and blood into her streets; and the wounded shall be judged in the midst of her by the sword upon her on every side; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And there shall be no more a pricking brier unto the house of Israel, nor _any_ grieving thorn of all _that are_ round about them, that despised them; and they shall know that I _am_ the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; When I shall have gathered the house of Israel from the people among whom they are scattered, and shall be sanctified in them in the sight of the heathen, then shall they dwell in their land that I have given to my servant Jacob.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And they shall dwell safely therein, and shall build houses, and plant vineyards; yea, they shall dwell with confidence, when I have executed judgments upon all those that despise them round about them; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God.  

## CHAPTER 29.

In the tenth year, in the tenth _month_, in the twelfth _day_ of the month, the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, set thy face against Pharaoh king of Egypt, and prophesy against him, and against all Egypt:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Speak, and say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I _am_ against thee, Pharaoh king of Egypt, the great dragon that lieth in the midst of his rivers, which hath said, My river _is_ mine own, and I have made _it_ for myself.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But I will put hooks in thy jaws, and I will cause the fish of thy rivers to stick unto thy scales, and I will bring thee up out of the midst of thy rivers, and all the fish of thy rivers shall stick unto thy scales.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And I will leave thee _thrown_ into the wilderness, thee and all the fish of thy rivers: thou shalt fall upon the open fields; thou shalt not be brought together, nor gathered: I have given thee for meat to the beasts of the field and to the fowls of the heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And all the inhabitants of Egypt shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, because they have been a staff of reed to the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>When they took hold of thee by thy hand, thou didst break, and rend all their shoulder: and when they leaned upon thee, thou brakest, and madest all their loins to be at a stand.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will bring a sword upon thee, and cut off man and beast out of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the land of Egypt shall be desolate and waste; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: because he hath said, The river _is_ mine, and I have made _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Behold, therefore I _am_ against thee, and against thy rivers, and I will make the land of Egypt utterly waste _and_ desolate, from the tower of Syene even unto the border of Ethiopia.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>No foot of man shall pass through it, nor foot of beast shall pass through it, neither shall it be inhabited forty years.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And I will make the land of Egypt desolate in the midst of the countries _that are_ desolate, and her cities among the cities _that are_ laid waste shall be desolate forty years: and I will scatter the Egyptians among the nations, and will disperse them through the countries.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Yet thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; At the end of forty years will I gather the Egyptians from the people whither they were scattered:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And I will bring again the captivity of Egypt, and will cause them to return _into_ the land of Pathros, into the land of their habitation; and they shall be there a base kingdom.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>It shall be the basest of the kingdoms; neither shall it exalt itself any more above the nations: for I will diminish them, that they shall no more rule over the nations.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And it shall be no more the confidence of the house of Israel, which bringeth _their_ iniquity to remembrance, when they shall look after them: but they shall know that I _am_ the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass in the seven and twentieth year, in the first _month_, in the first _day_ of the month, the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Son of man, Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon caused his army to serve a great service against Tyrus: every head _was_ made bald, and every shoulder _was_ peeled: yet had he no wages, nor his army, for Tyrus, for the service that he had served against it:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will give the land of Egypt unto Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon; and he shall take her multitude, and take her spoil, and take her prey; and it shall be the wages for his army.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>I have given him the land of Egypt _for_ his labour wherewith he served against it, because they wrought for me, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ In that day will I cause the horn of the house of Israel to bud forth, and I will give thee the opening of the mouth in the midst of them; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 30.

The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came again unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, prophesy and say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Howl ye, Woe worth the day!  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For the day _is_ near, even the day of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ near, a cloudy day; it shall be the time of the heathen.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the sword shall come upon Egypt, and great pain shall be in Ethiopia, when the slain shall fall in Egypt, and they shall take away her multitude, and her foundations shall be broken down.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Ethiopia, and Libya, and Lydia, and all the mingled people, and Chub, and the men of the land that is in league, shall fall with them by the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; They also that uphold Egypt shall fall; and the pride of her power shall come down: from the tower of Syene shall they fall in it by the sword, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they shall be desolate in the midst of the countries _that are_ desolate, and her cities shall be in the midst of the cities _that are_ wasted.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I have set a fire in Egypt, and _when_ all her helpers shall be destroyed.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>In that day shall messengers go forth from me in ships to make the careless Ethiopians afraid, and great pain shall come upon them, as in the day of Egypt: for, lo, it cometh.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will also make the multitude of Egypt to cease by the hand of Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He and his people with him, the terrible of the nations, shall be brought to destroy the land: and they shall draw their swords against Egypt, and fill the land with the slain.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And I will make the rivers dry, and sell the land into the hand of the wicked: and I will make the land waste, and all that is therein, by the hand of strangers: I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will also destroy the idols, and I will cause _their_ images to cease out of Noph; and there shall be no more a prince of the land of Egypt: and I will put a fear in the land of Egypt.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And I will make Pathros desolate, and will set fire in Zoan, and will execute judgments in No.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And I will pour my fury upon Sin, the strength of Egypt; and I will cut off the multitude of No.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And I will set fire in Egypt: Sin shall have great pain, and No shall be rent asunder, and Noph _shall have_ distresses daily.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>The young men of Aven and of Pi–beseth shall fall by the sword: and these _cities_ shall go into captivity.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>At Tehaphnehes also the day shall be darkened, when I shall break there the yokes of Egypt: and the pomp of her strength shall cease in her: as for her, a cloud shall cover her, and her daughters shall go into captivity.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Thus will I execute judgments in Egypt: and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass in the eleventh year, in the first _month_, in the seventh _day_ of the month, _that_ the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Son of man, I have broken the arm of Pharaoh king of Egypt; and, lo, it shall not be bound up to be healed, to put a roller to bind it, to make it strong to hold the sword.

<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And I will scatter the Egyptians among the nations, and will disperse them through the countries.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And I will strengthen the arms of the king of Babylon, and put my sword in his hand: but I will break Pharaoh’s arms, and he shall groan before him with the groanings of a deadly wounded _man_.

<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And I will scatter the Egyptians among the nations, and disperse them among the countries; and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 31.

And it came to pass in the eleventh year, in the third _month_, in the first _day_ of the month, _that_ the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, speak unto Pharaoh king of Egypt, and to his multitude; Whom art thou like in thy greatness?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, the Assyrian _was_ a cedar in Lebanon with fair branches, and with a shadowing shroud, and of an high stature; and his top was among the thick boughs.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The waters made him great, the deep set him up on high with her rivers running round about his plants, and sent out her little rivers unto all the trees of the field.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Therefore his height was exalted above all the trees of the field, and his boughs were multiplied, and his branches became long because of the multitude of waters, when he shot forth.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>All the fowls of heaven made their nests in his boughs, and under his branches did all the beasts of the field bring forth their young, and under his shadow dwelt all great nations.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Thus was he fair in his greatness, in the length of his branches: for his root was by great waters.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The cedars in the garden of God could not hide him: the fir trees were not like his boughs, and the chesnut trees were not like his branches; nor any tree in the garden of God was like unto him in his beauty.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>I have made him fair by the multitude of his branches: so that all the trees of Eden, that _were_ in the garden of God, envied him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because thou hast lifted up thyself in height, and he hath shot up his top among the thick boughs, and his heart is lifted up in his height;  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I have therefore delivered him into the hand of the mighty one of the heathen; he shall surely deal with him: I have driven him out for his wickedness.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And strangers, the terrible of the nations, have cut him off, and have left him: upon the mountains and in all the valleys his branches are fallen, and his boughs are broken by all the rivers of the land; and all the people of the earth are gone down from his shadow, and have left him.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Upon his ruin shall all the fowls of the heaven remain, and all the beasts of the field shall be upon his branches:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>To the end that none of all the trees by the waters exalt themselves for their height, neither shoot up their top among the thick boughs, neither their trees stand up in their height, all that drink water: for they are all delivered unto death, to the nether parts of the earth, in the midst of the children of men, with them that go down to the pit.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; In the day when he went down to the grave I caused a mourning: I covered the deep for him, and I restrained the floods thereof, and the great waters were stayed: and I caused Lebanon to mourn for him, and all the trees of the field fainted for him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>I made the nations to shake at the sound of his fall, when I cast him down to hell with them that descend into the pit: and all the trees of Eden, the choice and best of Lebanon, all that drink water, shall be comforted in the nether parts of the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>They also went down into hell with him unto _them that be_ slain with the sword; and _they that were_ his arm, _that_ dwelt under his shadow in the midst of the heathen.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ To whom art thou thus like in glory and in greatness among the trees of Eden? yet shalt thou be brought down with the trees of Eden unto the nether parts of the earth: thou shalt lie in the midst of the uncircumcised with _them that be_ slain by the sword. This _is_ Pharaoh and all his multitude, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 32.

And it came to pass in the twelfth year, in the twelfth month, in the first _day_ of the month, _that_ the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, take up a lamentation for Pharaoh king of Egypt, and say unto him, Thou art like a young lion of the nations, and thou _art_ as a whale in the seas: and thou camest forth with thy rivers, and troubledst the waters with thy feet, and fouledst their rivers.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will therefore spread out my net over thee with a company of many people; and they shall bring thee up in my net.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then will I leave thee upon the land, I will cast thee forth upon the open field, and will cause all the fowls of the heaven to remain upon thee, and I will fill the beasts of the whole earth with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And I will lay thy flesh upon the mountains, and fill the valleys with thy height.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>I will also water with thy blood the land wherein thou swimmest, _even_ to the mountains; and the rivers shall be full of thee.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And when I shall put thee out, I will cover the heaven, and make the stars thereof dark; I will cover the sun with a cloud, and the moon shall not give her light.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>All the bright lights of heaven will I make dark over thee, and set darkness upon thy land, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>I will also vex the hearts of many people, when I shall bring thy destruction among the nations, into the countries which thou hast not known.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Yea, I will make many people amazed at thee, and their kings shall be horribly afraid for thee, when I shall brandish my sword before them; and they shall tremble at _every_ moment, every man for his own life, in the day of thy fall.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; The sword of the king of Babylon shall come upon thee.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>By the swords of the mighty will I cause thy multitude to fall, the terrible of the nations, all of them: and they shall spoil the pomp of Egypt, and all the multitude thereof shall be destroyed.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>I will destroy also all the beasts thereof from beside the great waters; neither shall the foot of man trouble them any more, nor the hoofs of beasts trouble them.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Then will I make their waters deep, and cause their rivers to run like oil, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>When I shall make the land of Egypt desolate, and the country shall be destitute of that whereof it was full, when I shall smite all them that dwell therein, then shall they know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>This _is_ the lamentation wherewith they shall lament her: the daughters of the nations shall lament her: they shall lament for her, _even_ for Egypt, and for all her multitude, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ It came to pass also in the twelfth year, in the fifteenth _day_ of the month, _that_ the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Son of man, wail for the multitude of Egypt, and cast them down, _even_ her, and the daughters of the famous nations, unto the nether parts of the earth, with them that go down into the pit.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Whom dost thou pass in beauty? go down, and be thou laid with the uncircumcised.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>They shall fall in the midst of _them that are_ slain by the sword: she is delivered to the sword: draw her and all her multitudes.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>The strong among the mighty shall speak to him out of the midst of hell with them that help him: they are gone down, they lie uncircumcised, slain by the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Asshur _is_ there and all her company: his graves _are_ about him: all of them slain, fallen by the sword:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Whose graves are set in the sides of the pit, and her company is round about her grave: all of them slain, fallen by the sword, which caused terror in the land of the living.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>There _is_ Elam and all her multitude round about her grave, all of them slain, fallen by the sword, which are gone down uncircumcised into the nether parts of the earth, which caused their terror in the land of the living; yet have they borne their shame with them that go down to the pit.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>They have set her a bed in the midst of the slain with all her multitude: her graves _are_ round about him: all of them uncircumcised, slain by the sword: though their terror was caused in the land of the living, yet have they borne their shame with them that go down to the pit: he is put in the midst of _them that be_ slain.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>There _is_ Meshech, Tubal, and all her multitude: her graves _are_ round about him: all of them uncircumcised, slain by the sword, though they caused their terror in the land of the living.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And they shall not lie with the mighty _that are_ fallen of the uncircumcised, which are gone down to hell with their weapons of war: and they have laid their swords under their heads, but their iniquities shall be upon their bones, though _they were_ the terror of the mighty in the land of the living.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Yea, thou shalt be broken in the midst of the uncircumcised, and shalt lie with _them that are_ slain with the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>There _is_ Edom, her kings, and all her princes, which with their might are laid by _them that were_ slain by the sword: they shall lie with the uncircumcised, and with them that go down to the pit.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>There _be_ the princes of the north, all of them, and all the Zidonians, which are gone down with the slain; with their terror they are ashamed of their might; and they lie uncircumcised with _them that be_ slain by the sword, and bear their shame with them that go down to the pit.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Pharaoh shall see them, and shall be comforted over all his multitude, _even_ Pharaoh and all his army slain by the sword, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For I have caused my terror in the land of the living: and he shall be laid in the midst of the uncircumcised with _them that are_ slain with the sword, _even_ Pharaoh and all his multitude, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 33.

Again the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, speak to the children of thy people, and say unto them, When I bring the sword upon a land, if the people of the land take a man of their coasts, and set him for their watchman:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>If when he seeth the sword come upon the land, he blow the trumpet, and warn the people;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then whosoever heareth the sound of the trumpet, and taketh not warning; if the sword come, and take him away, his blood shall be upon his own head.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>He heard the sound of the trumpet, and took not warning; his blood shall be upon him. But he that taketh warning shall deliver his soul.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But if the watchman see the sword come, and blow not the trumpet, and the people be not warned; if the sword come, and take _any_ person from among them, he is taken away in his iniquity; but his blood will I require at the watchman’s hand.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ So thou, O son of man, I have set thee a watchman unto the house of Israel; therefore thou shalt hear the word at my mouth, and warn them from me.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>When I say unto the wicked, O wicked _man_, thou shalt surely die; if thou dost not speak to warn the wicked from his way, that wicked _man_ shall die in his iniquity; but his blood will I require at thine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Nevertheless, if thou warn the wicked of his way to turn from it; if he do not turn from his way, he shall die in his iniquity; but thou hast delivered thy soul.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore, O thou son of man, speak unto the house of Israel; Thus ye speak, saying, If our transgressions and our sins _be_ upon us, and we pine away in them, how should we then live?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Say unto them, _As_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked; but that the wicked turn from his way and live: turn ye, turn ye from your evil ways; for why will ye die, O house of Israel?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Therefore, thou son of man, say unto the children of thy people, The righteousness of the righteous shall not deliver him in the day of his transgression: as for the wickedness of the wicked, he shall not fall thereby in the day that he turneth from his wickedness; neither shall the righteous be able to live for his _righteousness_ in the day that he sinneth.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>When I shall say to the righteous, _that_ he shall surely live; if he trust to his own righteousness, and commit iniquity, all his righteousnesses shall not be remembered; but for his iniquity that he hath committed, he shall die for it.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Again, when I say unto the wicked, Thou shalt surely die; if he turn from his sin, and do that which is lawful and right;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>_If_ the wicked restore the pledge, give again that he had robbed, walk in the statutes of life, without committing iniquity; he shall surely live, he shall not die.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>None of his sins that he hath committed shall be mentioned unto him: he hath done that which is lawful and right; he shall surely live.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ Yet the children of thy people say, The way of the Lord is not equal: but as for them, their way is not equal.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>When the righteous turneth from his righteousness, and committeth iniquity, he shall even die thereby.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But if the wicked turn from his wickedness, and do that which is lawful and right, he shall live thereby.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Yet ye say, The way of the Lord is not equal. O ye house of Israel, I will judge you every one after his ways.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass in the twelfth year of our captivity, in the tenth _month_, in the fifth _day_ of the month, _that_ one that had escaped out of Jerusalem came unto me, saying, The city is smitten.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Now the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was upon me in the evening, afore he that was escaped came; and had opened my mouth, until he came to me in the morning; and my mouth was opened, and I was no more dumb.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Then the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Son of man, they that inhabit those wastes of the land of Israel speak, saying, Abraham was one, and he inherited the land: but we _are_ many; the land is given us for inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Wherefore say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Ye eat with the blood, and lift up your eyes toward your idols, and shed blood: and shall ye possess the land?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Ye stand upon your sword, ye work abomination, and ye defile every one his neighbour’s wife: and shall ye possess the land?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Say thou thus unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; _As_ I live, surely they that _are_ in the wastes shall fall by the sword, and him that _is_ in the open field will I give to the beasts to be devoured, and they that _be_ in the forts and in the caves shall die of the pestilence.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For I will lay the land most desolate, and the pomp of her strength shall cease; and the mountains of Israel shall be desolate, that none shall pass through.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Then shall they know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I have laid the land most desolate because of all their abominations which they have committed.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ Also, thou son of man, the children of thy people still are talking against thee by the walls and in the doors of the houses, and speak one to another, every one to his brother, saying, Come, I pray you, and hear what is the word that cometh forth from the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And they come unto thee as the people cometh, and they sit before thee _as_ my people, and they hear thy words, but they will not do them: for with their mouth they shew much love, _but_ their heart goeth after their covetousness.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And, lo, thou _art_ unto them as a very lovely song of one that hath a pleasant voice, and can play well on an instrument: for they hear thy words, but they do them not.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And when this cometh to pass, (lo, it will come,) then shall they know that a prophet hath been among them.  

## CHAPTER 34.

And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, prophesy against the shepherds of Israel, prophesy, and say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> unto the shepherds; Woe _be_ to the shepherds of Israel that do feed themselves! should not the shepherds feed the flocks?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Ye eat the fat, and ye clothe you with the wool, ye kill them that are fed: _but_ ye feed not the flock.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The diseased have ye not strengthened, neither have ye healed that which was sick, neither have ye bound up _that which was_ broken, neither have ye brought again that which was driven away, neither have ye sought that which was lost; but with force and with cruelty have ye ruled them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they were scattered, because _there is_ no shepherd: and they became meat to all the beasts of the field, when they were scattered.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>My sheep wandered through all the mountains, and upon every high hill: yea, my flock was scattered upon all the face of the earth, and none did search or seek _after them_.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore, ye shepherds, hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>_As_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, surely because my flock became a prey, and my flock became meat to every beast of the field, because _there was_ no shepherd, neither did my shepherds search for my flock, but the shepherds fed themselves, and fed not my flock;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Therefore, O ye shepherds, hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I _am_ against the shepherds; and I will require my flock at their hand, and cause them to cease from feeding the flock; neither shall the shepherds feed themselves any more; for I will deliver my flock from their mouth, that they may not be meat for them.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ For thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I, _even_ I, will both search my sheep, and seek them out.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>As a shepherd seeketh out his flock in the day that he is among his sheep _that are_ scattered; so will I seek out my sheep, and will deliver them out of all places where they have been scattered in the cloudy and dark day.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And I will bring them out from the people, and gather them from the countries, and will bring them to their own land, and feed them upon the mountains of Israel by the rivers, and in all the inhabited places of the country.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I will feed them in a good pasture, and upon the high mountains of Israel shall their fold be: there shall they lie in a good fold, and _in_ a fat pasture shall they feed upon the mountains of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>I will feed my flock, and I will cause them to lie down, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>I will seek that which was lost, and bring again that which was driven away, and will bind up _that which was_ broken, and will strengthen that which was sick: but I will destroy the fat and the strong; I will feed them with judgment.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And _as for_ you, O my flock, thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I judge between cattle and cattle, between the rams and the he goats.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>_Seemeth it_ a small thing unto you to have eaten up the good pasture, but ye must tread down with your feet the residue of your pastures? and to have drunk of the deep waters, but ye must foul the residue with your feet?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And _as for_ my flock, they eat that which ye have trodden with your feet; and they drink that which ye have fouled with your feet.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> unto them; Behold, I, _even_ I, will judge between the fat cattle and between the lean cattle.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Because ye have thrust with side and with shoulder, and pushed all the diseased with your horns, till ye have scattered them abroad;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Therefore will I save my flock, and they shall no more be a prey; and I will judge between cattle and cattle.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And I will set up one shepherd over them, and he shall feed them, _even_ my servant David; he shall feed them, and he shall be their shepherd.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will be their God, and my servant David a prince among them; I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And I will make with them a covenant of peace, and will cause the evil beasts to cease out of the land: and they shall dwell safely in the wilderness, and sleep in the woods.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And I will make them and the places round about my hill a blessing; and I will cause the shower to come down in his season; there shall be showers of blessing.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And the tree of the field shall yield her fruit, and the earth shall yield her increase, and they shall be safe in their land, and shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I have broken the bands of their yoke, and delivered them out of the hand of those that served themselves of them.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And they shall no more be a prey to the heathen, neither shall the beast of the land devour them; but they shall dwell safely, and none shall make _them_ afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And I will raise up for them a plant of renown, and they shall be no more consumed with hunger in the land, neither bear the shame of the heathen any more.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Thus shall they know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God _am_ with them, and _that_ they, _even_ the house of Israel, _are_ my people, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And ye my flock, the flock of my pasture, _are_ men, _and_ I _am_ your God, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 35.

Moreover the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, set thy face against mount Seir, and prophesy against it,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And say unto it, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, O mount Seir, I _am_ against thee, and I will stretch out mine hand against thee, and I will make thee most desolate.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I will lay thy cities waste, and thou shalt be desolate, and thou shalt know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Because thou hast had a perpetual hatred, and hast shed _the blood of_ the children of Israel by the force of the sword in the time of their calamity, in the time _that their_ iniquity _had_ an end:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Therefore, _as_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, I will prepare thee unto blood, and blood shall pursue thee: sith thou hast not hated blood, even blood shall pursue thee.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Thus will I make mount Seir most desolate, and cut off from it him that passeth out and him that returneth.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And I will fill his mountains with his slain _men_: in thy hills, and in thy valleys, and in all thy rivers, shall they fall that are slain with the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>I will make thee perpetual desolations, and thy cities shall not return: and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ Because thou hast said, These two nations and these two countries shall be mine, and we will possess it; whereas the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was there:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Therefore, _as_ I live, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, I will even do according to thine anger, and according to thine envy which thou hast used out of thy hatred against them; and I will make myself known among them, when I have judged thee.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And thou shalt know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _and that_ I have heard all thy blasphemies which thou hast spoken against the mountains of Israel, saying, They are laid desolate, they are given us to consume.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thus with your mouth ye have boasted against me, and have multiplied your words against me: I have heard _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; When the whole earth rejoiceth, I will make thee desolate.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>As thou didst rejoice at the inheritance of the house of Israel, because it was desolate, so will I do unto thee: thou shalt be desolate, O mount Seir, and all Idumea, _even_ all of it: and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 36.

Also, thou son of man, prophesy unto the mountains of Israel, and say, Ye mountains of Israel, hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because the enemy hath said against you, Aha, even the ancient high places are ours in possession:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore prophesy and say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because they have made _you_ desolate, and swallowed you up on every side, that ye might be a possession unto the residue of the heathen, and ye are taken up in the lips of talkers, and _are_ an infamy of the people:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Therefore, ye mountains of Israel, hear the word of the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> to the mountains, and to the hills, to the rivers, and to the valleys, to the desolate wastes, and to the cities that are forsaken, which became a prey and derision to the residue of the heathen that _are_ round about;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Surely in the fire of my jealousy have I spoken against the residue of the heathen, and against all Idumea, which have appointed my land into their possession with the joy of all _their_ heart, with despiteful minds, to cast it out for a prey.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Prophesy therefore concerning the land of Israel, and say unto the mountains, and to the hills, to the rivers, and to the valleys, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I have spoken in my jealousy and in my fury, because ye have borne the shame of the heathen:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I have lifted up mine hand, Surely the heathen that _are_ about you, they shall bear their shame.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ But ye, O mountains of Israel, ye shall shoot forth your branches, and yield your fruit to my people of Israel; for they are at hand to come.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For, behold, I _am_ for you, and I will turn unto you, and ye shall be tilled and sown:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And I will multiply men upon you, all the house of Israel, _even_ all of it: and the cities shall be inhabited, and the wastes shall be builded:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And I will multiply upon you man and beast; and they shall increase and bring fruit: and I will settle you after your old estates, and will do better _unto you_ than at your beginnings: and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Yea, I will cause men to walk upon you, _even_ my people Israel; and they shall possess thee, and thou shalt be their inheritance, and thou shalt no more henceforth bereave them _of men_.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Because they say unto you, Thou _land_ devourest up men, and hast bereaved thy nations;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Therefore thou shalt devour men no more, neither bereave thy nations any more, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Neither will I cause _men_ to hear in thee the shame of the heathen any more, neither shalt thou bear the reproach of the people any more, neither shalt thou cause thy nations to fall any more, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Moreover the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Son of man, when the house of Israel dwelt in their own land, they defiled it by their own way and by their doings: their way was before me as the uncleanness of a removed woman.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Wherefore I poured my fury upon them for the blood that they had shed upon the land, and for their idols _wherewith_ they had polluted it:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And I scattered them among the heathen, and they were dispersed through the countries: according to their way and according to their doings I judged them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And when they entered unto the heathen, whither they went, they profaned my holy name, when they said to them, These _are_ the people of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and are gone forth out of his land.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ But I had pity for mine holy name, which the house of Israel had profaned among the heathen, whither they went.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Therefore say unto the house of Israel, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I do not _this_ for your sakes, O house of Israel, but for mine holy name’s sake, which ye have profaned among the heathen, whither ye went.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And I will sanctify my great name, which was profaned among the heathen, which ye have profaned in the midst of them; and the heathen shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, when I shall be sanctified in you before their eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For I will take you from among the heathen, and gather you out of all countries, and will bring you into your own land.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ Then will I sprinkle clean water upon you, and ye shall be clean: from all your filthiness, and from all your idols, will I cleanse you.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>A new heart also will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh, and I will give you an heart of flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And I will put my spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes, and ye shall keep my judgments, and do _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And ye shall dwell in the land that I gave to your fathers; and ye shall be my people, and I will be your God.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>I will also save you from all your uncleannesses: and I will call for the corn, and will increase it, and lay no famine upon you.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And I will multiply the fruit of the tree, and the increase of the field, that ye shall receive no more reproach of famine among the heathen.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Then shall ye remember your own evil ways, and your doings that _were_ not good, and shall lothe yourselves in your own sight for your iniquities and for your abominations.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Not for your sakes do I _this_, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, be it known unto you: be ashamed and confounded for your own ways, O house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; In the day that I shall have cleansed you from all your iniquities I will also cause _you_ to dwell in the cities, and the wastes shall be builded.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And the desolate land shall be tilled, whereas it lay desolate in the sight of all that passed by.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And they shall say, This land that was desolate is become like the garden of Eden; and the waste and desolate and ruined cities _are become_ fenced, _and_ are inhabited.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Then the heathen that are left round about you shall know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> build the ruined _places, and_ plant that that was desolate: I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_, and I will do _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; I will yet _for_ this be enquired of by the house of Israel, to do _it_ for them; I will increase them with men like a flock.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>As the holy flock, as the flock of Jerusalem in her solemn feasts; so shall the waste cities be filled with flocks of men: and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 37.

The hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was upon me, and carried me out in the spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and set me down in the midst of the valley which _was_ full of bones,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And caused me to pass by them round about: and, behold, _there were_ very many in the open valley; and, lo, _they were_ very dry.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he said unto me, Son of man, can these bones live? And I answered, O Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, thou knowest.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Again he said unto me, Prophesy upon these bones, and say unto them, O ye dry bones, hear the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span> unto these bones; Behold, I will cause breath to enter into you, and ye shall live:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And I will lay sinews upon you, and will bring up flesh upon you, and cover you with skin, and put breath in you, and ye shall live; and ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>So I prophesied as I was commanded: and as I prophesied, there was a noise, and behold a shaking, and the bones came together, bone to his bone.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And when I beheld, lo, the sinews and the flesh came up upon them, and the skin covered them above: but _there was_ no breath in them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then said he unto me, Prophesy unto the wind, prophesy, son of man, and say to the wind, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Come from the four winds, O breath, and breathe upon these slain, that they may live.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>So I prophesied as he commanded me, and the breath came into them, and they lived, and stood up upon their feet, an exceeding great army.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Then he said unto me, Son of man, these bones are the whole house of Israel: behold, they say, Our bones are dried, and our hope is lost: we are cut off for our parts.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Therefore prophesy and say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, O my people, I will open your graves, and cause you to come up out of your graves, and bring you into the land of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And ye shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, when I have opened your graves, O my people, and brought you up out of your graves,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And shall put my spirit in you, and ye shall live, and I shall place you in your own land: then shall ye know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> have spoken _it_, and performed _it_, saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ The word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came again unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Moreover, thou son of man, take thee one stick, and write upon it, For Judah, and for the children of Israel his companions: then take another stick, and write upon it, For Joseph, the stick of Ephraim, and _for_ all the house of Israel his companions:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And join them one to another into one stick; and they shall become one in thine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And when the children of thy people shall speak unto thee, saying, Wilt thou not shew us what thou _meanest_ by these?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will take the stick of Joseph, which _is_ in the hand of Ephraim, and the tribes of Israel his fellows, and will put them with him, _even_ with the stick of Judah, and make them one stick, and they shall be one in mine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And the sticks whereon thou writest shall be in thine hand before their eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And say unto them, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I will take the children of Israel from among the heathen, whither they be gone, and will gather them on every side, and bring them into their own land:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And I will make them one nation in the land upon the mountains of Israel; and one king shall be king to them all: and they shall be no more two nations, neither shall they be divided into two kingdoms any more at all:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Neither shall they defile themselves any more with their idols, nor with their detestable things, nor with any of their transgressions: but I will save them out of all their dwellingplaces, wherein they have sinned, and will cleanse them: so shall they be my people, and I will be their God.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And David my servant _shall be_ king over them; and they all shall have one shepherd: they shall also walk in my judgments, and observe my statutes, and do them.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And they shall dwell in the land that I have given unto Jacob my servant, wherein your fathers have dwelt; and they shall dwell therein, _even_ they, and their children, and their children’s children for ever: and my servant David _shall be_ their prince for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Moreover I will make a covenant of peace with them; it shall be an everlasting covenant with them: and I will place them, and multiply them, and will set my sanctuary in the midst of them for evermore.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>My tabernacle also shall be with them: yea, I will be their God, and they shall be my people.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the heathen shall know that I the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> do sanctify Israel, when my sanctuary shall be in the midst of them for evermore.  

## CHAPTER 38.

And the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto me, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Son of man, set thy face against Gog, the land of Magog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal, and prophesy against him,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I _am_ against thee, O Gog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And I will turn thee back, and put hooks into thy jaws, and I will bring thee forth, and all thine army, horses and horsemen, all of them clothed with all sorts _of armour, even_ a great company _with_ bucklers and shields, all of them handling swords:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Persia, Ethiopia, and Libya with them; all of them with shield and helmet:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Gomer, and all his bands; the house of Togarmah of the north quarters, and all his bands: _and_ many people with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Be thou prepared, and prepare for thyself, thou, and all thy company that are assembled unto thee, and be thou a guard unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ After many days thou shalt be visited: in the latter years thou shalt come into the land _that is_ brought back from the sword, _and is_ gathered out of many people, against the mountains of Israel, which have been always waste: but it is brought forth out of the nations, and they shall dwell safely all of them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Thou shalt ascend and come like a storm, thou shalt be like a cloud to cover the land, thou, and all thy bands, and many people with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; It shall also come to pass, _that_ at the same time shall things come into thy mind, and thou shalt think an evil thought:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And thou shalt say, I will go up to the land of unwalled villages; I will go to them that are at rest, that dwell safely, all of them dwelling without walls, and having neither bars nor gates,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>To take a spoil, and to take a prey; to turn thine hand upon the desolate places _that are now_ inhabited, and upon the people _that are_ gathered out of the nations, which have gotten cattle and goods, that dwell in the midst of the land.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Sheba, and Dedan, and the merchants of Tarshish, with all the young lions thereof, shall say unto thee, Art thou come to take a spoil? hast thou gathered thy company to take a prey? to carry away silver and gold, to take away cattle and goods, to take a great spoil?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ Therefore, son of man, prophesy and say unto Gog, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; In that day when my people of Israel dwelleth safely, shalt thou not know _it_?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And thou shalt come from thy place out of the north parts, thou, and many people with thee, all of them riding upon horses, a great company, and a mighty army:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And thou shalt come up against my people of Israel, as a cloud to cover the land; it shall be in the latter days, and I will bring thee against my land, that the heathen may know me, when I shall be sanctified in thee, O Gog, before their eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; _Art_ thou he of whom I have spoken in old time by my servants the prophets of Israel, which prophesied in those days _many_ years that I would bring thee against them?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass at the same time when Gog shall come against the land of Israel, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, _that_ my fury shall come up in my face.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For in my jealousy _and_ in the fire of my wrath have I spoken, Surely in that day there shall be a great shaking in the land of Israel;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>So that the fishes of the sea, and the fowls of the heaven, and the beasts of the field, and all creeping things that creep upon the earth, and all the men that _are_ upon the face of the earth, shall shake at my presence, and the mountains shall be thrown down, and the steep places shall fall, and every wall shall fall to the ground.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And I will call for a sword against him throughout all my mountains, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>: every man’s sword shall be against his brother.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And I will plead against him with pestilence and with blood; and I will rain upon him, and upon his bands, and upon the many people that _are_ with him, an overflowing rain, and great hailstones, fire, and brimstone.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Thus will I magnify myself, and sanctify myself; and I will be known in the eyes of many nations, and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 39.

Therefore, thou son of man, prophesy against Gog, and say, Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Behold, I _am_ against thee, O Gog, the chief prince of Meshech and Tubal:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And I will turn thee back, and leave but the sixth part of thee, and will cause thee to come up from the north parts, and will bring thee upon the mountains of Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And I will smite thy bow out of thy left hand, and will cause thine arrows to fall out of thy right hand.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Thou shalt fall upon the mountains of Israel, thou, and all thy bands, and the people that _is_ with thee: I will give thee unto the ravenous birds of every sort, and _to_ the beasts of the field to be devoured.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Thou shalt fall upon the open field: for I have spoken _it_, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And I will send a fire on Magog, and among them that dwell carelessly in the isles: and they shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>So will I make my holy name known in the midst of my people Israel; and I will not _let them_ pollute my holy name any more: and the heathen shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, the Holy One in Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, it is come, and it is done, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; this _is_ the day whereof I have spoken.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And they that dwell in the cities of Israel shall go forth, and shall set on fire and burn the weapons, both the shields and the bucklers, the bows and the arrows, and the handstaves, and the spears, and they shall burn them with fire seven years:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>So that they shall take no wood out of the field, neither cut down _any_ out of the forests; for they shall burn the weapons with fire: and they shall spoil those that spoiled them, and rob those that robbed them, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass in that day, _that_ I will give unto Gog a place there of graves in Israel, the valley of the passengers on the east of the sea: and it shall stop the _noses_ of the passengers: and there shall they bury Gog and all his multitude: and they shall call _it_ The valley of Hamon–gog.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And seven months shall the house of Israel be burying of them, that they may cleanse the land.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Yea, all the people of the land shall bury _them_; and it shall be to them a renown the day that I shall be glorified, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they shall sever out men of continual employment, passing through the land to bury with the passengers those that remain upon the face of the earth, to cleanse it: after the end of seven months shall they search.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the passengers _that_ pass through the land, when _any_ seeth a man’s bone, then shall he set up a sign by it, till the buriers have buried it in the valley of Hamon–gog.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And also the name of the city _shall be_ Hamonah. Thus shall they cleanse the land.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And, thou son of man, thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Speak unto every feathered fowl, and to every beast of the field, Assemble yourselves, and come; gather yourselves on every side to my sacrifice that I do sacrifice for you, _even_ a great sacrifice upon the mountains of Israel, that ye may eat flesh, and drink blood.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Ye shall eat the flesh of the mighty, and drink the blood of the princes of the earth, of rams, of lambs, and of goats, of bullocks, all of them fatlings of Bashan.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And ye shall eat fat till ye be full, and drink blood till ye be drunken, of my sacrifice which I have sacrificed for you.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Thus ye shall be filled at my table with horses and chariots, with mighty men, and with all men of war, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And I will set my glory among the heathen, and all the heathen shall see my judgment that I have executed, and my hand that I have laid upon them.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>So the house of Israel shall know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God from that day and forward.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And the heathen shall know that the house of Israel went into captivity for their iniquity: because they trespassed against me, therefore hid I my face from them, and gave them into the hand of their enemies: so fell they all by the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>According to their uncleanness and according to their transgressions have I done unto them, and hid my face from them.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Therefore thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; Now will I bring again the captivity of Jacob, and have mercy upon the whole house of Israel, and will be jealous for my holy name;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>After that they have borne their shame, and all their trespasses whereby they have trespassed against me, when they dwelt safely in their land, and none made _them_ afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>When I have brought them again from the people, and gathered them out of their enemies’ lands, and am sanctified in them in the sight of many nations;  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Then shall they know that I _am_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> their God, which caused them to be led into captivity among the heathen: but I have gathered them unto their own land, and have left none of them any more there.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Neither will I hide my face any more from them: for I have poured out my spirit upon the house of Israel, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 40.

In the five and twentieth year of our captivity, in the beginning of the year, in the tenth _day_ of the month, in the fourteenth year after that the city was smitten, in the selfsame day the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was upon me, and brought me thither.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>In the visions of God brought he me into the land of Israel, and set me upon a very high mountain, by which _was_ as the frame of a city on the south.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he brought me thither, and, behold, _there was_ a man, whose appearance _was_ like the appearance of brass, with a line of flax in his hand, and a measuring reed; and he stood in the gate.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the man said unto me, Son of man, behold with thine eyes, and hear with thine ears, and set thine heart upon all that I shall shew thee; for to the intent that I might shew _them_ unto thee _art_ thou brought hither: declare all that thou seest to the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And behold a wall on the outside of the house round about, and in the man’s hand a measuring reed of six cubits _long_ by the cubit and an hand breadth: so he measured the breadth of the building, one reed; and the height, one reed.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Then came he unto the gate which looketh toward the east, and went up the stairs thereof, and measured the threshold of the gate, _which was_ one reed broad; and the other threshold _of the gate, which was_ one reed broad.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And _every_ little chamber _was_ one reed long, and one reed broad; and between the little chambers _were_ five cubits; and the threshold of the gate by the porch of the gate within _was_ one reed.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>He measured also the porch of the gate within, one reed.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then measured he the porch of the gate, eight cubits; and the posts thereof, two cubits; and the porch of the gate _was_ inward.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the little chambers of the gate eastward _were_ three on this side, and three on that side; they three _were_ of one measure: and the posts had one measure on this side and on that side.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And he measured the breadth of the entry of the gate, ten cubits; _and_ the length of the gate, thirteen cubits.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>The space also before the little chambers _was_ one cubit _on this side_, and the space _was_ one cubit on that side: and the little chambers _were_ six cubits on this side, and six cubits on that side.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>He measured then the gate from the roof of _one_ little chamber to the roof of another: the breadth _was_ five and twenty cubits, door against door.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>He made also posts of threescore cubits, even unto the post of the court round about the gate.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And from the face of the gate of the entrance unto the face of the porch of the inner gate _were_ fifty cubits.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And _there were_ narrow windows to the little chambers, and to their posts within the gate round about, and likewise to the arches: and windows _were_ round about inward: and upon _each_ post _were_ palm trees.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Then brought he me into the outward court, and, lo, _there were_ chambers, and a pavement made for the court round about: thirty chambers _were_ upon the pavement.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the pavement by the side of the gates over against the length of the gates _was_ the lower pavement.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Then he measured the breadth from the forefront of the lower gate unto the forefront of the inner court without, an hundred cubits eastward and northward.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And the gate of the outward court that looked toward the north, he measured the length thereof, and the breadth thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the little chambers thereof _were_ three on this side and three on that side; and the posts thereof and the arches thereof were after the measure of the first gate: the length thereof _was_ fifty cubits, and the breadth five and twenty cubits.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And their windows, and their arches, and their palm trees, _were_ after the measure of the gate that looketh toward the east; and they went up unto it by seven steps; and the arches thereof _were_ before them.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the gate of the inner court _was_ over against the gate toward the north, and toward the east; and he measured from gate to gate an hundred cubits.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ After that he brought me toward the south, and behold a gate toward the south: and he measured the posts thereof and the arches thereof according to these measures.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And _there were_ windows in it and in the arches thereof round about, like those windows: the length _was_ fifty cubits, and the breadth five and twenty cubits.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And _there were_ seven steps to go up to it, and the arches thereof _were_ before them: and it had palm trees, one on this side, and another on that side, upon the posts thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And _there was_ a gate in the inner court toward the south: and he measured from gate to gate toward the south an hundred cubits.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And he brought me to the inner court by the south gate: and he measured the south gate according to these measures;  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And the little chambers thereof, and the posts thereof, and the arches thereof, according to these measures: and _there were_ windows in it and in the arches thereof round about: _it was_ fifty cubits long, and five and twenty cubits broad.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the arches round about _were_ five and twenty cubits long, and five cubits broad.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the arches thereof _were_ toward the utter court; and palm trees _were_ upon the posts thereof: and the going up to it _had_ eight steps.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ And he brought me into the inner court toward the east: and he measured the gate according to these measures.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And the little chambers thereof, and the posts thereof, and the arches thereof, _were_ according to these measures: and _there were_ windows therein and in the arches thereof round about: _it was_ fifty cubits long, and five and twenty cubits broad.

<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The ephah and the bath shall be of one measure, that the bath may contain the tenth part of an homer, and the ephah the tenth part of an homer: the measure thereof shall be after the homer.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the shekel _shall be_ twenty gerahs: twenty shekels, five and twenty shekels, fifteen shekels, shall be your maneh.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ This _is_ the oblation that ye shall offer; the sixth part of an ephah of an homer of wheat, and ye shall give the sixth part of an ephah of an homer of barley:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Concerning the ordinance of oil, the bath of oil, _ye shall offer_ the tenth part of a bath out of the cor, _which is_ an homer of ten baths; for ten baths _are_ an homer:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And one lamb out of the flock, out of two hundred, out of the fat pastures of Israel; for a meat offering, and for a burnt offering, and for peace offerings, to make reconciliation for them, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>All the people of the land shall give this oblation for the prince in Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And it shall be the prince’s part _to give_ burnt offerings, and meat offerings, and drink offerings, in the feasts, and in the new moons, and in the sabbaths, in all solemnities of the house of Israel: he shall prepare the sin offering, and the meat offering, and the burnt offering, and the peace offerings, to make reconciliation for the house of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; In the first _month_, in the first _day_ of the month, thou shalt take a young bullock without blemish, and cleanse the sanctuary:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the priest shall take of the blood of the sin offering, and put _it_ upon the posts of the house, and upon the four corners of the settle of the altar, and upon the posts of the gate of the inner court.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And so thou shalt do the seventh _day_ of the month for every one that erreth, and for _him that is_ simple: so shall ye reconcile the house.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>In the first _month_, in the fourteenth day of the month, ye shall have the passover, a feast of seven days; unleavened bread shall be eaten.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And upon that day shall the prince prepare for himself and for all the people of the land a bullock _for_ a sin offering.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And seven days of the feast he shall prepare a burnt offering to the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, seven bullocks and seven rams without blemish daily the seven days; and a kid of the goats daily _for_ a sin offering.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And he shall prepare a meat offering of an ephah for a bullock, and an ephah for a ram, and an hin of oil for an ephah.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>In the seventh _month_, in the fifteenth day of the month, shall he do the like in the feast of the seven days, according to the sin offering, according to the burnt offering, and according to the meat offering, and according to the oil.  

## CHAPTER 46.

Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; The gate of the inner court that looketh toward the east shall be shut the six working days; but on the sabbath it shall be opened, and in the day of the new moon it shall be opened.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the prince shall enter by the way of the porch of _that_ gate without, and shall stand by the post of the gate, and the priests shall prepare his burnt offering and his peace offerings, and he shall worship at the threshold of the gate: then he shall go forth; but the gate shall not be shut until the evening.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Likewise the people of the land shall worship at the door of this gate before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the sabbaths and in the new moons.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the burnt offering that the prince shall offer unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the sabbath day _shall be_ six lambs without blemish, and a ram without blemish.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the meat offering _shall be_ an ephah for a ram, and the meat offering for the lambs as he shall be able to give, and an hin of oil to an ephah.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And in the day of the new moon _it shall be_ a young bullock without blemish, and six lambs, and a ram: they shall be without blemish.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he shall prepare a meat offering, an ephah for a bullock, and an ephah for a ram, and for the lambs according as his hand shall attain unto, and an hin of oil to an ephah.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And when the prince shall enter, he shall go in by the way of the porch of _that_ gate, and he shall go forth by the way thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ But when the people of the land shall come before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the solemn feasts, he that entereth in by the way of the north gate to worship shall go out by the way of the south gate; and he that entereth by the way of the south gate shall go forth by the way of the north gate: he shall not return by the way of the gate whereby he came in, but shall go forth over against it.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the prince in the midst of them, when they go in, shall go in; and when they go forth, shall go forth.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And in the feasts and in the solemnities the meat offering shall be an ephah to a bullock, and an ephah to a ram, and to the lambs as he is able to give, and an hin of oil to an ephah.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Now when the prince shall prepare a voluntary burnt offering or peace offerings voluntarily unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _one_ shall then open him the gate that looketh toward the east, and he shall prepare his burnt offering and his peace offerings, as he did on the sabbath day: then he shall go forth; and after his going forth _one_ shall shut the gate.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Thou shalt daily prepare a burnt offering unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _of_ a lamb of the first year without blemish: thou shalt prepare it every morning.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And thou shalt prepare a meat offering for it every morning, the sixth part of an ephah, and the third part of an hin of oil, to temper with the fine flour; a meat offering continually by a perpetual ordinance unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Thus shall they prepare the lamb, and the meat offering, and the oil, every morning _for_ a continual burnt offering.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; If the prince give a gift unto any of his sons, the inheritance thereof shall be his sons’; it _shall be_ their possession by inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But if he give a gift of his inheritance to one of his servants, then it shall be his to the year of liberty; after it shall return to the prince: but his inheritance shall be his sons’ for them.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Moreover the prince shall not take of the people’s inheritance by oppression, to thrust them out of their possession; _but_ he shall give his sons inheritance out of his own possession: that my people be not scattered every man from his possession.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ After he brought me through the entry, which _was_ at the side of the gate, into the holy chambers of the priests, which looked toward the north: and, behold, there _was_ a place on the two sides westward.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Then said he unto me, This _is_ the place where the priests shall boil the trespass offering and the sin offering, where they shall bake the meat offering; that they bear _them_ not out into the utter court, to sanctify the people.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Then he brought me forth into the utter court, and caused me to pass by the four corners of the court; and, behold, in every corner of the court _there was_ a court.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>In the four corners of the court _there were_ courts joined of forty _cubits_ long and thirty broad: these four corners _were_ of one measure.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And _there was_ a row _of building_ round about in them, round about them four, and _it was_ made with boiling places under the rows round about.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Then said he unto me, These _are_ the places of them that boil, where the ministers of the house shall boil the sacrifice of the people.  

## CHAPTER 47.

Afterward he brought me again unto the door of the house; and, behold, waters issued out from under the threshold of the house eastward: for the forefront of the house _stood toward_ the east, and the waters came down from under from the right side of the house, at the south _side_ of the altar.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then brought he me out of the way of the gate northward, and led me about the way without unto the utter gate by the way that looketh eastward; and, behold, there ran out waters on the right side.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And when the man that had the line in his hand went forth eastward, he measured a thousand cubits, and he brought me through the waters; the waters _were_ to the ankles.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Again he measured a thousand, and brought me through the waters; the waters _were_ to the knees. Again he measured a thousand, and brought me through; the waters _were_ to the loins.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Afterward he measured a thousand; _and it was_ a river that I could not pass over: for the waters were risen, waters to swim in, a river that could not be passed over.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And he said unto me, Son of man, hast thou seen _this_? Then he brought me, and caused me to return to the brink of the river.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Now when I had returned, behold, at the bank of the river _were_ very many trees on the one side and on the other.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Then said he unto me, These waters issue out toward the east country, and go down into the desert, and go into the sea: _which being_ brought forth into the sea, the waters shall be healed.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ every thing that liveth, which moveth, whithersoever the rivers shall come, shall live: and there shall be a very great multitude of fish, because these waters shall come thither: for they shall be healed; and every thing shall live whither the river cometh.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ the fishers shall stand upon it from En–gedi even unto En–eglaim; they shall be a _place_ to spread forth nets; their fish shall be according to their kinds, as the fish of the great sea, exceeding many.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But the miry places thereof and the marishes thereof shall not be healed; they shall be given to salt.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And by the river upon the bank thereof, on this side and on that side, shall grow all trees for meat, whose leaf shall not fade, neither shall the fruit thereof be consumed: it shall bring forth new fruit according to his months, because their waters they issued out of the sanctuary: and the fruit thereof shall be for meat, and the leaf thereof for medicine.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Thus saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; This _shall be_ the border, whereby ye shall inherit the land according to the twelve tribes of Israel: Joseph _shall have two_ portions.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And ye shall inherit it, one as well as another: _concerning_ the which I lifted up mine hand to give it unto your fathers: and this land shall fall unto you for inheritance.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And this _shall be_ the border of the land toward the north side, from the great sea, the way of Hethlon, as men go to Zedad;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Hamath, Berothah, Sibraim, which _is_ between the border of Damascus and the border of Hamath; Hazar–hatticon, which _is_ by the coast of Hauran.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the border from the sea shall be Hazar–enan, the border of Damascus, and the north northward, and the border of Hamath. And _this is_ the north side.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the east side ye shall measure from Hauran, and from Damascus, and from Gilead, and from the land of Israel _by_ Jordan, from the border unto the east sea. And _this is_ the east side.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the south side southward, from Tamar _even_ to the waters of strife _in_ Kadesh, the river to the great sea. And _this is_ the south side southward.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>The west side also _shall be_ the great sea from the border, till a man come over against Hamath. This _is_ the west side.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>So shall ye divide this land unto you according to the tribes of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And it shall come to pass, _that_ ye shall divide it by lot for an inheritance unto you, and to the strangers that sojourn among you, which shall beget children among you: and they shall be unto you as born in the country among the children of Israel; they shall have inheritance with you among the tribes of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And it shall come to pass, _that_ in what tribe the stranger sojourneth, there shall ye give _him_ his inheritance, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  

## CHAPTER 48.

Now these _are_ the names of the tribes. From the north end to the coast of the way of Hethlon, as one goeth to Hamath, Hazar–enan, the border of Damascus northward, to the coast of Hamath; for these are his sides east _and_ west; a _portion for_ Dan.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And by the border of Dan, from the east side unto the west side, a _portion for_ Asher.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And by the border of Asher, from the east side even unto the west side, a _portion for_ Naphtali.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And by the border of Naphtali, from the east side unto the west side, a _portion for_ Manasseh.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And by the border of Manasseh, from the east side unto the west side, a _portion for_ Ephraim.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And by the border of Ephraim, from the east side even unto the west side, a _portion for_ Reuben.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And by the border of Reuben, from the east side unto the west side, a _portion for_ Judah.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And by the border of Judah, from the east side unto the west side, shall be the offering which ye shall offer of five and twenty thousand _reeds in_ breadth, and _in_ length as one of the _other_ parts, from the east side unto the west side: and the sanctuary shall be in the midst of it.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>The oblation that ye shall offer unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _shall be_ of five and twenty thousand in length, and of ten thousand in breadth.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And for them, _even_ for the priests, shall be _this_ holy oblation; toward the north five and twenty thousand _in length_, and toward the west ten thousand in breadth, and toward the east ten thousand in breadth, and toward the south five and twenty thousand in length: and the sanctuary of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall be in the midst thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>_It shall be_ for the priests that are sanctified of the sons of Zadok; which have kept my charge, which went not astray when the children of Israel went astray, as the Levites went astray.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And _this_ oblation of the land that is offered shall be unto them a thing most holy by the border of the Levites.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And over against the border of the priests the Levites _shall have_ five and twenty thousand in length, and ten thousand in breadth: all the length _shall be_ five and twenty thousand, and the breadth ten thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they shall not sell of it, neither exchange, nor alienate the firstfruits of the land: for _it is_ holy unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And the five thousand, that are left in the breadth over against the five and twenty thousand, shall be a profane _place_ for the city, for dwelling, and for suburbs: and the city shall be in the midst thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And these _shall be_ the measures thereof; the north side four thousand and five hundred, and the south side four thousand and five hundred, and on the east side four thousand and five hundred, and the west side four thousand and five hundred.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the suburbs of the city shall be toward the north two hundred and fifty, and toward the south two hundred and fifty, and toward the east two hundred and fifty, and toward the west two hundred and fifty.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the residue in length over against the oblation of the holy _portion shall be_ ten thousand eastward, and ten thousand westward: and it shall be over against the oblation of the holy _portion_; and the increase thereof shall be for food unto them that serve the city.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And they that serve the city shall serve it out of all the tribes of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>All the oblation _shall be_ five and twenty thousand by five and twenty thousand: ye shall offer the holy oblation foursquare, with the possession of the city.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And the residue _shall be_ for the prince, on the one side and on the other of the holy oblation, and of the possession of the city, over against the five and twenty thousand of the oblation toward the east border, and westward over against the five and twenty thousand toward the west border, over against the portions for the prince: and it shall be the holy oblation; and the sanctuary of the house _shall be_ in the midst thereof.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Moreover from the possession of the Levites, and from the possession of the city, _being_ in the midst _of that_ which is the prince’s, between the border of Judah and the border of Benjamin, shall be for the prince.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>As for the rest of the tribes, from the east side unto the west side, Benjamin _shall have_ a _portion_.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And by the border of Benjamin, from the east side unto the west side, Simeon _shall have_ a _portion_.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And by the border of Simeon, from the east side unto the west side, Issachar a _portion_.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And by the border of Issachar, from the east side unto the west side, Zebulun a _portion_.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And by the border of Zebulun, from the east side unto the west side, Gad a _portion_.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And by the border of Gad, at the south side southward, the border shall be even from Tamar _unto_ the waters of strife _in_ Kadesh, _and_ to the river toward the great sea.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>This _is_ the land which ye shall divide by lot unto the tribes of Israel for inheritance, and these _are_ their portions, saith the Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ And these _are_ the goings out of the city on the north side, four thousand and five hundred measures.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And the gates of the city _shall be_ after the names of the tribes of Israel: three gates northward; one gate of Reuben, one gate of Judah, one gate of Levi.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And at the east side four thousand and five hundred: and three gates; and one gate of Joseph, one gate of Benjamin, one gate of Dan.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And at the south side four thousand and five hundred measures: and three gates; one gate of Simeon, one gate of Issachar, one gate of Zebulun.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>At the west side four thousand and five hundred, _with_ their three gates; one gate of Gad, one gate of Asher, one gate of Naphtali.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>_It was_ round about eighteen thousand _measures_: and the name of the city from _that_ day _shall be_, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ there.
