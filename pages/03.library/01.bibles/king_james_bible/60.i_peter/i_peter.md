# I Peter

### THE FIRST EPISTLE GENERAL OF PETER

## CHAPTER 1.

Peter, an apostle of Jesus Christ, to the strangers scattered throughout Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Elect according to the foreknowledge of God the Father, through sanctification of the Spirit, unto obedience and sprinkling of the blood of Jesus Christ: Grace unto you, and peace, be multiplied.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Blessed _be_ the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, which according to his abundant mercy hath begotten us again unto a lively hope by the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>To an inheritance incorruptible, and undefiled, and that fadeth not away, reserved in heaven for you,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Who are kept by the power of God through faith unto salvation ready to be revealed in the last time.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Wherein ye greatly rejoice, though now for a season, if need be, ye are in heaviness through manifold temptations:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>That the trial of your faith, being much more precious than of gold that perisheth, though it be tried with fire, might be found unto praise and honour and glory at the appearing of Jesus Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Whom having not seen, ye love; in whom, though now ye see _him_ not, yet believing, ye rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of glory:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Receiving the end of your faith, _even_ the salvation of _your_ souls.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Of which salvation the prophets have enquired and searched diligently, who prophesied of the grace _that should come_ unto you:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Searching what, or what manner of time the Spirit of Christ which was in them did signify, when it testified beforehand the sufferings of Christ, and the glory that should follow.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Unto whom it was revealed, that not unto themselves, but unto us they did minister the things, which are now reported unto you by them that have preached the gospel unto you with the Holy Ghost sent down from heaven; which things the angels desire to look into.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Wherefore gird up the loins of your mind, be sober, and hope to the end for the grace that is to be brought unto you at the revelation of Jesus Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>As obedient children, not fashioning yourselves according to the former lusts in your ignorance:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But as he which hath called you is holy, so be ye holy in all manner of conversation;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Because it is written, Be ye holy; for I am holy.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And if ye call on the Father, who without respect of persons judgeth according to every man’s work, pass the time of your sojourning _here_ in fear:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Forasmuch as ye know that ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, _as_ silver and gold, from your vain conversation _received_ by tradition from your fathers;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But with the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish and without spot:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Who verily was foreordained before the foundation of the world, but was manifest in these last times for you,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Who by him do believe in God, that raised him up from the dead, and gave him glory; that your faith and hope might be in God.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Seeing ye have purified your souls in obeying the truth through the Spirit unto unfeigned love of the brethren, _see that ye_ love one another with a pure heart fervently:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Being born again, not of corruptible seed, but of incorruptible, by the word of God, which liveth and abideth for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For all flesh _is_ as grass, and all the glory of man as the flower of grass. The grass withereth, and the flower thereof falleth away:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But the word of the Lord endureth for ever. And this is the word which by the gospel is preached unto you.  

## CHAPTER 2.

Wherefore laying aside all malice, and all guile, and hypocrisies, and envies, and all evil speakings,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>As newborn babes, desire the sincere milk of the word, that ye may grow thereby:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>If so be ye have tasted that the Lord _is_ gracious.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>To whom coming, _as unto_ a living stone, disallowed indeed of men, but chosen of God, _and_ precious,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Ye also, as lively stones, are built up a spiritual house, an holy priesthood, to offer up spiritual sacrifices, acceptable to God by Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Wherefore also it is contained in the scripture, Behold, I lay in Sion a chief corner stone, elect, precious: and he that believeth on him shall not be confounded.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Unto you therefore which believe _he is_ precious: but unto them which be disobedient, the stone which the builders disallowed, the same is made the head of the corner,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And a stone of stumbling, and a rock of offence, _even to them_ which stumble at the word, being disobedient: whereunto also they were appointed.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But ye _are_ a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Which in time past _were_ not a people, but _are_ now the people of God: which had not obtained mercy, but now have obtained mercy.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Dearly beloved, I beseech _you_ as strangers and pilgrims, abstain from fleshly lusts, which war against the soul;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Having your conversation honest among the Gentiles: that, whereas they speak against you as evildoers, they may by _your_ good works, which they shall behold, glorify God in the day of visitation.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ Submit yourselves to every ordinance of man for the Lord’s sake: whether it be to the king, as supreme;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Or unto governors, as unto them that are sent by him for the punishment of evildoers, and for the praise of them that do well.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For so is the will of God, that with well doing ye may put to silence the ignorance of foolish men:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>As free, and not using _your_ liberty for a cloke of maliciousness, but as the servants of God.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Honour all _men_. Love the brotherhood. Fear God. Honour the king.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Servants, _be_ subject to _your_ masters with all fear; not only to the good and gentle, but also to the froward.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For this _is_ thankworthy, if a man for conscience toward God endure grief, suffering wrongfully.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For what glory _is it_, if, when ye be buffeted for your faults, ye shall take it patiently? but if, when ye do well, and suffer _for it_, ye take it patiently, this _is_ acceptable with God.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For even hereunto were ye called: because Christ also suffered for us, leaving us an example, that ye should follow his steps:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Who did no sin, neither was guile found in his mouth:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Who, when he was reviled, reviled not again; when he suffered, he threatened not; but committed _himself_ to him that judgeth righteously:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Who his own self bare our sins in his own body on the tree, that we, being dead to sins, should live unto righteousness: by whose stripes ye were healed.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For ye were as sheep going astray; but are now returned unto the Shepherd and Bishop of your souls.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Likewise, ye wives, _be_ in subjection to your own husbands; that, if any obey not the word, they also may without the word be won by the conversation of the wives;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>While they behold your chaste conversation _coupled_ with fear.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Whose adorning let it not be that outward _adorning_ of plaiting the hair, and of wearing of gold, or of putting on of apparel;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But _let it be_ the hidden man of the heart, in that which is not corruptible, _even the ornament_ of a meek and quiet spirit, which is in the sight of God of great price.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For after this manner in the old time the holy women also, who trusted in God, adorned themselves, being in subjection unto their own husbands:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Even as Sara obeyed Abraham, calling him lord: whose daughters ye are, as long as ye do well, and are not afraid with any amazement.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Likewise, ye husbands, dwell with _them_ according to knowledge, giving honour unto the wife, as unto the weaker vessel, and as being heirs together of the grace of life; that your prayers be not hindered.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Finally, _be ye_ all of one mind, having compassion one of another, love as brethren, _be_ pitiful, _be_ courteous:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Not rendering evil for evil, or railing for railing: but contrariwise blessing; knowing that ye are thereunto called, that ye should inherit a blessing.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For he that will love life, and see good days, let him refrain his tongue from evil, and his lips that they speak no guile:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Let him eschew evil, and do good; let him seek peace, and ensue it.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the eyes of the Lord _are_ over the righteous, and his ears _are open_ unto their prayers: but the face of the Lord _is_ against them that do evil.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And who _is_ he that will harm you, if ye be followers of that which is good?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But and if ye suffer for righteousness’ sake, happy _are ye_: and be not afraid of their terror, neither be troubled;  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But sanctify the Lord God in your hearts: and _be_ ready always to _give_ an answer to every man that asketh you a reason of the hope that is in you with meekness and fear:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Having a good conscience; that, whereas they speak evil of you, as of evildoers, they may be ashamed that falsely accuse your good conversation in Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For _it is_ better, if the will of God be so, that ye suffer for well doing, than for evil doing.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For Christ also hath once suffered for sins, the just for the unjust, that he might bring us to God, being put to death in the flesh, but quickened by the Spirit:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>By which also he went and preached unto the spirits in prison;  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Which sometime were disobedient, when once the longsuffering of God waited in the days of Noah, while the ark was a preparing, wherein few, that is, eight souls were saved by water.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>The like figure whereunto _even_ baptism doth also now save us (not the putting away of the filth of the flesh, but the answer of a good conscience toward God,) by the resurrection of Jesus Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Who is gone into heaven, and is on the right hand of God; angels and authorities and powers being made subject unto him.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Forasmuch then as Christ hath suffered for us in the flesh, arm yourselves likewise with the same mind: for he that hath suffered in the flesh hath ceased from sin;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That he no longer should live the rest of _his_ time in the flesh to the lusts of men, but to the will of God.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For the time past of _our_ life may suffice us to have wrought the will of the Gentiles, when we walked in lasciviousness, lusts, excess of wine, revellings, banquetings, and abominable idolatries:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Wherein they think it strange that ye run not with _them_ to the same excess of riot, speaking evil of _you_:   
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Who shall give account to him that is ready to judge the quick and the dead.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For for this cause was the gospel preached also to them that are dead, that they might be judged according to men in the flesh, but live according to God in the spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ But the end of all things is at hand: be ye therefore sober, and watch unto prayer.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And above all things have fervent charity among yourselves: for charity shall cover the multitude of sins.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Use hospitality one to another without grudging.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>As every man hath received the gift, _even so_ minister the same one to another, as good stewards of the manifold grace of God.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>If any man speak, _let him speak_ as the oracles of God; if any man minister, _let him do it_ as of the ability which God giveth: that God in all things may be glorified through Jesus Christ, to whom be praise and dominion for ever and ever. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Beloved, think it not strange concerning the fiery trial which is to try you, as though some strange thing happened unto you:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But rejoice, inasmuch as ye are partakers of Christ’s sufferings; that, when his glory shall be revealed, ye may be glad also with exceeding joy.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>If ye be reproached for the name of Christ, happy _are ye_; for the spirit of glory and of God resteth upon you: on their part he is evil spoken of, but on your part he is glorified.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But let none of you suffer as a murderer, or _as_ a thief, or _as_ an evildoer, or as a busybody in other men’s matters.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Yet if _any man suffer_ as a Christian, let him not be ashamed; but let him glorify God on this behalf.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For the time _is come_ that judgment must begin at the house of God: and if _it_ first _begin_ at us, what shall the end _be_ of them that obey not the gospel of God?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And if the righteous scarcely be saved, where shall the ungodly and the sinner appear?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Wherefore let them that suffer according to the will of God commit the keeping of their souls _to him_ in well doing, as unto a faithful Creator.  

## CHAPTER 5.

The elders which are among you I exhort, who am also an elder, and a witness of the sufferings of Christ, and also a partaker of the glory that shall be revealed:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Feed the flock of God which is among you, taking the oversight _thereof_, not by constraint, but willingly; not for filthy lucre, but of a ready mind;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Neither as being lords over _God’s_ heritage, but being ensamples to the flock.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And when the chief Shepherd shall appear, ye shall receive a crown of glory that fadeth not away.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Likewise, ye younger, submit yourselves unto the elder. Yea, all _of you_ be subject one to another, and be clothed with humility: for God resisteth the proud, and giveth grace to the humble.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ Humble yourselves therefore under the mighty hand of God, that he may exalt you in due time:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Be sober, be vigilant; because your adversary the devil, as a roaring lion, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Whom resist stedfast in the faith, knowing that the same afflictions are accomplished in your brethren that are in the world.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But the God of all grace, who hath called us unto his eternal glory by Christ Jesus, after that ye have suffered a while, make you perfect, stablish, strengthen, settle _you_.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>To him _be_ glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ By Silvanus, a faithful brother unto you, as I suppose, I have written briefly, exhorting, and testifying that this is the true grace of God wherein ye stand.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>The _church that is_ at Babylon, elected together with _you_, saluteth you; and _so doth_ Marcus my son.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Greet ye one another with a kiss of charity. Peace _be_ with you all that are in Christ Jesus. Amen.
