# Galatians

### THE EPISTLE OF PAUL THE APOSTLE TO THE GALATIANS

## CHAPTER 1.

Paul, an apostle, (not of men, neither by man, but by Jesus Christ, and God the Father, who raised him from the dead;)  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And all the brethren which are with me, unto the churches of Galatia:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Grace _be_ to you and peace from God the Father, and _from_ our Lord Jesus Christ,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Who gave himself for our sins, that he might deliver us from this present evil world, according to the will of God and our Father:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>To whom _be_ glory for ever and ever. Amen.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ I marvel that ye are so soon removed from him that called you into the grace of Christ unto another gospel:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Which is not another; but there be some that trouble you, and would pervert the gospel of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But though we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel unto you than that which we have preached unto you, let him be accursed.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>As we said before, so say I now again, If any _man_ preach any other gospel unto you than that ye have received, let him be accursed.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ For do I now persuade men, or God? or do I seek to please men? for if I yet pleased men, I should not be the servant of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But I certify you, brethren, that the gospel which was preached of me is not after man.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For I neither received it of man, neither was I taught _it_, but by the revelation of Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For ye have heard of my conversation in time past in the Jews’ religion, how that beyond measure I persecuted the church of God, and wasted it:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And profited in the Jews’ religion above many my equals in mine own nation, being more exceedingly zealous of the traditions of my fathers.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But when it pleased God, who separated me from my mother’s womb, and called _me_ by his grace,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>To reveal his Son in me, that I might preach him among the heathen; immediately I conferred not with flesh and blood:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Neither went I up to Jerusalem to them which were apostles before me; but I went into Arabia, and returned again unto Damascus.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Then after three years I went up to Jerusalem to see Peter, and abode with him fifteen days.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But other of the apostles saw I none, save James the Lord’s brother.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Now the things which I write unto you, behold, before God, I lie not.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Afterwards I came into the regions of Syria and Cilicia;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And was unknown by face unto the churches of Judaea which were in Christ:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But they had heard only, That he which persecuted us in times past now preacheth the faith which once he destroyed.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they glorified God in me.  

## CHAPTER 2.

Then fourteen years after I went up again to Jerusalem with Barnabas, and took Titus with _me_ also.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And I went up by revelation, and communicated unto them that gospel which I preach among the Gentiles, but privately to them which were of reputation, lest by any means I should run, or had run, in vain.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But neither Titus, who was with me, being a Greek, was compelled to be circumcised:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And that because of false brethren unawares brought in, who came in privily to spy out our liberty which we have in Christ Jesus, that they might bring us into bondage:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>To whom we gave place by subjection, no, not for an hour; that the truth of the gospel might continue with you.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But of these who seemed to be somewhat, (whatsoever they were, it maketh no matter to me: God accepteth no man’s person:) for they who seemed _to be somewhat_ in conference added nothing to me:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But contrariwise, when they saw that the gospel of the uncircumcision was committed unto me, as _the gospel_ of the circumcision _was_ unto Peter;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>(For he that wrought effectually in Peter to the apostleship of the circumcision, the same was mighty in me toward the Gentiles:)  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And when James, Cephas, and John, who seemed to be pillars, perceived the grace that was given unto me, they gave to me and Barnabas the right hands of fellowship; that we _should go_ unto the heathen, and they unto the circumcision.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Only _they would_ that we should remember the poor; the same which I also was forward to do.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But when Peter was come to Antioch, I withstood him to the face, because he was to be blamed.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For before that certain came from James, he did eat with the Gentiles: but when they were come, he withdrew and separated himself, fearing them which were of the circumcision.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the other Jews dissembled likewise with him; insomuch that Barnabas also was carried away with their dissimulation.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But when I saw that they walked not uprightly according to the truth of the gospel, I said unto Peter before _them_ all, If thou, being a Jew, livest after the manner of Gentiles, and not as do the Jews, why compellest thou the Gentiles to live as do the Jews?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>We _who are_ Jews by nature, and not sinners of the Gentiles,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Knowing that a man is not justified by the works of the law, but by the faith of Jesus Christ, even we have believed in Jesus Christ, that we might be justified by the faith of Christ, and not by the works of the law: for by the works of the law shall no flesh be justified.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But if, while we seek to be justified by Christ, we ourselves also are found sinners, _is_ therefore Christ the minister of sin? God forbid.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For if I build again the things which I destroyed, I make myself a transgressor.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>For I through the law am dead to the law, that I might live unto God.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me: and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son of God, who loved me, and gave himself for me.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>I do not frustrate the grace of God: for if righteousness _come_ by the law, then Christ is dead in vain.  

## CHAPTER 3.

O foolish Galatians, who hath bewitched you, that ye should not obey the truth, before whose eyes Jesus Christ hath been evidently set forth, crucified among you?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>This only would I learn of you, Received ye the Spirit by the works of the law, or by the hearing of faith?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Are ye so foolish? having begun in the Spirit, are ye now made perfect by the flesh?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Have ye suffered so many things in vain? if _it be_ yet in vain.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>He therefore that ministereth to you the Spirit, and worketh miracles among you, _doeth he it_ by the works of the law, or by the hearing of faith?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Even as Abraham believed God, and it was accounted to him for righteousness.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Know ye therefore that they which are of faith, the same are the children of Abraham.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the scripture, foreseeing that God would justify the heathen through faith, preached before the gospel unto Abraham, _saying_, In thee shall all nations be blessed.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>So then they which be of faith are blessed with faithful Abraham.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For as many as are of the works of the law are under the curse: for it is written, Cursed _is_ every one that continueth not in all things which are written in the book of the law to do them.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>But that no man is justified by the law in the sight of God, _it is_ evident: for, The just shall live by faith.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the law is not of faith: but, The man that doeth them shall live in them.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us: for it is written, Cursed _is_ every one that hangeth on a tree:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>That the blessing of Abraham might come on the Gentiles through Jesus Christ; that we might receive the promise of the Spirit through faith.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Brethren, I speak after the manner of men; Though _it be_ but a man’s covenant, yet _if it be_ confirmed, no man disannulleth, or addeth thereto.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Now to Abraham and his seed were the promises made. He saith not, And to seeds, as of many; but as of one, And to thy seed, which is Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And this I say, _that_ the covenant, that was confirmed before of God in Christ, the law, which was four hundred and thirty years after, cannot disannul, that it should make the promise of none effect.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For if the inheritance _be_ of the law, _it is_ no more of promise: but God gave _it_ to Abraham by promise.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Wherefore then _serveth_ the law? It was added because of transgressions, till the seed should come to whom the promise was made; _and it was_ ordained by angels in the hand of a mediator.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Now a mediator is not _a mediator_ of one, but God is one.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>_Is_ the law then against the promises of God? God forbid: for if there had been a law given which could have given life, verily righteousness should have been by the law.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But the scripture hath concluded all under sin, that the promise by faith of Jesus Christ might be given to them that believe.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ But before faith came, we were kept under the law, shut up unto the faith which should afterwards be revealed.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Wherefore the law was our schoolmaster _to bring us_ unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But after that faith is come, we are no longer under a schoolmaster.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For ye are all the children of God by faith in Christ Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For as many of you as have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither bond nor free, there is neither male nor female: for ye are all one in Christ Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And if ye _be_ Christ’s, then are ye Abraham’s seed, and heirs according to the promise.  

## CHAPTER 4.

Now I say, _That_ the heir, as long as he is a child, differeth nothing from a servant, though he be lord of all;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>But is under tutors and governors until the time appointed of the father.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Even so we, when we were children, were in bondage under the elements of the world:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But when the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son, made of a woman, made under the law,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>To redeem them that were under the law, that we might receive the adoption of sons.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And because ye are sons, God hath sent forth the Spirit of his Son into your hearts, crying, Abba, Father.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Wherefore thou art no more a servant, but a son; and if a son, then an heir of God through Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Howbeit then, when ye knew not God, ye did service unto them which by nature are no gods.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But now, after that ye have known God, or rather are known of God, how turn ye again to the weak and beggarly elements, whereunto ye desire again to be in bondage?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Ye observe days, and months, and times, and years.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>I am afraid of you, lest I have bestowed upon you labour in vain.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Brethren, I beseech you, be as I _am_; for I _am_ as ye _are_: ye have not injured me at all.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Ye know how through infirmity of the flesh I preached the gospel unto you at the first.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And my temptation which was in my flesh ye despised not, nor rejected; but received me as an angel of God, _even_ as Christ Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Where is then the blessedness ye spake of? for I bear you record, that, if _it had been_ possible, ye would have plucked out your own eyes, and have given them to me.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Am I therefore become your enemy, because I tell you the truth?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>They zealously affect you, _but_ not well; yea, they would exclude you, that ye might affect them.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But _it is_ good to be zealously affected always in _a_ good _thing_, and not only when I am present with you.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>My little children, of whom I travail in birth again until Christ be formed in you,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>I desire to be present with you now, and to change my voice; for I stand in doubt of you.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ Tell me, ye that desire to be under the law, do ye not hear the law?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For it is written, that Abraham had two sons, the one by a bondmaid, the other by a freewoman.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But he _who was_ of the bondwoman was born after the flesh; but he of the freewoman _was_ by promise.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Which things are an allegory: for these are the two covenants; the one from the mount Sinai, which gendereth to bondage, which is Agar.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For this Agar is mount Sinai in Arabia, and answereth to Jerusalem which now is, and is in bondage with her children.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But Jerusalem which is above is free, which is the mother of us all.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For it is written, Rejoice, _thou_ barren that bearest not; break forth and cry, thou that travailest not: for the desolate hath many more children than she which hath an husband.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Now we, brethren, as Isaac was, are the children of promise.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>But as then he that was born after the flesh persecuted him _that was born_ after the Spirit, even so _it is_ now.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Nevertheless what saith the scripture? Cast out the bondwoman and her son: for the son of the bondwoman shall not be heir with the son of the freewoman.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>So then, brethren, we are not children of the bondwoman, but of the free.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Stand fast therefore in the liberty wherewith Christ hath made us free, and be not entangled again with the yoke of bondage.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ Behold, I Paul say unto you, that if ye be circumcised, Christ shall profit you nothing.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For I testify again to every man that is circumcised, that he is a debtor to do the whole law.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Christ is become of no effect unto you, whosoever of you are justified by the law; ye are fallen from grace.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For we through the Spirit wait for the hope of righteousness by faith.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For in Jesus Christ neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision; but faith which worketh by love.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Ye did run well; who did hinder you that ye should not obey the truth?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>This persuasion _cometh_ not of him that calleth you.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>A little leaven leaveneth the whole lump.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>I have confidence in you through the Lord, that ye will be none otherwise minded: but he that troubleth you shall bear his judgment, whosoever he be.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And I, brethren, if I yet preach circumcision, why do I yet suffer persecution? then is the offence of the cross ceased.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I would they were even cut off which trouble you.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For, brethren, ye have been called unto liberty; only _use_ not liberty for an occasion to the flesh, but by love serve one another.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For all the law is fulfilled in one word, _even_ in this; Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But if ye bite and devour one another, take heed that ye be not consumed one of another.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>_This_ I say then, Walk in the Spirit, and ye shall not fulfil the lust of the flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For the flesh lusteth against the Spirit, and the Spirit against the flesh: and these are contrary the one to the other: so that ye cannot do the things that ye would.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But if ye be led of the Spirit, ye are not under the law.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Now the works of the flesh are manifest, which are _these_; Adultery, fornication, uncleanness, lasciviousness,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Idolatry, witchcraft, hatred, variance, emulations, wrath, strife, seditions, heresies,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Envyings, murders, drunkenness, revellings, and such like: of the which I tell you before, as I have also told _you_ in time past, that they which do such things shall not inherit the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith,  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Meekness, temperance: against such there is no law.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they that are Christ’s have crucified the flesh with the affections and lusts.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>If we live in the Spirit, let us also walk in the Spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Let us not be desirous of vain glory, provoking one another, envying one another.  

## CHAPTER 6.

Brethren, if a man be overtaken in a fault, ye which are spiritual, restore such an one in the spirit of meekness; considering thyself, lest thou also be tempted.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Bear ye one another’s burdens, and so fulfil the law of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>For if a man think himself to be something, when he is nothing, he deceiveth himself.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But let every man prove his own work, and then shall he have rejoicing in himself alone, and not in another.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For every man shall bear his own burden.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Let him that is taught in the word communicate unto him that teacheth in all good things.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Be not deceived; God is not mocked: for whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For he that soweth to his flesh shall of the flesh reap corruption; but he that soweth to the Spirit shall of the Spirit reap life everlasting.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>As we have therefore opportunity, let us do good unto all _men_, especially unto them who are of the household of faith.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Ye see how large a letter I have written unto you with mine own hand.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>As many as desire to make a fair shew in the flesh, they constrain you to be circumcised; only lest they should suffer persecution for the cross of Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>For neither they themselves who are circumcised keep the law; but desire to have you circumcised, that they may glory in your flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But God forbid that I should glory, save in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, by whom the world is crucified unto me, and I unto the world.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision, but a new creature.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And as many as walk according to this rule, peace _be_ on them, and mercy, and upon the Israel of God.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>From henceforth let no man trouble me: for I bear in my body the marks of the Lord Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Brethren, the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ _be_ with your spirit. Amen.
