# Jude

### THE GENERAL EPISTLE OF JUDE

Jude, the servant of Jesus Christ, and brother of James, to them that are sanctified by God the Father, and preserved in Jesus Christ, _and_ called:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Mercy unto you, and peace, and love, be multiplied.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Beloved, when I gave all diligence to write unto you of the common salvation, it was needful for me to write unto you, and exhort _you_ that ye should earnestly contend for the faith which was once delivered unto the saints.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For there are certain men crept in unawares, who were before of old ordained to this condemnation, ungodly men, turning the grace of our God into lasciviousness, and denying the only Lord God, and our Lord Jesus Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>I will therefore put you in remembrance, though ye once knew this, how that the Lord, having saved the people out of the land of Egypt, afterward destroyed them that believed not.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the angels which kept not their first estate, but left their own habitation, he hath reserved in everlasting chains under darkness unto the judgment of the great day.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Even as Sodom and Gomorrha, and the cities about them in like manner, giving themselves over to fornication, and going after strange flesh, are set forth for an example, suffering the vengeance of eternal fire.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Likewise also these _filthy_ dreamers defile the flesh, despise dominion, and speak evil of dignities.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Yet Michael the archangel, when contending with the devil he disputed about the body of Moses, durst not bring against him a railing accusation, but said, The Lord rebuke thee.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But these speak evil of those things which they know not: but what they know naturally, as brute beasts, in those things they corrupt themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Woe unto them! for they have gone in the way of Cain, and ran greedily after the error of Balaam for reward, and perished in the gainsaying of Core.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>These are spots in your feasts of charity, when they feast with you, feeding themselves without fear: clouds _they are_ without water, carried about of winds; trees whose fruit withereth, without fruit, twice dead, plucked up by the roots;  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Raging waves of the sea, foaming out their own shame; wandering stars, to whom is reserved the blackness of darkness for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And Enoch also, the seventh from Adam, prophesied of these, saying, Behold, the Lord cometh with ten thousands of his saints,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>To execute judgment upon all, and to convince all that are ungodly among them of all their ungodly deeds which they have ungodly committed, and of all their hard _speeches_ which ungodly sinners have spoken against him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>These are murmurers, complainers, walking after their own lusts; and their mouth speaketh great swelling _words_, having men’s persons in admiration because of advantage.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ But, beloved, remember ye the words which were spoken before of the apostles of our Lord Jesus Christ;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>How that they told you there should be mockers in the last time, who should walk after their own ungodly lusts.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>These be they who separate themselves, sensual, having not the Spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But ye, beloved, building up yourselves on your most holy faith, praying in the Holy Ghost,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Keep yourselves in the love of God, looking for the mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ unto eternal life.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And of some have compassion, making a difference:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And others save with fear, pulling _them_ out of the fire; hating even the garment spotted by the flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Now unto him that is able to keep you from falling, and to present _you_ faultless before the presence of his glory with exceeding joy,  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>To the only wise God our Saviour, _be_ glory and majesty, dominion and power, both now and ever. Amen.
