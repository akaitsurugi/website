## Purpose

To glorify God, oppose evil, and promote Biblical literacy.

## Vision

There are two parts to our vision:  
1. To spread Biblical literacy and encourage reformation and revival by creating, collecting, and distributing sound educational materials for baby and mature Christians to grow in their walk with the Lord.
2. To translate the Bible into languages lacking an accurate and reliable translation of the Holy Scriptures using the King James Bible as the base text, since English is the universal language of the world. The original languages can be consulted when needed, such as when detail is lost (e.g., English only has one word for love, whereas Greek has three).

## The Name

![Akai Tsurugi in Japanese calligraphy](image://akai_calligraphy_vertical.png?lightbox&resize=140,280){.right}

Akai Tsurugi is the transliteration (Latin characters used to represent another language) of the Japanese words 赤い (_akai_, red) and 剣 (_tsurugi_, sword).

### Why Japanese?

Although this website is only available in English now, Japanese will be the first non-English language we will support with Japanese resources in the future.

### Why a red sword?

> For the word of God _is_ quick, and powerful, and sharper than any twoedged sword, piercing even to the dividing asunder of soul and spirit, and of the joints and marrow, and _is_ a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart.  
<span class="reference">- Hebrews 4:12</span>

The Bible is referred to as a sword in Scripture. The focus of this website is to provide resources to aid in the study of the Bible. And swords are cool.

As for red, I learned from the crayon box song that red is for the color of the blood that He shed.

Ultimately, the Bible is about Christ and His blood. The doctrine of the blood atonement has been assaulted by people who claim to be Christians, saying that the atonement has nothing to do with the blood or that blood is just a "symbol" of His death. Don't let those liberals fool you. What Christ accomplished through His sinless life, shed blood, vicarious death, burial, and resurrection (also referred to as the work of Christ) is the core of the Bible and the foundation of eternal salvation. No wonder it is under attack by Satan and his devils.

## The Holy Scriptures

We begin our beliefs with the doctrine of the Holy Scriptures for a critical reason: all of our following beliefs are based on what the Bible teaches. Every belief we firmly hold to must come from the Bible, so we need to be sure of what it is and that it is pure.

>  My heart is inditing a good matter: I speak of the things which I have made touching the king: my tongue _is_ the pen of a ready writer.  
<span class="reference">- Psalm 45:1</span>

It is difficult to encapsulate the Bible in one simple, succinct description, though an attempt may be: the Bible is the words of the Ready Writer.

### Inspiration

> For the prophecy came not in old time by the will of man: but holy men of God spake _as they were_ moved by the Holy Ghost.  
<span class="reference">- II Peter 1:21</span>

The true author of the Bible is God Himself, who used men to deliver the words, as a carpenter uses a hammer or a writer a pen.[^1] The tool affects the style of the work, but the tool is not the creator. This is not to say that when Satan speaks in the Bible that this is God, but that the record (the words we read in our Bible now) has been given to us by God.

The word "inspiration" as defined in the Oxford English Dictionary is:

<div class="quote"><p><strong>I.</strong> Literal (physical) senses. <strong>2.</strong> The action, or an act, of breathing in or inhaling; the drawing in of the breath into the lungs in respiration. (Opp. to expiration 2.)<br/><strong>II.</strong> Figurative senses. <strong>3. a.</strong> <em>spec.</em> (<em>Theol.</em>, etc.) A special immediate action or influence of the Spirit of God (or of some divinity or supernatural being) upon the human mind or soul; said esp. of that divine influence under which the books of Scripture are held to have been written.</p></div>

It is of utmost importance that we specify that what God authored is the words, not the general idea. The emphasis throughout the Bible is placed on the **words**, not the gist of it.[^2] When one places the "meaning" above the words, a vague message is substituted for the specific, true meaning. No where in the Bible does it state that God gave man the ideas and man wrote down these ideas in his own words; the Bible plainly teaches that God spoke the very words through men.

### Preservation

What good would it be if God gave us His words and then lost them? Thankfully, God promised to preserve His words[^3]—in more than just the original manuscripts.[^4] This promise is fulfilled supernaturally, since He is omnipotent after all. The original manuscripts were but the first step in the preservation of His Word.

How weak textual critics must think God is, to state that He only preserved His Word in the autographs! Does God only speak three or so tongues, all of which are dead languages today? Jehovah is not the God of the dead, but of the **living**. Jesus stated that His gospel would be brought to all nations; and in this He didn't mean that all people would have to learn ancient, dead languages.

God said that we'd be able to speak His words forever.[^5] How can His words be in our mouths, unless they are translated? How could His disciples teach others to observe His commandments, except that His words be brought, by His grace and power, into foreign languages?[^6]

Although God has preserved His Word, the children of Lucifer have been hard at work corrupting and perverting it for millennia.[^7] The most recent and prevalent manifestation of which is the abundance of English translations claiming to be Bibles, which in reality are based off of perverted Romish manuscripts known as the Nestle-Aland text.

### Conclusion

Perhaps the grandest chapter about the Bible itself is [Psalm 119](/library/bibles/king_james_bible/psalms#psalm-119).

> Thy word have I hid in mine heart, that I might not sin against thee.… This _is_ my comfort in my affliction: for thy word hath quickened me.…For ever, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, thy word is settled in heaven.… How sweet are thy words unto my taste! _yea, sweeter_ than honey to my mouth!… Thy word _is_ a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path.… Thy word _is_ true _from_ the beginning: and every one of thy righteous judgments _endureth_ for ever.  
<span class="reference">- Psalm 119:11, 50, 89, 103, 105, 160</span>

The Bible is a holy, living book. It is referred to by many analogies (a mirror, a lamb, seed[^8]), our favorite being the sword.[^9] It is to be held with utmost regard.[^10] We are to obey it: it is a guide for us, touching every area of our lives.[^11]

It is the **sole** authority for our faith and practice; not a denomination, the traditions of man, or our subjective experiences.

It is, from beginning to end, about our Lord Jesus Christ (who is also known as the Word).[^12] It contains many doctrines, chief of which is the good news about our Savior and how He has eternally saved His people by His blood atonement.

Because God gave us His words by inspiration, and supernaturally preserves them, we can be sure that the Bible is inerrant[^13] and infallible.[^14] It contains everything that we as believers must know.[^15]

The Word of God, which we can, and must, have in our mother tongue, is pure and perfect. For us English speakers, the Holy Scriptures are purely and perfectly preserved for us as the King James Bible. The rise and decline of English civilizations can be connected to this most incomparable of our English translations.

Does this mean that the King James can't be updated or improved? No, but the only changes that can improve it are spelling updates. This doesn't mean it's the only true Bible for all peoples, but for English speakers it is.

Furthermore, we believe that translating the Holy Bible into other languages can, and should, be done from the English text of the King James; instead of using the Hebrew and Greek texts. English is a living language and it is currently the universal language of the world. This is especially important for nations that only have access to a corrupted version of the Bible, or don't even have a Bible in their native tongue at all.
