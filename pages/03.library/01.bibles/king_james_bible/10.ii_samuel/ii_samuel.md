# II Samuel

### THE SECOND BOOK OF SAMUEL OTHERWISE CALLED THE SECOND BOOK OF THE KINGS

## CHAPTER 1.

Now it came to pass after the death of Saul, when David was returned from the slaughter of the Amalekites, and David had abode two days in Ziklag;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>It came even to pass on the third day, that, behold, a man came out of the camp from Saul with his clothes rent, and earth upon his head: and _so_ it was, when he came to David, that he fell to the earth, and did obeisance.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And David said unto him, From whence comest thou? And he said unto him, Out of the camp of Israel am I escaped.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And David said unto him, How went the matter? I pray thee, tell me. And he answered, That the people are fled from the battle, and many of the people also are fallen and dead; and Saul and Jonathan his son are dead also.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And David said unto the young man that told him, How knowest thou that Saul and Jonathan his son be dead?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the young man that told him said, As I happened by chance upon mount Gilboa, behold, Saul leaned upon his spear; and, lo, the chariots and horsemen followed hard after him.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And when he looked behind him, he saw me, and called unto me. And I answered, Here _am_ I.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he said unto me, Who _art_ thou? And I answered him, I _am_ an Amalekite.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>He said unto me again, Stand, I pray thee, upon me, and slay me: for anguish is come upon me, because my life _is_ yet whole in me.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>So I stood upon him, and slew him, because I was sure that he could not live after that he was fallen: and I took the crown that _was_ upon his head, and the bracelet that _was_ on his arm, and have brought them hither unto my lord.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ Then David took hold on his clothes, and rent them; and likewise all the men that _were_ with him:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And they mourned, and wept, and fasted until even, for Saul, and for Jonathan his son, and for the people of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and for the house of Israel; because they were fallen by the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And David said unto the young man that told him, Whence _art_ thou? And he answered, I _am_ the son of a stranger, an Amalekite.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And David said unto him, How wast thou not afraid to stretch forth thine hand to destroy the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> anointed?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And David called one of the young men, and said, Go near, _and_ fall upon him. And he smote him that he died.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And David said unto him, Thy blood _be_ upon thy head; for thy mouth hath testified against thee, saying, I have slain the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> anointed.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And David lamented with this lamentation over Saul and over Jonathan his son:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>(Also he bade them teach the children of Judah _the use of_ the bow: behold, _it is_ written in the book of Jasher.)  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>The beauty of Israel is slain upon thy high places: how are the mighty fallen!  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Tell _it_ not in Gath, publish _it_ not in the streets of Askelon; lest the daughters of the Philistines rejoice, lest the daughters of the uncircumcised triumph.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Ye mountains of Gilboa, _let there be_ no dew, neither _let there be_ rain, upon you, nor fields of offerings: for there the shield of the mighty is vilely cast away, the shield of Saul, _as though he had_ not _been_ anointed with oil.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>From the blood of the slain, from the fat of the mighty, the bow of Jonathan turned not back, and the sword of Saul returned not empty.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Saul and Jonathan _were_ lovely and pleasant in their lives, and in their death they were not divided: they were swifter than eagles, they were stronger than lions.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Ye daughters of Israel, weep over Saul, who clothed you in scarlet, with _other_ delights, who put on ornaments of gold upon your apparel.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>How are the mighty fallen in the midst of the battle! O Jonathan, _thou wast_ slain in thine high places.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>I am distressed for thee, my brother Jonathan: very pleasant hast thou been unto me: thy love to me was wonderful, passing the love of women.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>How are the mighty fallen, and the weapons of war perished!  

## CHAPTER 2.

And it came to pass after this, that David enquired of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, Shall I go up into any of the cities of Judah? And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto him, Go up. And David said, Whither shall I go up? And he said, Unto Hebron.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>So David went up thither, and his two wives also, Ahinoam the Jezreelitess, and Abigail Nabal’s wife the Carmelite.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And his men that _were_ with him did David bring up, every man with his household: and they dwelt in the cities of Hebron.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the men of Judah came, and there they anointed David king over the house of Judah. And they told David, saying, _That_ the men of Jabesh–gilead _were they_ that buried Saul.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And David sent messengers unto the men of Jabesh–gilead, and said unto them, Blessed _be_ ye of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that ye have shewed this kindness unto your lord, _even_ unto Saul, and have buried him.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And now the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shew kindness and truth unto you: and I also will requite you this kindness, because ye have done this thing.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Therefore now let your hands be strengthened, and be ye valiant: for your master Saul is dead, and also the house of Judah have anointed me king over them.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ But Abner the son of Ner, captain of Saul’s host, took Ish–bosheth the son of Saul, and brought him over to Mahanaim;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And made him king over Gilead, and over the Ashurites, and over Jezreel, and over Ephraim, and over Benjamin, and over all Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Ish–bosheth Saul’s son _was_ forty years old when he began to reign over Israel, and reigned two years. But the house of Judah followed David.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the time that David was king in Hebron over the house of Judah was seven years and six months.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And Abner the son of Ner, and the servants of Ish–bosheth the son of Saul, went out from Mahanaim to Gibeon.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Joab the son of Zeruiah, and the servants of David, went out, and met together by the pool of Gibeon: and they sat down, the one on the one side of the pool, and the other on the other side of the pool.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And Abner said to Joab, Let the young men now arise, and play before us. And Joab said, Let them arise.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Then there arose and went over by number twelve of Benjamin, which _pertained_ to Ish–bosheth the son of Saul, and twelve of the servants of David.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And they caught every one his fellow by the head, and _thrust_ his sword in his fellow’s side; so they fell down together: wherefore that place was called Helkath–hazzurim, which _is_ in Gibeon.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And there was a very sore battle that day; and Abner was beaten, and the men of Israel, before the servants of David.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And there were three sons of Zeruiah there, Joab, and Abishai, and Asahel: and Asahel _was as_ light of foot as a wild roe.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And Asahel pursued after Abner; and in going he turned not to the right hand nor to the left from following Abner.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Then Abner looked behind him, and said, _Art_ thou Asahel? And he answered, I _am_.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Abner said to him, Turn thee aside to thy right hand or to thy left, and lay thee hold on one of the young men, and take thee his armour. But Asahel would not turn aside from following of him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Abner said again to Asahel, Turn thee aside from following me: wherefore should I smite thee to the ground? how then should I hold up my face to Joab thy brother?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Howbeit he refused to turn aside: wherefore Abner with the hinder end of the spear smote him under the fifth _rib_, that the spear came out behind him; and he fell down there, and died in the same place: and it came to pass, _that_ as many as came to the place where Asahel fell down and died stood still.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Joab also and Abishai pursued after Abner: and the sun went down when they were come to the hill of Ammah, that _lieth_ before Giah by the way of the wilderness of Gibeon.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And the children of Benjamin gathered themselves together after Abner, and became one troop, and stood on the top of an hill.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Then Abner called to Joab, and said, Shall the sword devour for ever? knowest thou not that it will be bitterness in the latter end? how long shall it be then, ere thou bid the people return from following their brethren?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And Joab said, _As_ God liveth, unless thou hadst spoken, surely then in the morning the people had gone up every one from following his brother.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>So Joab blew a trumpet, and all the people stood still, and pursued after Israel no more, neither fought they any more.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And Abner and his men walked all that night through the plain, and passed over Jordan, and went through all Bithron, and they came to Mahanaim.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Joab returned from following Abner: and when he had gathered all the people together, there lacked of David’s servants nineteen men and Asahel.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>But the servants of David had smitten of Benjamin, and of Abner’s men, _so that_ three hundred and threescore men died.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ And they took up Asahel, and buried him in the sepulchre of his father, which _was in_ Beth–lehem. And Joab and his men went all night, and they came to Hebron at break of day.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Now there was long war between the house of Saul and the house of David: but David waxed stronger and stronger, and the house of Saul waxed weaker and weaker.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ And unto David were sons born in Hebron: and his firstborn was Amnon, of Ahinoam the Jezreelitess;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And his second, Chileab, of Abigail the wife of Nabal the Carmelite; and the third, Absalom the son of Maacah the daughter of Talmai king of Geshur;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the fourth, Adonijah the son of Haggith; and the fifth, Shephatiah the son of Abital;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the sixth, Ithream, by Eglah David’s wife. These were born to David in Hebron.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, while there was war between the house of Saul and the house of David, that Abner made himself strong for the house of Saul.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And Saul had a concubine, whose name _was_ Rizpah, the daughter of Aiah: and _Ish–bosheth_ said to Abner, Wherefore hast thou gone in unto my father’s concubine?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Then was Abner very wroth for the words of Ish–bosheth, and said, _Am_ I a dog’s head, which against Judah do shew kindness this day unto the house of Saul thy father, to his brethren, and to his friends, and have not delivered thee into the hand of David, that thou chargest me to day with a fault concerning this woman?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>So do God to Abner, and more also, except, as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath sworn to David, even so I do to him;  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>To translate the kingdom from the house of Saul, and to set up the throne of David over Israel and over Judah, from Dan even to Beer–sheba.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And he could not answer Abner a word again, because he feared him.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And Abner sent messengers to David on his behalf, saying, Whose _is_ the land? saying _also_, Make thy league with me, and, behold, my hand _shall be_ with thee, to bring about all Israel unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And he said, Well; I will make a league with thee: but one thing I require of thee, that is, Thou shalt not see my face, except thou first bring Michal Saul’s daughter, when thou comest to see my face.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And David sent messengers to Ish–bosheth Saul’s son, saying, Deliver _me_ my wife Michal, which I espoused to me for an hundred foreskins of the Philistines.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And Ish–bosheth sent, and took her from _her_ husband, _even_ from Phaltiel the son of Laish.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And her husband went with her along weeping behind her to Bahurim. Then said Abner unto him, Go, return. And he returned.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And Abner had communication with the elders of Israel, saying, Ye sought for David in times past _to be_ king over you:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Now then do _it_: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath spoken of David, saying, By the hand of my servant David I will save my people Israel out of the hand of the Philistines, and out of the hand of all their enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And Abner also spake in the ears of Benjamin: and Abner went also to speak in the ears of David in Hebron all that seemed good to Israel, and that seemed good to the whole house of Benjamin.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>So Abner came to David to Hebron, and twenty men with him. And David made Abner and the men that _were_ with him a feast.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Abner said unto David, I will arise and go, and will gather all Israel unto my lord the king, that they may make a league with thee, and that thou mayest reign over all that thine heart desireth. And David sent Abner away; and he went in peace.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, the servants of David and Joab came from _pursuing_ a troop, and brought in a great spoil with them: but Abner _was_ not with David in Hebron; for he had sent him away, and he was gone in peace.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>When Joab and all the host that _was_ with him were come, they told Joab, saying, Abner the son of Ner came to the king, and he hath sent him away, and he is gone in peace.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Then Joab came to the king, and said, What hast thou done? behold, Abner came unto thee; why _is_ it _that_ thou hast sent him away, and he is quite gone?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Thou knowest Abner the son of Ner, that he came to deceive thee, and to know thy going out and thy coming in, and to know all that thou doest.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And when Joab was come out from David, he sent messengers after Abner, which brought him again from the well of Sirah: but David knew _it_ not.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And when Abner was returned to Hebron, Joab took him aside in the gate to speak with him quietly, and smote him there under the fifth _rib_, that he died, for the blood of Asahel his brother.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ And afterward when David heard _it_, he said, I and my kingdom _are_ guiltless before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> for ever from the blood of Abner the son of Ner:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Let it rest on the head of Joab, and on all his father’s house; and let there not fail from the house of Joab one that hath an issue, or that is a leper, or that leaneth on a staff, or that falleth on the sword, or that lacketh bread.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>So Joab and Abishai his brother slew Abner, because he had slain their brother Asahel at Gibeon in the battle.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And David said to Joab, and to all the people that _were_ with him, Rend your clothes, and gird you with sackcloth, and mourn before Abner. And king David _himself_ followed the bier.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And they buried Abner in Hebron: and the king lifted up his voice, and wept at the grave of Abner; and all the people wept.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And the king lamented over Abner, and said, Died Abner as a fool dieth?  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Thy hands _were_ not bound, nor thy feet put into fetters: as a man falleth before wicked men, _so_ fellest thou. And all the people wept again over him.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And when all the people came to cause David to eat meat while it was yet day, David sware, saying, So do God to me, and more also, if I taste bread, or ought else, till the sun be down.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And all the people took notice _of it_, and it pleased them: as whatsoever the king did pleased all the people.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>For all the people and all Israel understood that day that it was not of the king to slay Abner the son of Ner.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And the king said unto his servants, Know ye not that there is a prince and a great man fallen this day in Israel?  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And I _am_ this day weak, though anointed king; and these men the sons of Zeruiah _be_ too hard for me: the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall reward the doer of evil according to his wickedness.  

## CHAPTER 4.

And when Saul’s son heard that Abner was dead in Hebron, his hands were feeble, and all the Israelites were troubled.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Saul’s son had two men _that were_ captains of bands: the name of the one _was_ Baanah, and the name of the other Rechab, the sons of Rimmon a Beerothite, of the children of Benjamin: (for Beeroth also was reckoned to Benjamin:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the Beerothites fled to Gittaim, and were sojourners there until this day.)  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Jonathan, Saul’s son, had a son _that was_ lame of _his_ feet. He was five years old when the tidings came of Saul and Jonathan out of Jezreel, and his nurse took him up, and fled: and it came to pass, as she made haste to flee, that he fell, and became lame. And his name _was_ Mephibosheth.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the sons of Rimmon the Beerothite, Rechab and Baanah, went, and came about the heat of the day to the house of Ish–bosheth, who lay on a bed at noon.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they came thither into the midst of the house, _as though_ they would have fetched wheat; and they smote him under the fifth _rib_: and Rechab and Baanah his brother escaped.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>For when they came into the house, he lay on his bed in his bedchamber, and they smote him, and slew him, and beheaded him, and took his head, and gat them away through the plain all night.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they brought the head of Ish–bosheth unto David to Hebron, and said to the king, Behold the head of Ish–bosheth the son of Saul thine enemy, which sought thy life; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath avenged my lord the king this day of Saul, and of his seed.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And David answered Rechab and Baanah his brother, the sons of Rimmon the Beerothite, and said unto them, _As_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> liveth, who hath redeemed my soul out of all adversity,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>When one told me, saying, Behold, Saul is dead, thinking to have brought good tidings, I took hold of him, and slew him in Ziklag, who _thought_ that I would have given him a reward for his tidings:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>How much more, when wicked men have slain a righteous person in his own house upon his bed? shall I not therefore now require his blood of your hand, and take you away from the earth?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And David commanded his young men, and they slew them, and cut off their hands and their feet, and hanged _them_ up over the pool in Hebron. But they took the head of Ish–bosheth, and buried _it_ in the sepulchre of Abner in Hebron.  

## CHAPTER 5.

Then came all the tribes of Israel to David unto Hebron, and spake, saying, Behold, we _are_ thy bone and thy flesh.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Also in time past, when Saul was king over us, thou wast he that leddest out and broughtest in Israel: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said to thee, Thou shalt feed my people Israel, and thou shalt be a captain over Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>So all the elders of Israel came to the king to Hebron; and king David made a league with them in Hebron before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and they anointed David king over Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ David _was_ thirty years old when he began to reign, _and_ he reigned forty years.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>In Hebron he reigned over Judah seven years and six months: and in Jerusalem he reigned thirty and three years over all Israel and Judah.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And the king and his men went to Jerusalem unto the Jebusites, the inhabitants of the land: which spake unto David, saying, Except thou take away the blind and the lame, thou shalt not come in hither: thinking, David cannot come in hither.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Nevertheless David took the strong hold of Zion: the same _is_ the city of David.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And David said on that day, Whosoever getteth up to the gutter, and smiteth the Jebusites, and the lame and the blind, _that are_ hated of David’s soul, _he shall be chief and captain_. Wherefore they said, The blind and the lame shall not come into the house.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>So David dwelt in the fort, and called it the city of David. And David built round about from Millo and inward.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And David went on, and grew great, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of hosts _was_ with him.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And Hiram king of Tyre sent messengers to David, and cedar trees, and carpenters, and masons: and they built David an house.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And David perceived that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had established him king over Israel, and that he had exalted his kingdom for his people Israel’s sake.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And David took _him_ more concubines and wives out of Jerusalem, after he was come from Hebron: and there were yet sons and daughters born to David.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And these _be_ the names of those that were born unto him in Jerusalem; Shammua, and Shobab, and Nathan, and Solomon,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Ibhar also, and Elishua, and Nepheg, and Japhia,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Elishama, and Eliada, and Eliphalet.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ But when the Philistines heard that they had anointed David king over Israel, all the Philistines came up to seek David; and David heard _of it_, and went down to the hold.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The Philistines also came and spread themselves in the valley of Rephaim.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And David enquired of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, saying, Shall I go up to the Philistines? wilt thou deliver them into mine hand? And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto David, Go up: for I will doubtless deliver the Philistines into thine hand.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And David came to Baal–perazim, and David smote them there, and said, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath broken forth upon mine enemies before me, as the breach of waters. Therefore he called the name of that place Baal–perazim.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And there they left their images, and David and his men burned them.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And the Philistines came up yet again, and spread themselves in the valley of Rephaim.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And when David enquired of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, he said, Thou shalt not go up; _but_ fetch a compass behind them, and come upon them over against the mulberry trees.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And let it be, when thou hearest the sound of a going in the tops of the mulberry trees, that then thou shalt bestir thyself: for then shall the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> go out before thee, to smite the host of the Philistines.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And David did so, as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had commanded him; and smote the Philistines from Geba until thou come to Gazer.  

## CHAPTER 6.

Again, David gathered together all _the_ chosen _men_ of Israel, thirty thousand.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And David arose, and went with all the people that _were_ with him from Baale of Judah, to bring up from thence the ark of God, whose name is called by the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts that dwelleth _between_ the cherubims.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And they set the ark of God upon a new cart, and brought it out of the house of Abinadab that _was_ in Gibeah: and Uzzah and Ahio, the sons of Abinadab, drave the new cart.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And they brought it out of the house of Abinadab which _was_ at Gibeah, accompanying the ark of God: and Ahio went before the ark.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And David and all the house of Israel played before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> on all manner of _instruments made of_ fir wood, even on harps, and on psalteries, and on timbrels, and on cornets, and on cymbals.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And when they came to Nachon’s threshingfloor, Uzzah put forth _his hand_ to the ark of God, and took hold of it; for the oxen shook _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the anger of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was kindled against Uzzah; and God smote him there for _his_ error; and there he died by the ark of God.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And David was displeased, because the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had made a breach upon Uzzah: and he called the name of the place Perez–uzzah to this day.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And David was afraid of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> that day, and said, How shall the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> come to me?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>So David would not remove the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> unto him into the city of David: but David carried it aside into the house of Obed–edom the Gittite.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> continued in the house of Obed–edom the Gittite three months: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> blessed Obed–edom, and all his household.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And it was told king David, saying, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath blessed the house of Obed–edom, and all that _pertaineth_ unto him, because of the ark of God. So David went and brought up the ark of God from the house of Obed–edom into the city of David with gladness.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And it was _so_, that when they that bare the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had gone six paces, he sacrificed oxen and fatlings.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And David danced before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> with all _his_ might; and David _was_ girded with a linen ephod.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So David and all the house of Israel brought up the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> with shouting, and with the sound of the trumpet.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And as the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came into the city of David, Michal Saul’s daughter looked through a window, and saw king David leaping and dancing before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; and she despised him in her heart.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And they brought in the ark of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and set it in his place, in the midst of the tabernacle that David had pitched for it: and David offered burnt offerings and peace offerings before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And as soon as David had made an end of offering burnt offerings and peace offerings, he blessed the people in the name of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And he dealt among all the people, _even_ among the whole multitude of Israel, as well to the women as men, to every one a cake of bread, and a good piece _of flesh_, and a flagon _of wine_. So all the people departed every one to his house.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Then David returned to bless his household. And Michal the daughter of Saul came out to meet David, and said, How glorious was the king of Israel to day, who uncovered himself to day in the eyes of the handmaids of his servants, as one of the vain fellows shamelessly uncovereth himself!  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And David said unto Michal, _It was_ before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, which chose me before thy father, and before all his house, to appoint me ruler over the people of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, over Israel: therefore will I play before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And I will yet be more vile than thus, and will be base in mine own sight: and of the maidservants which thou hast spoken of, of them shall I be had in honour.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Therefore Michal the daughter of Saul had no child unto the day of her death.  

## CHAPTER 7.

And it came to pass, when the king sat in his house, and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had given him rest round about from all his enemies;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>That the king said unto Nathan the prophet, See now, I dwell in an house of cedar, but the ark of God dwelleth within curtains.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Nathan said to the king, Go, do all that _is_ in thine heart; for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass that night, that the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto Nathan, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Go and tell my servant David, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Shalt thou build me an house for me to dwell in?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Whereas I have not dwelt in _any_ house since the time that I brought up the children of Israel out of Egypt, even to this day, but have walked in a tent and in a tabernacle.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>In all _the places_ wherein I have walked with all the children of Israel spake I a word with any of the tribes of Israel, whom I commanded to feed my people Israel, saying, Why build ye not me an house of cedar?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Now therefore so shalt thou say unto my servant David, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, I took thee from the sheepcote, from following the sheep, to be ruler over my people, over Israel:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And I was with thee whithersoever thou wentest, and have cut off all thine enemies out of thy sight, and have made thee a great name, like unto the name of the great _men_ that _are_ in the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Moreover I will appoint a place for my people Israel, and will plant them, that they may dwell in a place of their own, and move no more; neither shall the children of wickedness afflict them any more, as beforetime,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And as since the time that I commanded judges _to be_ over my people Israel, and have caused thee to rest from all thine enemies. Also the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> telleth thee that he will make thee an house.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And when thy days be fulfilled, and thou shalt sleep with thy fathers, I will set up thy seed after thee, which shall proceed out of thy bowels, and I will establish his kingdom.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>He shall build an house for my name, and I will stablish the throne of his kingdom for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I will be his father, and he shall be my son. If he commit iniquity, I will chasten him with the rod of men, and with the stripes of the children of men:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But my mercy shall not depart away from him, as I took _it_ from Saul, whom I put away before thee.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And thine house and thy kingdom shall be established for ever before thee: thy throne shall be established for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>According to all these words, and according to all this vision, so did Nathan speak unto David.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Then went king David in, and sat before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and he said, Who _am_ I, O Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>? and what _is_ my house, that thou hast brought me hitherto?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And this was yet a small thing in thy sight, O Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>; but thou hast spoken also of thy servant’s house for a great while to come. And _is_ this the manner of man, O Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And what can David say more unto thee? for thou, Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, knowest thy servant.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For thy word’s sake, and according to thine own heart, hast thou done all these great things, to make thy servant know _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Wherefore thou art great, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God: for _there is_ none like thee, neither _is there any_ God beside thee, according to all that we have heard with our ears.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And what one nation in the earth _is_ like thy people, _even_ like Israel, whom God went to redeem for a people to himself, and to make him a name, and to do for you great things and terrible, for thy land, before thy people, which thou redeemedst to thee from Egypt, _from_ the nations and their gods?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For thou hast confirmed to thyself thy people Israel _to be_ a people unto thee for ever: and thou, <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, art become their God.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And now, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God, the word that thou hast spoken concerning thy servant, and concerning his house, establish _it_ for ever, and do as thou hast said.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And let thy name be magnified for ever, saying, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts _is_ the God over Israel: and let the house of thy servant David be established before thee.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For thou, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> of hosts, God of Israel, hast revealed to thy servant, saying, I will build thee an house: therefore hath thy servant found in his heart to pray this prayer unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And now, O Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, thou _art_ that God, and thy words be true, and thou hast promised this goodness unto thy servant:  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Therefore now let it please thee to bless the house of thy servant, that it may continue for ever before thee: for thou, O Lord <span class="small-caps">God</span>, hast spoken _it_: and with thy blessing let the house of thy servant be blessed for ever.  

## CHAPTER 8.

And after this it came to pass, that David smote the Philistines, and subdued them: and David took Metheg–ammah out of the hand of the Philistines.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he smote Moab, and measured them with a line, casting them down to the ground; even with two lines measured he to put to death, and with one full line to keep alive. And _so_ the Moabites became David’s servants, _and_ brought gifts.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ David smote also Hadadezer, the son of Rehob, king of Zobah, as he went to recover his border at the river Euphrates.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And David took from him a thousand _chariots_, and seven hundred horsemen, and twenty thousand footmen: and David houghed all the chariot _horses_, but reserved of them _for_ an hundred chariots.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And when the Syrians of Damascus came to succour Hadadezer king of Zobah, David slew of the Syrians two and twenty thousand men.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Then David put garrisons in Syria of Damascus: and the Syrians became servants to David, _and_ brought gifts. And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> preserved David whithersoever he went.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And David took the shields of gold that were on the servants of Hadadezer, and brought them to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And from Betah, and from Berothai, cities of Hadadezer, king David took exceeding much brass.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ When Toi king of Hamath heard that David had smitten all the host of Hadadezer,  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Then Toi sent Joram his son unto king David, to salute him, and to bless him, because he had fought against Hadadezer, and smitten him: for Hadadezer had wars with Toi. And _Joram_ brought with him vessels of silver, and vessels of gold, and vessels of brass:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Which also king David did dedicate unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, with the silver and gold that he had dedicated of all nations which he subdued;  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Of Syria, and of Moab, and of the children of Ammon, and of the Philistines, and of Amalek, and of the spoil of Hadadezer, son of Rehob, king of Zobah.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And David gat _him_ a name when he returned from smiting of the Syrians in the valley of salt, _being_ eighteen thousand _men_.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And he put garrisons in Edom; throughout all Edom put he garrisons, and all they of Edom became David’s servants. And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> preserved David whithersoever he went.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And David reigned over all Israel; and David executed judgment and justice unto all his people.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Joab the son of Zeruiah _was_ over the host; and Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud _was_ recorder;  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And Zadok the son of Ahitub, and Ahimelech the son of Abiathar, _were_ the priests; and Seraiah _was_ the scribe;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Benaiah the son of Jehoiada _was over_ both the Cherethites and the Pelethites; and David’s sons were chief rulers.  

## CHAPTER 9.

And David said, Is there yet any that is left of the house of Saul, that I may shew him kindness for Jonathan’s sake?  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And _there was_ of the house of Saul a servant whose name _was_ Ziba. And when they had called him unto David, the king said unto him, _Art_ thou Ziba? And he said, Thy servant _is he_.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the king said, _Is_ there not yet any of the house of Saul, that I may shew the kindness of God unto him? And Ziba said unto the king, Jonathan hath yet a son, _which is_ lame on _his_ feet.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the king said unto him, Where _is_ he? And Ziba said unto the king, Behold, he _is_ in the house of Machir, the son of Ammiel, in Lo–debar.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ Then king David sent, and fetched him out of the house of Machir, the son of Ammiel, from Lo–debar.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Now when Mephibosheth, the son of Jonathan, the son of Saul, was come unto David, he fell on his face, and did reverence. And David said, Mephibosheth. And he answered, Behold thy servant!  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And David said unto him, Fear not: for I will surely shew thee kindness for Jonathan thy father’s sake, and will restore thee all the land of Saul thy father; and thou shalt eat bread at my table continually.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he bowed himself, and said, What _is_ thy servant, that thou shouldest look upon such a dead dog as I _am_?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Then the king called to Ziba, Saul’s servant, and said unto him, I have given unto thy master’s son all that pertained to Saul and to all his house.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Thou therefore, and thy sons, and thy servants, shall till the land for him, and thou shalt bring in _the fruits_, that thy master’s son may have food to eat: but Mephibosheth thy master’s son shall eat bread alway at my table. Now Ziba had fifteen sons and twenty servants.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then said Ziba unto the king, According to all that my lord the king hath commanded his servant, so shall thy servant do. As for Mephibosheth, _said the king_, he shall eat at my table, as one of the king’s sons.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Mephibosheth had a young son, whose name _was_ Micha. And all that dwelt in the house of Ziba _were_ servants unto Mephibosheth.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>So Mephibosheth dwelt in Jerusalem: for he did eat continually at the king’s table; and was lame on both his feet.  

## CHAPTER 10.

And it came to pass after this, that the king of the children of Ammon died, and Hanun his son reigned in his stead.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Then said David, I will shew kindness unto Hanun the son of Nahash, as his father shewed kindness unto me. And David sent to comfort him by the hand of his servants for his father. And David’s servants came into the land of the children of Ammon.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the princes of the children of Ammon said unto Hanun their lord, Thinkest thou that David doth honour thy father, that he hath sent comforters unto thee? hath not David _rather_ sent his servants unto thee, to search the city, and to spy it out, and to overthrow it?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Wherefore Hanun took David’s servants, and shaved off the one half of their beards, and cut off their garments in the middle, _even_ to their buttocks, and sent them away.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>When they told _it_ unto David, he sent to meet them, because the men were greatly ashamed: and the king said, Tarry at Jericho until your beards be grown, and _then_ return.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And when the children of Ammon saw that they stank before David, the children of Ammon sent and hired the Syrians of Beth–rehob, and the Syrians of Zoba, twenty thousand footmen, and of king Maacah a thousand men, and of Ish–tob twelve thousand men.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And when David heard of _it_, he sent Joab, and all the host of the mighty men.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the children of Ammon came out, and put the battle in array at the entering in of the gate: and the Syrians of Zoba, and of Rehob, and Ish–tob, and Maacah, _were_ by themselves in the field.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>When Joab saw that the front of the battle was against him before and behind, he chose of all the choice _men_ of Israel, and put _them_ in array against the Syrians:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the rest of the people he delivered into the hand of Abishai his brother, that he might put _them_ in array against the children of Ammon.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And he said, If the Syrians be too strong for me, then thou shalt help me: but if the children of Ammon be too strong for thee, then I will come and help thee.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Be of good courage, and let us play the men for our people, and for the cities of our God: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> do that which seemeth him good.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And Joab drew nigh, and the people that _were_ with him, unto the battle against the Syrians: and they fled before him.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And when the children of Ammon saw that the Syrians were fled, then fled they also before Abishai, and entered into the city. So Joab returned from the children of Ammon, and came to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And when the Syrians saw that they were smitten before Israel, they gathered themselves together.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Hadarezer sent, and brought out the Syrians that _were_ beyond the river: and they came to Helam; and Shobach the captain of the host of Hadarezer _went_ before them.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And when it was told David, he gathered all Israel together, and passed over Jordan, and came to Helam. And the Syrians set themselves in array against David, and fought with him.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the Syrians fled before Israel; and David slew _the men of_ seven hundred chariots of the Syrians, and forty thousand horsemen, and smote Shobach the captain of their host, who died there.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And when all the kings _that were_ servants to Hadarezer saw that they were smitten before Israel, they made peace with Israel, and served them. So the Syrians feared to help the children of Ammon any more.  

## CHAPTER 11.

And it came to pass, after the year was expired, at the time when kings go forth _to battle_, that David sent Joab, and his servants with him, and all Israel; and they destroyed the children of Ammon, and besieged Rabbah. But David tarried still at Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass in an eveningtide, that David arose from off his bed, and walked upon the roof of the king’s house: and from the roof he saw a woman washing herself; and the woman _was_ very beautiful to look upon.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And David sent and enquired after the woman. And _one_ said, _Is_ not this Bath–sheba, the daughter of Eliam, the wife of Uriah the Hittite?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And David sent messengers, and took her; and she came in unto him, and he lay with her; for she was purified from her uncleanness: and she returned unto her house.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the woman conceived, and sent and told David, and said, I _am_ with child.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ And David sent to Joab, _saying_, Send me Uriah the Hittite. And Joab sent Uriah to David.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And when Uriah was come unto him, David demanded _of him_ how Joab did, and how the people did, and how the war prospered.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And David said to Uriah, Go down to thy house, and wash thy feet. And Uriah departed out of the king’s house, and there followed him a mess _of meat_ from the king.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But Uriah slept at the door of the king’s house with all the servants of his lord, and went not down to his house.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And when they had told David, saying, Uriah went not down unto his house, David said unto Uriah, Camest thou not from _thy_ journey? why _then_ didst thou not go down unto thine house?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Uriah said unto David, The ark, and Israel, and Judah, abide in tents; and my lord Joab, and the servants of my lord, are encamped in the open fields; shall I then go into mine house, to eat and to drink, and to lie with my wife? _as_ thou livest, and _as_ thy soul liveth, I will not do this thing.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And David said to Uriah, Tarry here to day also, and to morrow I will let thee depart. So Uriah abode in Jerusalem that day, and the morrow.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And when David had called him, he did eat and drink before him; and he made him drunk: and at even he went out to lie on his bed with the servants of his lord, but went not down to his house.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass in the morning, that David wrote a letter to Joab, and sent _it_ by the hand of Uriah.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he wrote in the letter, saying, Set ye Uriah in the forefront of the hottest battle, and retire ye from him, that he may be smitten, and die.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when Joab observed the city, that he assigned Uriah unto a place where he knew that valiant men _were_.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the men of the city went out, and fought with Joab: and there fell _some_ of the people of the servants of David; and Uriah the Hittite died also.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Then Joab sent and told David all the things concerning the war;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And charged the messenger, saying, When thou hast made an end of telling the matters of the war unto the king,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And if so be that the king’s wrath arise, and he say unto thee, Wherefore approached ye so nigh unto the city when ye did fight? knew ye not that they would shoot from the wall?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Who smote Abimelech the son of Jerubbesheth? did not a woman cast a piece of a millstone upon him from the wall, that he died in Thebez? why went ye nigh the wall? then say thou, Thy servant Uriah the Hittite is dead also.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ So the messenger went, and came and shewed David all that Joab had sent him for.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the messenger said unto David, Surely the men prevailed against us, and came out unto us into the field, and we were upon them even unto the entering of the gate.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the shooters shot from off the wall upon thy servants; and _some_ of the king’s servants be dead, and thy servant Uriah the Hittite is dead also.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Then David said unto the messenger, Thus shalt thou say unto Joab, Let not this thing displease thee, for the sword devoureth one as well as another: make thy battle more strong against the city, and overthrow it: and encourage thou him.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And when the wife of Uriah heard that Uriah her husband was dead, she mourned for her husband.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And when the mourning was past, David sent and fetched her to his house, and she became his wife, and bare him a son. But the thing that David had done displeased the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  

## CHAPTER 12.

And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> sent Nathan unto David. And he came unto him, and said unto him, There were two men in one city; the one rich, and the other poor.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The rich _man_ had exceeding many flocks and herds:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But the poor _man_ had nothing, save one little ewe lamb, which he had bought and nourished up: and it grew up together with him, and with his children; it did eat of his own meat, and drank of his own cup, and lay in his bosom, and was unto him as a daughter.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And there came a traveller unto the rich man, and he spared to take of his own flock and of his own herd, to dress for the wayfaring man that was come unto him; but took the poor man’s lamb, and dressed it for the man that was come to him.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And David’s anger was greatly kindled against the man; and he said to Nathan, _As_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> liveth, the man that hath done this _thing_ shall surely die:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he shall restore the lamb fourfold, because he did this thing, and because he had no pity.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And Nathan said to David, Thou _art_ the man. Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> God of Israel, I anointed thee king over Israel, and I delivered thee out of the hand of Saul;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And I gave thee thy master’s house, and thy master’s wives into thy bosom, and gave thee the house of Israel and of Judah; and if _that had been_ too little, I would moreover have given unto thee such and such things.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Wherefore hast thou despised the commandment of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, to do evil in his sight? thou hast killed Uriah the Hittite with the sword, and hast taken his wife _to be_ thy wife, and hast slain him with the sword of the children of Ammon.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Now therefore the sword shall never depart from thine house; because thou hast despised me, and hast taken the wife of Uriah the Hittite to be thy wife.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, Behold, I will raise up evil against thee out of thine own house, and I will take thy wives before thine eyes, and give _them_ unto thy neighbour, and he shall lie with thy wives in the sight of this sun.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For thou didst _it_ secretly: but I will do this thing before all Israel, and before the sun.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And David said unto Nathan, I have sinned against the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>. And Nathan said unto David, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> also hath put away thy sin; thou shalt not die.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Howbeit, because by this deed thou hast given great occasion to the enemies of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> to blaspheme, the child also _that is_ born unto thee shall surely die.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And Nathan departed unto his house. And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> struck the child that Uriah’s wife bare unto David, and it was very sick.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>David therefore besought God for the child; and David fasted, and went in, and lay all night upon the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the elders of his house arose, _and went_ to him, to raise him up from the earth: but he would not, neither did he eat bread with them.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And it came to pass on the seventh day, that the child died. And the servants of David feared to tell him that the child was dead: for they said, Behold, while the child was yet alive, we spake unto him, and he would not hearken unto our voice: how will he then vex himself, if we tell him that the child is dead?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But when David saw that his servants whispered, David perceived that the child was dead: therefore David said unto his servants, Is the child dead? And they said, He is dead.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Then David arose from the earth, and washed, and anointed _himself_, and changed his apparel, and came into the house of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and worshipped: then he came to his own house; and when he required, they set bread before him, and he did eat.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Then said his servants unto him, What thing _is_ this that thou hast done? thou didst fast and weep for the child, _while it was_ alive; but when the child was dead, thou didst rise and eat bread.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And he said, While the child was yet alive, I fasted and wept: for I said, Who can tell _whether_ <span class="small-caps">God</span> will be gracious to me, that the child may live?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But now he is dead, wherefore should I fast? can I bring him back again? I shall go to him, but he shall not return to me.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And David comforted Bath–sheba his wife, and went in unto her, and lay with her: and she bare a son, and he called his name Solomon: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> loved him.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And he sent by the hand of Nathan the prophet; and he called his name Jedidiah, because of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And Joab fought against Rabbah of the children of Ammon, and took the royal city.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And Joab sent messengers to David, and said, I have fought against Rabbah, and have taken the city of waters.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Now therefore gather the rest of the people together, and encamp against the city, and take it: lest I take the city, and it be called after my name.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And David gathered all the people together, and went to Rabbah, and fought against it, and took it.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And he took their king’s crown from off his head, the weight whereof _was_ a talent of gold with the precious stones: and it was _set_ on David’s head. And he brought forth the spoil of the city in great abundance.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And he brought forth the people that _were_ therein, and put _them_ under saws, and under harrows of iron, and under axes of iron, and made them pass through the brickkiln: and thus did he unto all the cities of the children of Ammon. So David and all the people returned unto Jerusalem.  

## CHAPTER 13.

And it came to pass after this, that Absalom the son of David had a fair sister, whose name _was_ Tamar; and Amnon the son of David loved her.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Amnon was so vexed, that he fell sick for his sister Tamar; for she _was_ a virgin; and Amnon thought it hard for him to do any thing to her.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But Amnon had a friend, whose name _was_ Jonadab, the son of Shimeah David’s brother: and Jonadab _was_ a very subtil man.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, Why _art_ thou, _being_ the king’s son, lean from day to day? wilt thou not tell me? And Amnon said unto him, I love Tamar, my brother Absalom’s sister.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And Jonadab said unto him, Lay thee down on thy bed, and make thyself sick: and when thy father cometh to see thee, say unto him, I pray thee, let my sister Tamar come, and give me meat, and dress the meat in my sight, that I may see _it_, and eat _it_ at her hand.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ So Amnon lay down, and made himself sick: and when the king was come to see him, Amnon said unto the king, I pray thee, let Tamar my sister come, and make me a couple of cakes in my sight, that I may eat at her hand.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then David sent home to Tamar, saying, Go now to thy brother Amnon’s house, and dress him meat.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>So Tamar went to her brother Amnon’s house; and he was laid down. And she took flour, and kneaded _it_, and made cakes in his sight, and did bake the cakes.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And she took a pan, and poured _them_ out before him; but he refused to eat. And Amnon said, Have out all men from me. And they went out every man from him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Amnon said unto Tamar, Bring the meat into the chamber, that I may eat of thine hand. And Tamar took the cakes which she had made, and brought _them_ into the chamber to Amnon her brother.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And when she had brought _them_ unto him to eat, he took hold of her, and said unto her, Come lie with me, my sister.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And she answered him, Nay, my brother, do not force me; for no such thing ought to be done in Israel: do not thou this folly.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And I, whither shall I cause my shame to go? and as for thee, thou shalt be as one of the fools in Israel. Now therefore, I pray thee, speak unto the king; for he will not withhold me from thee.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Howbeit he would not hearken unto her voice: but, being stronger than she, forced her, and lay with her.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Then Amnon hated her exceedingly; so that the hatred wherewith he hated her _was_ greater than the love wherewith he had loved her. And Amnon said unto her, Arise, be gone.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And she said unto him, _There is_ no cause: this evil in sending me away _is_ greater than the other that thou didst unto me. But he would not hearken unto her.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Then he called his servant that ministered unto him, and said, Put now this _woman_ out from me, and bolt the door after her.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And _she had_ a garment of divers colours upon her: for with such robes were the king’s daughters _that were_ virgins apparelled. Then his servant brought her out, and bolted the door after her.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And Tamar put ashes on her head, and rent her garment of divers colours that _was_ on her, and laid her hand on her head, and went on crying.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Absalom her brother said unto her, Hath Amnon thy brother been with thee? but hold now thy peace, my sister: he _is_ thy brother; regard not this thing. So Tamar remained desolate in her brother Absalom’s house.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ But when king David heard of all these things, he was very wroth.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Absalom spake unto his brother Amnon neither good nor bad: for Absalom hated Amnon, because he had forced his sister Tamar.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass after two full years, that Absalom had sheepshearers in Baal–hazor, which _is_ beside Ephraim: and Absalom invited all the king’s sons.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And Absalom came to the king, and said, Behold now, thy servant hath sheepshearers; let the king, I beseech thee, and his servants go with thy servant.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the king said to Absalom, Nay, my son, let us not all now go, lest we be chargeable unto thee. And he pressed him: howbeit he would not go, but blessed him.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Then said Absalom, If not, I pray thee, let my brother Amnon go with us. And the king said unto him, Why should he go with thee?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>But Absalom pressed him, that he let Amnon and all the king’s sons go with him.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ Now Absalom had commanded his servants, saying, Mark ye now when Amnon’s heart is merry with wine, and when I say unto you, Smite Amnon; then kill him, fear not: have not I commanded you? be courageous, and be valiant.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And the servants of Absalom did unto Amnon as Absalom had commanded. Then all the king’s sons arose, and every man gat him up upon his mule, and fled.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, while they were in the way, that tidings came to David, saying, Absalom hath slain all the king’s sons, and there is not one of them left.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Then the king arose, and tare his garments, and lay on the earth; and all his servants stood by with their clothes rent.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And Jonadab, the son of Shimeah David’s brother, answered and said, Let not my lord suppose _that_ they have slain all the young men the king’s sons; for Amnon only is dead: for by the appointment of Absalom this hath been determined from the day that he forced his sister Tamar.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Now therefore let not my lord the king take the thing to his heart, to think that all the king’s sons are dead: for Amnon only is dead.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>But Absalom fled. And the young man that kept the watch lifted up his eyes, and looked, and, behold, there came much people by the way of the hill side behind him.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And Jonadab said unto the king, Behold, the king’s sons come: as thy servant said, so it is.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, as soon as he had made an end of speaking, that, behold, the king’s sons came, and lifted up their voice and wept: and the king also and all his servants wept very sore.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>¶ But Absalom fled, and went to Talmai, the son of Ammihud, king of Geshur. And _David_ mourned for his son every day.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>So Absalom fled, and went to Geshur, and was there three years.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And _the soul of_ king David longed to go forth unto Absalom: for he was comforted concerning Amnon, seeing he was dead.  

## CHAPTER 14.

Now Joab the son of Zeruiah perceived that the king’s heart _was_ toward Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Joab sent to Tekoah, and fetched thence a wise woman, and said unto her, I pray thee, feign thyself to be a mourner, and put on now mourning apparel, and anoint not thyself with oil, but be as a woman that had a long time mourned for the dead:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And come to the king, and speak on this manner unto him. So Joab put the words in her mouth.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And when the woman of Tekoah spake to the king, she fell on her face to the ground, and did obeisance, and said, Help, O king.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the king said unto her, What aileth thee? And she answered, I _am_ indeed a widow woman, and mine husband is dead.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And thy handmaid had two sons, and they two strove together in the field, and _there was_ none to part them, but the one smote the other, and slew him.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And, behold, the whole family is risen against thine handmaid, and they said, Deliver him that smote his brother, that we may kill him, for the life of his brother whom he slew; and we will destroy the heir also: and so they shall quench my coal which is left, and shall not leave to my husband _neither_ name nor remainder upon the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the king said unto the woman, Go to thine house, and I will give charge concerning thee.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the woman of Tekoah said unto the king, My lord, O king, the iniquity _be_ on me, and on my father’s house: and the king and his throne _be_ guiltless.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the king said, Whosoever saith _ought_ unto thee, bring him to me, and he shall not touch thee any more.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Then said she, I pray thee, let the king remember the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, that thou wouldest not suffer the revengers of blood to destroy any more, lest they destroy my son. And he said, _As_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> liveth, there shall not one hair of thy son fall to the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Then the woman said, Let thine handmaid, I pray thee, speak _one_ word unto my lord the king. And he said, Say on.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the woman said, Wherefore then hast thou thought such a thing against the people of God? for the king doth speak this thing as one which is faulty, in that the king doth not fetch home again his banished.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For we must needs die, and _are_ as water spilt on the ground, which cannot be gathered up again; neither doth God respect _any_ person: yet doth he devise means, that his banished be not expelled from him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Now therefore that I am come to speak of this thing unto my lord the king, _it is_ because the people have made me afraid: and thy handmaid said, I will now speak unto the king; it may be that the king will perform the request of his handmaid.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For the king will hear, to deliver his handmaid out of the hand of the man _that would_ destroy me and my son together out of the inheritance of God.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Then thine handmaid said, The word of my lord the king shall now be comfortable: for as an angel of God, so _is_ my lord the king to discern good and bad: therefore the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God will be with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Then the king answered and said unto the woman, Hide not from me, I pray thee, the thing that I shall ask thee. And the woman said, Let my lord the king now speak.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the king said, _Is not_ the hand of Joab with thee in all this? And the woman answered and said, _As_ thy soul liveth, my lord the king, none can turn to the right hand or to the left from ought that my lord the king hath spoken: for thy servant Joab, he bade me, and he put all these words in the mouth of thine handmaid:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>To fetch about this form of speech hath thy servant Joab done this thing: and my lord _is_ wise, according to the wisdom of an angel of God, to know all _things_ that _are_ in the earth.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ And the king said unto Joab, Behold now, I have done this thing: go therefore, bring the young man Absalom again.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Joab fell to the ground on his face, and bowed himself, and thanked the king: and Joab said, To day thy servant knoweth that I have found grace in thy sight, my lord, O king, in that the king hath fulfilled the request of his servant.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>So Joab arose and went to Geshur, and brought Absalom to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the king said, Let him turn to his own house, and let him not see my face. So Absalom returned to his own house, and saw not the king’s face.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ But in all Israel there was none to be so much praised as Absalom for his beauty: from the sole of his foot even to the crown of his head there was no blemish in him.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And when he polled his head, (for it was at every year’s end that he polled _it_: because _the hair_ was heavy on him, therefore he polled it:) he weighed the hair of his head at two hundred shekels after the king’s weight.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And unto Absalom there were born three sons, and one daughter, whose name _was_ Tamar: she was a woman of a fair countenance.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ So Absalom dwelt two full years in Jerusalem, and saw not the king’s face.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Therefore Absalom sent for Joab, to have sent him to the king; but he would not come to him: and when he sent again the second time, he would not come.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Therefore he said unto his servants, See, Joab’s field is near mine, and he hath barley there; go and set it on fire. And Absalom’s servants set the field on fire.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Then Joab arose, and came to Absalom unto _his_ house, and said unto him, Wherefore have thy servants set my field on fire?  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And Absalom answered Joab, Behold, I sent unto thee, saying, Come hither, that I may send thee to the king, to say, Wherefore am I come from Geshur? _it had been_ good for me _to have been_ there still: now therefore let me see the king’s face; and if there be _any_ iniquity in me, let him kill me.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>So Joab came to the king, and told him: and when he had called for Absalom, he came to the king, and bowed himself on his face to the ground before the king: and the king kissed Absalom.  

## CHAPTER 15.

And it came to pass after this, that Absalom prepared him chariots and horses, and fifty men to run before him.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Absalom rose up early, and stood beside the way of the gate: and it was _so_, that when any man that had a controversy came to the king for judgment, then Absalom called unto him, and said, Of what city _art_ thou? And he said, Thy servant _is_ of one of the tribes of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Absalom said unto him, See, thy matters _are_ good and right; but _there is_ no man _deputed_ of the king to hear thee.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Absalom said moreover, Oh that I were made judge in the land, that every man which hath any suit or cause might come unto me, and I would do him justice!  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And it was _so_, that when any man came nigh _to him_ to do him obeisance, he put forth his hand, and took him, and kissed him.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And on this manner did Absalom to all Israel that came to the king for judgment: so Absalom stole the hearts of the men of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass after forty years, that Absalom said unto the king, I pray thee, let me go and pay my vow, which I have vowed unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, in Hebron.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For thy servant vowed a vow while I abode at Geshur in Syria, saying, If the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> shall bring me again indeed to Jerusalem, then I will serve the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And the king said unto him, Go in peace. So he arose, and went to Hebron.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ But Absalom sent spies throughout all the tribes of Israel, saying, As soon as ye hear the sound of the trumpet, then ye shall say, Absalom reigneth in Hebron.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And with Absalom went two hundred men out of Jerusalem, _that were_ called; and they went in their simplicity, and they knew not any thing.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Absalom sent for Ahithophel the Gilonite, David’s counsellor, from his city, _even_ from Giloh, while he offered sacrifices. And the conspiracy was strong; for the people increased continually with Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And there came a messenger to David, saying, The hearts of the men of Israel are after Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And David said unto all his servants that _were_ with him at Jerusalem, Arise, and let us flee; for we shall not _else_ escape from Absalom: make speed to depart, lest he overtake us suddenly, and bring evil upon us, and smite the city with the edge of the sword.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And the king’s servants said unto the king, Behold, thy servants _are ready to do_ whatsoever my lord the king shall appoint.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the king went forth, and all his household after him. And the king left ten women, _which were_ concubines, to keep the house.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And the king went forth, and all the people after him, and tarried in a place that was far off.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And all his servants passed on beside him; and all the Cherethites, and all the Pelethites, and all the Gittites, six hundred men which came after him from Gath, passed on before the king.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Then said the king to Ittai the Gittite, Wherefore goest thou also with us? return to thy place, and abide with the king: for thou _art_ a stranger, and also an exile.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Whereas thou camest _but_ yesterday, should I this day make thee go up and down with us? seeing I go whither I may, return thou, and take back thy brethren: mercy and truth _be_ with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Ittai answered the king, and said, _As_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> liveth, and _as_ my lord the king liveth, surely in what place my lord the king shall be, whether in death or life, even there also will thy servant be.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And David said to Ittai, Go and pass over. And Ittai the Gittite passed over, and all his men, and all the little ones that _were_ with him.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And all the country wept with a loud voice, and all the people passed over: the king also himself passed over the brook Kidron, and all the people passed over, toward the way of the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And lo Zadok also, and all the Levites _were_ with him, bearing the ark of the covenant of God: and they set down the ark of God; and Abiathar went up, until all the people had done passing out of the city.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the king said unto Zadok, Carry back the ark of God into the city: if I shall find favour in the eyes of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, he will bring me again, and shew me _both_ it, and his habitation:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But if he thus say, I have no delight in thee; behold, _here am_ I, let him do to me as seemeth good unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>The king said also unto Zadok the priest, _Art not_ thou a seer? return into the city in peace, and your two sons with you, Ahimaaz thy son, and Jonathan the son of Abiathar.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>See, I will tarry in the plain of the wilderness, until there come word from you to certify me.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Zadok therefore and Abiathar carried the ark of God again to Jerusalem: and they tarried there.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>¶ And David went up by the ascent of _mount_ Olivet, and wept as he went up, and had his head covered, and he went barefoot: and all the people that _was_ with him covered every man his head, and they went up, weeping as they went up.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And _one_ told David, saying, Ahithophel _is_ among the conspirators with Absalom. And David said, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I pray thee, turn the counsel of Ahithophel into foolishness.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, that _when_ David was come to the top _of the mount_, where he worshipped God, behold, Hushai the Archite came to meet him with his coat rent, and earth upon his head:  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Unto whom David said, If thou passest on with me, then thou shalt be a burden unto me:  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>But if thou return to the city, and say unto Absalom, I will be thy servant, O king; _as_ I _have been_ thy father’s servant hitherto, so _will_ I now also _be_ thy servant: then mayest thou for me defeat the counsel of Ahithophel.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And _hast thou_ not there with thee Zadok and Abiathar the priests? therefore it shall be, _that_ what thing soever thou shalt hear out of the king’s house, thou shalt tell _it_ to Zadok and Abiathar the priests.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Behold, _they have_ there with them their two sons, Ahimaaz Zadok’s _son_, and Jonathan Abiathar’s _son_; and by them ye shall send unto me every thing that ye can hear.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>So Hushai David’s friend came into the city, and Absalom came into Jerusalem.  

## CHAPTER 16.

And when David was a little past the top _of the hill_, behold, Ziba the servant of Mephibosheth met him, with a couple of asses saddled, and upon them two hundred _loaves_ of bread, and an hundred bunches of raisins, and an hundred of summer fruits, and a bottle of wine.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the king said unto Ziba, What meanest thou by these? And Ziba said, The asses _be_ for the king’s household to ride on; and the bread and summer fruit for the young men to eat; and the wine, that such as be faint in the wilderness may drink.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the king said, And where _is_ thy master’s son? And Ziba said unto the king, Behold, he abideth at Jerusalem: for he said, To day shall the house of Israel restore me the kingdom of my father.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then said the king to Ziba, Behold, thine _are_ all that _pertained_ unto Mephibosheth. And Ziba said, I humbly beseech thee _that_ I may find grace in thy sight, my lord, O king.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And when king David came to Bahurim, behold, thence came out a man of the family of the house of Saul, whose name _was_ Shimei, the son of Gera: he came forth, and cursed still as he came.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he cast stones at David, and at all the servants of king David: and all the people and all the mighty men _were_ on his right hand and on his left.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And thus said Shimei when he cursed, Come out, come out, thou bloody man, and thou man of Belial:  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath returned upon thee all the blood of the house of Saul, in whose stead thou hast reigned; and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath delivered the kingdom into the hand of Absalom thy son: and, behold, thou _art taken_ in thy mischief, because thou _art_ a bloody man.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ Then said Abishai the son of Zeruiah unto the king, Why should this dead dog curse my lord the king? let me go over, I pray thee, and take off his head.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the king said, What have I to do with you, ye sons of Zeruiah? so let him curse, because the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath said unto him, Curse David. Who shall then say, Wherefore hast thou done so?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And David said to Abishai, and to all his servants, Behold, my son, which came forth of my bowels, seeketh my life: how much more now _may this_ Benjamite _do it_? let him alone, and let him curse; for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath bidden him.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>It may be that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will look on mine affliction, and that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will requite me good for his cursing this day.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And as David and his men went by the way, Shimei went along on the hill’s side over against him, and cursed as he went, and threw stones at him, and cast dust.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the king, and all the people that _were_ with him, came weary, and refreshed themselves there.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And Absalom, and all the people the men of Israel, came to Jerusalem, and Ahithophel with him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when Hushai the Archite, David’s friend, was come unto Absalom, that Hushai said unto Absalom, God save the king, God save the king.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And Absalom said to Hushai, _Is_ this thy kindness to thy friend? why wentest thou not with thy friend?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Hushai said unto Absalom, Nay; but whom the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and this people, and all the men of Israel, choose, his will I be, and with him will I abide.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And again, whom should I serve? _should I_ not _serve_ in the presence of his son? as I have served in thy father’s presence, so will I be in thy presence.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ Then said Absalom to Ahithophel, Give counsel among you what we shall do.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Ahithophel said unto Absalom, Go in unto thy father’s concubines, which he hath left to keep the house; and all Israel shall hear that thou art abhorred of thy father: then shall the hands of all that _are_ with thee be strong.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>So they spread Absalom a tent upon the top of the house; and Absalom went in unto his father’s concubines in the sight of all Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the counsel of Ahithophel, which he counselled in those days, _was_ as if a man had enquired at the oracle of God: so _was_ all the counsel of Ahithophel both with David and with Absalom.  

## CHAPTER 17.

Moreover Ahithophel said unto Absalom, Let me now choose out twelve thousand men, and I will arise and pursue after David this night:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And I will come upon him while he _is_ weary and weak handed, and will make him afraid: and all the people that _are_ with him shall flee; and I will smite the king only:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And I will bring back all the people unto thee: the man whom thou seekest _is_ as if all returned: _so_ all the people shall be in peace.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the saying pleased Absalom well, and all the elders of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Then said Absalom, Call now Hushai the Archite also, and let us hear likewise what he saith.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And when Hushai was come to Absalom, Absalom spake unto him, saying, Ahithophel hath spoken after this manner: shall we do _after_ his saying? if not; speak thou.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And Hushai said unto Absalom, The counsel that Ahithophel hath given _is_ not good at this time.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For, said Hushai, thou knowest thy father and his men, that they _be_ mighty men, and they _be_ chafed in their minds, as a bear robbed of her whelps in the field: and thy father _is_ a man of war, and will not lodge with the people.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Behold, he is hid now in some pit, or in some _other_ place: and it will come to pass, when some of them be overthrown at the first, that whosoever heareth it will say, There is a slaughter among the people that follow Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he also _that is_ valiant, whose heart _is_ as the heart of a lion, shall utterly melt: for all Israel knoweth that thy father _is_ a mighty man, and _they_ which _be_ with him _are_ valiant men.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Therefore I counsel that all Israel be generally gathered unto thee, from Dan even to Beer–sheba, as the sand that _is_ by the sea for multitude; and that thou go to battle in thine own person.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>So shall we come upon him in some place where he shall be found, and we will light upon him as the dew falleth on the ground: and of him and of all the men that _are_ with him there shall not be left so much as one.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Moreover, if he be gotten into a city, then shall all Israel bring ropes to that city, and we will draw it into the river, until there be not one small stone found there.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And Absalom and all the men of Israel said, The counsel of Hushai the Archite _is_ better than the counsel of Ahithophel. For the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had appointed to defeat the good counsel of Ahithophel, to the intent that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> might bring evil upon Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Then said Hushai unto Zadok and to Abiathar the priests, Thus and thus did Ahithophel counsel Absalom and the elders of Israel; and thus and thus have I counselled.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Now therefore send quickly, and tell David, saying, Lodge not this night in the plains of the wilderness, but speedily pass over; lest the king be swallowed up, and all the people that _are_ with him.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Now Jonathan and Ahimaaz stayed by En–rogel; for they might not be seen to come into the city: and a wench went and told them; and they went and told king David.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Nevertheless a lad saw them, and told Absalom: but they went both of them away quickly, and came to a man’s house in Bahurim, which had a well in his court; whither they went down.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the woman took and spread a covering over the well’s mouth, and spread ground corn thereon; and the thing was not known.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And when Absalom’s servants came to the woman to the house, they said, Where _is_ Ahimaaz and Jonathan? And the woman said unto them, They be gone over the brook of water. And when they had sought and could not find _them_, they returned to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, after they were departed, that they came up out of the well, and went and told king David, and said unto David, Arise, and pass quickly over the water: for thus hath Ahithophel counselled against you.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Then David arose, and all the people that _were_ with him, and they passed over Jordan: by the morning light there lacked not one of them that was not gone over Jordan.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And when Ahithophel saw that his counsel was not followed, he saddled _his_ ass, and arose, and gat him home to his house, to his city, and put his household in order, and hanged himself, and died, and was buried in the sepulchre of his father.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Then David came to Mahanaim. And Absalom passed over Jordan, he and all the men of Israel with him.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And Absalom made Amasa captain of the host instead of Joab: which Amasa _was_ a man’s son, whose name _was_ Ithra an Israelite, that went in to Abigail the daughter of Nahash, sister to Zeruiah Joab’s mother.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>So Israel and Absalom pitched in the land of Gilead.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, when David was come to Mahanaim, that Shobi the son of Nahash of Rabbah of the children of Ammon, and Machir the son of Ammiel of Lo–debar, and Barzillai the Gileadite of Rogelim,  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Brought beds, and basons, and earthen vessels, and wheat, and barley, and flour, and parched _corn_, and beans, and lentiles, and parched _pulse_,  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And honey, and butter, and sheep, and cheese of kine, for David, and for the people that _were_ with him, to eat: for they said, The people _is_ hungry, and weary, and thirsty, in the wilderness.  

## CHAPTER 18.

And David numbered the people that _were_ with him, and set captains of thousands and captains of hundreds over them.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And David sent forth a third part of the people under the hand of Joab, and a third part under the hand of Abishai the son of Zeruiah, Joab’s brother, and a third part under the hand of Ittai the Gittite. And the king said unto the people, I will surely go forth with you myself also.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>But the people answered, Thou shalt not go forth: for if we flee away, they will not care for us; neither if half of us die, will they care for us: but now _thou art_ worth ten thousand of us: therefore now _it is_ better that thou succour us out of the city.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the king said unto them, What seemeth you best I will do. And the king stood by the gate side, and all the people came out by hundreds and by thousands.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the king commanded Joab and Abishai and Ittai, saying, _Deal_ gently for my sake with the young man, _even_ with Absalom. And all the people heard when the king gave all the captains charge concerning Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ So the people went out into the field against Israel: and the battle was in the wood of Ephraim;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Where the people of Israel were slain before the servants of David, and there was there a great slaughter that day of twenty thousand _men_.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For the battle was there scattered over the face of all the country: and the wood devoured more people that day than the sword devoured.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And Absalom met the servants of David. And Absalom rode upon a mule, and the mule went under the thick boughs of a great oak, and his head caught hold of the oak, and he was taken up between the heaven and the earth; and the mule that _was_ under him went away.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And a certain man saw _it_, and told Joab, and said, Behold, I saw Absalom hanged in an oak.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Joab said unto the man that told him, And, behold, thou sawest _him_, and why didst thou not smite him there to the ground? and I would have given thee ten _shekels_ of silver, and a girdle.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the man said unto Joab, Though I should receive a thousand _shekels_ of silver in mine hand, _yet_ would I not put forth mine hand against the king’s son: for in our hearing the king charged thee and Abishai and Ittai, saying, Beware that none _touch_ the young man Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Otherwise I should have wrought falsehood against mine own life: for there is no matter hid from the king, and thou thyself wouldest have set thyself against _me_.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Then said Joab, I may not tarry thus with thee. And he took three darts in his hand, and thrust them through the heart of Absalom, while he _was_ yet alive in the midst of the oak.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And ten young men that bare Joab’s armour compassed about and smote Absalom, and slew him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Joab blew the trumpet, and the people returned from pursuing after Israel: for Joab held back the people.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And they took Absalom, and cast him into a great pit in the wood, and laid a very great heap of stones upon him: and all Israel fled every one to his tent.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Now Absalom in his lifetime had taken and reared up for himself a pillar, which _is_ in the king’s dale: for he said, I have no son to keep my name in remembrance: and he called the pillar after his own name: and it is called unto this day, Absalom’s place.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Then said Ahimaaz the son of Zadok, Let me now run, and bear the king tidings, how that the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath avenged him of his enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Joab said unto him, Thou shalt not bear tidings this day, but thou shalt bear tidings another day: but this day thou shalt bear no tidings, because the king’s son is dead.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Then said Joab to Cushi, Go tell the king what thou hast seen. And Cushi bowed himself unto Joab, and ran.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Then said Ahimaaz the son of Zadok yet again to Joab, But howsoever, let me, I pray thee, also run after Cushi. And Joab said, Wherefore wilt thou run, my son, seeing that thou hast no tidings ready?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But howsoever, _said he_, let me run. And he said unto him, Run. Then Ahimaaz ran by the way of the plain, and overran Cushi.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And David sat between the two gates: and the watchman went up to the roof over the gate unto the wall, and lifted up his eyes, and looked, and behold a man running alone.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And the watchman cried, and told the king. And the king said, If he _be_ alone, _there is_ tidings in his mouth. And he came apace, and drew near.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And the watchman saw another man running: and the watchman called unto the porter, and said, Behold _another_ man running alone. And the king said, He also bringeth tidings.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And the watchman said, Me thinketh the running of the foremost is like the running of Ahimaaz the son of Zadok. And the king said, He _is_ a good man, and cometh with good tidings.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And Ahimaaz called, and said unto the king, All is well. And he fell down to the earth upon his face before the king, and said, Blessed _be_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God, which hath delivered up the men that lifted up their hand against my lord the king.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And the king said, Is the young man Absalom safe? And Ahimaaz answered, When Joab sent the king’s servant, and _me_ thy servant, I saw a great tumult, but I knew not what _it was_.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the king said _unto him_, Turn aside, _and_ stand here. And he turned aside, and stood still.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And, behold, Cushi came; and Cushi said, Tidings, my lord the king: for the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath avenged thee this day of all them that rose up against thee.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And the king said unto Cushi, Is the young man Absalom safe? And Cushi answered, The enemies of my lord the king, and all that rise against thee to do _thee_ hurt, be as _that_ young man _is_.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ And the king was much moved, and went up to the chamber over the gate, and wept: and as he went, thus he said, O my son Absalom, my son, my son Absalom! would God I had died for thee, O Absalom, my son, my son!  

## CHAPTER 19.

And it was told Joab, Behold, the king weepeth and mourneth for Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the victory that day was _turned_ into mourning unto all the people: for the people heard say that day how the king was grieved for his son.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the people gat them by stealth that day into the city, as people being ashamed steal away when they flee in battle.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>But the king covered his face, and the king cried with a loud voice, O my son Absalom, O Absalom, my son, my son!  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And Joab came into the house to the king, and said, Thou hast shamed this day the faces of all thy servants, which this day have saved thy life, and the lives of thy sons and of thy daughters, and the lives of thy wives, and the lives of thy concubines;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>In that thou lovest thine enemies, and hatest thy friends. For thou hast declared this day, that thou regardest neither princes nor servants: for this day I perceive, that if Absalom had lived, and all we had died this day, then it had pleased thee well.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Now therefore arise, go forth, and speak comfortably unto thy servants: for I swear by the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, if thou go not forth, there will not tarry one with thee this night: and that will be worse unto thee than all the evil that befell thee from thy youth until now.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Then the king arose, and sat in the gate. And they told unto all the people, saying, Behold, the king doth sit in the gate. And all the people came before the king: for Israel had fled every man to his tent.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>¶ And all the people were at strife throughout all the tribes of Israel, saying, The king saved us out of the hand of our enemies, and he delivered us out of the hand of the Philistines; and now he is fled out of the land for Absalom.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And Absalom, whom we anointed over us, is dead in battle. Now therefore why speak ye not a word of bringing the king back?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And king David sent to Zadok and to Abiathar the priests, saying, Speak unto the elders of Judah, saying, Why are ye the last to bring the king back to his house? seeing the speech of all Israel is come to the king, _even_ to his house.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Ye _are_ my brethren, ye _are_ my bones and my flesh: wherefore then are ye the last to bring back the king?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And say ye to Amasa, _Art_ thou not of my bone, and of my flesh? God do so to me, and more also, if thou be not captain of the host before me continually in the room of Joab.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he bowed the heart of all the men of Judah, even as _the heart of_ one man; so that they sent _this word_ unto the king, Return thou, and all thy servants.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So the king returned, and came to Jordan. And Judah came to Gilgal, to go to meet the king, to conduct the king over Jordan.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And Shimei the son of Gera, a Benjamite, which _was_ of Bahurim, hasted and came down with the men of Judah to meet king David.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And _there were_ a thousand men of Benjamin with him, and Ziba the servant of the house of Saul, and his fifteen sons and his twenty servants with him; and they went over Jordan before the king.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And there went over a ferry boat to carry over the king’s household, and to do what he thought good. And Shimei the son of Gera fell down before the king, as he was come over Jordan;  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And said unto the king, Let not my lord impute iniquity unto me, neither do thou remember that which thy servant did perversely the day that my lord the king went out of Jerusalem, that the king should take it to his heart.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>For thy servant doth know that I have sinned: therefore, behold, I am come the first this day of all the house of Joseph to go down to meet my lord the king.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But Abishai the son of Zeruiah answered and said, Shall not Shimei be put to death for this, because he cursed the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> anointed?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And David said, What have I to do with you, ye sons of Zeruiah, that ye should this day be adversaries unto me? shall there any man be put to death this day in Israel? for do not I know that I _am_ this day king over Israel?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Therefore the king said unto Shimei, Thou shalt not die. And the king sware unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And Mephibosheth the son of Saul came down to meet the king, and had neither dressed his feet, nor trimmed his beard, nor washed his clothes, from the day the king departed until the day he came _again_ in peace.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when he was come to Jerusalem to meet the king, that the king said unto him, Wherefore wentest not thou with me, Mephibosheth?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And he answered, My lord, O king, my servant deceived me: for thy servant said, I will saddle me an ass, that I may ride thereon, and go to the king; because thy servant _is_ lame.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And he hath slandered thy servant unto my lord the king; but my lord the king _is_ as an angel of God: do therefore _what is_ good in thine eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For all _of_ my father’s house were but dead men before my lord the king: yet didst thou set thy servant among them that did eat at thine own table. What right therefore have I yet to cry any more unto the king?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And the king said unto him, Why speakest thou any more of thy matters? I have said, Thou and Ziba divide the land.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Mephibosheth said unto the king, Yea, let him take all, forasmuch as my lord the king is come again in peace unto his own house.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And Barzillai the Gileadite came down from Rogelim, and went over Jordan with the king, to conduct him over Jordan.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Now Barzillai was a very aged man, _even_ fourscore years old: and he had provided the king of sustenance while he lay at Mahanaim; for he _was_ a very great man.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And the king said unto Barzillai, Come thou over with me, and I will feed thee with me in Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And Barzillai said unto the king, How long have I to live, that I should go up with the king unto Jerusalem?  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>I _am_ this day fourscore years old: _and_ can I discern between good and evil? can thy servant taste what I eat or what I drink? can I hear any more the voice of singing men and singing women? wherefore then should thy servant be yet a burden unto my lord the king?  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Thy servant will go a little way over Jordan with the king: and why should the king recompense it me with such a reward?  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Let thy servant, I pray thee, turn back again, that I may die in mine own city, _and be buried_ by the grave of my father and of my mother. But behold thy servant Chimham; let him go over with my lord the king; and do to him what shall seem good unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And the king answered, Chimham shall go over with me, and I will do to him that which shall seem good unto thee: and whatsoever thou shalt require of me, _that_ will I do for thee.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And all the people went over Jordan. And when the king was come over, the king kissed Barzillai, and blessed him; and he returned unto his own place.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>¶ Then the king went on to Gilgal, and Chimham went on with him: and all the people of Judah conducted the king, and also half the people of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, all the men of Israel came to the king, and said unto the king, Why have our brethren the men of Judah stolen thee away, and have brought the king, and his household, and all David’s men with him, over Jordan?  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And all the men of Judah answered the men of Israel, Because the king _is_ near of kin to us: wherefore then be ye angry for this matter? have we eaten at all of the king’s _cost_? or hath he given us any gift?  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And the men of Israel answered the men of Judah, and said, We have ten parts in the king, and we have also more _right_ in David than ye: why then did ye despise us, that our advice should not be first had in bringing back our king? And the words of the men of Judah were fiercer than the words of the men of Israel.  

## CHAPTER 20.

And there happened to be there a man of Belial, whose name _was_ Sheba, the son of Bichri, a Benjamite: and he blew a trumpet, and said, We have no part in David, neither have we inheritance in the son of Jesse: every man to his tents, O Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>So every man of Israel went up from after David, _and_ followed Sheba the son of Bichri: but the men of Judah clave unto their king, from Jordan even to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And David came to his house at Jerusalem; and the king took the ten women _his_ concubines, whom he had left to keep the house, and put them in ward, and fed them, but went not in unto them. So they were shut up unto the day of their death, living in widowhood.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ Then said the king to Amasa, Assemble me the men of Judah within three days, and be thou here present.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>So Amasa went to assemble _the men of_ Judah: but he tarried longer than the set time which he had appointed him.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And David said to Abishai, Now shall Sheba the son of Bichri do us more harm than _did_ Absalom: take thou thy lord’s servants, and pursue after him, lest he get him fenced cities, and escape us.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And there went out after him Joab’s men, and the Cherethites, and the Pelethites, and all the mighty men: and they went out of Jerusalem, to pursue after Sheba the son of Bichri.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>When they _were_ at the great stone which _is_ in Gibeon, Amasa went before them. And Joab’s garment that he had put on was girded unto him, and upon it a girdle _with_ a sword fastened upon his loins in the sheath thereof; and as he went forth it fell out.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Joab said to Amasa, _Art_ thou in health, my brother? And Joab took Amasa by the beard with the right hand to kiss him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But Amasa took no heed to the sword that _was_ in Joab’s hand: so he smote him therewith in the fifth _rib_, and shed out his bowels to the ground, and struck him not again; and he died. So Joab and Abishai his brother pursued after Sheba the son of Bichri.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And one of Joab’s men stood by him, and said, He that favoureth Joab, and he that _is_ for David, _let him go_ after Joab.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Amasa wallowed in blood in the midst of the highway. And when the man saw that all the people stood still, he removed Amasa out of the highway into the field, and cast a cloth upon him, when he saw that every one that came by him stood still.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>When he was removed out of the highway, all the people went on after Joab, to pursue after Sheba the son of Bichri.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And he went through all the tribes of Israel unto Abel, and to Beth–maachah, and all the Berites: and they were gathered together, and went also after him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And they came and besieged him in Abel of Beth–maachah, and they cast up a bank against the city, and it stood in the trench: and all the people that _were_ with Joab battered the wall, to throw it down.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ Then cried a wise woman out of the city, Hear, hear; say, I pray you, unto Joab, Come near hither, that I may speak with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And when he was come near unto her, the woman said, _Art_ thou Joab? And he answered, I _am he_. Then she said unto him, Hear the words of thine handmaid. And he answered, I do hear.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Then she spake, saying, They were wont to speak in old time, saying, They shall surely ask _counsel_ at Abel: and so they ended _the matter_.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>I _am one of them that are_ peaceable _and_ faithful in Israel: thou seekest to destroy a city and a mother in Israel: why wilt thou swallow up the inheritance of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Joab answered and said, Far be it, far be it from me, that I should swallow up or destroy.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>The matter _is_ not so: but a man of mount Ephraim, Sheba the son of Bichri by name, hath lifted up his hand against the king, _even_ against David: deliver him only, and I will depart from the city. And the woman said unto Joab, Behold, his head shall be thrown to thee over the wall.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Then the woman went unto all the people in her wisdom. And they cut off the head of Sheba the son of Bichri, and cast _it_ out to Joab. And he blew a trumpet, and they retired from the city, every man to his tent. And Joab returned to Jerusalem unto the king.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ Now Joab _was_ over all the host of Israel: and Benaiah the son of Jehoiada _was_ over the Cherethites and over the Pelethites:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And Adoram _was_ over the tribute: and Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud _was_ recorder:  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And Sheva _was_ scribe: and Zadok and Abiathar _were_ the priests:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And Ira also the Jairite was a chief ruler about David.  

## CHAPTER 21.

Then there was a famine in the days of David three years, year after year; and David enquired of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>. And the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> answered, _It is_ for Saul, and for _his_ bloody house, because he slew the Gibeonites.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the king called the Gibeonites, and said unto them; (now the Gibeonites _were_ not of the children of Israel, but of the remnant of the Amorites; and the children of Israel had sworn unto them: and Saul sought to slay them in his zeal to the children of Israel and Judah.)  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Wherefore David said unto the Gibeonites, What shall I do for you? and wherewith shall I make the atonement, that ye may bless the inheritance of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And the Gibeonites said unto him, We will have no silver nor gold of Saul, nor of his house; neither for us shalt thou kill any man in Israel. And he said, What ye shall say, _that_ will I do for you.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they answered the king, The man that consumed us, and that devised against us _that_ we should be destroyed from remaining in any of the coasts of Israel,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Let seven men of his sons be delivered unto us, and we will hang them up unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in Gibeah of Saul, _whom_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> did choose. And the king said, I will give _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But the king spared Mephibosheth, the son of Jonathan the son of Saul, because of the <span class="small-caps">Lord’s</span> oath that _was_ between them, between David and Jonathan the son of Saul.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But the king took the two sons of Rizpah the daughter of Aiah, whom she bare unto Saul, Armoni and Mephibosheth; and the five sons of Michal the daughter of Saul, whom she brought up for Adriel the son of Barzillai the Meholathite:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he delivered them into the hands of the Gibeonites, and they hanged them in the hill before the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and they fell _all_ seven together, and were put to death in the days of harvest, in the first _days_, in the beginning of barley harvest.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And Rizpah the daughter of Aiah took sackcloth, and spread it for her upon the rock, from the beginning of harvest until water dropped upon them out of heaven, and suffered neither the birds of the air to rest on them by day, nor the beasts of the field by night.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And it was told David what Rizpah the daughter of Aiah, the concubine of Saul, had done.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And David went and took the bones of Saul and the bones of Jonathan his son from the men of Jabesh–gilead, which had stolen them from the street of Beth–shan, where the Philistines had hanged them, when the Philistines had slain Saul in Gilboa:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And he brought up from thence the bones of Saul and the bones of Jonathan his son; and they gathered the bones of them that were hanged.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the bones of Saul and Jonathan his son buried they in the country of Benjamin in Zelah, in the sepulchre of Kish his father: and they performed all that the king commanded. And after that God was intreated for the land.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ Moreover the Philistines had yet war again with Israel; and David went down, and his servants with him, and fought against the Philistines: and David waxed faint.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Ishbi–benob, which _was_ of the sons of the giant, the weight of whose spear _weighed_ three hundred _shekels_ of brass in weight, he being girded with a new _sword_, thought to have slain David.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But Abishai the son of Zeruiah succoured him, and smote the Philistine, and killed him. Then the men of David sware unto him, saying, Thou shalt go no more out with us to battle, that thou quench not the light of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And it came to pass after this, that there was again a battle with the Philistines at Gob: then Sibbechai the Hushathite slew Saph, which _was_ of the sons of the giant.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And there was again a battle in Gob with the Philistines, where Elhanan the son of Jaare–oregim, a Beth–lehemite, slew _the brother of_ Goliath the Gittite, the staff of whose spear _was_ like a weaver’s beam.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And there was yet a battle in Gath, where was a man of _great_ stature, that had on every hand six fingers, and on every foot six toes, four and twenty in number; and he also was born to the giant.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And when he defied Israel, Jonathan the son of Shimea the brother of David slew him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>These four were born to the giant in Gath, and fell by the hand of David, and by the hand of his servants.  

## CHAPTER 22.

And David spake unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> the words of this song in the day _that_ the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> had delivered him out of the hand of all his enemies, and out of the hand of Saul:  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>¶ And he said, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ my rock, and my fortress, and my deliverer;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The God of my rock; in him will I trust: _he is_ my shield, and the horn of my salvation, my high tower, and my refuge, my saviour; thou savest me from violence.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I will call on the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, _who is_ worthy to be praised: so shall I be saved from mine enemies.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>When the waves of death compassed me, the floods of ungodly men made me afraid;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>The sorrows of hell compassed me about; the snares of death prevented me;  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>In my distress I called upon the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and cried to my God: and he did hear my voice out of his temple, and my cry _did enter_ into his ears.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Then the earth shook and trembled; the foundations of heaven moved and shook, because he was wroth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>There went up a smoke out of his nostrils, and fire out of his mouth devoured: coals were kindled by it.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He bowed the heavens also, and came down; and darkness _was_ under his feet.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And he rode upon a cherub, and did fly: and he was seen upon the wings of the wind.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he made darkness pavilions round about him, dark waters, _and_ thick clouds of the skies.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Through the brightness before him were coals of fire kindled.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thundered from heaven, and the most High uttered his voice.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he sent out arrows, and scattered them; lightning, and discomfited them.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the channels of the sea appeared, the foundations of the world were discovered, at the rebuking of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, at the blast of the breath of his nostrils.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>He sent from above, he took me; he drew me out of many waters;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>He delivered me from my strong enemy, _and_ from them that hated me: for they were too strong for me.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>They prevented me in the day of my calamity: but the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was my stay.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>He brought me forth also into a large place: he delivered me, because he delighted in me.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> rewarded me according to my righteousness: according to the cleanness of my hands hath he recompensed me.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For I have kept the ways of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and have not wickedly departed from my God.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>For all his judgments _were_ before me: and _as for_ his statutes, I did not depart from them.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>I was also upright before him, and have kept myself from mine iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Therefore the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> hath recompensed me according to my righteousness; according to my cleanness in his eye sight.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>With the merciful thou wilt shew thyself merciful, _and_ with the upright man thou wilt shew thyself upright.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>With the pure thou wilt shew thyself pure; and with the froward thou wilt shew thyself unsavoury.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the afflicted people thou wilt save: but thine eyes _are_ upon the haughty, _that_ thou mayest bring _them_ down.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For thou _art_ my lamp, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> will lighten my darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>For by thee I have run through a troop: by my God have I leaped over a wall.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>_As for_ God, his way _is_ perfect; the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> _is_ tried: he _is_ a buckler to all them that trust in him.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For who _is_ God, save the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>? and who _is_ a rock, save our God?  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>God _is_ my strength _and_ power: and he maketh my way perfect.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>He maketh my feet like hinds’ _feet_: and setteth me upon my high places.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>He teacheth my hands to war; so that a bow of steel is broken by mine arms.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Thou hast also given me the shield of thy salvation: and thy gentleness hath made me great.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Thou hast enlarged my steps under me; so that my feet did not slip.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>I have pursued mine enemies, and destroyed them; and turned not again until I had consumed them.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And I have consumed them, and wounded them, that they could not arise: yea, they are fallen under my feet.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>For thou hast girded me with strength to battle: them that rose up against me hast thou subdued under me.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>Thou hast also given me the necks of mine enemies, that I might destroy them that hate me.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>They looked, but _there was_ none to save; _even_ unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, but he answered them not.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>Then did I beat them as small as the dust of the earth, I did stamp them as the mire of the street, _and_ did spread them abroad.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>Thou also hast delivered me from the strivings of my people, thou hast kept me _to be_ head of the heathen: a people _which_ I knew not shall serve me.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>Strangers shall submit themselves unto me: as soon as they hear, they shall be obedient unto me.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>Strangers shall fade away, and they shall be afraid out of their close places.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> liveth; and blessed _be_ my rock; and exalted be the God of the rock of my salvation.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>It _is_ God that avengeth me, and that bringeth down the people under me,  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>And that bringeth me forth from mine enemies: thou also hast lifted me up on high above them that rose up against me: thou hast delivered me from the violent man.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>Therefore I will give thanks unto thee, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, among the heathen, and I will sing praises unto thy name.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>_He is_ the tower of salvation for his king: and sheweth mercy to his anointed, unto David, and to his seed for evermore.  

## CHAPTER 23.

Now these _be_ the last words of David. David the son of Jesse said, and the man _who was_ raised up on high, the anointed of the God of Jacob, and the sweet psalmist of Israel, said,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>The Spirit of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> spake by me, and his word _was_ in my tongue.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>The God of Israel said, the Rock of Israel spake to me, He that ruleth over men _must be_ just, ruling in the fear of God.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And _he shall be_ as the light of the morning, _when_ the sun riseth, _even_ a morning without clouds; _as_ the tender grass _springing_ out of the earth by clear shining after rain.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Although my house _be_ not so with God; yet he hath made with me an everlasting covenant, ordered in all _things_, and sure: for _this is_ all my salvation, and all _my_ desire, although he make _it_ not to grow.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ But _the sons_ of Belial _shall be_ all of them as thorns thrust away, because they cannot be taken with hands:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But the man _that_ shall touch them must be fenced with iron and the staff of a spear; and they shall be utterly burned with fire in the _same_ place.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ These _be_ the names of the mighty men whom David had: The Tachmonite that sat in the seat, chief among the captains; the same _was_ Adino the Eznite: _he lift up his spear_ against eight hundred, whom he slew at one time.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And after him _was_ Eleazar the son of Dodo the Ahohite, _one_ of the three mighty men with David, when they defied the Philistines _that_ were there gathered together to battle, and the men of Israel were gone away:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He arose, and smote the Philistines until his hand was weary, and his hand clave unto the sword: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> wrought a great victory that day; and the people returned after him only to spoil.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And after him _was_ Shammah the son of Agee the Hararite. And the Philistines were gathered together into a troop, where was a piece of ground full of lentiles: and the people fled from the Philistines.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But he stood in the midst of the ground, and defended it, and slew the Philistines: and the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> wrought a great victory.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And three of the thirty chief went down, and came to David in the harvest time unto the cave of Adullam: and the troop of the Philistines pitched in the valley of Rephaim.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And David _was_ then in an hold, and the garrison of the Philistines _was_ then _in_ Beth–lehem.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And David longed, and said, Oh that one would give me drink of the water of the well of Beth–lehem, which _is_ by the gate!  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And the three mighty men brake through the host of the Philistines, and drew water out of the well of Beth–lehem, that _was_ by the gate, and took _it_, and brought _it_ to David: nevertheless he would not drink thereof, but poured it out unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he said, Be it far from me, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that I should do this: _is not this_ the blood of the men that went in jeopardy of their lives? therefore he would not drink it. These things did these three mighty men.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Abishai, the brother of Joab, the son of Zeruiah, was chief among three. And he lifted up his spear against three hundred, _and_ slew _them_, and had the name among three.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Was he not most honourable of three? therefore he was their captain: howbeit he attained not unto the _first_ three.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Benaiah the son of Jehoiada, the son of a valiant man, of Kabzeel, who had done many acts, he slew two lionlike men of Moab: he went down also and slew a lion in the midst of a pit in time of snow:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And he slew an Egyptian, a goodly man: and the Egyptian had a spear in his hand; but he went down to him with a staff, and plucked the spear out of the Egyptian’s hand, and slew him with his own spear.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>These _things_ did Benaiah the son of Jehoiada, and had the name among three mighty men.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>He was more honourable than the thirty, but he attained not to the _first_ three. And David set him over his guard.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Asahel the brother of Joab _was_ one of the thirty; Elhanan the son of Dodo of Beth–lehem,  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Shammah the Harodite, Elika the Harodite,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Helez the Paltite, Ira the son of Ikkesh the Tekoite,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Abiezer the Anethothite, Mebunnai the Hushathite,  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Zalmon the Ahohite, Maharai the Netophathite,  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Heleb the son of Baanah, a Netophathite, Ittai the son of Ribai out of Gibeah of the children of Benjamin,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Benaiah the Pirathonite, Hiddai of the brooks of Gaash,  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Abi–albon the Arbathite, Azmaveth the Barhumite,  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Eliahba the Shaalbonite, of the sons of Jashen, Jonathan,

## CHAPTER 24.

And again the anger of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was kindled against Israel, and he moved David against them to say, Go, number Israel and Judah.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For the king said to Joab the captain of the host, which _was_ with him, Go now through all the tribes of Israel, from Dan even to Beer–sheba, and number ye the people, that I may know the number of the people.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Joab said unto the king, Now the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God add unto the people, how many soever they be, an hundredfold, and that the eyes of my lord the king may see _it_: but why doth my lord the king delight in this thing?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Notwithstanding the king’s word prevailed against Joab, and against the captains of the host. And Joab and the captains of the host went out from the presence of the king, to number the people of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And they passed over Jordan, and pitched in Aroer, on the right side of the city that _lieth_ in the midst of the river of Gad, and toward Jazer:  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Then they came to Gilead, and to the land of Tahtim–hodshi; and they came to Dan–jaan, and about to Zidon,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And came to the strong hold of Tyre, and to all the cities of the Hivites, and of the Canaanites: and they went out to the south of Judah, _even_ to Beer–sheba.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>So when they had gone through all the land, they came to Jerusalem at the end of nine months and twenty days.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Joab gave up the sum of the number of the people unto the king: and there were in Israel eight hundred thousand valiant men that drew the sword; and the men of Judah _were_ five hundred thousand men.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And David’s heart smote him after that he had numbered the people. And David said unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I have sinned greatly in that I have done: and now, I beseech thee, O <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, take away the iniquity of thy servant; for I have done very foolishly.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For when David was up in the morning, the word of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> came unto the prophet Gad, David’s seer, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Go and say unto David, Thus saith the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, I offer thee three _things_; choose thee one of them, that I may _do it_ unto thee.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>So Gad came to David, and told him, and said unto him, Shall seven years of famine come unto thee in thy land? or wilt thou flee three months before thine enemies, while they pursue thee? or that there be three days’ pestilence in thy land? now advise, and see what answer I shall return to him that sent me.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And David said unto Gad, I am in a great strait: let us fall now into the hand of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>; for his mercies _are_ great: and let me not fall into the hand of man.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ So the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> sent a pestilence upon Israel from the morning even to the time appointed: and there died of the people from Dan even to Beer–sheba seventy thousand men.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And when the angel stretched out his hand upon Jerusalem to destroy it, the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> repented him of the evil, and said to the angel that destroyed the people, It is enough: stay now thine hand. And the angel of the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was by the threshingplace of Araunah the Jebusite.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And David spake unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> when he saw the angel that smote the people, and said, Lo, I have sinned, and I have done wickedly: but these sheep, what have they done? let thine hand, I pray thee, be against me, and against my father’s house.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And Gad came that day to David, and said unto him, Go up, rear an altar unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> in the threshingfloor of Araunah the Jebusite.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And David, according to the saying of Gad, went up as the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> commanded.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And Araunah looked, and saw the king and his servants coming on toward him: and Araunah went out, and bowed himself before the king on his face upon the ground.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And Araunah said, Wherefore is my lord the king come to his servant? And David said, To buy the threshingfloor of thee, to build an altar unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, that the plague may be stayed from the people.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And Araunah said unto David, Let my lord the king take and offer up what _seemeth_ good unto him: behold, _here be_ oxen for burnt sacrifice, and threshing instruments and _other_ instruments of the oxen for wood.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>All these _things_ did Araunah, _as_ a king, give unto the king. And Araunah said unto the king, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> thy God accept thee.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And the king said unto Araunah, Nay; but I will surely buy _it_ of thee at a price: neither will I offer burnt offerings unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> my God of that which doth cost me nothing. So David bought the threshingfloor and the oxen for fifty shekels of silver.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And David built there an altar unto the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span>, and offered burnt offerings and peace offerings. So the <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> was intreated for the land, and the plague was stayed from Israel.
