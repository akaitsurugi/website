# Luke

### THE GOSPEL ACCORDING TO LUKE

## CHAPTER 1.

Forasmuch as many have taken in hand to set forth in order a declaration of those things which are most surely believed among us,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Even as they delivered them unto us, which from the beginning were eyewitnesses, and ministers of the word;  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>It seemed good to me also, having had perfect understanding of all things from the very first, to write unto thee in order, most excellent Theophilus,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>That thou mightest know the certainty of those things, wherein thou hast been instructed.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ There was in the days of Herod, the king of Judæa, a certain priest named Zacharias, of the course of Abia: and his wife _was_ of the daughters of Aaron, and her name _was_ Elisabeth.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they were both righteous before God, walking in all the commandments and ordinances of the Lord blameless.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they had no child, because that Elisabeth was barren, and they both were _now_ well stricken in years.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that while he executed the priest’s office before God in the order of his course,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>According to the custom of the priest’s office, his lot was to burn incense when he went into the temple of the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the whole multitude of the people were praying without at the time of incense.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And there appeared unto him an angel of the Lord standing on the right side of the altar of incense.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And when Zacharias saw _him_, he was troubled, and fear fell upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But the angel said unto him, Fear not, Zacharias: for thy prayer is heard; and thy wife Elisabeth shall bear thee a son, and thou shalt call his name John.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And thou shalt have joy and gladness; and many shall rejoice at his birth.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For he shall be great in the sight of the Lord, and shall drink neither wine nor strong drink; and he shall be filled with the Holy Ghost, even from his mother’s womb.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And many of the children of Israel shall he turn to the Lord their God.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he shall go before him in the spirit and power of Elias, to turn the hearts of the fathers to the children, and the disobedient to the wisdom of the just; to make ready a people prepared for the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And Zacharias said unto the angel, Whereby shall I know this? for I am an old man, and my wife well stricken in years.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the angel answering said unto him, I am Gabriel, that stand in the presence of God; and am sent to speak unto thee, and to shew thee these glad tidings.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And, behold, thou shalt be dumb, and not able to speak, until the day that these things shall be performed, because thou believest not my words, which shall be fulfilled in their season.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the people waited for Zacharias, and marvelled that he tarried so long in the temple.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And when he came out, he could not speak unto them: and they perceived that he had seen a vision in the temple: for he beckoned unto them, and remained speechless.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that, as soon as the days of his ministration were accomplished, he departed to his own house.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And after those days his wife Elisabeth conceived, and hid herself five months, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Thus hath the Lord dealt with me in the days wherein he looked on _me_, to take away my reproach among men.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And in the sixth month the angel Gabriel was sent from God unto a city of Galilee, named Nazareth,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>To a virgin espoused to a man whose name was Joseph, of the house of David; and the virgin’s name _was_ Mary.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And the angel came in unto her, and said, Hail, _thou that art_ highly favoured, the Lord _is_ with thee: blessed _art_ thou among women.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And when she saw _him_, she was troubled at his saying, and cast in her mind what manner of salutation this should be.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the angel said unto her, Fear not, Mary: for thou hast found favour with God.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And, behold, thou shalt conceive in thy womb, and bring forth a son, and shalt call his name JESUS.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>He shall be great, and shall be called the Son of the Highest: and the Lord God shall give unto him the throne of his father David:  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And he shall reign over the house of Jacob for ever; and of his kingdom there shall be no end.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Then said Mary unto the angel, How shall this be, seeing I know not a man?  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the angel answered and said unto her, The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee, and the power of the Highest shall overshadow thee: therefore also that holy thing which shall be born of thee shall be called the Son of God.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And, behold, thy cousin Elisabeth, she hath also conceived a son in her old age: and this is the sixth month with her, who was called barren.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>For with God nothing shall be impossible.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And Mary said, Behold the handmaid of the Lord; be it unto me according to thy word. And the angel departed from her.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And Mary arose in those days, and went into the hill country with haste, into a city of Juda;  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And entered into the house of Zacharias, and saluted Elisabeth.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that, when Elisabeth heard the salutation of Mary, the babe leaped in her womb; and Elisabeth was filled with the Holy Ghost:  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And she spake out with a loud voice, and said, Blessed _art_ thou among women, and blessed _is_ the fruit of thy womb.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And whence _is_ this to me, that the mother of my Lord should come to me?  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>For, lo, as soon as the voice of thy salutation sounded in mine ears, the babe leaped in my womb for joy.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And blessed _is_ she that believed: for there shall be a performance of those things which were told her from the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And Mary said, My soul doth magnify the Lord,  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And my spirit hath rejoiced in God my Saviour.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>For he hath regarded the low estate of his handmaiden: for, behold, from henceforth all generations shall call me blessed.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>For he that is mighty hath done to me great things; and holy _is_ his name.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>And his mercy _is_ on them that fear him from generation to generation.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>He hath shewed strength with his arm; he hath scattered the proud in the imagination of their hearts.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>He hath put down the mighty from _their_ seats, and exalted them of low degree.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>He hath filled the hungry with good things; and the rich he hath sent empty away.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>He hath holpen his servant Israel, in remembrance of _his_ mercy;  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>As he spake to our fathers, to Abraham, and to his seed for ever.  
<span class="verse-number">56&ensp;</span>And Mary abode with her about three months, and returned to her own house.  
<span class="verse-number">57&ensp;</span>Now Elisabeth’s full time came that she should be delivered; and she brought forth a son.  
<span class="verse-number">58&ensp;</span>And her neighbours and her cousins heard how the Lord had shewed great mercy upon her; and they rejoiced with her.  
<span class="verse-number">59&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that on the eighth day they came to circumcise the child; and they called him Zacharias, after the name of his father.  
<span class="verse-number">60&ensp;</span>And his mother answered and said, Not _so_; but he shall be called John.  
<span class="verse-number">61&ensp;</span>And they said unto her, There is none of thy kindred that is called by this name.  
<span class="verse-number">62&ensp;</span>And they made signs to his father, how he would have him called.  
<span class="verse-number">63&ensp;</span>And he asked for a writing table, and wrote, saying, His name is John. And they marvelled all.  
<span class="verse-number">64&ensp;</span>And his mouth was opened immediately, and his tongue _loosed_, and he spake, and praised God.  
<span class="verse-number">65&ensp;</span>And fear came on all that dwelt round about them: and all these sayings were noised abroad throughout all the hill country of Judæa.  
<span class="verse-number">66&ensp;</span>And all they that heard _them_ laid _them_ up in their hearts, saying, What manner of child shall this be! And the hand of the Lord was with him.  
<span class="verse-number">67&ensp;</span>And his father Zacharias was filled with the Holy Ghost, and prophesied, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">68&ensp;</span>Blessed _be_ the Lord God of Israel; for he hath visited and redeemed his people,  
<span class="verse-number">69&ensp;</span>And hath raised up an horn of salvation for us in the house of his servant David;  
<span class="verse-number">70&ensp;</span>As he spake by the mouth of his holy prophets, which have been since the world began:  
<span class="verse-number">71&ensp;</span>That we should be saved from our enemies, and from the hand of all that hate us;  
<span class="verse-number">72&ensp;</span>To perform the mercy _promised_ to our fathers, and to remember his holy covenant;  
<span class="verse-number">73&ensp;</span>The oath which he sware to our father Abraham,  
<span class="verse-number">74&ensp;</span>That he would grant unto us, that we being delivered out of the hand of our enemies might serve him without fear,  
<span class="verse-number">75&ensp;</span>In holiness and righteousness before him, all the days of our life.  
<span class="verse-number">76&ensp;</span>And thou, child, shalt be called the prophet of the Highest: for thou shalt go before the face of the Lord to prepare his ways;  
<span class="verse-number">77&ensp;</span>To give knowledge of salvation unto his people by the remission of their sins,  
<span class="verse-number">78&ensp;</span>Through the tender mercy of our God; whereby the dayspring from on high hath visited us,  
<span class="verse-number">79&ensp;</span>To give light to them that sit in darkness and _in_ the shadow of death, to guide our feet into the way of peace.  
<span class="verse-number">80&ensp;</span>And the child grew, and waxed strong in spirit, and was in the deserts till the day of his shewing unto Israel.  

## CHAPTER 2.

And it came to pass in those days, that there went out a decree from Cæsar Augustus, that all the world should be taxed.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>(_And_ this taxing was first made when Cyrenius was governor of Syria.)  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And all went to be taxed, every one into his own city.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Joseph also went up from Galilee, out of the city of Nazareth, into Judæa, unto the city of David, which is called Bethlehem; (because he was of the house and lineage of David:)  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>To be taxed with Mary his espoused wife, being great with child.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And so it was, that, while they were there, the days were accomplished that she should be delivered.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And she brought forth her firstborn son, and wrapped him in swaddling clothes, and laid him in a manger; because there was no room for them in the inn.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And there were in the same country shepherds abiding in the field, keeping watch over their flock by night.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And, lo, the angel of the Lord came upon them, and the glory of the Lord shone round about them: and they were sore afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the angel said unto them, Fear not: for, behold, I bring you good tidings of great joy, which shall be to all people.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For unto you is born this day in the city of David a Saviour, which is Christ the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And this _shall be_ a sign unto you; Ye shall find the babe wrapped in swaddling clothes, lying in a manger.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And suddenly there was with the angel a multitude of the heavenly host praising God, and saying,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Glory to God in the highest, and on earth peace, good will toward men.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, as the angels were gone away from them into heaven, the shepherds said one to another, Let us now go even unto Bethlehem, and see this thing which is come to pass, which the Lord hath made known unto us.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And they came with haste, and found Mary, and Joseph, and the babe lying in a manger.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And when they had seen _it_, they made known abroad the saying which was told them concerning this child.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And all they that heard _it_ wondered at those things which were told them by the shepherds.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But Mary kept all these things, and pondered _them_ in her heart.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And the shepherds returned, glorifying and praising God for all the things that they had heard and seen, as it was told unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And when eight days were accomplished for the circumcising of the child, his name was called JESUS, which was so named of the angel before he was conceived in the womb.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And when the days of her purification according to the law of Moses were accomplished, they brought him to Jerusalem, to present _him_ to the Lord;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>(As it is written in the law of the Lord, Every male that openeth the womb shall be called holy to the Lord;)  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And to offer a sacrifice according to that which is said in the law of the Lord, A pair of turtledoves, or two young pigeons.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And, behold, there was a man in Jerusalem, whose name _was_ Simeon; and the same man _was_ just and devout, waiting for the consolation of Israel: and the Holy Ghost was upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And it was revealed unto him by the Holy Ghost, that he should not see death, before he had seen the Lord’s Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And he came by the Spirit into the temple: and when the parents brought in the child Jesus, to do for him after the custom of the law,  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Then took he him up in his arms, and blessed God, and said,  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Lord, now lettest thou thy servant depart in peace, according to thy word:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>For mine eyes have seen thy salvation,  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Which thou hast prepared before the face of all people;  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>A light to lighten the Gentiles, and the glory of thy people Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And Joseph and his mother marvelled at those things which were spoken of him.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And Simeon blessed them, and said unto Mary his mother, Behold, this _child_ is set for the fall and rising again of many in Israel; and for a sign which shall be spoken against;  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>(Yea, a sword shall pierce through thy own soul also,) that the thoughts of many hearts may be revealed.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And there was one Anna, a prophetess, the daughter of Phanuel, of the tribe of Aser: she was of a great age, and had lived with an husband seven years from her virginity;  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And she _was_ a widow of about fourscore and four years, which departed not from the temple, but served _God_ with fastings and prayers night and day.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And she coming in that instant gave thanks likewise unto the Lord, and spake of him to all them that looked for redemption in Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And when they had performed all things according to the law of the Lord, they returned into Galilee, to their own city Nazareth.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And the child grew, and waxed strong in spirit, filled with wisdom: and the grace of God was upon him.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>Now his parents went to Jerusalem every year at the feast of the passover.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And when he was twelve years old, they went up to Jerusalem after the custom of the feast.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And when they had fulfilled the days, as they returned, the child Jesus tarried behind in Jerusalem; and Joseph and his mother knew not _of it_.   
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>But they, supposing him to have been in the company, went a day’s journey; and they sought him among _their_ kinsfolk and acquaintance.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And when they found him not, they turned back again to Jerusalem, seeking him.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that after three days they found him in the temple, sitting in the midst of the doctors, both hearing them, and asking them questions.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And all that heard him were astonished at his understanding and answers.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And when they saw him, they were amazed: and his mother said unto him, Son, why hast thou thus dealt with us? behold, thy father and I have sought thee sorrowing.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, How is it that ye sought me? wist ye not that I must be about my Father’s business?  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>And they understood not the saying which he spake unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>And he went down with them, and came to Nazareth, and was subject unto them: but his mother kept all these sayings in her heart.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>And Jesus increased in wisdom and stature, and in favour with God and man.  

## CHAPTER 3.

Now in the fifteenth year of the reign of Tiberius Cæsar, Pontius Pilate being governor of Judæa, and Herod being tetrarch of Galilee, and his brother Philip tetrarch of Ituraea and of the region of Trachonitis, and Lysanias the tetrarch of Abilene,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Annas and Caiaphas being the high priests, the word of God came unto John the son of Zacharias in the wilderness.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he came into all the country about Jordan, preaching the baptism of repentance for the remission of sins;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>As it is written in the book of the words of Esaias the prophet, saying, The voice of one crying in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the Lord, make his paths straight.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Every valley shall be filled, and every mountain and hill shall be brought low; and the crooked shall be made straight, and the rough ways _shall be_ made smooth;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And all flesh shall see the salvation of God.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then said he to the multitude that came forth to be baptized of him, O generation of vipers, who hath warned you to flee from the wrath to come?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Bring forth therefore fruits worthy of repentance, and begin not to say within yourselves, We have Abraham to _our_ father: for I say unto you, That God is able of these stones to raise up children unto Abraham.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And now also the axe is laid unto the root of the trees: every tree therefore which bringeth not forth good fruit is hewn down, and cast into the fire.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the people asked him, saying, What shall we do then?  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>He answereth and saith unto them, He that hath two coats, let him impart to him that hath none; and he that hath meat, let him do likewise.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Then came also publicans to be baptized, and said unto him, Master, what shall we do?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Exact no more than that which is appointed you.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the soldiers likewise demanded of him, saying, And what shall we do? And he said unto them, Do violence to no man, neither accuse _any_ falsely; and be content with your wages.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And as the people were in expectation, and all men mused in their hearts of John, whether he were the Christ, or not;  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>John answered, saying unto _them_ all, I indeed baptize you with water; but one mightier than I cometh, the latchet of whose shoes I am not worthy to unloose: he shall baptize you with the Holy Ghost and with fire:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Whose fan _is_ in his hand, and he will throughly purge his floor, and will gather the wheat into his garner; but the chaff he will burn with fire unquenchable.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And many other things in his exhortation preached he unto the people.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>But Herod the tetrarch, being reproved by him for Herodias his brother Philip’s wife, and for all the evils which Herod had done,  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Added yet this above all, that he shut up John in prison.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Now when all the people were baptized, it came to pass, that Jesus also being baptized, and praying, the heaven was opened,  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the Holy Ghost descended in a bodily shape like a dove upon him, and a voice came from heaven, which said, Thou art my beloved Son; in thee I am well pleased.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And Jesus himself began to be about thirty years of age, being (as was supposed) the son of Joseph, which was _the son_ of Heli,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Matthat, which was _the son_ of Levi, which was _the son_ of Melchi, which was _the son_ of Janna, which was _the son_ of Joseph,  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Mattathias, which was _the son_ of Amos, which was _the son_ of Naum, which was _the son_ of Esli, which was _the son_ of Nagge,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Maath, which was _the son_ of Mattathias, which was _the son_ of Semei, which was _the son_ of Joseph, which was _the son_ of Juda,  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Joanna, which was _the son_ of Rhesa, which was _the son_ of Zorobabel, which was _the son_ of Salathiel, which was _the son_ of Neri,  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Melchi, which was _the son_ of Addi, which was _the son_ of Cosam, which was _the son_ of Elmodam, which was _the son_ of Er,  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Jose, which was _the son_ of Eliezer, which was _the son_ of Jorim, which was _the son_ of Matthat, which was _the son_ of Levi,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Simeon, which was _the son_ of Juda, which was _the son_ of Joseph, which was _the son_ of Jonan, which was _the son_ of Eliakim,  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Melea, which was _the son_ of Menan, which was _the son_ of Mattatha, which was _the son_ of Nathan, which was _the son_ of David,  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Jesse, which was _the son_ of Obed, which was _the son_ of Booz, which was _the son_ of Salmon, which was _the son_ of Naasson,  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Aminadab, which was _the son_ of Aram, which was _the son_ of Esrom, which was _the son_ of Phares, which was _the son_ of Juda,  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Jacob, which was _the son_ of Isaac, which was _the son_ of Abraham, which was _the son_ of Thara, which was _the son_ of Nachor,  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Saruch, which was _the son_ of Ragau, which was _the son_ of Phalec, which was _the son_ of Heber, which was _the son_ of Sala,  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Cainan, which was _the son_ of Arphaxad, which was _the son_ of Sem, which was _the son_ of Noe, which was _the son_ of Lamech,  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Mathusala, which was _the son_ of Enoch, which was _the son_ of Jared, which was _the son_ of Maleleel, which was _the son_ of Cainan,  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>Which was _the son_ of Enos, which was _the son_ of Seth, which was _the son_ of Adam, which was _the son_ of God.  

## CHAPTER 4.

And Jesus being full of the Holy Ghost returned from Jordan, and was led by the Spirit into the wilderness,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Being forty days tempted of the devil. And in those days he did eat nothing: and when they were ended, he afterward hungered.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And the devil said unto him, If thou be the Son of God, command this stone that it be made bread.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And Jesus answered him, saying, It is written, That man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word of God.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the devil, taking him up into an high mountain, shewed unto him all the kingdoms of the world in a moment of time.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the devil said unto him, All this power will I give thee, and the glory of them: for that is delivered unto me; and to whomsoever I will I give it.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>If thou therefore wilt worship me, all shall be thine.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Jesus answered and said unto him, Get thee behind me, Satan: for it is written, Thou shalt worship the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he brought him to Jerusalem, and set him on a pinnacle of the temple, and said unto him, If thou be the Son of God, cast thyself down from hence:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For it is written, He shall give his angels charge over thee, to keep thee:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And in _their_ hands they shall bear thee up, lest at any time thou dash thy foot against a stone.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering said unto him, It is said, Thou shalt not tempt the Lord thy God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And when the devil had ended all the temptation, he departed from him for a season.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And Jesus returned in the power of the Spirit into Galilee: and there went out a fame of him through all the region round about.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he taught in their synagogues, being glorified of all.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And he came to Nazareth, where he had been brought up: and, as his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day, and stood up for to read.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And there was delivered unto him the book of the prophet Esaias. And when he had opened the book, he found the place where it was written,  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>The Spirit of the Lord _is_ upon me, because he hath anointed me to preach the gospel to the poor; he hath sent me to heal the brokenhearted, to preach deliverance to the captives, and recovering of sight to the blind, to set at liberty them that are bruised,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>To preach the acceptable year of the Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And he closed the book, and he gave _it_ again to the minister, and sat down. And the eyes of all them that were in the synagogue were fastened on him.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And he began to say unto them, This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And all bare him witness, and wondered at the gracious words which proceeded out of his mouth. And they said, Is not this Joseph’s son?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Ye will surely say unto me this proverb, Physician, heal thyself: whatsoever we have heard done in Capernaum, do also here in thy country.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And he said, Verily I say unto you, No prophet is accepted in his own country.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But I tell you of a truth, many widows were in Israel in the days of Elias, when the heaven was shut up three years and six months, when great famine was throughout all the land;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But unto none of them was Elias sent, save unto Sarepta, _a city_ of Sidon, unto a woman _that was_ a widow.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And many lepers were in Israel in the time of Eliseus the prophet; and none of them was cleansed, saving Naaman the Syrian.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And all they in the synagogue, when they heard these things, were filled with wrath,  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And rose up, and thrust him out of the city, and led him unto the brow of the hill whereon their city was built, that they might cast him down headlong.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>But he passing through the midst of them went his way,  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And came down to Capernaum, a city of Galilee, and taught them on the sabbath days.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And they were astonished at his doctrine: for his word was with power.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ And in the synagogue there was a man, which had a spirit of an unclean devil, and cried out with a loud voice,  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Saying, Let _us_ alone; what have we to do with thee, _thou_ Jesus of Nazareth? art thou come to destroy us? I know thee who thou art; the Holy One of God.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And Jesus rebuked him, saying, Hold thy peace, and come out of him. And when the devil had thrown him in the midst, he came out of him, and hurt him not.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And they were all amazed, and spake among themselves, saying, What a word _is_ this! for with authority and power he commandeth the unclean spirits, and they come out.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And the fame of him went out into every place of the country round about.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>¶ And he arose out of the synagogue, and entered into Simon’s house. And Simon’s wife’s mother was taken with a great fever; and they besought him for her.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And he stood over her, and rebuked the fever; and it left her: and immediately she arose and ministered unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>¶ Now when the sun was setting, all they that had any sick with divers diseases brought them unto him; and he laid his hands on every one of them, and healed them.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And devils also came out of many, crying out, and saying, Thou art Christ the Son of God. And he rebuking _them_ suffered them not to speak: for they knew that he was Christ.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And when it was day, he departed and went into a desert place: and the people sought him, and came unto him, and stayed him, that he should not depart from them.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, I must preach the kingdom of God to other cities also: for therefore am I sent.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And he preached in the synagogues of Galilee.  

## CHAPTER 5.

And it came to pass, that, as the people pressed upon him to hear the word of God, he stood by the lake of Gennesaret,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And saw two ships standing by the lake: but the fishermen were gone out of them, and were washing _their_ nets.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he entered into one of the ships, which was Simon’s, and prayed him that he would thrust out a little from the land. And he sat down, and taught the people out of the ship.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Now when he had left speaking, he said unto Simon, Launch out into the deep, and let down your nets for a draught.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And Simon answering said unto him, Master, we have toiled all the night, and have taken nothing: nevertheless at thy word I will let down the net.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And when they had this done, they inclosed a great multitude of fishes: and their net brake.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they beckoned unto _their_ partners, which were in the other ship, that they should come and help them. And they came, and filled both the ships, so that they began to sink.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>When Simon Peter saw _it_, he fell down at Jesus’ knees, saying, Depart from me; for I am a sinful man, O Lord.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>For he was astonished, and all that were with him, at the draught of the fishes which they had taken:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And so _was_ also James, and John, the sons of Zebedee, which were partners with Simon. And Jesus said unto Simon, Fear not; from henceforth thou shalt catch men.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And when they had brought their ships to land, they forsook all, and followed him.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, when he was in a certain city, behold a man full of leprosy: who seeing Jesus fell on _his_ face, and besought him, saying, Lord, if thou wilt, thou canst make me clean.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And he put forth _his_ hand, and touched him, saying, I will: be thou clean. And immediately the leprosy departed from him.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he charged him to tell no man: but go, and shew thyself to the priest, and offer for thy cleansing, according as Moses commanded, for a testimony unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But so much the more went there a fame abroad of him: and great multitudes came together to hear, and to be healed by him of their infirmities.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ And he withdrew himself into the wilderness, and prayed.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And it came to pass on a certain day, as he was teaching, that there were Pharisees and doctors of the law sitting by, which were come out of every town of Galilee, and Judæa, and Jerusalem: and the power of the Lord was _present_ to heal them.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, men brought in a bed a man which was taken with a palsy: and they sought _means_ to bring him in, and to lay _him_ before him.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And when they could not find by what _way_ they might bring him in because of the multitude, they went upon the housetop, and let him down through the tiling with _his_ couch into the midst before Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And when he saw their faith, he said unto him, Man, thy sins are forgiven thee.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the scribes and the Pharisees began to reason, saying, Who is this which speaketh blasphemies? Who can forgive sins, but God alone?  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But when Jesus perceived their thoughts, he answering said unto them, What reason ye in your hearts?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Whether is easier, to say, Thy sins be forgiven thee; or to say, Rise up and walk?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>But that ye may know that the Son of man hath power upon earth to forgive sins, (he said unto the sick of the palsy,) I say unto thee, Arise, and take up thy couch, and go into thine house.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And immediately he rose up before them, and took up that whereon he lay, and departed to his own house, glorifying God.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And they were all amazed, and they glorified God, and were filled with fear, saying, We have seen strange things to day.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And after these things he went forth, and saw a publican, named Levi, sitting at the receipt of custom: and he said unto him, Follow me.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And he left all, rose up, and followed him.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And Levi made him a great feast in his own house: and there was a great company of publicans and of others that sat down with them.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>But their scribes and Pharisees murmured against his disciples, saying, Why do ye eat and drink with publicans and sinners?  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering said unto them, They that are whole need not a physician; but they that are sick.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>I came not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>¶ And they said unto him, Why do the disciples of John fast often, and make prayers, and likewise _the disciples_ of the Pharisees; but thine eat and drink?  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Can ye make the children of the bridechamber fast, while the bridegroom is with them?  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>But the days will come, when the bridegroom shall be taken away from them, and then shall they fast in those days.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ And he spake also a parable unto them; No man putteth a piece of a new garment upon an old; if otherwise, then both the new maketh a rent, and the piece that was _taken_ out of the new agreeth not with the old.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And no man putteth new wine into old bottles; else the new wine will burst the bottles, and be spilled, and the bottles shall perish.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>But new wine must be put into new bottles; and both are preserved.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>No man also having drunk old _wine_ straightway desireth new: for he saith, The old is better.  

## CHAPTER 6.

And it came to pass on the second sabbath after the first, that he went through the corn fields; and his disciples plucked the ears of corn, and did eat, rubbing _them_ in _their_ hands.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And certain of the Pharisees said unto them, Why do ye that which is not lawful to do on the sabbath days?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering them said, Have ye not read so much as this, what David did, when himself was an hungred, and they which were with him;  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>How he went into the house of God, and did take and eat the shewbread, and gave also to them that were with him; which it is not lawful to eat but for the priests alone?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, That the Son of man is Lord also of the sabbath.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And it came to pass also on another sabbath, that he entered into the synagogue and taught: and there was a man whose right hand was withered.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And the scribes and Pharisees watched him, whether he would heal on the sabbath day; that they might find an accusation against him.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>But he knew their thoughts, and said to the man which had the withered hand, Rise up, and stand forth in the midst. And he arose and stood forth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then said Jesus unto them, I will ask you one thing; Is it lawful on the sabbath days to do good, or to do evil? to save life, or to destroy _it_?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And looking round about upon them all, he said unto the man, Stretch forth thy hand. And he did so: and his hand was restored whole as the other.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And they were filled with madness; and communed one with another what they might do to Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And it came to pass in those days, that he went out into a mountain to pray, and continued all night in prayer to God.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And when it was day, he called _unto him_ his disciples: and of them he chose twelve, whom also he named apostles;  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Simon, (whom he also named Peter,) and Andrew his brother, James and John, Philip and Bartholomew,  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>Matthew and Thomas, James the _son_ of Alphæus, and Simon called Zelotes,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And Judas _the brother_ of James, and Judas Iscariot, which also was the traitor.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And he came down with them, and stood in the plain, and the company of his disciples, and a great multitude of people out of all Judæa and Jerusalem, and from the sea coast of Tyre and Sidon, which came to hear him, and to be healed of their diseases;  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they that were vexed with unclean spirits: and they were healed.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And the whole multitude sought to touch him: for there went virtue out of him, and healed _them_ all.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And he lifted up his eyes on his disciples, and said, Blessed _be ye_ poor: for yours is the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Blessed _are ye_ that hunger now: for ye shall be filled. Blessed _are ye_ that weep now: for ye shall laugh.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Blessed are ye, when men shall hate you, and when they shall separate you _from their company_, and shall reproach _you_, and cast out your name as evil, for the Son of man’s sake.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Rejoice ye in that day, and leap for joy: for, behold, your reward _is_ great in heaven: for in the like manner did their fathers unto the prophets.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>But woe unto you that are rich! for ye have received your consolation.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Woe unto you that are full! for ye shall hunger. Woe unto you that laugh now! for ye shall mourn and weep.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Woe unto you, when all men shall speak well of you! for so did their fathers to the false prophets.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ But I say unto you which hear, Love your enemies, do good to them which hate you,  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Bless them that curse you, and pray for them which despitefully use you.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And unto him that smiteth thee on the _one_ cheek offer also the other; and him that taketh away thy cloke forbid not _to take thy_ coat also.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Give to every man that asketh of thee; and of him that taketh away thy goods ask _them_ not again.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And as ye would that men should do to you, do ye also to them likewise.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For if ye love them which love you, what thank have ye? for sinners also love those that love them.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And if ye do good to them which do good to you, what thank have ye? for sinners also do even the same.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And if ye lend _to them_ of whom ye hope to receive, what thank have ye? for sinners also lend to sinners, to receive as much again.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>But love ye your enemies, and do good, and lend, hoping for nothing again; and your reward shall be great, and ye shall be the children of the Highest: for he is kind unto the unthankful and _to_ the evil.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Be ye therefore merciful, as your Father also is merciful.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Judge not, and ye shall not be judged: condemn not, and ye shall not be condemned: forgive, and ye shall be forgiven:  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>Give, and it shall be given unto you; good measure, pressed down, and shaken together, and running over, shall men give into your bosom. For with the same measure that ye mete withal it shall be measured to you again.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And he spake a parable unto them, Can the blind lead the blind? shall they not both fall into the ditch?  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>The disciple is not above his master: but every one that is perfect shall be as his master.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And why beholdest thou the mote that is in thy brother’s eye, but perceivest not the beam that is in thine own eye?  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>Either how canst thou say to thy brother, Brother, let me pull out the mote that is in thine eye, when thou thyself beholdest not the beam that is in thine own eye? Thou hypocrite, cast out first the beam out of thine own eye, and then shalt thou see clearly to pull out the mote that is in thy brother’s eye.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>For a good tree bringeth not forth corrupt fruit; neither doth a corrupt tree bring forth good fruit.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>For every tree is known by his own fruit. For of thorns men do not gather figs, nor of a bramble bush gather they grapes.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>A good man out of the good treasure of his heart bringeth forth that which is good; and an evil man out of the evil treasure of his heart bringeth forth that which is evil: for of the abundance of the heart his mouth speaketh.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>¶ And why call ye me, Lord, Lord, and do not the things which I say?  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>Whosoever cometh to me, and heareth my sayings, and doeth them, I will shew you to whom he is like:  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>He is like a man which built an house, and digged deep, and laid the foundation on a rock: and when the flood arose, the stream beat vehemently upon that house, and could not shake it: for it was founded upon a rock.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>But he that heareth, and doeth not, is like a man that without a foundation built an house upon the earth; against which the stream did beat vehemently, and immediately it fell; and the ruin of that house was great.  

## CHAPTER 7.

Now when he had ended all his sayings in the audience of the people, he entered into Capernaum.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And a certain centurion’s servant, who was dear unto him, was sick, and ready to die.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And when he heard of Jesus, he sent unto him the elders of the Jews, beseeching him that he would come and heal his servant.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And when they came to Jesus, they besought him instantly, saying, That he was worthy for whom he should do this:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>For he loveth our nation, and he hath built us a synagogue.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Then Jesus went with them. And when he was now not far from the house, the centurion sent friends to him, saying unto him, Lord, trouble not thyself: for I am not worthy that thou shouldest enter under my roof:  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Wherefore neither thought I myself worthy to come unto thee: but say in a word, and my servant shall be healed.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>For I also am a man set under authority, having under me soldiers, and I say unto one, Go, and he goeth; and to another, Come, and he cometh; and to my servant, Do this, and he doeth _it_.   
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>When Jesus heard these things, he marvelled at him, and turned him about, and said unto the people that followed him, I say unto you, I have not found so great faith, no, not in Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And they that were sent, returning to the house, found the servant whole that had been sick.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass the day after, that he went into a city called Nain; and many of his disciples went with him, and much people.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Now when he came nigh to the gate of the city, behold, there was a dead man carried out, the only son of his mother, and she was a widow: and much people of the city was with her.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And when the Lord saw her, he had compassion on her, and said unto her, Weep not.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he came and touched the bier: and they that bare _him_ stood still. And he said, Young man, I say unto thee, Arise.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he that was dead sat up, and began to speak. And he delivered him to his mother.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And there came a fear on all: and they glorified God, saying, That a great prophet is risen up among us; and, That God hath visited his people.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And this rumour of him went forth throughout all Judæa, and throughout all the region round about.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the disciples of John shewed him of all these things.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And John calling _unto him_ two of his disciples sent _them_ to Jesus, saying, Art thou he that should come? or look we for another?  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>When the men were come unto him, they said, John Baptist hath sent us unto thee, saying, Art thou he that should come? or look we for another?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And in that same hour he cured many of _their_ infirmities and plagues, and of evil spirits; and unto many _that were_ blind he gave sight.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Then Jesus answering said unto them, Go your way, and tell John what things ye have seen and heard; how that the blind see, the lame walk, the lepers are cleansed, the deaf hear, the dead are raised, to the poor the gospel is preached.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And blessed is _he_, whosoever shall not be offended in me.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And when the messengers of John were departed, he began to speak unto the people concerning John, What went ye out into the wilderness for to see? A reed shaken with the wind?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But what went ye out for to see? A man clothed in soft raiment? Behold, they which are gorgeously apparelled, and live delicately, are in kings’ courts.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But what went ye out for to see? A prophet? Yea, I say unto you, and much more than a prophet.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>This is _he_, of whom it is written, Behold, I send my messenger before thy face, which shall prepare thy way before thee.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For I say unto you, Among those that are born of women there is not a greater prophet than John the Baptist: but he that is least in the kingdom of God is greater than he.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And all the people that heard _him_, and the publicans, justified God, being baptized with the baptism of John.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>But the Pharisees and lawyers rejected the counsel of God against themselves, being not baptized of him.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And the Lord said, Whereunto then shall I liken the men of this generation? and to what are they like?  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>They are like unto children sitting in the marketplace, and calling one to another, and saying, We have piped unto you, and ye have not danced; we have mourned to you, and ye have not wept.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>For John the Baptist came neither eating bread nor drinking wine; and ye say, He hath a devil.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>The Son of man is come eating and drinking; and ye say, Behold a gluttonous man, and a winebibber, a friend of publicans and sinners!  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>But wisdom is justified of all her children.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ And one of the Pharisees desired him that he would eat with him. And he went into the Pharisee’s house, and sat down to meat.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And, behold, a woman in the city, which was a sinner, when she knew that _Jesus_ sat at meat in the Pharisee’s house, brought an alabaster box of ointment,  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And stood at his feet behind _him_ weeping, and began to wash his feet with tears, and did wipe _them_ with the hairs of her head, and kissed his feet, and anointed _them_ with the ointment.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>Now when the Pharisee which had bidden him saw _it_, he spake within himself, saying, This man, if he were a prophet, would have known who and what manner of woman _this is_ that toucheth him: for she is a sinner.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering said unto him, Simon, I have somewhat to say unto thee. And he saith, Master, say on.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>There was a certain creditor which had two debtors: the one owed five hundred pence, and the other fifty.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And when they had nothing to pay, he frankly forgave them both. Tell me therefore, which of them will love him most?  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>Simon answered and said, I suppose that _he_, to whom he forgave most. And he said unto him, Thou hast rightly judged.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And he turned to the woman, and said unto Simon, Seest thou this woman? I entered into thine house, thou gavest me no water for my feet: but she hath washed my feet with tears, and wiped _them_ with the hairs of her head.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>Thou gavest me no kiss: but this woman since the time I came in hath not ceased to kiss my feet.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>My head with oil thou didst not anoint: but this woman hath anointed my feet with ointment.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>Wherefore I say unto thee, Her sins, which are many, are forgiven; for she loved much: but to whom little is forgiven, _the same_ loveth little.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And he said unto her, Thy sins are forgiven.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>And they that sat at meat with him began to say within themselves, Who is this that forgiveth sins also?  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>And he said to the woman, Thy faith hath saved thee; go in peace.  

## CHAPTER 8.

And it came to pass afterward, that he went throughout every city and village, preaching and shewing the glad tidings of the kingdom of God: and the twelve _were_ with him,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And certain women, which had been healed of evil spirits and infirmities, Mary called Magdalene, out of whom went seven devils,  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Joanna the wife of Chuza Herod’s steward, and Susanna, and many others, which ministered unto him of their substance.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>¶ And when much people were gathered together, and were come to him out of every city, he spake by a parable:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>A sower went out to sow his seed: and as he sowed, some fell by the way side; and it was trodden down, and the fowls of the air devoured it.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And some fell upon a rock; and as soon as it was sprung up, it withered away, because it lacked moisture.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And some fell among thorns; and the thorns sprang up with it, and choked it.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And other fell on good ground, and sprang up, and bare fruit an hundredfold. And when he had said these things, he cried, He that hath ears to hear, let him hear.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And his disciples asked him, saying, What might this parable be?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he said, Unto you it is given to know the mysteries of the kingdom of God: but to others in parables; that seeing they might not see, and hearing they might not understand.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Now the parable is this: The seed is the word of God.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Those by the way side are they that hear; then cometh the devil, and taketh away the word out of their hearts, lest they should believe and be saved.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>They on the rock _are they_, which, when they hear, receive the word with joy; and these have no root, which for a while believe, and in time of temptation fall away.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And that which fell among thorns are they, which, when they have heard, go forth, and are choked with cares and riches and pleasures of _this_ life, and bring no fruit to perfection.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But that on the good ground are they, which in an honest and good heart, having heard the word, keep _it_, and bring forth fruit with patience.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>¶ No man, when he hath lighted a candle, covereth it with a vessel, or putteth _it_ under a bed; but setteth _it_ on a candlestick, that they which enter in may see the light.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>For nothing is secret, that shall not be made manifest; neither _any thing_ hid, that shall not be known and come abroad.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Take heed therefore how ye hear: for whosoever hath, to him shall be given; and whosoever hath not, from him shall be taken even that which he seemeth to have.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ Then came to him _his_ mother and his brethren, and could not come at him for the press.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And it was told him _by certain_ which said, Thy mother and thy brethren stand without, desiring to see thee.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And he answered and said unto them, My mother and my brethren are these which hear the word of God, and do it.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ Now it came to pass on a certain day, that he went into a ship with his disciples: and he said unto them, Let us go over unto the other side of the lake. And they launched forth.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But as they sailed he fell asleep: and there came down a storm of wind on the lake; and they were filled _with water_, and were in jeopardy.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they came to him, and awoke him, saying, Master, master, we perish. Then he arose, and rebuked the wind and the raging of the water: and they ceased, and there was a calm.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Where is your faith? And they being afraid wondered, saying one to another, What manner of man is this! for he commandeth even the winds and water, and they obey him.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>¶ And they arrived at the country of the Gadarenes, which is over against Galilee.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And when he went forth to land, there met him out of the city a certain man, which had devils long time, and ware no clothes, neither abode in _any_ house, but in the tombs.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>When he saw Jesus, he cried out, and fell down before him, and with a loud voice said, What have I to do with thee, Jesus, _thou_ Son of God most high? I beseech thee, torment me not.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>(For he had commanded the unclean spirit to come out of the man. For oftentimes it had caught him: and he was kept bound with chains and in fetters; and he brake the bands, and was driven of the devil into the wilderness.)  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Jesus asked him, saying, What is thy name? And he said, Legion: because many devils were entered into him.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And they besought him that he would not command them to go out into the deep.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And there was there an herd of many swine feeding on the mountain: and they besought him that he would suffer them to enter into them. And he suffered them.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Then went the devils out of the man, and entered into the swine: and the herd ran violently down a steep place into the lake, and were choked.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>When they that fed _them_ saw what was done, they fled, and went and told _it_ in the city and in the country.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Then they went out to see what was done; and came to Jesus, and found the man, out of whom the devils were departed, sitting at the feet of Jesus, clothed, and in his right mind: and they were afraid.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>They also which saw _it_ told them by what means he that was possessed of the devils was healed.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>¶ Then the whole multitude of the country of the Gadarenes round about besought him to depart from them; for they were taken with great fear: and he went up into the ship, and returned back again.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>Now the man out of whom the devils were departed besought him that he might be with him: but Jesus sent him away, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>Return to thine own house, and shew how great things God hath done unto thee. And he went his way, and published throughout the whole city how great things Jesus had done unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that, when Jesus was returned, the people _gladly_ received him: for they were all waiting for him.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, there came a man named Jairus, and he was a ruler of the synagogue: and he fell down at Jesus’ feet, and besought him that he would come into his house:  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>For he had one only daughter, about twelve years of age, and she lay a dying. But as he went the people thronged him.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>¶ And a woman having an issue of blood twelve years, which had spent all her living upon physicians, neither could be healed of any,  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>Came behind _him_, and touched the border of his garment: and immediately her issue of blood stanched.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And Jesus said, Who touched me? When all denied, Peter and they that were with him said, Master, the multitude throng thee and press _thee_, and sayest thou, Who touched me?  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And Jesus said, Somebody hath touched me: for I perceive that virtue is gone out of me.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And when the woman saw that she was not hid, she came trembling, and falling down before him, she declared unto him before all the people for what cause she had touched him, and how she was healed immediately.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And he said unto her, Daughter, be of good comfort: thy faith hath made thee whole; go in peace.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>¶ While he yet spake, there cometh one from the ruler of the synagogue’s _house_, saying to him, Thy daughter is dead; trouble not the Master.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>But when Jesus heard _it_, he answered him, saying, Fear not: believe only, and she shall be made whole.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>And when he came into the house, he suffered no man to go in, save Peter, and James, and John, and the father and the mother of the maiden.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>And all wept, and bewailed her: but he said, Weep not; she is not dead, but sleepeth.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>And they laughed him to scorn, knowing that she was dead.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>And he put them all out, and took her by the hand, and called, saying, Maid, arise.  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>And her spirit came again, and she arose straightway: and he commanded to give her meat.  
<span class="verse-number">56&ensp;</span>And her parents were astonished: but he charged them that they should tell no man what was done.  

## CHAPTER 9.

Then he called his twelve disciples together, and gave them power and authority over all devils, and to cure diseases.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he sent them to preach the kingdom of God, and to heal the sick.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Take nothing for _your_ journey, neither staves, nor scrip, neither bread, neither money; neither have two coats apiece.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And whatsoever house ye enter into, there abide, and thence depart.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And whosoever will not receive you, when ye go out of that city, shake off the very dust from your feet for a testimony against them.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they departed, and went through the towns, preaching the gospel, and healing every where.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Now Herod the tetrarch heard of all that was done by him: and he was perplexed, because that it was said of some, that John was risen from the dead;  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And of some, that Elias had appeared; and of others, that one of the old prophets was risen again.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Herod said, John have I beheaded: but who is this, of whom I hear such things? And he desired to see him.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>¶ And the apostles, when they were returned, told him all that they had done. And he took them, and went aside privately into a desert place belonging to the city called Bethsaida.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And the people, when they knew _it_, followed him: and he received them, and spake unto them of the kingdom of God, and healed them that had need of healing.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And when the day began to wear away, then came the twelve, and said unto him, Send the multitude away, that they may go into the towns and country round about, and lodge, and get victuals: for we are here in a desert place.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But he said unto them, Give ye them to eat. And they said, We have no more but five loaves and two fishes; except we should go and buy meat for all this people.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>For they were about five thousand men. And he said to his disciples, Make them sit down by fifties in a company.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And they did so, and made them all sit down.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Then he took the five loaves and the two fishes, and looking up to heaven, he blessed them, and brake, and gave to the disciples to set before the multitude.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And they did eat, and were all filled: and there was taken up of fragments that remained to them twelve baskets.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, as he was alone praying, his disciples were with him: and he asked them, saying, Whom say the people that I am?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>They answering said, John the Baptist; but some _say_, Elias; and others _say_, that one of the old prophets is risen again.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>He said unto them, But whom say ye that I am? Peter answering said, The Christ of God.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And he straitly charged them, and commanded _them_ to tell no man that thing;  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Saying, The Son of man must suffer many things, and be rejected of the elders and chief priests and scribes, and be slain, and be raised the third day.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And he said to _them_ all, If any _man_ will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross daily, and follow me.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For whosoever will save his life shall lose it: but whosoever will lose his life for my sake, the same shall save it.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For what is a man advantaged, if he gain the whole world, and lose himself, or be cast away?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For whosoever shall be ashamed of me and of my words, of him shall the Son of man be ashamed, when he shall come in his own glory, and _in his_ Father’s, and of the holy angels.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>But I tell you of a truth, there be some standing here, which shall not taste of death, till they see the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass about an eight days after these sayings, he took Peter and John and James, and went up into a mountain to pray.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And as he prayed, the fashion of his countenance was altered, and his raiment _was_ white _and_ glistering.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And, behold, there talked with him two men, which were Moses and Elias:  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Who appeared in glory, and spake of his decease which he should accomplish at Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>But Peter and they that were with him were heavy with sleep: and when they were awake, they saw his glory, and the two men that stood with him.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, as they departed from him, Peter said unto Jesus, Master, it is good for us to be here: and let us make three tabernacles; one for thee, and one for Moses, and one for Elias: not knowing what he said.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>While he thus spake, there came a cloud, and overshadowed them: and they feared as they entered into the cloud.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And there came a voice out of the cloud, saying, This is my beloved Son: hear him.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And when the voice was past, Jesus was found alone. And they kept _it_ close, and told no man in those days any of those things which they had seen.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, that on the next day, when they were come down from the hill, much people met him.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And, behold, a man of the company cried out, saying, Master, I beseech thee, look upon my son: for he is mine only child.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And, lo, a spirit taketh him, and he suddenly crieth out; and it teareth him that he foameth again, and bruising him hardly departeth from him.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And I besought thy disciples to cast him out; and they could not.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering said, O faithless and perverse generation, how long shall I be with you, and suffer you? Bring thy son hither.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And as he was yet a coming, the devil threw him down, and tare _him_. And Jesus rebuked the unclean spirit, and healed the child, and delivered him again to his father.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>¶ And they were all amazed at the mighty power of God. But while they wondered every one at all things which Jesus did, he said unto his disciples,  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>Let these sayings sink down into your ears: for the Son of man shall be delivered into the hands of men.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>But they understood not this saying, and it was hid from them, that they perceived it not: and they feared to ask him of that saying.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>¶ Then there arose a reasoning among them, which of them should be greatest.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And Jesus, perceiving the thought of their heart, took a child, and set him by him,  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And said unto them, Whosoever shall receive this child in my name receiveth me: and whosoever shall receive me receiveth him that sent me: for he that is least among you all, the same shall be great.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>¶ And John answered and said, Master, we saw one casting out devils in thy name; and we forbad him, because he followeth not with us.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>And Jesus said unto him, Forbid _him_ not: for he that is not against us is for us.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, when the time was come that he should be received up, he stedfastly set his face to go to Jerusalem,  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>And sent messengers before his face: and they went, and entered into a village of the Samaritans, to make ready for him.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>And they did not receive him, because his face was as though he would go to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>And when his disciples James and John saw _this_, they said, Lord, wilt thou that we command fire to come down from heaven, and consume them, even as Elias did?  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>But he turned, and rebuked them, and said, Ye know not what manner of spirit ye are of.  
<span class="verse-number">56&ensp;</span>For the Son of man is not come to destroy men’s lives, but to save _them_. And they went to another village.  
<span class="verse-number">57&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, that, as they went in the way, a certain _man_ said unto him, Lord, I will follow thee whithersoever thou goest.  
<span class="verse-number">58&ensp;</span>And Jesus said unto him, Foxes have holes, and birds of the air _have_ nests; but the Son of man hath not where to lay _his_ head.  
<span class="verse-number">59&ensp;</span>And he said unto another, Follow me. But he said, Lord, suffer me first to go and bury my father.  
<span class="verse-number">60&ensp;</span>Jesus said unto him, Let the dead bury their dead: but go thou and preach the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">61&ensp;</span>And another also said, Lord, I will follow thee; but let me first go bid them farewell, which are at home at my house.  
<span class="verse-number">62&ensp;</span>And Jesus said unto him, No man, having put his hand to the plough, and looking back, is fit for the kingdom of God.  

## CHAPTER 10.

After these things the Lord appointed other seventy also, and sent them two and two before his face into every city and place, whither he himself would come.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Therefore said he unto them, The harvest truly _is_ great, but the labourers _are_ few: pray ye therefore the Lord of the harvest, that he would send forth labourers into his harvest.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Go your ways: behold, I send you forth as lambs among wolves.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Carry neither purse, nor scrip, nor shoes: and salute no man by the way.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And into whatsoever house ye enter, first say, Peace _be_ to this house.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And if the son of peace be there, your peace shall rest upon it: if not, it shall turn to you again.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And in the same house remain, eating and drinking such things as they give: for the labourer is worthy of his hire. Go not from house to house.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And into whatsoever city ye enter, and they receive you, eat such things as are set before you:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And heal the sick that are therein, and say unto them, The kingdom of God is come nigh unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But into whatsoever city ye enter, and they receive you not, go your ways out into the streets of the same, and say,  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>Even the very dust of your city, which cleaveth on us, we do wipe off against you: notwithstanding be ye sure of this, that the kingdom of God is come nigh unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But I say unto you, that it shall be more tolerable in that day for Sodom, than for that city.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Woe unto thee, Chorazin! woe unto thee, Bethsaida! for if the mighty works had been done in Tyre and Sidon, which have been done in you, they had a great while ago repented, sitting in sackcloth and ashes.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But it shall be more tolerable for Tyre and Sidon at the judgment, than for you.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And thou, Capernaum, which art exalted to heaven, shalt be thrust down to hell.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>He that heareth you heareth me; and he that despiseth you despiseth me; and he that despiseth me despiseth him that sent me.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>¶ And the seventy returned again with joy, saying, Lord, even the devils are subject unto us through thy name.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, I beheld Satan as lightning fall from heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>Behold, I give unto you power to tread on serpents and scorpions, and over all the power of the enemy: and nothing shall by any means hurt you.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Notwithstanding in this rejoice not, that the spirits are subject unto you; but rather rejoice, because your names are written in heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ In that hour Jesus rejoiced in spirit, and said, I thank thee, O Father, Lord of heaven and earth, that thou hast hid these things from the wise and prudent, and hast revealed them unto babes: even so, Father; for so it seemed good in thy sight.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>All things are delivered to me of my Father: and no man knoweth who the Son is, but the Father; and who the Father is, but the Son, and _he_ to whom the Son will reveal _him_.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>¶ And he turned him unto _his_ disciples, and said privately, Blessed _are_ the eyes which see the things that ye see:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For I tell you, that many prophets and kings have desired to see those things which ye see, and have not seen _them_; and to hear those things which ye hear, and have not heard _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, a certain lawyer stood up, and tempted him, saying, Master, what shall I do to inherit eternal life?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>He said unto him, What is written in the law? how readest thou?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And he answering said, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy strength, and with all thy mind; and thy neighbour as thyself.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, Thou hast answered right: this do, and thou shalt live.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>But he, willing to justify himself, said unto Jesus, And who is my neighbour?  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering said, A certain _man_ went down from Jerusalem to Jericho, and fell among thieves, which stripped him of his raiment, and wounded _him_, and departed, leaving _him_ half dead.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And by chance there came down a certain priest that way: and when he saw him, he passed by on the other side.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And likewise a Levite, when he was at the place, came and looked _on him_, and passed by on the other side.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>But a certain Samaritan, as he journeyed, came where he was: and when he saw him, he had compassion _on him_,  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And went to _him_, and bound up his wounds, pouring in oil and wine, and set him on his own beast, and brought him to an inn, and took care of him.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And on the morrow when he departed, he took out two pence, and gave _them_ to the host, and said unto him, Take care of him; and whatsoever thou spendest more, when I come again, I will repay thee.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Which now of these three, thinkest thou, was neighbour unto him that fell among the thieves?  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And he said, He that shewed mercy on him. Then said Jesus unto him, Go, and do thou likewise.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>¶ Now it came to pass, as they went, that he entered into a certain village: and a certain woman named Martha received him into her house.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And she had a sister called Mary, which also sat at Jesus’ feet, and heard his word.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>But Martha was cumbered about much serving, and came to him, and said, Lord, dost thou not care that my sister hath left me to serve alone? bid her therefore that she help me.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And Jesus answered and said unto her, Martha, Martha, thou art careful and troubled about many things:  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>But one thing is needful: and Mary hath chosen that good part, which shall not be taken away from her.  

## CHAPTER 11.

And it came to pass, that, as he was praying in a certain place, when he ceased, one of his disciples said unto him, Lord, teach us to pray, as John also taught his disciples.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, When ye pray, say, Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name. Thy kingdom come. Thy will be done, as in heaven, so in earth.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Give us day by day our daily bread.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And forgive us our sins; for we also forgive every one that is indebted to us. And lead us not into temptation; but deliver us from evil.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Which of you shall have a friend, and shall go unto him at midnight, and say unto him, Friend, lend me three loaves;  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>For a friend of mine in his journey is come to me, and I have nothing to set before him?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And he from within shall answer and say, Trouble me not: the door is now shut, and my children are with me in bed; I cannot rise and give thee.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>I say unto you, Though he will not rise and give him, because he is his friend, yet because of his importunity he will rise and give him as many as he needeth.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And I say unto you, Ask, and it shall be given you; seek, and ye shall find; knock, and it shall be opened unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For every one that asketh receiveth; and he that seeketh findeth; and to him that knocketh it shall be opened.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>If a son shall ask bread of any of you that is a father, will he give him a stone? or if _he ask_ a fish, will he for a fish give him a serpent?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Or if he shall ask an egg, will he offer him a scorpion?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>If ye then, being evil, know how to give good gifts unto your children: how much more shall _your_ heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to them that ask him?  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>¶ And he was casting out a devil, and it was dumb. And it came to pass, when the devil was gone out, the dumb spake; and the people wondered.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>But some of them said, He casteth out devils through Beelzebub the chief of the devils.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And others, tempting _him_, sought of him a sign from heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>But he, knowing their thoughts, said unto them, Every kingdom divided against itself is brought to desolation; and a house _divided_ against a house falleth.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>If Satan also be divided against himself, how shall his kingdom stand? because ye say that I cast out devils through Beelzebub.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And if I by Beelzebub cast out devils, by whom do your sons cast _them_ out? therefore shall they be your judges.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But if I with the finger of God cast out devils, no doubt the kingdom of God is come upon you.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>When a strong man armed keepeth his palace, his goods are in peace:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But when a stronger than he shall come upon him, and overcome him, he taketh from him all his armour wherein he trusted, and divideth his spoils.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>He that is not with me is against me: and he that gathereth not with me scattereth.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>When the unclean spirit is gone out of a man, he walketh through dry places, seeking rest; and finding none, he saith, I will return unto my house whence I came out.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And when he cometh, he findeth _it_ swept and garnished.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Then goeth he, and taketh _to him_ seven other spirits more wicked than himself; and they enter in, and dwell there: and the last _state_ of that man is worse than the first.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, as he spake these things, a certain woman of the company lifted up her voice, and said unto him, Blessed _is_ the womb that bare thee, and the paps which thou hast sucked.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>But he said, Yea rather, blessed _are_ they that hear the word of God, and keep it.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>¶ And when the people were gathered thick together, he began to say, This is an evil generation: they seek a sign; and there shall no sign be given it, but the sign of Jonas the prophet.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>For as Jonas was a sign unto the Ninevites, so shall also the Son of man be to this generation.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>The queen of the south shall rise up in the judgment with the men of this generation, and condemn them: for she came from the utmost parts of the earth to hear the wisdom of Solomon; and, behold, a greater than Solomon _is_ here.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>The men of Nineve shall rise up in the judgment with this generation, and shall condemn it: for they repented at the preaching of Jonas; and, behold, a greater than Jonas _is_ here.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>No man, when he hath lighted a candle, putteth _it_ in a secret place, neither under a bushel, but on a candlestick, that they which come in may see the light.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>The light of the body is the eye: therefore when thine eye is single, thy whole body also is full of light; but when _thine eye_ is evil, thy body also _is_ full of darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Take heed therefore that the light which is in thee be not darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>If thy whole body therefore _be_ full of light, having no part dark, the whole shall be full of light, as when the bright shining of a candle doth give thee light.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>¶ And as he spake, a certain Pharisee besought him to dine with him: and he went in, and sat down to meat.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And when the Pharisee saw _it_, he marvelled that he had not first washed before dinner.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And the Lord said unto him, Now do ye Pharisees make clean the outside of the cup and the platter; but your inward part is full of ravening and wickedness.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>_Ye_ fools, did not he that made that which is without make that which is within also?  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>But rather give alms of such things as ye have; and, behold, all things are clean unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>But woe unto you, Pharisees! for ye tithe mint and rue and all manner of herbs, and pass over judgment and the love of God: these ought ye to have done, and not to leave the other undone.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>Woe unto you, Pharisees! for ye love the uppermost seats in the synagogues, and greetings in the markets.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! for ye are as graves which appear not, and the men that walk over _them_ are not aware _of them_.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>¶ Then answered one of the lawyers, and said unto him, Master, thus saying thou reproachest us also.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And he said, Woe unto you also, _ye_ lawyers! for ye lade men with burdens grievous to be borne, and ye yourselves touch not the burdens with one of your fingers.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>Woe unto you! for ye build the sepulchres of the prophets, and your fathers killed them.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>Truly ye bear witness that ye allow the deeds of your fathers: for they indeed killed them, and ye build their sepulchres.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>Therefore also said the wisdom of God, I will send them prophets and apostles, and _some_ of them they shall slay and persecute:  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>That the blood of all the prophets, which was shed from the foundation of the world, may be required of this generation;  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>From the blood of Abel unto the blood of Zacharias, which perished between the altar and the temple: verily I say unto you, It shall be required of this generation.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>Woe unto you, lawyers! for ye have taken away the key of knowledge: ye entered not in yourselves, and them that were entering in ye hindered.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>And as he said these things unto them, the scribes and the Pharisees began to urge _him_ vehemently, and to provoke him to speak of many things:  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>Laying wait for him, and seeking to catch something out of his mouth, that they might accuse him.  

## CHAPTER 12.

In the mean time, when there were gathered together an innumerable multitude of people, insomuch that they trode one upon another, he began to say unto his disciples first of all, Beware ye of the leaven of the Pharisees, which is hypocrisy.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>For there is nothing covered, that shall not be revealed; neither hid, that shall not be known.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Therefore whatsoever ye have spoken in darkness shall be heard in the light; and that which ye have spoken in the ear in closets shall be proclaimed upon the housetops.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And I say unto you my friends, Be not afraid of them that kill the body, and after that have no more that they can do.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>But I will forewarn you whom ye shall fear: Fear him, which after he hath killed hath power to cast into hell; yea, I say unto you, Fear him.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>Are not five sparrows sold for two farthings, and not one of them is forgotten before God?  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But even the very hairs of your head are all numbered. Fear not therefore: ye are of more value than many sparrows.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>Also I say unto you, Whosoever shall confess me before men, him shall the Son of man also confess before the angels of God:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But he that denieth me before men shall be denied before the angels of God.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And whosoever shall speak a word against the Son of man, it shall be forgiven him: but unto him that blasphemeth against the Holy Ghost it shall not be forgiven.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And when they bring you unto the synagogues, and _unto_ magistrates, and powers, take ye no thought how or what thing ye shall answer, or what ye shall say:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>For the Holy Ghost shall teach you in the same hour what ye ought to say.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And one of the company said unto him, Master, speak to my brother, that he divide the inheritance with me.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, Man, who made me a judge or a divider over you?  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Take heed, and beware of covetousness: for a man’s life consisteth not in the abundance of the things which he possesseth.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And he spake a parable unto them, saying, The ground of a certain rich man brought forth plentifully:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he thought within himself, saying, What shall I do, because I have no room where to bestow my fruits?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And he said, This will I do: I will pull down my barns, and build greater; and there will I bestow all my fruits and my goods.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And I will say to my soul, Soul, thou hast much goods laid up for many years; take thine ease, eat, drink, _and_ be merry.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>But God said unto him, _Thou_ fool, this night thy soul shall be required of thee: then whose shall those things be, which thou hast provided?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>So _is_ he that layeth up treasure for himself, and is not rich toward God.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>¶ And he said unto his disciples, Therefore I say unto you, Take no thought for your life, what ye shall eat; neither for the body, what ye shall put on.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>The life is more than meat, and the body _is more_ than raiment.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Consider the ravens: for they neither sow nor reap; which neither have storehouse nor barn; and God feedeth them: how much more are ye better than the fowls?  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And which of you with taking thought can add to his stature one cubit?  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>If ye then be not able to do that thing which is least, why take ye thought for the rest?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Consider the lilies how they grow: they toil not, they spin not; and yet I say unto you, that Solomon in all his glory was not arrayed like one of these.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>If then God so clothe the grass, which is to day in the field, and to morrow is cast into the oven; how much more _will he clothe_ you, O ye of little faith?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And seek not ye what ye shall eat, or what ye shall drink, neither be ye of doubtful mind.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>For all these things do the nations of the world seek after: and your Father knoweth that ye have need of these things.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ But rather seek ye the kingdom of God; and all these things shall be added unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Fear not, little flock; for it is your Father’s good pleasure to give you the kingdom.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Sell that ye have, and give alms; provide yourselves bags which wax not old, a treasure in the heavens that faileth not, where no thief approacheth, neither moth corrupteth.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Let your loins be girded about, and _your_ lights burning;  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And ye yourselves like unto men that wait for their lord, when he will return from the wedding; that when he cometh and knocketh, they may open unto him immediately.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Blessed _are_ those servants, whom the lord when he cometh shall find watching: verily I say unto you, that he shall gird himself, and make them to sit down to meat, and will come forth and serve them.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And if he shall come in the second watch, or come in the third watch, and find _them_ so, blessed are those servants.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And this know, that if the goodman of the house had known what hour the thief would come, he would have watched, and not have suffered his house to be broken through.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>Be ye therefore ready also: for the Son of man cometh at an hour when ye think not.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>¶ Then Peter said unto him, Lord, speakest thou this parable unto us, or even to all?  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And the Lord said, Who then is that faithful and wise steward, whom _his_ lord shall make ruler over his household, to give _them their_ portion of meat in due season?  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>Blessed _is_ that servant, whom his lord when he cometh shall find so doing.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>Of a truth I say unto you, that he will make him ruler over all that he hath.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>But and if that servant say in his heart, My lord delayeth his coming; and shall begin to beat the menservants and maidens, and to eat and drink, and to be drunken;  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>The lord of that servant will come in a day when he looketh not for _him_, and at an hour when he is not aware, and will cut him in sunder, and will appoint him his portion with the unbelievers.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And that servant, which knew his lord’s will, and prepared not _himself_, neither did according to his will, shall be beaten with many _stripes_.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>But he that knew not, and did commit things worthy of stripes, shall be beaten with few _stripes_. For unto whomsoever much is given, of him shall be much required: and to whom men have committed much, of him they will ask the more.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>¶ I am come to send fire on the earth; and what will I, if it be already kindled?  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>But I have a baptism to be baptized with; and how am I straitened till it be accomplished!  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>Suppose ye that I am come to give peace on earth? I tell you, Nay; but rather division:  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>For from henceforth there shall be five in one house divided, three against two, and two against three.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>The father shall be divided against the son, and the son against the father; the mother against the daughter, and the daughter against the mother; the mother in law against her daughter in law, and the daughter in law against her mother in law.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>¶ And he said also to the people, When ye see a cloud rise out of the west, straightway ye say, There cometh a shower; and so it is.  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>And when _ye see_ the south wind blow, ye say, There will be heat; and it cometh to pass.  
<span class="verse-number">56&ensp;</span>_Ye_ hypocrites, ye can discern the face of the sky and of the earth; but how is it that ye do not discern this time?  
<span class="verse-number">57&ensp;</span>Yea, and why even of yourselves judge ye not what is right?  
<span class="verse-number">58&ensp;</span>¶ When thou goest with thine adversary to the magistrate, _as thou art_ in the way, give diligence that thou mayest be delivered from him; lest he hale thee to the judge, and the judge deliver thee to the officer, and the officer cast thee into prison.  
<span class="verse-number">59&ensp;</span>I tell thee, thou shalt not depart thence, till thou hast paid the very last mite.  

## CHAPTER 13.

There were present at that season some that told him of the Galilæans, whose blood Pilate had mingled with their sacrifices.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering said unto them, Suppose ye that these Galilæans were sinners above all the Galilæans, because they suffered such things?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>I tell you, Nay: but, except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Or those eighteen, upon whom the tower in Siloam fell, and slew them, think ye that they were sinners above all men that dwelt in Jerusalem?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>I tell you, Nay: but, except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>¶ He spake also this parable; A certain _man_ had a fig tree planted in his vineyard; and he came and sought fruit thereon, and found none.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then said he unto the dresser of his vineyard, Behold, these three years I come seeking fruit on this fig tree, and find none: cut it down; why cumbereth it the ground?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he answering said unto him, Lord, let it alone this year also, till I shall dig about it, and dung _it_:  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And if it bear fruit, _well_: and if not, _then_ after that thou shalt cut it down.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he was teaching in one of the synagogues on the sabbath.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, there was a woman which had a spirit of infirmity eighteen years, and was bowed together, and could in no wise lift up _herself_.   
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And when Jesus saw her, he called _her to him_, and said unto her, Woman, thou art loosed from thine infirmity.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And he laid _his_ hands on her: and immediately she was made straight, and glorified God.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the ruler of the synagogue answered with indignation, because that Jesus had healed on the sabbath day, and said unto the people, There are six days in which men ought to work: in them therefore come and be healed, and not on the sabbath day.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>The Lord then answered him, and said, _Thou_ hypocrite, doth not each one of you on the sabbath loose his ox or _his_ ass from the stall, and lead _him_ away to watering?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And ought not this woman, being a daughter of Abraham, whom Satan hath bound, lo, these eighteen years, be loosed from this bond on the sabbath day?  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And when he had said these things, all his adversaries were ashamed: and all the people rejoiced for all the glorious things that were done by him.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>¶ Then said he, Unto what is the kingdom of God like? and whereunto shall I resemble it?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>It is like a grain of mustard seed, which a man took, and cast into his garden; and it grew, and waxed a great tree; and the fowls of the air lodged in the branches of it.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And again he said, Whereunto shall I liken the kingdom of God?  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>It is like leaven, which a woman took and hid in three measures of meal, till the whole was leavened.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And he went through the cities and villages, teaching, and journeying toward Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Then said one unto him, Lord, are there few that be saved? And he said unto them,  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ Strive to enter in at the strait gate: for many, I say unto you, will seek to enter in, and shall not be able.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>When once the master of the house is risen up, and hath shut to the door, and ye begin to stand without, and to knock at the door, saying, Lord, Lord, open unto us; and he shall answer and say unto you, I know you not whence ye are:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Then shall ye begin to say, We have eaten and drunk in thy presence, and thou hast taught in our streets.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>But he shall say, I tell you, I know you not whence ye are; depart from me, all _ye_ workers of iniquity.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>There shall be weeping and gnashing of teeth, when ye shall see Abraham, and Isaac, and Jacob, and all the prophets, in the kingdom of God, and you _yourselves_ thrust out.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And they shall come from the east, and _from_ the west, and from the north, and _from_ the south, and shall sit down in the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And, behold, there are last which shall be first, and there are first which shall be last.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ The same day there came certain of the Pharisees, saying unto him, Get thee out, and depart hence: for Herod will kill thee.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Go ye, and tell that fox, Behold, I cast out devils, and I do cures to day and to morrow, and the third _day_ I shall be perfected.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Nevertheless I must walk to day, and to morrow, and the _day_ following: for it cannot be that a prophet perish out of Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>O Jerusalem, Jerusalem, which killest the prophets, and stonest them that are sent unto thee; how often would I have gathered thy children together, as a hen _doth gather_ her brood under _her_ wings, and ye would not!  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Behold, your house is left unto you desolate: and verily I say unto you, Ye shall not see me, until _the time_ come when ye shall say, Blessed _is_ he that cometh in the name of the Lord.  

## CHAPTER 14.

And it came to pass, as he went into the house of one of the chief Pharisees to eat bread on the sabbath day, that they watched him.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And, behold, there was a certain man before him which had the dropsy.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering spake unto the lawyers and Pharisees, saying, Is it lawful to heal on the sabbath day?  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And they held their peace. And he took _him_, and healed him, and let him go;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And answered them, saying, Which of you shall have an ass or an ox fallen into a pit, and will not straightway pull him out on the sabbath day?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And they could not answer him again to these things.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ And he put forth a parable to those which were bidden, when he marked how they chose out the chief rooms; saying unto them,  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>When thou art bidden of any _man_ to a wedding, sit not down in the highest room; lest a more honourable man than thou be bidden of him;  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he that bade thee and him come and say to thee, Give this man place; and thou begin with shame to take the lowest room.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>But when thou art bidden, go and sit down in the lowest room; that when he that bade thee cometh, he may say unto thee, Friend, go up higher: then shalt thou have worship in the presence of them that sit at meat with thee.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>For whosoever exalteth himself shall be abased; and he that humbleth himself shall be exalted.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ Then said he also to him that bade him, When thou makest a dinner or a supper, call not thy friends, nor thy brethren, neither thy kinsmen, nor _thy_ rich neighbours; lest they also bid thee again, and a recompence be made thee.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>But when thou makest a feast, call the poor, the maimed, the lame, the blind:  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And thou shalt be blessed; for they cannot recompense thee: for thou shalt be recompensed at the resurrection of the just.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>¶ And when one of them that sat at meat with him heard these things, he said unto him, Blessed _is_ he that shall eat bread in the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Then said he unto him, A certain man made a great supper, and bade many:  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And sent his servant at supper time to say to them that were bidden, Come; for all things are now ready.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they all with one _consent_ began to make excuse. The first said unto him, I have bought a piece of ground, and I must needs go and see it: I pray thee have me excused.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And another said, I have bought five yoke of oxen, and I go to prove them: I pray thee have me excused.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And another said, I have married a wife, and therefore I cannot come.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>So that servant came, and shewed his lord these things. Then the master of the house being angry said to his servant, Go out quickly into the streets and lanes of the city, and bring in hither the poor, and the maimed, and the halt, and the blind.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And the servant said, Lord, it is done as thou hast commanded, and yet there is room.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And the lord said unto the servant, Go out into the highways and hedges, and compel _them_ to come in, that my house may be filled.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For I say unto you, That none of those men which were bidden shall taste of my supper.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And there went great multitudes with him: and he turned, and said unto them,  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>If any _man_ come to me, and hate not his father, and mother, and wife, and children, and brethren, and sisters, yea, and his own life also, he cannot be my disciple.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And whosoever doth not bear his cross, and come after me, cannot be my disciple.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For which of you, intending to build a tower, sitteth not down first, and counteth the cost, whether he have _sufficient_ to finish _it_?  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Lest haply, after he hath laid the foundation, and is not able to finish _it_, all that behold _it_ begin to mock him,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Saying, This man began to build, and was not able to finish.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>Or what king, going to make war against another king, sitteth not down first, and consulteth whether he be able with ten thousand to meet him that cometh against him with twenty thousand?  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Or else, while the other is yet a great way off, he sendeth an ambassage, and desireth conditions of peace.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>So likewise, whosoever he be of you that forsaketh not all that he hath, he cannot be my disciple.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>¶ Salt _is_ good: but if the salt have lost his savour, wherewith shall it be seasoned?  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>It is neither fit for the land, nor yet for the dunghill; _but_ men cast it out. He that hath ears to hear, let him hear.  

## CHAPTER 15.

Then drew near unto him all the publicans and sinners for to hear him.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the Pharisees and scribes murmured, saying, This man receiveth sinners, and eateth with them.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ And he spake this parable unto them, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>What man of you, having an hundred sheep, if he lose one of them, doth not leave the ninety and nine in the wilderness, and go after that which is lost, until he find it?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And when he hath found _it_, he layeth _it_ on his shoulders, rejoicing.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And when he cometh home, he calleth together _his_ friends and neighbours, saying unto them, Rejoice with me; for I have found my sheep which was lost.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>I say unto you, that likewise joy shall be in heaven over one sinner that repenteth, more than over ninety and nine just persons, which need no repentance.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ Either what woman having ten pieces of silver, if she lose one piece, doth not light a candle, and sweep the house, and seek diligently till she find _it_?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And when she hath found _it_, she calleth _her_ friends and _her_ neighbours together, saying, Rejoice with me; for I have found the piece which I had lost.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Likewise, I say unto you, there is joy in the presence of the angels of God over one sinner that repenteth.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And he said, A certain man had two sons:  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And the younger of them said to _his_ father, Father, give me the portion of goods that falleth _to me_. And he divided unto them _his_ living.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And not many days after the younger son gathered all together, and took his journey into a far country, and there wasted his substance with riotous living.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And when he had spent all, there arose a mighty famine in that land; and he began to be in want.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he went and joined himself to a citizen of that country; and he sent him into his fields to feed swine.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And he would fain have filled his belly with the husks that the swine did eat: and no man gave unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And when he came to himself, he said, How many hired servants of my father’s have bread enough and to spare, and I perish with hunger!  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>I will arise and go to my father, and will say unto him, Father, I have sinned against heaven, and before thee,  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And am no more worthy to be called thy son: make me as one of thy hired servants.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And he arose, and came to his father. But when he was yet a great way off, his father saw him, and had compassion, and ran, and fell on his neck, and kissed him.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And the son said unto him, Father, I have sinned against heaven, and in thy sight, and am no more worthy to be called thy son.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>But the father said to his servants, Bring forth the best robe, and put _it_ on him; and put a ring on his hand, and shoes on _his_ feet:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And bring hither the fatted calf, and kill _it_; and let us eat, and be merry:  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For this my son was dead, and is alive again; he was lost, and is found. And they began to be merry.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Now his elder son was in the field: and as he came and drew nigh to the house, he heard musick and dancing.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And he called one of the servants, and asked what these things meant.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, Thy brother is come; and thy father hath killed the fatted calf, because he hath received him safe and sound.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And he was angry, and would not go in: therefore came his father out, and intreated him.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And he answering said to _his_ father, Lo, these many years do I serve thee, neither transgressed I at any time thy commandment: and yet thou never gavest me a kid, that I might make merry with my friends:  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>But as soon as this thy son was come, which hath devoured thy living with harlots, thou hast killed for him the fatted calf.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, Son, thou art ever with me, and all that I have is thine.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>It was meet that we should make merry, and be glad: for this thy brother was dead, and is alive again; and was lost, and is found.  

## CHAPTER 16.

And he said also unto his disciples, There was a certain rich man, which had a steward; and the same was accused unto him that he had wasted his goods.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he called him, and said unto him, How is it that I hear this of thee? give an account of thy stewardship; for thou mayest be no longer steward.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>Then the steward said within himself, What shall I do? for my lord taketh away from me the stewardship: I cannot dig; to beg I am ashamed.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>I am resolved what to do, that, when I am put out of the stewardship, they may receive me into their houses.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>So he called every one of his lord’s debtors _unto him_, and said unto the first, How much owest thou unto my lord?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he said, An hundred measures of oil. And he said unto him, Take thy bill, and sit down quickly, and write fifty.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Then said he to another, And how much owest thou? And he said, An hundred measures of wheat. And he said unto him, Take thy bill, and write fourscore.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And the lord commended the unjust steward, because he had done wisely: for the children of this world are in their generation wiser than the children of light.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And I say unto you, Make to yourselves friends of the mammon of unrighteousness; that, when ye fail, they may receive you into everlasting habitations.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>He that is faithful in that which is least is faithful also in much: and he that is unjust in the least is unjust also in much.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>If therefore ye have not been faithful in the unrighteous mammon, who will commit to your trust the true _riches_?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And if ye have not been faithful in that which is another man’s, who shall give you that which is your own?  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ No servant can serve two masters: for either he will hate the one, and love the other; or else he will hold to the one, and despise the other. Ye cannot serve God and mammon.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And the Pharisees also, who were covetous, heard all these things: and they derided him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Ye are they which justify yourselves before men; but God knoweth your hearts: for that which is highly esteemed among men is abomination in the sight of God.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>The law and the prophets _were_ until John: since that time the kingdom of God is preached, and every man presseth into it.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And it is easier for heaven and earth to pass, than one tittle of the law to fail.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Whosoever putteth away his wife, and marrieth another, committeth adultery: and whosoever marrieth her that is put away from _her_ husband committeth adultery.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ There was a certain rich man, which was clothed in purple and fine linen, and fared sumptuously every day:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And there was a certain beggar named Lazarus, which was laid at his gate, full of sores,  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And desiring to be fed with the crumbs which fell from the rich man’s table: moreover the dogs came and licked his sores.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that the beggar died, and was carried by the angels into Abraham’s bosom: the rich man also died, and was buried;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And in hell he lift up his eyes, being in torments, and seeth Abraham afar off, and Lazarus in his bosom.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And he cried and said, Father Abraham, have mercy on me, and send Lazarus, that he may dip the tip of his finger in water, and cool my tongue; for I am tormented in this flame.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But Abraham said, Son, remember that thou in thy lifetime receivedst thy good things, and likewise Lazarus evil things: but now he is comforted, and thou art tormented.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And beside all this, between us and you there is a great gulf fixed: so that they which would pass from hence to you cannot; neither can they pass to us, that _would come_ from thence.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>Then he said, I pray thee therefore, father, that thou wouldest send him to my father’s house:  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>For I have five brethren; that he may testify unto them, lest they also come into this place of torment.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>Abraham saith unto him, They have Moses and the prophets; let them hear them.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And he said, Nay, father Abraham: but if one went unto them from the dead, they will repent.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, If they hear not Moses and the prophets, neither will they be persuaded, though one rose from the dead.  

## CHAPTER 17.

Then said he unto the disciples, It is impossible but that offences will come: but woe _unto him_, through whom they come!  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>It were better for him that a millstone were hanged about his neck, and he cast into the sea, than that he should offend one of these little ones.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Take heed to yourselves: If thy brother trespass against thee, rebuke him; and if he repent, forgive him.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And if he trespass against thee seven times in a day, and seven times in a day turn again to thee, saying, I repent; thou shalt forgive him.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And the apostles said unto the Lord, Increase our faith.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the Lord said, If ye had faith as a grain of mustard seed, ye might say unto this sycamine tree, Be thou plucked up by the root, and be thou planted in the sea; and it should obey you.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>But which of you, having a servant plowing or feeding cattle, will say unto him by and by, when he is come from the field, Go and sit down to meat?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And will not rather say unto him, Make ready wherewith I may sup, and gird thyself, and serve me, till I have eaten and drunken; and afterward thou shalt eat and drink?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Doth he thank that servant because he did the things that were commanded him? I trow not.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>So likewise ye, when ye shall have done all those things which are commanded you, say, We are unprofitable servants: we have done that which was our duty to do.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, as he went to Jerusalem, that he passed through the midst of Samaria and Galilee.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And as he entered into a certain village, there met him ten men that were lepers, which stood afar off:  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And they lifted up _their_ voices, and said, Jesus, Master, have mercy on us.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And when he saw _them_, he said unto them, Go shew yourselves unto the priests. And it came to pass, that, as they went, they were cleansed.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And one of them, when he saw that he was healed, turned back, and with a loud voice glorified God,  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And fell down on _his_ face at his feet, giving him thanks: and he was a Samaritan.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering said, Were there not ten cleansed? but where _are_ the nine?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>There are not found that returned to give glory to God, save this stranger.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, Arise, go thy way: thy faith hath made thee whole.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>¶ And when he was demanded of the Pharisees, when the kingdom of God should come, he answered them and said, The kingdom of God cometh not with observation:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Neither shall they say, Lo here! or, lo there! for, behold, the kingdom of God is within you.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And he said unto the disciples, The days will come, when ye shall desire to see one of the days of the Son of man, and ye shall not see _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they shall say to you, See here; or, see there: go not after _them_, nor follow _them_.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>For as the lightning, that lighteneth out of the one _part_ under heaven, shineth unto the other _part_ under heaven; so shall also the Son of man be in his day.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>But first must he suffer many things, and be rejected of this generation.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And as it was in the days of Noe, so shall it be also in the days of the Son of man.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>They did eat, they drank, they married wives, they were given in marriage, until the day that Noe entered into the ark, and the flood came, and destroyed them all.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Likewise also as it was in the days of Lot; they did eat, they drank, they bought, they sold, they planted, they builded;  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>But the same day that Lot went out of Sodom it rained fire and brimstone from heaven, and destroyed _them_ all.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Even thus shall it be in the day when the Son of man is revealed.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>In that day, he which shall be upon the housetop, and his stuff in the house, let him not come down to take it away: and he that is in the field, let him likewise not return back.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Remember Lot’s wife.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Whosoever shall seek to save his life shall lose it; and whosoever shall lose his life shall preserve it.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>I tell you, in that night there shall be two _men_ in one bed; the one shall be taken, and the other shall be left.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>Two _women_ shall be grinding together; the one shall be taken, and the other left.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Two _men_ shall be in the field; the one shall be taken, and the other left.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And they answered and said unto him, Where, Lord? And he said unto them, Wheresoever the body _is_, thither will the eagles be gathered together.  

## CHAPTER 18.

And he spake a parable unto them _to this end_, that men ought always to pray, and not to faint;  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>Saying, There was in a city a judge, which feared not God, neither regarded man:  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And there was a widow in that city; and she came unto him, saying, Avenge me of mine adversary.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And he would not for a while: but afterward he said within himself, Though I fear not God, nor regard man;  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>Yet because this widow troubleth me, I will avenge her, lest by her continual coming she weary me.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And the Lord said, Hear what the unjust judge saith.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And shall not God avenge his own elect, which cry day and night unto him, though he bear long with them?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>I tell you that he will avenge them speedily. Nevertheless when the Son of man cometh, shall he find faith on the earth?  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And he spake this parable unto certain which trusted in themselves that they were righteous, and despised others:  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Two men went up into the temple to pray; the one a Pharisee, and the other a publican.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>The Pharisee stood and prayed thus with himself, God, I thank thee, that I am not as other men _are_, extortioners, unjust, adulterers, or even as this publican.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>I fast twice in the week, I give tithes of all that I possess.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And the publican, standing afar off, would not lift up so much as _his_ eyes unto heaven, but smote upon his breast, saying, God be merciful to me a sinner.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>I tell you, this man went down to his house justified _rather_ than the other: for every one that exalteth himself shall be abased; and he that humbleth himself shall be exalted.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And they brought unto him also infants, that he would touch them: but when _his_ disciples saw _it_, they rebuked them.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But Jesus called them _unto him_, and said, Suffer little children to come unto me, and forbid them not: for of such is the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>Verily I say unto you, Whosoever shall not receive the kingdom of God as a little child shall in no wise enter therein.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And a certain ruler asked him, saying, Good Master, what shall I do to inherit eternal life?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And Jesus said unto him, Why callest thou me good? none _is_ good, save one, _that is_, God.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Thou knowest the commandments, Do not commit adultery, Do not kill, Do not steal, Do not bear false witness, Honour thy father and thy mother.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And he said, All these have I kept from my youth up.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Now when Jesus heard these things, he said unto him, Yet lackest thou one thing: sell all that thou hast, and distribute unto the poor, and thou shalt have treasure in heaven: and come, follow me.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And when he heard this, he was very sorrowful: for he was very rich.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And when Jesus saw that he was very sorrowful, he said, How hardly shall they that have riches enter into the kingdom of God!  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>For it is easier for a camel to go through a needle’s eye, than for a rich man to enter into the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And they that heard _it_ said, Who then can be saved?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And he said, The things which are impossible with men are possible with God.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Then Peter said, Lo, we have left all, and followed thee.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Verily I say unto you, There is no man that hath left house, or parents, or brethren, or wife, or children, for the kingdom of God’s sake,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Who shall not receive manifold more in this present time, and in the world to come life everlasting.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ Then he took _unto him_ the twelve, and said unto them, Behold, we go up to Jerusalem, and all things that are written by the prophets concerning the Son of man shall be accomplished.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>For he shall be delivered unto the Gentiles, and shall be mocked, and spitefully entreated, and spitted on:  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And they shall scourge _him_, and put him to death: and the third day he shall rise again.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And they understood none of these things: and this saying was hid from them, neither knew they the things which were spoken.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>¶ And it came to pass, that as he was come nigh unto Jericho, a certain blind man sat by the way side begging:  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And hearing the multitude pass by, he asked what it meant.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And they told him, that Jesus of Nazareth passeth by.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And he cried, saying, Jesus, _thou_ Son of David, have mercy on me.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And they which went before rebuked him, that he should hold his peace: but he cried so much the more, _Thou_ Son of David, have mercy on me.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And Jesus stood, and commanded him to be brought unto him: and when he was come near, he asked him,  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>Saying, What wilt thou that I shall do unto thee? And he said, Lord, that I may receive my sight.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And Jesus said unto him, Receive thy sight: thy faith hath saved thee.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And immediately he received his sight, and followed him, glorifying God: and all the people, when they saw _it_, gave praise unto God.  

## CHAPTER 19.

And _Jesus_ entered and passed through Jericho.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And, behold, _there was_ a man named Zacchæus, which was the chief among the publicans, and he was rich.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he sought to see Jesus who he was; and could not for the press, because he was little of stature.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And he ran before, and climbed up into a sycomore tree to see him: for he was to pass that _way_.   
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And when Jesus came to the place, he looked up, and saw him, and said unto him, Zacchæus, make haste, and come down; for to day I must abide at thy house.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he made haste, and came down, and received him joyfully.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And when they saw _it_, they all murmured, saying, That he was gone to be guest with a man that is a sinner.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Zacchæus stood, and said unto the Lord; Behold, Lord, the half of my goods I give to the poor; and if I have taken any thing from any man by false accusation, I restore _him_ fourfold.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And Jesus said unto him, This day is salvation come to this house, forsomuch as he also is a son of Abraham.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And as they heard these things, he added and spake a parable, because he was nigh to Jerusalem, and because they thought that the kingdom of God should immediately appear.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>He said therefore, A certain nobleman went into a far country to receive for himself a kingdom, and to return.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And he called his ten servants, and delivered them ten pounds, and said unto them, Occupy till I come.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But his citizens hated him, and sent a message after him, saying, We will not have this _man_ to reign over us.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that when he was returned, having received the kingdom, then he commanded these servants to be called unto him, to whom he had given the money, that he might know how much every man had gained by trading.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>Then came the first, saying, Lord, thy pound hath gained ten pounds.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, Well, thou good servant: because thou hast been faithful in a very little, have thou authority over ten cities.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the second came, saying, Lord, thy pound hath gained five pounds.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And he said likewise to him, Be thou also over five cities.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And another came, saying, Lord, behold, _here is_ thy pound, which I have kept laid up in a napkin:  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>For I feared thee, because thou art an austere man: thou takest up that thou layedst not down, and reapest that thou didst not sow.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And he saith unto him, Out of thine own mouth will I judge thee, _thou_ wicked servant. Thou knewest that I was an austere man, taking up that I laid not down, and reaping that I did not sow:  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>Wherefore then gavest not thou my money into the bank, that at my coming I might have required mine own with usury?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And he said unto them that stood by, Take from him the pound, and give _it_ to him that hath ten pounds.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>(And they said unto him, Lord, he hath ten pounds.)  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>For I say unto you, That unto every one which hath shall be given; and from him that hath not, even that he hath shall be taken away from him.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>But those mine enemies, which would not that I should reign over them, bring hither, and slay _them_ before me.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>¶ And when he had thus spoken, he went before, ascending up to Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, when he was come nigh to Bethphage and Bethany, at the mount called _the mount_ of Olives, he sent two of his disciples,  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Saying, Go ye into the village over against _you_; in the which at your entering ye shall find a colt tied, whereon yet never man sat: loose him, and bring _him hither_.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And if any man ask you, Why do ye loose _him_? thus shall ye say unto him, Because the Lord hath need of him.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And they that were sent went their way, and found even as he had said unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And as they were loosing the colt, the owners thereof said unto them, Why loose ye the colt?  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And they said, The Lord hath need of him.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And they brought him to Jesus: and they cast their garments upon the colt, and they set Jesus thereon.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And as he went, they spread their clothes in the way.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And when he was come nigh, even now at the descent of the mount of Olives, the whole multitude of the disciples began to rejoice and praise God with a loud voice for all the mighty works that they had seen;  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>Saying, Blessed _be_ the King that cometh in the name of the Lord: peace in heaven, and glory in the highest.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>And some of the Pharisees from among the multitude said unto him, Master, rebuke thy disciples.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And he answered and said unto them, I tell you that, if these should hold their peace, the stones would immediately cry out.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>¶ And when he was come near, he beheld the city, and wept over it,  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>Saying, If thou hadst known, even thou, at least in this thy day, the things _which belong_ unto thy peace! but now they are hid from thine eyes.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>For the days shall come upon thee, that thine enemies shall cast a trench about thee, and compass thee round, and keep thee in on every side,  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And shall lay thee even with the ground, and thy children within thee; and they shall not leave in thee one stone upon another; because thou knewest not the time of thy visitation.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And he went into the temple, and began to cast out them that sold therein, and them that bought;  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>Saying unto them, It is written, My house is the house of prayer: but ye have made it a den of thieves.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And he taught daily in the temple. But the chief priests and the scribes and the chief of the people sought to destroy him,  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And could not find what they might do: for all the people were very attentive to hear him.  

## CHAPTER 20.

And it came to pass, _that_ on one of those days, as he taught the people in the temple, and preached the gospel, the chief priests and the scribes came upon _him_ with the elders,  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And spake unto him, saying, Tell us, by what authority doest thou these things? or who is he that gave thee this authority?  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he answered and said unto them, I will also ask you one thing; and answer me:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>The baptism of John, was it from heaven, or of men?  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they reasoned with themselves, saying, If we shall say, From heaven; he will say, Why then believed ye him not?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>But and if we say, Of men; all the people will stone us: for they be persuaded that John was a prophet.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they answered, that they could not tell whence _it was_.   
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And Jesus said unto them, Neither tell I you by what authority I do these things.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then began he to speak to the people this parable; A certain man planted a vineyard, and let it forth to husbandmen, and went into a far country for a long time.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And at the season he sent a servant to the husbandmen, that they should give him of the fruit of the vineyard: but the husbandmen beat him, and sent _him_ away empty.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And again he sent another servant: and they beat him also, and entreated _him_ shamefully, and sent _him_ away empty.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And again he sent a third: and they wounded him also, and cast _him_ out.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>Then said the lord of the vineyard, What shall I do? I will send my beloved son: it may be they will reverence _him_ when they see him.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>But when the husbandmen saw him, they reasoned among themselves, saying, This is the heir: come, let us kill him, that the inheritance may be ours.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>So they cast him out of the vineyard, and killed _him_. What therefore shall the lord of the vineyard do unto them?  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>He shall come and destroy these husbandmen, and shall give the vineyard to others. And when they heard _it_, they said, God forbid.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he beheld them, and said, What is this then that is written, The stone which the builders rejected, the same is become the head of the corner?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>Whosoever shall fall upon that stone shall be broken; but on whomsoever it shall fall, it will grind him to powder.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And the chief priests and the scribes the same hour sought to lay hands on him; and they feared the people: for they perceived that he had spoken this parable against them.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And they watched _him_, and sent forth spies, which should feign themselves just men, that they might take hold of his words, that so they might deliver him unto the power and authority of the governor.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>And they asked him, saying, Master, we know that thou sayest and teachest rightly, neither acceptest thou the person _of any_, but teachest the way of God truly:  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Is it lawful for us to give tribute unto Cæsar, or no?  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But he perceived their craftiness, and said unto them, Why tempt ye me?  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>Shew me a penny. Whose image and superscription hath it? They answered and said, Cæsar’s.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Render therefore unto Cæsar the things which be Cæsar’s, and unto God the things which be God’s.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And they could not take hold of his words before the people: and they marvelled at his answer, and held their peace.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ Then came to _him_ certain of the Sadducees, which deny that there is any resurrection; and they asked him,  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Saying, Master, Moses wrote unto us, If any man’s brother die, having a wife, and he die without children, that his brother should take his wife, and raise up seed unto his brother.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>There were therefore seven brethren: and the first took a wife, and died without children.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And the second took her to wife, and he died childless.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And the third took her; and in like manner the seven also: and they left no children, and died.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Last of all the woman died also.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Therefore in the resurrection whose wife of them is she? for seven had her to wife.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And Jesus answering said unto them, The children of this world marry, and are given in marriage:  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>But they which shall be accounted worthy to obtain that world, and the resurrection from the dead, neither marry, nor are given in marriage:  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Neither can they die any more: for they are equal unto the angels; and are the children of God, being the children of the resurrection.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>Now that the dead are raised, even Moses shewed at the bush, when he calleth the Lord the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>For he is not a God of the dead, but of the living: for all live unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>¶ Then certain of the scribes answering said, Master, thou hast well said.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And after that they durst not ask him any _question at all_.   
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, How say they that Christ is David’s son?  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And David himself saith in the book of Psalms, The <span class="small-caps">Lord</span> said unto my Lord, Sit thou on my right hand,  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>Till I make thine enemies thy footstool.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>David therefore calleth him Lord, how is he then his son?  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>¶ Then in the audience of all the people he said unto his disciples,  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>Beware of the scribes, which desire to walk in long robes, and love greetings in the markets, and the highest seats in the synagogues, and the chief rooms at feasts;  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>Which devour widows’ houses, and for a shew make long prayers: the same shall receive greater damnation.  

## CHAPTER 21.

And he looked up, and saw the rich men casting their gifts into the treasury.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And he saw also a certain poor widow casting in thither two mites.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And he said, Of a truth I say unto you, that this poor widow hath cast in more than they all:  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>For all these have of their abundance cast in unto the offerings of God: but she of her penury hath cast in all the living that she had.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>¶ And as some spake of the temple, how it was adorned with goodly stones and gifts, he said,  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>_As for_ these things which ye behold, the days will come, in the which there shall not be left one stone upon another, that shall not be thrown down.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And they asked him, saying, Master, but when shall these things be? and what sign _will there be_ when these things shall come to pass?  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he said, Take heed that ye be not deceived: for many shall come in my name, saying, I am _Christ_; and the time draweth near: go ye not therefore after them.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>But when ye shall hear of wars and commotions, be not terrified: for these things must first come to pass; but the end _is_ not by and by.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>Then said he unto them, Nation shall rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom:  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And great earthquakes shall be in divers places, and famines, and pestilences; and fearful sights and great signs shall there be from heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>But before all these, they shall lay their hands on you, and persecute _you_, delivering _you_ up to the synagogues, and into prisons, being brought before kings and rulers for my name’s sake.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And it shall turn to you for a testimony.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Settle _it_ therefore in your hearts, not to meditate before what ye shall answer:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>For I will give you a mouth and wisdom, which all your adversaries shall not be able to gainsay nor resist.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>And ye shall be betrayed both by parents, and brethren, and kinsfolks, and friends; and _some_ of you shall they cause to be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And ye shall be hated of all _men_ for my name’s sake.  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>But there shall not an hair of your head perish.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>In your patience possess ye your souls.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And when ye shall see Jerusalem compassed with armies, then know that the desolation thereof is nigh.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>Then let them which are in Judæa flee to the mountains; and let them which are in the midst of it depart out; and let not them that are in the countries enter thereinto.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>For these be the days of vengeance, that all things which are written may be fulfilled.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>But woe unto them that are with child, and to them that give suck, in those days! for there shall be great distress in the land, and wrath upon this people.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And they shall fall by the edge of the sword, and shall be led away captive into all nations: and Jerusalem shall be trodden down of the Gentiles, until the times of the Gentiles be fulfilled.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>¶ And there shall be signs in the sun, and in the moon, and in the stars; and upon the earth distress of nations, with perplexity; the sea and the waves roaring;  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Men’s hearts failing them for fear, and for looking after those things which are coming on the earth: for the powers of heaven shall be shaken.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And then shall they see the Son of man coming in a cloud with power and great glory.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And when these things begin to come to pass, then look up, and lift up your heads; for your redemption draweth nigh.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And he spake to them a parable; Behold the fig tree, and all the trees;  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>When they now shoot forth, ye see and know of your own selves that summer is now nigh at hand.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>So likewise ye, when ye see these things come to pass, know ye that the kingdom of God is nigh at hand.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>Verily I say unto you, This generation shall not pass away, till all be fulfilled.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>Heaven and earth shall pass away: but my words shall not pass away.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>¶ And take heed to yourselves, lest at any time your hearts be overcharged with surfeiting, and drunkenness, and cares of this life, and _so_ that day come upon you unawares.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>For as a snare shall it come on all them that dwell on the face of the whole earth.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Watch ye therefore, and pray always, that ye may be accounted worthy to escape all these things that shall come to pass, and to stand before the Son of man.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And in the day time he was teaching in the temple; and at night he went out, and abode in the mount that is called _the mount_ of Olives.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And all the people came early in the morning to him in the temple, for to hear him.  

## CHAPTER 22.

Now the feast of unleavened bread drew nigh, which is called the Passover.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And the chief priests and scribes sought how they might kill him; for they feared the people.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>¶ Then entered Satan into Judas surnamed Iscariot, being of the number of the twelve.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And he went his way, and communed with the chief priests and captains, how he might betray him unto them.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they were glad, and covenanted to give him money.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>And he promised, and sought opportunity to betray him unto them in the absence of the multitude.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>¶ Then came the day of unleavened bread, when the passover must be killed.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And he sent Peter and John, saying, Go and prepare us the passover, that we may eat.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And they said unto him, Where wilt thou that we prepare?  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Behold, when ye are entered into the city, there shall a man meet you, bearing a pitcher of water; follow him into the house where he entereth in.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And ye shall say unto the goodman of the house, The Master saith unto thee, Where is the guestchamber, where I shall eat the passover with my disciples?  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>And he shall shew you a large upper room furnished: there make ready.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>And they went, and found as he had said unto them: and they made ready the passover.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And when the hour was come, he sat down, and the twelve apostles with him.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, With desire I have desired to eat this passover with you before I suffer:  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>For I say unto you, I will not any more eat thereof, until it be fulfilled in the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he took the cup, and gave thanks, and said, Take this, and divide _it_ among yourselves:  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>For I say unto you, I will not drink of the fruit of the vine, until the kingdom of God shall come.  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>¶ And he took bread, and gave thanks, and brake _it_, and gave unto them, saying, This is my body which is given for you: this do in remembrance of me.  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Likewise also the cup after supper, saying, This cup _is_ the new testament in my blood, which is shed for you.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>¶ But, behold, the hand of him that betrayeth me _is_ with me on the table.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And truly the Son of man goeth, as it was determined: but woe unto that man by whom he is betrayed!  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they began to enquire among themselves, which of them it was that should do this thing.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>¶ And there was also a strife among them, which of them should be accounted the greatest.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, The kings of the Gentiles exercise lordship over them; and they that exercise authority upon them are called benefactors.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>But ye _shall_ not _be_ so: but he that is greatest among you, let him be as the younger; and he that is chief, as he that doth serve.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>For whether _is_ greater, he that sitteth at meat, or he that serveth? _is_ not he that sitteth at meat? but I am among you as he that serveth.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>Ye are they which have continued with me in my temptations.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>And I appoint unto you a kingdom, as my Father hath appointed unto me;  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>That ye may eat and drink at my table in my kingdom, and sit on thrones judging the twelve tribes of Israel.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>¶ And the Lord said, Simon, Simon, behold, Satan hath desired _to have_ you, that he may sift _you_ as wheat:  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>But I have prayed for thee, that thy faith fail not: and when thou art converted, strengthen thy brethren.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And he said unto him, Lord, I am ready to go with thee, both into prison, and to death.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>And he said, I tell thee, Peter, the cock shall not crow this day, before that thou shalt thrice deny that thou knowest me.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, When I sent you without purse, and scrip, and shoes, lacked ye any thing? And they said, Nothing.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>Then said he unto them, But now, he that hath a purse, let him take _it_, and likewise _his_ scrip: and he that hath no sword, let him sell his garment, and buy one.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>For I say unto you, that this that is written must yet be accomplished in me, And he was reckoned among the transgressors: for the things concerning me have an end.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And they said, Lord, behold, here _are_ two swords. And he said unto them, It is enough.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>¶ And he came out, and went, as he was wont, to the mount of Olives; and his disciples also followed him.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And when he was at the place, he said unto them, Pray that ye enter not into temptation.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And he was withdrawn from them about a stone’s cast, and kneeled down, and prayed,  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>Saying, Father, if thou be willing, remove this cup from me: nevertheless not my will, but thine, be done.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And there appeared an angel unto him from heaven, strengthening him.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And being in an agony he prayed more earnestly: and his sweat was as it were great drops of blood falling down to the ground.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And when he rose up from prayer, and was come to his disciples, he found them sleeping for sorrow,  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And said unto them, Why sleep ye? rise and pray, lest ye enter into temptation.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>¶ And while he yet spake, behold a multitude, and he that was called Judas, one of the twelve, went before them, and drew near unto Jesus to kiss him.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>But Jesus said unto him, Judas, betrayest thou the Son of man with a kiss?  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>When they which were about him saw what would follow, they said unto him, Lord, shall we smite with the sword?  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>¶ And one of them smote the servant of the high priest, and cut off his right ear.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>And Jesus answered and said, Suffer ye thus far. And he touched his ear, and healed him.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>Then Jesus said unto the chief priests, and captains of the temple, and the elders, which were come to him, Be ye come out, as against a thief, with swords and staves?  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>When I was daily with you in the temple, ye stretched forth no hands against me: but this is your hour, and the power of darkness.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>¶ Then took they him, and led _him_, and brought him into the high priest’s house. And Peter followed afar off.  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>And when they had kindled a fire in the midst of the hall, and were set down together, Peter sat down among them.  
<span class="verse-number">56&ensp;</span>But a certain maid beheld him as he sat by the fire, and earnestly looked upon him, and said, This man was also with him.  
<span class="verse-number">57&ensp;</span>And he denied him, saying, Woman, I know him not.  
<span class="verse-number">58&ensp;</span>And after a little while another saw him, and said, Thou art also of them. And Peter said, Man, I am not.  
<span class="verse-number">59&ensp;</span>And about the space of one hour after another confidently affirmed, saying, Of a truth this _fellow_ also was with him: for he is a Galilæan.  
<span class="verse-number">60&ensp;</span>And Peter said, Man, I know not what thou sayest. And immediately, while he yet spake, the cock crew.  
<span class="verse-number">61&ensp;</span>And the Lord turned, and looked upon Peter. And Peter remembered the word of the Lord, how he had said unto him, Before the cock crow, thou shalt deny me thrice.  
<span class="verse-number">62&ensp;</span>And Peter went out, and wept bitterly.  
<span class="verse-number">63&ensp;</span>¶ And the men that held Jesus mocked him, and smote _him_.   
<span class="verse-number">64&ensp;</span>And when they had blindfolded him, they struck him on the face, and asked him, saying, Prophesy, who is it that smote thee?  
<span class="verse-number">65&ensp;</span>And many other things blasphemously spake they against him.  
<span class="verse-number">66&ensp;</span>¶ And as soon as it was day, the elders of the people and the chief priests and the scribes came together, and led him into their council, saying,  
<span class="verse-number">67&ensp;</span>Art thou the Christ? tell us. And he said unto them, If I tell you, ye will not believe:  
<span class="verse-number">68&ensp;</span>And if I also ask _you_, ye will not answer me, nor let _me_ go.  
<span class="verse-number">69&ensp;</span>Hereafter shall the Son of man sit on the right hand of the power of God.  
<span class="verse-number">70&ensp;</span>Then said they all, Art thou then the Son of God? And he said unto them, Ye say that I am.  
<span class="verse-number">71&ensp;</span>And they said, What need we any further witness? for we ourselves have heard of his own mouth.  

## CHAPTER 23.

And the whole multitude of them arose, and led him unto Pilate.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And they began to accuse him, saying, We found this _fellow_ perverting the nation, and forbidding to give tribute to Cæsar, saying that he himself is Christ a King.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And Pilate asked him, saying, Art thou the King of the Jews? And he answered him and said, Thou sayest _it_.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>Then said Pilate to the chief priests and _to_ the people, I find no fault in this man.  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And they were the more fierce, saying, He stirreth up the people, teaching throughout all Jewry, beginning from Galilee to this place.  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>When Pilate heard of Galilee, he asked whether the man were a Galilæan.  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>And as soon as he knew that he belonged unto Herod’s jurisdiction, he sent him to Herod, who himself also was at Jerusalem at that time.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>¶ And when Herod saw Jesus, he was exceeding glad: for he was desirous to see him of a long _season_, because he had heard many things of him; and he hoped to have seen some miracle done by him.  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>Then he questioned with him in many words; but he answered him nothing.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>And the chief priests and scribes stood and vehemently accused him.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And Herod with his men of war set him at nought, and mocked _him_, and arrayed him in a gorgeous robe, and sent him again to Pilate.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>¶ And the same day Pilate and Herod were made friends together: for before they were at enmity between themselves.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And Pilate, when he had called together the chief priests and the rulers and the people,  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>Said unto them, Ye have brought this man unto me, as one that perverteth the people: and, behold, I, having examined _him_ before you, have found no fault in this man touching those things whereof ye accuse him:  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>No, nor yet Herod: for I sent you to him; and, lo, nothing worthy of death is done unto him.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>I will therefore chastise him, and release _him_.   
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>(For of necessity he must release one unto them at the feast.)  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And they cried out all at once, saying, Away with this _man_, and release unto us Barabbas:  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>(Who for a certain sedition made in the city, and for murder, was cast into prison.)  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>Pilate therefore, willing to release Jesus, spake again to them.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But they cried, saying, Crucify _him_, crucify him.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>And he said unto them the third time, Why, what evil hath he done? I have found no cause of death in him: I will therefore chastise him, and let _him_ go.  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And they were instant with loud voices, requiring that he might be crucified. And the voices of them and of the chief priests prevailed.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And Pilate gave sentence that it should be as they required.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>And he released unto them him that for sedition and murder was cast into prison, whom they had desired; but he delivered Jesus to their will.  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>And as they led him away, they laid hold upon one Simon, a Cyrenian, coming out of the country, and on him they laid the cross, that he might bear _it_ after Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>¶ And there followed him a great company of people, and of women, which also bewailed and lamented him.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>But Jesus turning unto them said, Daughters of Jerusalem, weep not for me, but weep for yourselves, and for your children.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>For, behold, the days are coming, in the which they shall say, Blessed _are_ the barren, and the wombs that never bare, and the paps which never gave suck.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>Then shall they begin to say to the mountains, Fall on us; and to the hills, Cover us.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>For if they do these things in a green tree, what shall be done in the dry?  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And there were also two other, malefactors, led with him to be put to death.  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And when they were come to the place, which is called Calvary, there they crucified him, and the malefactors, one on the right hand, and the other on the left.  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>¶ Then said Jesus, Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do. And they parted his raiment, and cast lots.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And the people stood beholding. And the rulers also with them derided _him_, saying, He saved others; let him save himself, if he be Christ, the chosen of God.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>And the soldiers also mocked him, coming to him, and offering him vinegar,  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>And saying, If thou be the king of the Jews, save thyself.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And a superscription also was written over him in letters of Greek, and Latin, and Hebrew, THIS IS THE KING OF THE JEWS.  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>¶ And one of the malefactors which were hanged railed on him, saying, If thou be Christ, save thyself and us.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>But the other answering rebuked him, saying, Dost not thou fear God, seeing thou art in the same condemnation?  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And we indeed justly; for we receive the due reward of our deeds: but this man hath done nothing amiss.  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And he said unto Jesus, Lord, remember me when thou comest into thy kingdom.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And Jesus said unto him, Verily I say unto thee, To day shalt thou be with me in paradise.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And it was about the sixth hour, and there was a darkness over all the earth until the ninth hour.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>And the sun was darkened, and the veil of the temple was rent in the midst.  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>¶ And when Jesus had cried with a loud voice, he said, Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit: and having said thus, he gave up the ghost.  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>Now when the centurion saw what was done, he glorified God, saying, Certainly this was a righteous man.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And all the people that came together to that sight, beholding the things which were done, smote their breasts, and returned.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>And all his acquaintance, and the women that followed him from Galilee, stood afar off, beholding these things.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, _there was_ a man named Joseph, a counsellor; _and he was_ a good man, and a just:  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>(The same had not consented to the counsel and deed of them;) _he was_ of Arimathæa, a city of the Jews: who also himself waited for the kingdom of God.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>This _man_ went unto Pilate, and begged the body of Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>And he took it down, and wrapped it in linen, and laid it in a sepulchre that was hewn in stone, wherein never man before was laid.  
<span class="verse-number">54&ensp;</span>And that day was the preparation, and the sabbath drew on.  
<span class="verse-number">55&ensp;</span>And the women also, which came with him from Galilee, followed after, and beheld the sepulchre, and how his body was laid.  
<span class="verse-number">56&ensp;</span>And they returned, and prepared spices and ointments; and rested the sabbath day according to the commandment.  

## CHAPTER 24.

Now upon the first _day_ of the week, very early in the morning, they came unto the sepulchre, bringing the spices which they had prepared, and certain _others_ with them.  
<span class="verse-number">2&ensp;</span>And they found the stone rolled away from the sepulchre.  
<span class="verse-number">3&ensp;</span>And they entered in, and found not the body of the Lord Jesus.  
<span class="verse-number">4&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, as they were much perplexed thereabout, behold, two men stood by them in shining garments:  
<span class="verse-number">5&ensp;</span>And as they were afraid, and bowed down _their_ faces to the earth, they said unto them, Why seek ye the living among the dead?  
<span class="verse-number">6&ensp;</span>He is not here, but is risen: remember how he spake unto you when he was yet in Galilee,  
<span class="verse-number">7&ensp;</span>Saying, The Son of man must be delivered into the hands of sinful men, and be crucified, and the third day rise again.  
<span class="verse-number">8&ensp;</span>And they remembered his words,  
<span class="verse-number">9&ensp;</span>And returned from the sepulchre, and told all these things unto the eleven, and to all the rest.  
<span class="verse-number">10&ensp;</span>It was Mary Magdalene, and Joanna, and Mary _the mother_ of James, and other _women that were_ with them, which told these things unto the apostles.  
<span class="verse-number">11&ensp;</span>And their words seemed to them as idle tales, and they believed them not.  
<span class="verse-number">12&ensp;</span>Then arose Peter, and ran unto the sepulchre; and stooping down, he beheld the linen clothes laid by themselves, and departed, wondering in himself at that which was come to pass.  
<span class="verse-number">13&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, two of them went that same day to a village called Emmaus, which was from Jerusalem _about_ threescore furlongs.  
<span class="verse-number">14&ensp;</span>And they talked together of all these things which had happened.  
<span class="verse-number">15&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, that, while they communed _together_ and reasoned, Jesus himself drew near, and went with them.  
<span class="verse-number">16&ensp;</span>But their eyes were holden that they should not know him.  
<span class="verse-number">17&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, What manner of communications _are_ these that ye have one to another, as ye walk, and are sad?  
<span class="verse-number">18&ensp;</span>And the one of them, whose name was Cleopas, answering said unto him, Art thou only a stranger in Jerusalem, and hast not known the things which are come to pass there in these days?  
<span class="verse-number">19&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, What things? And they said unto him, Concerning Jesus of Nazareth, which was a prophet mighty in deed and word before God and all the people:  
<span class="verse-number">20&ensp;</span>And how the chief priests and our rulers delivered him to be condemned to death, and have crucified him.  
<span class="verse-number">21&ensp;</span>But we trusted that it had been he which should have redeemed Israel: and beside all this, to day is the third day since these things were done.  
<span class="verse-number">22&ensp;</span>Yea, and certain women also of our company made us astonished, which were early at the sepulchre;  
<span class="verse-number">23&ensp;</span>And when they found not his body, they came, saying, that they had also seen a vision of angels, which said that he was alive.  
<span class="verse-number">24&ensp;</span>And certain of them which were with us went to the sepulchre, and found _it_ even so as the women had said: but him they saw not.  
<span class="verse-number">25&ensp;</span>Then he said unto them, O fools, and slow of heart to believe all that the prophets have spoken:  
<span class="verse-number">26&ensp;</span>Ought not Christ to have suffered these things, and to enter into his glory?  
<span class="verse-number">27&ensp;</span>And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, he expounded unto them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself.  
<span class="verse-number">28&ensp;</span>And they drew nigh unto the village, whither they went: and he made as though he would have gone further.  
<span class="verse-number">29&ensp;</span>But they constrained him, saying, Abide with us: for it is toward evening, and the day is far spent. And he went in to tarry with them.  
<span class="verse-number">30&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, as he sat at meat with them, he took bread, and blessed _it_, and brake, and gave to them.  
<span class="verse-number">31&ensp;</span>And their eyes were opened, and they knew him; and he vanished out of their sight.  
<span class="verse-number">32&ensp;</span>And they said one to another, Did not our heart burn within us, while he talked with us by the way, and while he opened to us the scriptures?  
<span class="verse-number">33&ensp;</span>And they rose up the same hour, and returned to Jerusalem, and found the eleven gathered together, and them that were with them,  
<span class="verse-number">34&ensp;</span>Saying, The Lord is risen indeed, and hath appeared to Simon.  
<span class="verse-number">35&ensp;</span>And they told what things _were done_ in the way, and how he was known of them in breaking of bread.  
<span class="verse-number">36&ensp;</span>¶ And as they thus spake, Jesus himself stood in the midst of them, and saith unto them, Peace _be_ unto you.  
<span class="verse-number">37&ensp;</span>But they were terrified and affrighted, and supposed that they had seen a spirit.  
<span class="verse-number">38&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, Why are ye troubled? and why do thoughts arise in your hearts?  
<span class="verse-number">39&ensp;</span>Behold my hands and my feet, that it is I myself: handle me, and see; for a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye see me have.  
<span class="verse-number">40&ensp;</span>And when he had thus spoken, he shewed them _his_ hands and _his_ feet.  
<span class="verse-number">41&ensp;</span>And while they yet believed not for joy, and wondered, he said unto them, Have ye here any meat?  
<span class="verse-number">42&ensp;</span>And they gave him a piece of a broiled fish, and of an honeycomb.  
<span class="verse-number">43&ensp;</span>And he took _it_, and did eat before them.  
<span class="verse-number">44&ensp;</span>And he said unto them, These _are_ the words which I spake unto you, while I was yet with you, that all things must be fulfilled, which were written in the law of Moses, and _in_ the prophets, and _in_ the psalms, concerning me.  
<span class="verse-number">45&ensp;</span>Then opened he their understanding, that they might understand the scriptures,  
<span class="verse-number">46&ensp;</span>And said unto them, Thus it is written, and thus it behoved Christ to suffer, and to rise from the dead the third day:  
<span class="verse-number">47&ensp;</span>And that repentance and remission of sins should be preached in his name among all nations, beginning at Jerusalem.  
<span class="verse-number">48&ensp;</span>And ye are witnesses of these things.  
<span class="verse-number">49&ensp;</span>¶ And, behold, I send the promise of my Father upon you: but tarry ye in the city of Jerusalem, until ye be endued with power from on high.  
<span class="verse-number">50&ensp;</span>¶ And he led them out as far as to Bethany, and he lifted up his hands, and blessed them.  
<span class="verse-number">51&ensp;</span>And it came to pass, while he blessed them, he was parted from them, and carried up into heaven.  
<span class="verse-number">52&ensp;</span>And they worshipped him, and returned to Jerusalem with great joy:  
<span class="verse-number">53&ensp;</span>And were continually in the temple, praising and blessing God. Amen.
